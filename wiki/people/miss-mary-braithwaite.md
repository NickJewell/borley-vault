# Miss Mary E. Braithwaite, J.P.

Sister of [[wj-braithwaite|W. John Braithwaite]]; resident of Brook House, Long Melford, Suffolk. It was Mary Braithwaite who introduced [[harry-price|Harry Price]] to the Braithwaite family's Borley connection, and directed him to his brother's widow.

## Role

During Price's visit to Borley in May 1945, the Rev. [[rev-ac-henning|A. C. Henning]] introduced Price to Miss Braithwaite. She told Price that her brother had attended an extraordinary séance at the Rectory in August 1931, and that if he approached his widow — living at "Greenwood," Bidborough Ridge, Tunbridge Wells — she would perhaps allow him to see the report.

Mary Braithwaite confirmed that "from childhood" her brother had known Borley Rectory and the Bull family, and that he had heard of the haunting "even in those early days" — establishing the family's independent long-standing connection to the Borley story, prior to Price's involvement.

She also held the status of Justice of the Peace, lending social credibility to the chain of introduction.

## Significance

Without Mary Braithwaite's introduction at that May 1945 Borley visit, Price would never have known of her brother's 5,000-word séance report or the surviving paper-message — both of which entered the Borley archive only in the last months before Price completed *The End of Borley Rectory*. She is thus the inadvertent gateway through which the [[braithwaite-report|Braithwaite Report]] became part of the permanent record.
