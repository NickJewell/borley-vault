# A. H. Wesencraft

Curator of the **Harry Price Library** at the University of London from 1956 (succeeding Dr. H.P. Pafford). Cared for Price's extensive collection of books and files from that year onward. First met Harry Price in 1937, when Price was negotiating the gift of his library to the University.

## Views on Borley (Letter to Banks, June 10, 1985)

Wesencraft provided [[Ivan Banks]] a written assessment:

- Price was "sincere, devoted to his work" and liked talking about it to anyone who cared to listen
- After studying the Borley dossier, a case "could be made against Price on the grounds of exaggeration and possible distortion of evidence" — specifically, a letter exists in which Price admits Mrs Foyster was probably faking some phenomena, not mentioned in his books
- The [[the-borley-report|1956 SPR report]] was "very much a case for the prosecution and needed a suitable rejoinder" — provided by the Hastings report
- Met Robert Hastings who worked in the HPL; became convinced "the Borley story cannot be dismissed as a journalistic stunt"
- James Turner (Rectory Cottage 1947–50) "assured me there was definitely something very mysterious about the place"
- Wesencraft himself had visited Borley many times and noted "the extraordinary stillness which pervades the location" — though he never experienced psychic manifestations

## On the Locked Book

Wesencraft expressed doubts to [[Ivan Banks]] about [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]]'s claim to ownership of the Locked Book and his right to sell it for over £1,000. The then-curator (Dr. Pafford) had stated in 1959 that there was "a strong suspicion that the Locked Book was removed from the library 'under debatable circumstances'."

Wesencraft confirmed to Banks that Price had "an extensive knowledge of the paper-making business" — supporting Banks's theory that Price's Shropshire paper-trade links were real rather than invented.
