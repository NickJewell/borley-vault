# Rev. Father M. J. Moriarty

Roman Catholic priest at **Westminster Cathedral**; the first expert consulted by [[harry-price|Harry Price]] after the discovery of the copper pendant and gold pendant at the August 1943 Borley excavation. Although he did not visit [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] or witness any phenomena firsthand, he acted as a crucial expert consultant following the cellar excavations. Price publicly thanked him in the opening preface to *The End of Borley Rectory* for his generous help and his expert examination of the remains found at Borley.

## Identification of the Miraculous Medal

When Price visited Westminster Cathedral on August 25, 1943, Father Moriarty immediately identified the larger copper pendant as a specimen of the **"Miraculous Medal"** — a specific Catholic devotional item whose design was directly specified by visions experienced by [[catherine-laboure|Sister Catherine Labouré]] (born Zoé Labouré) in 1830 and which has been struck in huge numbers since 1832. According to Price, Moriarty established that the plaque was first struck in France in 1832 after Zoé Labouré received instructions directly from the Virgin Mary to record the visions she had experienced [2, 3].

Moriarty noted he had never seen one exactly like the Borley specimen, and believed most such medals were struck in France. He loaned Price a pamphlet — *The Miraculous Medal and Blessed Catherine Labouré* — which Price reproduces extensively in Chapter 15 of *The End of Borley Rectory*.

Regarding the gold pendant, Moriarty said he had never seen another specimen of its kind, that it was "unknown in this country," and that it was "undoubtedly of foreign make, and probably French." He noted it was "very old."

Both pendants, he confirmed, were "often attached to rosaries as worn by nuns."

After leaving Father Moriarty, Price took the copper plaque to Messrs Maurice Vanpoulle, a firm of church furnishers and Roman Catholic medallists in Vauxhall Bridge Road. The firm completely confirmed Father Moriarty's assessment and estimated that the specific copper plaque was about sixty years old [3].

## Examination of the Bones

Father Moriarty was also one of the experts to whom Price submitted the [[bones-excavation|human remains]] after the excavation — along with [[dr-eric-bailey|Dr. Bailey]] and [[leslie-godden|Leslie Godden]]. His role appears to have been consultation on the religious and historical context of the finds rather than forensic pathology.

## Significance

Moriarty's quick identification of the Miraculous Medal as of French origin, and his confirmation that both pendants were consistent with items worn by nuns, provided the cultural context that [[canon-phythian-adams|Canon Phythian-Adams]] incorporated into his revised analysis of the Borley evidence.
