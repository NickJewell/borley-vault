# Rev. Father M. J. Moriarty

Priest at Westminster Cathedral; the first expert consulted by [[harry-price|Harry Price]] after the discovery of the copper pendant and gold pendant at the August 1943 Borley excavation.

## Identification of the Miraculous Medal

When Price visited Westminster Cathedral on August 25, 1943, Father Moriarty immediately identified the larger copper pendant as a specimen of the **"Miraculous Medal"** — a specific Catholic devotional item whose design was directly specified by visions experienced by [[catherine-laboure|Sister Catherine Labouré]] in 1830 and which has been struck in huge numbers since 1832.

Moriarty noted he had never seen one exactly like the Borley specimen, and believed most such medals were struck in France. He loaned Price a pamphlet — *The Miraculous Medal and Blessed Catherine Labouré* — which Price reproduces extensively in Chapter 15.

Regarding the gold pendant, Moriarty said he had never seen another specimen of its kind, that it was "unknown in this country," and that it was "undoubtedly of foreign make, and probably French." He noted it was "very old."

Both pendants, he confirmed, were "often attached to rosaries as worn by nuns."

## Examination of the Bones

Father Moriarty was also one of the experts to whom Price submitted the [[bones-excavation|human remains]] after the excavation — along with [[dr-eric-bailey|Dr. Bailey]] and [[leslie-godden|Leslie Godden]]. His role appears to have been consultation on the religious and historical context of the finds rather than forensic pathology.

## Significance

Moriarty's quick identification of the Miraculous Medal as of French origin, and his confirmation that both pendants were consistent with items worn by nuns, provided the cultural context that [[canon-phythian-adams|Canon Phythian-Adams]] incorporated into his revised analysis of the Borley evidence.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Rev. Father M. J. Moriarty** was a Roman Catholic priest based at **Westminster Cathedral** [1, 2]. Although he did not visit Borley Rectory or witness any phenomena firsthand, he acted as a crucial expert consultant for psychical researcher Harry Price following the cellar excavations of 1943 [1, 2]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following specific facts and events regarding his involvement:

**Identifying the "Miraculous Medal"**
During the excavation of the ruined Rectory cellars in August 1943, Harry Price and his team unearthed a small copper plaque [2]. Seeking to identify the object's Roman Catholic origins, Price carefully cleaned the item and took it to Father Moriarty in London for expert examination [1, 2]. 

Father Moriarty successfully identified the object, establishing that the plaque was a copy of the **"Miraculous Medal,"** a religious medallion first struck in France in 1832 [2]. 

**The Pamphlet and Zoë Labouré**
To assist Price's research further, **Father Moriarty provided him with a pamphlet detailing the specific history of the medal** [3]. Price subsequently quoted from this pamphlet in his 1946 book, *The End of Borley Rectory*, recounting how **Zoë Labouré (who afterwards became the Blessed Catherine Labouré) received instructions directly from the mouth of the Virgin Mary** to strike a medallion recording the visions she experienced in 1830 [2, 3].

**Corroboration and Acknowledgement**
After leaving Father Moriarty, Price took the plaque to Messrs Maurice Vanpoulle, a firm of church furnishers and Roman Catholic medallists in Vauxhall Bridge Road [3]. **The firm completely confirmed Father Moriarty's assessment** and estimated that this specific copper plaque was about sixty years old [3]. 

In the opening preface to *The End of Borley Rectory*, **Harry Price publicly thanked Father Moriarty** for his generous help and his expert examination of the remains found at Borley [1].
