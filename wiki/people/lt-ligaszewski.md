# Second Lieutenant A. Ligaszewski

Polish Army officer; present at the first of the two Polish Army Medical Corps investigations of the [[borley-rectory|Borley ruins]], on the night of **June 28–29, 1943**.

## The Half-Brick Incident

Ligaszewski was part of the four-man party that included [[gb-nawrocki|Lt. G. B. Nawrocki]], [[lt-kujawa|Lt. W. Kujawa]], and [[col-wroblewski|Colonel J. Wroblewski]]. The party decided to spend the night in the ruined ground-floor dining room, improvising a table and seats out of garden bricks and a half-burnt pantry drawer.

At **2:40 a.m.**, the party was sitting in complete darkness when Ligaszewski made a remark to Kujawa. Kujawa hushed him with a warning:

> *"because a brick could easily be thrown from the ceiling upon our heads."*

He had "hardly finished speaking" when a half-brick was thrown from the direction of the ceiling and fell directly in the centre of their improvised table, making a great noise.

The timing — an instantaneous response to a casual, low-expectation remark about a possibility — is one of the more striking sequences in the post-fire record. Neither Ligaszewski nor any other member of the party was anywhere near the ceiling, and the ruins were confirmed deserted at the start of the visit.

He was present only for the first night; the second visit (July 28–29) involved a smaller party of [[lt-kujawa|Nawrocki]], Kujawa, and two English boys.

## SPR Hoax Accusation

While [[harry-price|Harry Price]] championed the Polish officers' report as vital evidence of ongoing poltergeist activity, Ligaszewski's experience falls under a broader contradiction raised by skeptical investigators. The authors of the 1956 [[the-borley-report|SPR *Borley Report*]] dismissed the entire Polish investigation as an **"elaborate hoax,"** noting it was highly suspicious for Ligaszewski and his companions to experience such sensational phenomena when members of the scientific [[cambridge-commission|Cambridge Commission]] were investigating the ruins during the exact same weeks in June and July 1943 and reporting absolutely nothing of interest.
