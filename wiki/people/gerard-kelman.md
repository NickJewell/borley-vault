# Gerard Kelman

Of Buckhurst Hill, Essex. In July 1966, Kelman and a few friends paid an overnight visit to Borley. In the early hours of Sunday 24 July, at approximately 5 am, all three witnesses — Kelman and the [[conolly-sisters|Conolly sisters]] — heard footsteps and saw a figure they could not account for near the Rectory site. Kelman sent his report to Underwood and Tabori immediately after the visit.

## Kelman's Account

> "I was standing with my back to the gate of the site of the old Rectory looking towards the church. I thought I heard sounds of someone moving on the gravel at a distance of about 20-30 yards behind me. I turned round and could see nothing. I continued to stare in the direction of the shed and then I saw a grey-blue shape appear in the half-light. At first, I could not distinguish what it was but then it seemed to move forward and take two paces to the left, after which it disappeared from view. I got the impression that it was a vaguely human shape covered from head to toe in a flowing garment, such as an old-fashioned night-dress but covering all of the body."

The figure was observed independently from three different positions. See also [[miss-jane-conolly|Miss Jane Conolly]] and [[miss-margaret-conolly|Miss Margaret Anne Conolly]] for the other two accounts.

## Significance

The 1966 Kelman/Conolly sighting is particularly evidentially interesting because the three witnesses were in different positions and gave independently consistent but not identical descriptions — Kelman saw a "vaguely human shape" from head to toe; Jane Conolly saw more clearly from waist upwards; Margaret Conolly saw only the lower half. This variation in what each could see, from their respective vantage points, is consistent with a three-dimensional figure rather than a shared hallucination.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Gerard Kelman** (who is also inconsistently referred to in the texts as **Gerald Kelman**) was a resident of **Buckhurst Hill, Essex** [1, 2]. He appears in the Borley sources as a witness to one of the notable "Later Borley" apparitions following the destruction of the Rectory. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific facts, dates, events, and quotes regarding his experience:

**The July 1966 Visit and Sighting**
Kelman made an overnight visit to the Borley Rectory site over the weekend of **July 23–24, 1966** [1]. He was accompanied by two friends: **Miss Jane Conolly** and **Miss Margaret Anne Conolly** (whose surname is alternately spelled "Connally" in one timeline) [1-3]. 

Early on Sunday morning, at approximately **5:00 a.m. on July 24**, just before daybreak, the trio experienced a distinct auditory and visual phenomenon [1, 2, 4]. While standing by the gate of the old Rectory site, they heard the sound of crunching footsteps on the gravel but could not see anyone making the noise [2, 4]. Shortly thereafter, looking in the direction of a shed on the Rectory grounds, they all witnessed a vague, grey-blue, nun-like shape in the half-light [1-3]. 

**Kelman's Official Report and Quote**
Immediately following the experience, Kelman submitted a formal report to psychical investigator Peter Underwood [2, 3]. The sources record his specific, definitive quote describing the encounter:
> **"I was standing with my back to the gate of the site of the old Rectory looking towards the church. I thought I heard sounds of someone moving on the gravel at a distance of about 20-30 yards behind me. I turned round and could see nothing. I continued to stare in the direction of the shed and then I saw a grey-blue shape appear in the half-light. At first, I could not distinguish what it was but then it seemed to move forward and take two paces to the left, after which it disappeared from view. I got the impression that it was a vaguely human shape covered from head to toe in a flowing garment, such as an old-fashioned night-dress but covering all of the body."** [2]

**Corroboration**
Kelman's account was fully corroborated by an independent report filed by his companion, Jane Conolly. She confirmed hearing the footsteps on the gravel and observing the small-headed, broad-shouldered grey figure by the shed, noting that it moved abnormally and was blurred from the waist down before disappearing near a large tree [4].
