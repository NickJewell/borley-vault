# Gerard Kelman

**Gerard Kelman** (also inconsistently referred to in the texts as Gerald Kelman [1, 2]) was a resident of **Buckhurst Hill, Essex**. In July 1966, Kelman and two friends paid an overnight visit to Borley. In the early hours of Sunday 24 July, at approximately 5 am, all three witnesses — Kelman and the [[miss-jane-conolly|Conolly sisters]] — heard footsteps and saw a figure they could not account for near the Rectory site. Kelman sent his report to [[peter-underwood|Underwood]] and Tabori immediately after the visit.

## The July 1966 Visit and Sighting

Kelman made an overnight visit to the Borley Rectory site over the weekend of **July 23–24, 1966** [1]. He was accompanied by two friends: [[miss-jane-conolly|Miss Jane Conolly]] and [[miss-margaret-conolly|Miss Margaret Anne Conolly]] (whose surname is alternately spelled "Connally" in one timeline) [1–3].

Early on Sunday morning, at approximately **5:00 a.m. on July 24**, just before daybreak, the trio experienced a distinct auditory and visual phenomenon [1, 2, 4]. While standing by the gate of the old Rectory site, they heard the sound of crunching footsteps on the gravel but could not see anyone making the noise [2, 4]. Shortly thereafter, looking in the direction of a shed on the Rectory grounds, they all witnessed a vague, grey-blue, nun-like shape in the half-light [1–3].

## Kelman's Account

> **"I was standing with my back to the gate of the site of the old Rectory looking towards the church. I thought I heard sounds of someone moving on the gravel at a distance of about 20-30 yards behind me. I turned round and could see nothing. I continued to stare in the direction of the shed and then I saw a grey-blue shape appear in the half-light. At first, I could not distinguish what it was but then it seemed to move forward and take two paces to the left, after which it disappeared from view. I got the impression that it was a vaguely human shape covered from head to toe in a flowing garment, such as an old-fashioned night-dress but covering all of the body."** [2]

## Significance

The 1966 Kelman/Conolly sighting is particularly evidentially interesting because the three witnesses were in different positions and gave independently consistent but not identical descriptions — Kelman saw a "vaguely human shape" from head to toe; [[miss-jane-conolly|Jane Conolly]] confirmed hearing the footsteps on the gravel and saw a small-headed, broad-shouldered grey figure by the shed that moved abnormally and was blurred from the waist down before disappearing near a large tree [4]; [[miss-margaret-conolly|Margaret Conolly]] saw only the lower half. This variation in what each could see, from their respective vantage points, is consistent with a three-dimensional figure rather than a shared hallucination (multiple witnesses).
