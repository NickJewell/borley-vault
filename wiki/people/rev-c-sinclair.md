# Rev. C. Sinclair

Rector of Chale, Isle of Wight, from 1940. Sinclair wrote a letter to the *Church Times* in October 1945, responding to an editorial about Harry Price's *Poltergeist Over England*, in which he described phenomena at Chale Rectory remarkably similar to those reported at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]].

## Chale Rectory Phenomena

Sinclair and his wife arrived at Chale in 1940. Former maids in the rectory claimed that strange things had happened there, including the driving into the yard at midnight of a carriage and pair — heard but not seen. The Sinclairs were "all agog on arrival for the manifestations, nor were they to be disappointed":

> "...they heard jingling as of harness, creaks, clangings, grinding and jarring sounds, and a rather stagey horses-hooves noise of a somewhat 'coconutty' quality."

Other disturbances included preserves and objects being knocked off shelves, stealthy footsteps on landings, "cold spots," and mysterious "vigils" of the cat and dog outside closed doors of empty rooms. Even pencil-scribbling was discovered — subsequently established as the work of a sub-tenant's child. Two people eventually arrived and conducted some kind of ritual with powders and "ancillary mutterings," returning three times at three-day intervals. After their final visit, "no more curious noises were heard," and thereafter Sinclair's reply to inquiries was simply: "rats."

## Significance

Underwood and Tabori cite Sinclair's letter as a striking parallel to the Borley case, noting: "Perhaps Mrs Smith did notice this resemblance and concluded that the 'phenomena' at Borley were due at least to a considerable extent to the same causes as at Chale." [[mabel-smith|Mrs Mabel Smith]] herself wrote to the *Church Times* the following week denying any haunting at Borley, citing "rats and local superstition." Underwood and Tabori pointedly questioned whether "rats can produce 'creaks, clangings, grinding and jarring sounds' unless they possess remarkable onomatopoeic talents."
