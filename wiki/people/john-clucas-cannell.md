# John Clucas Cannell

A London journalist, Vice-President of the Magician's Club and author of *The Secrets of Houdini* (1931). On the staff of the *Daily Sketch*.

## Borley Involvement

Cannell knew [[harry-price]] and wrote a sensationalist article on Borley entitled "Rectory Tale of Terror" (probably from information supplied by Price), published in February 1932 during the period that the Foysters were at the Rectory experiencing much [[foyster-phenomena|phenomena]].

He also wrote *When Fleet Street Calls* (1932), which includes a chapter "The Haunted Rectory." Although Borley is not named, identification is clear: Cannell mentions "a former rector, the Revd Harry Bull" and details of [[harry-price]]'s visits. His description of acknowledged events and the reactions of the participants makes this a firsthand and almost contemporary account — importantly, it predates the publication of [[mhh|MHH]] by eight years.

## Visit to the Rectory

Cannell visited [[borley-rectory]] late one night during the [[foyster-incumbency]]. The door was opened by [[marianne-foyster]] who was initially reluctant to discuss disturbances but eventually invited Cannell into the Drawing Room. He noticed a strong contrast between the rector and his wife: Foyster "tall and solemn and of a scholarly appearance" while Marianne was "lively and shrewd."

Marianne told Cannell directly that she knew people were suggesting she was responsible for the phenomena: "There are people who say that I am hysterical and that I have been doing these things." When Cannell asked if it was suggested she had been "giving herself black eyes and throwing bricks about," she shrugged resignedly. Cannell found this quite absurd given the manifestations had been reported for years before she arrived. He offered to stay the night but the rector declined.

Summing up, Cannell admitted he could not decide on the origin of the disturbances, but stated: "One fact does emerge with certainty — that people of the highest reputation and integrity have testified that these things have happened in the Rectory."

## Significance

The *Daily Sketch* article of February 1932 (described by the BRC as "Rectory Tale of Terror") is one of the earliest detailed press accounts of the Foyster-period phenomena, published while the Foysters were still in residence. Its existence, and the fact that *When Fleet Street Calls* appeared in 1932 with a chapter on Borley, forms part of the [[newspaper-articles|newspaper coverage]] that [[andrew-clarke]] later identified as crucial to understanding how the Borley legend built on itself.

[[guy-lestrange]] read Cannell's London press accounts and used them as the reason for requesting an invitation to visit Borley in January 1932.
