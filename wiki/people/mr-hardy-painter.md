# Mr. Hardy (Senior), Painter

Foreman painter; born in Borley; had lived there all his life and worked at the Rectory on many occasions. Encountered [[harry-price|Price's]] observer [[sidney-glanville]] in November 1937 when the Chelmsford Diocesan Dilapidations Board had authorised redecoration of the exterior. Sole named witness of the "smoking phenomenon." Listed in Appendix D as "Hardy, Senior" and Appendix E as "Mr. Hardy, Senior."

## The Smoking Phenomenon

At some period before Glanville's November 1937 visit, Hardy was working inside the Rectory on a "bright summer afternoon." A fellow workman called him into the library and pointed out the window: a column of smoke was rising vertically from the centre of the lawn. The phenomenon lasted for one or two minutes. Immediate investigation found no clue and no explanation was ever forthcoming.

Hardy did not speculate about the cause; he simply described what he and his colleague had seen. The "smoking phenomenon" is unique in the Borley record — no similar account is reported by any other witness — and Hardy's is the only firsthand account of it.

> **Note:** The name of Hardy's fellow workman who also witnessed the smoke column is not given in the source.

## Hardy's Assessment of the Haunting

Glanville records that Hardy "remarked that Borley Rectory had had the reputation of being haunted as long as he could remember, and that some years ago the villagers were afraid to pass it at night if they were alone." As a born-and-bred Borley resident with decades of contact with the property, Hardy provides local-knowledge context that supplements the testimony of outside investigators.

## Hardy Junior

A fortnight before Glanville's visit, Hardy's son — aged about nineteen — was returning from work at night when he heard singing or chanting coming from the church as he passed. The church was closed and empty. He did not stop to investigate. This incident is listed in Appendix D as "Church Music — Mr. Hardy, Junr."
