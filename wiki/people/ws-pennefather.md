# W. S. Pennefather

Wrote to [[harry-price]] during the preparation of *The Most Haunted House in England* to report that his father — a clergyman — had rented Borley Rectory for a six-week holiday before 1896. No phenomena were experienced during the stay.

## Account

Price contacted Pennefather after receiving his letter, asking whether he could fix the exact year of the visit. Pennefather could not, but noted that his father was appointed Vicar of Kensington in 1896, and the Borley holiday was before that appointment. If the year was 1895, the visit would fall during the incumbency of the Rev. [[harry-bull]], who succeeded his father as rector in 1892.

The letter states plainly: "to the best of my knowledge, no incidents occurred during our stay, and it is unlikely that the then incumbent said anything to my father, as he was desirous of letting the house."

Price accepts the negative report as legitimate — noting that after 45 years "it would be difficult to remember trivial, but unusual happenings, had they occurred" — and uses it as one of three "blank" reports out of all observers during his tenancy and investigations.

## Significance

Pennefather's account is valuable as evidence of the Rectory's appearance on the market during the Bull incumbency — confirming that Harry Bull periodically let the house out, presumably during periods when he and his sisters were not in residence. It also demonstrates that at least one outside party spent six weeks there in the 1880s–1890s without experiencing obvious phenomena.

> **Note:** Only his surname and one initial are recorded. His father's name and the exact year of the visit are unconfirmed.
