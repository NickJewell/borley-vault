# José Simmonds (née Bacon)

Daughter of Bob and Betty Bacon, who purchased and occupied [[rectory-cottage|Borley Rectory Cottage]] from [[james-turner]] in 1951. The Bacon family (including José, her brother Terry, and her maternal grandparents Mr and Mrs Williams) lived there until 1972. José was interviewed by Edward Babbs in 2002.

## Half-Figure at the Window — Late 1950s

On one occasion while descending the stairs at the cottage, José saw through the window at the foot of the stairs **the lower half of a person** walking past outside. The figure appeared to be clothed in a **full-length black skirt**, moving with a striding, masculine gait. No one should have been outside at that point. When she investigated, no one was there. The authors suggest the "skirt" may have been a priest's robes.

## Deep Breathing — Early 1960s

On an evening when she and a young man friend were sitting in the darkened living room of the cottage, **deep and heavy breathing** was heard from an empty corner of the room. They were frightened; her friend ran from the room, at which point the sounds stopped. No other living creature was present capable of producing the sound except the family's budgerigar.

## The Bacon Family at the Cottage

The family were interested in the alleged haunting and willing to discuss it with responsible visitors. Betty Bacon had spiritualist leanings and family séances were held. Her son Terry and her father also claimed to have seen the spectral nun on several occasions.
