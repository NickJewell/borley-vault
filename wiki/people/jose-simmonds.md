# José Simmonds (née Bacon)

Daughter of Bob and Betty Bacon, who purchased and occupied [[rectory-cottage|Borley Rectory Cottage]] (later called Borley Priory) from [[james-turner|James Turner]] in **April 1951** [1–4]. The Bacon family — including José, her brother Terrence (Terry), and her maternal grandparents Mr and Mrs Henry Williams — lived there for over twenty years, eventually moving away in **early 1972** [2, 4]. José was interviewed by Edward Babbs in 2002.

## Participation in Séances

During her youth at Borley, José was a direct participant in paranormal investigations. Between **1955 and 1957**, she took part in a series of planchette séances organised by investigator [[len-sewell|Len Sewell]] [5]. These sessions, held at the family's cottage, included José, her brother Terry, her parents, her grandmother Mrs Williams, and Sewell himself [5].

## Half-Figure at the Window — Late 1950s

On one occasion while descending the stairs at the cottage, José saw through the window at the foot of the stairs **the lower half of a person** walking past outside. The figure was clothed in a **full-length black skirt, yet was "striding in a masculine fashion"** [6]. No one should have been outside at that point. When she investigated, no one was there [6]. The authors suggest the "skirt" may have been a priest's robes [6]. (single witness)

## Deep Breathing — Early 1960s

One evening, José and a young male friend were sitting in the darkened living room of the cottage when **"deep and heavy breathing"** emanated from an empty corner of the room [7]. The terrifying sounds caused her friend to run out of the room, at which point the breathing abruptly stopped [7]. José noted the only other living creature in the room at the time was a pet budgerigar [7]. (multiple witnesses)

## The Bacon Family at the Cottage

The family were interested in the alleged haunting and willing to discuss it with responsible visitors. Betty Bacon had spiritualist leanings and family séances were held. Her son Terry and her father also reportedly claimed to have seen the spectral nun on several occasions.

## Later Contributions to Borley Research

In addition to sharing her first-hand accounts in her 2002 interview, José Simmonds supplied numerous original photographs from her family's time at the site for the 2003 book *Borley Rectory: The Final Analysis* [8].
