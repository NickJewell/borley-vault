# Elsie Bull

Daughter of [[henry-bull]], the builder of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]]. One of four sisters who witnessed the apparition of the nun on July 28, 1900 — and the one who attempted to confront it directly. Her full name was Emily Poundrow Bull; "Elsie" was the family nickname [1, 2]. She was baptized on 13 February 1876 [1, 2] and died in 1937, having married "shortly afterwards" the famous 1900 ghost sighting [1, 3].

## The 1900 Confrontation with the Nun

On July 28, 1900, her sisters [[ethel-bull]], [[freda-bull]], and [[mabel-bull]] were returning from a garden party and simultaneously saw a sombre female figure gliding along the [[nuns-walk]]. Terrified, one of them ran into the house to fetch Elsie.

Elsie's initial reaction was sceptical and bold:

> 'A ghost? Oh, what nonsense, I'll go and speak to it!'

She ran out into the garden and stepped onto the lawn to intercept the stranger [3, 4]. As she approached, the apparition stopped and turned its face directly toward her. She bore "an expression of intense grief on her face." Elsie stood dead — "terrified" — and the figure vanished.

[[harry-price|Harry Price]] notes that this makes Elsie's experience qualitatively different from her sisters': she approached close enough for the figure to react to her, and the face was clearly seen in its expression of grief before the vanishing. Her sceptical attitude at the outset makes her testimony harder to attribute to prior suggestion. Investigator Robert J. Hastings referred to Elsie as an "elder sister" who was called out to the garden by the others [5] — but having been born in 1876, she was in fact significantly younger than Ethel (b. 1868), Mabel (b. 1868), and Freda (b. 1865) [1, 2].

## Contradictions in the Record

> **Contradiction — identity and name:** Investigator [[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth|Geoffrey Croom-Hollingsworth]] claimed that "Elsie" was actually a mistaken nickname for the eldest sister, Caroline Sarah Elizabeth (known as Dodie) [7]. Another author lists Emily and Elsie as two separate children [8]. However, the *Borley Rectory Companion* definitively links the two names as one person: "Emily (Elsie) (1876–1937)" [1].

> **Contradiction — role in the sighting:** *Borley Rectory – The Final Analysis* contradicts the standard narrative. It claims that Elsie was actually one of the three sisters returning from the party who was initially frightened by the figure, and that they ran into the house to fetch *Dodie*, who then came out to look [9].

> **Contradiction — the face:** Ethel Bull explicitly contradicted the "intense grief" detail in a 1950 SPR interview, stating she saw a woman bent over in a flowing black robe and "could not see the face" [12, 13]. The detail therefore rests on Elsie's account alone.
