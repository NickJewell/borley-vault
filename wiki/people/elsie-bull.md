# Elsie Bull

Daughter of [[henry-bull]], the builder of Borley Rectory. One of four sisters who witnessed the apparition of the nun on July 28, 1900 — and the one who attempted to confront it directly.

## The 1900 Confrontation with the Nun

On July 28, 1900, her sisters [[ethel-bull]], [[freda-bull]], and [[mabel-bull]] were returning from a garden party and simultaneously saw a sombre female figure gliding along the [[nuns-walk]]. Frightened, one of them ran into the house to fetch Elsie.

Elsie's initial reaction was sceptical and bold:

> 'A ghost? Oh, what nonsense, I'll go and speak to it!'

She began to run across the lawn toward the figure. She had taken "hardly two steps" when the figure stopped and turned toward her. It bore "an expression of intense grief on her face." Elsie stopped dead — "stood on the lawn terrified" — and the figure vanished.

[[harry-price]] notes that this makes Elsie's experience qualitatively different from her sisters': she approached close enough for the figure to react to her, and the face was clearly seen in its expression of grief before the vanishing. Her sceptical attitude at the outset makes her testimony harder to attribute to prior suggestion.

Elsie is noted in Price's account as having "married shortly afterwards" — her later married name is not given in *The Most Haunted House in England*, and the post-marriage phase of her life is not documented in this source.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Elsie Bull** (whose real name was Emily Poundrow Bull) was one of the fourteen children of the Rev. Henry Dawson Ellis Bull and Caroline Sarah Foyster [1, 2]. She is famous in the Borley Rectory records for her central, dramatic role in the most renowned sighting of the phantom nun.

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following biographical details, events, quotes, and historical contradictions regarding her connection to the case:

**Biographical Details**
Baptized on February 13, 1876, she was known within the family as Elsie [1, 2]. The texts record very little about her personal life outside of the haunting, other than noting she died in 1937 and was married "shortly afterwards" the famous 1900 ghost sighting [1, 3]. 

**The Phantom Nun Sighting (July 28, 1900)**
Elsie is best known for her bold confrontation with the Borley nun. According to the most frequently cited version of the event (originally recorded by Harry Price's secretary, Lucie Kaye), three of the Bull sisters—Ethel, Freda, and Mabel—were returning to the Rectory from a garden party at twilight when they spotted a dark, nun-like figure gliding along the "Nun's Walk" [4, 5]. Terrified, one of the girls ran into the house and fetched Elsie [4, 6]. 

Refusing to believe there was anything supernatural about the figure, Elsie confidently declared: **"A ghost? Oh, what nonsense, I’ll go and speak to it!"** [3, 4]. 

She ran out into the garden and stepped onto the lawn to intercept the stranger [3, 4]. As she approached, the apparition stopped and turned its face directly toward her [3, 4, 6]. Elsie stood terrified as the figure instantly vanished [3]. 

**The Face of "Intense Grief"**
A crucial detail distinguishing Elsie's account from her sisters' is that she was the *only* one reported to have clearly seen the nun's face. While Ethel Bull later testified that the figure was bowed over and no face was visible to her, Elsie claimed the entity had **"an expression of intense grief on her face"** [5, 6]. 

**Contradictions in the Record**
Because the story was told and retold by various authors and investigators, several contradictions regarding Elsie exist in the Borley literature:
*   **Identity and Name Confusion:** Investigator Geoffrey Croom-Hollingsworth claimed that "Elsie" was actually a mistaken nickname for the eldest sister, Caroline Sarah Elizabeth (known as Dodie) [7]. Another author lists Emily and Elsie as two separate children [8]. However, the *Borley Rectory Companion* definitively links the two names as one person: "Emily (Elsie) (1876–1937)" [1].
*   **Role in the Sighting:** The book *Borley Rectory – The Final Analysis* contradicts the standard narrative of the 1900 sighting. It claims that Elsie was actually one of the three sisters returning from the party who was initially frightened by the figure, and that they ran into the house to fetch *Dodie*, who then came out to look [9]. 
*   **Age and Birth Order:** In reviewing Harry Price's notes, investigator Robert J. Hastings refers to Elsie as an "elder sister" who was called out to the garden by the others [5]. In reality, having been born in 1876, Elsie was significantly younger than Ethel (b. 1868), Mabel (b. 1868), and Freda (b. 1865) [1, 2].
