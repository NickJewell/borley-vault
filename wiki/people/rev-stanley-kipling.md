# Rev. Stanley C. Kipling

Rector of St Mary and St James, Barnoldswick (also spelled Bernoldswick), Lancashire. Kipling had known Borley for many years and had always been sceptical of the haunting — until he had a personal experience in August 1949 that made him "seriously reconsider the whole story."

## Sighting (August 1949)

Kipling visited [[borley-church|Borley Church]] in early August 1949 to read the lesson at the funeral of a friend. After changing in the vestry, he approached the porch of the west door to look for the local rector, [[rev-ac-henning|Rev. A. C. Henning]], and while still inside the church looking out near the porch he saw the figure of a veiled girl in the churchyard. As he watched:

> "she passed behind a shrub, crossed to another close by and then vanished."

He immediately went outside to the exact spot where the figure had disappeared but found nothing to explain the appearance or disappearance of what he described as "a lifelike and distinct figure."

Writing to [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] on 17 September 1949, Kipling described the figure and acknowledged the brief duration of the sighting:

> "I conceived that she was a 'frail' girl, by her stature I should say about 18 to 23. She had the shape of a nun's hood on her head from which hung the thick veiling — her features were not discernible."

> **Contradiction:** A slight textual discrepancy in one source records the descriptive word as "fair" rather than "frail."

He saw the figure only for "a split second," but the experience was sufficiently striking that he reversed his long-held scepticism about the Borley haunting.

## Significance

Kipling's account is notable as the testimony of a Church of England clergyman who had no prior expectation of seeing anything, had long been sceptical, and described a figure consistent with other accounts of [[the-nun|the Nun]] near [[borley-church|Borley Church]]. His sighting occurred in daylight, in the church porch, during a funeral visit — circumstances that make suggestion or hallucination especially unlikely.
