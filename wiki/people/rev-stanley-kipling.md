# Rev. Stanley C. Kipling

Rector of St Mary and St James, Barnoldswick, Lancashire. Kipling had known Borley for many years and had been sceptical of the haunting — until he had a personal experience in August 1949 that made him "seriously reconsider the whole story."

## Sighting (August 1949)

Kipling visited Borley to read the lesson at the funeral of a friend and changed in the vestry of [[borley-church|Borley Church]]. As he approached the porch of the west door, while still inside near the porch, he saw the figure of a veiled girl. As he looked at her:

> "she passed behind a shrub, crossed to another close by and then vanished."

He immediately went to the spot where she had disappeared but found nothing to explain the appearance or disappearance of what he described as "a lifelike and distinct figure."

Writing on 17 September 1949, Kipling described the figure:

> "I conceived that she was a 'frail' girl, by her stature I should say about 18 to 23. She had the shape of a nun's hood on her head from which hung the thick veiling — her features were not discernible."

He saw the figure only for "a split second," but the experience was sufficiently striking that he reversed his long-held scepticism about the Borley haunting.

## Significance

Kipling's account is notable as the testimony of a Church of England clergyman who had no prior expectation of seeing anything, had long been sceptical, and described a figure consistent with other accounts of [[the-nun|the Nun]] near [[borley-church|Borley Church]]. His sighting occurred in daylight, in the church porch, during a funeral visit — circumstances that make suggestion or hallucination especially unlikely.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Reverend Stanley C. Kipling** was a clergyman from St Mary and St James, Barnoldswick (also spelled Bernoldswick in the texts), Lancashire [1, 2]. He appears in the Borley sources as a long-time sceptic whose personal encounter with an apparition forced him to re-evaluate the haunting [1, 3]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific details, dates, and quotes regarding his experience:

**Prior Beliefs**
*   Before his paranormal encounter, Mr. Kipling had known the Borley area for many years and had **"always been sceptical of the haunting"** [1, 3]. 

**The August 1949 Sighting**
*   **The Funeral Visit:** In **early August 1949**, Rev. Kipling visited Borley Church to read the lesson at the funeral of a friend [1, 2]. 
*   **The Apparition:** After changing in the church vestry, he approached the porch of the west door to look for the local rector, the Rev. A. C. Henning [2]. While still inside the church looking out near the porch, he saw the lifelike figure of a veiled girl in the churchyard [1, 2]. 
*   **The Disappearance:** He watched as the figure passed behind a shrub, crossed to another nearby shrub, and then suddenly vanished [1, 2]. 
*   **Investigation:** Kipling immediately went outside to the exact spot where the distinct figure had disappeared, but he could find nothing to explain either her appearance or her disappearance [1, 2]. 

**The September 1949 Letter**
Rev. Kipling recorded his experience in a letter written to psychical investigator Peter Underwood on **17 September 1949** [1, 2]. In it, he noted that he only saw the figure for a split second, but provided the following specific description: 
> **"I conceived that she was a fair** [a slight textual contradiction in another source records this word as **'frail'**] **girl, by her stature I should say about eighteen to twenty-three. She had the shape of a nun’s hood on her head from which hung thick veiling – her features were not discernible"** [1, 3].

**Impact of the Encounter**
Because he could find no natural explanation for what he had seen in the churchyard that day, this personal experience caused the previously sceptical clergyman to **"seriously reconsider the whole story"** of the Borley hauntings [1, 3].
