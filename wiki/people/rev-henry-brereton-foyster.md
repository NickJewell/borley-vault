# Rev. Henry Brereton Foyster

Paternal uncle of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster]]; elder son of [[rev-henry-samuel-foyster|Rev. Henry Samuel Foyster]] of All Saints', Hastings. He was rector and patron of **St Clement's, Hastings**, holding the living from approximately 1861 to 1906, when he handed it to his own son [[rev-henry-charles-brereton-foyster|Rev. Henry Charles Brereton Foyster]].

## Background

Henry Brereton Foyster was born in 1834 and educated at Trinity College, Cambridge. He followed the family's clerical dynasty pattern, taking over one of the two family livings at Hastings. His elder brother [[rev-george-alfred-foyster|Rev. George Alfred Foyster]] (Lionel's father) held the other living, All Saints'.

## The Living Lionel Was Denied

The passing of St Clement's from Henry Brereton to his own son (rather than to his nephew Lionel) is one of the key biographical facts Robert Wood uses to suggest an early scandal in Lionel's career. By 1906 Lionel had been ordained for three years and was capable of holding a living, yet:

- The family patronage of St Clement's went to Henry Brereton's son (not to Lionel)
- His father's living at All Saints' was also not passed to Lionel when George Alfred retired in 1904
- Lionel instead served a second curacy — under his brother-in-law [[rev-edmund-wethered|Wethered]] at Oughtrington — before eventually emigrating to Canada in 1910

Wood interprets this as the consequence of some "scandal or family difficulty" that made Lionel unsuitable to take the family livings. Exactly what that difficulty was remains unknown.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Reverend Henry Brereton Foyster** was the uncle of the Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster (the famous rector of Borley) and part of a wealthy "Victorian ecclesiastical dynasty" based in Hastings [1, 2]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the sources reveals the following biographical details, family connections, and notable chronological contradictions regarding his life:

**Family and Education**
*   **Lineage:** He was the elder son of the Rev. Henry Samuel Foyster, making him the older brother of George Alfred Foyster [1, 2]. 
*   **Birth and Education:** He was **born in 1834** and educated at **Trinity College, Cambridge** [2]. 
*   **Descendants:** He had a son, the **Rev. Henry Charles Brereton Foyster**, who also followed him into the priesthood [2].

**Clerical Career and Textual Contradictions**
*   **Parish:** Like his father and brother, Henry Brereton Foyster benefited from his family's ecclesiastical influence; he served as both the **Rector and patron of St Clement’s Church in Hastings** [1, 2]. 
*   **Chronological Contradiction:** The source text (*The Widow of Borley*) contains a glaring chronological error regarding his tenure. While explicitly recording his birth year as 1834, the text contradictorily states he served as the Rector of St Clement’s **"from 1801 to 1906"** [2]. 
*   **Succession:** In **1906**, he officially handed over the living of St Clement’s directly to his son, Henry Charles Brereton Foyster [2]. The sources use this specific hand-over to illustrate how lucrative church livings were typically passed from father to son in the Foyster family [1, 2]. 

**Death**
*   He died in **1910**, which the sources note was **"almost exactly a year"** before the death of his younger brother, George Alfred, in 1911 [2].
