# Rev. Henry Brereton Foyster

Paternal uncle of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster]]; elder son of [[rev-henry-samuel-foyster|Rev. Henry Samuel Foyster]] of All Saints', Hastings. He was rector and patron of **St Clement's, Hastings**, holding the living from approximately 1861 to 1906, when he handed it to his own son [[rev-henry-charles-brereton-foyster|Rev. Henry Charles Brereton Foyster]].

## Background

Henry Brereton Foyster was born in 1834 and educated at Trinity College, Cambridge. He followed the family's clerical dynasty pattern, taking over one of the two family livings at Hastings. His elder brother [[rev-george-alfred-foyster|Rev. George Alfred Foyster]] (Lionel's father) held the other living, All Saints'.

## The Living Lionel Was Denied

The passing of St Clement's from Henry Brereton to his own son (rather than to his nephew Lionel) is one of the key biographical facts Robert Wood uses to suggest an early scandal in Lionel's career. By 1906 Lionel had been ordained for three years and was capable of holding a living, yet:

- The family patronage of St Clement's went to Henry Brereton's son (not to Lionel)
- His father's living at All Saints' was also not passed to Lionel when George Alfred retired in 1904
- Lionel instead served a second curacy — under his brother-in-law [[rev-edmund-wethered|Wethered]] at Oughtrington — before eventually emigrating to Canada in 1910

Wood interprets this as the consequence of some "scandal or family difficulty" that made Lionel unsuitable to take the family livings. Exactly what that difficulty was remains unknown.
