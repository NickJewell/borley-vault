# Rev. Henry Brereton Foyster

Paternal uncle of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster]]; elder son of [[rev-henry-samuel-foyster|Rev. Henry Samuel Foyster]] of All Saints', Hastings. Born in **1834** and educated at **Trinity College, Cambridge** [2]. He served as both the Rector and patron of **St Clement's Church, Hastings**, holding the living from approximately 1861 to 1906, when he handed it to his own son [[rev-henry-charles-brereton-foyster|Rev. Henry Charles Brereton Foyster]] [1, 2].

He died in **1910**, which the sources note was **"almost exactly a year"** before the death of his younger brother, [[rev-george-alfred-foyster|George Alfred]], in 1911 [2].

## The Living Lionel Was Denied

The passing of St Clement's from Henry Brereton to his own son (rather than to his nephew [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]]) is one of the key biographical facts Robert Wood uses to suggest an early scandal in Lionel's career. By 1906, Lionel had been ordained for three years and was capable of holding a living, yet:

- The family patronage of St Clement's went to Henry Brereton's son (not to Lionel)
- His father's living at All Saints' was also not passed to Lionel when [[rev-george-alfred-foyster|George Alfred]] retired in 1904
- Lionel instead served a second curacy — under his brother-in-law [[rev-edmund-wethered|Wethered]] at Oughtrington — before eventually emigrating to Canada in 1910

Wood interprets this as the consequence of some "scandal or family difficulty" that made Lionel unsuitable to take the family livings. Exactly what that difficulty was remains unknown.

> **Contradiction:** While explicitly recording his birth year as 1834, the source text (*The Widow of Borley*) contradictorily states he served as Rector of St Clement's **"from 1801 to 1906"** [2] — evidently an error, as 1801 predates his birth by over thirty years.
