# James M. Sugg

Companion of [[laurence-gafford|Laurence C. Gafford]] on an overnight visit to the Borley Rectory site on 4 August 1949. Both men had obtained permission from [[james-turner|James Turner]]. They deliberately refrained from making detailed enquiries about the haunting beforehand, and only glanced at photographs in *The End of Borley Rectory* to get an idea of the building's layout.

During the night, they heard footsteps and a bicycle-freewheeling sound from the lane — both of which ceased when they investigated, and no figure could be seen in bright moonlight. Sugg provided an independent written report. No further details about him appear in *Ghosts of Borley*.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**James M. Sugg** was a visitor and investigator who experienced auditory phenomena at the Borley Rectory site during the "Later Borley" period, several years after the main house had been destroyed by fire. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following specific details regarding his involvement:

**The Vigil of August 4, 1949**
On the night of **August 4, 1949**, James M. Sugg and his companion, Laurence (or Lawrence) C. Gafford, spent the night on the former Rectory site [1, 2]. They had obtained the permission of James Turner, the author and poet who was living in the adjacent Rectory Cottage at the time [2]. 

**The Phantom Bicyclist and Footsteps**
Sugg's notable experience occurred late that evening while he and Gafford were sitting inside their parked car [1]. They were joined by James and Cathy Turner, and the group was talking together [1]. 
*   At exactly **11:15 p.m.**, the group heard what were described as **"light, brisk footsteps"** coming from the direction of the lane and approaching the cottage [1]. 
*   Accompanying the footsteps, they clearly heard the distinct sound of a **"bicycle freewheeling downhill"** [1]. 
*   The group initially assumed a completely natural explanation, believing that someone was simply walking down the lane while wheeling a bicycle beside them [1]. 

**The Investigation**
To get a better look or listen, Laurence Gafford rolled down the car window [1]. Immediately upon doing so, all the sounds ceased completely [1]. Sugg and the rest of the party immediately got out of the car to search the area for the source of the noises, but they saw no one in the lane and found absolutely nothing that could account for the sounds they had all just heard [1].
