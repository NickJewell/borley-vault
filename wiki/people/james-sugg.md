# James M. Sugg

Companion of [[laurence-gafford|Laurence C. Gafford]] on an overnight visit to the Borley Rectory site on 4 August 1949. Both men had obtained permission from [[james-turner|James Turner]], who was then living at the adjacent Rectory Cottage. They deliberately refrained from making detailed enquiries about the haunting beforehand, and only glanced at photographs in *The End of Borley Rectory* to get an idea of the building's layout.

## The Vigil of 4 August 1949

Late that evening Sugg and Gafford were sitting in their parked car when they were joined by James and Cathy Turner. At exactly **11:15 p.m.**, the group reportedly heard what were described as **"light, brisk footsteps"** coming from the direction of the lane and approaching the cottage [1]. Accompanying the footsteps was the distinct sound of a **"bicycle freewheeling downhill"** [1]. The group initially assumed a natural explanation, believing that someone was simply walking down the lane while wheeling a bicycle.

To get a better look, Gafford rolled down the car window — and immediately all sounds ceased completely [1]. Sugg and the rest of the party got out to search the area but saw no one in the lane. In bright moonlight there was no figure visible to account for the sounds [1].

Sugg provided an independent written report of the experience. No further biographical details appear in *Ghosts of Borley*.
