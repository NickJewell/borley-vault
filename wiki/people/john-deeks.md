# John Deeks

(Also recorded as **John Decks** in one ecclesiastical list [1]) Rector of Borley during the Commonwealth period (1642–1660), listed in the Borley rectors' appendix as an "Intruder." He replaced [[robert-warren-rector|Robert Warren]], who had held the living since 1607 and was dismissed for refusing to conform to Puritanism under Cromwell's Commonwealth.

## Historical Context

During Oliver Cromwell's Commonwealth (1642–1660), clergy unwilling or unable to accept Puritanism could be dismissed and replaced by "intruders" — clergymen imposed on a parish by Cromwellian authorities. East Anglia was a stronghold of Puritanism; Cromwell was himself a Huntingdon man and MP for Cambridge. A local historian defined an intruder as "one who enters without welcome or right" and "an obnoxious minister imposed by a patron." Such clergymen disliked the paraphernalia of High Church worship and wore their hair cut short.

[[borley-church|Borley Church]], with its associations with the staunchly Catholic [[waldegrave-family|Waldegrave family]] and its rich Catholic heritage, would have been a particularly sensitive posting for a Puritan intruder. Legend describes Deeks as a **"stern character"** [2], and one chronological entry in the Borley literature makes the startling claim that in 1642 **"Vicar John Deeks murders many people"** [4] — though this appears to be unsubstantiated local legend.

## The Clairvoyant's Impression (30 August 1986)

During a visit to Borley Church on 30 August 1986, a local clairvoyant brought by a psychical research group reported an impression of a man standing between the altar and the altar rail. The description was specific: he wore a jacket with pleated patch pockets in a large black-and-tan check pattern, had dark hair parted in the centre, well-marked brows, and a squarish face; he was of medium height. The clairvoyant noted that the figure gave "the impression of being an intruder into this time and place" — and that this man intensely disliked the trappings of the High Church and wore his hair cut short [2, 8].

Babbs and Mathias note that the word "intruder" in the Cromwellian sense had a precise meaning, and suggest the clairvoyant's use of this term — almost certainly unconscious — may indicate a genuine impression of Deeks. A Puritan minister would have worn short hair. The patch-pocket jacket described is post-Commonwealth in style; the authors leave this as possible but unresolved.

Investigators had also reportedly witnessed an apparition inside the church in 1980 which they assumed to be John Deeks [7].

> **Contradiction:** One chronological source inaccurately refers to Deeks as the vicar from "1653–1658," while detailed historical accounts and official lists confirm he served from 1642 to 1660 [1–3, 7].

## The 2004 "Rescue" Séance

In 2004, [[john-dening|Revd John Cranmer Dening]] organised a "rescue" séance inside Borley Church [9, 10]. During this session, a medium reportedly made contact with Deeks. Remarkably, the spirit of **John Deeks claimed that he had actually been the cause of the haunting at Borley over the years** [10]. Following this alleged confession, the medium purportedly laid the former clergyman's spirit to rest [10].

## Legacy

When the Commonwealth ended in 1660, Robert Warren was reinstated as rector but served only one more year, presumably due to age or ill health. John Deeks's precise dates of incumbency are 1642–1660.
