# John Deeks

Rector of Borley during the Commonwealth period (1642–1660), listed in the Borley rectors' appendix as an "Intruder." He replaced [[robert-warren-rector|Robert Warren]], who had held the living since 1607 and was dismissed for refusing to conform to Puritanism under Cromwell's Commonwealth.

## Historical Context

During Oliver Cromwell's Commonwealth (1642–1660), clergy who were unwilling or unable to accept Puritanism could be dismissed and replaced by "intruders" — clergymen imposed on a parish by Cromwellian authorities. East Anglia was a stronghold of Puritanism; Cromwell was himself a Huntingdon man and MP for Cambridge. A local historian described an intruder as "one who enters without welcome or right" and "an obnoxious minister imposed by a patron." Such clergymen disliked the paraphernalia of High Church worship and wore their hair cut short.

Borley Church, with its associations with the staunchly Catholic [[waldegrave-family|Waldegrave family]] and its rich Catholic heritage, would have been a particularly sensitive posting for a Puritan intruder.

## The Clairvoyant's Impression (1986)

During a visit to Borley Church on 30 August 1986, a local clairvoyant brought by a psychical research group reported an impression of a man standing between the altar and the altar rail. The description was specific: he wore a jacket with pleated patch pockets in a large black-and-tan check pattern, had dark hair parted in the centre, well-marked brows, and a squarish face; he was of medium height. The clairvoyant noted that the figure gave "the impression of being an intruder into this time and place."

Babbs and Mathias note that the word "intruder" in the Cromwellian sense had a precise meaning — a clergyman imposed on a parish — and suggest that the clairvoyant's use of this term, almost certainly unconscious, may indicate a genuine impression of Deeks. A Puritan minister would have worn short hair and notably plain clothing, though the patch-pocket jacket described is post-Commonwealth in style; the authors leave this as possible but unresolved.

## Legacy

When the Commonwealth ended in 1660, Robert Warren was reinstated as rector but served only one more year, presumably due to age or ill health. John Deeks's precise dates of incumbency are 1642–1660.
