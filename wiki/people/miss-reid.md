# Miss Reid

Also identified in the sources as **Alice Reid** [1, 2]. A personal friend of [[rev-ac-henning|Rev. Alfred Clifford Henning]] and his wife, she was described in the official observer's report as a **"very level-headed person"** who **"does not in any way pretend to be psychic"** [2]. She visited [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] in August 1937 and experienced the most dramatic recorded encounter with the "cold spot" — the temperature anomaly on the first-floor landing outside the [[blue-room|Blue Room]].

## The August 1937 Experience

Because Miss Reid was interested in the alleged haunting, the Hennings showed her over the empty Rectory during her visit in August 1937 [1]. While walking on the first-floor landing outside the Blue Room, she was suddenly overcome despite it being a warm summer evening: she experienced a **"feeling of terror, a sensation of 'pins and needles' all over, and felt very cold"** [2, 4]. The other members of the group touched her hands to verify her distress and found they were **"indeed icy"** [2, 4, 5]. No one else in the party experienced any unusual coldness [1, 2, 5].

Miss Reid moved away from the landing and quickly recovered. However, when the group returned to the exact same spot about fifteen minutes later, the freezing sensations repeated themselves identically [1, 2, 4, 5].

Controlled thermometer experiments conducted at this specific point on the landing later indicated a temperature drop of as much as **11°F** lower than the surrounding area [1].

## Significance

Miss Reid's experience became a benchmark account for the cold spot phenomenon — the clearest early documentation that the effect involved a physical chill measurable to third parties, not merely a subjective impression. [[rev-ac-henning|Rev. Henning]] referenced her experience when telling Price about [[mrs-martin-officer|Mrs Martin]]'s similar September 1943 encounter. [[sqn-ldr-horniman|Squadron-Leader Horniman]] also referenced her experience when his unnamed friend independently felt cold at the same spot in the same month.

The [[cambridge-commission|Cambridge Commission]] (June 1943) subsequently confirmed that the cold spot represents a measurable 11°F temperature anomaly — see [[longmuir-heap]]. Miss Reid's experience thus represents the first well-documented subjective encounter with what was subsequently established as a genuine physical phenomenon.

## Chronological Contradiction

> **Contradiction:** One chronological entry places the event on **5 August 1937** and notes that [[sidney-glanville|Sidney Glanville]] was inspecting the house with them [5]. Other entries and the direct quotation of the official report place the event on or around **23 August 1937**, witnessed by [[mark-kerr-pearse|Mark Kerr-Pearse]] during his extended stay [2, 3].
