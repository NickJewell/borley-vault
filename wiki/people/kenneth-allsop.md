# Kenneth Allsop

Journalist for *Picture Post* magazine who visited Borley in late 1954 with photographer [[godfrey-thurston-hopkins|Godfrey Thurston Hopkins]] to gather material for an article on the alleged haunting. He later became very well known as a television presenter and interviewer.

## The Picture Post Visit (~1954)

The editors of *Picture Post*, then a popular and respected magazine, decided to investigate the Borley mystery. They dispatched Allsop as reporter and Hopkins as photographer. Their visit resulted in the controversial photograph taken by Hopkins that showed an unexplained shadowy figure at the rectory gate — a figure that had not been visible in his viewfinder when he took the shot (see [[picture-post-photograph]]).

The article, "Is this the Borley Rectory ghost?", was published in *Picture Post* on 1 January 1955, illustrated with both Hopkins's original photograph and a clean comparison photograph taken from the same position.

## Later Career

At the time of the Borley visit, Allsop was working in print journalism. He subsequently became a prominent presence on British television as a presenter and interviewer, making his name considerably better known than it was during the *Picture Post* years. He is best remembered for presenting programmes such as *Tonight* on the BBC and for writing popular natural history books. He died in 1973.

## Significance

The Allsop/Hopkins visit represents a rare instance of a major mainstream magazine conducting a serious photographic investigation at Borley, with professional journalistic and photographic standards. The editorial decision to publish Hopkins's photograph — with caveats but without dismissal — and to commission a comparison photograph, was an unusually careful approach to paranormal reportage for a mass-circulation magazine of the period.
