# Ronald R. Russell

Professional photographer and psychical researcher; Chairman and co-founder of the North London-based **Enfield Parapsychical Research Group**, which he established alongside electrical engineer Frank Perry and mechanical engineer John Fay. Russell carried out investigations at [[borley-church]] from around 1974 through the late 1970s.

## Investigations at Borley Church

Because [[borley-rectory|the Rectory]] had long since been demolished, Russell and his colleagues focused their night-time vigils inside and around [[borley-church|Borley Church]]. During a typical vigil, the group's six-man team would divide into pairs — two on the Rectory site, two on the roadway, and two in the churchyard — rotating every two hours and communicating via walkie-talkie.

**Photographic Anomalies**: As an experienced professional photographer, Russell was unable to explain anomalies that appeared on certain frames of his film after developing. Perfectly normal photographs of the church and churchyard were interspersed with pictures showing unusual shadow effects, lights, and what he explicitly described as "ectoplasm" [4].

**Audio Recordings**: The Enfield Group claimed to have recorded hundreds of unexplained noises inside the locked church building, including footsteps, knocking, crashing sounds, clicks, sighs, and groans. Russell reported recording a deep, grunting voice that reminded him "irresistibly of Lee Marvin singing 'Wandering Star'" [4]. The whereabouts of these recordings — if they still survive — is unknown, though some did survive to 1997.

**The Waldegrave Tomb Anomaly**: Describing a physical encounter inside the church, Russell stated: "On one occasion, we located a centre of disturbance near the [[waldegrave-monument|Waldegrave tomb]]; it was tangible, like a swirling column of energy. When you passed your hand through it, you felt a sort of crackle, like static electricity" [6].

**The Seasonal Theory**: In an interview with Frank Smyth in the early 1980s, Russell theorized that a paranormal power was concentrated in Borley Church which "waxed and waned according to the seasons." He noted the energy was sporadic at the beginning of the year but reached "full flood" in August (close to the traditional [[july-28th]] date for the sighting of the nun), and believed the power was boosted by the presence of human observers.

## Vincent O'Neil Visit

During a trip to England in September 1997, [[vincent-oneil|Vincent O'Neil]] met Russell at the London offices of the [[spr|Society for Psychical Research]] and heard the tape recordings of allegedly paranormal noises that Russell and his team had obtained inside the church. O'Neil noted that the sounds gave him "goose bumps."

## Rivalry with Croom-Hollingsworth

[[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth|Geoffrey Croom-Hollingsworth]] dismissed Russell's group dismissively in a letter to [[richard-lee-van-den-daele|Richard Lee-Van den Daele]] in July 1984: "I regard as a joke as I only bumped into them once, with some rubbish equipment. Most of it was junk, but looks impressive on the photo." This suggests professional rivalry between the two teams operating in the same space in the 1970s–80s.
