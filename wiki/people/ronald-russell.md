# Ronald R. Russell

Professional photographer and psychical researcher who carried out investigations at [[borley-church]] in the mid to late 1970s, as part of the [[enfield-group|Enfield Parapsychical Research Group]] he had founded.

## Investigations at Borley Church

Russell was an experienced photographer and was unable to explain anomalies discovered on certain frames of film following developing — unusual shadow effects, lights and what he described as "ectoplasm" occurred between perfectly normal photographs of the churchyard and the church.

The Enfield Group claimed to have recorded hundreds of unusual noises inside Borley Church including footsteps, crashing sounds and on one occasion a deep grunting voice which reminded Russell of the actor Lee Marvin singing *Wandering Star*. The whereabouts of these recordings — if they still survive — is unknown.

Through his experiences, Russell stated he felt a power of some kind was concentrated in Borley Church that waxed and waned according to the seasons. Through the experiences of the Enfield Group he described examples of the force being very sporadic at the beginning of the year, but in August at "full flood" — close to the traditional [[july-28th]] date for the sighting of the nun.

## Vincent O'Neil Visit

During a trip to England in September 1997 to visit Borley, [[vincent-oneil]] met Russell at the London offices of the [[society-for-psychical-research]] and heard tape recordings of the allegedly paranormal noises that Russell and his team had obtained inside the church.

## Rivalry with Croom-Hollingsworth

[[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth]] dismissed Russell's group dismissively in a letter to [[richard-lee-van-den-daele]] in July 1984: "I regard as a joke as I only bumped into them once, with some rubbish equipment. Most of it was junk, but looks impressive on the photo." This suggests professional rivalry between the two teams operating in the same space in the 1970s-80s.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Ronald R. Russell** (also referred to as Ron Russell) was a professional photographer and psychical researcher who conducted extensive investigations at Borley during the 1970s. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following biographical details, investigations, specific quotes, and rivalries regarding his involvement in the case:

**The Enfield Parapsychical Research Group**
*   **Founding:** Russell was the Chairman and a founding member of the North London-based **Enfield Parapsychical Research Group**, which he established alongside electrical engineer Frank Perry and mechanical engineer John Fay [1, 2]. 
*   **The 1970s Investigations:** The group was active at Borley from around **1974 through the late 1970s** [1]. Because the Rectory had long since been demolished, Russell and his colleagues focused their night-time vigils inside and around Borley Church [1, 3]. 
*   **Findings:** Unlike some other contemporary investigators, Russell’s group did not report spectacular visual encounters with the famous phantom nun [1]. However, they were absolutely convinced they had captured genuine paranormal phenomena on film and audio tape [1]. 

**Photographic and Audio Evidence**
*   **Photographic Anomalies:** As an experienced professional photographer, Russell was unable to explain anomalies that appeared on certain frames of his film after developing. Perfectly normal photographs of the church and churchyard were interspersed with pictures showing unusual shadow effects, lights, and what he explicitly described as **"ectoplasm"** [4]. 
*   **Audio Recordings:** The group claimed to have recorded hundreds of unexplained noises inside the locked church building. These recordings purported to capture footsteps, knocking, crashing sounds, clicks, sighs, and groans [4, 5]. 

**Specific Quotes and Key Experiences**
Russell gave several interviews detailing specific, highly unusual encounters his group had inside Borley Church:
*   **The "Lee Marvin" Voice:** He reported recording a deep, grunting voice that he said reminded him **"irresistibly of Lee Marvin singing 'Wandering Star'"** [4, 6].
*   **The Waldegrave Tomb Anomaly:** Describing a physical encounter inside the church, Russell stated: **"On one occasion, we located a centre of disturbance near the Waldegrave tomb; it was tangible, like a swirling column of energy. When you passed your hand through it, you felt a sort of crackle, like static electricity"** [6].
*   **The Seasonal Theory:** In an interview with Frank Smyth in the early 1980s, Russell theorized that a paranormal power was concentrated in Borley Church which **"waxed and waned according to the seasons"** [7]. He noted the energy was sporadic at the beginning of the year but reached **"full flood"** in August (close to the traditional July 28th anniversary of the phantom nun's appearance), adding his belief that the power was boosted by the presence of human observers [7].

**Rivalries and Contradictions**
Russell's work was not universally respected by his peers, and he was the target of professional jealousy from rival Borley investigator Geoffrey Croom-Hollingsworth [8]. In a letter dated July 7, 1984, Croom-Hollingsworth harshly dismissed Russell and the Enfield Group, writing that he regarded them as **"a joke"** who used **"rubbish equipment"** that was mostly **"junk, but looks impressive on the photo"** [8].

**The 1997 Meeting with Vincent O'Neil**
Russell's audio evidence survived for decades. On **September 19, 1997**, Vincent O'Neil (the adopted son of Marianne Foyster) attended a ghost tour in England where Russell gave an illustrated talk featuring his slides, photographs, and tape recordings [2, 3]. O'Neil met Russell at the London offices of the Society for Psychical Research, where he was finally able to listen to the actual tape recordings made in the 1970s, noting that the sounds gave him **"goose bumps"** [3, 9].
