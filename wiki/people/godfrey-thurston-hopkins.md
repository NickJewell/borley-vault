# Godfrey Thurston Hopkins

Photographer working for the magazine *Picture Post*. Dispatched to Borley with reporter Kenneth Allsop in late 1954 to investigate the Borley haunting for an article.

## The Ghost Photograph

Hopkins attempted to photograph one of the two gatepost entrances to the former rectory driveway — the specific spot where [[fred-cartwright|Fred Cartwright]] had claimed to see the [[the-nun|phantom nun]] on four occasions in 1927. The intention was to capture the gatepost and picket fencing against the clear sky.

When the film was developed, the photograph showed **a wispy, shadowy outline of a figure** superimposed upon or standing in front of the picket fencing stakes — not visible in the viewfinder when the picture was taken. Hopkins stated:
- It was mid-afternoon with good visibility
- He was emphasising the gatepost against the clear sky
- The dark shadow was **not visible in the viewfinder**
- He could not account for its appearance

*Picture Post*'s photographic department confirmed the negative was genuine and had not been tampered with. A second photographer was sent to take a picture from exactly the same position — it showed only the gatepost and fencing.

*Picture Post* published both pictures in their issue dated **1 January 1955** under the headline "Is this the Borley Rectory ghost?"

The authors note this may be another example of mechanical/photographic equipment behaving anomalously at Borley, consistent with the [[ley-lines-at-borley|ley-lines hypothesis]]. They compare it to Dr C. Hilton-Rowe's light meter malfunctioning at exactly the same gate in March 1947.
