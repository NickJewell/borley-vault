# Bernard Foyster

Brother of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]] and family lawyer; one of the people who helped arrange the Foysters' move into [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] in October 1930.

## Role

When the Foysters were staying in lodgings at Great Cornard in the summer of 1930 and considering where to settle, [[gerald-bull|Gerald Bull]] suggested they consider living at Borley Rectory itself, which was standing empty.

The following Friday, Lionel's cousin Bernard Foyster, who was both the **family lawyer** and had **a say in the gift of the living**, came to stay with the Bulls for the weekend. He also thought it would be beneficial to have relatives in the Rectory again — the property would only continue to deteriorate if left unoccupied. Bernard took the keys and accompanied Lionel and [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] to Borley to view the vacant house.

His dual role — family lawyer and patron of the living — made him the key person who unlocked both the practical and ecclesiastical barriers to the Foysters taking the Rectory.

> **Note:** Bernard Foyster is distinct from [[arthur-foyster|Arthur Foyster]], Lionel's other brother.
