# Bernard Foyster

Cousin of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]] and family lawyer; one of the key figures who arranged the Foysters' move into [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] in October 1930. He also later played a direct legal role in extracting [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] from the volatile [[jonquille-et-cie|Jonquille et Cie]] arrangement.

## Role in the Move to Borley

When the Foysters were staying in lodgings at Great Cornard in the summer of 1930 and considering where to settle, [[gerald-bull|Gerald Bull]] suggested they consider living at Borley Rectory itself, which was standing empty.

The following Friday, Bernard Foyster — who was both the **family lawyer** and **had a say in the gift of the living** — came to stay with the Bulls for the weekend [2, 3]. He expressed that it would be very nice to have a relative residing in Borley Rectory once more, noting that the vast property would only continue to deteriorate if left unoccupied [2, 3]. Acting in his capacity as patron, **Bernard took the keys and personally accompanied Lionel and Marianne to view the vacant house**, which ultimately led to their fateful decision to make the Rectory their home [3].

His dual role — family lawyer and patron of the living — made him the key person who unlocked both the practical and ecclesiastical barriers to the Foysters taking the Rectory.

## Intervening with François d'Arles

Bernard later played a direct legal role in extracting Marianne from a volatile situation. When Marianne was running the *Jonquille et Cie* flower shop in Wimbledon with [[francois-darles|François d'Arles]] (Frank Pearless), the relationship became highly strained [1]. Marianne later claimed that d'Arles was a "very dictatorial man" who constantly started arguments, making the situation "terribly unbearable" [1]. Desperate for an escape, **Marianne went to Bernard Foyster for legal help**, and according to her 1958 interview with Robert Swanson, **Bernard Foyster and his clerk subsequently intervened and "got rid of d'Arles"** [1].

> **Note:** Bernard Foyster is distinct from [[arthur-foyster|Arthur Foyster]], Lionel's other brother.
