# Miss Audrey Sawrey-Cookson

One of the four-person party that accompanied [[miss-susanna-dudley|Miss Susanna Dudley]] to Borley on 20 February 1949. On the path to [[borley-church|Borley Church]], she was one of three members of the group (all except [[sub-lt-crossley|Sub-Lt Jonathan Crossley]]) who encountered the unexplained intense smell. She described it as smelling "like incense" — a description that matched accounts from previous visitors including [[james-turner|James Turner]] and others who had noticed inexplicable odours at [[borley-church|Borley Church]] and the Rectory site.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Miss Audrey Sawrey-Cookson** (also spelled Sawrey-Cooksen in one account) was a visitor to Borley whose minor but notable connection to the saga involves an encounter with an unexplained olfactory phenomenon in the late 1940s. 

A brief reference profile drawn from the sources reveals the following specific details about her experience:

**The February 20, 1949 Visit**
*   **The Excursion:** On Sunday, February 20, 1949, Miss Sawrey-Cookson traveled to Borley in the company of three friends: Miss Susanna Dudley (a resident of Stradishall Manor, near Newmarket), Sub-Lieutenant Alastair McIver, RN, and Sub-Lieutenant Jonathan Crossley, RN [1-3].
*   **The Objective:** The group made the trip specifically hoping to catch a glimpse of the famous Borley "Nun" [3]. 
*   **The Weather and the Church:** Like many sightseers before them, the group was disappointed and failed to see the elusive apparition [3]. The weather that day was very windy and chilly, so the four friends sought shelter by crossing the road to take a look at Borley Church [2, 3].

**The Strange Odor Phenomenon**
Miss Sawrey-Cookson's primary inclusion in the Borley records stems from what happened as the group approached the church. 
*   While walking halfway up the church path, Miss Sawrey-Cookson, Miss Dudley, and Sub-Lt. McIver suddenly encountered a strange, overpowering, and highly localized smell [1, 2]. 
*   Miss Dudley specifically likened the scent to the smell of an **"embalmed body"** [2]. 
*   However, demonstrating the often subjective nature of the Borley phenomena, the fourth member of their party, Jonathan Crossley, could not smell anything at all [2]. 

The provided texts contain no further biographical details or events regarding Miss Sawrey-Cookson beyond this single group sighting.
