# Miss Audrey Sawrey-Cookson

One of the four-person party that accompanied [[miss-susanna-dudley|Miss Susanna Dudley]] to Borley on **20 February 1949**. Also spelled "Sawrey-Cooksen" in at least one account.

## The February 20, 1949 Visit

Miss Sawrey-Cookson traveled to Borley in the company of Miss Susanna Dudley (a resident of Stradishall Manor, near Newmarket), [[sub-lt-mciever|Sub-Lieutenant Alastair McIver, RN]], and [[sub-lt-crossley|Sub-Lieutenant Jonathan Crossley, RN]] [1–3]. The group made the trip specifically hoping to catch a glimpse of the famous Borley [[the-nun|"Nun"]] [3]. They were disappointed on that count. The weather was very windy and chilly, so the four friends sought shelter by crossing the road to inspect [[borley-church|Borley Church]] [2, 3].

## The Strange Odour Phenomenon

Halfway up the church path, Miss Sawrey-Cookson, Miss Dudley, and Sub-Lt McIver suddenly encountered a strange, overpowering, and highly localised smell [1, 2]. She described it as smelling **"like incense"** — a description that matched accounts from previous visitors including [[james-turner|James Turner]] and others who had noticed inexplicable odours at the church and Rectory site.

> **Contradiction:** Miss Dudley likened the scent to the smell of an "embalmed body"; Sub-Lt McIver called it "the most disgusting thing he had ever smelled." All three found it overwhelming. The fourth member of the party, Jonathan Crossley, could not smell anything at all [2] — an anomaly that resists simple explanation, as his sense of smell was verified to be functional before and after the event.

The provided texts record no further biographical details or events regarding Miss Sawrey-Cookson beyond this single group sighting.
