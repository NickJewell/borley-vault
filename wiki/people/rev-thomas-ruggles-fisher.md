# Rev. Thomas Ruggles Fisher

Rector of Liston from 1857 until his death in 1893; the father of [[rev-stewart-fisher|Rev Stewart Travers Fisher]] who succeeded him. Together they held the Liston living for nearly 80 years. During his tenure he resided in the "Old Rectory" at Liston, where his son spent his boyhood.

## The Fisher Dynasty at Liston

Thomas Ruggles Fisher established what became a two-generation family incumbency at Liston running from 1857 to 1936 — almost exactly parallel in duration to the Bull family's hold on the Borley living (1862–1927). The two adjacent parishes were thus simultaneously dominated by prominent local clerical families for much of the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

The sources explicitly highlight this historical parallel, describing the Fisher family as having established a local "Fisher dynasty" at Liston that ran concurrently with the famous Bull dynasty at neighbouring Borley and Pentlow [1].

In the context of the Borley story, Thomas Ruggles Fisher's significance is primarily as the grandfather of [[nancy-goodale|Nancy Goodale (née Fisher)]], who became a close friend of [[claudine-mathias|Claudine Mathias]] during the Mathias incumbency at Borley and Liston (1955–1967). Nancy's account of life in the area, and her memories of the Bull and Smith periods at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]], form part of the testimony gathered by Babbs and Mathias for *Borley Rectory: The Final Analysis*.
