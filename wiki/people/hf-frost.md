# H.H. Frost

> **Contradiction:** The page slug follows the original "H.F. Frost" designation, but the Borley sources consistently record this individual as **H.H. Frost** [1].

**Secretary** of the [[marks-tey-spiritualist-circle|Marks Tey Spiritualist Circle]], an Essex spiritualist group based about eight miles from Borley [1, 2]. His address is recorded as **The Mill House, Layer-de-la-Haye, Colchester** [2]. He travelled to [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] with [[george-warren|George Warren]] and [[captain-deane|Captain Deane]] on the night of 22 January 1932, and participated in the January 1932 séance.

## The January 1932 Intervention

In **January 1932**, following a period of intense poltergeist activity at Borley Rectory, Frost and Warren visited [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]] and his wife [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]], offering to come and help the family "get rid of ghosts" [3, 4].

Frost arrived with Warren and Captain Deane unexpectedly after midnight on **January 22, 1932**, informing Lionel Foyster that the "helpers on the other side" had warned that Marianne's spirit protectors could not hold out much longer. The intrusion became a significant overnight event. Because poltergeist throwing phenomena began immediately upon the group's arrival, they decided to spend the night [3]. Frost and Warren left the medium at the Rectory while they went to fetch other members of their circle [3].

The resulting overnight vigil on **January 23–24, 1932** produced what Foyster described as "great demonstrations," including bottles dashing down the stairs, broken glass strewn in the kitchen passage, and violent bell-ringing [3]. The spiritualist party stayed until 5:00 a.m. and left believing the trouble had been arrested [3]. Notably, the violent phenomena at the Rectory did indeed abruptly cease for several years following their intervention [3, 5].

## Documentary Evidence

Because Frost was the secretary of the spiritualist group, he held the official documentation of the night's events. In his *Summary of Experiences*, [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Foyster]] directed [[harry-price|Harry Price]] to Frost for the official record:

> **"(I have a copy of the circle's own report of the proceedings. If you would like to have one, the secretary is: H.H. Frost, the Mill House, Layer-de-la-Haye, Colchester, who I think would probably be pleased to furnish any information.)"** [2]

Years later, during her 1958 interviews with American investigator A. Robert Swanson, [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] also specifically recalled Frost's presence:

> **"There was a Mr. Frost and a Mr. and Mrs. - I think it was Warren - and I don't remember the name of the medium that they brought with them, but I did sit in on a seance a couple of times."** [6]
