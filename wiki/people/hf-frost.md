# H.F. Frost

Member of the [[marks-tey-spiritualist-circle|Marks Tey Spiritualist Circle]] who travelled to [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] with [[george-warren|George Warren]] and [[captain-deane|Captain Deane]] on the night of 22 January 1932, and participated in the January 1932 séance.

## Role

Frost arrived with Warren and Captain Deane unexpectedly after midnight on 22 January 1932, informing [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] that the "helpers on the other side" had warned that [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]]'s spirit protectors could not hold out much longer. He returned the following night for the full séance conducted by medium [[guy-lestrange|Guy L'Estrange]].

Beyond his presence as one of the Circle's representatives at Borley, Frost's individual role is not described in detail in the sources.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

The Borley sources record this individual specifically as **H.H. Frost** (rather than H.F. Frost). He was a member of the **Marks Tey Spiritualist Circle**, an Essex spiritualist group based about eight miles from Borley [1]. 

A reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following biographical details, events, and specific quotes regarding his involvement during the intensely active Foyster incumbency:

**Biographical Details and Role**
H.H. Frost served as the **secretary** of the Marks Tey Spiritualist Circle [1, 2]. The sources record his specific address as **The Mill House, Layer-de-la-Haye, Colchester** [2]. 

**The January 1932 Intervention**
In **January 1932**, following a period of intense poltergeist activity at Borley Rectory, Frost and a fellow circle member, Mr. Warren, visited the Rev. Lionel Foyster and his wife Marianne. They offered to come and help the family "get rid of ghosts" [3, 4]. 

This initial meeting culminated in the famous seance and exorcism of **January 23–24, 1932** [1, 3]. Frost and Warren arrived with a medium on January 23. Because poltergeist throwing phenomena began immediately upon their arrival, the group decided to spend the night [3]. Frost and Warren left the medium at the Rectory while they went to fetch other members of their circle [3]. The resulting overnight vigil produced what Foyster described as "great demonstrations," including bottles dashing down the stairs, broken glass strewn in the kitchen passage, and violent bell-ringing [3]. The spiritualist party stayed until 5:00 a.m. and left believing the trouble had been arrested [3]. Following their intervention, the violent phenomena at the Rectory did indeed abruptly cease for several years [3, 5].

**Documentary Evidence and Specific Quotes**
Because Frost was the secretary of the spiritualist group, he held the official documentation of the night's events. In his *Summary of Experiences*, the Rev. Lionel Foyster provided a direct quote directing Harry Price to Frost for the official record:
> **"(I have a copy of the circle's own report of the proceedings. If you would like to have one, the secretary is: H.H. Frost, the Mill House, Layer-de-la-Haye, Colchester, who I think would probably be pleased to furnish any information.)"** [2].

Years later, during her 1958 interviews with American investigator A. Robert Swanson, Marianne Foyster also specifically recalled Frost's presence during the spiritualist group's visits, noting: 
> **"There was a Mr. Frost and a Mr. and Mrs. - I think it was Warren - and I don't remember the name of the medium that they brought with them, but I did sit in on a seance a couple of times."** [6].
