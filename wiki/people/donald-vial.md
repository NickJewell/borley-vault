# Donald Frederick Pratt Vial

(Died 2 June 1979) Little-known Borley resident. [[alan-roper]] established that Vial lived at a number of addresses in the Borley district in the 1930s, one of which was [[borley-rectory]] towards the end of the [[foyster-incumbency]], possibly around 1934. He was presumably taken in as a lodger by the [[lionel-foyster|Revd Lionel Foyster]].

Vial does not feature in any book or account on the Rectory haunting. He died before Roper had a chance to meet and interview him.

## Significance

Vial's existence demonstrates that even the most detailed accounts of the Borley case leave undocumented residents. If he was at the Rectory during the final year of the Foyster period, he would have been a witness to any phenomena occurring in 1934 — a period about which little is recorded since the most intense [[foyster-phenomena|phenomena]] of 1930–32 had largely subsided. His death before researchers could interview him represents yet another lost opportunity for first-hand testimony.
