# Donald Frederick Pratt Vial

(Died 2 June 1979) Little-known Borley resident uncovered through the research of [[alan-roper|Alan Roper]] [1]. Vial lived at a number of addresses in the Borley district in the 1930s, one of which was [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] towards the end of the Foyster incumbency, possibly around 1934 — presumably taken in as a lodger by the [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]] [1].

## Absence from the Record

Despite lodging at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] during the [[foyster-phenomena|Foyster period]], which [[harry-price|Harry Price]] famously described as the "most violent, and most dangerous period" in the haunting's history [2], Vial "does not feature in any book or account on the Rectory haunting" [1].

## Significance

Vial's existence demonstrates that even the most detailed accounts of the Borley case leave undocumented residents. If he was at the Rectory during the final year of the Foyster period, he would have been a witness to any phenomena occurring in 1934 — a period about which little is recorded since the most intense [[foyster-phenomena|phenomena]] of 1930–32 had largely subsided. He died on **2 June 1979**, before [[alan-roper|Alan Roper]] had the chance to track him down and interview him [1] — yet another lost opportunity for first-hand testimony.
