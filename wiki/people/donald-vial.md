# Donald Frederick Pratt Vial

(Died 2 June 1979) Little-known Borley resident. [[alan-roper]] established that Vial lived at a number of addresses in the Borley district in the 1930s, one of which was [[borley-rectory]] towards the end of the [[foyster-incumbency]], possibly around 1934. He was presumably taken in as a lodger by the [[lionel-foyster|Revd Lionel Foyster]].

Vial does not feature in any book or account on the Rectory haunting. He died before Roper had a chance to meet and interview him.

## Significance

Vial's existence demonstrates that even the most detailed accounts of the Borley case leave undocumented residents. If he was at the Rectory during the final year of the Foyster period, he would have been a witness to any phenomena occurring in 1934 — a period about which little is recorded since the most intense [[foyster-phenomena|phenomena]] of 1930–32 had largely subsided. His death before researchers could interview him represents yet another lost opportunity for first-hand testimony.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Donald Fredrick Pratt Vial** is described in the Borley sources as a "little known Borley resident" whose history was uncovered by investigator Alan Roper [1]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following limited but highly specific details regarding his life and connection to the Rectory:

**Biographical Details and Rectory Residence**
Vial lived at a number of different addresses in the Borley district throughout the 1930s [1]. Crucially, Alan Roper established that one of these addresses was Borley Rectory itself [1]. Vial resided at the house towards the end of the Foyster incumbency, possibly around the year 1934, and the sources note he was "presumably taken in as a lodger by the Revd Lionel Foyster" [1]. 

**Absence from the Record and Death**
A notable detail for any reference profile is his complete absence from the famous lore of the house. Despite lodging at Borley Rectory during the highly publicized Foyster period—which Harry Price famously described as the "most violent, and most dangerous period" in the haunting's history [2]—Vial **"does not feature in any book or account on the Rectory haunting"** [1]. 

Unfortunately, his firsthand knowledge of the Foyster household remains lost to history. He died on **June 2, 1979**, before Alan Roper had the chance to track him down and interview him [1].
