# Peter Eton

BBC producer who played a significant role in bringing the Borley Rectory story to a national audience through radio. He co-wrote (alongside [[alan-burgess|Alan Burgess]]) and produced the 45-minute BBC Home Service broadcast **"The Haunted Rectory"**, transmitted at 9:30 p.m. on **Sunday, 29 June 1947** as No. 10 in the *True Escape Stories* series [2, 3, 4]. The programme was billed as an "impartial investigation" [2, 3].

## Recording Schedule

Eton coordinated a detailed recording schedule. He visited Borley on **17 June 1947** to record interviews with key witnesses, including Mr and Mrs Cooper, Miss [[ethel-bull|Ethel Bull]], and [[samuel-seal|Samuel Seal]], and subsequently recorded with investigator [[harry-price|Harry Price]] on **20 June 1947** [5].

## The Tapping Incident

Prior to recording the witness interviews, Eton and Burgess visited the ruined Rectory site, where they personally experienced a striking auditory phenomenon in broad daylight [6, 7]. While Eton stood on a concrete slab at one side of the ruined cellars and Burgess faced him from the opposite side, the two men heard a number of short, sharp raps. Eton actively engaged with the phenomenon — **asking the raps to stop and start again** — and the sounds obeyed his commands, ceasing and continuing exactly as he asked [6, 7]. A recording of this specific incident was successfully captured and included in the final June broadcast [6, 7].

During the actual broadcast recording of the tapping sounds in the cellar ruins, Eton's unscripted, real-time reaction was also documented:

> **"It's peculiar... Speak to me whoever you are."** [10]

## Scepticism and Shift

Eton initially approached the Borley haunting with deep scepticism, a stance he explicitly highlighted in the broadcast script [8, 9]:

> **"Ghosts? Nonsense. All these things you've just heard about – noises, manifestations, apparitions? Impossible. That was how we thought anyway... But not true. Not the sort of thing that actually happens. And yet – we'd read Mr. Harry Price's two well known books on the alleged hauntings, and there was no doubt that the evidence he presented was extremely impressive... Could there possibly be something in it? And if so could we find out?"** [8]
