# Peter Eton

BBC. Visited the Borley Rectory site in June 1947, two days before the BBC's broadcast "The Haunted Rectory" (transmitted 29 June 1947), accompanied by BBC colleague [[alan-burgess|Alan Burgess]].

During their visit, while Eton stood on a concrete slab at one side of the ruins and Burgess faced him on the opposite side, a number of short, sharp raps were heard coming from the former cellars. Eton asked the raps to stop and start again — and they complied. This occurred in broad daylight. A recording of the raps was made and included in the broadcast.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Peter Eton** was a BBC producer who played a significant role in bringing the Borley Rectory story to a national audience through radio. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the sources reveals the following specific details, events, dates, and quotes regarding his involvement in the case:

**"The Haunted Rectory" Broadcast (1947)**
Eton's primary contribution to the Borley saga was his role as the producer and co-writer (alongside Alan Burgess) of a 45-minute BBC radio broadcast titled **"The Haunted Rectory"** [1, 2]. 
*   The program was billed as an "impartial investigation" and was broadcast as No. 10 in the *True Escape Stories* series [2, 3]. 
*   It aired on the BBC Home Service at **9:30 p.m. on Sunday, June 29, 1947** [3, 4].
*   Eton coordinated a detailed recording schedule for the program, visiting Borley on **June 17, 1947**, to record interviews with key witnesses, including Mr. and Mrs. Cooper, Miss Ethel Bull, and Samuel Seal, and subsequently recording with investigator Harry Price on **June 20, 1947** [5].

**The Tapping Incident**
Prior to recording the witness interviews, Eton and Burgess visited the ruined Rectory site in mid-May 1947, where they personally experienced a striking paranormal auditory phenomenon in broad daylight [6, 7]. 
*   While Eton stood on a concrete slab at one side of the ruined cellars and Burgess faced him from the opposite side, the two men heard a number of short, sharp raps [6, 7]. 
*   Eton actively engaged with the phenomenon, **asking the raps to stop and start again** [6, 7]. The sounds obeyed his commands, ceasing and continuing exactly as he asked [6, 7]. 
*   Eton was highly intrigued by the responsive nature of the sounds, and a recording of this specific incident was successfully captured and included in the final June broadcast [6, 7]. 

**Quotes and Skepticism**
Eton initially approached the Borley haunting with deep skepticism, a stance he explicitly highlighted in the script of his broadcast [8, 9]. In the program, he detailed his shift from disbelief to curiosity with the following quote:
> **"Ghosts? Nonsense. All these things you’ve just heard about – noises, manifestations, apparitions? Impossible. That was how we thought anyway... But not true. Not the sort of thing that actually happens. And yet – we’d read Mr. Harry Price’s two well known books on the alleged hauntings, and there was no doubt that the evidence he presented was extremely impressive... Could there possibly be something in it? And if so could we find out?"** [8]

During the actual broadcast recording of the tapping sounds in the cellar ruins, Eton's unscripted, real-time reaction to the noises was also documented:
> **"It’s peculiar... Speak to me whoever you are."** [10]
