# Sir George Whitehouse

Husband of [[lady-whitehouse]]; lived at Arthur Hall, Sudbury, approximately one mile from Borley Rectory. Personally witnessed Foyster-period phenomena on at least two occasions, including fire in the skirting-board and stones thrown by an unseen force. He died before [[harry-price]] and [[sidney-glanville]] visited [[lady-whitehouse]] on March 29, 1939, when Price was told "with regret, that Sir George had passed away."

## Personal Experiences at Borley

**May 4, 1931** — Sir George and Lady Whitehouse visited the Rectory for a church meeting (the Rural Dean also attended). When the Rural Dean left, Mrs. Foyster showed Lady Whitehouse the state of the kitchen cupboard — its contents "strewn all over the place." Sir George and [[lionel-foyster]] joined the women in the kitchen, and the bells began ringing.

After dinner they returned to support the Foysters through the evening. Lady Whitehouse writes:

> "My husband and Mr. Foyster went out to inspect the backyard... As they came in, a large flint came down the stairs by the back door."

The evening culminated in Sir George and Lady Whitehouse and the Foysters discovering a skirting-board fire (with a glowing hole charred in the centre); they extinguished it by carrying water from the bathroom. While they watched to ensure the fire was out, a flint "about the size of a hen's egg fell between us: the window was shut and there were no holes in the ceiling. My husband picked it up and put it in his pocket." Two more flints fell before they descended.

After the house was fumigated with dried lavender, Sir George and Foyster inspected the back yard for signs of fire origin — none were found. They then took the entire Foyster family back to Arthur Hall at 11:30 p.m.

**A second visit, summer 1931** — The Foysters were in acute distress, and the Whitehouses drove to the Rectory at the call of Lady Whitehouse's nephew ([[dom-richard-whitehouse]]). Sir George accompanied Lady Whitehouse; she describes hearing falling objects as she entered the front door, and the two small children "crouching at the dining-room door, saying: 'We don't like so many things falling.'"

## Significance

Sir George Whitehouse is listed in Appendix D as a witness to bell-ringing and fire phenomena — independent confirmation of the Foyster diary accounts. He and his wife were the principal neighbours who provided the Foysters with refuge during the worst poltergeist peaks (on at least two occasions the entire family moved temporarily to [[arthur-hall-sudbury|Arthur Hall]]).

[[lady-whitehouse]], writing to Price in 1939, describes Sir George and herself as having "never questioned the truth of what Mr. Bull and Mr. Foyster told us." She credits their sympathy as having been "a great help to the Foysters."
