# Hugh E. Fee-Smith

(Sometimes referred to as H. E. Fee-Smith.) One of [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s "official observers" during the 1937–38 tenancy investigation of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] [1]. He conducted his investigations in the company of his friends [[dr-hf-bellamy|Dr. Henry F. Bellamy]] and [[fc-bellamy|Francis C. Bellamy]] [2, 3]. Experienced tactual phenomena on both visits, listed in Appendix D under "Tactual Phenomena ('Touchings')."

## First Visit: The "Bell Alarm" Incident (October 16–17, 1937)

During the weekend of **October 16–17, 1937**, Fee-Smith and the Bellamys conducted an observational vigil [3]. The team had rigged a simple electrical contact-breaker connected to a bell alarm, placed under a pile of books on the dining-room mantelpiece, designed so that if a book was disturbed the circuit would close and ring the bell [3].

At **12:50 a.m.** on October 17, while the men were resting upstairs in the Blue Room, the bell began ringing and continued for about a minute before ceasing abruptly [4]. When they investigated, they found the books had been completely displaced, with the bottom book pushed entirely off the contact breaker [4]. The observers were unable to account for this physical phenomenon [4, 5].

> **Contradiction:** The authors of the 1956 *Borley Report* pointed out a significant omission in Harry Price's published account: in the original report furnished to Price, the investigators noted that a cat had entered the house the evening before the disturbance [5]. Although the observers reported ejecting the cat at 6:20 p.m., the SPR critics argued it was entirely possible the animal had found its way back in (as the room's door was unlatched) and disturbed the books — a crucial detail Price deliberately left out of *The Most Haunted House in England* [5].

During this same visit, the observers also noted two fresh pencil marks and heard a faint knocking from the Base Room [2].

## Second Visit: Tactile Phenomena (May 26–27, 1938)

Fee-Smith returned for a second observation period on **May 26–27, 1938** [6]. It was during this visit that he experienced startling tactile manifestations while standing outside the two large cupboards in the short corridor leading to the side garden door:

- At about **7:00 p.m.**, Fee-Smith had a distinct feeling that someone had lightly ruffled his hair [7].
- At about **10:00 p.m.**, taking up the exact same position in the dark, Fee-Smith had the clear sensation of someone touching his face [7].

In the official report, [[fc-bellamy|Francis Bellamy]] pondered the significance of the location, providing the specific quote: **"Was it not through this garden door that the nun sometimes was seen to enter?"** [7] — the passage where the tactual phenomena occurred was the approach route from the area of the [[nuns-walk|Nun's Walk]] and drive gate.

At about **1:00 a.m.** during this same visit, the party also discovered and marked a fresh pencil line in Room No. 7, situated next to the [[blue-room|Blue Room]] [7].

Fee-Smith's experiences are a subset of the Borley "touching" phenomenon, which includes [[mark-kerr-pearse|Mark Kerr-Pearse]] having his coat pulled twice and [[fa-mansbridge|Mrs. F. A. Mansbridge]] having her coat belt lifted and dropped.
