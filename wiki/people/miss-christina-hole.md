# Miss Christina Hole

A **"well-known writer and distinguished authority on witchcraft and hauntings"** [1], recognised as one of the leading specialists in British folklore and paranormal phenomena of the mid-twentieth century. She participated in a BBC broadcast with [[harry-price|Harry Price]] about the Borley hauntings [1], and later joined [[dr-letitia-fairfield|Dr Letitia Fairfield]] and Peter Underwood for a [[ghost-club|Ghost Club]] discussion of the case.

## Ghost Club Discussion — 11 May 1960

On **11 May 1960**, Miss Hole participated in a Ghost Club discussion focused on the Borley haunting in general and the [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]] occupancy in particular [1, 2]. She agreed with Underwood and Tabori's view that the critics' case against [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] was "largely concerned with putting Harry Price into the dock as a sort of secondary or unconscious accomplice of Marianne — which he certainly was not" [3].

## Avenbury Church

Demonstrating her broad expertise in paranormal phenomena, Miss Hole also provided researchers with information about **unaccountable organ music reported from Avenbury church, Herefordshire** — an instance she had personally investigated [4]. She noted it as part of the broader pattern of church-related paranormal phenomena in Britain, of which [[borley-church|Borley Church]] is the most documented example.
