# Miss Christina Hole

A well-known writer and recognised authority on witchcraft and hauntings in Britain. She participated in a BBC broadcast with [[harry-price|Harry Price]] about the Borley hauntings, and later joined [[dr-letitia-fairfield|Dr Letitia Fairfield]] and Peter Underwood for a [[ghost-club|Ghost Club]] discussion of the case on 11 May 1960.

## Ghost Club Discussion (11 May 1960)

At the Ghost Club discussion of the Borley haunting and the [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]] occupancy, Miss Hole agreed with Underwood and Tabori's view that the critics' case against [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] was "largely concerned with putting Harry Price into the dock as a sort of secondary or unconscious accomplice of Marianne — which he certainly was not." She was also the source of a note about "unaccountable organ music" reportedly heard from Avenbury church, Herefordshire — a phenomenon she cited in parallel with the [[borley-church|Borley Church]] organ incidents.

## Avenbury Church

Miss Hole informed Underwood and Tabori of "unaccountable organ music reported from Avenbury church, Herefordshire" — an instance she had investigated. Similar music has been heard in a number of other churches, and she noted it as part of the broader pattern of church-related paranormal phenomena in Britain, of which Borley is the most documented example.
