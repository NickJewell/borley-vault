# Eric G. Calcraft

Photographer of Bulmer, Essex — a village close to Borley. Calcraft served as the official photographer at the reburial of the supposed [[marie-lairre|Marie Lairre]] remains at Liston Churchyard on May 29, 1945, and was one of a small group of five people present at the ceremony.

## Role at the Reburial

On the evening of May 29, 1945, [[harry-price|Price]], [[rev-ac-henning|Rev. Henning]], Mrs Henning with their young son Stephen, the sexton, and Calcraft assembled in Liston Churchyard. The cedar casket containing the skull fragments, jaw-bone, pendants, and a brass tablet was lowered into the prepared grave at 5:42 p.m. B.D.S.T.

Calcraft photographed the proceedings, and one of his photographs (Plate XXIV in *The End of Borley Rectory*) records the ceremony. His images are the only photographic record of the reburial.

## Demolition Materials

Calcraft also told Price on the June 1945 visit that he was building a garage from Rectory materials, which included some eleven-inch oak beams — Price noted that the Rectory had been "exceptionally well built." Calcraft was watchful for any supernatural incidents connected to the salvaged materials, saying he was "waiting to see whether 'anything happens' when the garage is completed."

He also contributed photographs used in *The End of Borley Rectory*, with his credit appearing in the book's acknowledgements alongside [[david-scherman|David Scherman]] and [[harry-marshall-oxford|Harry Marshall]].
