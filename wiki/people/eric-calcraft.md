# Eric G. Calcraft

Eric G. Calcraft was a local photographer based in the Sudbury and Bulmer area of Essex — the sources refer to him both as "Eric G. Calcraft, of Sudbury" and "Mr Eric G. Calcraft, the photographer, of Bulmer" [3, 4]. He contributed significantly to the visual documentation of the latter stages of the Borley Rectory story, and his photographs are the only record of the [[reburial-marie-lairre|reburial]] ceremony.

## Role at the Reburial

Calcraft served as the official photographer at the reburial of the supposed [[marie-lairre|Marie Lairre]] remains at Liston Churchyard on 29 May 1945, and was one of a small group of five people present at the ceremony. On that evening, [[harry-price|Price]], [[rev-ac-henning|Rev. Henning]], Mrs Henning with their young son Stephen, the sexton, and Calcraft assembled in Liston Churchyard. The cedar casket containing the skull fragments, jaw-bone, pendants, and a brass tablet was lowered into the prepared grave at 5:42 p.m. B.D.S.T.

Calcraft captured two specific photographs of the proceedings:

- **The principal image** (Plate XXIV in *The End of Borley Rectory*): a widely reproduced image showing Harry Price standing alongside Mrs. Annette Henning and her young son Stephen, watching as the Rev. Henning placed the small wooden casket containing the bones into the ground [2].
- **A second, posed photograph**: showing the Rev. Henning officiating over the buried casket while Harry Price stands to the side looking reverently on [2].

His images are the only photographic record of the reburial.

## Demolition Materials

Calcraft also told Price on the June 1945 visit that he was building a garage from Rectory materials, specifically "some eleven-inch oak beams" [3]. Price noted that the Rectory had been "exceptionally well built." Calcraft was watchful for any supernatural incidents connected to the salvaged materials, saying he was "waiting to see whether 'anything happens' when the garage is completed" — wondering if the ghostly phenomena would transfer with the wood [3].

## Acknowledgements

In the preface to his 1946 book *The End of Borley Rectory*, Price explicitly thanked Calcraft alongside *Life* magazine photographer [[david-scherman|David Scherman]] and [[harry-marshall-oxford|Harry Marshall]], stating that the value of the monograph had been greatly enhanced by their "documentary — and beautiful — photographs," and offered the "highest praise and thanks for their skill" [4].
