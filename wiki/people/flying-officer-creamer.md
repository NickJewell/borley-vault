# Flying-Officer A. A. Creamer, D.F.C., LL.B.

RAF officer and civil servant; holder of the Distinguished Flying Cross and a First Class Honours Law degree from the University of London. One of the group present at the August 17–18, 1943 excavation of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]]'s cellars.

## The 1943 Cellar Excavations

Creamer was specifically invited by [[harry-price|Harry Price]] to join the excavation party searching for human remains beneath the ruined Rectory [1, 2]. He attended the first two days of the dig — **Tuesday, August 17, and Wednesday, August 18, 1943** — working alongside [[rev-ac-henning|Rev. Alfred Henning]], pathologist [[dr-eric-bailey|Dr. Eric Bailey]], historian [[georgina-dawson|Mrs. Georgina Dawson]], and the core digging team including [[jackson-labourer|Jackson]] and [[alex-english|Mrs. English]] [1–3].

Price lists Creamer, Dr. Eric Bailey, and [[roland-bailey|Roland F. Bailey]] in the same group as being present as civil servants able to provide independent corroboration of the discoveries. After the first day's digging, Creamer returned to Sudbury with the Bailey brothers.

## Significance as a Witness

His legal qualification (First Class Honours LL.B.) and his professional standing as a civil servant made him an appropriate witness to the evidential aspects of the excavation — particularly the chain of custody and the circumstances under which the [[bones-excavation|human remains]] and [[borley-medals|pendants]] were found. Price notes no specific observations by Creamer beyond his presence; his significance is primarily as an independent, professionally credentialed witness whose attendance strengthens the evidential chain of the bone discovery.
