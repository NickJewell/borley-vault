# Flying-Officer A. A. Creamer, D.F.C., LL.B.

RAF officer and civil servant; holder of the Distinguished Flying Cross and a First Class Honours Law degree from the University of London. One of the group present at the August 17–18, 1943 excavation of Borley Rectory's cellars.

Creamer attended alongside [[dr-eric-bailey|Dr. Eric Bailey]] and [[roland-bailey|Roland F. Bailey]] and the core digging team ([[rev-ac-henning|Rev. Henning]], [[jackson-labourer|Jackson]], [[alex-english|Mrs. English]]). He is listed as a civil servant.

His legal qualification (First Class Honours LL.B.) made him an appropriate witness to the evidential aspects of the excavation — particularly the chain of custody and the circumstances under which the [[bones-excavation|human remains]] and [[borley-medals|pendants]] were found. [[harry-price|Price]] lists Creamer, Dr. Eric Bailey, and Roland Bailey in the same group as being present and as civil servants able to provide independent corroboration of the discoveries.

After the first day's digging, Creamer returned to Sudbury with the Bailey brothers. Price notes no specific observations by Creamer beyond his presence; his significance is primarily as an independent, professionally credentialed witness whose presence strengthens the evidential chain of the bone discovery.
