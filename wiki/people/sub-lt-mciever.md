# Sub-Lt Alastair McIver, RN

Also spelled **MacIver** in some sources. One of [[miss-susanna-dudley|Miss Susanna Dudley]]'s companions on a visit to [[borley-church|Borley Church]] on **Sunday, 20 February 1949** [1–3]. He was accompanied by fellow Royal Navy officer [[sub-lt-crossley|Sub-Lt Jonathan Crossley, RN]] and [[miss-audrey-sawrey-cookson|Miss Audrey Sawrey-Cookson]]. The group made the excursion specifically hoping to catch a glimpse of the famous phantom "Nun," but were ultimately disappointed [3].

## The Unexplained Smell

On the path to the church, the party encountered a **"strange localised smell half way up the church path"** [1–3]. McIver was one of three who clearly detected it. Miss Dudley described the overpowering odour as being **"like an embalmed body"** [2].

The most anomalous element of the encounter was a specific sensory contradiction: while McIver and the two women clearly smelled the odour, the fourth member of the party, [[sub-lt-crossley|Sub-Lt Crossley]], smelled **nothing** — despite having a reportedly normal sense of smell both before and after the incident [2].

McIver reportedly said it was **"the most disgusting thing he had ever smelt."**

The details of this visit and the full report of the unusual smell were documented and provided to researchers by his companion, Miss Susanna Dudley [3].
