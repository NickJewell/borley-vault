# Adelaide Julia Tillard

Mother of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster]]; wife of [[rev-george-alfred-foyster|Rev. George Alfred Foyster]], Rector of All Saints', Hastings. She was the third daughter of **Philip Tillard JP** of Stukely Hall, Huntingdon (see [[philip-tillard-jp]]).

## Significance

Through his mother's Tillard family, Lionel Algernon had what Robert Wood describes as "another set of relations of considerable social standing and influence, at least some of whom would have helped him had he so requested during the period of his supposed imprisonment by his wife."

Wood uses this point to argue against the theory that Lionel was helplessly held captive by Marianne: he had wealthy, influential maternal relatives to whom he could have appealed at any time, but chose not to — consistent with Wood's thesis that Foyster was a willing accomplice rather than a passive victim.

Her name "Adelaide" appears twice in Lionel's life: his sister and his adopted daughter at Borley were both named Adelaide. Whether this reflects sentiment for his mother is not documented.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

Based on the provided Borley sources, **Adelaide Julia Tillard** is a genealogical figure in the saga, recorded as the mother of the **Reverend Lionel Algernon Foyster** (the Rector of Borley from 1930 to 1935) [1]. 

While she did not play a direct role in the hauntings, a thorough reference profile of her background provides important context regarding Lionel Foyster's upbringing and family resources:

*   **Family Background and Origins:** Adelaide Julia was the third daughter of **Philip Tillard, JP**, whose family seat was **Stukely Hall in Huntingdon** [1]. 
*   **Marriage and Children:** She married the **Rev. George Alfred Foyster**, who came from a long, established dynasty of clergymen and served as the rector of All Saints', Hastings [1, 2]. Together they had four sons (Lionel Algernon being the fourth) and two daughters. Notably, one of her daughters was also named **Adelaide** (alongside a sister named Hilda) [1].
*   **Social Standing and Lionel's "Imprisonment":** Adelaide Julia's aristocratic background provided her son Lionel with a network of relations who possessed **"considerable social standing and influence"** [1]. Borley researchers highlight this detail to underscore the tragic irony of Lionel's later life at the Rectory. Historian Robert Wood points out that these influential Tillard relatives **"would have helped him had he so requested during the period of his supposed imprisonment by his wife"** (Marianne Foyster), but he never reached out to them for rescue [1]. 
*   **Borley Namesake:** As noted in our previous conversation regarding the toddler Adelaide Foyster (born Barbara Alice Tower), Lionel Foyster deliberately chose his mother's first name, Adelaide, when renaming his newly adopted daughter.
