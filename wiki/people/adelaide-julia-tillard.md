# Adelaide Julia Tillard

Mother of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster]]; wife of [[rev-george-alfred-foyster|Rev. George Alfred Foyster]], Rector of All Saints', Hastings. She was the third daughter of **Philip Tillard JP** of Stukely Hall, Huntingdon (see [[philip-tillard-jp]]) [1].

## Family and Children

Adelaide Julia and Rev. George Alfred Foyster had four sons (Lionel Algernon being the fourth) and two daughters. Notably, one of her daughters was also named **Adelaide** (alongside a sister named [[hilda-foyster|Hilda]]) [1].

## Significance

Through his mother's Tillard family, Lionel Algernon had what Robert Wood describes as "another set of relations of considerable social standing and influence, at least some of whom would have helped him had he so requested during the period of his supposed imprisonment by his wife."

Wood uses this point to argue against the theory that Lionel was helplessly held captive by [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]]: he had wealthy, influential maternal relatives to whom he could have appealed at any time, but chose not to — consistent with Wood's thesis that Foyster was a willing accomplice rather than a passive victim.

Her name "Adelaide" appears twice in Lionel's life: his sister and his adopted daughter at Borley were both named Adelaide. Lionel Foyster deliberately chose his mother's first name, Adelaide, when renaming his newly adopted daughter (born Barbara Alice Tower — see [[adelaide-foyster|Adelaide Foyster]]). Whether this reflects sentiment for his mother is not documented.
