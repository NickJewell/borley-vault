# A.C. Cooper

Governing director of Messrs A.C. Cooper Ltd., photographers to the Royal Family; studios in the West End of London; famous for photographing Royal Academy pictures, *objets d'art*, old china. Known to [[harry-price|Price]] slightly through a publisher introduction. Had no prior interest in or knowledge of psychical research.

## The Studio Phenomena

On September 13, 1943, Price brought the Borley relics (human skull fragment, gold pendant, Miraculous Medal, other objects) to Cooper's studio for photography. That afternoon — the only afternoon in Cooper's 25-year career when such things occurred:

- **Two oil paintings fell from their easels** for no reason, while Cooper was in the studio but not near them. Neither was damaged. "The odds against two paintings normally falling off their respective easels, for no particular reason, on one afternoon, and in the same studio, were astronomical."
- **Two batches of photographic films ruined** by failing to set the lens stop — something Cooper had never done before.
- **A dark-room clock that had not worked for ten years began ticking** spontaneously while Cooper was occupied in the studio. It ran for twenty minutes, then stopped permanently and would not go again.

Cooper's greeting to Price the following morning: "Thank goodness you've come — the sooner your property is off my premises the better!" He then described the preceding afternoon.

Price carefully declines to assert a causal link between the relics and the events — he states only the facts. The relics had been in his own possession for weeks without incident. He calls the events "occurrences" rather than "phenomena" — acknowledging ambiguity.

## The Studio Destroyed

Five months later (February 23, 1944), Price brought Cooper rare pamphlets on Poltergeists to photograph for another book. He had an "intuition" that they should not remain in London that night and collected them the same day. That night, Cooper's studios were destroyed in a Blitz air raid. Cooper had taken the prints home with him that evening — he too had an intuition. Neither was in the building; no one was harmed; Cooper soon re-established his business at new premises.
