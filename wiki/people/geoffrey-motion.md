# Geoffrey H. Motion

Friend of [[harry-price]] who owned a notably large car. Motion accompanied Price on his final visit to Borley Rectory as tenant on the night of May 9, 1938, helping to collect Price's belongings from the [[base-room]].

## The Gold Wedding Ring Discovery

Motion's presence at the Rectory produced one of the most striking incidents of the entire tenancy. Price and Motion searched the [[blue-room]] half a dozen times throughout the day and evening before their midnight departure, specifically examining the floor on each occasion and finding nothing. At midnight, on their final inspection, the first thing they saw as they entered with a powerful lamp was something shining on the floor.

Motion ran and picked it up. It was a nearly new (though manufactured in 1864) woman's twenty-two-carat gold wedding ring — an object that had not been there during any of the previous searches that day. The ring was a classic [[apports|apport]]: appearing in a controlled space after repeated confirmed absence.

> **Cross-reference:** The Foyster diary records a wedding ring in the bathroom as early as March 10, 1931 — that ring disappeared next morning. Whether the two rings were the same object cannot be established.

Price made many inquiries about the ring but never found an owner. The ring was later psychometrized by [[ian-aitchison|Lieutenant Ian Aitchison]], whose reaction was immediate and violent: he dropped it and shouted "Murder!" refusing to touch it again.

## Significance

Motion's involvement is a footnote to Price's investigation but his presence at the ring discovery makes him one of the two witnesses (with Price) to what Price considered among the most striking apports in the Borley record — appearing at the precise moment of departure, after an exhaustive search, in the most-investigated room in the house.
