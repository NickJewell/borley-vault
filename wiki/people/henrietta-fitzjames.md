# Henrietta FitzJames (1670–1730)

Natural daughter of King James II and his mistress [[arabella-churchill|Arabella Churchill]]; wife of Henry, first Baron [[henry-lord-waldegrave|Waldegrave]]; mother of [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella Waldegrave]].

## Background

Henrietta was born in 1670, one of James's illegitimate children by Arabella Churchill. When James II fled England in 1688 she accompanied the exiled court to France. She married Henry Waldegrave, a staunch Catholic and loyalist peer; they had two sons and two daughters, of whom Arabella was the most historically notable.

## Espionage

Henrietta is documented to have become a spy for the British Government — working against the Stuart Pretenders she had nominally served. She was **expelled from the French Court in 1695** on this charge, sent to a convent, and subsequently escaped to England. She continued to oppose the Stuart cause and died in 1730.

## Relevance to Borley

Her documented espionage career is the basis for [[georgina-dawson|Mrs. Dawson]]'s suggestion that her daughter [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella]] similarly became a spy and may have been murdered at Borley. The argument by analogy has its limits — [[canon-phythian-adams|Canon Phythian-Adams]] rejected the Arabella theory on other grounds — but Henrietta's own history establishes that political intrigue within the Waldegrave family was real, not invented.

Her mother [[arabella-churchill|Arabella Churchill]]'s brother became John Churchill, first Duke of Marlborough — the great soldier and ancestor of [[harry-price|Winston Churchill]]'s collateral line. Price notes this connection explicitly.

Henrietta died in 1730, aged about sixty. Her mother-in-law [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella Waldegrave's grandmother]] — the elder Arabella Churchill — also died in 1730, aged about eighty.
