# Willy Palmer

A Borley village boy who, according to [[ian-greenwood|Ian Greenwood Shaw]], acted as [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s confederate in faking ghostly effects at Borley Rectory during the [[foyster-phenomena|Foyster period]] (1930–1935).

> **Note:** Not to be confused with [[johnnie-palmer|Johnnie Palmer]], the Borley Place farm worker who witnessed the 1943 excavation and maintained the bones were "awd Sows Bunz" (pig bones).

## Account

Robert Wood, drawing on Ian Shaw's testimony, writes:

> "According to Ian, who lived at Borley for a short time in early 1933, one village boy named Willy Palmer was actually Marianne's confederate in faking ghostly effects; he rigged up a collapsing shelf in the pantry which at the tug of a string deposited pots and pans on the floor with a great clatter, no doubt to Foyster's wonder." [1]

Ian also separately described finding a string dangling from ivy in the courtyard wall which, when tugged, rang some of the house bells — connected to exposed bell-wires. This may or may not have been Willy Palmer's handiwork.

## Significance

Palmer's involvement complicates the theories of sceptical investigators. While critics often suggest that [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] was solely responsible for faking the phenomena, Palmer's alleged participation demonstrates that "village boys — perhaps men and women too — played tricks at the 'haunted' Rectory" [1], creating a chaotic environment where locals were actively contributing to the physical "poltergeist" disturbances alongside the Rectory's own inhabitants. If accurate, Willy Palmer represents an external (non-household) participant in the Borley deceptions. The [[bell-ringing|bell-ringing]] was one of the phenomena most frequently cited as inexplicable by investigators; a string connecting ivy-covered bell-wires to the pantry provides a simple mechanical explanation for at least some of these incidents.

> **Contested:** Ian Shaw is the sole source for this allegation, and Wood acknowledges that his testimony "must be treated cautiously" due to his "loathing and contempt" for his mother.
