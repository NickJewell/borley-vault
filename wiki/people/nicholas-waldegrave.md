# Nicholas Waldegrave (c.1550–1621)

Second son of Sir [[sir-edward-waldegrave|Edward Waldegrave]] (died in the Tower 1561) and Frances Neville. Inherited the Borley property from his father, and from him all subsequent Borley Waldegraves are descended. He is the direct ancestor of the Waldegrave line that held the Borley Manor until the death of [[philip-waldegrave-last|Philip Waldegrave]] in 1720–21.

## Life and Holdings

Nicholas was born between 1550 and 1561 (his father died when Nicholas was an infant or child). He died on June 19, 1621, possessed of:
- The Manor of Borley Hall
- Ten acres (probably woodland) in Bulmer
- An unspecified share in the church and Manor of "Langhooo" (Langenhoo)

## Significance

Nicholas is the link between the famous Sir Edward Waldegrave of the tomb in [[borley-church|Borley Church]] and the seventeenth-century Borley Waldegraves implicated in the [[marie-lairre|Marie Lairre]] narrative. His eldest son Philip Waldegrave married twice; Philip's eldest son John had only one son Philip, listed as a recusant in 1715 and last of the direct Borley Waldegrave line.

[[georgina-dawson|Mrs. Georgina Dawson]]'s 20,000-word dossier on the Waldegrave family traced this descent and established the biographical details reproduced by [[harry-price|Price]] in Chapter XII. Nicholas is the root of the local branch of the family; the "senior" Waldegrave family (lords of the Chewton estate in Somerset) was a separate branch.
