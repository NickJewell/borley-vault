# Louis Mayerling (born George Carter, 4 September 1913)

Author of *We Faked the Ghosts of Borley Rectory* (Pen Press, 2000), claiming the entire [[borley-rectory|Borley haunting]] was "an ingeniously orchestrated hoax." Subsequent investigation exposed his claims as fiction or fabrication; on BBC Television he admitted he had "exaggerated here and there."

## Identity

**Born:** George Carter, 4 September 1913, at 63 Mayes Road, Wood Green, London. Father: James Carter (kitchen porter). Mother: Amy Carter (formerly Cook). Name changed to "Louis Mayerling" by deed poll (marriage certificate dated 1953 records the change).

His book claimed he was born in Vienna "of Amelia Romanov," placed in foster care with the Carter family, became a child prodigy violinist, knew Marilyn Monroe, George Bernard Shaw, T.E. Lawrence, and the Prince of Wales, and was a driver-companion to the Prince.

## The Book's Claims

Mayerling claimed to have spent years at Borley Rectory among the Bulls, Foysters, and Smiths, "constantly being invited to accept the place as his second home," and to have been personally responsible for most of the alleged phenomena. He also claimed correspondence with Marianne Foyster.

## Refutation

[[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]]'s review identified eight specific factual impossibilities. Alan Wesencraft (Harry Price Library, 42 years' experience) confirmed he had never encountered any mention of Mayerling or George Carter in the files of Price, Baines, Dingwall, or Hall, or from any of the many people he had interviewed. Edward Babbs: "Far from Borley Rectory being Mayerling's second home, one has to consider if he ever went there."

[[vincent-oneil|Vincent O'Neil]] (Marianne's adopted son): "I no longer believe that Mayerling knew my mother, and if he met her at all, it was only for a brief period of time. As with the other Borley-related memories from his book, the time spent with my mother happened only in his imagination."

BBC Television, December 2000 (*The Ghosts of Christmas Past*): Mayerling admitted, "I have exaggerated here and there."

## The Stewart Evans Interview (17 March 2001)

Evans conducted a "friendly and totally non-aggressive" interview. Key exchange: Mayerling said Price's book "got me interested in the first place." Price's *Most Haunted House in England* was published in 1940. Mayerling's claimed 1919 (age 6) activities at Borley therefore predate his stated source of interest by 21 years — an impossibility Evans called "condemned out of his own mouth."

Mayerling also claimed: "I started The Ghost Club with Eric Dingwall in 1938"; "I worked with Frank Whittle on the jets, on his first jet engine"; and that he went "to Singapore on a one-man submarine to help blow up the harbour along with two others, they were killed."

## Colin Wilson's Assessment

"It seems fairly clear that the book is intended either as fiction or as a hoax." (*Daily Mail*, 3 January 2001.)
