# Joseph W. Burden

Christ Church, Oxford. Official observer at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]], 15–19 December 1937; accompanied initially by [[t-stainton|T. Stainton]] and later remaining alone in the house for approximately 24 hours — one of only two observers ever to do so without a companion (the other being [[mark-kerr-pearse|Mark Kerr-Pearse]]) [3]. Burden borrowed Mr. Arbon's cat for behavioural experiments, and independently corroborated several phenomena including wailing sounds, dragging noises, and footsteps.

## The Visit with Stainton (15–17 December)

Arrived with T. Stainton; conducted routine sealing and inspection. The pair stretched a black thread across the passage between Rooms 9 and 10 as a trap [2].

**Auditory phenomena (16 December, 6:10–6:25 p.m.):**
- A "whining or hooting" from the direction of the courtyard;
- Then "the sound of shuffling footsteps coming from the direction of the scullery";
- Then at approximately 6:25, "sound of swishing garments as though someone were walking in upstairs hall."

**1:30 a.m. on 17 December:** "Continual wailing sound heard (higher and more continuous note than previous wailing) ... seemed to be coming from the [[blue-room|Blue Room]], but seemed to penetrate everywhere as it could be heard equally clearly in Base Room, hall, or kitchen passage." It stopped at 1:42 a.m. — an independently-timed 12-minute episode.

**12:10 a.m.:** "Noise heard (too loud to be mistaken) of something being dragged along floor. Two seconds duration, at two second intervals." — confirmed by both Burden and Stainton. They emphasised the noises were **"much too loud to admit of their having been mistaken"** [7].

**The cat experiment** (Stainton's watch): Borrowed [[mr-and-mrs-arbon|Arbon]]'s cat and tested its reactions in the house. The cat:
- Was frightened by moonlight on the floor;
- Rubbed her back against the "strange coat" apport as if recognising an old friend;
- Was "particularly interested in Room 7."
- She did not appear to react to the "fields of known psychic activity" in any dramatically abnormal way beyond the above.

## Solo Overnight (18–19 December)

After Stainton left, Burden spent approximately 24 hours alone in the Rectory.

At 6:10 p.m.: heard "talking and footsteps coming from direction of the main hall while sitting in Base Room." Upon investigation, he deduced the sounds must actually be coming from the Arbons' cottage — a significant acoustic discovery. Researcher [[robert-hastings|Robert Hastings]] later credited Burden with discovering that **the Rectory possessed acoustic properties that acted as an amplifier for voices originating in the stable cottage** — a natural phenomenon [[harry-price|Price]] had previously been unaware of [12].

During this period, Burden had "an uncanny sense of being watched in Room 2" — one of the few accounts of a subjective "presence" feeling in a specific named room.

He spent five minutes sitting in the dark in the Rectory Chapel, lighting two candles and hanging a small silver cross between them; he noted that the Chapel **"seemed to have evil atmosphere, but this probably imagination"** [2, 6].

At 10:35 p.m.: made his last inspection, found no new marks, and went to sleep until 8:30 a.m. Final check found nothing disturbed.

## Objectivity and Scepticism

Burden proved to be a highly objective and critical observer. Writing to Price on **21 December 1937**, he expressed doubts about the caretaker's potential involvement in faking phenomena:

> **"Lastly I would hesitate to place some of the manifestations beyond Arbin [sic] because he might easily realise that 'Phenomena appearing strong' would mean more observers and consequently more tips, not forgetting the fact that you would be more inclined to rent or buy the house if the ghosts were active."** [13, 14]

## Corroboration

When [[sg-welles|S. G. Welles]] and his party visited the Rectory in February 1938 and found a 42-inch wooden lath in a new position on the floor, they consulted Burden to verify its original placement [15, 16]. Burden explicitly remembered the lath stretched diagonally across the lead sink of the scullery pump, where he had chalked it into position, and was even able to confirm the exact placement of a slightly crumpled cigarette card two feet from the wall [16].

Appendix D lists Burden under: "Footsteps and Similar Sounds," "Dragging Noise," "Wailing Sounds," "Sensation of a Presence," "Reaction by Animals," and "Wall and Paper-writings."

> **Contradiction:** Harry Price's text states Stainton left "early on the morning of December 19" but later describes Burden retiring on the same evening and sleeping "until 8:30 a.m. [December 19]" [9] — a minor chronological inconsistency.
