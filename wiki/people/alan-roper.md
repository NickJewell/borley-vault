# Alan Roper

A member of the Ghost Club Society and its precursors for over fifty years. Deeply interested in the Borley haunting, Roper conducted research in America and elsewhere over several decades concerning many of the leading figures in the Borley drama, especially [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]. [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] and [[paul-tabori|Dr. Paul Tabori]] explicitly acknowledged their special gratitude to Roper in the introduction to their 1973 book, *The Ghosts of Borley*, thanking him for his "much original research in respect of some of the chief actors in the Borley drama" [2].

## Tracing Adelaide Foyster

Roper's most significant contribution to the Borley story was succeeding in tracing [[adelaide-foyster|Adelaide Foyster]] — [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] and [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s adopted daughter, born Barbara Alice Tower on 20 March 1928 — and reuniting her with her true biological relatives in America [1, 3, 4]. Adelaide had been left in a convent in Braintree when Marianne moved to the USA after the war, and had subsequently been adopted under a different name. Roper traveled to the United States where he personally spoke with [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] [3]. His research confirmed that Adelaide's parents ([[seward-tower|Seward Tower]] and [[alice-tower|Alice]]) had died, and that she had surviving relatives.

## Research Contributions

Roper discovered innumerable birth and death certificates of considerable interest, conducting what the BRC describes as "unrivalled research into the whole Borley story" [1]. Extracts of his research were published at one time on [[vincent-oneil|Vincent O'Neil]]'s Borley Rectory website [1].

He also established the existence of [[donald-vial|Donald Frederick Pratt Vial]], who apparently lodged at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] towards the end of the Foyster incumbency around 1934 — a previously unknown figure who is not mentioned in any Borley book.

### Exposing the Louis Mayerling Hoax

When a man calling himself "[[louis-mayerling|Louis Mayerling]]" published the sensational book *[[we-faked-the-ghosts-of-borley-rectory|We Faked the Ghosts of Borley Rectory]]* — claiming to have lived at the Rectory since 1913 and to have faked all the phenomena — Roper was instrumental in proving it was a literary hoax. He unearthed the author's true birth certificate, proving he was actually born George Carter on September 4, 1913, at 63 Mayes Road, Wood Green, to James Carter (a kitchen porter) and Amy Carter [5]. Roper also found a 1953 marriage certificate for "Louis Mayerling," noting that the name had been changed by Deed Poll — completely destroying the author's claims of an aristocratic, haunted Borley childhood [5].

## On-Site Investigations at Borley

Roper also participated in active field research at Borley, taking part in night vigils alongside fellow Ghost Club members Tony and [[georgina-dawson|Georgina]] Broughall and Charles Doerrer:

- **May 30–31, 1974:** The group conducted an all-night vigil at [[borley-church|Borley Church]]. Temperature readings were taken, but nothing unusual was recorded [6].
- **May 28–29, 1976:** The same four investigators carried out another night vigil in the church. Their tape recorder picked up no unusual sounds. However, during this visit, they successfully duplicated all of the allegedly paranormal noises that had been famously recorded in the church a few years earlier by [[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth|Geoffrey Croom-Hollingsworth]] and his associates, demonstrating that the sounds could be produced by normal means [7].
