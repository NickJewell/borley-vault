# Alan Roper

A Member of the Ghost Club Society and its precursors for over fifty years. Deeply interested in the Borley haunting, Roper conducted research in America and elsewhere over several decades concerning many of the leading figures in the Borley drama, especially [[marianne-foyster]].

## Tracing Adelaide Foyster

Roper's most significant contribution to the Borley story was succeeding in tracing [[adelaide-foyster]] — [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] and [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s adopted daughter, born Barbara Alice Tower on 20 March 1928 — and reuniting her with her true relatives in America. Adelaide had been left in a convent in Braintree when Marianne moved to the USA after the war, and had subsequently been adopted under a different name. Roper's research confirmed that Adelaide's parents (Seward Tower and Alice) had died, and that she had surviving relatives.

## Research Contributions

Roper discovered innumerable birth and death certificates of considerable interest, conducting what the BRC describes as "unrivalled research into the whole Borley story." Extracts of his research were published at one time on [[vincent-oneil]]'s Borley Rectory website.

He also established the existence of [[donald-vial|Donald Frederick Pratt Vial]], who apparently lodged at [[borley-rectory]] towards the end of the [[foyster-incumbency]] around 1934 — a previously unknown figure who is not mentioned in any Borley book.
