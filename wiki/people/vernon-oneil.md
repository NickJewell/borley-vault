# Vernon O'Neil

The name "Vernon O'Neil" is an inaccurate designation used in Robert Wood's *The Widow of Borley* to refer to **Robert Vincent O'Neil Jr.**, who was better known to Borley researchers as **[[vincent-oneil|Vincent O'Neil]]** (or "Vince"), the adopted son of [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] and her fourth husband [[robert-oneil|Robert Vincent O'Neil]] [1, 2].

As of 1972, when *Ghosts of Borley* was published, "Vernon O'Neil" was described as "a member of a religious sect in America" — a reference that almost certainly reflects the fact that Vincent had been sent as a teenager to live with Mormon friends in Utah, where he eventually married and started a family. It is this move to Utah that prompted Wood to assert he was now a member of a religious sect [1].

His existence is noted here primarily as part of the biographical record of [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s post-Borley life. For the full account of his life, identity, research, and publications, see [[vincent-oneil|Vincent O'Neil]].
