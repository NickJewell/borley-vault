# Dr. H. F. Bellamy

**Dr. Henry F. Bellamy** was an individual engaged in medical research who acted as one of [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s "official observers" during the year-long tenancy of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] from 1937 to 1938 [1]. He frequently investigated the site alongside his son, [[fc-bellamy|Francis C. Bellamy]], and was considered by Price's chief associate, [[sidney-glanville|Sidney Glanville]], to be a highly reliable witness [1, 2]. Bellamy participated in several important séances and is notable for introducing his own Planchette board to one of the sessions.

## The Electrical Contact-Breaker Experiment (October 1937)

Dr. Bellamy is most famous in the Borley annals for a "Harry Price type" electrical experiment he rigged up during a vigil on the night of **16–17 October 1937**, accompanied by his son and Mr. [[hugh-fee-smith|Hugh E. Fee-Smith]] [3, 4]. Dr. Bellamy placed a simple electric bell contact-breaker on the dining-room mantelpiece, holding the metal contacts apart with a pile of books; if the books were displaced, the circuit would close and ring the bell [1]. At 12:50 a.m., while the party was quietly watching the moonlight in the upstairs [[blue-room|Blue Room]], the bell suddenly started ringing and continued for about a minute before abruptly stopping [5]. Upon rushing to the dining room, they discovered that **every book had been displaced from its original position**, and the bottom book was pushed completely off the contact-breaker [5].

> **Contradiction:** In the highly sceptical [[the-borley-report|*Borley Report*]], investigators [[eric-dingwall|Dingwall]], [[kathleen-goldney|Goldney]], and [[trevor-hall|Hall]] pointed out that **a cat had been found to have entered the house the evening before** the disturbance [6]. The critics argued the cat could easily have knocked over the books, noting that the door to the dining room had been left unlocked [6]. Furthermore, [[harry-price|Harry Price]] suspiciously **omitted all mention of the cat** when he published the account in *The Most Haunted House in England* [6]. However, Dr. Bellamy and his son "rejected totally this explanation," maintaining that the window was sealed, the house regularly patrolled, and that they had tested the delicate apparatus at 6:00 a.m. and found it in perfect working order [4, 5, 7].

## Other Rectory Vigils and Paranormal Experiences

- **The Swinging Blind Incident (20–21 November 1937):** While sitting with [[sidney-glanville|Sidney Glanville]] and [[capt-hg-harrison|Captain H. G. Harrison]], the men observed the edges of a lowered blind waving with an irregular, palpitating action for about five minutes. They unanimously concluded there was no draft to account for it [2, 8].
- **Tactile Phenomena and Wall Writings (26–27 May 1938):** During his final visit with his son and Fee-Smith, Dr. Bellamy helped locate a fresh pencil line in Room No. 7. During this same visit, Fee-Smith reported the distinct sensation of unseen hands lightly ruffling his hair and touching his face in the dark near the garden door [9, 10].
- He also participated in uneventful visits, including one on 3–4 December 1937 while house painters were on-site [11], and another on 12–13 January 1938 alongside the former rector, the [[rev-eric-smith|Rev. Guy E. Smith]], during which they conducted a fruitless planchette experiment [12, 13].

## Séance Participation

Bellamy took part in three séances specifically documented in the [[locked-book|Locked Book]]:

**20–21 November 1937 (Borley Rectory):** Bellamy sat alongside [[capt-hg-harrison|Captain H. G. Harrison]], [[sidney-glanville|S. H. Glanville]], and [[roger-glanville|R. H. Glanville]] at a night séance at the Rectory itself. Notably, at this session **two Planchette boards were used successively**, as Bellamy brought his own — Price remarks "it made no difference, apparently." Some of the answers at this séance were written in "mirror-writing" — backward — which Price notes as "not at all an easy way to write." The session confirmed that the dining-room window was bricked up on account of the nun, and that "[[marie-lairre|Marie Lairre]]" was responsible for the lighted schoolroom window.

**14 December 1937 (Lancaster Gate, London W.2):** This séance was hosted at Bellamy's own home, attended by Bellamy and his wife, [[sidney-glanville|S. H. Glanville]], and [[roger-glanville|Roger Glanville]] [14, 15]. The "Joseph Glanvill" entity communicated and gave the specific instruction to **"look under the brick floor in the cellar"** at Borley — directly predicting the 1943 excavation — specifying "Well" as the location and writing the letters "P-E-N," later interpreted as referring to the *pendants* found there [14, 16]. This séance directly influenced Price's decision to excavate the Borley cellars, where bone fragments and two French medallions were ultimately discovered [15].

**12–13 January 1938 (Borley Rectory):** Bellamy also attended a night séance at the Rectory with the Rev. and Mrs [[rev-eric-smith|G. Eric Smith]] and the Glanvilles. A "Roman centurion" entity communicated, and the "Harry Bull" entity was questioned about the origin of the skull found by Mrs Smith in the library cupboard.

## Significance

Bellamy's hosting of the December 14, 1937 séance places him at a session that produced the most specific directional instruction in the entire Planchette record — the instruction to search under the cellar floor, near the well, at Borley. That instruction was not acted upon until 1943, and by that point the scripts that produced it had been locked away unread for five years.
