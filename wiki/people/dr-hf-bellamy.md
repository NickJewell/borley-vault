# Dr. H. F. Bellamy

Psychical researcher and one of [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s collaborators during the 1937–38 Borley tenancy. Bellamy participated in several important séances and is notable for introducing his own Planchette board to one of the sessions.

## Séance Participation

Bellamy took part in two séances specifically documented in the [[locked-book|Locked Book]]:

**November 20–21, 1937 (Borley Rectory):** Bellamy sat alongside [[capt-hg-harrison|Captain H. G. Harrison]], [[sidney-glanville|S. H. Glanville]], and [[roger-glanville|R. H. Glanville]] at a night séance at the Rectory itself. Notably, at this session **two Planchette boards were used successively**, as Bellamy brought his own — Price remarks "it made no difference, apparently." Some of the answers at this séance were written in "mirror-writing" — backward — which Price notes as "not at all an easy way to write." The session confirmed that the dining-room window was bricked up on account of the nun, and that "Marie Lairre" was responsible for the lighted schoolroom window.

**December 14, 1937 (Lancaster Gate, London W.2):** This séance was hosted at Bellamy's own home, attended by Bellamy and his wife, [[sidney-glanville|S. H. Glanville]], and [[roger-glanville|Roger Glanville]]. The "Joseph Glanvill" entity communicated and gave the specific instruction to **"look under the brick floor in the cellar"** at Borley — directly predicting the 1943 excavation — specifying "Well" as the location and writing the letters "P-E-N," later interpreted as referring to the *pendants* found there. This was one of the most specific and later-verified predictions in the séance record.

**January 12–13, 1938 (Borley Rectory):** Bellamy also attended a night séance at the Rectory with the Rev. and Mrs [[rev-eric-smith|G. Eric Smith]] and the Glanvilles. A "Roman centurion" entity communicated, and the "Harry Bull" entity was questioned about the origin of the skull found by Mrs Smith in the library cupboard.

## Significance

Bellamy's hosting of the December 14, 1937 séance places him at a session that produced the most specific directional instruction in the entire Planchette record — the instruction to search under the cellar floor, near the well, at Borley. That instruction was not acted upon until 1943, and by that point the scripts that produced it had been locked away unread for five years.
