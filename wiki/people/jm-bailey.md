# J. M. Bailey

Oxford undergraduate. Official observer at Borley Rectory, 18–24 July 1937, with co-observer [[cv-wintour|C. V. Wintour]]. During their visit several new pencil marks appeared under conditions they found difficult to attribute to any normal cause — including one that "both Bailey and Wintour thought they saw grow in front of their eyes."

## The Visit

Arrived at 9 p.m. on 18 July with Wintour. The Rev. [[rev-ac-henning|Henning]] and [[mark-kerr-pearse]] also arrived that evening, and the group attempted an informal table-rapping séance without significant result.

**New pencil marks appearing during their visit (Appendix C):**

- After returning from Long Melford on the evening of 22 July, Bailey found "a mark like the beginning of an 'M'" in Room 3 — which, on closer inspection, "both Bailey and Wintour thought they saw grow in front of their eyes. Both are now inclined to think it was an optical illusion."
- On 19 July, found a "deep scratch or incision" — a new mark in the form of an arrow shaft and head — on a wall that they had closely inspected the previous day.
- At 11:23 on 20 July, discovered "three circles" had been added behind a door in Room 8: "We are both certain that these were not there at 11:15."
- Later confirmed new marks found by [[sj-de-lotbiniere|de Lotbinière]] and Price during the 21 July visit.

On the afternoon of **21 July 1937**, at 4:00 p.m., Harry Price arrived at the Rectory accompanied by de Lotbinière and H. Douglas-Home, finding Bailey and Wintour "still in possession." Bailey and his companion left an hour later and "slept out" for the evening [1]. During his time at Borley, Bailey also reportedly slept overnight in the Rectory's large summer-house, where he reported being completely undisturbed [3].

On **23 July**, Bailey and Wintour carried out the usual inspections but noted no new pencillings and experienced no other phenomena [3]. That same evening, Bailey slept in the notoriously haunted [[blue-room|Blue Room]] and "neither saw nor heard anything unusual" [3].

## Departure Contradiction

> **Contradiction:** Price's detailed text records that Bailey and Wintour **"left for home at 10:15 a.m. on July 24"** [3], while the compiled Borley chronology explicitly states: **"23 July – Mr J.M. Bailey and Mr C.W. Wintour leave at 10:15 a.m."** [2] — a discrepancy of one day.

## Role in Price's Analysis

Price cites Bailey and Wintour in Chapter 24 (*The Marianne Messages*) as examples of independent witnesses to the appearance of new pencil marks. Their "thought they saw it grow" observation is quoted as illustrative of the phenomenon, with the important caveat that both subsequently considered the possibility of optical illusion.

Appendix D lists Bailey under "Wall and Paper-writings" and "The Light in the Window."
