# J. M. Bailey

Oxford undergraduate. Official observer at Borley Rectory, July 18–24, 1937, with co-observer [[cv-wintour|C. V. Wintour]]. During their visit several new pencil marks appeared under conditions they found difficult to attribute to any normal cause — including one that "both Bailey and Wintour thought they saw grow in front of their eyes."

## The Visit

Arrived at 9 p.m. on July 18 with Wintour. The Rev. [[rev-ac-henning|Henning]] and [[mark-kerr-pearse]] also arrived that evening, and the group attempted an informal table-rapping séance without significant result.

**New pencil marks appearing during their visit (Appendix C):**

- After returning from Long Melford on the evening of July 22, Bailey found "a mark like the beginning of an 'M'" in Room 3 — which, on closer inspection, "both Bailey and Wintour thought they saw grow in front of their eyes. Both are now inclined to think it was an optical illusion."
- On July 19, found a "deep scratch or incision" — a new mark in the form of an arrow shaft and head — on a wall that they had closely inspected the previous day.
- At 11:23 on July 20, discovered "three circles" had been added behind a door in Room 8: "We are both certain that these were not there at 11:15."
- Later confirmed new marks found by [[sj-de-lotbiniere|de Lotbinière]] and Price during the July 21 visit.

Bailey slept in the Blue Room on his last night alone, undisturbed.

## Role in Price's Analysis

Price cites Bailey and Wintour in Chapter 24 (*The Marianne Messages*) as examples of independent witnesses to the appearance of new pencil marks. Their "thought they saw it grow" observation is quoted as illustrative of the phenomenon, with the important caveat that both subsequently considered the possibility of optical illusion.

Appendix D lists Bailey under "Wall and Paper-writings" and "The Light in the Window."

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**J. M. Bailey** appears in the Borley Rectory sources as an undergraduate student who served as one of Harry Price’s "official observers" during the 1937 tenancy investigation [1]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific details, events, and contradictions regarding his involvement:

**The July 1937 Vigil**
Bailey conducted a multi-day observation at the Rectory in July 1937 alongside fellow undergraduate C. V. Wintour (also referred to as C. W. Wintour in the chronology) [1, 2]. 
*   On the afternoon of **July 21, 1937**, at 4:00 p.m., Harry Price arrived at the Rectory accompanied by S.J. de Lotbiniere and H. Douglas-Home, finding Bailey and Wintour "still in possession" of the house [1]. Bailey and his companion left an hour later and "slept out" for the evening [1]. 
*   During his time at Borley, Bailey is recorded as sleeping overnight in the Rectory's famous summer-house, where he reported being completely undisturbed [3]. 
*   During the day of **July 23**, Bailey and Wintour carried out the usual inspections of the property, but noted no new pencilings on the walls and experienced no other phenomena [3].
*   On the evening of **July 23**, Bailey slept in the notoriously haunted Blue Room, but reported that he "neither saw nor heard anything unusual" [3].

**Departure Contradiction**
The sources contain a direct contradiction regarding the exact date Bailey and Wintour concluded their investigation. 
*   Harry Price's detailed text records that Bailey slept in the Blue Room on the night of July 23 and that the two men **"left for home at 10:15 a.m. on July 24"** [3].
*   However, the compiled Borley Chronology explicitly lists their departure a day earlier, stating: **"23 July – Mr J.M. Bailey and Mr C.W. Wintour leave at 10:15 a.m."** [2].
