# Francis Cowley Whitehouse

Brother of [[lady-whitehouse|Sir George Whitehouse]]; father of **Leslie Edwin Whitehouse**, who became [[dom-richard-whitehouse|Dom Richard Whitehouse]] — the Benedictine monk and key Foyster-period witness at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]].

## Background

Francis Cowley Whitehouse served as **canon to the British Embassy in Istanbul** before the First World War. He married the daughter of J. W. Whittall, who headed a successful trading empire exporting mohair, hazelnuts, barley, maize, seeds, and opium from Turkey. Their son Leslie Edwin Whitehouse was born in Istanbul in 1899 — a fact that connects the Whitehouse family to the Ottoman world and explains Edwin's multilingual, cosmopolitan outlook.

## Significance

Francis Cowley Whitehouse is the biographical link between the prominent local family of [[lady-whitehouse|Sir George and Lady Whitehouse]] of Arthur Hall (key Foyster-period witnesses) and the monk [[dom-richard-whitehouse|Dom Richard Whitehouse]] (also known as Edwin, Leslie Edwin Whitehouse) who made approximately thirty visits to the Rectory during the Foyster period and became one of its most important witnesses.

It was Francis's wife's connection with the Whittall family trading empire that gave Edwin his financial independence and his international background, and probably contributed to his psychological fragility — the shell shock, the broken romance, and the nervous breakdown that preceded his arrival at Borley.
