# Dr Albert von Schrenck-Notzing

Munich-based psychical researcher and the primary European promoter of the [[willi-schneider|Schneider]] brothers; he invited [[harry-price|Harry Price]] and [[eric-dingwall|Eric Dingwall]] to Munich in 1922 to witness [[willi-schneider|Willi Schneider]], and his death in February 1929 triggered Price's takeover of [[rudi-schneider|Rudi Schneider]]'s career.

## Background

Schrenck-Notzing was a wealthy, well-connected Munich physician with a private income who devoted considerable resources to psychical research. He heard sensational reports about the young [[willi-schneider|Willi Schneider]]'s abilities — telekinesis and materialisation — and persuaded Willi's father to allow him to take the boy under his wing, having him trained as a dentist in Munich while conducting dozens of sittings.

He promoted Rudi Schneider in the same way, financially supporting his career from the mid-1920s.

## The Munich Invitation

In May 1922, Schrenck-Notzing invited Price and Dingwall to Munich to observe Willi. Price was impressed enough to declare Willi genuine, producing one of the key moments in his public career — the so-called "conversion of Harry Price." Dingwall was less enthusiastic.

## Death and Aftermath

Schrenck-Notzing died in **February 1929**. Immediately, Price travelled to Munich to secure the twenty-year-old Rudi's services for London. Price effectively took over Schrenck-Notzing's role as Rudi's patron and promoter. The transition from Munich-based European research to London tabloid celebrity — "the Austrian psychical superman" — was entirely Price's creation and would not have been possible without Schrenck-Notzing's earlier groundwork.

> O'Connor notes a melancholy irony: the Schneider brothers' hometown of Braunau had, by this period, become famous not for a celebrated medium but as the birthplace of Adolf Hitler.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Dr. Albert von Schrenck-Notzing** (also referred to in the texts as **Baron von Schrenck-Notzing**) was a prominent investigator of physical mediumship based in Munich, Germany, and was recognized by Harry Price as one of the key personalities in the international psychical research community during the 1920s [1]. He also served as an **Honorary Vice-President of Harry Price's National Laboratory of Psychical Research** [2].

A thorough reference profile based on the Borley sources includes the following details:

**Patronage of the Schneider Brothers**
Schrenck-Notzing played a crucial role in discovering and promoting the famous Austrian physical mediums, Willi and Rudi Schneider. After hearing sensational stories in the local press about nineteen-year-old Willi's telekinetic and materialization abilities, Schrenck-Notzing visited the Schneider family home in Braunau, Austria [3]. He **persuaded Willi’s father to let him take the boy under his wing, bringing him to Munich to train as a dentist** [3]. Schrenck-Notzing hosted dozens of experimental séances with Willi in his Munich apartment [3, 4]. 

When Willi's powers began to fade and his younger brother Rudi emerged as a medium, Schrenck-Notzing promoted Rudi's skills in Munich and provided him with financial support [5, 6].

**Influence on Harry Price's Career**
Schrenck-Notzing inadvertently altered the course of British psychical research by inviting Harry Price and Eric J. Dingwall to Munich in May 1922 to observe Willi Schneider's abilities under controlled conditions [3, 7]. This visit was Price's true introduction to the serious study of physical mediumship [8]. 

The authors of the highly critical *Borley Report* noted that this experience set Price's life course, observing that **his thoughts turned towards "making himself in England what he thought Schrenck-Notzing was in Germany"** [8]. While Price ultimately went far beyond the German investigator's aims by establishing his own National Laboratory and becoming a prolific psychic journalist, Schrenck-Notzing was the original template for his ambitions [8]. 

**Death and Aftermath**
Dr. von Schrenck-Notzing died in **February 1929** [6]. His death had an immediate ripple effect on the psychical research community. Because he had been financially supporting Rudi Schneider, his death left the young medium without a patron [6]. **Harry Price immediately traveled to Munich to secure the twenty-year-old's services for sittings in London**, effectively taking over Schneider's career and turning him into an international media celebrity [6].
