# Richard Lee-Van den Daele

Qualified librarian from Shipley, West Yorkshire, and a dedicated researcher, archivist, and authority on the Borley Rectory case. Richard Lee's interest in Borley began in the early 1970s when an annual containing an article on the haunting and photographs of a dilapidated piggery — mistakenly claimed as the remains of [[borley-rectory]] — caught his imagination. Using his local library in Yorkshire, he proved adept at tracking down out-of-print books by [[harry-price|Harry Price]], [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]], and various SPR publications.

## Defence of Harry Price

Unlike many late-twentieth-century commentators who relied on the highly critical 1956 SPR *Borley Report*, Lee-Van den Daele was a staunch defender of Price. He provided the following assessment:

> *"In my opinion, Harry Price's years of painstaking work on the haunting of Borley Rectory was undone in a frenzied and shameful free-for-all following his untimely death in 1948."*

He characterised critics as having been "too unsure of their facts to tackle Price whilst he was still in a position to sue them," concluding that "perhaps Price's most ardent supporter over the years has turned out to be Borley itself."

## Tracking the Gregsons

Trying to shed light on the truth behind the Rectory fire, Lee-Van den Daele wrote to government departments at home and abroad to track down the sons of the late [[captain-gregson|Captain Gregson]]. Despite receiving no initial response, his dogged letter-writing eventually paid off: both Gregson sons were tracked down at either end of the globe.

[[alan-gregson|Alan Gregson]]'s letters from Australia stated that the fire was accidental. [[anthony-gregson|Anthony Gregson]] told Lee-Van den Daele quite bluntly that his father bought the rectory as a real estate venture to capitalise on its haunted reputation, and when that failed, "torched the place for the insurance money." Both accounts were included in [[ivan-banks]]' *The Enigma of Borley Rectory* (1996).

Lee-Van den Daele also discovered that Captain Gregson, while living at Maldon, Essex, had been an area organiser for the British Union of Fascists, Oswald Mosley's Blackshirts.

## Site Investigations

Visits to the Rectory site made with his brother Sean convinced the pair that there was still much occurring that could not be explained away. During visits to Borley in July and August 1984, Sean and his group slept in the church porch and recorded anomalous "sibilant whisperings" on studio-quality tape equipment — recordings that left both brothers convinced that unexplained phenomena were still occurring at the site.

## Harry Price Research

Lee-Van den Daele came into contact with the late [[ivan-banks]], [[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth]], [[ah-wesencraft|Alan Wesencraft]], and [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]], with whom he engaged in lengthy correspondence. He also wrote his degree course thesis on the works of Price, aided by research trips to the [[national-laboratory|Harry Price Library]] at the University of London. In a letter dated November 1984, [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]] dismissively told him that in his view "the case is closed" and that it was difficult to see what more could usefully be written about it.

## Gate Posts

Lee-Van den Daele discovered in 1984 that the last surviving original driveway gate posts from [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] were in the garden of the final bungalow built on the site. He corresponded with [[ivan-banks]] who travelled to Borley and bought the posts for £20, eventually giving them a home in his garden in Maidenhead.

## The Kate Boreham Mystery

During a visit to the Borley churchyard, Lee-Van den Daele noted a highly suspicious anomaly regarding the locally famous [[kate-boreham|Kate Boreham]] legend: he was completely unable to find her grave anywhere in the churchyard, despite her death certificate claiming she was buried there.

## Borleyrectory.com

Lee-Van den Daele contributed extensively to the website formerly run by [[vincent-oneil]], and was a member of O'Neil's Internet-based Borley Ghost Society.
