# Major Forbes-Mitchell

A Government Survey Office official based in **St John, New Brunswick**. He became involved with [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] ca. 1929 while [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] was in England seeking a new living, and was deceived by Marianne's false identity.

## The Incident

When Marianne was involved in a minor car collision with Forbes-Mitchell, she introduced herself as **"Mrs von Kiergraff"** — one of her recurring aristocratic personae. With Foyster away in England, she was able to pursue the acquaintance, visiting the Major's house and accepting expensive presents.

The deception was exposed by her son [[ian-greenwood|Ian Shaw]]: when he smeared gun-oil on his jacket while inspecting the Major's gun collection, Forbes-Mitchell had the jacket cleaned and discovered a letter in one of Ian's pockets bearing his real name and address. Suspicious, he subsequently asked Ian what school he attended, then telephoned the school to confirm who Ian and Marianne really were.

He then complained to the police. The acquaintance ended with the return of presents and "a certain amount of scandal."

## Significance

The Forbes-Mitchell episode is one of [[ian-greenwood|Ian Shaw]]'s uncorroborated stories, but Wood considers it "plausible when her activities in England, for which documentary evidence exists, are considered." It illustrates Marianne's established pattern of approaching men under a false identity, extracting gifts or favours, and retreating when exposed. Her use of "Mrs von Kiergraff" here anticipates the "Countess Sarah von Kiergraff" fantasy she would elaborate for the Fentons in Wimbledon in 1933–34.
