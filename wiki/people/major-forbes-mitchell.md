# Major Forbes-Mitchell

An official attached to the **Government Survey Office in St John, New Brunswick**, Canada. He had no direct involvement with the haunting of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] itself, but is recorded in the Borley sources as the unwitting victim of one of [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s elaborate impostures during her life in Canada, prior to her arrival at Borley [1].

## The Incident

While [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] was in England seeking a new living, Marianne and her son [[ian-greenwood|Ian Shaw]] were involved in a minor car collision with a vehicle driven by Major Forbes-Mitchell [1]. Marianne introduced herself to him using one of her favourite aristocratic aliases: **"Mrs von Kiergraff"** [1]. Taking advantage of her husband's absence, she cultivated the acquaintance, visiting the Major's house and accepting **"expensive presents"** from him [1].

The deception was exposed through an accidental discovery involving Ian. While inspecting Forbes-Mitchell's gun collection, Ian smeared gun-oil on his jacket. The Major gallantly insisted on having the jacket cleaned, but while emptying the pockets he found a letter bearing Ian's actual name and true address [1]. His suspicions aroused, he asked Ian which school he attended, then telephoned the school and successfully established the real identities of both Ian and his mother [1].

Forbes-Mitchell subsequently complained to the police. The acquaintance ended with the return of the expensive presents and "a certain amount of scandal" [1].

## Significance

The Forbes-Mitchell episode is one of [[ian-greenwood|Ian Shaw]]'s uncorroborated stories, but Wood considers it "plausible when her activities in England, for which documentary evidence exists, are considered." It illustrates [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]]'s established pattern of approaching men under a false identity, extracting gifts or favours, and retreating when exposed. Her use of "Mrs von Kiergraff" here anticipates the "Countess Sarah von Kiergraff" fantasy she would elaborate for the [[mr-and-mrs-fenton|Fentons]] in Wimbledon in 1933–34.
