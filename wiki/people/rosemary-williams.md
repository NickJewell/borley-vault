# Miss Rosemary M. Williams

Resident of Borley Lodge, a property near the Rectory. On the night of Sunday, March 26, 1939 — approximately one month after the Rectory fire of February 27–28 — Williams and [[cg-browne|Mr. C. G. Browne]] of Pound Hall, Long Melford, were among a group examining the ruins by moonlight. She is the primary witness to one of the most discussed post-fire apparition accounts.

## The Blue Room Apparition

Standing on the [[nuns-walk|Nun's Walk]], Williams and Browne both observed a figure at what had been the [[blue-room|Blue Room]] window. Quoting from Williams's own report:

> *"I had the impression of someone in a light buff-coloured coat, but as the figure approached the window, I could see it was a woman clothed in blue. She remained for several seconds and then turned towards the wall and, as it seemed, walked through it."*

[[cg-browne|Mr. Browne]] confirmed her statement in full.

## Physical Impossibility

[[harry-price|Price]] makes the physical argument explicit: the floor of the [[blue-room|Blue Room]] had been destroyed in the fire. Anyone standing at that window would have needed to stand on either nothing or charred and rotten rafters. [[captain-gregson|Captain Gregson]] had placed "Keep Out" signs on the ruins due to their dangerous condition. In Price's assessment, no human being could have climbed up to that window under the existing conditions, let alone done so unseen from the Nun's Walk below.

The figure was clothed in blue — consistent with numerous earlier descriptions of the Borley Nun, who was sometimes described as wearing blue or dark garments.

## Significance

Williams's account was submitted in writing to Price and is reproduced verbatim. As the principal tenant of Borley Lodge — a property whose name reflects its long local association with the Rectory's grounds — she had both local knowledge and a direct view of the ruins. Her simultaneous sighting with [[cg-browne|Browne]], in moonlight with two witnesses, under conditions that preclude a living interloper, makes this one of the strongest post-fire apparition accounts in the record.
