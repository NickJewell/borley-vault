# Lieutenant G.B. Nawrocki

**Dr. G.B. Nawrocki** (sometimes referred to as Lt. Nawrocki) was a doctor of medicine attached to the **Polish Army Medical Corps**, temporarily stationed in Britain during the Second World War, residing at 45 William Street, Kirkcaldy, Fife [1]. Having taken a long interest in psychical research and read most of [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s books, he contacted Price on **August 1, 1943**, after reading Price's 1943 article on Borley in *Everybody's Weekly*, to share detailed minute-by-minute protocols of his paranormal experiences at the ruined [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] [1, 3].

## First Night (June 28–29, 1943)

Nawrocki visited the Rectory ruins alongside his commanding officer, [[col-wroblewski|Col. J. Wroblewski]], [[lt-kujawa|Lieut. W. Kujawa]], and [[lt-ligaszewski|Second Lieut. A. Ligaszewski]] [4].

- **8:10 p.m.:** A stone was thrown at Nawrocki in the kitchen passage on entering [4].
- **8:30 p.m.:** More stones thrown at him in the bathroom passage and the Chapel [5].
- **9:05 p.m.:** Kitchen door (shut when they ascended to the first floor) opens and closes again with a great noise; no one was on the ground floor.
- **9:10 p.m.:** Party built seats and an improvised table from bricks and a half-burnt drawer in the dining-room.
- **11:05 p.m.:** Nawrocki observed a **"black shadow moving slowly between the trees on the Nun's Walk"**, which vanished near the wall [6, 7].
- **2:40 a.m.:** Half a brick thrown from the ceiling, falling directly in the centre of the makeshift table — immediately after Kujawa jokingly said "a brick could easily be thrown from the ceiling upon our heads."
- **4:30 a.m.:** Nawrocki reported hearing **"many whisperings"** in the kitchen passage, though he could not distinguish any specific words [7, 8].

## Second Night (July 28–29, 1943)

Nawrocki returned a month later with Lieut. Kujawa and "two English boys" [9]. The visit fell on the traditional "nun's anniversary" (July 28), though Nawrocki was reportedly unaware of this tradition.

> **Contradiction:** One source timeline mistakenly dates this second visit to 1944 [10].

- **8:30 p.m.:** Two stones were thrown at Nawrocki in the Blue Room [9].
- The party improvised by **rebuilding the floor of the ruined Blue Room**, laying planks across the burnt rafters and bringing in straw for a night vigil [9, 11].
- **11:25 p.m.:** Nawrocki again saw the shadow figure on the Nun's Walk — stopping and vanishing in the same location as the first visit [12].
- **00:30 a.m.:** After the English boys reported seeing a shadowy figure, Nawrocki investigated and saw **"a black shape or a shadow of a man silhouetted on the wall of the chapel,"** standing motionless for up to twenty seconds before vanishing slowly [12].
- **2:35 a.m. and 3:30 a.m.:** Dull thumps from the ground floor.

In a covering letter sent to Price on August 8, 1943, Nawrocki stated definitively: **"I am quite sure that I twice saw the shadow on the Nun's Walk, and once a shadow of a man in Room No. 3"** [13]. He explicitly asked Price whether these apparitions might have been the phantasms of the legendary ghost nun and the late [[harry-bull|Rev. Harry Bull]] [13].

## SPR Assessment: A Probable Hoax

The 1956 SPR Report regards the Polish account as "so open to suspicion that it is difficult to resist the impression that it was a hoax of some kind," bluntly stating, **"Frankly, we cannot take this account seriously"** [14, 15]:

- Stone-throwing had previously occurred at Borley only when [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] or [[harry-price|Harry Price]] was present — never during the official observer period (1937–38) and not during the Cambridge Commission visits.
- The Cambridge Commission made visits on 22 June and 16–17 July 1943 — immediately bracketing the Polish visits on 28 June and 28 July — and "produced no results of any kind at all."
- The party allegedly "rebuilt the floor of the Blue Room in the space of an hour," yet Price had written in 1939 that the Blue Room was entirely inaccessible: "No human being could have climbed up to that window... there was nothing to stand on."
- The Harry Bull apparition had been seen only by Marianne Foyster during the entire history of the case; Price himself wrote "I doubt whether the ghost of Harry Bull has ever been seen at the Rectory."
- The "whisperings" in the kitchen passage were not reported during the entire official observer period (confirmed by [[sidney-glanville|Sidney Glanville]]).
- [[robert-fordyce-aickman|Robert Fordyce Aickman]], visiting the ruins on 24 and 31 July 1943, reported being approached by someone demanding a fee to spend the night in the ruins, with "reference to a group of Polish officers who had paid £5" [14, 16].

The SPR was unable to locate the original documents for this account.

## Defences of Nawrocki's Integrity

Defenders of the Borley haunting, such as [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] and Paul Tabori, argued that the Polish officers were mature military men — including a doctor and a commanding officer — with **"no axe to grind"** [2], and that it would be difficult to suggest any logical motive for supplying completely fabricated reports to a man they did not personally know [2].
