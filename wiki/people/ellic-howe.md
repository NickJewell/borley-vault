# Ellic Howe

(Born 20 September 1910; died 28 September 1991) As a young undergraduate, accompanied [[harry-price]] on his first visit to [[borley-rectory]] on 2 June 1937, after Price had arranged to rent the Rectory for his year-long investigation (see [[price-tenancy]]).

## Borley Involvement

He and Price spent two nights in the vicinity. On the first night, Howe told [[peter-underwood]] that there was movement of small objects and sounds: "taps, thumps, and the sound of a door being closed, that Price could not possibly have been responsible for." This is significant, as Howe — who later became sceptical of Price — vouches for unexplained phenomena witnessed in conditions where Price had no opportunity to fake them.

Under Price's supervision, Howe produced the [[blue-book-of-instructions|*Blue Book of Instructions*]] for use during the Rectory investigation — the first such printed guide in the history of psychical research.

Howe also testified to Peter Underwood that the [[strange-coat-incident|strange coat]] appeared in the Rectory during the time he and Price were present inside the building, "and there was no way that either Price or anyone else could have put it there."

## War Work

During the Second World War, Howe was involved in important secret propaganda work for the British government, locating a unique font of German lettering that enabled the production of seemingly authentic German propaganda leaflets to deceive the enemy.

## Views on the Borley Report

Howe claimed in a 1968 letter to Underwood to be a member of the Harry Price Ghost Club, but his name does not appear on the Members' list. He took part at Underwood's invitation in the Ghost Club Centenary Celebration of the Life of Harry Price at the Lansdowne Club, London, and addressed the Ghost Club on several occasions.

He was a member of several esoteric and magic groups, including The Golden Dawn, about which he produced authoritative books.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Ellic Howe** (recorded variably in the sources as "Elliot Howe" and "Ellie Howe") was an Oxford undergraduate who assisted Harry Price at the outset of his year-long tenancy of Borley Rectory in 1937. He is most notable in the Borley case for co-authoring the famous manual of instructions for investigators. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following biographical details, events, quotes, and contradictions regarding his involvement:

**Lesser-Known Biographical Details**
*   **Dates:** He was born on September 20, 1910, and died on September 28, 1991 [1].
*   **World War II Espionage:** Howe shared an interest in printing with Harry Price. During the Second World War, he worked for the Special Operations Executive (SOE), becoming an expert in forging propaganda and the "manipulation of the truth" [2]. Specifically, he located a unique font of German lettering that enabled the British to produce seemingly authentic leaflets to deceive the Germans [2, 3]. 
*   **Esoteric Interests:** He was a member of several esoteric and magic groups, including **The Golden Dawn**, about which he produced an "excellent and authoritative" book [3].

**Creation of the "Blue Book" (May 1937)**
Under Price's supervision, Howe produced the printed pamphlet titled *The Alleged Haunting at B— Rectory – Instructions for Observers* [1, 4, 5]. Commonly known as the "Blue Book," this confidential guide was distributed to all official observers during the tenancy [5, 6]. Sceptics later heavily criticized the manual, arguing that it acted as a "barrage of suggestion" that told the inexperienced observers exactly what to expect to see and hear [7-9]. 

**The Rectory Vigils and Paranormal Experiences (June 1937)**
Howe accompanied Price to Borley to establish a "Base Room" in the old library and prepare the house for the official observers [2, 10, 11]. During these visits, Howe experienced several allegedly paranormal events:
*   **June 2, 1937:** After "ringing" every movable object in the house with chalk, Price and Howe discovered a dirty, moth-eaten dark blue serge lady's coat hanging behind the Blue Room door [11-13]. Later, while resting in the deathly cold Base Room, they were disturbed by tapping noises from the passageway, followed by two loud thumps and the sound of an upstairs door slamming shut [11, 14]. A search revealed no obvious cause [11].
*   **June 16, 1937:** Returning from dinner in Sudbury during a second visit, Price and Howe found that two of their control objects had moved outside their chalk circles—a tobacco tin by 3 inches, and a small box by 7 feet [5, 15]. The texts explicitly note that upon discovering this, **Howe "became scared", so they locked up the house and motored back to London** [5, 15].

**Contradictions in the Record**
The sources record two specific contradictions regarding Howe:
*   **The Length of the Stay:** There is a discrepancy regarding the June 2nd visit. One account states that Price and Howe **"spent two nights in the vicinity"** [1]. However, another account claims that because the night turned so cold, they **"abandoned the idea of staying the night"** and packed up and returned to London [11]. 
*   **Ghost Club Membership:** In a letter to Peter Underwood dated May 24, 1968, Howe claimed that he was a member of the Harry Price Ghost Club [1]. However, the records show that **his name does not actually appear on the Members' list** [1]. (He did, however, speak at the Ghost Club's Centenary Celebration of Harry Price's life on January 18, 1981, at the Lansdowne Club) [3, 16].

**Defense of Harry Price**
Despite the later attacks on Price's integrity, Howe staunchly defended his former colleague. Discussing the case years later with Peter Underwood, Howe stated verbally and in writing that the movement of objects, taps, thumps, and the door slamming that he witnessed in 1937 **"could not possibly have been engineered by Harry Price"** [1, 12]. Furthermore, regarding the mysterious coat in the Blue Room, Howe was completely convinced that it had appeared in the Rectory *while* they were inside the building, and insisted there was **"no way that either Price or anyone else could have put it there"** [17].
