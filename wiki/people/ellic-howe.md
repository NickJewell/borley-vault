# Ellic Howe

(Born 20 September 1910; died 28 September 1991) Oxford undergraduate who assisted [[harry-price|Harry Price]] at the outset of his year-long tenancy of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] in 1937. He is recorded variably in the sources as "Elliot Howe" and "Ellie Howe" [1].

## Background

Howe shared an interest in printing with Harry Price. During the Second World War he worked for the Special Operations Executive (SOE), becoming an expert in forging propaganda and the "manipulation of the truth" [2]. He located a unique font of German lettering that enabled the British to produce seemingly authentic propaganda leaflets to deceive the enemy [2, 3]. He was a member of several esoteric and magic groups, including **The Golden Dawn**, about which he produced an "excellent and authoritative" book [3].

## The Blue Book (May 1937)

Under Price's supervision, Howe produced the printed pamphlet titled *The Alleged Haunting at B— Rectory – Instructions for Observers*, commonly known as the [[blue-book-of-instructions|"Blue Book"]] — the first such printed guide in the history of psychical research [1, 4, 5]. It was distributed to all official observers during the tenancy [5, 6]. Sceptics later criticised the manual heavily, arguing that it acted as a "barrage of suggestion" that told inexperienced observers exactly what to expect to see and hear [7–9].

## Borley Vigils and Paranormal Experiences (June 1937)

Howe accompanied Price to establish a "Base Room" in the old library and prepare the house for the official observers [2, 10, 11].

**2 June 1937:** After "ringing" every movable object in the house with chalk, Price and Howe discovered a dirty, moth-eaten dark blue serge lady's coat hanging behind the [[blue-room|Blue Room]] door [11–13]. Later, while resting in the deathly cold Base Room, they were disturbed by tapping noises from the passageway, followed by two loud thumps and the sound of an upstairs door slamming shut [11, 14]. A search revealed no obvious cause [11].

**16 June 1937:** Returning from dinner in Sudbury during a second visit, Price and Howe found that two of their control objects had moved outside their chalk circles — a tobacco tin by 3 inches, and a small box by 7 feet [5, 15]. Upon discovering this, **Howe "became scared"**, so they locked up the house and motored back to London [5, 15].

> **Contradiction regarding the length of the stay:** One account states that Price and Howe "spent two nights in the vicinity" [1]. However, another account claims that because the night turned so cold, they "abandoned the idea of staying the night" and returned to London [11].

## Defence of Harry Price

Despite the later attacks on Price's integrity, Howe staunchly defended his former colleague in verbal and written statements to [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]]. He insisted that the movement of objects, taps, thumps, and the door slamming that he witnessed in 1937 **"could not possibly have been engineered by Harry Price"** [1, 12]. Regarding the mysterious coat in the Blue Room, Howe was completely convinced that it had appeared in the Rectory *while* they were inside the building, and there was **"no way that either Price or anyone else could have put it there"** [17].

## Views on the Borley Report

> **Contradiction regarding Ghost Club membership:** In a letter to Peter Underwood dated 24 May 1968, Howe claimed he was a member of the Harry Price [[ghost-club|Ghost Club]] [1]. However, his name does not appear on the Members' list [1]. He did address the Ghost Club on several occasions and spoke at the Centenary Celebration of Harry Price's life on 18 January 1981 at the Lansdowne Club [3, 16].

He was invited by Underwood to take part in the Ghost Club Centenary Celebration of the Life of Harry Price at the Lansdowne Club, London, on 18 January 1981.
