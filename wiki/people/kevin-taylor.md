# Kevin Taylor

Essex police officer who spent fourteen years tracing his birth mother **[[astrid-fisher|Astrid Fisher]]**, eventually finding her in Western Australia; his story was the subject of an episode of the BBC2 documentary series *Picture This* titled "Haunted" (1994).

## Background

Kevin was born out of wedlock in **1958** to [[astrid-fisher|Astrid Fisher]], who had been adopted by [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] (under her bigamous marriage to Henry Fisher) in the late 1930s. Astrid originally named him **Michael John** and gave him up to the Catholic Children's Society, after which she emigrated to Australia and all traces of her disappeared.

As an adult and a police officer in Essex, Kevin applied his investigative skills to tracing Astrid. He placed an advertisement in the *East Anglian Daily Times* asking for information about **Astrid Fisher**. This brought him into contact with [[john-knights|John Knights]] (formerly John Fisher), who knew Astrid from their shared childhood as Marianne Foyster's adopted children and was still living in the area. John was able to provide Kevin with an address for Astrid in Western Australia.

## The Documentary and Reunion

In **October 1994**, the BBC2 *Picture This* episode "Haunted" followed Kevin and his wife as they:
- Visited the convent in Braintree where Astrid had been raised
- Discovered she had been abandoned there when her "mother" [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] abruptly left for America after the war
- Met [[john-knights|John Knights]], who had lost contact with Astrid decades earlier

Arrangements were made for Astrid to fly from Australia to the UK. The documentary crew recorded the emotional reunion at the airport, after which Kevin personally drove Astrid to [[john-knights|John Knights's]] house — facilitating a tearful reunion between his mother and the brother she had not seen since they were children. The footage of Astrid and John embracing — reduced to children again in each other's presence, weeping: *"I never forgot you"* — was the documentary's climax.

## Significance

Kevin Taylor's search is the final human thread in the long Borley story. Through his tenacious detective work, the scattered remnants of [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster's]] family network — the Tower siblings, the Knights children — were briefly reconnected. The BBC documentary, broadcast in the year after Marianne's death, also introduced her son [[vernon-oneil|Vince O'Neil]] (who watched it) to the fuller story of his mother's past.
