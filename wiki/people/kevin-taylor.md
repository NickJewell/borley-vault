# Kevin Taylor

Essex police officer who spent fourteen years tracing his birth mother **[[astrid-fisher|Astrid Fisher]]**, eventually finding her in Western Australia; his story was the subject of the BBC2 documentary *Haunted* (1994).

## Background

Kevin was born in 1958 to [[astrid-fisher|Astrid Fisher]], who placed him for adoption through the Catholic Children's Society. He grew up not knowing his origins. His birth mother had originally named him Michael John but subsequently emigrated to Australia, where all traces of her disappeared.

As an adult and a police officer in Essex, Kevin applied his investigative skills to tracing Astrid. He placed an advertisement in the *East Anglian Daily Times* asking for information about **Astrid Fisher**. This brought him into contact with [[john-knights|John Knights]], who knew Astrid from their shared childhood as Marianne Foyster's adopted children.

## The Documentary

The BBC2 documentary *Haunted* (1994) followed Kevin and his wife as they:
- Visited the convent in Braintree where Astrid had been raised
- Discovered she had been abandoned when her "mother" [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] left for America after the war
- Met John Knights, who had lost contact with Astrid decades earlier
- Filmed the emotional reunion in which Astrid flew back from Australia to meet Kevin and be reunited with John Knights

The footage of Astrid and John embracing — reduced to children again in each other's presence, weeping: *"I never forgot you"* — was the documentary's climax.

## Significance

Kevin Taylor's search is the final human thread in the long Borley story. Through his tenacious detective work, the scattered remnants of [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s family network — the Tower siblings, the Knights children — were briefly reconnected. The BBC documentary, broadcast in the year after Marianne's death, also introduced her son [[vernon-oneil|Vince O'Neil]] (who watched it) to the fuller story of his mother's past.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Kevin Taylor** is a peripheral but emotionally significant figure in the later history of the Borley Rectory saga, connected to the case through his biological mother, Astrid Fisher, who had been adopted by Marianne Foyster (under her bigamous marriage to Henry Fisher) in the late 1930s [1, 2]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following specific biographical details and events regarding his life and his search for his mother:

**Birth and Early Life**
*   **Original Identity:** Kevin was born out of wedlock in **1958** [2]. His birth mother, Astrid, originally named him **Michael John** [2]. 
*   **Adoption:** Astrid gave him up to the Catholic Children’s Society, after which she emigrated to Australia and all traces of her went dead [2].
*   **Profession:** As an adult, Kevin Taylor worked as a young police officer in Essex [2]. 

**The 14-Year Search and the BBC Documentary**
Kevin spent over fourteen years trying to trace his birth mother [2]. His long search culminated in a television broadcast:
*   In **October 1994**, the BBC2 documentary series *Picture This* featured Kevin's story in an episode titled "Haunted" (which also included reconstructions of some of the Foyster-era Borley phenomena) [1, 2].
*   The documentary crew followed Kevin and his wife as they visited the convent in Braintree where Astrid had been raised [3]. There, they discovered the heartbreaking detail that Astrid had been abandoned at the convent when her adoptive 'mother' (Marianne Foyster) abruptly left for America after the Second World War [3].

**The Advertisement and the Knights Connection**
*   During his search, Kevin placed an advertisement in the *_East Anglian Daily Times_* asking for any information about Astrid Fisher [3]. 
*   This advertisement successfully caught the attention of John Knights (formerly John Fisher), who was Astrid's adopted brother and was still living in the area [3]. 
*   John Knights was able to provide Kevin with an address for Astrid in Western Australia, paving the way for a reunion [4]. 

**The 1994 Reunion**
Arrangements were made for Astrid to fly from Australia to the UK to be reunited with the son she had given up decades earlier [4]. 
*   The documentary crew recorded the highly emotional moment when Kevin was finally reunited with his mother at the airport [5]. 
*   Following their airport meeting, Kevin personally drove Astrid to John Knights’s house, facilitating the tearful reunion between his mother and the brother she had not seen since they were children [5].
