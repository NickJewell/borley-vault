# Rev. S. Austin

Rev. S. Austin was a clergyman who accompanied [[kathleen-goldney|KMG (Mrs K. M. Goldney)]] and [[william-salter|W. H. Salter]] (Hon. Secretary of the SPR) on a visit to [[ethel-bull|Miss Ethel Bull]] at her home in Sudbury on 11 August 1950.

The purpose of the visit was to gather testimony for the SPR's investigation of the Borley case. During this meeting Miss Bull provided her account of the 1900 four-sister nun sighting, specifying that it occurred at "late twilight in the summer, about 9 p.m." — a detail which allowed the investigators to check the sunset time for 28 July 1900 (7:53 p.m.) and thus assess the lighting conditions.

Nothing further is known about Rev. Austin from the SPR report.
