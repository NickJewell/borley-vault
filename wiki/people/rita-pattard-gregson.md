# Rita Pattard (Mrs Gregson)

Wife of [[captain-gregson|Captain William Hart Gregson]], the final owner of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]]; daughter of a Guernsey farmer; married Gregson 1920, divorced 1934.

## Background

Rita Pattard was from **Guernsey**, the daughter of a Channel Islands farmer. She met Gregson while he was taking leave on the Channel Islands following his service as an officer in the Royal Engineers during the First World War. They married in **1920** and settled on **Canvey Island**, Essex, where Gregson accumulated a large portfolio of private homes and commercial properties, eventually becoming the largest single landowner on the island by the mid-1920s. As a homage to his wife, many of Gregson's bungalows were given French Channel Island names: Jethou, Sailly Laurette, Herm.

They had two sons — **[[alan-gregson|Alan]]** (born 1921) and **[[anthony-gregson|Anthony]]** (born 1925).

## Marriage and Divorce

The marriage did not last. Gregson and Rita divorced in **1934**, four years before he purchased [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] in late 1938. By the time of the fire, Gregson was living at the Rectory with only his sons, not Rita.

## Significance

As the mother of [[alan-gregson|Alan Gregson]] and [[anthony-gregson|Anthony Hart Gregson]] — the two sons whose testimony about their father's acquisition and disposal of the Rectory proved significant — Rita's relationship to the story is indirect but real. Anthony later told researcher [[richard-lee-van-den-daele|Richard Lee-Van den Daele]] that his father had "torched it for the insurance," while Alan's 1985 letters from Australia maintained the [[fire-1939|fire]] was accidental.
