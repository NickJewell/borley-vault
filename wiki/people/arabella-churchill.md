# Arabella Churchill (c.1648–1730)

Maid of honour who became the acknowledged mistress of the future King James II; mother of [[henrietta-fitzjames|Henrietta FitzJames]], who married into the [[waldegrave-family|Waldegrave family]] and became the grandmother of [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella Waldegrave]] — the proposed alternative Borley nun-ghost.

## Background

Arabella Churchill was the sister of John Churchill, who later became the **first Duke of Marlborough** [4, 5]. According to the historian Lord Macaulay, she was the daughter of a "poor Cavalier knight who haunted Whitehall, and made himself ridiculous by publishing a dull and affected folio, long forgotten, in praise of monarchy and monarchs" [6]. Price quotes Macaulay's rather brutal assessment directly:

> *"The necessities of the Churchills were pressing: their loyalty was ardent; and their only feeling about Arabella's seduction seems to have been joyful surprise that so homely a girl should have attained such high preferment."*

Macaulay also offered a highly uncomplimentary assessment of her physical appearance: "The young lady was plain: but the taste of James was not nice" [6]. She served as one of the maids of honour waiting on the first wife of King James II, and during the "gay and dissolute" era of the Restoration, James took her as his "avowed mistress" [6].

## Connections

- Her illegitimate daughter by James was **[[henrietta-fitzjames|Henrietta FitzJames (1670–1730)]]** [3, 7]
- Her son by James was the Duke of Berwick, a Marshal of France
- Her brother John Churchill became the **first Duke of Marlborough** — the great general of the War of the Spanish Succession
- Price notes that **Winston Churchill** is a collateral descendant of this family

## Relevance to Borley

Arabella Churchill is not a Borley witness or participant. She appears in the investigation only because her daughter Henrietta married Henry Lord Waldegrave, and their daughter [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella Waldegrave]] (named after her grandmother) became the subject of [[georgina-dawson|Mrs. Dawson]]'s theory about the Borley nun-ghost. Early researchers heavily confused Arabella Churchill with her daughter Henrietta FitzJames — specifically confusing Arabella FitzJames (who became the adult nun "Sister Ignatia" at Pontoise) with the young schoolgirl Arabella Waldegrave who attended the same convent [5, 22].

Borley researchers have noted that the granddaughter's rebellious stirrings against becoming a nun may have been influenced by her grandmother's notorious legacy [9].

> **Contradiction:** One passage in the sources contains a glaring genealogical error, stating that the estates passed to "James Waldegrave (who became the 1st Earl Waldegrave in 1729)," incorrectly describing *him* as the "illegitimate son of King James II and Arabella Churchill" [4]. In reality, James Waldegrave was Arabella Churchill's grandson, born to her daughter Henrietta.

Price includes Macaulay's passage to establish the social context of the Waldegrave family's relationship to the exiled Stuart court — the milieu in which the Arabella Waldegrave "spy" hypothesis is set.
