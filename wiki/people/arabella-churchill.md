# Arabella Churchill (c.1648–1730)

Maid of honour who became the acknowledged mistress of the future King James II; mother of [[henrietta-fitzjames|Henrietta FitzJames]], who married into the [[waldegrave-family|Waldegrave family]] and became the grandmother of [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella Waldegrave]] — the proposed alternative Borley nun-ghost.

## Background

Arabella Churchill was described by Lord Macaulay as plain in appearance but chosen by James in his "ardent" pursuit of pleasure. Her father was "a poor Cavalier knight who haunted Whitehall" and had published "a dull and affected folio ... in praise of monarchy and monarchs." Price quotes Macaulay's rather brutal assessment directly:

> *"The necessities of the Churchills were pressing: their loyalty was ardent; and their only feeling about Arabella's seduction seems to have been joyful surprise that so homely a girl should have attained such high preferment."*

## Connections

- Her son by James was the Duke of Berwick, a Marshal of France
- Her brother John Churchill became the **first Duke of Marlborough** — the great general of the War of the Spanish Succession
- Price notes that **Winston Churchill** is a collateral descendant of this family

## Relevance to Borley

Arabella Churchill is not a Borley witness or participant. She appears in the investigation only because her daughter [[henrietta-fitzjames|Henrietta]] married Henry Lord Waldegrave, and their daughter [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella Waldegrave]] (named after her grandmother) became the subject of [[georgina-dawson|Mrs. Dawson]]'s theory about the Borley nun-ghost.

Price includes Macaulay's passage to establish the social context of the Waldegrave family's relationship to the exiled Stuart court — the milieu in which the Arabella Waldegrave "spy" hypothesis is set.
