# Arabella Churchill (c.1648–1730)

Maid of honour who became the acknowledged mistress of the future King James II; mother of [[henrietta-fitzjames|Henrietta FitzJames]], who married into the [[waldegrave-family|Waldegrave family]] and became the grandmother of [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella Waldegrave]] — the proposed alternative Borley nun-ghost.

## Background

Arabella Churchill was described by Lord Macaulay as plain in appearance but chosen by James in his "ardent" pursuit of pleasure. Her father was "a poor Cavalier knight who haunted Whitehall" and had published "a dull and affected folio ... in praise of monarchy and monarchs." Price quotes Macaulay's rather brutal assessment directly:

> *"The necessities of the Churchills were pressing: their loyalty was ardent; and their only feeling about Arabella's seduction seems to have been joyful surprise that so homely a girl should have attained such high preferment."*

## Connections

- Her son by James was the Duke of Berwick, a Marshal of France
- Her brother John Churchill became the **first Duke of Marlborough** — the great general of the War of the Spanish Succession
- Price notes that **Winston Churchill** is a collateral descendant of this family

## Relevance to Borley

Arabella Churchill is not a Borley witness or participant. She appears in the investigation only because her daughter [[henrietta-fitzjames|Henrietta]] married Henry Lord Waldegrave, and their daughter [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella Waldegrave]] (named after her grandmother) became the subject of [[georgina-dawson|Mrs. Dawson]]'s theory about the Borley nun-ghost.

Price includes Macaulay's passage to establish the social context of the Waldegrave family's relationship to the exiled Stuart court — the milieu in which the Arabella Waldegrave "spy" hypothesis is set.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Arabella Churchill (1648–1730)** is connected to the Borley Rectory saga not through direct involvement with the haunting, but as the maternal grandmother of Arabella Waldegrave—one of the leading historical candidates proposed by researchers (such as Georgina Dawson and Mary Lycett) as the true identity of the famous "phantom nun" [1-3]. 

A thorough reference profile based on the Borley sources reveals the following biographical details, quotes, and textual contradictions regarding her life:

**Family Background and Royal Affair**
Arabella was the sister of John Churchill, who later became the first Duke of Marlborough [4, 5]. According to the historian Lord Macaulay, she was the daughter of a **"poor Cavalier knight who haunted Whitehall, and made himself ridiculous by publishing a dull and affected folio, long forgotten, in praise of monarchy and monarchs"** [6]. 

Soon after the Restoration, Arabella served as one of the maids of honour waiting on the first wife of King James II [6]. During this "gay and dissolute" era, the young King James was attracted to Arabella and took her as his **"avowed mistress"** [6]. 

**Appearance and Family Reaction**
Lord Macaulay offered a highly uncomplimentary assessment of her physical appearance, noting that **"The young lady was plain: but the taste of James was not nice"** [6]. Macaulay also provided a wry commentary on how the financially struggling Churchill family reacted to the royal scandal:
> **"The necessities of the Churchills were pressing: their loyalty was ardent; and their only feeling about Arabella’s seduction seems to have been joyful surprise that so homely a girl should have attained such high preferment"** [6].

**Descendants and the Borley Connection**
Arabella Churchill and King James II had an illegitimate (or "natural") daughter named **Henrietta FitzJames (1670–1730)** [3, 7]. Henrietta subsequently married Henry, the first Lord Waldegrave, intertwining Arabella's royal bloodline with the Catholic Waldegrave family who held the manor of Borley for three centuries [7]. It was Arabella Churchill's granddaughter, Arabella Waldegrave (born 1687), whose mysterious disappearance into a French convent led to the theory that she was murdered at Borley as a spy and became the phantom nun [1, 3, 8]. Borley researchers have pointed out that her granddaughter's rebellious stirrings against becoming a nun may have been influenced by her grandmother's notorious legacy [9]. 

**Biographical Contradiction in the Sources**
The texts contain a specific genealogical contradiction regarding Arabella Churchill's offspring. 
* Most passages correctly identify **Henrietta FitzJames** as the illegitimate daughter born to Arabella Churchill and King James II [3, 7]. 
* However, one earlier passage contains a glaring error, stating instead that the estates passed to **James Waldegrave (who became the 1st Earl Waldegrave in 1729)**, incorrectly describing *him* as the **"illegitimate son of King James II and Arabella Churchill"** [4]. (In reality, James Waldegrave was Arabella Churchill's grandson, born to her daughter Henrietta).
