# St John Saunders

A publisher from Chelmsford. On Saturday 8 April 1961, Saunders spent an all-night vigil at Borley in the company of [[captain-ben-martindale|Captain Ben Martindale]] and [[karl-barton|Karl Barton, BSc]]. In a letter written on 11 April 1969, he described how during the vigil in the churchyard, "torches, car headlights, and a flashlight camera all 'gave out' simultaneously" — a phenomenon also noted during Croom-Hollingsworth's later technical investigations.

All three reported hearing footsteps and seeing "bright lights" for which they could not account. Saunders was "quite sure that street lighting, reflections, and similar natural explanations did not provide the answer to the pin-points of light that hovered over the Rectory garden at a height of ten to fifteen feet."

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**S. John Saunders** (also referred to in the texts as **St John Saunders**) was a **publisher from Chelmsford**, as well as a **lay investigator and journalist** [1, 2]. He paid multiple visits to Borley during the 1950s and was personally interviewed by Borley researchers Peter Underwood and Dr. Peter Hilton-Rowe [1]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the sources reveals the following specific details, events, and dates regarding his involvement in the case:

**The April 1961 Vigil and Companions**
Saunders is best known for organizing a team of fellow enthusiasts for an all-night vigil at the haunted site on **Saturday, 8 April 1961** [1-3]. He was accompanied by **Captain Ben Martindale from Ockenham** and **Mr. Karl Barton, BSc**, a civil engineer [2]. 

Arriving on Saturday afternoon at 4:00 p.m., the party assembled in the churchyard because the church was locked. After making several excursions to the former Rectory site, they eventually hid among the trees to wait for ghosts [1]. 

**Paranormal Phenomena Recorded**
During the early hours of the morning, Saunders and his companions reported experiencing a diverse range of auditory, visual, and mechanical anomalies:
*   **Voices and Footsteps:** At roughly 3:00 a.m., the party heard a **disembodied voice whispering** on the roadside opposite the Rectory site, approximately 10 yards behind their parked cars, though nothing was visible and the road was clear [1, 2]. A quarter of an hour later, they heard **loud "and menacing" footsteps** that seemed to approach them, pause about 20 feet away, and completely cease [1]. 
*   **Visual Anomalies:** At 4:00 a.m., the group observed **"vivid blue pinpoints of light"** hovering and dancing among the trees over the Rectory garden at a height of about ten to fifteen feet [1, 2]. Saunders was absolutely certain that these lights could not be explained by natural causes such as street lighting or reflections [2]. 
*   **Mechanical and Battery Interference:** During the night vigil in the churchyard, the group suffered extreme and unexplained equipment failures. **Two new torches, their car headlights, and a press photographer's floodlight (or flashlight camera) all ceased functioning simultaneously** [1, 2]. Furthermore, one of their cars inexplicably stalled on Hall Lane, and **Saunders discovered that his own watch had suddenly stopped** [1]. 

**Writings and Records**
Saunders documented the dramatic events of the 1961 vigil in several written formats:
*   He described the equipment failure and the strange lights in a letter written to Peter Underwood on **11 April 1969** [2].
*   He later published a full account of the visit in an article titled **"The Ghosts of Borley,"** which appeared in the **December 1978 issue of the _East Anglian Monthly_** [1]. The sources note that while the article contained his first-hand details of the vigil, the remainder of his piece was devoted to hearsay evidence gathered from various correspondents and visitors [1].
