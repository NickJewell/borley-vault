# St John Saunders

A publisher from Chelmsford. On Saturday 8 April 1961, Saunders spent an all-night vigil at Borley in the company of [[captain-ben-martindale|Captain Ben Martindale]] and [[karl-barton|Karl Barton, BSc]]. In a letter written on 11 April 1969, he described how during the vigil in the churchyard, "torches, car headlights, and a flashlight camera all 'gave out' simultaneously" — a phenomenon also noted during Croom-Hollingsworth's later technical investigations.

All three reported hearing footsteps and seeing "bright lights" for which they could not account. Saunders was "quite sure that street lighting, reflections, and similar natural explanations did not provide the answer to the pin-points of light that hovered over the Rectory garden at a height of ten to fifteen feet."
