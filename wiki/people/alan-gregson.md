# Alan Gregson

Son of [[captain-gregson|Capt William Hart Gregson]], the final owner of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]]. At the time of writing (1985) he was living in Australia. He corresponded with the researchers and provided two previously unpublished letters containing the most detailed account of the fire and the family's experiences at Borley.

## Letters of 1985

### First Letter (16 July 1985)

Key revelations:
- Purchase price was **£350** (not £500 as reported elsewhere) — the family's own knowledge should be definitive
- The family **never slept or ate in the rectory** — they lived in the flat above the coach-house while intending to renovate the main house
- Captain's library and furniture were stored in the ground-floor rooms of the rectory
- The fire was "a pure mischance not attributable to Poltergeist, Marianne, elemental or any mortal person"
- **The two dogs:** Peter (a spaniel-retriever cross, "a happy, reliable, uncomplicated dog") panicked in the Borley courtyard and ran howling away toward Long Melford, never seen again; weeks later a second dog suffered the same fate
- **The well cover:** the circular well in the cellar was completely unprotected; the family covered it themselves with a heavy framed door found on the property
- **Carriage tracks in snow:** "Not only footprints in overnight snow, but the tracks of ironshod wheels, as of a carriage, certainly ran from the front of the rectory, down the lawn past the cedar tree and they seemed to lift-off at the shrubbery one winter's morning. The thing to remember is that no physical access for anything bigger than a wheelbarrow led to that part of the grounds."

### Second Letter (18 October 1985)

**The fire in detail:**
> "...my father wanted a book or document from the rectory; apparently he stood the lamp on a table or box while he rummaged about and then a stack of books suddenly keeled over onto the lamp, overturning it, breaking the glass and spilling kerosene which then caught alight and speedily ignited surrounding articles."

The boys were sent to Borley Green (the nearest telephone) to call the Sudbury fire brigade. Alan Gregson himself did **not** witness any apparitions in or around the fire. The "man and lady" seen in the grounds, and the figure at the upstairs window, were reported by others. The fire was centred on the main staircase.

The family opened a visitors' book; Capt Gregson approached coach companies about organizing trips to Borley for profit. In January 1940 the captain wrote to [[harry-price|Harry Price]] saying they would like to return to live at Borley. The war ended any such plans.

## Significance

Alan Gregson's letters effectively clear his father of the arson charge. The captain owned other properties in Essex, had lost irreplaceable books, furniture and valuables in the fire, and his son's detailed account is internally consistent with an accident. The out-of-court settlement with the insurers for £750 reflects the insurers' commercial decision, not proof of guilt.
