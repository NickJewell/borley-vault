# Miss Byford (catechism teacher)

The **Sunday school and catechism teacher** at [[borley-church|Borley Church]] during the incumbency of [[rev-ac-henning|Reverend A. C. Henning]], who instructed the village children there during the mid-1940s [1–3].

She is not to be confused with [[mrs-byford|Mrs E. Byford]], the nursemaid who was employed at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] in 1886 and reported hearing ghostly footsteps.

## The 1946 Locked Door Incident

Miss Byford's inclusion in the Borley literature stems from a strange event that occurred one afternoon in **1946** before her catechism class [1–3]:

- The village children had arrived early and were waiting in the front pews for their teacher [1, 3]
- After a few minutes, the children distinctly heard **footsteps enter the church porch, followed by the sound of a key turning in the church door's lock** [1–3]
- Assuming Miss Byford had arrived, a thirteen-year-old student named **[[kathleen-king|Kathleen Finch]]** went to the door and called out, "We are here, Miss Byford" — but there was only total silence [1, 3]
- As the children became increasingly frightened, Kathleen tried to calm them by suggesting someone was playing a practical joke [1]
- A few minutes later the **real Miss Byford arrived and was completely astonished to find the door locked against her** [1–3]

## Aftermath and Investigation

[[rev-ac-henning|Rev. Henning]] initially suspected a practical joke and questioned young Kathleen Finch [1]. However, no satisfactory explanation was ever discovered, and he eventually detailed the event in a letter to [[harry-price|Harry Price]], recording it as a genuine, unexplained mystery of [[borley-church|Borley Church]] [3]. The incident is particularly notable because both children and teacher independently confirmed that the footsteps and key-turning sound had been heard before the real teacher's arrival.
