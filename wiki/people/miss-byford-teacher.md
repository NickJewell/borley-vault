# Miss Byford (catechism teacher)

The catechism teacher at [[borley-church|Borley Church]] during the incumbency of [[rev-ac-henning|Reverend A. C. Henning]]. She is not to be confused with [[mrs-byford|Mrs E. Byford]], the nursemaid who was employed at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] in 1886.

During one catechism class, the children arrived early and sat down to wait for Miss Byford. From inside, they heard footsteps come up the path to the church and the sound of a key turning in the door. Kathleen Finch (later [[kathleen-king|Kathleen King]]), a girl of about thirteen, immediately went to the door and called out "Miss Byford?" — but there was no reply. After a few minutes, the teacher arrived and was astonished to find the door locked. No satisfactory explanation was ever found.

The incident is particularly notable because [[rev-ac-henning|Mr Henning]] recorded it in a letter to [[harry-price|Harry Price]] as one of several apparently paranormal events at the church — and because both children and teacher independently confirmed that footsteps and a key-turning sound had been heard before the real teacher's arrival.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Miss Byford** is recorded in the Borley sources as a **Sunday school and catechism teacher** who instructed the village children at Borley Church during the mid-1940s [1-3]. 

*(Note: She should not be confused with Mrs. E. Byford, formerly Martha Seaber Wilson, who worked as an under-nursemaid at Borley Rectory in 1886 and reported hearing ghostly footsteps).*

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals her involvement in a single, highly specific incident of alleged paranormal activity at the church:

**The 1946 Locked Door Incident**
Miss Byford’s primary inclusion in the Borley literature stems from a strange event that occurred one afternoon in **1946** before her catechism class began [1-3]. 
*   **The Setup:** The village children had arrived early for Sunday school and were waiting in the front pews of the church for Miss Byford to arrive [1, 3]. 
*   **The Phenomena:** After a few minutes, the children distinctly heard **footsteps enter the church porch, followed by the sound of a key turning in the church door's lock** [1-3]. 
*   **The Reaction:** Assuming their teacher had arrived, a thirteen-year-old student named Kathleen Finch went to the door and called out, **"We are here, Miss Byford"** [1, 3]. However, there was no reply, only total silence [1-3]. 
*   **The Climax:** The children became increasingly frightened and alarmed by the silence, prompting Kathleen to try and calm them by suggesting someone was playing a practical joke [1]. A few minutes later, **the real Miss Byford arrived at the church and was completely astonished to find that the door had been locked** against her [1-3]. 

**Aftermath and Investigation**
The rector at the time, the Rev. Alfred Clifford Henning, initially suspected that the incident was merely a practical joke and questioned young Kathleen Finch about what had happened [1]. However, no satisfactory explanation was ever discovered, and Henning eventually detailed the event in a letter to psychical investigator Harry Price, recording it as a genuine, unexplained mystery of Borley Church [3]. 

The provided sources contain no further biographical details, events, or quotes regarding Miss Byford beyond this specific 1946 incident.
