# Captain V.M. Deane

A member of [[harry-price]]'s [[ghost-club]], Number 136, and a much-respected participant in the [[marks-tey-spiritualist-circle]]. Captain Deane of Braiswick, Colchester, studied the Borley case for many years and visited the haunted Rectory on several occasions.

## Views on Borley

Deane theorised that the [[foyster-phenomena]] during the Foyster incumbency was consistent with what one would expect from a "demented female" spirit, and felt sure that the Marks Tey group had been successful in laying the ghost following their January 1932 visit that brought the *Fifteen Months in a Haunted House* period to a close.

Deane was present at a séance in the Rectory on 26 April 1932 when the medium was apparently controlled by the ghost nun herself.

## Testimony

[[peter-underwood]] talked with Captain Deane at a Ghost Club meeting in 1948. Deane stated he had cross-examined the principal witnesses at Borley for hours on end; was in possession of the entire records of the Marks Tey Circle for twelve months of 1932; and "believed that there was not the slightest shadow of a doubt that in full lamplight bottles and stones fell amongst batches of from three to five witnesses who saw, heard and handled them."

This is a significant endorsement from an experienced investigator who was present for some of the most spectacular phenomena of the [[foyster-incumbency]].
