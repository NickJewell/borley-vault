# Rev. George Alfred Foyster

The father of [[lionel-foyster|the Rev Lionel Algernon Foyster]] and the rector and patron of the living at All Saints Church, Hastings from 1862 until 1904. He is notable as one of the executors and trustees named in the will of [[henry-bull|the Rev Henry Dawson Ellis Bull]].

## Family Connection to the Bulls

Caroline Sarah Bull, wife of the Rev Henry Dawson Ellis Bull, was originally a member of the Foyster family of Hastings before her marriage. The will of Henry Bull (dated 22 March 1877) named George Alfred Foyster as one of three executors and trustees, the others being Mrs Caroline Sarah Bull and a London solicitor, Walter Scadding. The connection of the Foyster family to the Bull family in this formal legal capacity explains why the Foyster and Bull families remained closely linked across generations.

Babbs and Mathias note that the Bulls and Foysters were said to have been related to each other even before Mrs Bull's marriage — suggesting a family that was already interconnected.

## Significance

George Alfred Foyster held the Hastings living from 1862 until 1904. It was at this living that young [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Algernon Foyster]] could have been offered a placement by his father upon his ordination in 1904 — but chose instead to serve elsewhere, first in Yorkshire, then in Cheshire. The authors note this would have been an invitation to accusations of nepotism.

When Lionel eventually became rector of Borley in 1930, the family circle was thus connecting back to the very family that had entrusted a Foyster with the guardianship of its estate in 1877.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Rev. George Alfred Foyster** was the father of the Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster, the famous rector who resided at Borley Rectory from 1930 to 1935 [1, 2]. He was part of a wealthy Victorian ecclesiastical dynasty in Sussex where church livings were typically passed down from father to son [3]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the sources reveals the following biographical details, career events, dates, and controversies surrounding his life:

**Background and Family**
*   **Education and Lineage:** He was the younger son of the Rev. Henry Samuel Foyster [3]. Following in his family's footsteps, he was educated at Trinity College, Cambridge [3]. 
*   **Marriage:** He married Adelaide Julia Tillard, the third daughter of Philip Tillard, J.P., of Stukely Hall in Huntingdon [4]. Through this marriage, his family gained connections to relatives of considerable social standing and influence [4, 5].
*   **Children:** Together, George Alfred and Adelaide had at least four sons and two daughters (Hilda and Adelaide) [5]. Lionel Algernon was the fourth son, but the only one to follow his father into the priesthood [5]. 

**Clerical Career and Borley Connections**
*   **Rector of All Saints':** For forty-two years, from **1862 until his resignation in 1904**, George Alfred served as both the Rector and the patron of the living at All Saints' Church in Hastings, Sussex [2, 6, 7]. 
*   **Connection to Borley Rectory:** George Alfred's sister, Caroline Sarah Foyster, spent her childhood at All Saints' Rectory before marrying the Rev. Henry Dawson Ellis Bull in 1862, the very year Henry Bull was inducted at Borley [7]. This made George Alfred the brother-in-law to the builder of Borley Rectory, and the uncle to the Rev. Harry Bull [7]. 
*   **Harry Bull's Will:** He maintained close ties with the Borley Bulls and was officially appointed as one of the executors and trustees of the Rev. Harry Bull's will [6]. 

**The Succession Controversy**
A major point of interest in the sources regarding George Alfred is his specific role in denying his son Lionel the family living. 
*   In **1904**, the exact same year that Lionel was fully ordained as a priest, George Alfred decided to retire and resigned from All Saints' [2, 5, 8]. 
*   As the patron of the living, he was legitimately empowered to pass the highly desirable parish directly to Lionel, but inexplicably chose not to, instead returning the living to the diocese [2, 8, 9]. 
*   Lionel was instead sent to a curacy in Cheshire to work under his own brother-in-law [8]. 
*   Author Robert Wood highlights this decision to raise questions about Lionel's early character, theorizing that George Alfred bypassed his own son because he had discovered some terrible family difficulty or sexual scandal. Wood provides the quote: **"It is not unreasonable to conclude that there was some scandal or family difficulty involving Lionel Algernon, which not only persuaded his father that he could not be allowed to take over All Saints', but also led to his serving his second curacy under the watchful eye of his brother-in-law"** [8]. 

**Retirement and Death**
Following his resignation in 1904, George Alfred Foyster retired to Guise House in Aspley Guise, Bedfordshire [8]. He died there on **11 December 1911**, almost exactly one year after the death of his elder brother, the Rev. Henry Brereton Foyster [3, 8].
