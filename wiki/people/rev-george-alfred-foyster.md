# Rev. George Alfred Foyster

The father of [[lionel-foyster|the Rev Lionel Algernon Foyster]] and the rector and patron of the living at All Saints' Church, Hastings, from **1862 until 1904**. He was the younger son of the Rev. [[rev-henry-samuel-foyster|Henry Samuel Foyster]] [3], part of a wealthy Victorian ecclesiastical dynasty in Sussex where church livings were typically passed down from father to son [3].

## Background and Family

He was educated at Trinity College, Cambridge [3]. He married **Adelaide Julia Tillard**, the third daughter of [[philip-tillard-jp|Philip Tillard, J.P.]], of Stukely Hall in Huntingdon [4]. Through this marriage, his family gained connections to relatives of considerable social standing and influence [4, 5]. Together, George Alfred and Adelaide had at least four sons and two daughters (Hilda and Adelaide) [5]. Lionel Algernon was the fourth son, but the only one to follow his father into the priesthood [5].

## Family Connection to the Bulls

George Alfred's sister, **Caroline Sarah Foyster**, spent her childhood at All Saints' Rectory before marrying the Rev. [[henry-bull|Henry Dawson Ellis Bull]] in 1862 — the very year George Alfred was inducted at Borley [7]. This made George Alfred the brother-in-law to the builder of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] and the uncle to the Rev. [[harry-bull|Harry Bull]] [7]. He maintained close ties with the Borley Bulls and was officially appointed as one of the executors and trustees of the Rev. Harry Bull's will [6] — the document naming him is recorded in [[henry-bull-will]]. Babbs and Mathias note that the Bulls and Foysters were said to have been related to each other even before Mrs Bull's marriage — suggesting a family that was already interconnected.

When [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] eventually became rector of Borley in 1930, the family circle was thus connecting back to the very family that had entrusted a Foyster with the guardianship of its estate in 1877.

## The Succession Controversy

A major point of interest in the sources regarding George Alfred is his specific role in denying his son Lionel the family living.

In **1904**, the exact same year that Lionel was fully ordained as a priest, George Alfred decided to retire and resigned from All Saints' [2, 5, 8]. As the patron of the living, he was legitimately empowered to pass the highly desirable parish directly to Lionel, but inexplicably chose not to, instead returning the living to the diocese [2, 8, 9]. Lionel was sent instead to a curacy in Cheshire to work under his own brother-in-law, the [[rev-edmund-wethered|Rev. Edmund Peel Wethered]] [8].

Author Robert Wood highlights this decision to raise questions about Lionel's early character, theorising that George Alfred bypassed his own son because he had discovered some terrible family difficulty or sexual scandal:

> **"It is not unreasonable to conclude that there was some scandal or family difficulty involving Lionel Algernon, which not only persuaded his father that he could not be allowed to take over All Saints', but also led to his serving his second curacy under the watchful eye of his brother-in-law."** [8]

## Retirement and Death

Following his resignation in 1904, George Alfred retired to Guise House in Aspley Guise, Bedfordshire [8]. He died there on **11 December 1911**, almost exactly one year after the death of his elder brother, the Rev. [[rev-henry-brereton-foyster|Henry Brereton Foyster]], in 1910 [3, 8].
