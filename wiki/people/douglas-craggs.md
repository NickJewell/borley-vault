# Douglas Craggs

Vice president of the **Magic Circle** and the first person to publicly denounce [[harry-price|Harry Price]] after his death in March 1948.

## The Denunciation

Within twenty-four hours of Price's death, Craggs made an extraordinarily contentious claim to the press: Price had "bamboozled the public into believing things in which he placed no credence whatsoever."

Craggs's resentment had a specific source: Price had explicitly barred Magic Circle members from participating in his observer programme at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] during the tenancy years 1937–38. Several Magic Circle members — including Craggs himself — had applied and been rejected. The [[the-borley-report|Borley Report]] later noted this as suspicious: Price was supposedly conducting a scientific investigation, so why exclude expert illusionists and conjurors who would be best placed to detect trickery?

## Significance

Craggs's immediate post-mortem attack opened the floodgates for the subsequent campaign against Price's reputation led by [[william-salter|Salter]], [[kathleen-goldney|Mollie Goldney]], and [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]]. His claim that Price had cynically deceived the public, combined with [[charles-sutton|Charles Sutton]]'s later published accusation (the *Inky Way Annual*, December 1948), formed the opening salvos of what became the [[the-borley-report|Borley Report]].

> O'Connor characterises Craggs's denunciation as driven at least partly by professional pique: he had been shut out of Price's investigation.
