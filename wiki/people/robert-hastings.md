# Robert J. Hastings (died 15 August 1973)

Lay researcher and psychical investigator based at Tan-y-coed, Llandre, Cardiganshire, Wales. He died from a stroke in Llandre on 15 August 1973 — four years after the publication of his report. Before their first meeting in September 1956, he described himself to [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] as "a middle-aged type, height about 6ft, fair hair which may be untidy," adding that he would be carrying a briefcase and a copy of the SPR Journal. In addition to the Borley case, Hastings conducted in-depth studies of two other famous spontaneous-phenomena cases: the **Versailles Timeslip** and the **Dieppe Raid case**.

His *An Examination of the "Borley Report"* (1969) is the most important and most rigorous formal rebuttal of the SPR's 1956 dismissal of the Borley haunting.

## The Hastings Report

**Genesis:** Over the Christmas holiday in 1956, Hastings read the newly published *Borley Report* alongside [[harry-price|Price]]'s books and immediately noticed that every mistake he found tended "in a direction unfavourable to Price." He wrote to SPR Honorary Secretary [[william-salter|William Salter]] to say so; Salter subsequently warned [[kathleen-goldney|Goldney]] that he regarded Hastings "with some suspicion." When Hastings tried to publish a reply immediately, his 1956 critical paper was rejected by the SPR Journal editor, and he was explicitly denied access to the working material of Dingwall, Goldney, and Hall for several years.

**Commissioned** by the SPR's Research Advisory Committee in 1965; funded with a small grant of just £50; published March 1969 *Proceedings of the SPR* (Vol. 55, Part 201). Hastings had full access to all Harry Price Library materials at the University of London, relevant SPR files, and worked in collaboration with [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] (who gave him access to his unique correspondence files including the Dom Richard Whitehouse archive). He also commissioned Dr Alan Gauld to conduct a fresh interview with Sutton in June 1966 — 37 years after the alleged incident, revealing crucial admissions HBR had never elicited.

**Scope:** 10 chapters covering every major allegation in HBR, plus three appendices (Sutton's signed statement, Gauld's 1966 interview notes, Dom Richard Whitehouse's 1956 letter to Underwood) and a detailed chronology.

**Key findings by chapter:**

| Chapter | Allegation examined | Verdict |
|---------|--------------------|---------| 
| 1–2 | Sutton stone-throwing accusation | Not proven; Sutton's own admissions to Gauld undermine every key detail |
| 3 | Mrs Smith's testimony | Comprehensively unreliable; systematized repressive memory; Mr Smith's private letters confirm belief in paranormality |
| 4 | Medal substitution | Logically incoherent — Price would have been manufacturing evidence for a theory he repeatedly and publicly disbelieved |
| 5 | Planted bone fragments | Physically impossible — Price couldn't dig; Henning and Glanville present and suspicious of nothing |
| 6 | Douglas-Home's cellophane accusation | Douglas-Home himself admitted "I have no idea how the sound was produced"; twelve-year-old memory |
| 7 | Price's "duplicity" re. Marianne | His evolution was honest and gradual, driven by new testimony (Lady Whitehouse, Dom Richard) unavailable during his skeptical period |
| 8 | Flying brick | Price never claimed it was paranormal; Thompson's account internally contradictory |
| 9 | Minor allegations | Glanville's Locked Book confirms Price's accounts; squiggle markings evidence carefully demolished by SPR but actually strong |
| 10 | Price's aims | He was a scientific journalist depositing his archive for scrutiny — a fraudster would not do this |

**Key defences in detail:**

- **The Open Archive Argument**: A large part of the SPR case rested on Price's own private files, which Price had voluntarily deposited at the University of London Library for anyone to consult during his lifetime. Hastings argued Price clearly did not view them as incriminating.
- **The Charles Sutton "Exposure"**: Sutton claimed he caught Price faking poltergeist phenomena by throwing pebbles. Hastings revealed Sutton admitted he never actually *saw* Price throw any stones. Hastings hypothesised that Price suffered from severe angina, and that the shock of the dark house, sudden loud noises, and being violently seized had likely triggered a heart attack — leaving Price too incapacitated to properly defend himself.
- **The Cellar Bones**: Hastings pointed out Price was physically incapable of clandestine grave-digging due to his weak heart, and demonstrated that the wartime lack of local labour and accommodation would have made it impossible to secretly transport and bury bones without detection. He also proved that the battery lighting set found in the cellar was actually installed by [[captain-gregson|Captain Gregson]] for giving tours of the ruins.
- **The Flying Brick**: Hastings showed Price had merely commented on the occurrence without ever claiming it was paranormal.
- **[[dom-richard-whitehouse|Dom Richard Whitehouse]]**: Hastings proved Whitehouse had fully recovered from his 1924–25 nervous breakdown long before his Borley visits, and secured Whitehouse's own August 1956 letter confirming he drew his MHH account from *contemporary notes made in June 1931*.

**Appendix C (Dom Richard Whitehouse):** Whitehouse confirms: he drew his MHH account from *contemporary notes made in June 1931*; the stiletto landed on his lap while he was watching Marianne "who had not moved hand or foot"; HBR suppressed all reference to these notes; his health in 1931 was good.

**Overall verdict:** "The allegations made against him in HBR were very grossly exaggerated... it can hardly be denied that the material I have brought forward in Price's defence ought — and I use this word in its strongest possible sense — to have been made public at the time the allegations were made."

Hastings viewed Price not as a fraudster but as a man of "upright character" and "complete integrity."

## The Ongoing Battle

The publication of the Hastings Report caused an immediate media sensation, with many interpreting it as the official exoneration of Price. The SPR felt compelled to issue a statement reminding the public that the Society held no corporate views. In September 1969, Dingwall, Goldney, and Hall published a fierce rebuttal, *Mr Hastings and the Borley Report*. Hastings published a final reply, *Reply to the Authors of the "Borley Report"*, in March 1970.

The Hastings Report has never been substantively refuted. Modern researchers including [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] and [[ivan-banks]] explicitly support its findings.
