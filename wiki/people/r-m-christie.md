# R. M. Christie

R. M. Christie was one of [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s official observers at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]]. He visited on the night of 8 December 1937 with [[dr-pe-ryberg|Dr P. E. Ryberg]] and [[l-g-cooper|L. G. Cooper]].

Their joint report — the contents of which Price suppressed — recorded that no unexplained sounds were heard, that mice were observed actively ravaging boxes under the table, and that gnawed paper was found. This was one of several observer reports that contradicted Price's published claim that rats and mice were never mentioned by any observer at Borley.
