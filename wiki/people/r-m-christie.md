# R. M. Christie

R. M. Christie was one of [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s official observers at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]]. He visited on the night of **8 December 1937** with [[dr-pe-ryberg|Dr P. E. Ryberg]] and [[l-g-cooper|L. G. Cooper]] [1].

## The December 1937 Vigil and Suppressed Report

Their joint report — the contents of which Price suppressed — recorded that no unexplained sounds were heard, that mice were observed actively ravaging boxes under the table, and that gnawed paper was found. In their official report submitted to Price, Christie and the team explicitly recorded:

> **"No sounds of unexplained origin were heard. . . . Mice were heard ravaging in some boxes under the table. Gnawed paper was found in these boxes. . . . Mice were seen to scuttle around . . ."** [1]

This was one of several observer reports that contradicted Price's published claim that rats and mice were never mentioned by any observer at Borley.

Christie's involvement was highlighted years later by the authors of the critical [[spr-investigation|*Borley Report*]] (Dingwall, Goldney, and Hall) to demonstrate Price's biased methodology. The authors noted that **Harry Price completely omitted Christie's report from his published works** [1], thereby allowing the natural noises to remain "unexplained" in his books to artificially bolster the narrative that the Rectory was haunted [1].
