# Beverly St Jacques

The woman with whom [[robert-oneil|Robert O'Neil]] left [[hokah-minnesota|Hokah, Minnesota]], precipitating his first separation from [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] after their arrival in America.

## Account

Robert Wood records that "O'Neil left Hokah with a woman named Beverly St Jacques" following the failure of the Hokah household (O'Neil was described as "a work-shy drunkard"). As a consequence, the infant [[robert-oneil|Robert Vincent O'Neil]] ("Vincent") was placed temporarily in a children's home in La Crosse.

The separation was not permanent: O'Neil later rejoined Marianne in Jamestown, North Dakota, found work as a plumber, but continued to drink heavily and rely on Marianne's earnings. She is listed as "widow of Robert O'Neil" in Fargo street directories from 1961, though O'Neil was apparently still alive but divorced (1959).

## Significance

Beverly St Jacques appears only in passing in Wood's account, but she represents the first evidence of the fundamental incompatibility of the O'Neil marriage — begun out of expedience on Marianne's part and alcoholic irresponsibility on O'Neil's. The pattern mirrors Marianne's earlier relationships: the cycle of dependency, betrayal, and partial reconciliation.
