# Ursula Pennell

Wife of [[canon-leslie-pennell|Canon James Henry Leslie Pennell]], who became the rector of Foxearth and Pentlow in November 1965 and later took on the combined parishes of Borley and Liston from 1967 [1–3]. A native of East Anglia, she came with her husband following his retirement as the provost of St Andrew's Cathedral in Inverness [1].

Together with her husband, she witnessed a hooded figure in December 1965 that nearly caused a collision with their Dormobile on the road between the Borley and Liston turnings.

## The "Dormobile" Apparition (3 December 1965)

Only a few days after moving to the area, Ursula and her husband experienced a startling encounter. At approximately 8:00 p.m. on a wet evening, they were driving back from the Long Melford railway station after picking up their son, David [2]. As they drove their Dormobile camper van along the road between the turnings for Borley and [[liston-rectory|Liston]], a bowed figure wearing a hood and a long, dark grey robe stepped out directly in front of their vehicle, crossing the road from right to left [2].

Seeing the figure, Ursula shouted out **"you fool"** — directed at the figure, not at her husband — prompting him to brake hard [2]. When the local press later wrote about this incident, a reporter created the unfortunate impression that Ursula had been shouting at her husband [4]; in reality, she was directing her exclamation entirely at the mysterious figure in the road. When the couple got out of the van to investigate, the road was completely deserted. Their son David, sitting in the back of the Dormobile and not paying attention to the road until his father braked sharply, did not see the figure [2, 4] (two witnesses).

## The Phantom Coach Sighting

Beyond the robed figure, the Pennells were also reportedly linked to sightings of Borley's legendary [[phantom-coach|phantom coach]]. According to a letter submitted by a Mr. Poore, both the rector and his wife reportedly saw the ghostly coach in a ploughed field near the old road route to the church [5]. However, Mr. Poore noted that the couple were "evidently unwilling to discuss it" [5] (reportedly, single correspondent source).

## The 1974 BBC Broadcast

Despite any reluctance to discuss the phantom coach, Ursula Pennell appeared in a BBC television programme about Borley filmed in **August 1974**, describing the Dormobile experience alongside [[hilary-wright|Hilary Wright]], [[marie-poore|Marie Poore]], and Canon Pennell. The segment was filmed at Pond Cottage (the home of Borley witness Hilary Wright), and each participant reportedly received £10 for their contribution [6].
