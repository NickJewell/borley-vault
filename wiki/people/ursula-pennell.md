# Ursula Pennell

Wife of [[canon-leslie-pennell]]; a native of East Anglia. Together with her husband, she witnessed the hooded figure in December 1965 that nearly caused a collision with their Dormobile on the road between the Borley and Liston turnings.

She shouted "you fool" — directed at the figure, not at her husband (press reports created the mistaken impression she was addressing him). She outlived her husband and continued to reside in the area.

She later appeared in a BBC television programme about Borley filmed in August 1974, where she described the experience along with [[hilary-wright]], [[marie-poore]], and Canon Pennell.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Ursula Pennell** was the wife of Canon James Henry Leslie Pennell, who became the rector of Foxearth and Pentlow in November 1965, and later took on the combined parishes of Borley and Liston from 1967 [1-3]. As a native of East Anglia, her regional roots played a significant part in the couple's decision to move to the Borley area following her husband's retirement as the provost of St Andrew’s Cathedral in Inverness [1]. 

The Borley sources record her involvement in a dramatic paranormal sighting, a specific press contradiction, and her later participation in a television broadcast:

**The "Dormobile" Apparition (December 3, 1965)**
Only a few days after moving to the area, Ursula and her husband experienced a startling encounter. At approximately 8:00 p.m. on a wet evening, they were driving back from the Long Melford railway station after picking up their son, David [2]. 
*   **The Sighting:** As they drove their *Dormobile* camper van along the road between the turnings for Borley and Liston, a bowed figure wearing a hood and a long, dark grey robe stepped out directly in front of their vehicle, crossing the road from right to left [2].
*   **The Quote and the Press Contradiction:** Seeing the figure step in front of them, Ursula shouted out, **"you fool"**, prompting her husband to brake hard [2]. When the local press later wrote about this incident, the reporter **created the unfortunate impression that Ursula had been shouting at her husband**. In reality, she had been directing her exclamation entirely at the mysterious figure in the road [4]. 
*   **The Empty Road:** When the couple got out of the van to investigate, the road was completely deserted. Their son David, who had been sitting in the back of the *Dormobile* and was not paying attention to the road conditions until his father braked sharply, did not see the figure [2, 4].

**The Phantom Coach Sighting**
Beyond the robed figure, Ursula and her husband were also linked to sightings of Borley's legendary phantom coach. According to a letter submitted by a Mr. Poore, both the rector and his wife reportedly saw the ghostly coach in a ploughed field near the old road route to the church [5]. However, Mr. Poore noted that Ursula and her husband were **"evidently unwilling to discuss it"** [5]. 

**The 1974 BBC Broadcast**
Despite any reluctance to discuss the phantom coach, Ursula Pennell and her husband did agree to publicly discuss their experiences on television. In **August 1974**, they took part in a BBC television programme hosted by ghost hunter Peter Underwood [6]. The segment was filmed nearby at Pond Cottage (the home of Borley witness Hilary Wright) and **for their participation in the broadcast, Ursula and the other participants each received a generous reward of £10** [6].
