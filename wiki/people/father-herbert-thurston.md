# Father Herbert Thurston, S.J.

Jesuit priest and respected authority on spiritualism and the paranormal, consulted by [[dom-richard-whitehouse|Edwin Whitehouse]] before his first visits to [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] in 1931.

## Background

Thurston was a Jesuit priest who was highly esteemed in Catholic intellectual circles but whose attitude to the paranormal was considered by some orthodox Catholics to be too sympathetic to be compatible with his calling. He had published extensively on spiritualism and paranormal phenomena from a Catholic perspective.

## Role at Borley

When [[dom-richard-whitehouse|Edwin Whitehouse]] received [[lady-whitehouse|Lady Whitehouse]]'s letter describing the extraordinary events at Borley and decided to visit the Rectory, he first consulted Father Thurston in London. Thurston was impressed by the phenomena described in the letter and advised Edwin:

1. To **say prayers** at the house
2. To **bless it with holy water**
3. But to be warned that "in almost all the recorded cases of poltergeist phenomena, this had had little effect"

This measured but engaged response — acknowledging the phenomena as real while tempering expectations of exorcism — is consistent with Thurston's published view that poltergeist phenomena are genuine but resist purely spiritual remedies.

## Significance

Thurston's consultation was one of several clerical interventions sought by the Foysters and their associates. The pattern of Anglo-Catholic and Roman Catholic clergy being drawn to Borley — Edwin, [[dom-richard-whitehouse|Dom Richard]] in his later monk persona, [[arthur-sellwood|Arthur Sellwood]], [[bernard-smith-rector|Bernard Smith]], and eventually Father Wright who said the Requiem Mass for Marie Lairre — reflects the Catholic character of the haunting as Price and others perceived it.
