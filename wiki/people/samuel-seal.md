# Samuel Seal

Sergeant with the searchlight battery stationed at Belchamp Water during World War II. Witnessed a phantom light phenomenon near the Borley Rectory site in 1942, recorded in the 1947 BBC broadcast.

## Testimony (1942)

Returning from leave after midnight, Seal was cycling toward Borley. A policeman stopped him to check his lamp and advised him not to go through Borley: "I shouldn't go that way if I were you. I should go through Foxearth." Seal ignored the advice.

Halfway up the hill toward the Rectory site, he saw "two lights coming round the corner... coming at a devil of a speed" — he assumed it was a car coasting downhill. As the lights drew to within about 100 yards, "they switched across the road... as though they just shot across the road and there was a dark shape following them." He assumed they had turned into the Rectory garden gate — but on investigation, "there was no gate that had been opened as the grass was still up by the gate." He investigated the hedgeside and was just against the burnt-out Rectory tower when "there was a yelping scream" — he fled by bicycle.

His report back at the battery: "Talk about seeing one... I've heard one too." He had never heard anything about the Rectory haunting.

## Significance

This is an independently-witnessed light phenomenon at the [[borley-rectory|Rectory site]] in 1942, three years after the fire, from a witness with no prior knowledge of the Borley haunting. The lights behaved as the phantom coach lights are described — appearing from the direction of the church, crossing the road, entering the former Rectory grounds — and the witness was shocked enough to flee.
