# Fred Tatum

The eventual husband of [[mary-pearson|Mary Pearson]], who had been a maid at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] during the [[rev-eric-smith|Smith]] incumbency. His connection to Borley was used by critics of the haunting as a possible mundane explanation for some of the apparitions reported during the late 1920s.

## As Sceptical Explanation

[[mabel-smith|Mrs Smith]], in one of her later accounts, revealed that Fred Tatum was frequently at the Rectory during her time there — though she claimed to have known nothing about it at the time. The suggestion made by critics was that Tatum's unauthorised presence in or around the Rectory grounds might account for some of the "shadowy figures" and unexplained presences reported during the Smith incumbency. 

[[harry-price|Harry Price]] noted that [[mary-pearson|Mary Pearson]] had also told him she had seen "a man apparently without a head behind a tree" — and that by 1952 she had "admitted that her statement was untrue," with the implication being that the figure was Tatum, whom she was reluctant to identify at the time.

This explanation cannot account for sightings predating the Smith period, including those attributed to the [[harry-bull|Reverend Harry Bull]] — who had also spoken about seeing a headless figure in the garden, and had described it to Lady Whitehouse and others.
