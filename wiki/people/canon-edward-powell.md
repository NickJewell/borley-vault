# Canon Edward Powell (d. 14 November 1994)

Vicar of Belchamp St Paul (only a few miles from Borley) until his retirement in 1989 at the age of 80. Rural dean; elected to serve on Braintree District Council; instrumental in securing a community centre and main drainage for Belchamp St Paul. Ran educational courses preparing young people for public schools — self-described as "the last of the autocratic clergy." He was the person who, during a locum at Belchamp St Paul, first directed the Mathiases toward the Borley living.

## Cycling Encounter — Early 1960s

On an afternoon in the early 1960s, Canon Powell cycled over to the Rodbridge area of Borley to visit a sick friend (not "poaching" in [[rev-lanfranc-mathias|Lanfranc's]] parish). His return route took him past Borley church (on his right) and the old rectory grounds (on his left).

When he reached the top of Hall Lane at the point opposite the church, a **shadowy figure dressed in long robes moved out onto the road** in front of him, coming from his left, and then "**melted back into the hedge** from whence it had appeared."

He stopped, got off his bicycle, and went into the former rectory garden to search thoroughly. No one was there. He noted that the area had been largely cleared in preparation for the bungalows being built, so there was little cover. The light was still good.

He was "definite that he saw what he saw," at a complete loss for a logical explanation, and expressed the firm opinion to his acquaintances that **Borley was indeed a haunted place**.
