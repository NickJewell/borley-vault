# Miss Marion Pitt

The **village schoolmistress** at Borley during the early 1930s, and one of the few local residents who became friends with [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]. She appears in the 1935 electoral roll for the parish, listed as living "near the Green" (i.e., at Borley Green) [1, 2].

## Life at Borley

Marion Pitt occupied one of two adjoining semi-detached cottages at Borley Green. The other cottage was the home of [[alfred-finch|Alfred]] and [[margaret-finch|Margaret Finch]] for most of their lives [1]. While many of the local villagers were suspicious of or bewildered by the young, vivacious [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]], Miss Pitt was a notable exception — described as "one of the few villagers who became friends with Marianne Foyster" [2].

Alfred and Margaret Finch recalled that Marianne used to visit Miss Pitt's cottage frequently during the Foyster incumbency (1930–35), and that the two women could be heard **"shrieking with laughter"** together [1] — a striking contrast to the poltergeist assaults and domestic gloom typically associated with Marianne's life at the Rectory.

According to Alfred Finch, Marianne had designs on a local resident, [[herbert-mayes|Herbert Mayes]], and this may have been among the subjects of their hilarious conversations [1]. Herbert Mayes was a very delicate man who worked as a casual gardener at the rectory during the Foyster years, and later became chauffeur-gardener to the subsequent rector, [[rev-ac-henning|Rev Alfred Clifford Henning]].

## Significance

Miss Pitt is one of a small number of individuals who maintained a genuinely warm relationship with the controversial [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]. Her presence in the 1935 electoral roll confirms the detail of the Finch family testimony.
