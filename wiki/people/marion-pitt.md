# Miss Marion Pitt

The village schoolmistress at Borley during the early 1930s, and one of the few local residents who became friends with [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]. She appears in the 1935 electoral roll for the parish, listed as living "near the Green" (i.e., at Borley Green).

## Life at Borley

Marion Pitt occupied one of two adjoining semi-detached cottages at Borley Green. The other cottage was the home of [[alfred-finch|Alfred]] and [[margaret-finch|Margaret Finch]] for most of their lives. Alfred and Margaret Finch recalled that Marianne Foyster used to visit Miss Pitt frequently during the Foyster incumbency (1930–35), and that the two women could be heard shrieking with laughter together.

According to Alfred Finch, Marianne had designs on a local resident, Herbert Mayes, and this may have been among the subjects of their hilarious conversations. [[herbert-mayes|Herbert Mayes]] was a very delicate man who worked as a casual gardener at the rectory during the Foyster years, and later became chauffeur-gardener to the subsequent rector, Rev Alfred Clifford Henning.

## Significance

Miss Pitt is one of a small number of individuals who had a friendly relationship with the controversial Marianne Foyster — an unusual position in a community where the rector's wife was largely regarded with bewilderment or suspicion. Her presence in the 1935 electoral roll confirms the detail of the Finch family testimony.
