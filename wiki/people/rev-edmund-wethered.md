# Rev. Edmund Peel Wethered

Brother-in-law of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster]]; married Lionel's sister [[adelaide-wethered|Adelaide Foyster]]. He was the curate of **Oughtrington, Cheshire**, and it was under his supervision that Lionel served his second curacy (1905–1910).

## Significance

In 1905, Lionel Foyster left his first curacy in Yorkshire and moved to a second curacy at Oughtrington in Cheshire, where his superior was the Rev. Edmund Peel Wethered [1]. The placement of Lionel as curate under his own brother-in-law is one of Robert Wood's key pieces of circumstantial evidence for an early scandal in Lionel's clerical career. Wood reasons:

> **"It is not unreasonable to conclude that there was some scandal or family difficulty involving Lionel Algernon, which not only persuaded his father that he could not be allowed to take over All Saints', but also led to his serving his second curacy under the watchful eye of his brother-in-law at Oughtrington."** [1]

Lionel remained at Oughtrington from 1905 until his departure to Canada in 1910. Oughtrington is about a mile from Lymm, Warrington — the very town where the Shaw family (including the young [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]]) were living. Wood suggests Foyster's attraction to the girl he had baptized as a child (1906) may have been part of what motivated his continued presence there before his Canadian departure.
