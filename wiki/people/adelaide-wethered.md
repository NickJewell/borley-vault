# Adelaide Foyster (Mrs Wethered)

Sister of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster]]; daughter of Rev. George Alfred Foyster and [[adelaide-julia-tillard|Adelaide Julia Tillard]]. She married [[rev-edmund-wethered|Rev. Edmund Peel Wethered]], curate of Oughtrington, Cheshire.

## Known Facts

Adelaide was one of two sisters in the Foyster family (the other being Hilda — see [[hilda-foyster]]). When Lionel's second curacy was arranged at Oughtrington under her husband's supervision, Adelaide was part of the "watchful eye" arrangement that Wood infers was placed over Lionel following an unspecified early scandal.

Her name is significant in another sense: Lionel and Marianne later adopted a child they named Adelaide — the child who became [[adelaide-foyster|Adelaide Foyster]]. Whether the name was given in honour of Lionel's sister (or his mother, also named Adelaide) is not stated in the sources.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Adelaide Foyster (Mrs. Wethered)** is a background genealogical figure in the Borley Rectory story. She is distinct from both her mother (Adelaide Julia Tillard) and her adopted niece (the toddler Adelaide Foyster). 

**Family Background and Marriage**
Adelaide was the daughter of the Rev. George Alfred Foyster (who served as the Rector of All Saints', Hastings) and his wife, Adelaide Julia Tillard [1]. She was one of eight children, having six brothers—including the Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster, the controversial Rector of Borley—and one sister, Hilda [1]. She eventually married the **Reverend Edmund Peel Wethered** [2].

**Connection to Lionel Foyster's Early Career**
While Adelaide did not reside at Borley Rectory or participate directly in the hauntings, her marriage to Rev. Wethered is cited by Borley researchers to explain a mysterious phase in her brother Lionel's early career. In 1905, Lionel Foyster moved to Oughtrington in Cheshire to take up his second curacy; his superior in this new parish was his brother-in-law, Rev. Wethered [2].

**Historical Speculation and the "Watchful Eye"**
Borley historian Robert Wood uses Adelaide's marriage to Wethered to theorize that Lionel Foyster had been involved in a hushed-up sexual scandal or "family difficulty" in his youth [2]. Wood points out that Lionel was the only son to follow the family's clerical tradition, yet he was inexplicably passed over for the highly desirable family living at All Saints', Hastings, when his father retired in 1904 [1, 2]. 

Instead of receiving this lucrative post, Lionel remained a lowly curate and was relocated to Oughtrington [2]. Wood concludes that Lionel's father deliberately orchestrated this move so that **Lionel would be forced to serve his curacy "under the watchful eye of his brother-in-law"**, Adelaide's husband, to keep him in line and monitor his behaviour [2].
