# Henry Foyster (Harry Bull's cousin)

Anglican clergyman who led the funeral service for [[harry-bull|Rev. Harry Foyster Bull]] at Borley on 14 June 1927.

## Identity and Family Connection

Henry Foyster was the rector of **All Saints Church, Hastings** — the same church at which his father, Henry Samuel Foyster, had served before him, and at which the grandfather shared by both Henry and Harry Bull had also served. The connection runs through the intermarriage of the Foyster and Bull clerical dynasties: [[caroline-bull|Caroline Foyster]] married [[henry-bull|Rev. Henry Dawson Bull]] in 1862, making the Foyster and Bull families cousins by descent.

This means Henry Foyster was also a relative of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]], the rector who would succeed the Smiths at Borley Rectory from 1930.

## Role at the Funeral

Harry Bull's funeral on 14 June 1927 was a large county affair with standing-room only in the small church. Henry Foyster led the service. O'Connor notes that this was particularly fitting given that the Foyster and Bull families had been intertwined since [[henry-bull|Henry Dawson Bull]] married Henry Samuel Foyster's daughter Caroline in 1862.

The Bishop of Chelmsford ([[dr-guy-warman|Dr Guy Warman]]) gave the address; the organist [[ernest-ambrose|Ernest Ambrose]] played the setting of *Nunc Dimittis*.
