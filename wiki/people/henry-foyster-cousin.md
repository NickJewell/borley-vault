# Henry Foyster (Harry Bull's cousin)

Anglican clergyman who led the funeral service for [[harry-bull|Rev. Harry Foyster Bull]] at Borley on 14 June 1927.

## Identity and Family Connection

Henry Foyster was the rector of **All Saints Church, Hastings** — the same church at which his father, [[henry-samuel-foyster|Henry Samuel Foyster]], had served before him. Henry and his cousin Harry Bull shared a grandfather who had also served as rector of All Saints in Hastings [1]. The connection runs through the intermarriage of the Foyster and Bull clerical dynasties: [[caroline-bull|Caroline Foyster]] married [[henry-bull|Rev. Henry Dawson Bull]] in 1862, making the Foyster and Bull families cousins by descent.

This means Henry Foyster was also a relative of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]], the rector who would succeed the Smiths at Borley Rectory from 1930.

## Role at the Funeral

Harry Bull's funeral on 14 June 1927 was a large county affair with standing-room only in the small church. Henry Foyster led the service, **opening by leading the congregation in the hymn "There is a Land of Pure Delight"** [1]. The choice of officiant was particularly fitting given that the Foyster and Bull families had been intertwined since [[henry-bull|Henry Dawson Bull]] married [[henry-samuel-foyster|Henry Samuel Foyster]]'s daughter Caroline in 1862 [1, 3].

The Bishop of Chelmsford ([[dr-guy-warman|Dr Guy Warman]]) gave the address; the organist [[ernest-ambrose|Ernest Ambrose]] played the setting of *Nunc Dimittis*.

There is no record in the sources of Henry Foyster having any personal experiences with the ghosts or phenomena of Borley Rectory, nor does he appear again in the narrative following this 1927 funeral service.
