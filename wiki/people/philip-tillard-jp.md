# Philip Tillard JP, of Stukely Hall

Maternal grandfather of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster]]; a Justice of the Peace whose seat was **Stukely Hall, Huntingdon**. His third daughter, [[adelaide-julia-tillard|Adelaide Julia Tillard]], married [[rev-george-alfred-foyster|Rev. George Alfred Foyster]] and became Lionel's mother.

## Significance

Robert Wood mentions Philip Tillard specifically to establish that [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] had influential and socially prominent relations on his mother's side as well as his father's. This matters for Wood's argument that Lionel was not a passive prisoner of Marianne's deceptions, but a willing accomplice:

> "Through his mother's family Lionel Algernon had another set of relations of considerable social standing and influence, at least some of whom would have helped him had he so requested during the period of his supposed imprisonment by his wife."

That Lionel never appealed to the Tillards (or to his Foyster clerical cousins) is, for Wood, evidence that he had no desire to be "rescued".
