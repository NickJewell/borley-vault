# Miss M. S. Quinn

Sub-Librarian, University of London. She was the custodian of the [[locked-book|Locked Book]] — [[sidney-glanville|S. H. Glanville]]'s 360-page Borley dossier — during the five years it was deposited in the strong-room of the Harry Price Library at the University of London.

## Custody of the Locked Book

The Locked Book was deposited by [[harry-price|Harry Price]] in January 1940. When Price withdrew it temporarily in November 1944 to use it in compiling *The End of Borley Rectory*, Quinn wrote to him confirming its condition:

> *"Dear Mr. PRICE, Under separate cover and by registered post, I am forwarding to you the locked book and its keys, from the Harry Price Library, as you request. It has been packed away in the Strong-room here since you sent it to me and has never been opened."*

The letter is dated November 29, 1944, and is quoted verbatim by Price. Its significance lies in the chain of custody: Quinn's written confirmation that the Locked Book's contents were sealed and inaccessible for five years — the Bramah-locked book had never been opened by anyone except Price and Glanville — is an important evidential element when Price later argues that the Planchette predictions recorded in the book could not have been adjusted after the 1943 excavation confirmed them.

Her brief letter is reproduced in the book precisely because it serves this authenticating function.
