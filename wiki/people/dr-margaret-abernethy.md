# Dr Margaret Abernethy

Senior partner in a medical group based at Long Melford; the family doctor to the [[rev-lanfranc-mathias|Mathias family]] during their incumbency at Borley and Liston. [[claudine-mathias|CM]] describes her as "a blunt, no-nonsense Scotswoman" — a highly credible witness.

## The Nun Sighting — 26 July 1949

Driving up Hall Lane towards Borley to visit a patient, Dr Abernethy saw the figure of a nun standing by the hedge at the point outside the rectory garden. She drove past for a few yards, then thought she should offer the nun a lift and reversed. No one was to be seen. She described the figure as **solid in appearance, not wraith-like**, and said she had a clear view of the face: the nun appeared to be **about 40 years old**. Both she and her predecessor [[rev-ac-henning]] made local enquiries to find whether any nun had been in the area that day, without success.

The sighting was published in [[peter-underwood|Underwood]] and [[paul-tabori|Tabori]]'s *The Ghosts of Borley* (1973).

She appeared in an ITV programme (*About Religion*) transmitted on **31 October 1955** describing this experience.

## The Vanishing Rosary

Not previously published before *The Final Analysis*: Dr Abernethy later drove up Hall Lane and, at exactly the same spot as the 1949 sighting, saw what appeared to be a **rosary lying shining on the road**. She stopped, got out to pick it up — and it had vanished. CM notes the Roman Catholic theme of the rosary is consistent with the broader pattern of the haunting.

## The Age of the Nun

Her estimate that the nun appeared about 40 years old is significant. It conflicts with the [[marie-lairre]] Planchette theory (which describes a young novice of about 19, murdered in 1667) but might indicate a different entity — perhaps a nun associated with the Waldegrave household, who were staunch Catholics. [[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth]] similarly estimated the nun appeared elderly during his 1970 sighting.
