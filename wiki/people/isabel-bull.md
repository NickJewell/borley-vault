# Isabel Bull

One of the daughters of the Reverend [[henry-bull|Henry Dawson Ellis Bull]] and his wife, and a sister of [[ethel-bull|Ethel Bull]], [[freda-bull|Freda Bull]], [[walter-bull|Walter Bull]], and [[gerald-bull|Gerald Bull]].

> **Contradiction:** The comprehensive genealogical records and rosters of the Bull family in multiple sources do not list any daughter by the name "Isabel" [1–4]. The Rev. Henry Dawson Ellis Bull and his wife Caroline Sarah Foyster had up to fourteen children, whose names (including Henry/Harry, Caroline/Dodie, Winifred/Freda, Alfred, Basil, Ethel, Adelaide/Mabel, Edward/Gerald, Constance, Hubert/Herbert, Emily/Elsie, Cyril, Alice/Katie, and a possible Milly) do not include an "Isabel" in any of these family lists [1–4]. The single mention of the name "Isabel" in the texts is ambiguous: "As related by Ethel Bull (one of his daughters), the Misses Isabel, Freda and Mary H. Gladwin gave full accounts to Harry Price and Sidney Glanville" [5]. This phrasing could group an "Isabel" and "Freda" together as Bull sisters alongside a separate person named Mary H. Gladwin, or it could refer to an "Isabel Gladwin" [5].

Isabel Bull is named as one of the daughters who gave full accounts of the [[the-nun|Borley Nun]] and other phenomena to [[harry-price|Harry Price]] and [[sidney-glanville|Sidney Glanville]]. She is grouped with Freda Bull and "Mary H. Gladwin" (possibly a married name of another Bull daughter) as having provided detailed testimony about life at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] during their father's incumbency — specifically regarding unexplained footsteps, taps at night, and an occasion where all twenty of the house's bells pealed out simultaneously for no apparent reason [5].

## 1900 Nun Sighting

Isabel was almost certainly one of the four sisters present at the famous July 1900 [[the-nun|Nun]] sighting. On a July afternoon three sisters returning from a garden party entered by the lower gate and immediately glimpsed a dark female figure gliding with bowed head towards the little stream along the [[nuns-walk|Nun's Walk]]. Two sisters stopped by the large summer-house and watched, while a third ran into the house to fetch a fourth sister. All four saw the figure; the fourth advanced to intercept it, whereupon the apparition turned its face toward the Rectory and vanished.

> **Contradiction:** [[ethel-bull|Ethel Bull]] later told Underwood that [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s published account was "not entirely accurate" — the figure was seen in poor light at about a hundred yards; no features were distinguishable and no white or light colouring was noticeable. Nevertheless, all four sisters told "identical, consistent stories of the experience for over fifty years."

## Later Life

Isabel and surviving siblings settled at [[chilton-lodge|Chilton Lodge]], Great Cornard, where [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] visited them on several occasions. She died before 1963 (when the last surviving sister, Constance, died, leaving about £50,000).
