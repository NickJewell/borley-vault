# Mabel Bull

Daughter of [[henry-bull]], the builder of Borley Rectory. One of four sisters who simultaneously witnessed the apparition of the nun on July 28, 1900 — the canonical event in the Borley legend.

## The 1900 Nun Sighting

On July 28, 1900, the Misses Ethel, Freda, and Mabel Bull were returning home from a garden party in full daylight. Entering the property at the postern gate at the end of the garden, all three simultaneously saw a female figure with bowed head, dressed entirely in black in the garb of a nun, slowly gliding — rather than walking — along the [[nuns-walk]] in the direction of the stream.

> "Without knowing why, the three girls felt terrified. The figure was slowly gliding — rather than walking — along the Nun's Walk in the direction of the stream... She looked intensely sad and ill."

All three were frightened by the strangeness of the figure's movement. One of them ran into the house to fetch their sister [[elsie-bull]]. Elsie initially dismissed it as nonsense, ran toward the figure — but when the figure stopped and turned to face her with "an expression of intense grief," Elsie froze on the lawn, terrified. The figure then vanished.

[[harry-price]] cites this four-witness simultaneous sighting as one of the strongest pieces of evidence for the objective reality of the apparition: the figure was seen collectively, behaved in a way that frightened observers who had no prior belief in the haunting, and vanished definitively.

## Wider Context

Mabel is listed in Appendix D of *The Most Haunted House in England* among the witnesses to "the Nun figure in its different forms." She is one of the few witnesses to the central Borley apparition who saw it in broad daylight, in a group, under conditions that all but exclude suggestion or individual hallucination.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Adelaide Mabel Bull** (frequently referred to simply as Mabel) was a daughter of the Rev. Henry Dawson Ellis Bull and Caroline Sarah Foyster. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following biographical details, events, and contradictions regarding her life and involvement in the haunting:

**Biographical Details and the Gardiner Rhyme**
Adelaide Mabel was born in 1868 and was formally baptized on **Christmas Day, December 25, 1868** [1, 2]. The texts record that she died in **1936** [1, 3, 4]. 

A lesser-known biographical detail about her lifestyle is preserved in a poem composed in the early part of the twentieth century by Mildred and May Gardiner, who sat near the Bull family in Borley Church [5]. The rhyme characterizes her as an independent woman, noting: 
> **"Mabel was the third one who was not often at home, / She had her digs in London where often she would roam"** [5]. 

**The July 28, 1900 "Nun" Sighting and Contradictions**
Mabel is historically tied to the most famous collective sighting of the Borley phantom nun, which took place in the Rectory garden during the twilight hours of July 28, 1900 [6, 7]. However, the sources present a specific contradiction regarding her exact presence during the event:
*   Several accounts state that **Ethel, Freda, and Mabel** were returning from a garden party when they saw the nun, prompting one of them to run into the house to fetch either their sister Dodie or Elsie [6, 8]. 
*   Conversely, another detailed account omits Mabel completely, explicitly stating that it was **Ethel, Freda, and Elsie** who saw the nun before fetching Dodie [9]. 

**The Blue Room Seance (June 12–13, 1929)**
Mabel played a direct role during Harry Price's first investigative visit to Borley. On the night of June 12–13, 1929, Mabel (recorded in Price's notes as "Adelaide (also known as Mabel)") and her sister Ethel traveled from their home at Chilton Lodge to join Harry Price, Lucie Kaye, reporter Vernon Wall, and the Smiths for an impromptu seance in the infamous Blue Room [10]. 

During this sitting:
*   **The Tapping Mirror:** The sisters questioned a "tapping mirror" that claimed to be the spirit of their late brother, the Rev. Harry Bull [10, 11]. Believing their brother's widow had cheated them out of their inheritance, the sisters asked the entity specific, leading questions, including: **"Is there money trouble?"** and **"Were you killed?"** (to which the mirror tapped 'Yes') [11, 12]. 
*   **The Jumping Soap:** Mabel was present for the dramatic conclusion of the seance when a cake of soap inexplicably leaped from the washstand and struck a china water jug on the floor [13, 14]. Although Price reported that everyone witnessed the phenomenon, Mabel's sister Ethel later contradicted this, insisting that **"We were none of us in the room when the piece of soap jumped"** [3].
