# Mabel Bull (Adelaide Mabel Bull, 1868–1936)

Daughter of [[henry-bull|Rev. Henry Dawson Ellis Bull]] and Caroline Sarah Foyster; one of the Bull sisters who claimed to see the [[the-nun|phantom nun]] on July 28, 1900. She was formally baptized on **Christmas Day, December 25, 1868**, and died in **1936**.

A lesser-known biographical detail is preserved in a poem composed by Mildred and May Gardiner, who sat near the Bull family in [[borley-church|Borley Church]]: **"Mabel was the third one who was not often at home, / She had her digs in London where often she would roam"** — characterising her as an independent woman who kept her own rooms in London.

## The 1900 Nun Sighting and Contradictions

On July 28, 1900, the Misses Ethel, Freda, and Mabel Bull were returning home from a garden party. Entering the property at the postern gate at the end of the garden, they reportedly simultaneously saw a female figure with bowed head, dressed entirely in black in the garb of a nun, slowly gliding — rather than walking — along the [[nuns-walk]] in the direction of the stream.

> "Without knowing why, the three girls felt terrified. The figure was slowly gliding — rather than walking — along the Nun's Walk in the direction of the stream... She looked intensely sad and ill."

One of them ran into the house to fetch their sister [[elsie-bull|Elsie]]. Elsie initially dismissed it as nonsense, ran toward the figure — but when the figure stopped and turned to face her with "an expression of intense grief," Elsie froze on the lawn, terrified. The figure then vanished.

> **Contradiction:** Several accounts state that Ethel, Freda, and Mabel were the initial witnesses who then fetched Elsie or Dodie. Conversely, another detailed account omits Mabel completely, explicitly stating it was Ethel, Freda, and Elsie who saw the nun before fetching Dodie.

[[harry-price|Harry Price]] cites this four-witness simultaneous sighting as one of the strongest pieces of evidence for the objective reality of the apparition: the figure was seen collectively, behaved in a way that frightened observers who had no prior belief in the haunting, and vanished definitively.

## The Blue Room Seance (June 12–13, 1929)

Mabel (recorded in Price's notes as "Adelaide (also known as Mabel)") and her sister [[ethel-bull|Ethel]] travelled from their home at [[chilton-lodge|Chilton Lodge]] to join [[harry-price|Harry Price]], [[lucie-kaye|Lucie Kaye]], reporter [[vc-wall|Vernon Wall]], and the Smiths for an impromptu séance in the [[blue-room|Blue Room]] on the night of Price's first investigative visit.

During this sitting:

- **The Tapping Mirror:** The sisters questioned a "tapping mirror" that claimed to be the spirit of their late brother, the Rev. [[harry-bull|Harry Bull]]. Believing their brother's widow had cheated them out of their inheritance, the sisters asked the entity specific, leading questions, including: **"Is there money trouble?"** and **"Were you killed?"** — to which the mirror reportedly tapped 'Yes.'
- **The Jumping Soap:** Mabel was reportedly present for the dramatic conclusion of the séance when a cake of soap inexplicably leaped from the washstand and struck a china water jug on the floor. Although Price reported that everyone witnessed the phenomenon, Mabel's sister Ethel later contradicted this, insisting that **"We were none of us in the room when the piece of soap jumped."**

## Wider Context

Mabel is listed in Appendix D of *The Most Haunted House in England* among the witnesses to "the Nun figure in its different forms." She is one of the few witnesses to the central Borley apparition who allegedly saw it in broad daylight (or twilight — see [[ethel-bull]] for the contradictions in lighting conditions), in a group, under conditions that all but exclude suggestion or individual hallucination.
