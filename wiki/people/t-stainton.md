# T. Stainton

Christ Church, Oxford. Official observer at Borley Rectory; visited December 15–17, 1937 with [[joseph-burden|Joseph W. Burden]]. Witnessed wailing sounds and dragging noises alongside Burden, and conducted the cat behavioural experiment using Mr. Arbon's borrowed animal.

## Experiences

On the night of December 16–17, 1937, Stainton independently confirmed:

- The dragging sound at 12:10 a.m. ("too loud to be mistaken") — he "thinks he heard sounds down the lower north passage" but was "not certain of that exact location."
- The prolonged wailing sound from 1:30 to 1:42 a.m. that "seemed to be coming from the Blue Room but penetrated everywhere."

He also conducted the cat experiment during his watch: brought [[mr-and-mrs-arbon|Arbon]]'s cat into the Rectory to test its reactions in areas of known psychic activity. His observation that the cat "seemed particularly interested in Room 7" and "rubbed her back against the strange coat" (the mysterious coat apport) was noted by [[joseph-burden|Burden]].

Stainton's black thread, stretched across a doorway to detect passage, was found broken at 4:05 a.m. on December 16 — though Burden notes "Stainton claims cat did not pass it as he watched her during her tour," leaving its breakage unexplained.

Stainton left on the morning of December 19, after which Burden remained alone.

Appendix D lists Stainton under "Footsteps and Similar Sounds," "Dragging Noise," and "Wailing Sounds."
