# T. Stainton

Undergraduate student at Christ Church, Oxford [1, 2]. Official observer at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]]; visited December 15–19, 1937 with fellow Christ Church undergraduate [[joseph-burden|J. W. Burden]]. Witnessed wailing sounds and dragging noises alongside Burden, and conducted the cat behavioural experiment using [[mr-and-mrs-arbon|Mr. Arbon]]'s borrowed animal.

## Visit and Methodology

Stainton and Burden arrived at the Rectory on **December 15, 1937**, conducted a **"very thorough inspection"** of the property, and sealed the doors [2]. As a physical control measure, they set a trap by tying a **black thread across the passage between Rooms No. 9 and 10** [2]. Stainton left early on the morning of December 19, leaving Burden to continue the vigil alone [3].

## Auditory Phenomena

On the night of December 16–17, 1937, Stainton independently confirmed:

- A variety of unexplained noises including **shuffling footsteps, swishing garments, and faint whining or hooting** [1].
- At **12:10 a.m.**, a loud dragging sound lasting two seconds at two-second intervals. Stainton was standing opposite the door of Room 8 and noted that he "thinks he heard sounds down the lower north passage" but was "not certain of that exact location" [4].
- Between 1:30 and 1:42 a.m., a **"continuous wailing sound coming from the Blue Room"** [1] — described as so "subtle and penetrating that it could be heard all over the house" [1].

Appendix D lists Stainton under "Footsteps and Similar Sounds," "Dragging Noise," and "Wailing Sounds."

## The Broken Thread and the Cat Experiment

Stainton and Burden borrowed a cat from the caretaker, [[mr-and-mrs-arbon|Mr. Arbon]], and tempted it into areas of known psychic activity using condensed milk [5]. They observed that the cat was frightened by moonlight on the floor, rubbed her back against the mysterious "strange coat" as if recognising an old friend, and showed particular interest in Room No. 7 [5].

At **4:05 a.m. on December 18**, the investigators discovered that the black thread trap between Rooms 9 and 10 had been broken [4]. While Burden initially assumed the cat had snapped it, **Stainton explicitly contradicted this, claiming the cat had not passed the thread because he had been actively watching the animal during her tour of the house** [4] — leaving its breakage unexplained.
