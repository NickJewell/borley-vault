# T. Stainton

Christ Church, Oxford. Official observer at Borley Rectory; visited December 15–17, 1937 with [[joseph-burden|Joseph W. Burden]]. Witnessed wailing sounds and dragging noises alongside Burden, and conducted the cat behavioural experiment using Mr. Arbon's borrowed animal.

## Experiences

On the night of December 16–17, 1937, Stainton independently confirmed:

- The dragging sound at 12:10 a.m. ("too loud to be mistaken") — he "thinks he heard sounds down the lower north passage" but was "not certain of that exact location."
- The prolonged wailing sound from 1:30 to 1:42 a.m. that "seemed to be coming from the Blue Room but penetrated everywhere."

He also conducted the cat experiment during his watch: brought [[mr-and-mrs-arbon|Arbon]]'s cat into the Rectory to test its reactions in areas of known psychic activity. His observation that the cat "seemed particularly interested in Room 7" and "rubbed her back against the strange coat" (the mysterious coat apport) was noted by [[joseph-burden|Burden]].

Stainton's black thread, stretched across a doorway to detect passage, was found broken at 4:05 a.m. on December 16 — though Burden notes "Stainton claims cat did not pass it as he watched her during her tour," leaving its breakage unexplained.

Stainton left on the morning of December 19, after which Burden remained alone.

Appendix D lists Stainton under "Footsteps and Similar Sounds," "Dragging Noise," and "Wailing Sounds."

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**T. Stainton** was an **undergraduate student at Christ Church, Oxford**, who served as an official observer during Harry Price's tenancy investigation of Borley Rectory [1, 2]. He conducted his investigation alongside a fellow Christ Church undergraduate, J. Burden [1]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following specific details, dates, and events regarding his involvement:

**Visit Dates and Methodology**
* Stainton and Burden visited Borley Rectory between **December 15 and December 19, 1937** [2]. Stainton left the Rectory to return home early on the morning of December 19, leaving Burden to continue the vigil alone [3]. 
* Upon arriving at the house, the two men conducted a **"very thorough inspection" of the property and sealed the doors** [2]. 
* As a physical control measure, they set a trap by tying a **black thread across the passage between Rooms No. 9 and 10** [2]. 

**Auditory Phenomena**
* Stainton and Burden reported hearing a wide variety of inexplicable noises during their stay, including **shuffling footsteps, swishing garments, and faint whining or hooting** [1]. 
* They also documented a **"continuous wailing sound coming from the Blue Room"**, which they described as being so "subtle and penetrating that it could be heard all over the house" [1]. 
* At **12:10 a.m. on December 18**, a loud noise of something being dragged along the floor was heard, lasting for two seconds at two-second intervals [4]. At the time, Stainton was standing opposite the door of Room 8 and noted that he thought he heard the sounds coming down the lower north passage, though he was not entirely certain of the exact location [4]. 

**The Broken Thread and the Cat Experiment**
* A lesser-known detail of Stainton's investigation involved experimenting with an animal to see if it would react to psychic influences. Stainton and Burden borrowed a cat from the caretaker, Mr. Arbon, and tempted it into areas of known psychic activity using condensed milk [5]. They observed that the cat was frightened by moonlight on the floor, rubbed her back against the mysterious "strange coat" as if recognizing an old friend, and showed a particular interest in Room No. 7, while the other rooms did not seem to affect her [5]. 
* At **4:05 a.m. on December 18**, the investigators discovered that the black thread trap between Rooms 9 and 10 had been broken [4]. While Burden initially assumed the cat had snapped it, **Stainton explicitly contradicted this, claiming the cat had not passed the thread because he had been actively watching the animal during her tour of the house** [4].
