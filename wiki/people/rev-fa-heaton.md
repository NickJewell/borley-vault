# Rev. F. A. Heaton

Vicar of St Michael's Vicarage, Tenterden, Kent. In a letter of **25 January 1941**, Heaton made one of the more perceptive early observations about the [[wall-writings|Borley wall-messages]], offering detailed analytical interpretations of the famous "Marianne" writings as a correspondent of [[harry-price|Harry Price]].

## Analysis of the Writing Method

Heaton suggested that the physical appearance of the script provided clues to how it was executed. He proposed the messages were written by someone **"either (a) when they were not looking at what they were doing; or (b) in the dark"** [1]. He based this on the observation that the letters were **"all joined in one continuous line, as if the person could not take the pencil off lest they should put it down to continue in the wrong place"** [1], and that the overlapping "writing over writing" likely occurred because **"the writer could not see and did get muddled"** [1].

He proposed that the writer was "imprisoned in the dark" and that the cry for "light" was therefore literal — the plea of someone writing in absolute darkness, who desperately needed light. This reading — the writing as the act of someone confined in darkness — gives an additional layer of pathos to the wall-messages and is consistent with [[canon-phythian-adams|Canon Phythian-Adams]]'s later analysis of the messages as showing physical constraint, confusion, and urgency.

## Specific Textual Interpretations

Heaton challenged several of Price's transcriptions of the wall-writings, offering the following specific corrections:

- **The "Bottom Well":** Regarding a message Price read as *'Marianne-at-Get-Help-entant-Bottom-Me'*, Heaton suggested it should read **'... Bottom-We'**, indicating the "bottom well" — noting that **"the line of the e, leading up like it does, suggests another letter to follow"** [1]. This reading converged independently with [[canon-phythian-adams|Canon Phythian-Adams]]'s "Well-Tank-Bottom-Me" interpretation, which Price regarded as the correct decipherment.
- **"And Open":** He suggested that a message read as *'and O'* was actually meant to be **'and open'** [1].
- **The Inverted "Light":** He noticed that certain **"odd marks"** in a message appearing near the Blue Room on 16 June 1931 looked **"very near looking like 'light' again"** when the image was turned upside down [1, 2]. This interpretation converged independently with that of [[he-adshead|H. E. Adshead]].
- **Ancient Script:** He observed that the bottom word in one message reminded him of **"ancient script, with the delight they seemed to take in 'flourishes'"** [1].

## Theological Correction

Finally, Heaton pointed out a specific ecclesiastical inaccuracy in the phantom's requests. Addressing the repeated appeals for a "light Mass," he provided the quote: **"There is no such usage as 'light Mass,' though there is, of course, 'low Mass'"** [1].
