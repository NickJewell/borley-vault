# Constance Price (née Constance Mary Knight, 1881–c.1969)

Widow of [[harry-price|Harry Price]]. Childhood sweetheart turned long-term wife; they grew up together at New Cross in south-east London. Married at Pulborough Church; lived next door for a year before building "Arun Bank" on the River Arun, moving in around 1907–1909.

## Character and Domestic Life

Harry Price's home: goldfish in an octagonal pond (a heron eventually ate them all), a Scottie dog, a workshop with the [[borley-rectory|Borley]] bell over the door. Constance was deaf and used the bell occasionally to summon her brother and sister-in-law next door. At 87 (October 1968) she still lived alone with many relatives nearby. The house still felt as though Price "had just popped out for a moment" — his coats, hats, and walking sticks still at the rack by the front door.

## Harry Price's Death

Price died on **28 March 1948** in his study chair. He had posted a letter, returned home, and sat down. Constance went to make tea; when she returned he was dead. She was "inundated with letters, literally hundreds of them" in the days after his death.

## On the SPR Attack

Constance had not read the [[the-borley-report|SPR book]] (1956): her Midland Bank trustee advised against it and she complied. On [[kathleen-goldney|Mollie Goldney]] and [[eric-dingwall|Eric Dingwall]]: "I think Mrs Goldney was a two-faced woman and Eric Dingwall was no better. They had often been there and she had looked on them as friends." On the unnamed man who had been "snooping about" (almost certainly [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]]): "asking the rector and other people questions about what kind of man Harry had been."

She always liked [[sidney-glanville|Sidney Glanville]].

## *Borley III*

Price had "just written the first chapter for a third book on haunted Borley Rectory and drafted out what the book would contain." She did not know what would happen to it.

## The Borley Bell

Constance used the bell hung over the workshop to summon family. After her death, the University of London and Midland Bank (executors) presented the bell to [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]], who hung it in his garden.

## Underwood's Visit

Underwood sat in Price's favourite study chair at Constance's invitation. She served exquisite sherry on a silver tray on the balcony overlooking the River Arun and the Sussex Downs.
