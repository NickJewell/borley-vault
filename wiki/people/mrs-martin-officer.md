# Mrs Martin

An officer's wife who was staying with the Rev. and Mrs [[rev-ac-henning|Henning]] at Liston Rectory in September 1943.

## Cold Spot Experience

Mrs Martin went to look at the ruins of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] one Sunday evening on her way to [[borley-church|church]], with no prior knowledge of the "cold spot" or of what the relevant experience felt like. While there, she experienced a sudden and intense thermal drop, becoming **"cold all over."**

Her reaction directly mirrored the famous "Cold Spot" phenomenon previously recorded at the Rectory, specifically echoing the extreme chill experienced by [[miss-reid|Miss Alice Reid]] in August 1937. Crucially, Mrs Martin's experience was entirely unprimed: she had not read [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s published book on the hauntings, and had absolutely no prior knowledge of Miss Reid's experience until the Hennings informed her of the parallel afterward.

Henning documented the event in a letter to Price dated **September 15, 1943**:

> "An officer's wife (Mrs Martin) who is staying with us, went to look at the Rectory on Sunday night on the way to the church, and became cold all over, as Miss Reid did. She had not read your book, and did not know of Miss Reid's experience until we told her later."

## Significance

The "cold spot" on the first-floor landing outside the [[blue-room|Blue Room]] had by this point been experienced by numerous independent visitors, and the [[cambridge-commission|Cambridge Commission]]'s measurements in June 1943 had confirmed that the temperature anomaly was physically real, not imagined. Mrs Martin's experience in September 1943, made in ignorance of all prior accounts, provides one more independent data point.

The significance of her experience, like that of [[miss-reid|Miss Reid]] in 1937 and [[rupert-haig|Rupert Haig]] in 1937, lies precisely in its independence: she was not expecting it, had not read about it, and had it anyway (single witness, unprimed).

The available sources contain no further biographical details regarding Mrs Martin beyond this single recorded encounter.
