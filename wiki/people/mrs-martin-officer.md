# Mrs Martin

An officer's wife who was staying with the Rev. and Mrs [[rev-ac-henning|Henning]] at Liston Rectory in September 1943. Mentioned by Henning in a letter of September 15, 1943.

## Cold Spot Experience

Mrs Martin went to look at the Rectory one Sunday evening on her way to church, with no prior knowledge of the "cold spot" or of what the relevant experience felt like.

Henning wrote: *"An officer's wife (Mrs Martin) who is staying with us, went to look at the Rectory on Sunday night on the way to the church, and became cold all over, as Miss Reid did. She had not read your book, and did not know of Miss Reid's experience until we told her later."*

The [[cold-spot|cold spot]] on the first-floor landing outside the [[blue-room|Blue Room]] had by this point been experienced by numerous independent visitors, and the [[cambridge-commission|Cambridge Commission]]'s measurements in June 1943 had confirmed that the temperature anomaly was physically real, not imagined. Mrs Martin's experience in September 1943, made in ignorance of all prior accounts, provides one more independent data point.

The significance of Mrs Martin's experience, like that of [[miss-reid|Miss Reid]] in 1937 and [[rupert-haig|Rupert Haig]] in 1937, lies precisely in its independence: she was not expecting it, had not read about it, and had it anyway.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Mrs. Martin** appears in the Borley sources briefly as an "officer's wife" who experienced an unprompted physical phenomenon while visiting the ruins of Borley Rectory [1]. At the time, she was staying as a guest of the Rev. Alfred Clifford Henning (the rector of Borley-cum-Liston) [1].

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific details, dates, and quotes regarding her involvement:

**The Incident and Date**
Mrs. Martin's experience was documented in a letter sent by the Rev. Henning to investigator Harry Price, dated **September 15, 1943** [1]. 
*   According to the letter, on the previous **Sunday night**, Mrs. Martin was on her way to Borley Church and decided to detour to look at the remains of the Rectory [1]. 
*   While there, she experienced a sudden and intense thermal drop, becoming **"cold all over"** [1].

**The "Blind" Experience and Miss Reid**
Her reaction was notable to investigators because it directly mirrored the famous "Cold Spot" phenomenon previously recorded at the Rectory, specifically echoing an extreme, terrifying chill experienced by another visitor, Miss Alice Reid, in August 1937 [1]. 
*   Crucially, Mrs. Martin's experience was entirely unprimed: she had not read Harry Price's published book on the hauntings, and she had absolutely no prior knowledge of Miss Reid's experience until the Hennings informed her of the parallel afterward [1].

**Direct Quote**
The Rev. Henning detailed the event to Price with the following specific quote:
> **"An officer’s wife (Mrs Martin) who is staying with us, went to look at the Rectory on Sunday night on the way to the church, and became cold all over, as Miss Reid did. She had not read your book, and did not know of Miss Reid’s experience until we told her later."** [1].

The available sources contain no further biographical details, events, or contradictions regarding Mrs. Martin beyond this single recorded encounter.
