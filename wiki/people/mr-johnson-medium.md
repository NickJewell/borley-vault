# Mr Johnson (medium)

The professional medium who conducted the séance at Borley Rectory on August 11, 1931, during the [[marianne-foyster|Foyster]] incumbency. Attended at the invitation of [[wj-braithwaite|W. John Braithwaite]] alongside [[ernest-meads|Ernest Meads]] and with the Rev. [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] present.

## The Séance

Johnson employed the technique of successive entity impersonation — assuming the personality of each communicating "spirit" in turn. His "spirit controls" were a **Red Indian** and a **"Dr. Haslam of Harley Street"** (deceased), through whom he made the initial diagnoses and set the stage.

The entities who communicated through Johnson during the four-hour séance (6 p.m. to 10 p.m.) were:

1. **"Dr. Haslam"**: Diagnosed Foyster as suffering from rheumatism; advised avoiding potatoes, tapioca, sago, and starchy foods generally; noted that Foyster suffered from depression (which Foyster admitted was accurate)
2. **"Joe Miles"**: A drunken ex-publican; called for "drinks all round"; could not be persuaded he was dead; associated with the Rectory disturbances in some vague way
3. **"Mary"**: A young woman who said she had gone to London, had a baby, and murdered it; described being turned out of the village in the rain and sleeping under a hedge; "felt very rebellious"
4. **"John Wesley"**: Described by Braithwaite as "very well done, and much better than the ordinary sermon, and much simpler, and in good language and taste" — five minutes that impressed the sitters
5. **"Cardinal Newman"**: The séance's closing "entity"

## Significance

The "Mary" entity is potentially significant: a woman who murdered a child and was driven from the village, seeking rest and forgiveness. This is distinct from [[marie-lairre|Marie Lairre]], [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella Waldegrave]], and "Evangeline Westcott" — the other named nun-candidates — and represents a fourth non-nun possibility for the haunting. Price files this as one of the "four claimants to the honour of haunting Borley," alongside the séance at Marks Tey.

Whether this "Mary" is connected to [[kate-boreham|Katie Boreham]] or to any other documented historical person has not been established.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Mr. Johnson** was a Spiritualist medium brought to Borley Rectory during the Foyster incumbency to investigate and potentially stop the severe poltergeist disturbances. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following biographical details, specific events, quotes, and contradictions regarding his involvement:

**Biographical Details and Background**
*   **Appearance and Age:** In 1931, Johnson was described as being **sixty-seven years old, short, stubby, tanned, and wearing glasses** [1].
*   **Occupation:** Outside of his mediumship, he worked as a **masseur** and possessed **"some medical knowledge"** [1]. 
*   **Demeanor:** The psychical researcher W. John Braithwaite, who brought him to the Rectory, personally found the medium to be **"very simple minded"** [1]. 

**The August 1931 Library Séance**
Mr. Johnson's entire contribution to the Borley literature centers on a single, marathon séance he conducted at the Rectory alongside Braithwaite and an interrogator named Ernest Meads. 
*   **Chronological Contradiction:** The exact date of this séance is recorded contradictorily in the sources; some accounts place it on **August 11, 1931** [2, 3], while others firmly date it as Wednesday, **August 12, 1931** [1, 4].
*   **The Trance:** After declaring the house had a "haunted atmosphere," Johnson anticipated the sitting in the library would last only half an hour, but it ultimately stretched to an exhausting **four hours** [1, 5]. During this time, he fell into a trance and acted as a channel for numerous spirits, assuming their different personalities [2, 3, 6].

**Spirit Guides and Communicators**
Johnson utilized two primary spirit "guides" or "controls":
*   **The Indian:** A Native American guide who acted as a **"sort of chucker out"** for the spirit world [3, 7, 8].
*   **Dr. Haslam:** A deceased Harley Street doctor who utilized Johnson's medical knowledge to correctly diagnose the Rev. Lionel Foyster's rheumatism and depression, advising the rector to **"avoid potatoes, and starchy foods, such as tapioca and sago"** [3, 6].

Through Johnson, several other distinct entities manifested:
*   A local girl who confessed to murdering her child, and a village idiot named "Silly Billy" [6].
*   **"Joe Miles,"** a drunken ex-publican who constantly asked for a drink and who promised to stop causing disturbances at the Rectory [6, 8]. 
*   Historical religious figures, including **Cardinal Newman** and **John Wesley**, the latter of whom delivered an impressive five-minute sermon through Johnson in "good language and taste" [9]. 

**Quotes and Conclusions on Marianne Foyster**
Marianne Foyster initially refused to take part in the sitting, but after Johnson encouraged the group to pray for the late Harry Bull, she suddenly collapsed down the stairs in a fit [1, 5]. Upon recovering, she claimed to have been in a trance herself [5]. 

This incident led Johnson to a highly specific conclusion regarding the haunting:
*   He became **"absolutely convinced"** that Marianne was a very advanced psychic medium [5, 10]. 
*   He concluded that the spirits were drawn specifically to her, and that she was the unconscious author of everything happening in the house; if she could control her emotions and hysteria, the phenomena would cease [10]. 
*   He solemnly impressed this responsibility upon her with a direct quote: **"Thou hast a door through which more might come to light. Why dost thou keep it closed?"** [5]. 

**Pseudonym in the Foyster Manuscript**
In the Rev. Lionel Foyster's written manuscript detailing the haunting (*Fifteen Months in a Haunted House*), the rector heavily disguised the names of the participants in this séance [8, 11]. Consequently, Mr. Johnson the medium is recorded in Foyster's text under the pseudonym **"Mr. Thompson"** [7, 8].
