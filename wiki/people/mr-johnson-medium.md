# Mr Johnson (medium)

The professional medium who conducted the séance at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] on August 11, 1931 (also recorded in some sources as August 12), during the [[marianne-foyster|Foyster]] incumbency. In 1931 Johnson was sixty-seven years old, short, stubby, tanned, and wearing glasses. Outside his mediumship he worked as a masseur and possessed "some medical knowledge." Attended at the invitation of [[wj-braithwaite|W. John Braithwaite]] — who personally found him "very simple minded" — alongside [[ernest-meads|Ernest Meads]] and with the Rev. [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] present.

> **Contradiction:** The exact date of this séance is recorded contradictorily in the sources; some accounts place it on August 11, 1931, while others firmly date it as Wednesday, August 12, 1931.

After declaring the house had a "haunted atmosphere," Johnson anticipated the sitting in the library would last only half an hour; it ultimately stretched to an exhausting four hours (6 p.m. to 10 p.m.). In the Rev. Lionel Foyster's manuscript *Fifteen Months in a Haunted House*, Johnson appears under the pseudonym **"Mr. Thompson."**

## Spirit Guides and Communicators

Johnson employed two primary spirit "guides" or "controls":

- **A Red Indian:** a Native American guide who acted as a "sort of chucker out" for the spirit world
- **"Dr. Haslam of Harley Street" (deceased):** who utilised Johnson's medical knowledge to correctly diagnose Foyster's rheumatism and depression, advising the rector to "avoid potatoes, and starchy foods, such as tapioca and sago"

The entities who communicated through Johnson during the séance were:

1. **"Joe Miles"**: a drunken ex-publican; called for "drinks all round"; could not be persuaded he was dead; associated with the Rectory disturbances in some vague way
2. **"Mary"**: a young woman who said she had gone to London, had a baby, and murdered it; described being turned out of the village in the rain and sleeping under a hedge; "felt very rebellious"
3. **"John Wesley"**: described by Braithwaite as "very well done, and much better than the ordinary sermon, and much simpler, and in good language and taste" — five minutes that impressed the sitters
4. **"Cardinal Newman"**: the séance's closing "entity"
5. A local girl who confessed to murdering her child, and a village idiot named "Silly Billy"

## Marianne Foyster's Collapse

[[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] initially refused to take part in the sitting, but after Johnson encouraged the group to pray for the late [[harry-bull|Harry Bull]], she suddenly collapsed down the stairs in a fit. Upon recovering, she claimed to have been in a trance herself.

This incident led Johnson to a highly specific conclusion: he became "absolutely convinced" that Marianne was a very advanced psychic medium and that the spirits were drawn specifically to her — that she was the unconscious author of everything happening in the house. If she could control her emotions and hysteria, the phenomena would cease. He impressed this responsibility upon her with the words: **"Thou hast a door through which more might come to light. Why dost thou keep it closed?"**

## Significance

The "Mary" entity is potentially significant: a woman who murdered a child and was driven from the village, seeking rest and forgiveness. This is distinct from [[marie-lairre|Marie Lairre]], [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella Waldegrave]], and "Evangeline Westcott" — the other named nun-candidates — and represents a fourth non-nun possibility for the haunting. [[harry-price|Price]] files this as one of the "four claimants to the honour of haunting Borley," alongside the séance at [[marks-tey-spiritualist-circle|Marks Tey]].

Whether this "Mary" is connected to [[kate-boreham|Katie Boreham]] or to any other documented historical person has not been established.
