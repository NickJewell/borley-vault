# Dr Letitia Fairfield, CBE, MD, DPH

A barrister-at-law and medical doctor who was a member of the [[ghost-club|Ghost Club]] and participated in the May 1960 Ghost Club discussion of the Borley case with Peter Underwood. She was qualified as a barrister in addition to her medical credentials, a combination that gave her a distinctly forensic approach to evidence.

## Ghost Club Discussion (11 May 1960)

On 11 May 1960, Fairfield participated in a Ghost Club discussion about the Borley haunting and the [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]] occupancy in particular, alongside [[miss-christina-hole|Miss Christina Hole]] and Peter Underwood.

Fairfield "seemed to be impressed with the critics' case" and cited what she described as "some unpublished evidence regarding Marianne." This mainly involved irregularities in [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s various marriages. Underwood and Tabori's response was that "these charges, proven or unproven, could make no difference to the Borley hauntings" as a whole.

Fairfield replied that it "certainly made a difference to the acceptability of the Foysters' evidence" — a position consistent with the [[the-borley-report|SPR Report]]'s skeptical framework. She agreed with [[miss-christina-hole|Miss Hole]] that the skeptics' case was largely concerned with putting [[harry-price|Harry Price]] into the dock as "a sort of secondary or unconscious accomplice of Marianne."

## Assessment

Underwood and Tabori recorded her position without endorsing it, concluding: "by and large, the 'mysteries of Marianne' were recorded without much reliance being placed on them — they form part of the immensely varied and rich human tapestry that is the background of Borley."

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Dr. Letitia Fairfield, CBE, MD, DPH**, was a barrister-at-law and the **Senior Medical Officer of the London County Council** [1, 2]. She is also notable for being **one of the first qualified female doctors in Britain** [3]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following specific facts, events, and quotes regarding her involvement with the case:

**The 1939 Correspondence and Critique of the Borley Legend**
Following a lantern lecture on Borley Rectory given by Harry Price to the Ghost Club on April 18, 1939, which was subsequently reported in *The Times*, Dr. Fairfield initiated a public correspondence challenging the historical validity of the famous Borley legends [2, 3]. 

She astutely pointed out major anachronisms and myths within the story:
*   The presence of a convent and a nun placed the tale before the Reformation, yet **coaches were unknown in England until after 1550** [3]. 
*   She stated that the idea of **bricking up individuals alive as a punishment had long been proved to be mythical** [3, 4]. 
*   She humorously concluded that the doomed monk and nun **"seem to have been singularly ill-fitted for the religious life"** [3].

In an amusing and sarcastic remark regarding Price's findings, she expressed fear that the **"unscrupulous phantoms"** had been pulling Price’s leg, protesting that it was unthinkable that Price **"has been pulling ours"** [3]. 

Price responded to what he called her "good-natured criticisms" by claiming she had jumped to conclusions, asserting that the legend was not his own invention and that he did not actually believe in spirits [3, 4]. He did, however, concede that Dr. Fairfield was **"probably right about the bricking-up of nuns, and undoubtedly right about there being no 'black coaches'"** at the time the supposed monastery flourished [4]. 

**The 1960 Ghost Club Discussion and Marianne Foyster**
Decades later, on **May 11, 1960**, Dr. Fairfield participated in a Ghost Club discussion alongside writer Christina Hole and investigator Peter Underwood, focusing specifically on the Borley haunting during the Foyster incumbency [1]. 

During this debate, Dr. Fairfield expressed that she was impressed with the highly skeptical case put forward by the SPR critics [5]. She cited unpublished evidence regarding irregularities and scandals in Marianne Foyster's various marriages [5]. When her opponents argued that Marianne's marital irregularities made no difference to the reality of the Borley hauntings, Dr. Fairfield firmly countered that **"it certainly made a difference to the acceptability of the Foysters' evidence"** [5].

**Other Appearances**
In addition to her Ghost Club debate, Dr. Fairfield is also recorded as having attended an extensive 100-slide lecture on the Borley haunting delivered by Peter Underwood at Essex Hall in London [6].
