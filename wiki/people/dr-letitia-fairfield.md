# Dr Letitia Fairfield, CBE, MD, DPH

A barrister-at-law and medical doctor who was a member of the [[ghost-club|Ghost Club]] and participated in the May 1960 Ghost Club discussion of the Borley case with Peter Underwood. She was qualified as a barrister in addition to her medical credentials, a combination that gave her a distinctly forensic approach to evidence.

## Ghost Club Discussion (11 May 1960)

On 11 May 1960, Fairfield participated in a Ghost Club discussion about the Borley haunting and the [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]] occupancy in particular, alongside [[miss-christina-hole|Miss Christina Hole]] and Peter Underwood.

Fairfield "seemed to be impressed with the critics' case" and cited what she described as "some unpublished evidence regarding Marianne." This mainly involved irregularities in [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s various marriages. Underwood and Tabori's response was that "these charges, proven or unproven, could make no difference to the Borley hauntings" as a whole.

Fairfield replied that it "certainly made a difference to the acceptability of the Foysters' evidence" — a position consistent with the [[the-borley-report|SPR Report]]'s skeptical framework. She agreed with [[miss-christina-hole|Miss Hole]] that the skeptics' case was largely concerned with putting [[harry-price|Harry Price]] into the dock as "a sort of secondary or unconscious accomplice of Marianne."

## Assessment

Underwood and Tabori recorded her position without endorsing it, concluding: "by and large, the 'mysteries of Marianne' were recorded without much reliance being placed on them — they form part of the immensely varied and rich human tapestry that is the background of Borley."
