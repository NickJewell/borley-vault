# W. John Braithwaite

A man who had known Borley Rectory and the Bull family "from childhood." He attended a séance at Borley Rectory on August 11, 1931, during the [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]] incumbency, and compiled a verbatim 5,000-word report of the event — later identified by Price as the "missing link" he had needed when writing *The Most Haunted House in England*.

Braithwaite died before Price obtained the report; it was supplied to Price by his widow, [[mrs-wj-braithwaite|Mrs. W. John Braithwaite]].

## The August 1931 Séance

The séance was held at the Rectory on August 11, 1931, attended by Braithwaite, [[ernest-meads|Ernest Meads]], and a medium named [[mr-johnson-medium|Johnson]]. The Rev. [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] and, during part of the time, [[marianne-foyster|Mrs. Foyster]] were also present. The séance lasted from 6 p.m. to 10 p.m.

Johnson channeled several "entities" in sequence:
- A **"Dr. Haslam of Harley Street"** (deceased) who diagnosed Foyster with rheumatism and told him to avoid starchy foods
- A **drunken ex-publican named "Joe Miles"** who called for "drinks all round" and could not believe he was dead
- **"Mary"**: a young woman who said she "went to London and I had a baby, and I murdered it." She gave her name as 'Mary' and said she "felt very rebellious" because of the cruelty with which she was met on returning to the village — turned out at night in the rain and sleeping under a hedge
- **"John Wesley"**: a notably well-done impression described as "better than the ordinary sermon"
- **"Cardinal Newman"**

The most significant "entity" in retrospect was "Mary," who admitted infanticide and described specific social details about the village. Whether this connects to any known Borley figure has not been established.

## The Paper-Message

Among Braithwaite's effects, his widow found the only surviving **paper-message** in [[marianne-foyster|Marianne's]] handwriting: an old envelope on which the single word "Marianne" appeared first (identical in every way to the wall-message handwriting), followed by Marianne's own written question "What do you want?" and then — apparently added later — the word "Rest." Price reproduces it as Plate XXVI.

## Significance

Braithwaite's report fills a gap left in *The Most Haunted House in England*, where Price had noted a 1931 séance and the medium "Joe Miles" but had no further details. The 5,000-word verbatim document is the most detailed account of a professional séance held during the Foyster period, conducted independently of Price's own investigations.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**W. John Braithwaite** (frequently referred to in the sources as **Sir John Braithwaite** or simply **Mr. Braithwaite**) was a civil servant based in London with a deep interest in psychical research, whose investigations and subsequent conclusions severely challenged the authenticity of the Borley Rectory hauntings during the incumbency of the Rev. Lionel Foyster [1-3].

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following biographical details, events, and contradictions regarding his involvement:

**Background and Local Connections**
*   **Family:** He was the brother of Miss Mary E. Braithwaite, J.P., and Miles Braithwaite (the town clerk and former mayor of Sudbury), who lived at Brook House in Long Melford [2, 4]. His father, Francis J. Braithwaite, had served as the rector of All Saints Church in nearby Acton [2]. 
*   **Local Network:** The Braithwaites had known the Rectory and the Bull family since childhood, and John Braithwaite was a friend and neighbor to Sir George and Lady Whitehouse of Arthur Hall [1, 2]. The Rev. Lionel Foyster recorded him as being a "cousin of the [Braithwaite]s of Norton" [5].
*   **Later Life:** The sources note that he eventually married, and after his death, his widow, Mrs. W. John Braithwaite, lived at "Greenwood," Bidborough Ridge, Tunbridge Wells [4, 6].

**The 1931 Investigations and the "Joe Miles" Séance**
Braithwaite visited the Foysters at Borley Rectory in **May 1931**, during the absolute height of the reported disturbances [1]. He was highly disinclined to accept a paranormal origin for the events, but knowing the Foysters were desperate for a resolution, he informed Lionel Foyster that he knew two "wonderful men" who could come and "make everything right" [1, 3, 5]. 
*   **The Séance:** On **August 11, 1931**, Braithwaite brought a 67-year-old masseur and medium named Mr. Johnson and an interrogator named Ernest Meads to the Rectory [3, 6, 7]. The séance lasted from 6:00 p.m. to 10:00 p.m. in the library [6, 7].
*   **The Entities:** During the sitting, the medium was allegedly controlled by a Red Indian, a "Dr Haslam of Harley Street," and an ex-publican named "Joe Miles," who gave an inebriated grunt claiming responsibility for the haunting [6, 8]. 
*   **The Contradiction in Foyster's Diary:** The Rev. Lionel Foyster slightly altered the identities of these men in his manuscript *Fifteen Months in a Haunted House*, incorrectly referring to them as "Mr. Thompson, the medium, and Mr. Teed, the interrogator" [9]. 

**Physical Phenomena Witnessed**
During his time at the Rectory, Braithwaite did report experiencing striking physical phenomena. He recorded seeing at least twenty-four **"curious old-fashioned coloured crockery egg-cups"** suddenly appear from nowhere [10]. Twelve of these egg-cups were then thrown violently across the room, denting the door but falling to the floor without breaking [10]. 

**The Confrontation and the 5,000-Word Report**
Following the séance and his careful observations, Braithwaite and his team concluded that the spirits were coming specifically to Marianne Foyster, and that she was the author of everything going on in the house [11, 12]. 
*   **The Verdict:** Braithwaite determined that Marianne was hysterical, not in a normal condition, and was the sole root cause of the poltergeist activity [1, 13]. 
*   **The Message to Foyster:** Braithwaite took it upon himself to confront the Rector. According to Foyster's own writings, Braithwaite visited him and **"informed me that with great hesitation and diffidence he must tell me the truth of the whole matter. It was my wife that was behind it all"** [14].
*   **The Written Report:** Braithwaite immediately compiled an exhaustive, 5,000-word report detailing his findings, completing it on **August 13, 1931** [6, 13]. In it, he emphatically stated that the mysterious Borley wall-writings were **"obviously done by Mrs Foyster"** [13]. 

**Documentary Legacy**
Braithwaite's conclusions had a lasting impact on the skeptical investigations of Borley Rectory:
*   **The SPR:** His sister, Mary Braithwaite, forwarded his report to William H. Salter of the Society for Psychical Research on **August 17, 1931**, pleading with the organization to intervene and stop the "intolerable nuisance" at Borley [13, 15]. 
*   **Harry Price:** Many years later, in May 1945, Harry Price approached Braithwaite's widow and was allowed to peruse and quote from John Braithwaite's "voluminous" original notes for his book *The End of Borley Rectory*, calling it the "missing link" he required [4, 6]. 
*   **The Paper Message:** The Braithwaite family also retained physical evidence from the haunting. They were in possession of one of the original mysterious paper messages addressed to Marianne, which had fluttered down from nowhere; this document was eventually photographed and reproduced in Price's book [1, 13].
