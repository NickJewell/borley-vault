# W. John Braithwaite

A man who had known Borley Rectory and the Bull family "from childhood." He attended a séance at Borley Rectory on August 11, 1931, during the [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]] incumbency, and compiled a verbatim 5,000-word report of the event — later identified by Price as the "missing link" he had needed when writing *The Most Haunted House in England*.

Braithwaite died before Price obtained the report; it was supplied to Price by his widow, [[mrs-wj-braithwaite|Mrs. W. John Braithwaite]].

## The August 1931 Séance

The séance was held at the Rectory on August 11, 1931, attended by Braithwaite, [[ernest-meads|Ernest Meads]], and a medium named [[mr-johnson-medium|Johnson]]. The Rev. [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] and, during part of the time, [[marianne-foyster|Mrs. Foyster]] were also present. The séance lasted from 6 p.m. to 10 p.m.

Johnson channeled several "entities" in sequence:
- A **"Dr. Haslam of Harley Street"** (deceased) who diagnosed Foyster with rheumatism and told him to avoid starchy foods
- A **drunken ex-publican named "Joe Miles"** who called for "drinks all round" and could not believe he was dead
- **"Mary"**: a young woman who said she "went to London and I had a baby, and I murdered it." She gave her name as 'Mary' and said she "felt very rebellious" because of the cruelty with which she was met on returning to the village — turned out at night in the rain and sleeping under a hedge
- **"John Wesley"**: a notably well-done impression described as "better than the ordinary sermon"
- **"Cardinal Newman"**

The most significant "entity" in retrospect was "Mary," who admitted infanticide and described specific social details about the village. Whether this connects to any known Borley figure has not been established.

## The Paper-Message

Among Braithwaite's effects, his widow found the only surviving **paper-message** in [[marianne-foyster|Marianne's]] handwriting: an old envelope on which the single word "Marianne" appeared first (identical in every way to the wall-message handwriting), followed by Marianne's own written question "What do you want?" and then — apparently added later — the word "Rest." Price reproduces it as Plate XXVI.

## Significance

Braithwaite's report fills a gap left in *The Most Haunted House in England*, where Price had noted a 1931 séance and the medium "Joe Miles" but had no further details. The 5,000-word verbatim document is the most detailed account of a professional séance held during the Foyster period, conducted independently of Price's own investigations.
