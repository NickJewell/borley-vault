# W. John Braithwaite

Also referred to in sources as **Sir John Braithwaite** or simply **Mr. Braithwaite** [1–3]. A civil servant based in London with a deep interest in psychical research. He had known Borley Rectory and the [[henry-bull|Bull family]] "from childhood" and was a friend and neighbour to [[sir-george-whitehouse|Sir George]] and Lady Whitehouse of Arthur Hall [1, 2]. His father, Francis J. Braithwaite, had served as rector of All Saints Church in nearby Acton [2]. He was the brother of Miss Mary E. Braithwaite, J.P., and Miles Braithwaite (the town clerk and former mayor of Sudbury), who lived at Brook House in [[long-melford|Long Melford]] [2, 4]. The Rev. [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] recorded him as being a "cousin of the [Braithwaite]s of Norton" [5].

He died before [[harry-price|Price]] obtained his report; it was supplied to Price by his widow, [[mrs-wj-braithwaite|Mrs. W. John Braithwaite]], who lived at "Greenwood," Bidborough Ridge, Tunbridge Wells [4, 6].

## The 1931 Investigations

Braithwaite visited the Foysters at Borley Rectory in **May 1931**, during the absolute height of the reported disturbances [1]. Highly disinclined to accept a paranormal origin for the events, but knowing the Foysters were desperate for a resolution, he told [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] that he knew two "wonderful men" who could come and "make everything right" [1, 3, 5].

During his time at the Rectory, Braithwaite did report experiencing striking physical phenomena: he saw at least twenty-four **"curious old-fashioned coloured crockery egg-cups"** suddenly appear from nowhere. Twelve of these egg-cups were then thrown violently across the room, denting the door but falling to the floor without breaking [10].

## The August 1931 Séance

The séance was held at the Rectory on **11 August 1931**, attended by Braithwaite, **[[ernest-meads|Ernest Meads]]** (the interrogator), and a 67-year-old masseur and medium named **[[mr-johnson-medium|Johnson]]** [3, 6, 7]. The Rev. [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] and, during part of the time, [[marianne-foyster|Mrs. Foyster]] were also present. The séance lasted from 6 p.m. to 10 p.m. in the library [6, 7].

> **Contradiction:** In his manuscript *Fifteen Months in a Haunted House*, [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]] slightly altered the identities of these men, incorrectly referring to them as "Mr. Thompson, the medium, and Mr. Teed, the interrogator" [9].

Johnson channelled several "entities" in sequence:
- A **"Dr. Haslam of Harley Street"** (deceased) who diagnosed Foyster with rheumatism and told him to avoid starchy foods
- A **drunken ex-publican named "Joe Miles"** who called for "drinks all round" and could not believe he was dead
- **"Mary"**: a young woman who said she "went to London and I had a baby, and I murdered it." She gave her name as 'Mary' and said she "felt very rebellious" because of the cruelty with which she was met on returning to the village — turned out at night in the rain and sleeping under a hedge
- **"John Wesley"**: a notably well-done impression described as "better than the ordinary sermon"
- **"Cardinal Newman"**

The most significant "entity" in retrospect was "Mary," who admitted infanticide and described specific social details about the village. Whether this connects to any known Borley figure has not been established.

## The Confrontation and the 5,000-Word Report

Following the séance and his careful observations, Braithwaite and his team concluded that the spirits were coming specifically to [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]], and that she was the author of everything going on in the house [11, 12].

Braithwaite took it upon himself to confront [[lionel-foyster|the Rector]]. According to Foyster's own writings, Braithwaite visited him and **"informed me that with great hesitation and diffidence he must tell me the truth of the whole matter. It was my wife that was behind it all"** [14].

Braithwaite immediately compiled an exhaustive, **5,000-word report** detailing his findings, completing it on **13 August 1931** [6, 13]. In it, he emphatically stated that the mysterious Borley [[wall-writings|wall-writings]] were **"obviously done by Mrs Foyster"** [13]. His sister, Mary Braithwaite, forwarded this report to [[william-salter|William H. Salter]] of the [[spr|Society for Psychical Research]] on **17 August 1931**, pleading with the organisation to intervene and stop the "intolerable nuisance" at Borley [13, 15].

## The Paper-Message

Among Braithwaite's effects, his widow found the only surviving **paper-message** in [[marianne-foyster|Marianne's]] handwriting: an old envelope on which the single word "Marianne" appeared first (identical in every way to the [[wall-writings|wall-message]] handwriting), followed by Marianne's own written question "What do you want?" and then — apparently added later — the word "Rest." [[harry-price|Price]] reproduces it as Plate XXVI.

## Significance

In May 1945, many years after Braithwaite's death, [[harry-price|Harry Price]] approached Braithwaite's widow and was allowed to peruse and quote from John Braithwaite's "voluminous" original notes for *The End of Borley Rectory*, calling it the "missing link" he required to fill a gap left in *The Most Haunted House in England* [4, 6]. The 5,000-word verbatim document is the most detailed account of a professional séance held during the Foyster period, conducted independently of Price's own investigations.
