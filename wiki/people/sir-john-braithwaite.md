# Sir John Braithwaite

A neighbour of [[lady-whitehouse|Sir George and Lady Whitehouse]], and like them known to the [[bull-family]] and the Foysters at Borley in the early 1930s.

## Visit and Assessment

Sir John and Lady Braithwaite visited [[borley-rectory]] in May 1931 at the height of the disturbances. They were disinclined to accept a paranormal origin for the [[foyster-phenomena]] and came to the conclusion that [[marianne-foyster]] was hysterical and the root cause of the disturbing events.

During August 1931, Sir John Braithwaite compiled a detailed report in which he put forward his view that the [[wall-writings]] were carried out by Marianne Foyster herself (see Chronology, 13–17 August 1931). This was sent to [[william-salter]] at the [[society-for-psychical-research]], but the SPR took no action on the Borley case at this time.

His sister Miss Mary Braithwaite was also associated with the case and later passed to [[harry-price]] a report by her brother on the [[joe-miles-seance]] — the August 1931 sitting involving a medium named Johnson, during which a drunken publican called "Joe Miles" apparently claimed responsibility for the haunting. Braithwaite's report was included in _EBR_. Miss Braithwaite was of the opinion that the wall writings were not produced by poltergeists but by Marianne Foyster.

## The Séance Connection

At one time the Braithwaites were in possession of one of the paper messages addressed to Marianne — one of the early apported paper slips bearing her name — which later became part of [[peter-underwood]]'s Borley collection.

## Significance

Braithwaite's early report to the SPR identifying Marianne as the source of the wall writings is significant as an early, independent sceptical assessment that predated the SPR's formal investigation by over twenty years. His view anticipated the conclusion ultimately reached by [[trevor-hall]] in the 1956 [[the-borley-report|Borley Report]].
