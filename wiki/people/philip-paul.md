# Philip Paul

A clerk and journalist turned psychic investigator who was Vice-President of the old Spiritualist Association of Great Britain. Active at Borley primarily through digging and excavation on the Rectory site, 1954–57.

## Ghost Club Involvement

Philip Paul was active for a time with [[peter-underwood]], Percival Seward, Cyril Wilkinson, Leonard Kingston and Dr C. Nicholson in reviving the [[ghost-club]] in 1954, but friction quickly developed and Paul left the Club.

## Excavations

Paul organised and led excavations on the former [[borley-rectory|Rectory]] site over a five-year period (see [[bones-excavation|Bones and Excavations]]). His work continued the digging started by the Turners and others after World War II. He was assisted by [[len-sewell]], [[steuart-kiernander|Steuart Kiernander]], Leonard Kingston and [[john-dening]], who continued digging after Paul became convinced there was nothing further of interest. None of his four original objectives were achieved: finding the rest of the nun's skeleton, locating an ancient tunnel, confirming a plague pit, or finding lost church plate.

In his book *Some Unseen Power* (Robert Hale, 1985), Paul wrote that "even were the whole of Price's work at Borley to be utterly and finally dismissed, the case for paranormalities having occurred there would remain unscathed." He maintained that "there was firm evidence that the 'nun' phantom was seen in 1885, 1900, 1916–19 and 1927" and claimed to have "gathered reliable testimonies to sightings of the same or a comparable apparition in 1949, 1953 and 1955."

## The Tunnel Discovery

Philip Paul's mechanical digger work at the Borley site came to an end in 1956, and it was [[len-sewell]] who was on site in September 1957 when the long-sought [[tunnels|Tudor-brick tunnel]] under the road between [[borley-place]] and the former Rectory grounds was accidentally cut through by water-main contractors.
