# Andrew Malcolm Green

**(Born 28 July 1927; died 21 May 2004)** Psychic investigator who founded the Ealing Society for the Investigation of Psychic Phenomena in 1949; original members included [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]]. An amateur organisation consisting at its height of fewer than a dozen friends [2, 3].

## Borley Visit (1949)

Over the weekend of **August 20–21, 1949**, Green and three other members (Roger and Grace Pyatt and Victor Bovington) spent a weekend at Borley — the first time any of them had been there [2, 4]. The Society ultimately produced only one report during its existence, which detailed this visit [2].

One member (Roger Pyatt) claimed to see "a figure in white" close to the [[borley-rectory|Rectory]] site "standing by the side of a bush... then moving" toward a building at the south-east end. A week later Pyatt made a sworn statement to this sighting. Mrs Pyatt heard footsteps "like those of a limping man." Green reported seeing "the vague outline of a head, some five feet from the ground, in the hedge" near the [[nuns-walk|Nun's Walk]] [1, 2, 4].

## Self-Contradiction

The BRC notes one curious episode: Green claimed to see "something like a face in bushes near the Nun's Walk" in 1949, but later claimed he had *never* seen anything of the kind at Borley — until confronted with the original published account he himself had written [1].

## Later Assessment

Green was generally sceptical of the Borley haunting but had little personal knowledge of the case. Writing in 1999, he stated:

> "...I now firmly believe the rectory was 'haunted' only by Marianne Foyster's various problems; effect of the mosquitoes following the track of the underground stream leading from the summer-house; the sound of badgers, foxes and rats in any of the three tunnels known to exist in the locality and beneath the church and its graveyard; the sound of the partial collapse of the roofs at varying times; and prior to the arrival of Harry Price, the self-deceiving and hysterical claims by the Bulls and members of their staff, arising at times from the activities of local vandals and groups of young farm workers." [5]

## Publications, Media, and Editorial Work

Green was author of several books on hauntings including *Our Haunted Kingdom* (1973), *Ghost Hunting: A Practical Guide* (1973), and *Ghosts of the South East* (1977) [1]. He featured as a contributor in the BBC television film *The Ghost Hunters*, broadcast on December 4, 1975, appearing alongside investigators like [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] and [[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth|Geoffrey Croom-Hollingsworth]] [6, 7].

## Editing of Ivan Banks

Although he edited [[ivan-banks|Ivan Banks]]' *The Enigma of Borley Rectory* for publication, Green considered [[robert-wood|Robert Wood]]'s *The Widow of Borley* as "one of the best indictments yet" on the Borley case, and was sorry he was unable to persuade Banks to include "more emphasis of this work in his own examination" [8].
