# Andrew Malcolm Green

(Born 28 July 1927; died 21 May 2004) Psychic investigator who founded the Ealing Society for the Investigation of Psychic Phenomena in 1949; original members included [[peter-underwood]].

## Borley Visit (1949)

On 20 August 1949, four members of the Society (Andrew Green, Roger and Grace Pyatt and Victor Bovington) spent a weekend at Borley — the first time any of them had been there. Subsequently the Society produced a report — their only report.

One member (Roger Pyatt) claimed to see "a figure in white" close to the [[borley-rectory|Rectory]] site "standing by the side of a bush... then moving" toward a building at the south-east end. A week later Pyatt made a sworn statement to this sighting. Mrs Pyatt heard footsteps "like those of a limping man." Green reported seeing "the vague outline of a head, some five feet from the ground, in the hedge." The Society was essentially an amateur organisation and consisted at its height of less than a dozen friends.

## Later Assessment

Green was generally sceptical of the Borley haunting but had little personal knowledge of the case. Writing in 1999, he stated:

> "...I now firmly believe the rectory was 'haunted' only by Marianne Foyster's various problems; effect of the mosquitoes following the track of the underground stream leading from the summer-house; the sound of badgers, foxes and rats in any of the three tunnels known to exist in the locality and beneath the church and its graveyard; the sound of the partial collapse of the roofs at varying times; and prior to the arrival of Harry Price, the self-deceiving and hysterical claims by the Bulls and members of their staff, arising at times from the activities of local vandals and groups of young farm workers."

## Self-Contradiction

The BRC notes one curious episode: Green claimed to see "something like a face in bushes near the [[nuns-walk|Nun's Walk]]" in 1949, but later said he had never seen anything of the kind — until confronted with a published account he himself had written.

## Editing of Ivan Banks

Although he edited [[ivan-banks]]' *The Enigma of Borley Rectory* for publication, Green considered [[robert-wood]]'s *The Widow of Borley* as "one of the best indictments yet" on the Borley case, and was sorry he was unable to persuade Banks to include "more emphasis of this work in his own examination."

## Publications

Green was author of several books on hauntings including *Our Haunted Kingdom* (1973), *Ghost Hunting: A Practical Guide* (1973), and *Ghosts of the South East* (1977).

**Andrew Malcolm Green (July 28, 1927 – May 21, 2004)** was a psychic investigator and author of several books on hauntings [1]. While he maintained a generally skeptical stance on Borley Rectory, his involvement yielded several notable events, publications, and a significant personal contradiction. 

A thorough reference profile based on the Borley sources includes the following details:

**The Ealing Society and the 1949 Visit**
In 1949, Green founded the **Ealing Society for the Investigation of Psychic Phenomena**, an amateur organization consisting of fewer than a dozen friends [2, 3]. Over the weekend of **August 20–21, 1949**, Green and three other members (Roger and Grace Pyatt, and Victor Bovington) visited Borley for the first time [2, 4]. The society ultimately produced only one report during its existence, which detailed this visit [2]. 

**The "Head in the Hedge" Contradiction**
During his 1949 visit, **Green officially reported seeing the "vague outline of a head, some five feet from the ground, in the hedge"** near the Nun's Walk [1, 2, 4]. However, the sources record a striking biographical contradiction: later in life, Green adamantly claimed that he had *never* seen anything of the kind at Borley. He maintained this denial until he was confronted with the original published account that he had written himself [1].

**Theories and Skepticism**
Despite admitting to having little personal knowledge of the case, Green firmly dismissed the haunting [1]. In 1999, he outlined his rationalizations for the phenomena in a comprehensive quote: 
**"I now firmly believe the rectory was “haunted” only by Marianne Foyster’s various problems; effect of the mosquitoes following the track of the underground stream leading from the summer-house; the sound of badgers, foxes and rats in any of the three tunnels known to exist in the locality and beneath the church and its graveyard; the sound of the partial collapse of the roofs at varying times; and prior to the arrival of Harry Price, the self-deceiving and hysterical claims by the Bulls and members of their staff, arising at times from the activities of local vandals and groups of young farm workers."** [5]

**Publications, Media, and Editorial Work**
*   **Authored Works:** Green wrote several books on the paranormal, including *_Our Haunted Kingdom_ (1973)*, *_Ghost Hunting: A Practical Guide_ (1973)*, and *_Ghosts of the South East_ (1977)* [1]. 
*   **BBC Appearance:** He featured as a contributor in the BBC television film **_The Ghost Hunters_**, which was broadcast on December 4, 1975, appearing alongside investigators like Peter Underwood and Geoffrey Croom-Hollingsworth [6, 7].
*   **Editorial Friction regarding _The Widow of Borley_:** Green edited Ivan Banks's book *_The Enigma of Borley Rectory_* for publication [8]. However, Green's own views aligned much more closely with Robert Wood's highly critical and controversial biography of Marianne Foyster, *_The Widow of Borley_*. Green considered Wood's book to be **"one of the best indictments yet"** on the Borley case, and he later expressed regret that he was unable to persuade Ivan Banks to place more emphasis on Wood's theories in his final manuscript [8].
