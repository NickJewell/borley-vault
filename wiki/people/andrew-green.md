# Andrew Malcolm Green

(Born 28 July 1927; died 21 May 2004) Psychic investigator who founded the Ealing Society for the Investigation of Psychic Phenomena in 1949; original members included [[peter-underwood]].

## Borley Visit (1949)

On 20 August 1949, four members of the Society (Andrew Green, Roger and Grace Pyatt and Victor Bovington) spent a weekend at Borley — the first time any of them had been there. Subsequently the Society produced a report — their only report.

One member (Roger Pyatt) claimed to see "a figure in white" close to the [[borley-rectory|Rectory]] site "standing by the side of a bush... then moving" toward a building at the south-east end. A week later Pyatt made a sworn statement to this sighting. Mrs Pyatt heard footsteps "like those of a limping man." Green reported seeing "the vague outline of a head, some five feet from the ground, in the hedge." The Society was essentially an amateur organisation and consisted at its height of less than a dozen friends.

## Later Assessment

Green was generally sceptical of the Borley haunting but had little personal knowledge of the case. Writing in 1999, he stated:

> "...I now firmly believe the rectory was 'haunted' only by Marianne Foyster's various problems; effect of the mosquitoes following the track of the underground stream leading from the summer-house; the sound of badgers, foxes and rats in any of the three tunnels known to exist in the locality and beneath the church and its graveyard; the sound of the partial collapse of the roofs at varying times; and prior to the arrival of Harry Price, the self-deceiving and hysterical claims by the Bulls and members of their staff, arising at times from the activities of local vandals and groups of young farm workers."

## Self-Contradiction

The BRC notes one curious episode: Green claimed to see "something like a face in bushes near the [[nuns-walk|Nun's Walk]]" in 1949, but later said he had never seen anything of the kind — until confronted with a published account he himself had written.

## Editing of Ivan Banks

Although he edited [[ivan-banks]]' *The Enigma of Borley Rectory* for publication, Green considered [[robert-wood]]'s *The Widow of Borley* as "one of the best indictments yet" on the Borley case, and was sorry he was unable to persuade Banks to include "more emphasis of this work in his own examination."

## Publications

Green was author of several books on hauntings including *Our Haunted Kingdom* (1973), *Ghost Hunting: A Practical Guide* (1973), and *Ghosts of the South East* (1977).
