# Squadron-Leader Horniman

RAF officer; official observer at Borley Rectory during [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s 1937–38 tenancy. He visited the Rectory on September 20, 1937, with an unnamed friend.

## Cold Spot Corroboration

Horniman reported that his friend, brought to the Rectory without any prior information about the "cold spot," experienced the same physical cold at the landing outside the [[blue-room|Blue Room]] that [[miss-reid|Miss Reid]] had reported in August 1937.

In Horniman's own words:

> *"What is interesting is that, without any previous information, this friend came over desperately cold on the landing where Kerr-Pearse's friend did.... His hands were much below normal temperature."*

The friend had not been told about Miss Reid's experience, or about the cold spot's location, or about [[rupert-haig|Rupert Haig]]'s experience the following day.

## Pattern of Independent Corroboration

September 1937 at Borley produced a remarkable cluster of cold-spot corroborations: [[miss-reid|Miss Reid]] in August; Horniman's unnamed friend on September 20; and [[rupert-haig|Rupert Haig]] (Kerr-Pearse's cousin) on September 21. Three independent visitors, over three separate visits within a few weeks, all reporting the same physical effect at the same location without prior knowledge.

This pattern of independent replication — from different people, at different times, who had not communicated with one another — is one of the stronger elements in the cold-spot evidence and was later experimentally confirmed by the [[cambridge-commission|Cambridge Commission]] in 1943.
