# Ivan Banks

Author of *The Enigma of Borley Rectory* (1994), published from Maidstone, Kent. A lay researcher (by his own description "without a scientific education") who conducted approximately ten years of original archival research into the historical background of the [[Borley Rectory|Borley Rectory]] case. Generally pro-[[Harry Price|Price]], strongly influenced by the [[Robert Hastings|Hastings Report]] of 1969.

## Research

Banks's research involved:
- Harry Price Library, London University (with A.H. Wesencraft's assistance)
- Institute of Historical Research, London
- National Archives and Public Record Office
- Essex County Archives, Chelmsford
- Suffolk Record Office, Bury St Edmunds
- Department of Paleography and Diplomatic, Durham University
- South Tyneside Metropolitan Borough Council archives
- Archives in France: Seine Maritime (Rouen), Mairie du Havre, Département de l'Eure (Evreux), Archive du France (Paris)
- Belgian National Archives and Rijksarchief te Antwerpen
- Multiple Catholic conventual archives including Stanbrook Abbey, Downside Abbey, Darlington Carmel, Oulton Abbey, Kylemore, St Scholastica's Teignmouth, Colwich
- Canterbury Cathedral Library and Archives
- Consulted Professor Bernard Knight (Cardiff University Medical School) on bone fragments and Kate Boreham's possible death from lead poisoning
- Mrs. D. Davies of Shrewsbury Meole Brace for Harry Price's Shropshire connections
- Countess Mary Waldegrave for Waldegrave family conventual records

## Key Original Findings

1. Henry Dawson Bull died from **Locomotor Ataxia** (a manifestation of tertiary syphilis) — documented by death certificate
2. **[[kate-boreham|Kate Boreham's]]** death was probably due to lead poisoning (sugar of lead as abortifacient) rather than the stated "cerebritis"
3. [[sir-edward-waldegrave|Sir Edward Waldegrave]] is a plausible ghost candidate — betrayed to Elizabeth I's authorities in 1561, died in the Tower; séance "Tfismong" = "Forster the fishmonger" from the *Calendars of State Papers*
4. The likely identity of the ghost nun is **[[barbara-waldegrave|Barbara Waldegrave (Sister Apollonia)]]** — born at Borley 1603, died Brussels 1638 aged 36
5. Captain Gregson's son Anthony confirmed that his father torched the Rectory for the insurance

## Limitations

Banks is not an academic historian and his theories, while well-researched, remain speculative. His framework interpreting séance results as factual evidence of historical events is unverified. He strongly favors Price and Hastings throughout.
