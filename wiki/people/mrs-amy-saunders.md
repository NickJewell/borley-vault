# Mrs Amy Saunders

Marianne's Ipswich landlady; retained contact with her from her first Ipswich lodgings in the mid-1930s until her departure to America in 1946.

## Properties

Mrs Saunders rented out multiple properties in Ipswich:
- **12 Gyppeswyck Road** (see [[gyppeswyck-road-ipswich]]) — where Marianne first lodged as she began her Ipswich masquerade as the single "Miss Foyster" or "Miss Voyster", ca. 1934–1935
- A property in **Ranelagh Road** — which Saunders described as "notorious" and which had degenerated to a slum by the late 1950s; Marianne rented rooms here ca. 1945–1946, her final English address before leaving for America. This address also appears fictitiously on Marianne's 1945 marriage certificate to [[robert-oneil|Robert O'Neil]] (as "229 Ranelagh Road" — a number that doesn't actually exist due to odd street arrangement)

## Significance

Mrs Saunders provides an independent thread connecting Marianne's Ipswich life across more than a decade. Trevor Hall visited the area in the mid-1950s and confirmed through neighbours and other sources that "an attractive dark-haired woman" who used many names had lived at No. 12 Gyppeswyck Road.

That Marianne returned to the same landlady for her final lodging in 1945–46 suggests Mrs Saunders was one of the few consistent and trusted contacts in her English life — though whether Saunders was aware of the true situation is not recorded.
