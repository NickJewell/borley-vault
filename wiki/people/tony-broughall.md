# Tony Broughall

Former Ghost Club Society area representative. Long-standing, enthusiastic and careful investigator of spontaneous and induced psychic phenomena who spent many years visiting Borley and examining the evidence.

## Discovery of Borley

In 1948 Broughall first heard of the Borley haunting when a visiting Spiritualist friend showed him a copy of [[mhh|MHH]] borrowed from his local church library. He did not visit Borley until February 1974 — a gap of over 25 years during which he developed his investigative skills at other locations.

## Borley Investigations

His later work at Borley is detailed in Peter Underwood's *Hauntings* (1977), including precise accounts of an all-night vigil in [[borley-church]] on 28–29 May 1976 with three other investigators. He became extremely sceptical of [[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth]]'s experiences, particularly the lengthy sighting of the [[the-nun|phantom nun]] in 1970 and the genuineness of the audio recordings made by [[densham-denny|Denny Densham]].

Two of the ghostly sounds on the Densham recordings — the opening of a "phantom" door and an eerie sigh — Broughall found he could replicate easily inside the church during his own vigil. After his findings were published by [[peter-underwood]] in 1977, he received a phone call from Croom-Hollingsworth saying the BBC had "selected sounds of the party themselves walking about, locking and unlocking doors, etc. in error." Croom-Hollingsworth continued to champion the recordings as late as 1984, despite this apparent backtracking.

Broughall also became the person to whom [[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth]] confided accounts of his own changed stories, and he became a key critical source on the reliability of Croom-Hollingsworth's claims.

## Card Index

During the 1980s Broughall compiled an exhaustive card index system for the Borley case, using all published literature plus his own experiences. One of us (PA) used this card index as the basis for the Chronology in the [[brc|Borley Rectory Companion]]. In terms of systematic data gathering on the Borley case, it has never been equalled.

## Conclusion

Broughall eventually concluded that the Rectory was probably never haunted — "only perhaps by a succession of very strange occupants that fate had brought together." However, he was less sure about the church, [[rectory-cottage]], road, and churchyard. He also investigated Chicksands Priory, where his wife and a party heard ghostly footsteps inside a locked building. His interest in Borley remained undiminished after more than thirty years.
