# R. Hawkin

R. Hawkin (also referred to in the texts as **Hawkins**; likely named **Robert**, as he signed his official report to [[harry-price|Harry Price]] as **"Robt. Hawk."** [2]) was an undergraduate at University College, Oxford [1]. He visited [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] on the night of **16–17 February 1938** alongside three other University College undergraduates: [[sg-welles|S. G. Welles]] (a Rhodes Scholar), Samuel J. Millberg, and [[m-knox|M. P. Knox]] [1, 3].

## The February 1938 Vigil

During their overnight stay, the group reported several phenomena including a luminous patch on the Blue Room ceiling, unexplained footsteps, and the sudden appearance of objects [1, 3].

**The Wooden Lath and Dried Grass:** At one point, Welles and Millberg found a 42-inch wooden lath on the scullery floor near a crumpled cigarette card. When they brought Hawkin to see it, he confirmed that he remembered the card from a previous round but had not seen the lath [4]. On the next round at 10:20 p.m., Hawkin and Welles found a 9-inch piece of dried grass lying perfectly parallel to the lath. Because Hawkin and Knox had just come from the long dry grasses of the summer-house path, Welles theorised that Hawkin or Knox might have unconsciously tracked it in on the sole of a shoe — though he noted it was strange that the grass fell so perfectly parallel without anyone stepping on the chalked lath [5].

**The Base Room Footsteps:** Shortly after midnight, Hawkin was left alone in the [[base-room|Base Room]] while the others were in distant parts of the house. At 12:20 a.m., Millberg heard a noise that sounded like "somebody is throwing things against the floor carelessly" and assumed it was Hawkin; however, upon checking, Hawkin declared he had made no noise [6].

Confirming Welles's detailed log, Hawkin submitted a shorter report from University College:

> **"I confirm the report of Mr. Welles with whom I was most of the night, making particular mention of (a) We clearly heard footsteps at 12:17, when I was alone in the Base Room, and the others in distant parts of the house. The footsteps appeared to come from the kitchen, along the passage to the front hall, and towards the drawing-room. I imagined at the time that it was one of the other investigators and, therefore, did not leave the Base Room; and (b) the peculiar thumping noise at 12.30 a.m. (approx.), which could not be traced to any apparent cause."** [2]

Their joint visit confirmed that the building was easily accessible without a key throughout Price's tenancy — a fact potentially relevant to "fresh" markings and moved objects discovered during visits.

> **Contradiction:** Within S. G. Welles's single narrative account, the surname alternates unpredictably between **"Hawkin"** and **"Hawkins"** [1, 4, 5].
