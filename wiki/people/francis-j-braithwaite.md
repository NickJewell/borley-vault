# Francis J. Braithwaite

Anglican clergyman; father of [[miss-mary-braithwaite|Mary Braithwaite]], [[miles-braithwaite|Miles Braithwaite]] and [[wj-braithwaite|John Braithwaite]] — the Long Melford family centrally involved in the Borley story; rector of **All Saints Church in the village of Acton**, near Long Melford.

## Background

Francis served as rector of All Saints, Acton — one of the small villages in the district surrounding Sudbury and Long Melford. This gave the Braithwaite family a deep rootedness in the same clerical-gentry milieu as the Bulls and the district surrounding [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]]. They had known the Rectory and the Bull family since childhood.

His three children lived together at **Brook House**, opposite The Bull in Long Melford, after his death: daughter [[miss-mary-braithwaite|Mary]], and sons [[miles-braithwaite|Miles]] (town clerk and former mayor of Sudbury) and [[wj-braithwaite|John]] (civil servant in London).

## Significance

The Braithwaites' network of connections — to the Bulls, to the Whitehouses, to [[william-salter|William Salter]] of the SPR through a family friendship — made them the pivotal intermediaries who brought the Society for Psychical Research into the Borley case during the Foyster period. Without Francis Braithwaite's establishment of the family in the district and their relationships with all the principals, the Borley Report might never have been commissioned.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Francis J. Braithwaite** has a very brief presence in the Borley Rectory sources. Rather than being an active participant in the ghost-hunting narrative, he appears in the historical record exclusively as the patriarch of the Braithwaite family, whose children played a significant critical role in the investigations during the early 1930s.

A thorough reference profile drawn from the sources reveals the following biographical details and connections to the case:

**Biographical Details and Family**
Francis J. Braithwaite was an Anglican clergyman who served as the **rector of All Saints Church** in the village of Acton, located near Borley [1]. He was the head of a **"highly respected local family"** [1]. 

He was the father of three children who actively feature in the Borley investigations:
*   **Mary Braithwaite:** A Justice of the Peace who lived at Brook House, opposite The Bull inn in Long Melford [2], [1]. 
*   **Miles Braithwaite:** The town clerk of Sudbury (and a former mayor of the town) who shared Brook House with his sister Mary [1].
*   **John Braithwaite** (also referred to in the texts as W. John Braithwaite or Sir John Braithwaite): A civil servant based in London [1]. 

**Connection to the Borley Haunting**
While the sources contain no record of Francis J. Braithwaite personally visiting or investigating Borley Rectory, his family had deep ties to the area and its residents. His son John had known Borley Rectory and the Bull family since childhood, and had heard tales of the haunting even in those early days [3]. 

Francis J. Braithwaite's legacy in the Borley narrative is carried out entirely through the actions of his children during the Foyster incumbency. When intense poltergeist phenomena broke out in 1931, it was Francis's son, John Braithwaite, who attended a dramatic séance at the Rectory on August 11, 1931, and subsequently compiled a 5,000-word report concluding that the phenomena—including the famous wall writings—were fraudulently produced by the rector's wife, Marianne Foyster [4], [2], [5]. Furthermore, it was Francis's daughter, Mary, and son, Miles, who initiated the formal involvement of the Society for Psychical Research (SPR) by contacting the organization's secretary, William H. Salter, in an effort to **"get this intolerable nuisance stopped"** [6], [7].
