# Francis J. Braithwaite

Anglican clergyman; head of a **"highly respected local family"** [1]; rector of **All Saints Church in the village of Acton**, near [[long-melford|Long Melford]] and Borley; father of [[miss-mary-braithwaite|Mary Braithwaite]], [[miles-braithwaite|Miles Braithwaite]], and [[wj-braithwaite|John Braithwaite]] — the Long Melford family centrally involved in the Borley story.

## Background

Francis served as rector of All Saints, Acton — one of the small villages in the district surrounding Sudbury and [[long-melford|Long Melford]]. This gave the Braithwaite family a deep rootedness in the same clerical-gentry milieu as the Bulls and the district surrounding [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]].

His three children lived together at **Brook House**, opposite The Bull in Long Melford, after his death:
- **[[miss-mary-braithwaite|Mary Braithwaite]]:** A Justice of the Peace [2, 1].
- **[[miles-braithwaite|Miles Braithwaite]]:** Town clerk and former mayor of Sudbury [1].
- **[[wj-braithwaite|John Braithwaite]]** (also referred to as W. John Braithwaite or Sir John Braithwaite): A civil servant based in London [1].

## Connection to the Borley Haunting

The sources contain no record of Francis J. Braithwaite personally visiting or investigating Borley Rectory. His legacy in the Borley narrative is carried entirely through the actions of his children during the Foyster incumbency. His son John had known Borley Rectory and the Bull family since childhood [3].

When intense poltergeist phenomena broke out in 1931, it was [[wj-braithwaite|John Braithwaite]] who attended a dramatic séance at the Rectory on August 11, 1931, and subsequently compiled a 5,000-word report concluding that the phenomena — including the famous wall writings — were fraudulently produced by the rector's wife, [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] [4, 2, 5]. Furthermore, it was [[miss-mary-braithwaite|Mary]] and [[miles-braithwaite|Miles]] who initiated the formal involvement of the Society for Psychical Research by contacting the organisation's secretary, [[william-salter|William H. Salter]], in an effort to **"get this intolerable nuisance stopped"** [6, 7].

## Significance

The Braithwaites' network of connections — to the Bulls, to the Whitehouses, to [[william-salter|William Salter]] of the SPR through a family friendship — made them the pivotal intermediaries who brought the Society for Psychical Research into the Borley case during the Foyster period. Without Francis Braithwaite's establishment of the family in the district and their relationships with all the principals, the [[the-borley-report|Borley Report]] might never have been commissioned.
