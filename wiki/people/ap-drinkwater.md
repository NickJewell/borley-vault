# A. P. Drinkwater

**Allen P. Drinkwater** — member of the executive board at Messrs. Longmans Green & Co., Ltd. (the firm that published [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s books) [1, 2]; official observer at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] on November 13–14, 1937. Visited with three unnamed friends and submitted a detailed, methodical report. Notable because the mysterious round blue box (first found by [[colonel-westland|Col. Westland]]) moved twice in a single night during his watch, and so many new pencil marks appeared that Drinkwater ran out of chalk before he could ring them all.

## Recruitment

On September 22, 1937, Price invited Drinkwater to lunch to discuss the publication of his upcoming book *Fifty Years of Psychical Research*, as well as a projected book about Borley Rectory [3]. During this meeting, Drinkwater was impressed by Price's careful, scientific approach to the investigation and suggested he would be interested in visiting the site as an observer [3]. Price formally reiterated his invitation two days later, and Drinkwater signed the official Haunted House Declaration Form on October 16, 1937 [3, 4].

## The Visit

Arrived at 2 p.m. on November 13; weather "clear and cold, temperature about 32° Fahrenheit, and no wind." Conducted a thorough inspection of the house, re-sealed doors and windows, and organised systematic hourly patrols:

> "My friends and I took duty in spells of two hours each, the other two resting in the Base Room. The two on duty spent periods of half an hour on each landing, remaining in the dark, and using no lights." [2]

**Phenomena recorded:**

- **4:15 p.m.** — two distinct percussive knocks from apparently the [[blue-room|Blue Room]] above the Base Room.
- **6 p.m.** — the round tin in the small cupboard had moved one inch to the left of its chalked ring. New position marked with a second ring.
- **1:41 a.m.** — a heavy thump from the Blue Room overhead; "like one heavy step... believed to be the last of several which woke them up."
- **4 a.m.** — numerous new light pencil marks discovered throughout the house (dining-room, Blue Room, Room 7, pillar between Blue and Room 7). Chalk supply ran out before all could be ringed.
- **Further inspection** — the blue tin in the cupboard had moved again: two paranormal displacements in a single night.
- **5:20 a.m.** — three more scratches on the wall of Room 3.
- **8 a.m.** — pencil mark in the Sewing Room; marks just outside the "Light-Mass-Prayers" message chalk ring.

Drinkwater's report emphasises that all seals were found undisturbed on the final inspection. He comments: "the house was remarkably quiet for an empty house of some age" [6].

At 9:00 a.m. the following morning, the entire party left the house for a meal, returning at 10:30 a.m. for a final inspection [6]. Finding nothing altered since their previous 6:00 a.m. check, they departed for London an hour later [6].

## Significance

The double displacement of the blue box in one night is one of the cleanest examples of controlled-object movement in the entire observer program: the object was on a chalked ring at the start of each patrol and had moved — by itself — between them, under conditions where all external entrances were sealed and all observers were accounted for. [[mark-kerr-pearse|Mark Kerr-Pearse]] had previously noted the box's initial anomalous appearance; [[colonel-westland|Col. Westland]] had marked its first movement; Drinkwater confirmed it moved again two months later, and twice in his single night.
