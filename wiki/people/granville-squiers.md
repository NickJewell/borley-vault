# Granville Squiers

Distinguished author of *Secret Hiding-Places* (1934), the original work on priest holes and secret passages in English houses. A long-standing member (Number 377) of the old Harry Price [[ghost-club]]; later a keen participant and consultant in haunted house investigations. Personal friend of [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]].

## Borley Connection

Squiers advised [[harry-price|Harry Price]] on the various tunnels and conduits at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]], and visited the site personally [1]. He is explicitly quoted as stating that Borley Rectory was **"one of the most interesting houses he had ever visited"** [1]. Despite the popular legends and theories attempting to link the site's subterranean passages to the ghostly phenomena, Squiers was unconvinced by this connection. He **"did not feel the tunnels there had any significance with regard to the haunting"** [1].

He commented that well-constructed sewers were sometimes used as tunnels and hiding places in later years — a view consistent with the [[tunnels|Borley tunnel]] identified by [[len-sewell|Len Sewell]] in 1957 (a Tudor-brick structure most likely originally built as drainage).

## Significance

Squiers' expertise on priest holes and hiding places was directly relevant to the Borley case, since the [[tunnels|Tudor-brick tunnel]] and the possible [[borley-church-crypt|church crypt]] have been associated with the Catholic [[waldegrave-family|Waldegrave family]]'s need to hide priests during the Elizabethan era. His opinion — that the tunnels did not have paranormal significance — represents the view of a specialist in such structures who examined the site first-hand.
