# Granville Squiers

Distinguished author of *Secret Hiding-Places* (1934), the original work on priest holes and secret passages in English houses. A long-standing member (Number 377) of the old Harry Price [[ghost-club]]; later a keen participant and consultant in haunted house investigations.

## Borley Connection

Squiers advised [[harry-price]] on the various tunnels and conduits at [[borley-rectory]]. He visited Borley and was a personal friend of [[peter-underwood]]. He said Borley Rectory was one of the most interesting houses he had ever visited but he did not feel the tunnels there had any significance with regard to the haunting.

He commented that well-constructed sewers were sometimes used as tunnels and hiding places in later years — a view consistent with the [[tunnels|Borley tunnel]] identified by [[len-sewell]] in 1957 (a Tudor-brick structure most likely originally built as drainage).

## Significance

Squiers' expertise on priest holes and hiding places was directly relevant to the Borley case, since the [[tunnels|Tudor-brick tunnel]] and the possible [[borley-church-crypt|church crypt]] have been associated with the Catholic [[waldegrave-family|Waldegrave family]]'s need to hide priests during the Elizabethan era. His opinion — that the tunnels did not have paranormal significance — represents the view of a specialist in such structures who examined the site first-hand.
