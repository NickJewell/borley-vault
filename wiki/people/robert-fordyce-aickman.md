# Robert Fordyce Aickman

Writer and investigator. Visited [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] (ruins) multiple times in July–August 1943, motivated by "solely scientific interest." His party on the first visit included a Press Officer of the Dominions Office, the Chief Executive Secretary of *Vry Nederland* (organ of the Netherlands Government), a young Czech scientist, and his wife E.R. Gregorson.

## Visits (1943)

**First visit (July 24)**: 9:15 p.m. to 11 p.m. Only notable incident: a loud crash from inside a cupboard to the west of the drawing-room door, with dust rising in the passage. "On a later occasion when a considerable wind was blowing [we] did not hear any disturbance at all similar." The evening was perfectly still.

**Second visit (July 31 – August 1)**: Spent the night during a thunderstorm of exceptional intensity — Long Melford's electrical supply was knocked out. Nothing happened during the storm. After the storm, at 12:30 a.m.: "a sharp crack, as of a pebble thrown inside an adjoining room." At 1:30 a.m. on inspection: a wooden floor rectangle in the scullery had been lifted — in place on the 24th, now behind the scullery door. On a third visit the same day (August 1): Miss George was struck by a pebble.

Aickman reported that the pseudo-Gothic summer-house at the far end of the garden had been demolished by the military, who had excavated "on the site of the summer-house" for no obvious reason.

> **Note:** Robert Fordyce Aickman (1914–1981) was later known as an important writer of "strange tales" and co-founder of the Inland Waterways Association. His participation in Borley investigations at age 29 is consistent with his lifelong interest in the uncanny.
