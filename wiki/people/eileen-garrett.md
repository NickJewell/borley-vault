# Eileen Garrett (1893–1970)

Famous psychic medium, publisher, and founder of the **Parapsychology Foundation** in New York. Born Eileen Jeanette Garrett on 14 March 1893 in Beaupare, County Meath, Ireland — she described herself as "extremely Irish," a remark that coloured her sympathy for [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] when they met. Hired private detective **Robert Swanson** at the instigation of [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]] to interview Marianne in 1958.

## Background

Garrett's personal life was marked by profound tragedy. Her parents committed suicide shortly after her birth and she was raised by her aunt and uncle [1]. During her childhood she suffered from tuberculosis and other respiratory issues, which led to her leaving Ireland for England at 15 [1]. She was married three times: her first marriage ended in divorce; her second husband was killed at Ypres in the First World War [1]. Three of her four children died young; her surviving daughter Eileen Coly and granddaughter Lisette Coly later served as President and Executive Director of the Parapsychology Foundation [3].

Her psychic abilities developed after the First World War, trained by J. Hewat McKenzie, the founder of the College of Psychic Studies [2]. In 1932–33 she toured the United States, conducting sittings with institutions including Johns Hopkins University and the New York Psychiatric Institute [2]. Forced out of France as a refugee in 1940, she established the Creative Age Press in New York and published a monthly literary magazine [2]. In 1951, alongside Francis P. Bolton, she founded the Parapsychology Foundation to provide a worldwide forum for the scientific study of psychic phenomena [2].

Her published works include *Awareness* (1943), *Adventures in the Supernormal* (1949), *The Sense and Nonsense of Prophecy* (1950), *Beyond the Five Senses* (1957), and her autobiography *Many Voices* (1968) [3]. She died of bone cancer on 15 September 1970 [1, 3].

## The R101 Séance

Garrett's most famous association with [[harry-price|Harry Price]] occurred in October 1930 during a séance at his National Laboratory of Psychical Research [5]. During the sitting she was apparently taken over by the spirit of Flight Lieutenant H. Carmichael Irwin, who had died two days earlier in the crash of the R101 airship [5]. The entity provided a highly technical and detailed appraisal of what caused the crash, which an Air Ministry official later evaluated as 70% absolutely correct and 20% highly likely [5].

## Role in the Marianne Interviews

In 1957, [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]] began a private investigation into Marianne Foyster's past and in 1958 contacted Garrett requesting her assistance [3, 6]. Garrett agreed and hired private investigator A. Robert Swanson to locate and interview Marianne in Jamestown, North Dakota, in February 1958 [3, 4]. Dissatisfied with the initial results, Garrett invited Marianne to New York for a second round of questioning in May 1958 [4, 8].

Garrett funded both rounds of interviews. She met personally with Marianne in New York, took her to dinner at her favourite restaurant, and reported back to Hall in extraordinary detail. Hall and Garrett later had a falling out over their collaboration [7].

During the New York dinner, Garrett deliberately **"pressed the bottle close to her"** to see if alcohol would loosen Marianne's tongue, but noted that Marianne **"drank like a lady and showed no outside or visible signs of being affected by it"** [9, 10].

## Garrett's Assessment of Marianne

Her personal assessment of Marianne (letter to Hall, 10 May 1958):

- "Extremely Irish... filled with all the imagination, superstition, legends... of my race"
- "She quite unblushingly told Mr. Swanson that she had every intention of lying to him... her lying is a game"
- "She lives in a perpetual temperature of romance"
- When asked point-blank if she had killed Foyster: Marianne "answered equally candidly that it had often been in her mind, but that he was too cunning for anything like that to happen to him" [10]
- "I am perfectly willing to believe that if Price wanted some phenomena, and Mrs. Goldney obviously did, she would have no hesitation in producing it" [10]
- Marianne's "mischievous make-up... might well lend itself to a form of poltergeist phenomena" [10]

To entice her cooperation, Garrett dangled a scholarship for [[vincent-oneil|Vincent]] as incentive for Marianne to "come clean": "She seemed to take great pleasure in this" [11]. Despite her role as Hall's operative, Garrett was more sympathetic to Marianne than Hall: "I felt sorry for her in spite of her willfulness and mischievousness."

Her explanation for Marianne's inconsistencies: "I honestly think that so much more has happened to her that she forgets, funnily or seriously" [11].

## Contradictions Regarding Marianne's Mediumship

> **Contradiction:** Marianne vehemently denied having any psychic power [10]. However, following the interviews, Garrett wrote to Hall stating she firmly believed Marianne possessed mediumistic powers, regardless of what Marianne claimed [13].

Garrett also relayed to Hall that Marianne had strongly insinuated that her husband Lionel and [[dom-richard-whitehouse|Edwin Whitehouse]] "were exceedingly intimate, which suggested a spot of homosexuality in the relationship," though Marianne also emphatically denied having any sexual relationship with Whitehouse herself [14, 15].

The Parapsychology Foundation archives subsequently suffered a basement flood that destroyed some of the Swanson interview transcripts.
