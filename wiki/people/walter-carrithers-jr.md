# Walter A. Carrithers Jr.

(Born 14 August 1924; died 21 August 1994) American Theosophical historian. Established the Blavatsky Foundation to promote public knowledge of the life and works of Helena Petrovna Blavatsky (1831–1891), and wrote many essays and articles championing her against critics. Under the pseudonym **Adlai E. Waterman** he wrote *Obituary, the 'Hodgson Report' on Madame Blavatsky 1885–1960*, attacking Dr Richard Hodgson's SPR-commissioned examination that had exposed Blavatsky as an impostor.

## Borley Involvement

Not surprisingly given his antipathy to the SPR, following the publication of the [[the-borley-report|Borley Report]] in 1956, Carrithers wrote three essays criticising the report's authors and methods:

1. **"Borley Rectory and the Society for Psychical Research (Dissection of the Technique of 'Exposing')"** — a draft chapter of a reply to the Borley Report, extensively criticising the methods used by the SPR investigators, using the [[polish-officers]] episode as an example of flawed approach.

2. **"The Hand and Arm of Mrs Foyster"** — criticises the Report for its presentation of the [[wall-writings]] episodes.

3. **"Tricks From Teeds to Tey"** — defends [[marianne-foyster]] against charges that she produced fraudulent phenomena to persuade her husband [[lionel-foyster]] to leave the Rectory.

These essays, together with much of his writing, are available on the Blavatsky Foundation website.

## Significance

Carrithers represents a significant body of pro-Price (or at least anti-SPR) opinion that emerged in the late 1950s to 1960s. Dr Vernon Harrison, a forgery expert and former SPR member, took a similar view about Marianne and the wall writings; his analysis is contained in *H.P. Blavatsky and the SPR* (Theosophical University Press, 1997). The Theosophical community's hostility to the SPR — stemming from the Hodgson report's attack on Blavatsky — gave Carrithers a motivation for scrutinising the SPR's methods that was independent of the question of whether Borley was genuinely haunted.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Walter A. Carrithers Jr.** (born **14 August 1924**; died **21 August 1994**) was an American Theosophical historian who became a vocal critic of the Society for Psychical Research (SPR) and a staunch defender of the Borley Rectory hauntings against the Society's skeptical investigations [1]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following biographical details, publications, and specific arguments regarding his involvement in the case:

**Biographical Details and Theosophical Background**
*   **The Blavatsky Foundation:** Carrithers established the Blavatsky Foundation to promote public knowledge regarding the life and works of the controversial occultist Helena Petrovna Blavatsky (1831–1891) [1]. 
*   **The Pseudonym and the SPR Animosity:** Writing under the pseudonym **Adlai E. Waterman**, Carrithers authored a book titled *Obituary, the ‘Hodgson Report’ on Madame Blavatsky 1885–1960* [1]. This book was a direct attack on Dr. Richard Hodgson and an 1884 investigation commissioned by the SPR, which had effectively exposed Blavatsky as "one of the most accomplished and interesting impostors in history" [1]. Because of this historical conflict regarding Blavatsky, Carrithers harbored a deep animosity toward the SPR and was "exceptionally critical" of the organization and its methods [1].

**The Three Borley Essays**
Following the SPR’s publication of the highly skeptical *Borley Report* in 1956 (which sought to expose Harry Price as a fraud), Carrithers brought his anti-SPR focus to the Borley case. He wrote **three specific essays** taking the authors (Eric Dingwall, Kathleen Goldney, and Trevor Hall) to task for their efforts [1]:

1.  **‘Borley Rectory and the Society for Psychical Research (Dissection of the Technique of “Exposing”)’**: Written in the 1960s as a draft chapter for a larger reply, this essay lengthily criticized the methods the SPR investigators used in their attempt to "expose" Harry Price [2, 3]. Carrithers specifically highlighted the SPR's dismissive treatment of the **Polish officers' wartime visits** to the ruins, using it as a prime example of the authors' entirely flawed approach to dismantling the haunting [2, 3]. 
2.  **‘The Hand and Arm of Mrs Foyster’**: This second essay specifically targeted and criticized the *Borley Report*'s presentation and skeptical conclusions regarding the famous **wall-writing episodes** [2].
3.  **‘Tricks From Teeds to Tey’**: In his third essay, Carrithers vigorously defended **Marianne Foyster** against the SPR's charges that she had deliberately faked the poltergeist phenomena in an effort to persuade her husband, the Rev. Lionel Foyster, to leave the Rectory [2]. 

**Archival Availability**
The sources note as a final detail that Carrithers's specific essays defending the Borley haunting, along with much of his other writing, were eventually made available to the public online via the Blavatsky Foundation's website [4].
