# Walter A. Carrithers Jr.

(Born **14 August 1924**; died **21 August 1994**.) American Theosophical historian. Established the Blavatsky Foundation to promote public knowledge of the life and works of Helena Petrovna Blavatsky (1831–1891) [1]. Writing under the pseudonym **Adlai E. Waterman**, he authored *Obituary, the 'Hodgson Report' on Madame Blavatsky 1885–1960* — a direct attack on Dr. Richard Hodgson's SPR-commissioned examination that had exposed Blavatsky as "one of the most accomplished and interesting impostors in history" [1]. Because of this historical conflict, Carrithers harboured a deep animosity toward the [[spr|SPR]] and was "exceptionally critical" of the organisation and its methods [1].

## Borley Involvement

Not surprisingly given his antipathy to the SPR, following the publication of the [[the-borley-report|Borley Report]] in 1956, Carrithers wrote **three essays** criticising the report's authors ([[eric-dingwall|Eric Dingwall]], [[kathleen-goldney|Kathleen Goldney]], and [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]]) and their methods [1]:

1. **"Borley Rectory and the Society for Psychical Research (Dissection of the Technique of 'Exposing')"** — a draft chapter of a reply to the Borley Report, written in the 1960s, which lengthily criticised the methods the SPR investigators used in their attempt to "expose" Harry Price [2, 3]. Carrithers specifically highlighted the SPR's dismissive treatment of the **Polish officers' wartime visits** to the ruins as a prime example of the authors' entirely flawed approach [2, 3].

2. **"The Hand and Arm of Mrs Foyster"** — specifically targeted and criticised the Report's presentation and skeptical conclusions regarding the famous [[wall-writings|wall-writing episodes]] [2].

3. **"Tricks From Teeds to Tey"** — vigorously defended [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] against the SPR's charges that she had deliberately faked the poltergeist phenomena to persuade her husband [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] to leave the Rectory [2].

These essays, together with much of his writing, are available on the Blavatsky Foundation website [4].

## Significance

Carrithers represents a significant body of pro-Price (or at least anti-SPR) opinion that emerged in the late 1950s to 1960s. Dr [[dr-vernon-harrison|Vernon Harrison]], a forgery expert and former SPR member, took a similar view about Marianne and the [[wall-writings|wall writings]]; his analysis is contained in *H.P. Blavatsky and the SPR* (Theosophical University Press, 1997). The Theosophical community's hostility to the SPR — stemming from the Hodgson report's attack on Blavatsky — gave Carrithers a motivation for scrutinising the SPR's methods that was independent of the question of whether Borley was genuinely haunted.
