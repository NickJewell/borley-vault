# Walter A. Carrithers Jr.

(Born 14 August 1924; died 21 August 1994) American Theosophical historian. Established the Blavatsky Foundation to promote public knowledge of the life and works of Helena Petrovna Blavatsky (1831–1891), and wrote many essays and articles championing her against critics. Under the pseudonym **Adlai E. Waterman** he wrote *Obituary, the 'Hodgson Report' on Madame Blavatsky 1885–1960*, attacking Dr Richard Hodgson's SPR-commissioned examination that had exposed Blavatsky as an impostor.

## Borley Involvement

Not surprisingly given his antipathy to the SPR, following the publication of the [[the-borley-report|Borley Report]] in 1956, Carrithers wrote three essays criticising the report's authors and methods:

1. **"Borley Rectory and the Society for Psychical Research (Dissection of the Technique of 'Exposing')"** — a draft chapter of a reply to the Borley Report, extensively criticising the methods used by the SPR investigators, using the [[polish-officers]] episode as an example of flawed approach.

2. **"The Hand and Arm of Mrs Foyster"** — criticises the Report for its presentation of the [[wall-writings]] episodes.

3. **"Tricks From Teeds to Tey"** — defends [[marianne-foyster]] against charges that she produced fraudulent phenomena to persuade her husband [[lionel-foyster]] to leave the Rectory.

These essays, together with much of his writing, are available on the Blavatsky Foundation website.

## Significance

Carrithers represents a significant body of pro-Price (or at least anti-SPR) opinion that emerged in the late 1950s to 1960s. Dr Vernon Harrison, a forgery expert and former SPR member, took a similar view about Marianne and the wall writings; his analysis is contained in *H.P. Blavatsky and the SPR* (Theosophical University Press, 1997). The Theosophical community's hostility to the SPR — stemming from the Hodgson report's attack on Blavatsky — gave Carrithers a motivation for scrutinising the SPR's methods that was independent of the question of whether Borley was genuinely haunted.
