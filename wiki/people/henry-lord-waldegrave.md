# Henry Waldegrave, first Baron Waldegrave (1660–1689)

Father of [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella Waldegrave]] and husband of [[henrietta-fitzjames|Henrietta FitzJames]] (natural daughter of King James II). Created Baron Waldegrave of Chewton in 1686 by James II. He belongs to the senior (Chewton) branch of the Waldegrave family, not the Borley branch — though his family connections to Borley are significant for the [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella Waldegrave]] theory.

## Life

Henry was born in 1660 into a staunchly Catholic family. He received the Baron title from James II in 1686. A loyal supporter of James, he accompanied the King into exile in France when James fled England in 1688. Henry died in exile in 1689, aged twenty-nine, as a member of the Jacobite court at Saint-Germain.

He married Henrietta FitzJames (1670–1730), the natural daughter of James II and [[arabella-churchill|Arabella Churchill]]. Their children included:
- **James**, later first Earl Waldegrave (1684–1741)
- **Henry**, who died a bachelor (1792) — their second son
- **Arabella Waldegrave** — who vanished from the historical record after entering a Paris convent

## Significance

Henry's marriage to a natural daughter of James II established a royal blood connection in the Waldegrave family. His daughter [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella]] is the focus of [[georgina-dawson|Mrs. Dawson]]'s theory that the Borley Nun was a royal granddaughter brought to England as a spy and murdered at Borley. [[canon-phythian-adams|Canon Phythian-Adams]] rejected this theory, but Henry's biography establishes the historical framework within which the theory operates.

The Henry Waldegrave who allegedly "strangled" [[marie-lairre|Marie Lairre]] in the [[communicating-entities|Planchette scripts]] (c.1667) cannot be this Henry, who was born in 1660 — only seven years old in 1667 — nor his son of the same name (not yet born). Price explicitly notes this problem.
