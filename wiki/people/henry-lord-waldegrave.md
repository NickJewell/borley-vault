# Henry Waldegrave, first Baron Waldegrave (1660–1689)

Father of [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella Waldegrave]] and husband of [[henrietta-fitzjames|Henrietta FitzJames]] (natural daughter of King James II). Created Baron Waldegrave of Chewton in 1686 by James II. He belongs to the senior (Chewton) branch of the Waldegrave family, not the Borley branch — though his family connections to Borley are significant for the [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella Waldegrave]] theory.

## Life

Born in **1660** into a staunchly Catholic family, Henry Waldegrave possessed close ties to the royal court [1]. He was created a peer by King James II, officially becoming the **first Baron Waldegrave of Chewton in 1686** [1].

He was intimately connected to the royal family through his marriage to [[henrietta-fitzjames|Henrietta FitzJames (1670–1730)]], the natural (illegitimate) daughter of King James II and his mistress [[arabella-churchill|Arabella Churchill]] [1]. Sources note that this lineage makes Winston Churchill a collateral descendant of the family [1]. Together, Henry and Henrietta had three children:

- **James (1684–1741)**, who later became the first Earl Waldegrave [1]
- **Henry**, who died a bachelor in 1792 — their second son
- **[[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella Waldegrave]]** (born 1687), who vanished from the historical record after entering a Paris convent [1, 2]

A loyal supporter of James, Henry followed the King into exile in France when James fled England in **1688** [2, 3]. He died in exile in **1689**, aged twenty-nine, as a member of the Jacobite court at Saint-Germain [1].

## The Borley Séance Contradiction

Henry Waldegrave's name was dragged into the Borley Rectory legends nearly 250 years after his death during the Glanville planchette séances in late 1937. The communicating entities wove a dramatic tale claiming that the famous Borley ghost nun, "[[marie-lairre|Marie Lairre]]," had been strangled to death by a **"Henry Waldegrave"** on **May 17, 1667** [4, 5].

However, investigators like [[harry-price|Harry Price]] highlighted a massive historical contradiction in this séance testimony: **because the first Lord Waldegrave was born in 1660, he would have been only seven years old when the murder supposedly took place** [6]. Furthermore, his son (the only other known Henry Waldegrave in the immediate lineage) had not even been born yet [6]. Consequently, researchers used these specific dates to prove that the first Baron Waldegrave could not possibly have been the "Henry Waldegrave" accused by the spirits of murdering the Borley nun [1, 6].

## Significance

Henry's marriage to a natural daughter of James II established a royal blood connection in the Waldegrave family. His daughter [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella]] is the focus of [[georgina-dawson|Mrs. Dawson]]'s theory that the Borley Nun was a royal granddaughter brought to England as a spy and murdered at Borley. [[canon-phythian-adams|Canon Phythian-Adams]] rejected this theory, but Henry's biography establishes the historical framework within which the theory operates.
