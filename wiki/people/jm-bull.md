# J. M. Bull

Author of *A Short History of Borley and Liston Churches* (1937), a booklet describing the architecture and history of [[borley-church|Borley Church]] and Liston Church. This work is the primary architectural source cited by Underwood and Tabori in their Chapter 6 description of the church building: the twelfth-century origin, the c.1500 rebuilding of chancel and north wall, the addition of the tower and south porch, and the replacement of original windows in the fourteenth and fifteenth centuries.

J. M. Bull's relationship to the [[henry-bull|Bull family]] of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] is not explicitly stated in *Ghosts of Borley*. Given the family's long incumbency and their surname, a family connection is plausible but not confirmed.
