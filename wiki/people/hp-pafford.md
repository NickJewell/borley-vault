# Dr. H. P. Pafford

Curator of the Harry Price Library at London University who preceded [[ah-wesencraft|A. H. Wesencraft]]. Pafford made a significant allegation about the removal of [[locked-book|Glanville's Locked Book]] from the library.

## The Locked Book Controversy

In 1959, Pafford implied that there was "a strong suspicion that the Locked Book was removed from the library 'under debatable circumstances'" — a reference to Trevor Hall's acquisition of the volume from Sidney Glanville. Hall had claimed the book was a gift from Glanville "in appreciation" of his help and encouragement. Pafford's statement suggests the library's position was that the book had been taken without clear authority.

Banks reports that Wesencraft (Pafford's successor) voiced similar doubts: "the current curator... voiced his doubts to me about Hall's claim to the ownership and his right to eventually sell it to an American for over £1000."

> **Contradiction:** Roger Glanville (Sidney's son) felt the ownership rested with his father and understood the gift to Hall was arranged during a lengthy meeting at his home. Pafford, however, had stated that Sidney Glanville once *asked to borrow* the book from the library — implying Glanville himself regarded it as library property, not his to give.

## Significance

Pafford's 1959 statement is the earliest documented challenge to Trevor Hall's ownership claim of the Locked Book. It establishes that concerns about Hall's conduct were not retrospective but arose shortly after the book changed hands. His allegation aligns with subsequent criticism of Hall from multiple Borley researchers.
