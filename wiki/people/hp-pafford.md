# Dr. H. P. Pafford

Curator of the Harry Price Library at London University who preceded [[ah-wesencraft|A. H. Wesencraft]]. Pafford made a significant allegation about the removal of [[locked-book|Glanville's Locked Book]] from the library.

## The Locked Book Controversy

In 1959, Pafford implied that there was "a strong suspicion that the Locked Book was removed from the library 'under debatable circumstances'" — a reference to Trevor Hall's acquisition of the volume from Sidney Glanville. Hall had claimed the book was a gift from Glanville "in appreciation" of his help and encouragement. Pafford's statement suggests the library's position was that the book had been taken without clear authority.

Banks reports that Wesencraft (Pafford's successor) voiced similar doubts: "the current curator... voiced his doubts to me about Hall's claim to the ownership and his right to eventually sell it to an American for over £1000."

> **Contradiction:** Roger Glanville (Sidney's son) felt the ownership rested with his father and understood the gift to Hall was arranged during a lengthy meeting at his home. Pafford, however, had stated that Sidney Glanville once *asked to borrow* the book from the library — implying Glanville himself regarded it as library property, not his to give.

## Significance

Pafford's 1959 statement is the earliest documented challenge to Trevor Hall's ownership claim of the Locked Book. It establishes that concerns about Hall's conduct were not retrospective but arose shortly after the book changed hands. His allegation aligns with subsequent criticism of Hall from multiple Borley researchers.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

Based on the Borley sources, **Dr. J. H. Pafford** (whose name is occasionally misspelled as "Dr. Patford" in the texts) was a key figure in the preservation and administration of the original Borley Rectory evidence following Harry Price's death. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific facts, events, dates, quotes, and controversies regarding his involvement:

**Professional Role and Early Assistance**
Dr. Pafford served as the **Goldsmiths' Librarian at the University of London**, making him the official custodian of the massive Harry Price Library of Magical Literature—including the famous "Borley Dossier"—which Price bequeathed to the University upon his death in 1948 [1-3]. 

When Dr. Eric Dingwall, Kathleen Goldney, and Trevor Hall were commissioned by the Society for Psychical Research (SPR) to re-examine the Borley case in the 1950s, it was Dr. Pafford who facilitated their research. The authors specifically thanked him in the preface of the 1956 *Borley Report* for **"giving us facilities to remove temporarily some of these documents for extended examination"** [3]. 

**The *Locked Book* Controversy and Contradiction**
Dr. Pafford is most notable in the Borley literature for his direct contradiction of the skeptical investigator Trevor Hall regarding the legal ownership of Sidney Glanville's *Locked Book of Private Information*. 

In **1948**, Dr. Pafford gave Sidney Glanville permission to borrow the *Locked Book* from the University of London library so Glanville could reference his own original notes while working on the projected *Borley Symposium* [1, 4]. However, in **1953**, Glanville inscribed the borrowed book and "gave" it to Trevor Hall [1, 4]. Hall later published articles claiming that Harry Price had "oddly" included the book in his university bequest, asserting that it was never Price's to give away and that Glanville had legally "withdrawn" it in 1948 [2]. 

**The 1959 Memorandum and Direct Quotes**
Dr. Pafford vehemently disputed Hall's claims. On **June 16, 1959**, Trevor Hall called at the University of London and gave the library a copy of the *International Journal of Parapsychology* containing his assertions about the *Locked Book* [2]. 

Three days later, on **June 19, 1959**, Dr. Pafford drafted an internal University memorandum to formally record the Library's opposing view, providing the following definitive quotes:
*   **"It is clear that Mr. Price considered that the *Locked Book* had been given to him by Mr. Glanville and that he, therefore, naturally presented it to us—not 'oddly.'"** [2].
*   **"It is, furthermore, quite certain that Mr. Glanville did not 'withdraw' the *Locked Book* in 1948 as if it were his property. There is no question of this whatsoever."** [2].
*   **"I knew Mr. Glanville and he visited me several times in the Library. He never, at any time, said that the *Locked Book* was his property, and he asked me if he could borrow it. What is accordingly odd about the matter is that Mr. Glanville then gave the book to Mr. Hall."** [2].

**Legacy of the Dispute**
Because of this incident, Dr. Pafford is recorded alongside researchers Ivan Banks and Alan Wesencraft as one of the key figures who "raised their eyebrows" over Hall's possession of the artifact, with Wesencraft going so far as to state outright that Hall had "stolen" the book from the University [5].
