# Wallace Tower

Eldest of the Tower siblings; brother of [[adelaide-foyster|Adelaide Tower Cartwright]] (formerly Barbara Tower); the man who, after a forty-six-year search, finally located his long-lost youngest sister in 1974.

## Background

Wallace was the oldest of the seven children of [[alice-tower|Alice]] and [[seward-tower|Seward Tower]] of Westcock, New Brunswick. When their parents died in quick succession in early 1928, the baby Barbara was given to [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] and [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]], who renamed her Adelaide. The remaining children were distributed among relatives.

For decades, the Tower siblings received no information about Adelaide. [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] had instructed Adelaide to refer to her biological sister [[edna-tower|Edna]] only as a "cousin" and suppressed all knowledge of the Tower family from her. After Lionel died in 1945, even the letters from Edna stopped. The last letter was returned marked "Address Unknown."

## The Search

Wallace felt it was his responsibility as the eldest to find Barbara/Adelaide. In 1973, he travelled to England, placing advertisements in local newspapers in Suffolk. On his last day, he was contacted by [[john-knights|John Knights]] in Woodbridge, who knew Marianne's family background. Marianne, whom Wallace then visited in La Crosse, Wisconsin, at first denied knowing Adelaide's whereabouts, then reluctantly provided two photographs and a last known address in England.

When Wallace went to that address, it had been demolished. He then enlisted the writer [[alan-roper|Alan Roper]], who had been researching the Borley case, and who eventually tracked the Cartwrights down in Manchester.

## The Reunion

Wallace telephoned Adelaide in November 1974 and invited her to Rhode Island for Christmas. *"It was not until we set foot at Kennedy Airport,"* Adelaide said, *"that we knew it was not a fairy story."* Her family were at the gate to greet their youngest sister. They recognised her immediately. *"The most marvellous reunion of a lifetime."*

After forty-six years of separation, the Tower children were reunited.
