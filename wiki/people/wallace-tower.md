# Wallace Tower

Eldest of the Tower siblings; biological brother of [[adelaide-foyster|Adelaide Tower Cartwright]] (born Barbara Alice Tower); the man who, after a forty-six-year search, finally located his long-lost youngest sister in 1974. By the 1970s, Wallace was living in **Chepachet, Rhode Island** [2].

## Background

Wallace was the oldest of the seven children of [[seward-tower|Seward]] and [[alice-tower|Alice Tower]], a farming family from the isolated rural village of Westcock, located near Sackville in New Brunswick, Canada [1]. In January 1929, Wallace's mother Alice died of a brain tumour [1]. Only a month later, his father Seward fractured his skull after falling off a load of marsh hay, leaving Wallace and his six siblings orphans [1]. Following their father's death, Wallace and his older siblings (Edna, Fred, Royal, and Merton) were separated and parcelled out among various aunts and uncles [1]. The youngest child, Barbara — less than a year old — was given to [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] and [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]], who renamed her Adelaide [1].

For decades, the Tower siblings lost all contact with Adelaide. Their sister [[edna-tower|Edna]] had managed to write to Adelaide regularly for a time, but [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] strictly commanded that Edna must refer to herself only as a "cousin," not a sister [2]. When [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] died in 1945, Marianne cut off all contact, and Edna's final letter was returned marked "Address Unknown" [2]. For the next twenty-nine years, Wallace and his siblings heard absolutely nothing about their youngest sister's fate. As his brother Fred noted: "It really bothered us, but what could we do? We didn't have the time to do anything else" [2].

## The Search

Wallace felt it was his responsibility as the eldest to find Barbara/Adelaide. In 1973, he travelled to England, placing advertisements in local newspapers in Suffolk. On his last day, he was contacted by [[john-knights|John Knights]] in Woodbridge, who knew Marianne's family background. [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]], whom Wallace then visited in La Crosse, Wisconsin, at first denied knowing Adelaide's whereabouts, then reluctantly provided two photographs and a last known address in England.

When Wallace went to that address, it had been demolished. He then enlisted the writer and [[ghost-club|Ghost Club]] researcher [[alan-roper|Alan Roper]], who had been meticulously investigating the Borley case and successfully tracked Adelaide down to where she was living with her English family (under her married name, Cartwright) in Manchester [3]. Roper gave Wallace her address and telephone number [3].

## The Reunion

In **November 1974**, Wallace telephoned Adelaide — a call that left her entirely "speechless," as [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] had raised her to believe she was an only child with no brothers or sisters [2]. Wallace invited her to travel to Rhode Island for Christmas [2, 3]. When Adelaide walked through the gate at Kennedy Airport, Wallace and her other siblings were waiting to greet the sister who had "magically returned from the dead." They recognised her immediately. *"It was not until we set foot at Kennedy Airport,"* Adelaide said, *"that we knew it was not a fairy story."* Her family were at the gate — "the most marvellous reunion of a lifetime" after forty-six years of separation [3].
