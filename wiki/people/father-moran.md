# Father Moran

Roman Catholic priest at **Sudbury** during the Foyster period; asked by [[dom-richard-whitehouse|Edwin Whitehouse]] to say a Mass at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]], but declined.

## The Refused Request

Following the novena that Edwin and [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] prayed together on the Rectory landing in June 1931, and after fresh wall-writing appeared directing them to *"Get Light, Mass, Prayers,"* Edwin became convinced that a Roman Catholic Mass should be held in the Rectory itself. He asked Father Moran, the local Catholic priest in Sudbury, if he would do this.

Moran, however, realised that Edwin was "not exactly himself" — a reference to Edwin's unstable mental state following his breakdown and incomplete treatment at Bowdon House. Moran "had too much sense for this kind of foolishness and ignored it in a diplomatic way." The Mass was never held at the Rectory during the Foyster period.

## Significance

Father Moran's refusal is a small but telling moment. It suggests that the Catholic clergy in the area, while not unsympathetic to the idea of a distressed spirit seeking requiem, were wary of the specific dynamic at play — particularly Edwin's emotional vulnerability and [[dom-richard-whitehouse|his religious obsessiveness]]. The Mass that was eventually said for the soul of "Marie Lairre" did not take place until 30 October 1943, when [[father-john-wright|Father Wright]] of St Philip Neri's in Arundel said the Requiem.
