# Paul Tabori (PT)

Co-author (with [[peter-underwood]]) of *Ghosts of Borley* (1973). Identified in the book as "PT."

## Role

Was [[harry-price|Harry Price's]] literary executor for twenty-five years. Wrote Price's biography. His long personal association with Price gives him unusual insight into Price's character and the accusations made against him.

## Declared Position

Believes some phenomena were genuine. Rejects the indictment in the 1956 SPR critical report.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Dr. Paul Tabori** (born Budapest, 8 May 1908; died London, 9 November 1974) was a Jewish-Hungarian academic, author, and journalist who became a central figure in the Borley Rectory saga as the Literary Executor of the Harry Price Estate. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following biographical details, publications, contradictions, and quotes regarding his involvement:

**Biographical Details and Background**
*   **Early Life and Exile:** Tabori obtained a PhD in drama at Berlin University and a doctorate of political science and economics at Budapest University [1]. He fled the Continent with his mother in 1937 and settled in London during the Blitz; tragically, his father died in Auschwitz in 1944 [2].
*   **Career and Achievements:** He lived in 17 countries working as a foreign correspondent and translator, and was an immensely prolific author of children’s books, adult fiction (over 40 works), and non-fiction [1]. During the war, he served as an editor, film critic, Reuters feature writer, and official lecturer to the RAF [1]. From 1943 to 1948, he was a contract writer for Sir Alexander Korda’s London Films [1, 2]. By 1951, he had written over 30 feature film scripts and 140 television plays, spending 18 months as a scriptwriter in Hollywood and teaching at the Maren Elwood College [1]. 
*   **Associations:** He was instrumental in establishing the International Writers Guild, becoming its first Executive Director, and was associated with International P.E.N. for over 30 years, serving as Chairman of the P.E.N. Centre for Writers in Exile from 1954 to 1957 [1].
*   **Personality and Citizenship Anecdote:** Remembered as a man of immense erudition and charm whose only vices were said to be "brandy and big cigars" [3]. When he became a naturalised British citizen, he returned home and told his wife, Kate. She jumped for joy, exclaiming, "Oh! We're English!" Tabori practically replied: **"No, not English, dear, British, like the Zulus"** [3].

**Role in Psychical Research**
*   Tabori claimed to have an open mind regarding the paranormal, stating he was **"neither a spiritualist nor a die-hard sceptic"** [2].
*   He was a member of the old Harry Price Ghost Club, and in 1958, at the invitation of Peter Underwood, he became Vice-Chairman of The Ghost Club, holding the position until his death [3].
*   Following the sudden death of Harry Price in 1948, and after Mrs. Cecil Ryan Baines withdrew from the project in disgust, Price's trustees appointed Tabori as the Literary Executor of the Harry Price Estate [2, 4, 5].

**The Borley Publications and Textual Contradictions**
As Literary Executor, Tabori had unhindered access to Price's vast archives at the University of London [6, 7]. His involvement produced several key Borley works, alongside some notable historical errors:
*   **Harry Price: The Biography of a Ghost-Hunter (1950):** Tabori admitted to Peter Underwood that he wrote this biography "in something of a rush to try to get some money into the estate" [4]. 
    *   *The Foyster Quote:* Discussing the Foyster incumbency in the book, Tabori stated: **"Copious extracts were included in the two Borley books and there is no doubt that Mr Foyster, his wife, his little adopted daughter and, later, a small boy who stayed there as a guest, went through some amazing, terrifying and inexplicable experiences"** [8].
    *   *Contradiction 1 (Price's Birthplace):* In the biography, Tabori erroneously recorded Price's birthplace as Shrewsbury instead of London [6, 9]. This error apparently occurred because Tabori misunderstood Price's Shropshire family links and misinterpreted a childhood story, creating a lasting geographical error that skeptical investigator Trevor Hall later used to falsely accuse Price of deliberately inventing his background [9-11].
    *   *Contradiction 2 (Glanville's Role):* Tabori also stated that Sidney Glanville was "in charge" of the Borley investigation [12]. The authors of the highly critical *Borley Report* flagged this as misleading, noting that Glanville himself deplored the laxity of the investigation's organization and considered himself merely an observer and accumulator of evidence, not the man in charge [13].
*   **The Borley Symposium (Borley III):** Tabori was slated to be the general editor of Price's unfinished third Borley book, coordinating contributions from Sidney Glanville, Peter Underwood, and Mrs. Baines [14, 15]. The project suffered from internal friction; Mrs. Baines complained that Tabori was "hedging" and trying to dictate her output to meet publishers' requirements [16, 17]. The *Symposium* was ultimately abandoned in 1954 due to Glanville's death, Tabori's prolonged stay in America, and the publication of the Society for Psychical Research's highly critical *Borley Report* [18, 19].
*   **The Ghosts of Borley (1973):** To counteract the skeptical attacks on Price, Tabori co-authored *The Ghosts of Borley - Annals of the Haunted Rectory* with Peter Underwood in 1973 [20, 21]. A lesser-known detail about this collaboration is that the book was in fact written entirely by Peter Underwood; Paul Tabori’s name was added as joint author purely so there would be no legal or copyright difficulties over the use of Harry Price's archive material [4].
