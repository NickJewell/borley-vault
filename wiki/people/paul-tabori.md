# Paul Tabori (PT)

(Born Budapest, 8 May 1908; died London, 9 November 1974) Co-author (with [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]]) of *Ghosts of Borley* (1973), identified in the book as "PT." A Jewish-Hungarian academic, author, and journalist who became a central figure in the Borley saga as the Literary Executor of the Harry Price Estate.

## Life and Career

Tabori obtained a PhD in drama at Berlin University and a doctorate of political science and economics at Budapest University. He fled the Continent with his mother in 1937 and settled in London during the Blitz; his father died in Auschwitz in 1944. He lived in 17 countries working as a foreign correspondent and translator, and was an immensely prolific author of children's books, adult fiction (over 40 works), and non-fiction. During the war he served as an editor, film critic, Reuters feature writer, and official lecturer to the RAF; from 1943 to 1948 he was a contract writer for Sir Alexander Korda's London Films. By 1951 he had written over 30 feature film scripts and 140 television plays, and spent 18 months as a scriptwriter in Hollywood. He was instrumental in establishing the International Writers Guild, becoming its first Executive Director, and served as Chairman of the P.E.N. Centre for Writers in Exile from 1954 to 1957.

Remembered as a man of immense erudition and charm whose only vices were said to be "brandy and big cigars." When he became a naturalised British citizen, he told his wife, Kate, who jumped for joy, exclaiming, "Oh! We're English!" Tabori practically replied: "No, not English, dear, British, like the Zulus."

## Role in Psychical Research

Tabori claimed an open mind regarding the paranormal, stating he was "neither a spiritualist nor a die-hard sceptic." He was a member of the old Harry Price Ghost Club, and in 1958, at the invitation of [[peter-underwood|Underwood]], became Vice-Chairman of The Ghost Club, holding the position until his death.

Following the sudden death of [[harry-price|Harry Price]] in 1948, and after [[mrs-cc-baines|Mrs Cecil Ryan Baines]] withdrew from the project in disgust, Price's trustees appointed Tabori as the Literary Executor of the Harry Price Estate. As Literary Executor, he had unhindered access to Price's vast archives at the University of London.

## Declared Position

Believes some phenomena were genuine. Rejects the indictment in the 1956 SPR critical report.

## The Borley Publications and Textual Contradictions

**Harry Price: The Biography of a Ghost-Hunter (1950):** Tabori admitted to [[peter-underwood|Underwood]] that he wrote this biography "in something of a rush to try to get some money into the estate."

> **Contradiction — Price's Birthplace:** In the biography, Tabori erroneously recorded Price's birthplace as Shrewsbury instead of London. This error apparently occurred because Tabori misunderstood Price's Shropshire family links and misinterpreted a childhood story. Sceptical investigator [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]] later used this error to falsely accuse Price of deliberately inventing his own background.

> **Contradiction — Glanville's Role:** Tabori also stated that [[sidney-glanville|Sidney Glanville]] was "in charge" of the Borley investigation. The authors of the *Borley Report* flagged this as misleading, noting that Glanville himself deplored the laxity of the investigation's organisation and considered himself merely an observer and accumulator of evidence, not the man in charge.

**The Borley Symposium:** Tabori was slated to be the general editor of Price's unfinished third Borley book ("Borley III"), co-ordinating contributions from [[sidney-glanville|Sidney Glanville]], [[peter-underwood|Underwood]], and [[mrs-cc-baines|Mrs Baines]]. The project suffered from internal friction — Mrs Baines complained that Tabori was "hedging" and trying to dictate her output to meet publishers' requirements. It was ultimately abandoned in 1954 due to Glanville's death, Tabori's prolonged stay in America, and the publication of the highly critical *Borley Report*.

**The Ghosts of Borley (1973):** Tabori co-authored *The Ghosts of Borley — Annals of the Haunted Rectory* with [[peter-underwood|Underwood]] in 1973 to counteract the sceptical attacks on Price. A lesser-known detail: the book was in fact written entirely by Underwood; Tabori's name was added as joint author purely so there would be no legal or copyright difficulties over the use of Harry Price's archive material.
