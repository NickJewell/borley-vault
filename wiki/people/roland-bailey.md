# Roland F. Bailey

Barrister and civil servant; brother of [[dr-eric-bailey|Dr. Eric H. Bailey]], M.R.C.S., L.R.C.P., Senior Assistant Pathologist at the Staines County Hospital in Ashford, Middlesex. Present at the August 17–18, 1943 excavation of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory's]] cellars alongside his brother and [[flying-officer-creamer|Flying-Officer Creamer]].

Bailey attended in his capacity as a legal professional — his presence was part of [[harry-price|Price]]'s effort to ensure the excavation had appropriately credentialed witnesses, particularly for the chain of evidence surrounding the [[bones-excavation|human remains]] found. A barrister's understanding of evidential standards was directly relevant to any future assessment of what was discovered.

He was present on **17 August 1943** (Day 1 of the excavations) — the exact day that a human jawbone and skull fragment were discovered. Harry Price formally included him in his published list of official participants, noting he was among those who "either assisted at the digging, or watched the proceedings."

> **Textual Anomaly:** In the formal attendance table for Day 1, Roland is listed as "Mr R. Bailey." However, the same list also includes a mysterious "Mr F. Bailey." Given his middle initial, this is likely a typographical error splitting his name rather than a third Bailey brother. He is not listed as having attended the subsequent days of digging on 18 August or 30 August.

Like [[dr-eric-bailey|his brother]] and Creamer, Bailey returned to Sudbury after the first day's digging. His role is corroborative rather than analytical; the medical expertise was provided by his brother, and the physical work by [[jackson-labourer|Jackson]] and [[rev-ac-henning|Henning]]. 

See [[bones-excavation]] for the full account of the excavation and finds.
