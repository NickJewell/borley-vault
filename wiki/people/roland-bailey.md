# Roland F. Bailey

Barrister; brother of [[dr-eric-bailey|Dr. Eric H. Bailey]]. Present at the August 17–18, 1943 excavation of Borley Rectory's cellars alongside his brother and [[flying-officer-creamer|Flying-Officer Creamer]].

Bailey attended in his capacity as a legal professional — his presence was part of [[harry-price|Price]]'s effort to ensure the excavation had appropriately credentialed witnesses, particularly for the chain of evidence surrounding the [[bones-excavation|human remains]] found. A barrister's understanding of evidential standards was directly relevant to any future assessment of what was discovered.

Like [[dr-eric-bailey|his brother]] and Creamer, Bailey returned to Sudbury after the first day's digging on August 17. His role is corroborative rather than analytical; the medical expertise was provided by his brother, and the physical work by [[jackson-labourer|Jackson]] and [[rev-ac-henning|Henning]]. Price records him as a civil servant alongside Creamer.

See [[bones-excavation]] for the full account of the excavation and finds.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Roland F. Bailey** appears briefly in the Borley Rectory sources, featuring solely because of his presence during the famous wartime excavations of the Rectory ruins. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following biographical details and facts regarding his involvement:

**Biographical Details**
*   **Profession:** Roland F. Bailey was a **barrister** and is also explicitly identified in the texts as a **civil servant** [1, 2].
*   **Family:** He was the brother of **Dr. Eric H. Bailey**, M.R.C.S., L.R.C.P., who served as the Senior Assistant Pathologist at the Staines County Hospital in Ashford, Middlesex [1, 2]. 

**The August 1943 Excavations**
*   Roland F. Bailey accompanied his brother to Borley Rectory to assist Harry Price during his search for human remains in the ruined cellars [1]. 
*   He was present on **August 17, 1943** (Day 1 of the excavations)—the exact day that a human jawbone and skull fragment were discovered [3]. 
*   Harry Price formally included him in his published list of official participants, noting that he was among those who **"either assisted at the digging, or watched the proceedings"** [2]. 

**Textual Anomalies**
*   In the formal table of attendees for the three-day excavation, Roland is listed under the attendance for Day 1 as **"Mr R. Bailey"** [3]. However, the same list for that day also includes a mysterious **"Mr F. Bailey"** [3]. Given his middle initial, it is highly likely that this is a typographical error splitting his name, rather than a third Bailey brother. 
*   He is not listed as having attended the subsequent days of digging on August 18 or August 30 [3].

The provided Borley sources contain no specific quotes, later statements, or further biographical details regarding Roland F. Bailey beyond his attendance at this single event [1-3].
