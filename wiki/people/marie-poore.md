# Marie Poore

Hilary Wright's mother; lived at "The Willows," Lower Road, Borley, with her late husband following his retirement from the Middlesex area. One of several locally based witnesses interviewed by Babbs and Mathias for *The Final Analysis*.

## Phantom Coach Sighting — Early 1970s

On a clear night around midnight, returning from a pantomime rehearsal at the Theatre Royal in Bury St Edmunds, both Mrs Poore and her husband saw what appeared to be an **old-fashioned horse-drawn coach with small lamps** crossing the large sloping field to the rear of their house, moving toward them. It vanished suddenly before it could reach them.

They were certain it was not a modern tractor — it was horse-drawn and had characteristic period lamps.

**Investigation:** Her husband examined the field the next morning. There were **no tyre marks, wheel marks, or hoof marks** anywhere in the area.

**The old road:** An elderly local resident then told Mrs Poore of a tradition that the original road between Borley and Rodbridge had been **straight** rather than following the present L-shaped route of Hall Lane and Lower Road. The coach appeared to be travelling on exactly this line. This tradition was subsequently confirmed by a map dated **1777**, which shows it was indeed the original highway.

This links the coach sighting to the more general phenomenon of the [[phantom-coach]] and to the idea that paranormal phenomena may follow geographical or historical paths.

## Ethel Bull's Diary

Through a mutual friend, Mrs Poore saw a diary kept by [[ethel-bull]] when she was young. It was "more like a school exercise book than a pocket book" with pencil entries, including comments to the general effect that "we saw the nun in the garden today." The location of this diary is unknown.

## Phantom Cat Sighting — 1974

Also reported a large cat dashing into the road and then vanishing, at the same spot as her daughter [[hilary-wright]] had seen the same phenomenon in the same year.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Marie Poore** (wife of Mr. A. C. Poore and mother of fellow Borley witness Hilary Wright) was a resident of Borley whose connection to the haunting saga comes from both her personal experiences and her discovery of a highly significant, yet now lost, historical document. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following biographical details, sightings, and specific facts regarding her involvement:

**Biographical Details and Life at Borley**
*   **Move to Borley:** Following her husband's retirement, Marie and A. C. Poore relocated from the Middlesex area to Borley [1]. 
*   **The Willows:** They purchased a bungalow named **The Willows**, situated on Lower Road [1]. During their time there, it was the very first residence encountered when approaching the hamlet of Borley from the direction of Rodbridge Corner [1].
*   **Sightseer Nuisances:** Mrs. Poore recalled that they were **frequently disturbed by strangers knocking to ask for directions to the church and the "haunted rectory"**, an enduring nuisance that continued more than thirty years after the Rectory had been destroyed by fire [1]. 

**The Phantom Coach Sighting (Early 1970s)**
Marie and her husband shared a highly unusual visual experience that they suspected was the famous phantom coach. In November 1973, Mr. A. C. Poore wrote to investigator Peter Underwood detailing an event they had both witnessed in the early 1970s [2]. 
*   Returning from Bury St Edmunds at around 12:30 a.m., the couple crossed the River Stour at Rodbridge and suddenly saw **"a shape with two lights coming towards us just beyond our bungalow on the sloping fields between us and Borley Church"** [2]. 
*   Their initial rationalization was that it must be a tractor ploughing the field at night, but before they could process the improbability of this, **the shape and the lights abruptly disappeared** [2].
*   Mr. Poore noted that there was absolutely no natural explanation—no mist, gate posts, hedges, water, or heaps of earth—only a gentle slope of a ploughed field [3]. Because the old road to the church was reputed to have taken that exact route, he questioned: **"So was it the coach?"** [3]. 

**The Lost Diary of Ethel Bull**
Perhaps Marie Poore's most historically significant contribution to the Borley saga is a tantalizing link to the Bull family's early years [4]. 
*   While living in Borley, Marie made many friends, including the Payne family of Borley Place [4]. Through one of these contacts, she met a Sudbury resident named Eileen Livock, who had known the Bull family well [4]. 
*   **Eileen Livock showed Marie Poore a diary that had been kept by Miss Ethel Bull when she was a young girl** [4]. 
*   Marie Poore described the diary as resembling a school exercise book rather than a pocket book, with the entries written in pencil [4]. 
*   Most astonishingly, Mrs. Poore recalled that the diary contained casual, matter-of-fact entries to the general effect of: **"we saw the nun in the garden today"** [4]. 

Despite the obvious historical value such a contemporary document would hold for psychical researchers, investigators have so far been unsuccessful in locating this lost diary [4].
