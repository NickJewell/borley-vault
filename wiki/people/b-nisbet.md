# B. Nisbet

B. Nisbet contributed an observation about the [[wall-writings|Borley wall-writings]] in the *Journal of the S.P.R.* (February 1948, p. 179). He pointed out that [[marianne-foyster|Mrs Foyster]] had a distinctive habit of dotting the letter 'i' in her name by means of a downward dash rather than the usual dot — and that this same unusual feature appeared in at least one of the wall-messages at Borley Rectory.

This observation was cited by the SPR investigators in Chapter V of the 1956 report as additional support for their conclusion that Mrs Foyster was the author of the wall-writings:

> "As Mr B. Nisbet has pointed out (*Journal of the S.P.R.*, February 1948, p. 179) there is the matter of Mrs Foyster's curious way of dotting the 'i' in her Christian name by means of a downward dash in her normal signature, which is also a feature of the lower wall message."

The investigators combined Nisbet's observation with testimony from [[miss-mary-braithwaite|Miss Mary Braithwaite J.P.]] (who described the writing as "undoubtedly Mrs Foyster's") and [[sir-john-braithwaite|Sir John Braithwaite]] (who concluded "obviously done by Mrs Foyster"), and with their own comparison of the wall-message letterforms with Mrs Foyster's signature on the response she wrote beneath one of the messages ("I cannot understand. Tell me more").
