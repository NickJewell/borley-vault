# Joanna Waldegrave / Sister Theodosia

A Waldegrave woman from the Borley branch of the family who became a Brussels Benedictine nun and was provisionally identified by Ivan Banks as a possible candidate for the ghost nun of Borley Rectory — before his research eliminated her.

## Background

Joanna Waldegrave entered the Brussels English Benedictine community in 1663, at the age of fifteen, and was professed a nun in 1666. Her monastic name was Sister Theodosia.

The date of her profession — 1666 — caught Banks's attention, because the planchette sessions of 1937 consistently produced the claim that the ghost nun "Marie Lairre" was murdered at Borley on May 16th or 17th, **1667** — just one year after Joanna's final vows. Furthermore, according to Banks's calculation, Joanna would have been **nineteen years old** in 1667, which matches the entity's stated age at death.

## Banks's Analysis

Banks noted several intriguing convergences:

- The planchette sessions produced the names "Janen" and "Jeane Waldegrave." Banks asks: could the entity have been attempting to convey "Joanna"?
- Examining the planchette writing reproduced in *The End of Borley Rectory* (p. 226), Banks suggests the letter taken to be "L" at the start of "Lairre" could be a "J," making the word "Joanne" or "Joanna."
- The seance contact from Streatham (Helen Glanville, October 1937) produced the entity name "Lairre." Banks wonders whether "J" was misread as "L" in the original handwriting.

> "Was it perhaps the name Joanna that was meant to be conveyed to the sitters at those sessions? On top of all this, the Borley nun, or Marie Lairre as the case may be, is supposed to have been 19 years old at the time of her murder, if murder it was. In 1667, Joanna Waldegrave would have been 19 years of age!"

## Elimination

Banks's research into the Brussels archives definitively resolved the question: Joanna Waldegrave did not die in 1667. She became **Abbess** of the Brussels Benedictines at some point, and the chronicles list her death year as **1719** — fifty-two years after the supposed murder of Marie Lairre.

Her long life as Abbess rules her out entirely as a murder victim. Banks then refocuses attention on her near-contemporary, [[barbara-waldegrave|Barbara Waldegrave (Sister Apollonia)]], who *did* die young, in 1638 aged about 36.

## Lier Theory Connexion

Banks was also investigating whether "Lairre" might be a corruption of "Lier" (or "Lierre"), a town in Belgium with Carmelite English nuns. He discovered that although Joanna/Theodosia did exist and was a Brussels Benedictine, the two orders (Lier Carmelites and Brussels Benedictines) never communicated with each other, further closing off that line of enquiry.
