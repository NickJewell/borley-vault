# Gilbert Hayes

Film comedian; appeared in approximately 350 films; had travelled 75,000 miles in two years; had adventured widely including seal-hunting in a ninety-ton schooner. His own description: "the strangest things in my life" were his September 1939 visit to Borley.

## The Tea Garden Adventure

In September 1939, Hayes and his wife answered an advertisement in *Dalton's Weekly* for a property near Sudbury suitable for conversion to tea-gardens. The address was given as "Borley Priory" — Hayes had never heard of the haunting and did not know who [[harry-price|Harry Price]] was.

Walking the grounds, Hayes heard footsteps following him continuously — through the lean-to greenhouse, round the house, and up to the well. He assumed it was his wife. He spoke to her over his shoulder throughout, explaining the property's potential. The footsteps stopped at the well. When he turned to look, no one was there. He then discovered his wife had never been with him at all — she had gone to fetch the key and only joined him at the well.

Upstairs in the cottage (the rooms over the stables where [[edward-cooper|Edward Cooper]] had lived), Hayes found some grocery receipts in a paper hold-all, which he folded and stuffed in. He walked fifteen paces. When he turned back, the bills had been relocated two feet to a shelf. His wife, who had been elsewhere, confirmed the bills had been moved. Meanwhile, his wife had heard someone moving upstairs while she was there — which proved to be no one, as Hayes was outside.

When told the name "Borley Priory," a postman at Sudbury station identified it at once as "the most haunted house in all England" and described the psychical research investigations.

Hayes did not rent the property. Writing in his letter of August 8, 1943: "I don't know Harry Price; he doesn't know me; but our experiences are facts."

> **Note:** Price identifies Hayes's cottage experience as analogous to the haunting of the [[rectory-cottage|Rectory Cottage]] which [[edward-cooper|Cooper]] and [[james-turner|Turner]] also experienced in the same rooms.
