# Rev. Henry Charles Brereton Foyster

Cousin of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster]]; son of [[rev-henry-brereton-foyster|Rev. Henry Brereton Foyster]]. He inherited the family living of **St Clement's, Hastings**, from his father in **1906** [2], in the standard pattern of the Foyster clerical dynasty in which lucrative church livings passed directly from father to son [1, 2].

## Significance

His inheritance of St Clement's in 1906 — rather than its passing to his cousin [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] — is one of the clues Robert Wood reads as evidence of an early scandal in Lionel's career. By 1906, Lionel was a fully ordained priest with three years of experience, yet the family patronage went within the direct line rather than to him.

Wood lists him among the five Foysters in the 1911 edition of *Crockford's Clerical Directory* (all related), illustrating the dynasty's extent and the network of relatives who would have known if Lionel was in genuine distress during his later years of supposed "imprisonment" by [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]].

His smooth succession serves as a stark historical contrast to the circumstances surrounding his cousin Lionel: while Henry Charles Brereton was readily given St Clement's by his father, Lionel was inexplicably bypassed for the equally desirable family living of All Saints' by his own father. Wood uses this comparison to argue that there must have been some underlying family scandal or difficulty that forced Lionel to be excluded from the family's direct patronage [4].
