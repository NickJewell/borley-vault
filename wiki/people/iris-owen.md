# Iris Owen

Parapsychology researcher and author, based in Toronto, Canada. Co-author (with Pauline Mitchell) of the 1979 report *Marianne's Story — The Alleged Haunting of Borley Rectory*, published in the *Journal of the British Society for Psychical Research* (September 1979). One of the last researchers to interview Marianne Foyster before her death.

## Background

Grew up in Cambridgeshire, England — "not very far away" from Borley. Read about the Borley haunting in the 1930s as it was happening. After the war, met and married **George Owen** (scholar of poltergeist phenomena) and lived in Cambridge for 20 years, where they met [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]] and became interested in psychical research.

Moved to Toronto with her husband. Became co-founder and researcher at the **New Horizons Foundation**, a parapsychology research organisation. George Owen authored *Can We Explain the Poltergeist?*

## The Philip Experiment (1972)

With a group of eight people, Owen conducted a landmark experiment: creating a wholly fictional historical character named "Philip" and attempting to communicate with him through séances.

Philip — invented, with a completely fabricated biography — communicated through rapping, table-moving, and switching lights on and off. He displayed consistent character traits, a sense of humour, and performed on live television. **Yet there never was a real Philip.**

Key implications for Borley:
- Séance communicators identify as whoever participants expect
- Planchette messages reflect participants' subconscious minds
- The Blue Room séance at which "Harry Bull" appeared was most likely this type of communication
- The planchette sessions identifying "Marie Lairre" are similarly explicable

## Contact with Marianne (1977–1986)

Pauline Mitchell tracked down Marianne in La Crosse, Wisconsin in 1977 and enlisted Owen's help. Owen corresponded with and interviewed Marianne over several years under a promise of confidentiality (nothing published until Marianne's death). The report was written in 1986; the full version was circulated privately to a few investigators.

Owen's assessment of Marianne: "I completely believe that her version of what happened is the correct one." She described her as "a delightful person — witty, charming... clever — but someone to whom life had dealt a hard hand."

Her central finding: the dominant factor in the Foysters' lives was not the haunting but **Lionel's terminal prognosis** and their financial anxiety. Lionel conceived a fictional Borley book to make money, as Walter Hubbell had with the Amherst poltergeist. Most Foyster-period phenomena were either this fiction or genuine minor poltergeist activity.

Owen believed Edwin Whitehouse ([[dom-richard-whitehouse]]) was the most likely focus for any genuine poltergeist events during the Foyster period.

## Contact with Vincent O'Neil

In May 1995, O'Neil wrote to Owen through the Parapsychology Foundation; she replied within two weeks. Over subsequent months she became "one of my most ardent supporters, supplying me with vast quantities of research and books." She gave him a first edition of *The Most Haunted House* and all her notes for the New Horizons report.

Owen died before the publication of O'Neil's web book.
