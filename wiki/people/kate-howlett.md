# Kate Howlett

Framlingham insurance agent; birth mother of **Peter** (later **Robert Vincent O'Neil**, known as "Vince"), the baby [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] adopted in October 1945 and took to America as her son by [[robert-oneil|Robert O'Neil]].

## Background

Kate Howlett came to collect insurance premiums from [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] in the Rendlesham area during the later war years. She confided in Marianne that she had been having an affair with an American serviceman who had returned home. When her husband returned from service and discovered she was pregnant with the American's child, he agreed to reconcile only on the condition she had the baby aborted. Kate didn't know what to do.

[[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] undertook to take the baby when it was born. Mrs Howlett gave birth to a little boy — **Peter** — on 8 October 1945.

## The Adoption and Deception

Marianne immediately wrote to [[robert-oneil|Robert O'Neil]] (her new American boyfriend, about to be demobilised to France) saying he was now the father of a baby boy named after him, **Robert Vincent**. The naive and devout Catholic O'Neil was "thrilled and proud." Marianne sent photographs of "Junior" to O'Neil's mother in Wisconsin.

This deception was central to Marianne's plan to emigrate to America: by presenting Kate Howlett's baby as O'Neil's son, she secured the marriage, the visa, and the passage to a new life in America. The child — Robert Vincent O'Neil, called "Vince" — was brought up by Marianne in America and knew nothing of his true origins.

## Significance

Kate Howlett's surrender of her baby under difficult circumstances set in motion the final chapter of Marianne's story. Her son Vince would later become the means by which the full history of his mother's past was uncovered, via his genealogical internet searches in 1994.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Kate Howlett** (born 1906) is a vital figure in the post-war biographical history of Marianne Foyster. A woman from Framlingham, she was the biological mother of the "war baby" that Marianne adopted, Peter Richard Howlett (later known as Vincent O'Neil), who subsequently wrote about his search for his mother's identity in *The Ghosts That Will Not Die*. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following biographical details, specific events, quotes, and a date contradiction regarding her life:

**Early Life, Personality, and Family**
*   **Birth and Siblings:** Kate was born in **1906** [1]. She came from a family that included a brother who was a lay preacher and two brothers who fought in World War I with the Coldstream Guards [1]. 
*   **Education and Traits:** She attended Helmingham School, where she was highly respected and clever enough to be **"put in charge when the teacher had to leave the room"** [1]. The sources record her as a bright woman who took piano lessons and enjoyed crossword puzzles [1]. However, she also suffered from chronic depression, a trait her biological son believed he inherited [2]. 

**Marriage, the World War II Affair, and the Ultimatum**
*   **A Difficult Marriage:** Kate was married to a master builder, though the union was reportedly unhappy [3]. She worked hard her entire life, and during the 1940s, she made a living collecting insurance premiums [1, 4].
*   **The Affair:** During the Second World War, while her husband was away, Kate had an affair with an American serviceman [4]. A relative later reflected that **"Katie was never so happy as during those two years"** she spent with the American [3]. 
*   **The Ultimatum:** When her husband was demobbed and returned home, he discovered she was pregnant with the American's child [4]. He offered to reconcile with her, but gave her a strict ultimatum: **she had to have the baby aborted** [4]. 

**Meeting Marianne Foyster and a Date Contradiction**
Distraught and unsure of what to do, Kate met Marianne Foyster (who was then living at Deben Avenue in Martlesham) while calling at her house to collect insurance premiums [4]. Kate confessed her predicament, and Marianne—who had a habit of adopting children—offered to take the baby once it was born [4, 5]. 

*   **The Date Contradiction:** There is a minor contradiction in the texts regarding the exact date Kate gave birth to the child. One historical summary states she gave birth on **October 8, 1945** [4], while the official chronology explicitly dates the birth to **October 9, 1945** [6]. 
*   **Naming the Child:** Kate named the boy **Peter Richard Howlett**. (Her biological son later speculated that "Peter" was likely after a cousin, but since "Richard" was an unfamiliar name in the family, he wondered if it might have been the name of his American father) [3]. 
*   **The Handover:** Marianne took the baby in **November 1945** [6], eventually having him baptized as Robert Vincent O'Neil Jr. on July 11, 1946, before taking him to America [7]. 

**Later Life and Death**
Kate Howlett remained in England and had other children with her husband, including her eldest son John (born June 1925), a son named Billy, and another son born in 1948 [8]. Her husband eventually died of coronary thrombosis [3]. 

Kate died in **1980** from congestion, having also suffered from breast cancer [1]. Having lived a life of hard work, the sources note the poignant detail that **"she managed to save just enough when it was all over to pay for her cremation,"** which took place in Ipswich [1, 9]. 

**The "Howlett" Alias**
In a bizarre footnote to her connection with the Borley saga, Marianne Foyster later "borrowed" Kate's surname as part of a disguise in the United States. A fanciful person, Marianne whimsically registered at a local American school district under the alias **"Ena Howlett O'Neil"** [10].
