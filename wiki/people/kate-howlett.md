# Kate Howlett

Framlingham insurance agent (born 1906); birth mother of **Peter** (later **Robert Vincent O'Neil**, known as "Vince"), the baby [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] adopted in October 1945 and took to America as her son by [[robert-oneil|Robert O'Neil]].

## Background

Kate was born in 1906 into a family that included a brother who was a lay preacher and two brothers who fought in World War I with the Coldstream Guards. She attended Helmingham School, where she was highly respected and reportedly clever enough to be put in charge when the teacher had to leave the room. She took piano lessons, enjoyed crossword puzzles, and — according to her biological son — suffered from chronic depression, a trait he believed he inherited.

Kate married a master builder, though the union was reportedly unhappy, and worked hard her entire life. During the 1940s she made a living collecting insurance premiums.

## The Affair and the Ultimatum

During the Second World War, while her husband was away, Kate had an affair with an American serviceman. A relative later reflected that "Katie was never so happy as during those two years" she spent with the American. When her husband was demobbed and returned home, he discovered she was pregnant with the American's child. He offered to reconcile but gave her a strict ultimatum: she had to have the baby aborted.

## Meeting Marianne Foyster

Distraught and unsure what to do, Kate met [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] (then living at Deben Avenue in Martlesham) while calling at her house to collect insurance premiums. She confided her predicament, and Marianne — who had a habit of adopting children — offered to take the baby once it was born.

> **Contradiction:** One historical summary states Kate gave birth on October 8, 1945; the official chronology explicitly dates the birth to October 9, 1945.

Kate named the boy **Peter Richard Howlett**. Her biological son later speculated that "Peter" was likely after a cousin; since "Richard" was an unfamiliar name in the family, he wondered if it might have been the name of his American father.

## The Adoption and Deception

Marianne took the baby in November 1945 and immediately wrote to [[robert-oneil|Robert O'Neil]] (her new American boyfriend, about to be demobilised from France) saying he was now the father of a baby boy named after him, **Robert Vincent**. The naive and devout Catholic O'Neil was reportedly "thrilled and proud." Marianne sent photographs of "Junior" to O'Neil's mother in Wisconsin. The child was baptized as Robert Vincent O'Neil Jr. on July 11, 1946 before Marianne took him to America.

This deception was central to Marianne's plan to emigrate: by presenting Kate Howlett's baby as O'Neil's son, she secured the marriage, the visa, and the passage to a new life. The child — Robert Vincent O'Neil, called "Vince" — was brought up by Marianne in America and knew nothing of his true origins. In a later footnote to her connection with the Borley saga, Marianne whimsically registered at a local American school district under the alias **"Ena Howlett O'Neil,"** borrowing Kate's surname as part of a disguise.

## Later Life and Death

Kate remained in England and had other children with her husband, including her eldest son John (born June 1925), a son named Billy, and another son born in 1948. Her husband eventually died of coronary thrombosis. Kate died in **1980** from congestion, having also suffered from breast cancer. The sources note the poignant detail that she managed to save just enough when it was all over to pay for her cremation, which took place in Ipswich.

## Significance

Kate Howlett's surrender of her baby under difficult circumstances set in motion the final chapter of Marianne's story. Her son Vince would later become the means by which the full history of his mother's past was uncovered, via his genealogical internet searches in 1994.
