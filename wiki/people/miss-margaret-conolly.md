# Miss Margaret Anne Conolly

One of [[gerard-kelman|Gerard Kelman]]'s companions on a July 1966 overnight visit to Borley. In the early hours of Sunday 24 July, at approximately 5 am, she provided a third independent account of the figure seen by Kelman and her sister [[miss-jane-conolly|Miss Jane Conolly]].

## Her Account

> "I was standing by the gate of the cottage looking down the gravel path at the shed. I heard footfalls on the gravel and thinking it was the owner of the property, turned to leave. Turning back I saw a figure about halfway down the pathway but it faded into the halflight before I could distinguish who or what it was. Then I noticed a slight movement by the shed and gradually could make out the lower half of a figure standing by the shed. It seemed to fade then reappear gradually, each time getting nearer. But there was no definite movement. This happened twice then it took two steps backwards to the left and faded into or behind the tree. The only time I was aware of definite movement was when it retreated. I could only see part of the figure (from the waist down), the garments were of pale grey or blue and long, flaring slightly at the bottom."

Unlike [[miss-jane-conolly|Jane Conolly]], who could see the figure from the waist up, Margaret could only see the lower portion — a difference consistent with each observing from a different angle and distance.

Underwood and Tabori noted: "It is characteristic that of these three witnesses one described what he saw as a 'vaguely human shape', another saw the figure more or less plainly from the waist upwards and the third witness could see only the lower part."

**Miss Margaret Anne Conolly** (sometimes recorded in chronological entries as **Margaret Anne Connally**) was a visitor to the ruined site of Borley Rectory who provided an independent, first-hand account of a ghostly sighting in the 1960s. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific details, dates, and quotes regarding her experience, particularly how it compared to her fellow witnesses:

**The Visit of July 23–24, 1966**
Miss Conolly paid an overnight visit to Borley over the weekend of **July 23–24, 1966**. She was accompanied by a ghost-hunter from Buckhurst Hill named Gerald Kelman, and another woman named Miss Jane Conolly [1-3]. 

**Auditory and Visual Phenomena**
At around 5:00 a.m. on July 24, in the half-light just before daybreak, the group experienced a notable paranormal event [1, 2]. According to Margaret Anne Conolly's own independent written report:
*   She was **"standing by the gate of the cottage looking down the gravel path at the shed"** when she first heard footfalls on the gravel. Thinking the noise was just the owner of the property, she initially turned to leave [4].
*   Upon turning back, she caught sight of a figure halfway down the path, but it **"faded into the halflight before I could distinguish who or what it was"** [4].
*   Shortly after, she noticed a slight movement near the shed and gradually made out the **"lower half of a figure standing by the shed"** [4].

**Specific Observations and Quotes**
Miss Conolly's account provided distinct details on how the apparition moved and what it wore:
*   **The Movement:** She noted that the figure seemed to **"fade then reappear gradually, each time getting nearer,"** even though she saw no definite movement [4]. After this happened twice, the entity **"took two steps backwards to the left and faded into or behind the tree"** [4]. She explicitly recorded that the only time she was aware of definite movement was when the figure retreated [4]. 
*   **The Appearance:** She reported that she could **"only see part of the figure (from the waist down)"** and described its garments as being **"of pale grey or blue and long, flaring slightly at the bottom"** [4].

**Contradictions and Witness Discrepancies**
A fascinating aspect of the Borley records is how Margaret Anne Conolly's sighting contrasts with the other two witnesses who were present at the exact same time. The texts highlight the subjective nature of the apparition:
*   **Gerald Kelman** described seeing a "vaguely human shape" that was covered from head to toe in a flowing garment [3].
*   **Jane Conolly** saw the figure plainly from the broad shoulders down to the waist, noting that the lower half was completely blurred [5].
*   **Margaret Anne Conolly**, conversely, could **see only the lower part of the figure** from the waist down [4, 6].
