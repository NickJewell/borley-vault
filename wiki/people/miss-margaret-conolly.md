# Miss Margaret Anne Conolly

One of [[gerard-kelman|Gerard Kelman]]'s companions on a July 1966 overnight visit to Borley. Her surname is also recorded in chronological entries as **Margaret Anne Connally**. In the early hours of Sunday 24 July, at approximately 5 a.m., she provided a third independent account of the figure seen by Kelman and her companion [[miss-jane-conolly|Miss Jane Conolly]].

## The Visit of July 23–24, 1966

Miss Conolly paid an overnight visit to the ruined site of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] over the weekend of **23–24 July 1966**, accompanied by ghost-hunter Gerald Kelman from Buckhurst Hill, Essex, and [[miss-jane-conolly|Miss Jane Conolly]] [1–3].

## The Apparition — 5 a.m., 24 July

At around 5:00 a.m. in the half-light just before daybreak, Margaret Anne Conolly was **"standing by the gate of the cottage looking down the gravel path at the shed"** when she first heard footfalls on the gravel. Thinking the noise was just the owner of the property, she initially turned to leave [4].

Upon turning back, she caught sight of a figure halfway down the path, but it **"faded into the halflight before I could distinguish who or what it was"** [4]. Shortly after, she noticed a slight movement near the shed and gradually made out the **lower half of a figure** standing by the shed. She recorded:

> "I was standing by the gate of the cottage looking down the gravel path at the shed. I heard footfalls on the gravel and thinking it was the owner of the property, turned to leave. Turning back I saw a figure about halfway down the pathway but it faded into the halflight before I could distinguish who or what it was. Then I noticed a slight movement by the shed and gradually could make out the lower half of a figure standing by the shed. It seemed to fade then reappear gradually, each time getting nearer. But there was no definite movement. This happened twice then it took two steps backwards to the left and faded into or behind the tree. The only time I was aware of definite movement was when it retreated. I could only see part of the figure (from the waist down), the garments were of pale grey or blue and long, flaring slightly at the bottom." [4]

## Significance: Witness Discrepancies as Corroboration

A key aspect of this sighting is how the three simultaneous accounts differ from each other — yet in ways consistent with each observer standing at a different angle and distance:

- [[gerard-kelman|Kelman]] described a "vaguely human shape" covered head to toe in a flowing garment [3]
- [[miss-jane-conolly|Jane Conolly]] saw the figure plainly from the broad shoulders to the waist, with the lower half blurred [5]
- Margaret Anne Conolly could see **only the lower part**, from the waist down [4, 6]

Underwood and Tabori noted: "It is characteristic that of these three witnesses one described what he saw as a 'vaguely human shape', another saw the figure more or less plainly from the waist upwards and the third witness could see only the lower part." The partial but complementary nature of these accounts is treated as mutual corroboration of an actual three-dimensional form.
