# Miss May Walker

One of the party [[harry-price|Harry Price]] brought to [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] on 13 October 1931 for his first visit during the [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]] incumbency. The party comprised Price, Miss Walker, [[mrs-henry-richards|Mrs Henry Richards]], [[kathleen-goldney|Mrs K. M. Goldney]], and [[james-ballantyne|Mr James Ballantyne]] (chauffeur), all staying at the Bull Hotel in [[long-melford|Long Melford]].

## October 1931 Visit

During the supper that followed the house inspection, Miss Walker was dispensing the Sauterne when she discovered it smelled "like eau-de-cologne." At the same moment Mrs Richards's Burgundy turned to black ink. Miss Walker's role in the subsequent all-night sitting — during which the group confined themselves to the kitchen to test phenomena — is not separately recorded.

Miss Walker returned to London the following morning before Price, Mrs Richards, and Mrs Goldney went back to confront the Foysters. No further details about her background or subsequent involvement with Borley are recorded in *Ghosts of Borley*.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Miss May Walker** was a visitor to Borley Rectory who is recorded in the sources solely for her participation in a single, highly significant psychical investigation led by Harry Price. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific details, dates, and contradictions regarding her involvement:

**The Visit of October 13, 1931**
Miss Walker accompanied Harry Price to Borley Rectory on the evening of **October 13, 1931** [1, 2]. She traveled to the site from London alongside Price and two council members of the National Laboratory of Psychical Research—Mrs. Clarice Richards and Mrs. Kathleen 'Mollie' Goldney [2, 3]. The group was driven to the Rectory in Mrs. Richards's large Packard car by a chauffeur named James Ballantyne, arriving just as it struck eight o'clock [2, 3]. 

**Early Departure**
While Price, Goldney, and Richards stayed to conduct further investigations the following evening, the records explicitly note that **Miss May Walker returned to London by train early the next day** (October 14) [4, 5]. Consequently, she was absent on the second night when Price famously confronted the Rev. Lionel Foyster and directly accused his wife, Marianne, of fraudulently producing the poltergeist phenomena [4].

**Contradiction in the Foyster Manuscript**
Miss Walker's presence during this visit is central to a specific factual error highlighted by the authors of the skeptical 1956 *Borley Report*. In his manuscript *Fifteen Months in a Haunted House*, the Rev. Lionel Foyster inaccurately described the arrival of Price's party, stating: **"Mr Price arrived with another gentleman and two ladies"** [6]. Investigators point out that Foyster's memory was at fault here, as the party actually consisted of Price, his three female companions (Goldney, Richards, and Walker), and the chauffeur [6]. This discrepancy is used by critics to demonstrate that Foyster's recording of basic details was sometimes flawed [6].

The provided sources contain no further biographical details, personal quotes, or subsequent visits regarding Miss May Walker beyond her inclusion in the index of observers and her brief participation in this October 1931 investigation [7].
