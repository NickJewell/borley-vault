# Miss May Walker

A visitor to [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] during the [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]] incumbency, recorded in the sources solely for her participation in a single overnight investigation led by [[harry-price|Harry Price]].

## October 1931 Visit

Miss Walker was one of the party [[harry-price|Harry Price]] brought to [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] on **13 October 1931** for his first visit during the Foyster incumbency. The party comprised Price, Miss Walker, [[mrs-henry-richards|Mrs Henry Richards]], [[kathleen-goldney|Mrs K. M. Goldney]], and [[james-ballantyne|Mr James Ballantyne]] (chauffeur), all travelling from London in Mrs Richards's large Packard car and arriving just as it struck eight o'clock [2, 3]. They stayed at the Bull Hotel in Long Melford.

During the supper that followed the house inspection, Miss Walker was dispensing the Sauterne when she discovered it smelled "like eau-de-cologne." At the same moment, Mrs Richards's Burgundy turned to black ink — one of the [[wine-into-ink-trick|wine-into-ink]] incidents documented from the Foyster period. Miss Walker's role in the subsequent all-night sitting — during which the group confined themselves to the kitchen to test phenomena — is not separately recorded.

## Early Departure

While Price, Goldney, and Richards stayed to conduct further investigations the following evening, Miss Walker **returned to London by train early on 14 October** [4, 5]. She was consequently absent when Price famously confronted [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]] and directly accused [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] of fraudulently producing the poltergeist phenomena [4].

## Contradiction in the Foyster Manuscript

Miss Walker's presence is central to a specific factual error highlighted in the 1956 *Borley Report*. In his manuscript *Fifteen Months in a Haunted House*, [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Foyster]] inaccurately described the arrival of Price's party as "Mr Price arrived with another gentleman and two ladies" [6]. Investigators point out that the party actually consisted of Price, three female companions (Goldney, Richards, and Walker), and the chauffeur — demonstrating that Foyster's recording of basic details was sometimes unreliable [6].

The sources contain no further biographical details, personal quotes, or subsequent visits regarding Miss May Walker beyond her inclusion in the index of observers [7].
