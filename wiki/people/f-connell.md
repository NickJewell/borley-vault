# F. Connell

Frank (F.) Connell was an investigator who carried out a series of observational vigils at Borley between 1970 and 1973 alongside colleagues [[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth|Geoffrey Croom-Hollingsworth]] and [[roy-potter|Roy Potter]] [1, 2]. He was a regular companion in their extended series of investigations and was present at the most significant sighting of the investigation — the June 1970 twelve-minute [[the-nun|Nun]] encounter.

## Investigative Methods

Connell and his companions took a modern, technological approach to their vigils at Borley. To assist in their overnight observations, the three men employed **infra-red photography, sound-recording apparatus, and portable two-way radios (walkie-talkies)** to maintain contact while stationed at different vantage points [1, 2].

## Earlier Visits and the Church Path Sighting

Connell accompanied Croom-Hollingsworth during a visit when they reported seeing a "shrouded figure walking down the church path" at 12:20 am on 20 June — the same night as the later nun sighting. He was one of the two who also saw what appeared to be a light in a chancel window of the locked church moving toward the altar.

## June 1970 Nun Sighting

Connell is best known for his participation in a dramatic sighting of the famous ghost nun on the bright, moonlit night of **20 June 1970** [1, 2].

At 1:50 a.m., Croom-Hollingsworth — keeping watch in the back garden of a bungalow that occupied part of the old [[nuns-walk|"Nun's Walk"]] — felt extremely cold and saw a figure glide across the gardens, appearing to move about a foot above the ground and passing cleanly through an old fence. He immediately contacted Connell and Potter via the two-way radio, reporting that he had seen an apparition and that **"she" was headed their way** [1, 2].

Connell remained stationed on the roadway between [[borley-church|Borley Church]] and the [[rectory-cottage|Rectory Cottage]] as a control point while Potter went to join Croom-Hollingsworth [1, 2]. Just before the radio message came through, Potter suddenly became aware of feeling exceedingly cold. Alerted by the message, Connell and Potter looked in the expected direction. Potter ran down the road and saw what appeared to be two figures close together — one being Croom-Hollingsworth and the other the ghost [1]. **Connell also claimed to have seen the figure himself** [1].

Croom-Hollingsworth described the apparition not as the young novice normally reported in Borley lore but specifically as a **"woman in her sixties"** [1].

## Follow-Up and Documentation

Following this encounter, Connell, Potter, and Croom-Hollingsworth **all submitted independent written reports** of their experiences to [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] [1]. Underwood and [[paul-tabori|Paul Tabori]] met with all three men again on 13 July 1972 to discuss the experience.
