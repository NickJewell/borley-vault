# F. Connell

A regular companion of [[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth|G. Croom-Hollingsworth]] and [[roy-potter|R. Potter]] in their extended series of investigations at Borley from 1970. Connell was present at the most significant sighting of the investigation — the June 1970 twelve-minute [[the-nun|Nun]] encounter.

## June 1970 Nun Sighting

In the early morning of 20 June 1970, at 1:50 am, while Croom-Hollingsworth was keeping watch in the Mills family's back garden, he contacted Connell and Potter by portable two-way radio to report that he had seen a figure and that it was headed toward the road. Connell remained on watch outside the [[rectory-cottage|Rectory cottage]] while Potter went to join Croom-Hollingsworth.

Potter and Connell had been running up and down the road "waiting for the Nun to appear" when the second appearance was called in. While Potter entered the back garden of the Mills bungalow, Connell remained stationed at the cottage entrance as a control point. Potter then saw the figure — clothed in grey material "draped over her head" — turn and walk through the fence, cross a ditch, and disappear at a pile of rubble in the direction of the field behind the old Rectory grounds.

All three men supplied a written report, and Underwood and Tabori met with them again on 13 July 1972 to discuss the experience.

## Earlier Visits

Connell also accompanied Croom-Hollingsworth during a June 1970 visit when they reported seeing a "shrouded figure walking down the church path" at 12:20 am on 20 June (the same night as the later nun sighting). He was one of the two who saw what appeared to be a light in a chancel window of the locked church moving toward the altar.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Frank (F.) Connell** was an investigator who carried out a series of observational vigils at Borley between 1970 and 1973 alongside his colleagues, Geoffrey Croom-Hollingsworth and Roy Potter [1, 2]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following facts, dates, and events regarding his involvement in the case:

**Investigative Methods**
Connell and his companions took a modern, technological approach to their vigils at Borley. To assist in their overnight observations, the three men employed **infra-red photography, sound-recording apparatus, and portable two-way radios (walkie-talkies)** to maintain contact while stationed at different vantage points [1, 2]. 

**The Phantom Nun Sighting (June 20, 1970)**
Connell is best known for his participation in a dramatic sighting of the famous ghost nun on the bright, moonlit night of **June 20, 1970** [1, 2]. 

Shortly after midnight, a shrouded figure was seen walking along the church path before disappearing behind some yew trees [1]. About an hour and a half later, the group had a much closer encounter:
*   **The Positions:** Connell and Potter were stationed on the roadway between Borley Church and the Rectory Cottage. Croom-Hollingsworth was keeping watch in the back garden of a bungalow that occupied part of the old "Nun's Walk" [1, 2]. 
*   **The Apparition:** At 1:50 a.m., Croom-Hollingsworth felt extremely cold and saw a figure glide across the gardens, appearing to move about a foot above the ground and passing cleanly through an old fence. He immediately contacted Connell and Potter via the two-way radio, reporting that he had seen an apparition and that **"she" was headed their way** [1, 2].
*   **The Sighting:** Just before Croom-Hollingsworth's radio message came through, Potter suddenly became aware that he felt exceedingly cold. Alerted by the message, Connell and Potter looked in the direction of the expected path [2]. Potter ran down the road and clearly saw what appeared to be two figures close together—one being Croom-Hollingsworth and the other the ghost [1]. **Connell also claimed to have seen the figure himself** [1]. 

(A unique detail of this sighting is that Croom-Hollingsworth described the apparition not as the young novice normally reported in Borley lore, but specifically as a **"woman in her sixties"** [1].)

**Follow-Up and Documentation**
Following this remarkable encounter, Connell, Potter, and Croom-Hollingsworth **all submitted independent written reports** of their experiences to investigator Peter Underwood [1]. As a result of these submissions, Connell and his colleagues were subsequently interviewed several times regarding what they had witnessed that night [1].
