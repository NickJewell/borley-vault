# F. Connell

A regular companion of [[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth|G. Croom-Hollingsworth]] and [[roy-potter|R. Potter]] in their extended series of investigations at Borley from 1970. Connell was present at the most significant sighting of the investigation — the June 1970 twelve-minute [[the-nun|Nun]] encounter.

## June 1970 Nun Sighting

In the early morning of 20 June 1970, at 1:50 am, while Croom-Hollingsworth was keeping watch in the Mills family's back garden, he contacted Connell and Potter by portable two-way radio to report that he had seen a figure and that it was headed toward the road. Connell remained on watch outside the [[rectory-cottage|Rectory cottage]] while Potter went to join Croom-Hollingsworth.

Potter and Connell had been running up and down the road "waiting for the Nun to appear" when the second appearance was called in. While Potter entered the back garden of the Mills bungalow, Connell remained stationed at the cottage entrance as a control point. Potter then saw the figure — clothed in grey material "draped over her head" — turn and walk through the fence, cross a ditch, and disappear at a pile of rubble in the direction of the field behind the old Rectory grounds.

All three men supplied a written report, and Underwood and Tabori met with them again on 13 July 1972 to discuss the experience.

## Earlier Visits

Connell also accompanied Croom-Hollingsworth during a June 1970 visit when they reported seeing a "shrouded figure walking down the church path" at 12:20 am on 20 June (the same night as the later nun sighting). He was one of the two who saw what appeared to be a light in a chancel window of the locked church moving toward the altar.
