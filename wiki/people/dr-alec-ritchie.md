# Dr Alec Ritchie

A medical practitioner with a practice at Cavendish, another nearby village. Both Dr Ritchie and his son Ted have died. His son Ted described his father's experiences to Nancy Goodale, who relayed them on behalf of Babbs and Mathias. One of Dr Ritchie's patients during the Foyster period was Marianne Foyster.

## The Airborne Medicine Bottle

On a visit to [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] who was ill and in bed, Dr Ritchie witnessed a **medicine bottle rise from the bedside table, hover, and return** — without breaking and without any human assistance. He stated definitively that Marianne remained in bed and did not touch the bottle.

The authors note this is consistent with [[poltergeist-activity|poltergeist phenomena]] reported by other witnesses during the Foyster period: it occurred when Marianne could not have directly manipulated the object.

## The Staircase Force

On another visit to a bedridden Marianne, after leaving her room and descending the main staircase, the doctor experienced the sensation of being **forced and pushed down the stairs** by an invisible presence — with the strong impression that some entity wanted him to leave the house. No one else was on the staircase.

## Car Engine Failure

While driving along Hall Lane and reaching the section **between the church and the rectory**, Dr Ritchie's engine cut out without explanation. When he got the car moving again, it performed normally elsewhere. His son Ted had a similar experience at the same location years later. This pattern of vehicle malfunction at Borley is discussed by Babbs and Mathias in connection with the [[ley-lines-at-borley|ley lines]] hypothesis.
