# Felix Bull

Brother of [[harry-bull|Rev. Harry Foyster Bull]] and son of [[henry-bull|Rev. Henry Dawson Ellis Bull]]. After the death of his father, Felix became Rector of Pentlow — the Bull family's ancestral village four miles from Borley.

## Family Position

Felix is one of the thirteen children baptised at Borley Parish Church under Henry Dawson Bull, though his name does not appear in the baptismal register there. Banks suggests this is because Felix was "neither born nor baptised at Borley" — perhaps born at the family home in Pentlow before the family moved to the newly built Borley Rectory, or baptised elsewhere.

Banks notes that the Pentlow Rectory (now known as Pentlow Tower) remained in the Bull family's hands: "In later years, it passed to Harry Bull's brother Felix, and in his time, Harry recalled one of the maids there having the ability to make objects move by themselves."

## The Poltergeist Maid

The incident Banks records from Harry Bull's recollections is notable:

> "On one particular afternoon, Harry Bull had gone to Pentlow Rectory with a friend and the maid, upon being asked to show them her apparent powers, said nothing and went on with her chores, but as she did so, the **fire irons flew out across the drawing room floor with a clatter**."

This is one of very few documented instances of apparent poltergeist activity within the Bull family's orbit *before* the Borley haunting became public. Banks presents it as evidence that the family had some pre-existing familiarity with such phenomena — which may have shaped their relaxed attitude toward ghost-watching at Borley Rectory.

## Significance

Felix Bull does not otherwise feature in the Borley literature. His main function in Banks's account is to establish:
1. That poltergeist-type phenomena appeared in the Bull family's ambit prior to 1929
2. A further member of the Bull family line connected to the Pentlow rectory, which Banks notes was built by the same family as Borley Rectory and shares its distinctive red-brick architecture

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Rev. Felix Bull** (1852–1927) was an Anglican clergyman and a member of the prominent Bull family dynasty that dominated the ecclesiastical life of the Borley district for decades [1, 2]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following facts, events, quotes, and a surprisingly high number of textual contradictions regarding his life and connection to the paranormal legends:

**Biographical Details**
*   **Family:** Felix was the son of the Rev. Edward Bull and the younger brother of the Rev. Henry Dawson Ellis Bull (the builder and first occupant of Borley Rectory) [1, 2]. 
*   **Descendants:** He had at least one daughter who married a man named Yellowlee [3]. Their son later became a solicitor in the Welsh border town of Presteigne, but the family frequently returned to visit Pentlow [3]. 
*   **Residence:** Felix resided at Pentlow Rectory (often called Pentlow Tower), an eccentric red-brick building about four miles from Borley featuring a 95-foot high folly built by his father [2, 4, 5]. 

**The Pentlow Poltergeist (August 1886)**
Felix is featured in the Borley records primarily because of a spectacular poltergeist outbreak at his own home. In **August 1886**, Felix experienced intense supernatural disturbances and traveled to Borley Rectory for lunch to tell his family that he was "having trouble with a Poltergeist" [6]. 

The next day, his nephew Harry Bull and Harry's Oxford friend, P. Shaw Jeffrey, borrowed the family coach and drove over to Pentlow to investigate [6]. Felix was out when they arrived, but his cook directed them to a new housemaid named Mary, stating: **"Well, Master Harry, if there’s any foolishness going on, it’s all along of our new housemaid, that’s what I say!"** [6, 7].

Harry and Shaw Jeffrey found the maid making beds in the large best bedroom [6]. Addressing her directly, Harry challenged her: **"Well, Mary, cook says you can show us a few tricks. What about it?"** [6]. The maid remained completely silent, but immediately a tooth glass flew across the room from behind her back, circling gracefully to hit the door jamb just above Shaw Jeffrey's head [8]. This was followed by the room's fender and fire-irons flying across the floor with a loud clatter [5, 8]. 

**Contradictions in the Historical Record**
The Borley sources are plagued by a highly unusual number of contradictions and factual errors regarding Felix Bull's life:
*   **Incumbency Dates:** One source states that Felix was the Rector of Pentlow from **1877 to 1927** [1]. However, another passage claims he became Rector in **1887** [4]. 
*   **Relationship to Harry Bull:** The texts contain major genealogical confusion. While Felix is correctly identified as Henry D. E. Bull's younger brother [1, 2, 6] (which makes him Harry Bull's uncle), one passage mistakenly refers to Felix as **"Harry Bull's brother"** [5]. Even more bizarrely, another passage reverses the relationship completely, referring to Harry as Felix's uncle: *"When he died in 1927, the same year his uncle, Harry Bull, passed away..."* [4]. 
*   **Death and Chronology:** It is recorded that Felix and his nephew Harry Bull both died in the same year, **1927**, bringing the Bull family's long connection with the Pentlow and Borley parishes to a definitive end [4]. However, another passage contains a glaring mathematical contradiction, stating that Felix and his brother Henry were rectors concurrently, **"although Felix outlived Henry by 3 years"** [9]. Since Henry died in 1892 and Felix died in 1927, Felix actually outlived his brother by 35 years [2, 4, 9].
