# Kathleen King (née Finch)

The daughter of [[alfred-finch|Alfred]] and [[margaret-finch|Margaret Finch]], lifelong residents of Borley and among the most important local witnesses consulted by Babbs and Mathias for *Borley Rectory: The Final Analysis*. She contributed her own firsthand testimony about phenomena in [[borley-church|Borley Church]] and later supplied a significant body of historical photographs for the 2003 publication.

## The Sunday School Incident (1946)

One of the most striking incidents Kathleen King recalls occurred in **1946**, when she was approximately 13 years old, during a Sunday catechism class at [[borley-church|Borley Church]]. The children — including Kathleen — arrived and sat in the front pews waiting for their teacher, [[miss-byford-teacher|Miss Byford]]. After a few minutes they distinctly heard footsteps entering the church porch and the key being turned in the lock from outside. Kathleen called out **"We are here, Miss Byford"** — but was met with total silence.

The children became increasingly frightened. Kathleen attempted to calm them by suggesting a joke was being played on them, telling them that "everything would be all right in a few minutes." But when Miss Byford finally arrived, she was astonished to find that the church door had been locked against her.

[[rev-ac-henning|Rev. Henning]] initially suspected a practical joke and questioned Kathleen about the incident. She informed him she had spoken with the girls living at the Rectory cottage, but they strongly denied having played a prank. Henning concluded the locking was paranormal.

Years later, when discussing the episode with researchers, Kathleen stated she had never been able to come to a definite conclusion about what had actually happened. She conceded that human trickery could not be entirely ruled out, but pointed to a specific detail that always puzzled her: she **"does not understand why footsteps were heard approaching the door before the key was turned in the lock, but that this was not followed by the sound of departing footsteps."**

> Years later, when asked her opinion, Kathleen King told the authors: "I never have been able to come to any definite conclusion."

## Memories of Rev. Henning

Kathleen remembered [[rev-ac-henning|Rev. Henning]] and his wife well from her youth. She recalled that he "would become very annoyed whenever anyone expressed their doubts about the matter" of the haunting.

## Her Parents' Legacy and Contributions to Research

As the daughter of Alfred and Margaret Finch, Kathleen King forms the third generation of family testimony. Her parents' recollections of life at Borley Rectory during the 1920s under the Bulls are the most detailed firsthand accounts of day-to-day conditions in the house. Kathleen's own experiences in the church add a dimension her parents could not provide.

In her later years, she assisted investigators Edward Babbs and [[claudine-mathias|Claudine Mathias]] with their research for *Borley Rectory – The Final Analysis* (2003), supplying a significant number of original historical photographs for the publication (Figures 4, 8, 9, 17, 18, 30, and 91, as well as images on the book's jacket).
