# Kathleen King (née Finch)

The daughter of [[alfred-finch|Alfred]] and [[margaret-finch|Margaret Finch]], lifelong residents of Borley and among the most important local witnesses consulted by Babbs and Mathias for *Borley Rectory: The Final Analysis*. She contributed her own firsthand testimony about phenomena in Borley Church.

## The Sunday School Incident

One of the most striking incidents Kathleen King recalls occurred when she was about 13 years old, during a Sunday school class at Borley Church. The children — including Kathleen — arrived and sat in the front pews waiting for their teacher, Miss Byford. After a few minutes they distinctly heard footsteps entering the church porch and the key being turned in the lock from outside. Kathleen called out "We are here, Miss Byford!" but was met with total silence.

The children became increasingly frightened. Kathleen tried to calm them by suggesting it was a prank, but she herself could not understand why footsteps had been heard approaching the door before the lock turned — yet there were no departing footsteps afterward. When Miss Byford later arrived, she was astonished to find the door locked.

Kathleen reported this to [[rev-ac-henning|Rev Henning]], who investigated and was told by the girls living in the rectory cottage that they had not been involved. He concluded the locking was paranormal.

> Years later, when asked her opinion, Kathleen King told the authors: "I never have been able to come to any definite conclusion."

## Her Parents' Legacy

As the daughter of Alfred and Margaret Finch, Kathleen King forms the third generation of family testimony. Her parents' recollections of life at Borley Rectory during the 1920s under the Bulls are the most detailed firsthand accounts of day-to-day conditions in the house. Kathleen's own experiences in the church add a dimension her parents could not provide.
