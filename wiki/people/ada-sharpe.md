# Miss Ada M. Sharpe

Resident of "Beth-oni," Tackley, Oxfordshire. Between 1905 and 1908, Miss Sharpe's house was infested by a Poltergeist that made her life miserable — one of the few cases [[harry-price|Price]] cites where exorcism proved genuinely effective.

## The Hauntings (1905–1908)

For three years "cannon-bursts," bangs, bumps, flashes of light, hammerings on the roof, footsteps throughout the building, apparitions, and the flight of bedclothes during sleep afflicted Beth-oni. The phenomena persisted despite normal investigation.

## First Exorcism (July 12, 1907)

Miss Sharpe called in the **Rev. L. de Clare** ([[rev-l-de-clare|separate page]]), who exorcized the building with prayers, incense, and holy water on July 12, 1907. The atmosphere of the house immediately changed and all was quiet — **until the following January**, when phenomena resumed: "a dog walking round her bed," something pushing against the bedroom door, and a "form" huddled in her bed.

## Second Exorcism

Miss Sharpe then called in the **Rev. J. C. Fitzgerald** ([[rev-jc-fitzgerald|separate page]]), of the **Mirfield Community**, who exorcized the house again with the same technique. The phenomena stopped immediately. Writing five years later, Miss Sharpe confirmed there had been **no further disturbances**.

## Significance

Beth-oni is the principal example Price cites of successful exorcism in an extensive literature on the subject. Price uses the comparison with Borley to make the point that at Borley, the multiple exorcisms performed by Dom Richard Whitehouse and visiting clergy *failed*, while at Beth-oni two sequential exorcisms eventually produced permanent cessation. His explanation: the Borley entities were not evil spirits suitable for exorcism, but "a miserable mind" (in [[canon-phythian-adams|Phythian-Adams]]'s phrase) that needed Requiem Mass and Christian burial, not driving out.

It was not until the [[reburial-marie-lairre|reburial of 1945]] that the Borley phenomena appear to have genuinely diminished.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Miss Ada M. Sharpe** is not a direct participant in the Borley Rectory hauntings, but she features in the Borley literature as a comparative case study. In *The End of Borley Rectory*, Harry Price recounts her story to illustrate a rare example of a successful exorcism, contrasting it with his general observation that exorcisms of haunted houses are very seldom effective, usually only providing temporary relief, and sometimes even annoying the "entities" [1].

Based on the Borley sources, a thorough reference profile of her experiences includes the following details:

**The Poltergeist Infestation at 'Beth-oni' (1905–1908)**
Miss Sharpe occupied a house named **'Beth-oni' located in Tackley, Oxfordshire** [1]. Between **1905 and 1908**, her home was subjected to a severe poltergeist infestation that "made her life miserable" [1]. Over a period of three years, she endured intense and terrifying phenomena that nearly drove her out of the house [1]. The disturbances included apparitions, flashes of light, "cannon-bursts," bangs, bumps, hammerings on the roof, footsteps throughout the building, and the paranormal flight of her bed-clothes while she slept [1]. 

**The First Exorcism (July 1907)**
Desperate for relief, Miss Sharpe called in a priest, the **Rev. L. de Clare**, who exorcised the building using prayers, incense, and holy water on **July 12, 1907** [1]. Following this rite, the atmosphere of the house immediately changed, and the property remained entirely quiet for roughly six months [1]. 

**The Return of Phenomena (January 1908)**
The peace was broken the following January when the paranormal activity returned [1]. Miss Sharpe reported that she **"heard a dog walking round her bed"** [1]. The physical manifestations quickly escalated; on a later occasion, she was unable to close her bedroom door because an unseen force on the other side was physically pushing against it [1]. The haunting culminated when she discovered a terrifying, unidentified **"form" huddled up in her bed** [1]. 

**The Second Exorcism and Resolution**
Following the discovery of the "form" in her bed, Miss Sharpe called in a second clergyman, the **Rev. J. C. Fitzgerald of the Mirfield Community** [1]. He performed another exorcism using the "usual technique," and this time the rite was completely successful [1]. The paranormal phenomena ceased immediately [1]. Writing about her ordeal five years later, Miss Sharpe confirmed that there had been no further disturbances at 'Beth-oni' [1].
