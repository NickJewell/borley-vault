# Miss Ada M. Sharpe

Resident of "Beth-oni," Tackley, Oxfordshire. Between 1905 and 1908, Miss Sharpe's house was infested by a poltergeist that made her life miserable — one of the few cases [[harry-price|Price]] cites where exorcism proved genuinely effective.

## The Hauntings (1905–1908)

For three years "cannon-bursts," bangs, bumps, flashes of light, hammerings on the roof, footsteps throughout the building, apparitions, and the flight of bedclothes during sleep afflicted Beth-oni [1]. The phenomena persisted despite normal investigation and nearly drove Miss Sharpe out of the house entirely [1].

## First Exorcism (July 12, 1907)

Miss Sharpe called in the **Rev. L. de Clare** ([[rev-l-de-clare|separate page]]), who exorcised the building with prayers, incense, and holy water on July 12, 1907 [1]. Following the rite, the atmosphere of the house immediately changed and all was quiet [1] — **until the following January**, when phenomena resumed: reportedly "a dog walking round her bed," something pushing against the bedroom door, and a "form" huddled in her bed [1].

## Second Exorcism and Resolution

Miss Sharpe then called in the **Rev. J. C. Fitzgerald** ([[rev-jc-fitzgerald|separate page]]), of the **Mirfield Community**, who exorcised the house again with the "usual technique" [1]. This time the rite was completely successful [1]. The phenomena stopped immediately, and writing about her ordeal five years later, Miss Sharpe confirmed there had been **no further disturbances** at Beth-oni [1].

## Significance

Beth-oni is the principal example Price cites of successful exorcism in an extensive literature on the subject. Price uses the comparison with [[borley-rectory|Borley]] to make the point that at Borley, the multiple exorcisms performed by [[dom-richard-whitehouse|Dom Richard Whitehouse]] and visiting clergy *failed*, while at Beth-oni two sequential exorcisms eventually produced permanent cessation. His explanation: the Borley entities were not evil spirits suitable for exorcism, but "a miserable mind" (in [[canon-phythian-adams|Phythian-Adams]]'s phrase) that needed Requiem Mass and Christian burial, not driving out.

It was not until the [[reburial-marie-lairre|reburial of 1945]] that the Borley phenomena appear to have genuinely diminished.
