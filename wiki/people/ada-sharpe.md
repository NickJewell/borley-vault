# Miss Ada M. Sharpe

Resident of "Beth-oni," Tackley, Oxfordshire. Between 1905 and 1908, Miss Sharpe's house was infested by a Poltergeist that made her life miserable — one of the few cases [[harry-price|Price]] cites where exorcism proved genuinely effective.

## The Hauntings (1905–1908)

For three years "cannon-bursts," bangs, bumps, flashes of light, hammerings on the roof, footsteps throughout the building, apparitions, and the flight of bedclothes during sleep afflicted Beth-oni. The phenomena persisted despite normal investigation.

## First Exorcism (July 12, 1907)

Miss Sharpe called in the **Rev. L. de Clare** ([[rev-l-de-clare|separate page]]), who exorcized the building with prayers, incense, and holy water on July 12, 1907. The atmosphere of the house immediately changed and all was quiet — **until the following January**, when phenomena resumed: "a dog walking round her bed," something pushing against the bedroom door, and a "form" huddled in her bed.

## Second Exorcism

Miss Sharpe then called in the **Rev. J. C. Fitzgerald** ([[rev-jc-fitzgerald|separate page]]), of the **Mirfield Community**, who exorcized the house again with the same technique. The phenomena stopped immediately. Writing five years later, Miss Sharpe confirmed there had been **no further disturbances**.

## Significance

Beth-oni is the principal example Price cites of successful exorcism in an extensive literature on the subject. Price uses the comparison with Borley to make the point that at Borley, the multiple exorcisms performed by Dom Richard Whitehouse and visiting clergy *failed*, while at Beth-oni two sequential exorcisms eventually produced permanent cessation. His explanation: the Borley entities were not evil spirits suitable for exorcism, but "a miserable mind" (in [[canon-phythian-adams|Phythian-Adams]]'s phrase) that needed Requiem Mass and Christian burial, not driving out.

It was not until the [[reburial-marie-lairre|reburial of 1945]] that the Borley phenomena appear to have genuinely diminished.
