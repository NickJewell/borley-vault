# Rev. Harry Carpenter

Clergyman of 122 Woodstock Road, Oxford. His letter of October 30, 1941, forwarded to [[harry-price]] by [[mrs-chb-gowan|Mrs C. H. B. Gowan]], contains an important independent account of [[harry-bull|Harry Bull]]'s personal experiences of the Borley phenomena, heard directly from Bull during the First World War.

## The Wartime Account

Carpenter served as a special constable in the First World War, with Harry Bull as his superior officer. During patrol duties, Bull described experiences that he had had "years before":

> *"Walking home from Sudbury one night, he became aware of galloping horses; then he saw lights coming towards him and he stepped off the road to let the carriage pass. He saw distinctly one man or two on the box, driving; they had no heads, only hands and the lower part of the body. As he watched the whole thing vanished."*

A second incident, in daylight in the dining-room:

> *"A dog with him was agitated, and he saw in the conservatory a man dressed in the same coloured clothes as the coachman he had seen before. He got up and went into the conservatory; the man went out and vanished."*

## Significance

Carpenter's account is notable for several reasons:

1. **Harry Bull's own words**: Most testimony about Bull's experiences comes through his sisters after his death. Carpenter records Bull speaking in the first person about what he saw.
2. **The headless coachman**: The detail of headless (or rather upper-body-absent) figures on the coach box corroborates the testimony of [[ethel-bull]] about the same incident, as independently recorded by Price in *The Most Haunted House in England*.
3. **Dog reaction**: The dog's agitation in the conservatory parallels [[edward-cooper]]'s padding-dog experience and [[captain-gregson]]'s two lost spaniels — a consistent thread across decades.
4. **Independent pathway**: The account reached Price via an entirely independent chain: Mrs Gowan received it from her mother, who received it from Carpenter. It was thus free of contamination from Price's own published version.
