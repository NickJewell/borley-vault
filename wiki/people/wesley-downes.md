# Wesley H. Downes

Publisher and lay investigator who visited Borley many times, sometimes spending a night there. His writings on the case include *The Ghosts of Borley* (1993) — part of the Wesley's Essex Collection series — and he ran Wesley's Publications, which included the quarterly *Essex Ghosts and Hauntings* with periodical articles on Borley.

## Borley Involvement

As a friend of [[peter-underwood]], [[hilton-rowe-peter|Dr Peter Hilton-Rowe]] and many other Borley enthusiasts, Downes became deeply interested in the mysteries of Borley. He was also friendly with ghost writer Wesley H. Downes and Paul Moss-Kemp (see [[east-anglian-group]]).

Downes holds notes of various investigations by the [[east-anglian-group|East Anglian Group for Psychic Research Studies]], including details of their access to the [[borley-church-crypt|church crypt]], and documented multiple occasions when some of the lead Waldegrave coffins were found displaced when they had previously been straightened up, before the authorities sealed up the crypt entrance.

## The Campsey Ashe Vigil

Downes and Paul Moss-Kemp went to St John the Baptist Church at [[campsey-ashe]] on the anniversary of [[lionel-foyster]]'s death and held a vigil in the churchyard. According to their account (given to [[tony-broughall]]), around midnight they saw mist or smoke rise from Foyster's grave, move horizontally, then rise vertically and drift through the door of the closed and locked church. When Broughall asked what they did: "We ran for our lives."

## Publications

*The Ghosts of Borley* (1993) presents a condensed account of the traditional Borley story plus brief accounts of several modern investigations in Borley Church in the 1970s and 1980s, including the "veiled bride" photograph taken in the churchyard by Geoff and Anthea Holland.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Wesley H. Downes** was a dedicated **publisher, lay investigator, and ghost writer** who maintained a deep and ongoing interest in the mysteries of Borley Rectory [1, 2]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following biographical details, publications, specific investigations, and unique legends regarding his involvement in the case:

**Biographical Details and Professional Background**
*   **Professional Roles:** Downes was a **long-time member and area representative of the Ghost Club Society** [2]. 
*   **Publishing:** He founded and ran **Wesley’s Publications** for many years, which produced the quarterly magazine ***Essex Ghosts and Hauntings*** containing periodical articles on Borley [2]. 
*   **Personal Connections:** He was a friend of prominent Borley enthusiasts, including investigator Peter Underwood and Dr. Peter Hilton-Rowe [2]. Downes visited the Borley site many times and occasionally spent the night there [2].

**Publications**
*   Downes authored the 60-page book ***The Ghosts of Borley* (1993)** as part of the Wesley’s Essex Collection series [2, 3]. 
*   The first thirty pages were devoted to the Borley haunting, while the rest covered other haunted sites along the Essex and Suffolk border (such as Polstead and Acton) [3]. 
*   The book provided a condensed account of the traditional Borley story alongside brief accounts of modern investigations in Borley Church during the 1970s and 1980s, and uniquely featured the **"veiled bride" photograph** taken by Geoff and Anthea Holland [3].

**The 1990s Campsey Ashe Vigil**
One of the most dramatic events involving Downes occurred during the 1990s, when he and fellow investigator Paul Moss-Kemp went to St John the Baptist Church at Campsey Ashe to hold a night vigil on the anniversary of the Rev. Lionel Foyster’s death [1]. 
*   **The Sighting:** Around midnight, the two men claimed to see mist or smoke rise directly from Foyster’s grave [1]. 
*   They watched as the smoke moved horizontally, rose vertically, and then drifted across the churchyard until it passed straight through the door of the closed and locked church [1]. 
*   **The Quote:** When fellow researcher Tony Broughall later asked what they did after witnessing this, they simply admitted: **"We ran for our lives!"** [1].

**Archival Findings and Crypt Anomalies**
Downes acted as an archivist for other local investigators. Paul Moss-Kemp sent his personal notes, titled ‘About My Investigations,’ directly to Downes [4]. Through these records, Downes retained notes suggesting that on a number of occasions when the Borley church crypt was entered, **the coffins inside were found inexplicably displaced**, even though they had been previously straightened up [5]. 

**Gruesome and Lesser-Known Legends Recorded by Downes**
In his writings, Downes recorded a few highly disturbing local tales that deviate from the traditional Borley lore:
*   **The Cromwellian Crucifixion:** Downes documented a gruesome variation of the classic monk and nun theory, dating it to between 1655 and 1658 [6]. According to this report, a Catholic priest (named Dominic or Enoch) fell in love with a nun [6]. Following a "terrible tragedy" that "evoked the utmost evil of mankind," the nun was murdered [7]. The priest was then **nailed to a cross and crucified inside Borley Church** by Oliver Cromwell's soldiers [6, 7]. While he was still half dead, the soldiers cut him down and **hung him upside down in a nearby well**, leaving his body to fall into the water three days later [6, 7]. 
*   **The "Embodiment of Evil":** Downes also told a story about a 17th-century cottage near Borley Lodge that held the reputation of being haunted by a "Thing" described specifically as an **"embodiment of evil,"** though the local residents refused to discuss the matter [7].
