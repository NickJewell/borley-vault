# Wesley H. Downes

Publisher and lay investigator who visited Borley many times, sometimes spending a night there. His writings on the case include *The Ghosts of Borley* (1993) — part of the Wesley's Essex Collection series — and he ran Wesley's Publications, which included the quarterly *Essex Ghosts and Hauntings* with periodical articles on Borley.

## Borley Involvement

As a friend of [[peter-underwood]], [[hilton-rowe-peter|Dr Peter Hilton-Rowe]] and many other Borley enthusiasts, Downes became deeply interested in the mysteries of Borley. He was also friendly with ghost writer Wesley H. Downes and Paul Moss-Kemp (see [[east-anglian-group]]).

Downes holds notes of various investigations by the [[east-anglian-group|East Anglian Group for Psychic Research Studies]], including details of their access to the [[borley-church-crypt|church crypt]], and documented multiple occasions when some of the lead Waldegrave coffins were found displaced when they had previously been straightened up, before the authorities sealed up the crypt entrance.

## The Campsey Ashe Vigil

Downes and Paul Moss-Kemp went to St John the Baptist Church at [[campsey-ashe]] on the anniversary of [[lionel-foyster]]'s death and held a vigil in the churchyard. According to their account (given to [[tony-broughall]]), around midnight they saw mist or smoke rise from Foyster's grave, move horizontally, then rise vertically and drift through the door of the closed and locked church. When Broughall asked what they did: "We ran for our lives."

## Publications

*The Ghosts of Borley* (1993) presents a condensed account of the traditional Borley story plus brief accounts of several modern investigations in Borley Church in the 1970s and 1980s, including the "veiled bride" photograph taken in the churchyard by Geoff and Anthea Holland.
