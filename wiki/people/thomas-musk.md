# Thomas Musk

A local resident interviewed by Babbs and Mathias in November 1995. He had lived not far from Borley for many years and was interested in the alleged haunting.

## Camping Vigil — Late Autumn 1987

With three friends, Musk set up a tent and camping equipment near Borley church for an overnight watch. The early part of the night produced nothing. At approximately **4am**, all four men were awakened by the sound of **footsteps walking around their tent**. The night was cold and frosty and the footsteps produced a crisp sound on the whitened ground and leaves. The sounds lasted about one minute and then ceased. The tent flap was opened immediately — no one was outside.

## Organ Music from the Locked Church

About half an hour later, the group heard what appeared to be **music coming from Borley church**, which was locked. The music lasted **five to ten minutes**. Musk's description is detailed:
- Properly constructed music, not jumbled sounds
- Instrumental rather than vocal
- Sounded "**like an old gramophone record**" — which the authors note is an apt description of Borley church's small, old organ heard from outside a sealed building

This matches almost exactly the experiences of [[rev-ac-henning]] and Mrs Walrond in October 1947, E. W. Jephcott in September 1947, and [[maud-stearn]] in 1955.

## Mechanical Interference

The repeated tangling and destruction of tape equipment in Borley church (by the Harlow group and by Denny Densham's BBC team) is cited by Babbs and Mathias as potentially connected to electromagnetic effects of [[ley-lines-at-borley|ley lines]] intersecting at Borley.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Thomas Musk** was a local resident who lived near Borley for many years and maintained a strong interest in the alleged hauntings surrounding the site [1]. He was interviewed by investigators in November 1995 about an experience he had eight years prior [1]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following details regarding his involvement:

**The Autumn 1987 Vigil**
During the **late autumn of 1987**, Musk and three friends decided to conduct their own investigation by spending a whole night at Borley [1, 2]. They set up a tent and camping equipment near Borley Church to watch and listen for paranormal activity [1]. Disappointed by a lack of phenomena during the early part of the night, the group eventually went to sleep [1].

**The Phantom Footsteps**
At exactly **4:00 a.m.**, the four friends were suddenly awakened by the sound of footsteps walking around the outside of their tent [1]. Musk recalled that it was a cold, frosty morning, and the footsteps produced a distinct **"crisp sound on the whitened ground and leaves"** [1]. 
*   The sounds lasted for approximately one minute before ceasing [1]. 
*   The group was initially alarmed, thinking someone had come to order them to pack up their camp and leave [1]. 
*   When they immediately unfastened the tent flap to look outside, they found the area completely deserted, leaving them to realize there was no normal explanation for the noises [1]. The sources note that it is unknown whether Musk and his friends checked for physical footprints left in the frost [1].

**The Gramophone Music**
Approximately half an hour after the footsteps incident, the group experienced a second auditory anomaly [3]. They heard music emanating directly from the locked church, which lasted for **between five and ten minutes** [2, 3]. 
*   Musk specifically detailed that the sound was **"properly constructed music rather than jumbled sounds"**, and that it was entirely instrumental rather than vocal [3]. 
*   Most notably, Musk described the music as sounding exactly like an **"old gramophone record"** [2, 3]. 
*   Investigators later pointed out that this specific description was highly interesting, as the church possessed a relatively small and old organ (originally hand-pumped before being fitted with an electrical device) which would likely sound very similar to a scratchy gramophone record when heard from outside the locked building [3].
