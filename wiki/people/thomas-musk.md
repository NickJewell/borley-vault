# Thomas Musk

A local resident interviewed by Babbs and Mathias in November 1995. He had lived not far from Borley for many years and was interested in the alleged haunting.

## Camping Vigil — Late Autumn 1987

With three friends, Musk set up a tent and camping equipment near Borley church for an overnight watch. The early part of the night produced nothing. At approximately **4am**, all four men were awakened by the sound of **footsteps walking around their tent**. The night was cold and frosty and the footsteps produced a crisp sound on the whitened ground and leaves. The sounds lasted about one minute and then ceased. The tent flap was opened immediately — no one was outside.

## Organ Music from the Locked Church

About half an hour later, the group heard what appeared to be **music coming from Borley church**, which was locked. The music lasted **five to ten minutes**. Musk's description is detailed:
- Properly constructed music, not jumbled sounds
- Instrumental rather than vocal
- Sounded "**like an old gramophone record**" — which the authors note is an apt description of Borley church's small, old organ heard from outside a sealed building

This matches almost exactly the experiences of [[rev-ac-henning]] and Mrs Walrond in October 1947, E. W. Jephcott in September 1947, and [[maud-stearn]] in 1955.

## Mechanical Interference

The repeated tangling and destruction of tape equipment in Borley church (by the Harlow group and by Denny Densham's BBC team) is cited by Babbs and Mathias as potentially connected to electromagnetic effects of [[ley-lines-at-borley|ley lines]] intersecting at Borley.
