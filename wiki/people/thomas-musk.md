# Thomas Musk

A local resident who lived not far from Borley for many years and maintained a strong interest in the alleged haunting. He was interviewed by Babbs and Mathias in November 1995 about an experience he had eight years prior.

## Camping Vigil — Late Autumn 1987

During the late autumn of 1987, Musk and three friends decided to conduct their own investigation by spending a whole night at Borley. They set up a tent and camping equipment near [[borley-church]] to watch and listen for paranormal activity. Disappointed by a lack of phenomena during the early part of the night, the group eventually went to sleep.

At approximately **4:00 a.m.**, all four men were suddenly awakened by the sound of **footsteps walking around the outside of their tent**. The night was cold and frosty, and the footsteps produced a distinct crisp sound on the whitened ground and leaves. The group was initially alarmed, thinking someone had come to order them to pack up and leave. The sounds lasted approximately one minute before ceasing. When they immediately unfastened the tent flap to look outside, the area was completely deserted, leaving no normal explanation for the noises. The sources note that it is unknown whether Musk and his friends checked for physical footprints left in the frost [1].

## Organ Music from the Locked Church

About half an hour later, the group experienced a second auditory anomaly: music emanating directly from the locked church, which lasted **between five and ten minutes** [2, 3]. Musk's description is detailed:

- The sound was **"properly constructed music rather than jumbled sounds"** [3]
- Entirely instrumental rather than vocal
- Most notably, he described it as sounding exactly like an **"old gramophone record"** [2, 3]

Investigators later pointed out that this specific description was highly apt, as the church possessed a relatively small and old organ — originally hand-pumped before being fitted with an electrical device — which would likely sound very similar to a scratchy gramophone record when heard from outside the locked building [3].

This matches almost exactly the experiences of [[rev-ac-henning]] and Mrs Walrond in October 1947, E. W. Jephcott in September 1947, and [[maud-stearn]] in 1955.

## Mechanical Interference

The repeated tangling and destruction of tape equipment in Borley church — by the Harlow group and by [[denny-densham|Denny Densham]]'s BBC team — is cited by Babbs and Mathias as potentially connected to electromagnetic effects of [[ley-lines-at-borley|ley lines]] intersecting at Borley.
