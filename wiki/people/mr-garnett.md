# Mr Garnett

Landlord of lodgings at Westcliff-on-Sea where journalist [[montague-elelman|Montague Elelman]] was staying after moving from his sister's London home. Elelman had brought with him a piece of charred oak beam taken from the ruins of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] as a souvenir.

For three consecutive evenings — Friday, Saturday, and Sunday — Garnett and his wife were disturbed by prolonged ringing of their front doorbell, always at eight o'clock in the evening. No one was at the door when they answered. On Sunday, Garnett closed the glass inner hall door and watched: the bell rang again at 8 pm, and he could see from the hall that no one was at the front door — yet the bell continued to ring as he watched.

On Monday afternoon, Mrs Garnett was chatting with a neighbour at the door when the neighbour asked about "the darkly clad figure she had just glimpsed on the stair landing behind Mrs Garnett." Mrs Garnett was alone in the house at the time. Elelman subsequently maintained that for the nine years he kept the piece of charred Borley wood, "odd and inexplicable things continued to happen around it."

No further details about the Garnett family appear in *Ghosts of Borley*.
