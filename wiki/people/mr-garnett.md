# Mr Garnett

Landlord of lodgings at Westcliff-on-Sea where journalist [[montague-elelman|Montague Elelman]] was staying after moving from his sister's London home. Elelman had brought with him a piece of charred oak beam taken from the ruins of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] as a souvenir.

For three consecutive evenings — Friday, Saturday, and Sunday — Garnett and his wife were disturbed by prolonged ringing of their front doorbell, always at eight o'clock in the evening. No one was at the door when they answered. On Sunday, Garnett closed the glass inner hall door and watched: the bell rang again at 8 pm, and he could see from the hall that no one was at the front door — yet the bell continued to ring as he watched.

On Monday afternoon, Mrs Garnett was chatting with a neighbour at the door when the neighbour asked about "the darkly clad figure she had just glimpsed on the stair landing behind Mrs Garnett." Mrs Garnett was alone in the house at the time. Elelman subsequently maintained that for the nine years he kept the piece of charred Borley wood, "odd and inexplicable things continued to happen around it."

No further details about the Garnett family appear in *Ghosts of Borley*.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Mr. Garnett** (along with his wife) was a landlord who ran lodgings in a "cheerful little house" in Westcliff-on-Sea, Essex [1]. Described as part of a "charming middle-aged couple," he became inadvertently entangled in the Borley Rectory saga in 1946 when he took in a journalist named Montague Elelman as a lodger [1]. Elelman had unknowingly brought a "cursed" piece of charred oak beam, salvaged from the ruined Rectory, into the Garnetts' home concealed inside his luggage [1].

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific events, dates, and quotes regarding his experience:

**The Phantom Doorbells (1946)**
Shortly after Elelman arrived on a Friday, he departed again for a weekend in Surrey, leaving his belongings behind [1]. During his absence, Mr. and Mrs. Garnett experienced a series of bizarre auditory phenomena:
*   On both Friday and Saturday, the couple was disturbed by a prolonged ringing of their doorbell at exactly 8:00 p.m., but found no one there when they answered it [1].
*   Suspecting practical jokers, Mr. Garnett took proactive measures on Sunday evening to catch the culprits by closing only the glass inner door [1].
*   At exactly 8:00 p.m. that Sunday, the bell shrilled again. Mr. Garnett "hastened along the hall" and witnessed firsthand that "although the ringing continued, no one was at the front door" [1].

**The "Darkly Clad Figure"**
The visual phenomena extended to the Garnett household the following Monday afternoon [2]. While Mrs. Garnett was chatting with a neighbor at the front door, the neighbor suddenly inquired about a **"darkly clad figure"** she had just glimpsed on the stair landing behind Mrs. Garnett [2]. This was highly alarming, as Mrs. Garnett believed herself to be completely alone in the house at the time [2].

**Reactions and Quotes**
When Elelman returned on Monday evening, he decided it was "only fair" to tell the Garnetts about the piece of haunted Borley wood he had brought into the house [2]. The couple responded with measured concern:
*   Mr. Garnett thoughtfully noted, **"In your suitcase,"** to which Mrs. Garnett murmured, **"Been there all weekend"** [2]. 
*   Mr. Garnett then pointedly asked his new lodger: **"You did say that you were thinking of staying a month or two?"** [2].

Despite the unsettling weekend, the texts record that nothing else strange occurred during Elelman's stay, and the Garnetts eventually **"enjoyed relating the yarn to their friends"** [2].
