# Father Hayden, S.J.

Jesuit priest. Not directly connected to the haunting of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] itself; he features in the Borley sources as a central figure in the famous haunting of Ballechin House, Perthshire. Psychical researcher [[harry-price|Harry Price]] drew a direct comparison between the Ballechin and Borley cases, specifically noting that **in both hauntings it was a priest who originally reported the phenomena to a psychical researcher** — the [[rev-eric-smith|Rev. G. Eric Smith]] at Borley (to Harry Price), and Father Hayden at Ballechin (to [[lord-bute|Lord Bute]]) [1, 2].

## Nine Nights at Ballechin (July 1892)

Hayden occupied Ballechin House (then furnished but the Steuarts not in residence) from Thursday 14 July to Saturday 23 July 1892, sleeping in two different rooms. He was disturbed every night except the last:

- **Loud inexplicable noises** each night — including **"sounds like continuous explosions of petards"** occurring between his bed and the ceiling [3]
- **"Battering"** sounds at the room doors, described as sounding as if **"a calf or a big dog"** had fallen against the wood [1]
- **Screams and raps** throughout the building
- The noises followed him from room to room

In his own words:

> "I slept at Ballechin for nine nights, or rather one night, because I was disturbed by very queer and extraordinary noises every night except the last, which I spent in Mr Steuart's dressing-room." [1]

He sprinkled the rooms with holy water and recited the *Visita quaesumus* prayer for the divine protection of a house, and had a Roman Catholic Archbishop attempt exorcism. Both failed to stop the disturbances entirely. Only on the ninth night — when he moved to a different room in the house — did he sleep undisturbed.

He concluded his testimony with a frank admission regarding his fear:

> "One of the oddest things in my case, in connexion with the house, is that it appeared to me (a) that somebody was relieved by my departure; (b) that nothing could induce me to pass another night there, at all events alone, though in other respects I do not think I am a coward." [1]

## The 1893 Corroboration

After leaving, Hayden spoke of his experiences to almost no one for a year. Then, in August 1893, he had an accidental encounter with a former governess to the Steuart family at Ballechin [4]. Without Father Hayden ever mentioning that he had visited the estate, she volunteered that she had quit her job there because she became frightened after so many people complained of queer noises [4]. Upon further questioning, **Father Hayden discovered that the noises the governess described had occurred in the exact same bedrooms he had occupied** [4].

## Meeting with Lord Bute and the Genesis of the Investigation

In 1892, Hayden related his Ballechin experiences to [[lord-bute|Lord Bute]], who stored the information. When further reports accumulated in 1896 from the Spanish family's butler, Bute decided to investigate. Hayden's account was thus the seed of the entire Ballechin investigation — the parallel that Price sought to establish with Borley.
