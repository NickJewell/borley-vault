# Father Hayden, S.J.

Jesuit priest. In 1892, he spent nine nights at Ballechin House, Perthshire — disturbed by extraordinary phenomena every night except the last — and his account, related to [[lord-bute|Lord Bute]] at a chance meeting, triggered the systematic investigation of Ballechin.

## Nine Nights at Ballechin (July 1892)

Hayden occupied Ballechin (then furnished but the Steuarts not in residence) from July 14–23, 1892. He was disturbed every night except the last, sleeping in two different rooms. Phenomena included:

- **Loud inexplicable noises** each night — including "sounds like continuous explosions of petards" occurring between his bed and the ceiling
- **"Battering"** sounds at the room doors as if something were falling heavily against them — he described it as "like a calf or big dog falling against the door"
- **Screams and raps** throughout the building
- Noises followed him from room to room

He sprinkled the rooms with holy water and recited the *Visita quaesumus* prayer for the divine protection of a house. He also had a Roman Catholic Archbishop attempt exorcism. Both failed to stop the disturbances. Only on the ninth night — when he moved to a different room in the house — did he sleep undisturbed.

After leaving, Hayden spoke of his experiences to almost no one for a year. Then, in August 1893, he met by chance a former governess to the Steuart family, who *without any prompting* said she had left her employment because so many people complained of queer noises in the house. The noises had occurred in the identical bedrooms Hayden had occupied.

## Meeting with Lord Bute

In 1892 Hayden related his Ballechin experiences to [[lord-bute|Lord Bute]], who stored the information. When further reports accumulated in 1896 from the Spanish family's butler, Bute decided to investigate. Hayden's account was thus the seed of the entire Ballechin investigation.

[[harry-price|Price]] draws the explicit parallel: it was a Roman Catholic priest ([[rev-eric-smith|the Rev. G. Eric Smith]]) who reported Borley to a psychical researcher ([[harry-price|Price]]); it was a Roman Catholic priest (Father Hayden) who reported Ballechin to a psychical researcher ([[lord-bute|Lord Bute]]). The two cases begin with the same institutional pathway.
