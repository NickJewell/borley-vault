# Kate Fernie

A London woman who had a relationship with [[francois-darles]] (Frank Pearless) and was the mother of Douglas Pearless — the child Frank brought to [[borley-rectory]] under the name "François d'Arles Jr."

## Background

[[andrew-clarke]] identifies her in essay 14 ("Lawless, the Lodger"):

> *Douglas was the result of Frank's short-lived liaison with Kate Fernie in London. He was, of course, not a widower as Lionel had thought.*
> — *Sidelights on Borley Rectory*, essay 14

Frank Pearless presented himself to the Foysters as a widower when he arrived at Borley with Douglas in spring 1931. This was false. He had left Kate Fernie and Douglas, and at the time of the Borley period he had not divorced his first wife Ada Ewens. Douglas is confirmed as Kate's son by Frank, not as an orphan or the child of a deceased wife as Frank implied.

## Douglas at Borley

Douglas (known as "François d'Arles Jr") was brought to Borley ostensibly so Adelaide Foyster would have child company. [[marianne-foyster]] and Lionel became very fond of him: "He was a beautiful child, a few months younger than Adelaide, and we offered him a month's vacation. But long before that time was up, Lion and I adored him." Douglas remained at the Rectory full-time while Frank visited weekends.

The paternity question extended to the baby John Emery, whom [[marianne-foyster]] adopted in 1932. Clarke notes the alternative theory that John was the child of Frank and [[marjorie-emery]], rather than of Frank and Marianne as Frank believed.

## Significance

Kate Fernie establishes that Frank's arrival at Borley was built on falsehood from the outset: he was not a widower, Douglas was not an orphan, and his need to find a foster home for Douglas was driven by the breakdown of his relationship with Kate Fernie rather than by bereavement.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Kate Emily Fernie** is a peripheral but genealogically significant figure in the Borley Rectory story, known primarily for her relationship with Frank Charles Pearless—the man who notoriously embedded himself in the Foyster household under the alias "François d'Arles" [1]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following biographical details, specific dates, and contradictions regarding her hidden connection to the haunted Rectory:

**Liaison and Offspring**
*   **The Relationship:** Kate Fernie had a **"short-lived liaison"** in London with Frank Pearless [2]. At the time, Pearless was actually still legally married to his first wife, Ada Ewens (whom he did not divorce until November 1933) [2, 3]. 
*   **Birth of Douglas Stuart:** Fernie and Pearless had a son together, **Douglas Stuart Pearless**, who was born on **July 31, 1928** [1]. 
*   **Residence:** The sources record that Kate Fernie, Frank Pearless, and their young son lived together for a time in **Wood Green, London** [1]. 

**The "Widower" Contradiction**
Kate Fernie's existence exposes a major fabricated contradiction in the backstory Pearless presented when he entered the Borley saga. 
*   When Pearless arrived at Borley Rectory in **March 1931**, he completely concealed his relationships with both his legal wife and Kate Fernie, presenting himself to the Rev. Lionel Foyster as a **French-Canadian widower** named François d'Arles [2, 4]. 
*   The sources note the stark reality of this deception: Pearless was not a grieving widower at all, but a man who had simply **"left his two families"** [2]. 

**Impact on the Borley Saga**
Because of Pearless's deception and his subsequent move to Essex, Kate Fernie's young son Douglas was taken to Borley Rectory, stripped of his real name, and introduced to the Foysters as **"François d'Arles Jr."** [1]. There, he lived in the Rectory as a companion and playmate for the Foysters' adopted daughter, Adelaide [1].
