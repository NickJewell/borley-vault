# Kate Fernie

A London woman who had a relationship with [[francois-darles]] (Frank Pearless) and was the mother of Douglas Pearless — the child Frank brought to [[borley-rectory]] under the name "François d'Arles Jr."

## Background

[[andrew-clarke]] identifies her in essay 14 ("Lawless, the Lodger"):

> *Douglas was the result of Frank's short-lived liaison with Kate Fernie in London. He was, of course, not a widower as Lionel had thought.*
> — *Sidelights on Borley Rectory*, essay 14

Frank Pearless presented himself to the Foysters as a widower when he arrived at Borley with Douglas in spring 1931. This was false. He had left Kate Fernie and Douglas, and at the time of the Borley period he had not divorced his first wife Ada Ewens. Douglas is confirmed as Kate's son by Frank, not as an orphan or the child of a deceased wife as Frank implied.

## Douglas at Borley

Douglas (known as "François d'Arles Jr") was brought to Borley ostensibly so Adelaide Foyster would have child company. [[marianne-foyster]] and Lionel became very fond of him: "He was a beautiful child, a few months younger than Adelaide, and we offered him a month's vacation. But long before that time was up, Lion and I adored him." Douglas remained at the Rectory full-time while Frank visited weekends.

The paternity question extended to the baby John Emery, whom [[marianne-foyster]] adopted in 1932. Clarke notes the alternative theory that John was the child of Frank and [[marjorie-emery]], rather than of Frank and Marianne as Frank believed.

## Significance

Kate Fernie establishes that Frank's arrival at Borley was built on falsehood from the outset: he was not a widower, Douglas was not an orphan, and his need to find a foster home for Douglas was driven by the breakdown of his relationship with Kate Fernie rather than by bereavement.
