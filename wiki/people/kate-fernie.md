# Kate Fernie

**Kate Emily Fernie** is a peripheral but genealogically significant figure in the Borley Rectory story, known primarily for her relationship with Frank Charles Pearless — the man who notoriously embedded himself in the Foyster household under the alias "[[francois-darles|François d'Arles]]" [1].

## Liaison and Offspring

Kate Fernie had a **"short-lived liaison"** in London with Frank Pearless [2]. At the time, Pearless was actually still legally married to his first wife, [[ada-ewens|Ada Ewens]], whom he did not divorce until November 1933 [2, 3]. Their son, **Douglas Stuart Pearless**, was born on **31 July 1928** [1]. Fernie, Pearless, and their young son subsequently lived together for a time in **Wood Green, London** [1].

[[andrew-clarke|Andrew Clarke]] identifies her in essay 14 ("Lawless, the Lodger"):

> *Douglas was the result of Frank's short-lived liaison with Kate Fernie in London. He was, of course, not a widower as Lionel had thought.*
> — *Sidelights on Borley Rectory*, essay 14

## The "Widower" Deception

Kate Fernie's existence exposes a major fabricated contradiction in the backstory Pearless presented when he entered the Borley saga. When Pearless arrived at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] in **March 1931**, he completely concealed his relationships with both his legal wife and Kate Fernie, presenting himself to [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]] as a **French-Canadian widower** named François d'Arles [2, 4]. The sources note the stark reality of this deception: Pearless was not a grieving widower at all, but a man who had simply **"left his two families"** [2].

## Douglas at Borley

Because of Pearless's deception, Kate Fernie's young son Douglas was taken to Borley Rectory, stripped of his real name, and introduced to the Foysters as **"François d'Arles Jr."** [1]. He was brought ostensibly so [[adelaide-foyster|Adelaide Foyster]] would have child company; [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] and Lionel became very fond of him — "He was a beautiful child, a few months younger than Adelaide, and we offered him a month's vacation. But long before that time was up, Lion and I adored him." Douglas remained at the Rectory full-time while Frank visited at weekends.

Clarke also notes the alternative theory that the baby [[john-emery|John Emery]], whom Marianne adopted in 1932, was the child of Frank and [[marjorie-emery|Marjorie Emery]], rather than of Frank and Marianne as Frank apparently believed.

## Significance

Kate Fernie establishes that Frank's arrival at Borley was built on falsehood from the outset: he was not a widower, Douglas was not an orphan, and his need to find a foster home for Douglas was driven by the breakdown of his relationship with Kate Fernie rather than by bereavement.
