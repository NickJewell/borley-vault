# Dr Nandor Fodor

(Born Berengszasz, Hungary, 13 May 1895; died 17 May 1964) Practising New York psychoanalyst and psychical researcher. One-time leading light of the Metaphysical Society and Research Officer of the International Institute for Psychical Research.

Fodor studied law and then worked as a journalist. Much interested in the Borley case, he mentioned it in several books including *On the Trail of the Poltergeist* (1958), *Mind Over Space* (1962) and the monumental *Encyclopaedia of Psychic Science* (1933).

## Relationship with Harry Price

Fodor's relationship with [[harry-price]] was complex. He wrote candidly about it in a review in [[eileen-garrett]]'s *Tomorrow* (winter 1956):

> "I am not trying to defend Harry Price on the basis of personal feelings. I have never been his friend, nor his enemy. I did not like him because he was a very difficult man to like, and easy to resent ... He was far too selfish, intent on his own glory and intensely jealous. That was his character. I accepted him for what he was, admiring his courage, his ability and his achievements. In fact, I openly acknowledged him, in a court case in London, in 1939, as the outstanding psychical researcher in England of that day. I still hold to this opinion."

## Attack on the Borley Report

Fodor deplored the attacks on Price after Price's death. His scathing review of the [[the-borley-report|Borley Report]] (1956) became famous:

> "Never before in the history of psychical research has there been such a scandalous piece of writing... the book will do more discredit to psychical research than all the fraud laid at Harry Price's door will do to him... _Dingwall_ has been out to wreck psychical research in Great Britain for thirty years... Mrs Goldney has a penetrating mind but God help her subjects. There is no limit to her imagination on the negative side... She is eminently capable of convincing almost any witness that he did not see what he saw."

This review, published in Garrett's *Tomorrow*, became a key document in the pro-Price camp's response to the SPR report.

## Assessment

[[colin-wilson]] described Fodor as a man "who deserves to be remembered as one of the prominent ghost-hunters of the twentieth century" and noted his investigation of a poltergeist case at Thornton Heath, London (published in *The Haunted Mind*, Helix Press, 1959) as "deserving the status of a classic." Wilson observed that if Fodor had Price's talent for publicity, his Thornton Heath case might have been as famous as Borley.

Fodor was also notable for bringing psychoanalytic methods to poltergeist investigation, proposing that poltergeist agents were releasing unconscious psychological conflicts — an approach that influenced later researchers including [[iris-owen]]'s Philip Experiment.
