# K.J.T. Keer MRCS

Doctor based at **Wickham Market**, Suffolk; the regular physician to the [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]]–Fisher household during their time at [[dairy-cottage-rendlesham|Dairy Cottage, Rendlesham]] (1941–1945). He signed [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]]'s death certificate on 18 April 1945.

## The Death Certificate

The certificate recorded the place of death as Dairy Cottage, Rendlesham, with [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] as the informant present at death. The stated causes of death were:

> **"Exhaustion, bedsores, and rheumatoid arthritis."**

Robert Wood consulted several doctors about these causes and reports that "all of them considered the causes to be insufficient." No one dies from bedsores as a standalone cause of death (bedsores can cause septicaemia, but then septicaemia is the stated cause); "exhaustion" is similarly vague and unqualified as a cause of death for an invalid.

## Wood's Suspicions

Wood is careful but pointed:

> "Keer's signature is not as impressive as it might be when Marianne's hold over a local solicitor is known... The insufficient causes may have been his equivalent of a child's crossing its fingers when making a promise it does not intend to keep."

Wood raises the possibility that Marianne had compromised Keer in some way — possibly through the same pattern of sexual entanglement and implicit blackmail she used on [[cj-parry|C.J. Parry]] the solicitor.

> **Contested:** No evidence beyond circumstantial reasoning supports the suggestion of Keer's complicity. His death certificate may simply reflect the limited diagnostic specificity of wartime rural practice when signing the certificate of a long-established, clearly terminal patient.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**K.J.T. Keer, MRCS** (Member of the Royal College of Surgeons), was a British civilian doctor based in Wickham Market [1]. In the Borley sources, he is recorded as the regular physician for the Rev. Lionel and Marianne Foyster during the later years of their lives when they resided at Dairy Cottage in Rendlesham [1, 2]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific events, dates, and highly controversial theories regarding his involvement in the Borley saga:

**The 1945 Death Certificate**
Dr. Keer's primary historical significance in the Borley case stems from his signing of the Rev. Lionel Foyster's death certificate. Following the rector's death at Dairy Cottage on **April 18, 1945**, Keer officially recorded the causes of death as **"exhaustion, bedsores, and rheumatoid arthritis"** [2]. 

**Medical Anomalies and Contradictions**
Subsequent researchers, notably Robert Wood (author of *_The Widow of Borley_*), subjected this death certificate to intense scrutiny and discovered significant medical contradictions. Wood consulted with several doctors, all of whom agreed that the causes of death listed by Dr. Keer were medically "insufficient" [2]. Wood highlighted that **"No one dies from bed sores"** (though they can cause fatal septicaemia, which Keer did not list), and noted that stating "exhaustion" without further qualification is highly irregular for an official cause of death [2]. 

**The Blackmail and Murder Cover-Up Theory**
Because of these dubious medical entries, Dr. Keer became inadvertently tied to one of the darkest theories in the Borley literature: the allegation that Marianne Foyster murdered her husband. Noting that Marianne had a history of manipulating and allegedly blackmailing men (such as a local solicitor who helped her quickly prove Foyster's will), Wood theorized that she had compromised her physician as well [2]. 

Wood speculated that Marianne entangled Dr. Keer in an affair and blackmailed him, obliging the doctor **"to keep quiet about any doubts he might have had about the circumstances of Foyster’s death and sign a certificate"** [2]. Addressing the strangely insufficient causes of death written by Keer, Wood provided a memorable quote, suggesting that the medically inadequate certificate may have been the doctor's silent protest or **"his equivalent of a child’s crossing its fingers when making a promise it does not intend to keep"** [2].

**The Arrival of Robert O'Neil**
Dr. Keer also indirectly played a role in Marianne Foyster's subsequent bigamous marriage. On an unspecified earlier date, Lionel Foyster suffered a severe "collapse," and Marianne attempted to summon Dr. Keer [1]. Because Keer was unavailable at that exact moment, Marianne was forced to ask for medical assistance from a nearby American military camp [1]. It was as a direct result of Dr. Keer's absence that the American G.I. Robert Vincent O'Neil visited Dairy Cottage, beginning a relationship with Marianne that led to their marriage shortly after Foyster's death [1].
