# K.J.T. Keer MRCS

Doctor based at **Wickham Market**, Suffolk; the regular physician to the [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]]–Fisher household during their time at [[dairy-cottage-rendlesham|Dairy Cottage, Rendlesham]] (1941–1945). He signed [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]]'s death certificate on 18 April 1945.

## The Death Certificate

Following the rector's death at Dairy Cottage on **April 18, 1945**, Keer officially recorded the causes of death as:

> **"Exhaustion, bedsores, and rheumatoid arthritis."**

with [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] as the informant present at death.

## Medical Anomalies

Subsequent researchers, notably Robert Wood (author of *The Widow of Borley*), subjected this death certificate to intense scrutiny. Wood consulted with several doctors, all of whom agreed that the causes listed by Dr. Keer were medically "insufficient." Wood highlighted that **"No one dies from bed sores"** (though they can cause fatal septicaemia, which Keer did not list), and noted that stating "exhaustion" without further qualification is highly irregular for an official cause of death.

## The Blackmail and Murder Cover-Up Theory

Because of these dubious entries, Keer became inadvertently tied to one of the darkest theories in the Borley literature: the allegation that Marianne Foyster murdered her husband. Noting that Marianne had a history of allegedly manipulating and blackmailing men (such as [[cj-parry|C.J. Parry]] the solicitor), Wood theorised that she had compromised her physician as well.

Wood speculated that Marianne entangled Dr. Keer in an affair and blackmailed him, obliging the doctor "to keep quiet about any doubts he might have had about the circumstances of Foyster's death and sign a certificate." Addressing the strangely insufficient causes of death, Wood offered the memorable observation that the medically inadequate certificate may have been the doctor's silent protest — **"his equivalent of a child's crossing its fingers when making a promise it does not intend to keep."**

> **Contested:** No evidence beyond circumstantial reasoning supports the suggestion of Keer's complicity. His death certificate may simply reflect the limited diagnostic specificity of wartime rural practice when signing the certificate of a long-established, clearly terminal patient.

## The Arrival of Robert O'Neil

Dr. Keer also inadvertently played a role in Marianne's subsequent bigamous marriage. On an earlier date, Lionel Foyster suffered a severe "collapse," and Marianne attempted to summon Dr. Keer. Because Keer was unavailable at that exact moment, Marianne was forced to ask for medical assistance from a nearby American military camp. It was as a direct result of Dr. Keer's absence that the American G.I. [[robert-oneil|Robert Vincent O'Neil]] visited Dairy Cottage, beginning a relationship with Marianne that led to their marriage shortly after Foyster's death.
