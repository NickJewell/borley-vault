# Mrs R. A. Butler

Wife of the future Chancellor of the Exchequer and Master of Trinity, R. A. Butler (later Lord Butler of Saffron Walden). On 21 June 1939, Mrs Butler opened the "Psychic Fête" held in the grounds of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] — an event organised by the [[rev-ac-henning|Reverend A. C. Henning]] and his church council to raise funds for the restoration of [[borley-church|Borley Church]]. [[harry-price|Harry Price]] attended, as did a number of members of the old [[ghost-club|Ghost Club]]. Her name on the fête programme was the only external connection to mainstream political life recorded in the Borley story.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Mrs. R. A. Butler** makes a brief but notable appearance in the Borley Rectory sources in connection with a single, highly publicized public event held on the grounds of the ruined house. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific biographical details, dates, and events regarding her involvement:

**Biographical Details**
*   At the time of her involvement with Borley in 1939, she was the **wife of the then Under-Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs** [1, 2]. 
*   The texts note that her husband would later become the **Chancellor of the Exchequer and the Master of Trinity College** [3]. 
*   Later retrospective accounts refer to her as the "late" Mrs. R. A. Butler, indicating she predeceased some of the subsequent publications chronicling the case [3]. 

**The Psychic Fête (June 21, 1939)**
Mrs. Butler's sole recorded contribution to the Borley saga occurred on **Wednesday, June 21, 1939**, which was the longest day of the year and just forty days before the outbreak of the Second World War [1, 2]. 
*   She was invited to officially open a **"Psychic Fête"** held among the ruins of the Rectory (which had been gutted by fire exactly four months earlier) [2-4]. 
*   The fête was organized by the local rector, the Rev. Alfred Clifford Henning, and his Church Council, with the active cooperation of investigator Harry Price [1, 3]. Its purpose was to **raise funds for the restoration of Borley Church** [1-3].
*   **The Speech and Quote:** As the guest of honor, **Mrs. Butler delivered the opening address to the crowd**, which included locals, sightseers, and twenty-five members of Harry Price's Ghost Club [1]. Price personally recorded that she delivered a **"charming speech of welcome,"** after which the attendees all sat down to have tea [2]. 

The available sources contain no further contradictions, quotes, or details regarding Mrs. Butler beyond her ceremonial role at this single 1939 fundraising event.
