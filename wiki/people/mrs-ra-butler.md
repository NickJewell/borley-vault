# Mrs R. A. Butler

Wife of the then Under-Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs — subsequently the Chancellor of the Exchequer and Master of Trinity College — R. A. Butler (later Lord Butler of Saffron Walden). Later retrospective accounts refer to her as the "late" Mrs R. A. Butler. On **Wednesday, June 21, 1939** — the longest day of the year and just forty days before the outbreak of the Second World War — Mrs Butler opened the [[psychic-fete-1939|"Psychic Fête"]] held among the ruins of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] (gutted by fire exactly four months earlier).

## The Psychic Fête

The fête was organised by the local rector, the Rev. [[rev-ac-henning|Alfred Clifford Henning]], and his church council, with the active cooperation of investigator [[harry-price|Harry Price]]. Its purpose was to **raise funds for the restoration of [[borley-church|Borley Church]]**. The event was attended by locals, sightseers, and twenty-five members of the old [[ghost-club|Ghost Club]].

As the guest of honour, **Mrs Butler delivered the opening address to the crowd**. Price personally recorded that she delivered a **"charming speech of welcome"** after which the attendees sat down to have tea.

Her name on the fête programme was the only external connection to mainstream political life recorded in the Borley story. The available sources contain no further details regarding Mrs Butler beyond her ceremonial role at this single event.
