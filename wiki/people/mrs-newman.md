# Mrs Newman

(née unknown; married surname Newman). Cook employed by the [[henry-bull|Bull]] family at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] from early 1924 until the death of the Rev. Harry Bull in 1927 — a period of three and a half years. Interviewed by [[harry-price|Price]] on May 30, 1945, at Rectory Cottages, Liston. Described by Price as "very intelligent and willing to relate her psychic adventures."

## Phenomena

Newman's sleeping quarters was a large room over the kitchen (Room 3 on Plan III), with a window overlooking the courtyard. The room had two doors — one to the bathroom passage, one to the "pear room" (where pears and fruit were stored). She locked both doors every night before retiring.

**Clothes disturbance**: A curtain divided her room in half. Her clothes hung on wall-pegs on the window side. On several occasions, just as light was beginning to appear through the window, she was awakened and could see through the semi-transparent curtain the silhouettes of her garments being disturbed and moved back and forth — as if "something was examining her belongings." She never discovered what moved them; there was no draught.

**Door-unlocking**: The door to the pear room was found unlocked and standing open "morning after morning," despite being locked and the key left inside the room on the window side. Price compares this to [[mark-kerr-pearse|Kerr-Pearse]]'s experience of being locked in the Base Room by something that remained in the room (October 26, 1937). Newman eventually removed the key after locking the pear room door, and from that point the door was not interfered with. The bathroom passage door and its key were never disturbed.

Whatever was opening her door "must have been inside the room while she was undressing" — as Price notes, echoing the long history of Borley's door and lock phenomena during all periods of occupation.
