# Geoffrey Shaw

Older brother of [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]; eldest child and only brother of the Shaw family. Born **1897** [1], two years before Marianne (born 1899 as Mary Anne Emily Rebecca Shaw). Son of [[william-steele-shaw|William Steele Shaw]], a school teacher and private tutor, and [[annie-shaw|Annie Elizabeth Shaw]] (née Woodyatt) [1, 2].

## The 1914 Belfast Marriage

Geoffrey Shaw's only specifically recorded involvement in the events surrounding the Borley narrative occurred on **November 12, 1914** [2, 3]. On this date, his fifteen-year-old sister Marianne married a twenty-one-year-old clerk named Harold Gifford Greenwood at St Anne's Cathedral in Belfast [2, 3]. Geoffrey, alongside his mother, **acted as an official witness to the ceremony** [2, 3]. At this wedding, Marianne falsely gave her age as seventeen — a deception that Geoffrey and his mother must have been aware of as witnesses [2, 3].

## Visit to Canada

Geoffrey visited [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] in Canada at [[salmonhurst-new-brunswick|Salmonhurst Rectory]] before [[ian-greenwood|Ian Greenwood Shaw]] did, suggesting the family maintained contact across the Atlantic. Marianne confirmed this in her 1958 interview with Swanson:

> "My older brother Geoffrey had been out there to visit and then Ian came because he suffered from asthma and it was thought that the air out there would be good for him."

## Significance

Geoffrey plays a minor but confirming role in the Borley narrative: his witness signature at Marianne's underage 1914 marriage establishes family awareness of her tendency toward deception from an early age. His visit to Salmonhurst establishes that the Shaw family actively engaged with Marianne's Canadian household, and that [[ian-greenwood|Ian]] was sent partly for health reasons — information Wood uses to moderate Ian's more hostile testimony about Marianne.
