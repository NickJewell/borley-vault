# Astrid Fisher

Baby adopted by [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] in 1935 as **Mary Lex**, renamed **Astrid Fisher**, baptised as a Roman Catholic at St Mary's Church, Ipswich; later abandoned at a convent in Braintree; mother of **[[kevin-taylor|Kevin Taylor]]**, a police officer from Essex who spent fourteen years tracing her.

## Origins

Marianne adopted **Mary Lex** as a second adopted child to deepen her hold on [[henry-fisher|Johnny Fisher]]. She passed the baby off as Fisher's daughter, having him believe she was pregnant before presenting the child. Fisher had doubts from the start. Astrid was baptised at St Mary's Catholic Church in Ipswich and she and [[john-knights|John]] were brought up as brother and sister — styled and dressed as twins by Marianne.

## Life at the Convent

Astrid was frequently unwell as a child and spent much of her time boarding at a **convent in Braintree** run by the Sisters of the Immaculate Conception. When Marianne abandoned the family in 1946 to emigrate to America, Astrid was told she would remain at the convent because she was too ill to travel. Marianne's departure ended their contact.

After Marianne abandoned the family, John Knights was advised at his seminary that since he and Astrid were not blood relations, it would be inappropriate for him to maintain a close relationship with her. He wrote her a letter ending contact — a decision that haunted him for decades.

## Reunion

Astrid eventually emigrated to Western Australia. In 1994, her son **[[kevin-taylor|Kevin Taylor]]** — an Essex police officer who had been placed for adoption by Astrid as an illegitimate child in 1958 — traced her after a fourteen-year search. The BBC2 documentary *Haunted* (1994) filmed the reunion, including the emotional meeting between Astrid and [[john-knights|John Knights]] in which they embraced and Astrid, reduced to a girl again, wept: *"I never forgot you."*

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Astrid Fisher** was originally an illegitimate child named **Mary Lex**, whom Marianne Foyster covertly adopted to pass off as her own daughter in order to tighten her hold on her bigamous husband, Henry Francis Fisher [1]. Marianne had falsely claimed to be pregnant, and the child was subsequently baptised as **Astrid Marianne Zaida** at St Mary's Roman Catholic Church in Ipswich on March 22, 1937, with Marianne providing a false date of birth to the priest [1-3]. 

Astrid was raised alongside Marianne's other adopted son, John, whom she dressed and styled so that the two children looked like twins [1]. Despite this elaborate deception, **Henry Fisher had strong doubts that the baby girl was his biological child**, and he shared these suspicions with his mother and sister [1]. 

**Childhood and the Braintree Convent**
Described as a **sickly child**, Astrid spent the vast majority of her early life boarding at a convent in Braintree, Essex [4, 5]. Her adopted brother, John Fisher, recalled that on the rare occasions she was home with them at Dairy Cottage in Rendlesham, she almost always seemed to be ill in bed [5]. Nevertheless, the two children maintained a close bond; they corresponded by letter, and Astrid would send John holy pictures that he kept inside his missal [5, 6].

**Abandonment by Marianne**
When Marianne fled to America in 1946 as a G.I. bride, **Astrid was abandoned at the convent** [6]. For a brief period, Marianne continued to send letters and gifts to Astrid from the United States, with the correspondence being closely supervised by the nuns [7]. The final break in their relationship occurred when Astrid was about nine years old, after Marianne sent her a parcel containing a bride and groom doll set [7]. When Astrid wrote a thank-you letter addressed to "Dear Mum," Marianne coldly replied, insisting that Astrid must never call her "Mum" again, but only "Mama" or "Morny" [7]. Following this exchange, Astrid never heard from her adopted mother again [7]. 

**Later Life and the BBC Documentary**
In 1958, Astrid herself had a child out of wedlock [8]. She originally named the boy Michael John, but ultimately gave him up to the Catholic Children’s Society, after which he was adopted and renamed Kevin Taylor [8]. Following this, **Astrid emigrated to Australia**, where all traces of her completely disappeared [8-10]. 

Her tragic history returned to the public eye decades later when Kevin Taylor, who became an Essex police officer, spent fourteen years trying to track down his birth mother [8]. This search was the subject of a 1994 BBC2 documentary series called *Picture This* (in an episode titled *Haunted*), which followed Kevin and his wife as they visited the Braintree convent [6, 8, 9]. Although Astrid was never found, an advertisement placed in the *East Anglian Daily Times* during the search led Kevin to John Fisher (now John Knights), ultimately reuniting the two adopted siblings' families [6].
