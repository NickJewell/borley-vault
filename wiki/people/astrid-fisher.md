# Astrid Fisher

Baby adopted by [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] in 1935 as **Mary Lex**, renamed **Astrid Fisher**, baptised as a Roman Catholic at St Mary's Church, Ipswich; later abandoned at a convent in Braintree; mother of **[[kevin-taylor|Kevin Taylor]]**, a police officer from Essex who spent fourteen years tracing her.

## Origins

Marianne covertly adopted Mary Lex — an illegitimate child — to deepen her hold on [[henry-fisher|Johnny Fisher]], whom she had bigamously married. She passed the baby off as Fisher's daughter, having him believe she was pregnant before presenting the child [1]. Marianne provided a false date of birth to the priest [1–3]. The child was baptised as **Astrid Marianne Zaida** at St Mary's Roman Catholic Church in Ipswich on **March 22, 1937** [1–3]. Henry Fisher had strong doubts from the start that the baby girl was his biological child, and shared these suspicions with his mother and sister [1].

Astrid was raised alongside Marianne's other adopted son, [[john-knights|John]], whom Marianne dressed and styled so that the two children looked like twins [1].

## Life at the Convent

Described as a sickly child, Astrid spent the vast majority of her early life boarding at a **convent in Braintree** run by the Sisters of the Immaculate Conception [4, 5]. Her adopted brother, John Fisher, recalled that on the rare occasions she was home with them at [[dairy-cottage-rendlesham|Dairy Cottage in Rendlesham]], she almost always seemed to be ill in bed [5]. Nevertheless, the two children maintained a close bond; they corresponded by letter, and Astrid would send John holy pictures that he kept inside his missal [5, 6].

## Abandonment by Marianne

When Marianne fled to America in 1946 as a G.I. bride, Astrid was abandoned at the convent [6]. For a brief period, Marianne continued to send letters and gifts to Astrid from the United States, closely supervised by the nuns [7]. The final break occurred when Astrid wrote a thank-you letter addressed to "Dear Mum." Marianne coldly replied, insisting that Astrid must never call her "Mum" again, but only "Mama" or "Morny" [7]. Following this exchange, Astrid never heard from her adopted mother again [7].

After [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] abandoned the family, [[john-knights|John Knights]] was advised at his seminary that since he and Astrid were not blood relations, it would be inappropriate for him to maintain a close relationship with her. He wrote her a letter ending contact — a decision that haunted him for decades.

## Later Life

In 1958, Astrid herself had a child out of wedlock [8]. She originally named the boy Michael John, but ultimately gave him up to the Catholic Children's Society, after which he was adopted and renamed [[kevin-taylor|Kevin Taylor]] [8]. Following this, Astrid emigrated to Australia, where all traces of her completely disappeared [8–10].

## Reunion

Her tragic history returned to the public eye decades later when Kevin Taylor, who became an Essex police officer, spent fourteen years trying to track down his birth mother [8]. This search was the subject of a 1994 BBC2 documentary series called *Picture This* (in an episode titled *Haunted*), which followed Kevin and his wife as they visited the Braintree convent [6, 8, 9]. Although Astrid was never found, an advertisement placed in the *East Anglian Daily Times* led Kevin to John Fisher (now John Knights), ultimately reuniting the two adopted siblings' families [6]. The BBC2 documentary *Haunted* (1994) filmed the emotional meeting between Astrid and [[john-knights|John Knights]] in which they embraced and Astrid, reduced to a girl again, wept: *"I never forgot you."*
