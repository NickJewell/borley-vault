# Astrid Fisher

Baby adopted by [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] in 1935 as **Mary Lex**, renamed **Astrid Fisher**, baptised as a Roman Catholic at St Mary's Church, Ipswich; later abandoned at a convent in Braintree; mother of **[[kevin-taylor|Kevin Taylor]]**, a police officer from Essex who spent fourteen years tracing her.

## Origins

Marianne adopted **Mary Lex** as a second adopted child to deepen her hold on [[henry-fisher|Johnny Fisher]]. She passed the baby off as Fisher's daughter, having him believe she was pregnant before presenting the child. Fisher had doubts from the start. Astrid was baptised at St Mary's Catholic Church in Ipswich and she and [[john-knights|John]] were brought up as brother and sister — styled and dressed as twins by Marianne.

## Life at the Convent

Astrid was frequently unwell as a child and spent much of her time boarding at a **convent in Braintree** run by the Sisters of the Immaculate Conception. When Marianne abandoned the family in 1946 to emigrate to America, Astrid was told she would remain at the convent because she was too ill to travel. Marianne's departure ended their contact.

After Marianne abandoned the family, John Knights was advised at his seminary that since he and Astrid were not blood relations, it would be inappropriate for him to maintain a close relationship with her. He wrote her a letter ending contact — a decision that haunted him for decades.

## Reunion

Astrid eventually emigrated to Western Australia. In 1994, her son **[[kevin-taylor|Kevin Taylor]]** — an Essex police officer who had been placed for adoption by Astrid as an illegitimate child in 1958 — traced her after a fourteen-year search. The BBC2 documentary *Haunted* (1994) filmed the reunion, including the emotional meeting between Astrid and [[john-knights|John Knights]] in which they embraced and Astrid, reduced to a girl again, wept: *"I never forgot you."*
