# Sub-Lt Jonathan Crossley, RN

One of [[miss-susanna-dudley|Miss Susanna Dudley]]'s companions on a visit to [[borley-church|Borley Church]] on 20 February 1949. On the path to the church, the other three members of the party encountered an intense and overwhelming smell — but Crossley could not detect it at all.

> "Jonathan Crossley could not smell it at all. It was terribly strong — so strong that the rest of us had to walk over and breathe clean air — but Jonathan could not smell it. Yet his sense of smell was (before and after) as good as ours." — Miss Dudley's report

His inability to smell what the others found overpowering is the most anomalous element of the encounter and resists any conventional explanation. Miss Dudley noted he "said it was our imagination" — though she and [[miss-audrey-sawrey-cookson|Miss Audrey Sawrey-Cookson]] and [[sub-lt-mciever|Sub-Lt Alastair McIver]] all experienced the same thing independently, and it persisted in the same location after they returned from the church ten minutes later.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Sub-Lieutenant Jonathan Crossley, RN**, appears briefly in the Borley Rectory sources as a post-demolition visitor who experienced an unexplained olfactory phenomenon near the church. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following details regarding his involvement:

*   **The Visit and Companions:** On **Sunday, 20 February 1949**, Sub-Lt Crossley visited Borley accompanied by three friends: fellow Royal Navy officer **Sub-Lt Alastair McIver, RN**, **Miss Audrey Sawrey-Cookson**, and **Miss Susanna Dudley** (of Stradishall Manor, near Newmarket) [1, 2]. 
*   **Purpose:** The group made the excursion specifically hoping to catch a glimpse of the famous **phantom "Nun"**, but they were ultimately disappointed by the "elusive lady" and failed to see any apparitions [2]. 
*   **The Phenomenon:** Finding the weather chilly, the party decided to seek shelter and crossed the road to examine Borley Church [2]. It was during this walk that Crossley and his friends encountered a **"strange localised smell half way up the church path"** [1].
*   **Documentation:** The details of this visit, and the full report of the unusual smell, were documented and provided by his companion, Miss Susanna Dudley [2]. 

The sources contain no further biographical details or subsequent involvement regarding Jonathan Crossley beyond this specific 1949 incident.
