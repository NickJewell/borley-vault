# Sub-Lt Jonathan Crossley, RN

One of [[miss-susanna-dudley|Miss Susanna Dudley]]'s companions on a visit to [[borley-church|Borley Church]] on **Sunday, 20 February 1949**. He was accompanied by fellow Royal Navy officer [[sub-lt-mciever|Sub-Lt Alastair McIver, RN]] and [[miss-audrey-sawrey-cookson|Miss Audrey Sawrey-Cookson]] [1, 2]. The group made the excursion specifically hoping to catch a glimpse of the famous phantom "Nun," but were ultimately disappointed and failed to see any apparitions [2].

## The Unexplained Smell

On the path to the church, the other three members of the party encountered an intense and overwhelming smell "half way up the church path" [1] — described by Miss Dudley as being **"like an embalmed body"** [2]. Crossley could not detect it at all.

> "Jonathan Crossley could not smell it at all. It was terribly strong — so strong that the rest of us had to walk over and breathe clean air — but Jonathan could not smell it. Yet his sense of smell was (before and after) as good as ours." — Miss Dudley's report

His inability to smell what the others found overpowering is the most anomalous element of the encounter and resists any conventional explanation. Miss Dudley noted he **"said it was our imagination"** — though she, [[miss-audrey-sawrey-cookson|Miss Audrey Sawrey-Cookson]], and [[sub-lt-mciever|Sub-Lt McIver]] all experienced the same thing independently, and it persisted in the same location after they returned from the church ten minutes later.

The details of this visit and the full report of the unusual smell were documented and provided by his companion, Miss Susanna Dudley [2].
