# Alfred Richard Graham Bull

**Alfred Richard Graham Bull (1866–1956)** — son of [[henry-bull|the Rev Henry Dawson Ellis Bull]] and brother of [[harry-bull|the Rev Harry Foyster Bull]]; born on February 23, 1866 [1]. The patron of the Borley living after Harry's death in 1927, and one of the executors named in [[harry-bull-will|Harry Bull's will]]. He was a retired schoolmaster — described by [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] as being "of the Mr. Chips type" [4] — who lived for many years near Broadstairs, Kent, before retiring to Chilton Lodge in Great Cornard, Sudbury with his surviving sisters [[ethel-bull|Ethel]] and [[constance-bull|Constance]] [5, 6].

Known within the family almost exclusively by the nickname **"Ally"** [1, 2]. According to local resident [[nancy-goodale|Nancy Goodale]], the Bull family had a quirky habit of adding "er" to the ends of their names when addressing one another (e.g., "Ethel-er", "Harry-er"), but Alfred was the only sibling who escaped this peculiar custom [3].

## Skepticism

Despite the legends surrounding his childhood home, Alfred was a staunch sceptic of the Borley hauntings. When interviewed by SPR investigators W. H. Salter and K. M. Goldney in August 1950, Alfred insisted that he had never experienced any objective paranormal phenomena during his entire life at the Rectory [7, 8]. He specifically pointed out that he had slept in the exact same bedroom as his brother's friend, P. Shaw Jeffrey (who claimed to witness poltergeist activity like moving boots and dictionaries), but that he himself "had experienced nothing odd" [7].

## Role as Patron

After the death of Harry Bull, the patronage of the Borley living passed to Alfred as surviving brother. In 1955, following the unexpected death of the [[rev-ac-henning|Rev Alfred Clifford Henning]], Alfred exercised this patronage by helping select a new rector.

## The March 1931 Incident

On **Friday, March 13, 1931**, Alfred visited the Rectory with a Mr. Sellwood and asked [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] for a demonstration of the phenomena, to which Lionel replied that "the goblins didn't perform to order" [9]. Just as Alfred and Sellwood were leaving out the front door, [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] gave a loud cry from inside the house [2, 10]. Rushing back inside, they found Marianne at the bottom of the back stairs, claiming she had been struck on the head by a piece of metal thrown from above [2, 10]. Demonstrating a highly practical response to the "supernatural," Alfred immediately seized a stick from the hallstand and rushed up the back stairs to catch the culprit, but found no one there [2, 10].

## Meeting with the Mathiases (1955)

Alfred Richard Graham Bull, together with his sister Ethel Bull, invited [[rev-lanfranc-mathias|Lanfranc Mathias]] and [[claudine-mathias|Claudine Mathias]] to a "luncheon" at the Four Swans Hotel in North Street, Sudbury, as part of the pre-induction consultations. The hotel stood immediately across the road from The White Horse public house, where [[fred-cartwright|Fred Cartwright]] had reportedly told [[harry-price|Harry Price]] of his four sightings of the phantom nun.

CM's description of the meeting captures something of the atmosphere: Alfred was a tall, thin, slightly stooping man, less talkative than his sister Ethel. During the meal he began to ask: "How do you feel about the Borley gh...?" — but was immediately cut off by Ethel: "Shush! They don't know anything about it!"

Alfred died on **March 17, 1956**, at the age of 90 [12, 16], shortly after the induction ceremony he had attended.

## Harry Bull's Will

Alfred was named as one of three executors of [[harry-bull-will|Harry Bull's will]] (dated 31 January 1912), alongside Bernard Brereton Foyster (a London solicitor and [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]]'s brother) and Henry White (also a solicitor, Winchester).

## Other Details

- On **April 18, 1945**, Alfred attended a talk given by Annette Henning at the Cambridge Women's Luncheon Club and personally informed her afterward that their cousin, Lionel Foyster, had died earlier that same day [14].
- He once gifted paranormal investigator [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] a wooden inkwell that had been handmade by his brother Harry, though it was unfortunately later stolen from Underwood's collection [15].
