# Alfred Richard Graham Bull

Son of [[henry-bull|the Rev Henry Dawson Ellis Bull]] and brother of [[harry-bull|the Rev Harry Foyster Bull]]; the patron of the Borley living after Harry's death in 1927, and one of the executors named in Harry Bull's will. He was a retired schoolmaster who lived for many years near Broadstairs, Kent.

## Role as Patron

After the death of Harry Bull, the patronage of the Borley living passed according to the terms of Harry's will to his executors and heirs. Alfred Richard Graham Bull was the surviving brother who held this patronage role and exercised it when it came to selecting a new rector in 1955 following the unexpected death of the [[rev-ac-henning|Rev Alfred Clifford Henning]].

## Meeting with the Mathiases (1955)

Alfred Richard Graham Bull, together with his sister [[ethel-bull|Ethel Bull]], invited [[rev-lanfranc-mathias|Lanfranc Mathias]] and [[claudine-mathias|Claudine Mathias]] to a "luncheon" at the Four Swans Hotel in North Street, Sudbury, as part of the pre-induction consultations. The hotel stood immediately across the road from The White Horse public house, where [[fred-cartwright|Fred Cartwright]] had reportedly told [[harry-price|Harry Price]] of his four sightings of the phantom nun.

CM's description of the meeting captures something of the atmosphere: Alfred was a tall, thin, slightly stooping man, less talkative than his sister Ethel. During the meal he began to ask: "How do you feel about the Borley gh...?" — but was immediately cut off by Ethel: "Shush! They don't know anything about it!"

Alfred died on 17 March 1956, shortly after the induction ceremony he had attended.

## Harry Bull's Will

Alfred was named as one of three executors of [[harry-bull-will|Harry Bull's will]] (dated 31 January 1912), alongside Bernard Brereton Foyster (a London solicitor and Lionel Foyster's brother) and Henry White (also a solicitor, Winchester).

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Alfred Richard Graham Bull** (1866–1956) was the fourth child and second son of the Rev. Henry Dawson Ellis Bull and Caroline Sarah Foyster. Born on February 23, 1866, he grew up in Borley Rectory but spent much of his adult life away from the village. 

A thorough reference profile of his life and involvement in the Borley case yields the following details:

**Early Life and "Ally"**
Educated at Cambridge University [1], Alfred was known within the family almost exclusively by the nickname **"Ally"** [1, 2]. According to local resident Nancy Goodale, the Bull family had a quirky habit of adding "er" to the ends of their names when addressing one another (e.g., "Ethel-er", "Harry-er"), but Alfred was the only sibling who escaped this peculiar custom [3]. 

**Profession and Skepticism**
Unlike his father and his older brother Harry, Alfred did not enter the clergy. Instead, he became a schoolmaster—described by Marianne Foyster as being **"of the Mr. Chips type"** [4]. He did not teach locally in Borley, but lived and taught for many years near Broadstairs, Kent [5, 6].

Despite the legends surrounding his childhood home, **Alfred was a staunch skeptic of the Borley hauntings.** When interviewed by Society for Psychical Research (SPR) investigators W. H. Salter and K. M. Goldney in August 1950, Alfred insisted that he had never experienced any objective paranormal phenomena during his entire life at the Rectory [7, 8]. He specifically pointed out that he had slept in the exact same bedroom as his brother's friend, P. Shaw Jeffrey (who claimed to have witnessed poltergeist activity like moving boots and dictionaries), but that he himself "had experienced nothing odd" [7]. 

**The March 1931 Incident**
Though skeptical of ghosts, Alfred remained protective of his family and the Rectory's reputation. When he heard that his cousin, the Rev. Lionel Foyster, and his wife Marianne were enduring severe "poltergeist" outbreaks, he was concerned that the family would suffer more unwanted notoriety [9]. 

On **Friday, March 13, 1931**, Alfred visited the Rectory with a Mr. Sellwood and asked Lionel for a demonstration of the phenomena, to which Lionel replied that "the goblins didn't perform to order" [9]. Just as Alfred and Sellwood were leaving out the front door, Marianne gave a loud cry from inside the house [2, 10]. Rushing back inside, they found Marianne at the bottom of the back stairs, claiming she had been struck on the head by a piece of metal thrown from above [2, 10]. Demonstrating a highly practical response to the "supernatural," **Alfred immediately seized a stick from the hallstand and rushed up the back stairs** to catch the culprit, but found no one there [2, 10].

**Legal and Parish Responsibilities**
Alfred took on several official responsibilities for the Bull family:
*   **Executor of Harry's Will:** He was named as one of the three executors and trustees of his brother Harry Bull's 1912 will, which ordered the sale of the Rectory upon Harry's death and established a trust for Harry's widow, Ivy [6].
*   **Patron of the Living:** In his later years, Alfred held the patronage of the Borley living. In 1955, he and his sister Ethel hosted the prospective new rector, Lanfranc Mathias, and his wife Claudine for a "genteel" luncheon before officially approving the appointment [11, 12].

**Later Life at Chilton Lodge**
In his retirement, Alfred lived with his surviving sisters, Ethel and Constance, at Chilton Lodge in Great Cornard, Sudbury [12, 13]. Claudine Mathias described him in 1955 as **"a tall, thin man and was slightly stooping,"** noting that he was far less talkative than his sister [12]. 

During this 1955 visit, Alfred attempted to ask his guests about the Rectory's reputation, inquiring, **"How do you feel about the Borley gh........?"** [12]. Before he could finish the word "ghosts," Ethel swiftly interrupted and admonished him: **"Shush! They don’t know anything about it!"** [12]. A deflated Alfred dropped the subject entirely [12]. 

**Other Lesser-Known Details**
*   On **April 18, 1945**, Alfred attended a talk given by Annette Henning at the Cambridge Women's Luncheon Club and personally informed her afterward that their cousin, Lionel Foyster, had died earlier that same day [14].
*   He once gifted paranormal investigator Peter Underwood a wooden inkwell that had been handmade by his brother Harry, though it was unfortunately later stolen from Underwood's collection [15].

Alfred Bull passed away on **March 17, 1956**, at the age of 90 [12, 16].
