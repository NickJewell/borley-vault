# Mrs Pearson (church caretaker)

The person who looked after the sanctuary lamp in [[borley-church|Borley Church]] — distinct from [[mary-pearson|Mary Pearson]], the Smiths' maid who witnessed the phantom coach. She reported a series of unexplained incidents to the Rev. [[rev-ac-henning|A. C. Henning]] in the spring of 1942.

## The Sanctuary Lamp Incidents

The sanctuary lamp was kept burning near the Tabernacle on the altar where the Sacrament was reserved. Mrs Pearson lit the small wick each morning and put it out at night. For approximately a fortnight in spring 1942, the **wick was frequently moved** during the night in a locked church.

Attempts to prevent this: she placed a psalter over the lamp glass after putting it out and locking the church for the night. Next morning the psalter was on the floor. She then placed a book-cover over the lamp — also removed on two occasions. After this, nothing further happened.

Henning communicated these incidents to Price in a letter of April 16, 1942:

> *"I suggested putting a book or cover of some kind over the lamp glass. She put a psalter over, after putting the light out, and then locked up for the night. She was very surprised next morning to find the book on the floor, especially as both doors were locked and no one could possibly have got in during the night."*

Henning initially suggested a mouse might explain the phenomenon, then retreated from this explanation: "we don't quite see how a bird or bat could remove the book and cover — at least, it seems unlikely to me." He had not found a bird in the church for some time.

## Significance

The incidents are consistent with the long record of minor phenomena in [[borley-church|Borley Church]] — displaced objects, heard footsteps, organ music — and with the Borley Rectory pattern of interference with specifically religious objects (holy water, relics, the Foysters' candlesticks). Price regards the church as an independent locus of psychic activity, separate from but parallel to the Rectory. See [[borley-church]] for related phenomena.
