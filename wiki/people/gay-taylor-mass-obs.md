# Gay Taylor (Mass-Observation reporter)

Journalist working for the **Mass-Observation Archive** — the social research organisation that had been recording everyday life in Britain since 1937 — who investigated the Borley case in the late 1940s and became a useful source for [[eric-dingwall|Dingwall]] and [[kathleen-goldney|Mollie Goldney]].

## Background

Mrs Gay Taylor had been corresponding with [[harry-price|Harry Price]] since 1942, discussing the Waldegrave and Catholic backgrounds to the Borley story. She visited the village herself and became acquainted with [[ethel-bull|Ethel Bull]] and the Turners at the [[rectory-cottage|Rectory Cottage]]. She experienced what she believed to be "a variety of small experiences" at the site, though local opinion was not encouraging — she was repeatedly told that "there waunt no ghosts till Harry Price brought 'um."

## The Christmas 1949 Séance Story

In December 1949, one of Gay Taylor's friends, **Wanda Haines**, took part in a séance in Gloucestershire at which Borley was mentioned. Another sitter at the séance, **Edith Shaw**, had "flared up" at the mention of [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s name, declaring her "a liar, an adulteress and a bigamist." This was significant: Edith Shaw was the sister of **Johnny Fisher** ([[henry-fisher|Henry Francis Fisher]]), the man Marianne had bigamously married. Edith had first-hand knowledge of Marianne's deceptions.

Gay Taylor relayed this information to [[eric-dingwall|Dingwall]] and [[kathleen-goldney|Goldney]], which led them to Edith Shaw and eventually to the full story of Marianne's post-Borley life — a key thread in both the [[the-borley-report|Borley Report]] and [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]]'s subsequent investigation.

## Significance

Gay Taylor's role is primarily as an investigative intermediary: she connected the Borley researchers with the crucial lead about Edith Shaw, which unlocked the documentation of Marianne's bigamy.

> **Note:** Gay Taylor (Mass-Observation journalist) is distinct from [[mrs-g-taylor|Mrs G. Taylor of Chelsea]], a séance participant mentioned in connection with the Turners.
