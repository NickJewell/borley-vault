# Gay Taylor (Mass-Observation reporter)

**Mrs Gay Taylor** (also referred to as Mrs. G. Taylor or Mrs. Guy Taylor) was a journalist working for the **Mass-Observation Archive** — the social research organisation that had been recording everyday life in Britain since 1937 — who lived in Chelsea, London [2, 3]. She investigated the Borley case in the late 1940s and became a useful source for [[eric-dingwall|Dingwall]] and [[kathleen-goldney|Mollie Goldney]].

> **Note:** Gay Taylor (Mass-Observation journalist) is distinct from [[mrs-g-taylor|Mrs G. Taylor of Chelsea]], a séance participant mentioned in connection with the Turners.

## Early Involvement and Correspondence with Harry Price

Mrs. Taylor began corresponding with [[harry-price|Harry Price]] in **1942**, discussing the Waldegrave family and the Roman Catholic background of the Borley story [1]. In 1947, she wrote to Price to report that during a séance she had received a message specifically intended for him: the communicating entity claimed that the reputed ghost nun, [[marie-lairre|Marie Lairre]], had been born near Le Havre on **September 27, 1647**, before entering a nunnery [2].

## Visits to Borley and Local Scepticism

Taylor visited Borley village herself, becoming acquainted with [[ethel-bull|Ethel Bull]] and with James and [[james-turner|Catherine Turner]], who resided at the [[rectory-cottage|Rectory Cottage]] [1]. Although she claimed to have had **"a variety of small experiences"** while there, she was disappointed by the attitude of the villagers, who told her **"there waunt no ghosts till Harry Price brought 'um"** [1].

## The June 1948 Séance

Following Harry Price's death, Mrs. Taylor was one of five sitters at a notable planchette séance held in the Rectory Cottage on the evening of **June 8, 1948**. Sitting alongside Mr. and Mrs. Turner, the Rev. John C. Dening, and a Cambridge undergraduate named Tommy Frankland, the group allegedly established contact with entities claiming to be the recently deceased Harry Price, as well as the former rector, [[harry-bull|Harry Bull]] [2, 4].

## The "Black Magic" and "Satanism" Theories

Mrs. Taylor held several séances devoted to the Borley site in 1947, which led her to adopt a highly sinister view of the hauntings [3]. In a series of letters sent to Society for Psychical Research investigator [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]] in 1957, she outlined her belief that the Rectory's occupants were possessed and that the site was dominated by evil:

- **The 1480 Origin (January 26, 1957):** **"I am unalterably convinced that something happened in that countryside in the past that caused it to be disturbed, at times, to this day . . . it was begun by [a priest and two others] who round about 1480 raised something they couldn't control"** [3].
- **Satanism (February 22, 1957):** She theorised that a local group practised black magic in Long Melford and Borley: **"When we asked [in a seance] if murder or some such crime had occurred at Borley before the 17th century, we got 'Death - Violent,' and that it was due to Satanism"** [5].
- **The Terrified Medium:** She told Hall about a medium who went into a trance at Borley and **"woke up in a terrible state, declaring that she had seen the devil and that the whole place was reeking in Black Magic. After that, no medium for miles around would go into trance at Borley"** [6].
- **Harry Price's "Possession":** Taylor went so far as to allege that even [[harry-price|Harry Price]] had fallen under this evil spell, and that demonic possession had caused him to manufacture or misrepresent paranormal evidence [7].

Trevor Hall ultimately dismissed Taylor's dramatic theories. In his unpublished papers, he concluded: **"I do not at any rate accept Mrs. Taylor's theory that there is evidence to show that certain people behaved criminally because they came to live in the supposedly evil orbit of Borley"** [8].

## Meetings with SPR Investigators and the Marianne Foyster "Exposure"

As [[eric-dingwall|Dingwall]] and [[kathleen-goldney|Goldney]] compiled their critical *Borley Report*, they arranged to meet Mrs. Taylor at the SPR headquarters in Tavistock Square [1]. During this meeting, she relayed two notable pieces of secondhand information:

1. **Local Women's Confessions:** Mrs. Henning (wife of the rector of Borley) had confided that whenever she gave lectures on Borley to the local Women's Institute, women would approach her afterward and, **"under a pledge of secrecy would tell her very similar stories of paranormal happenings in their own households"** [9].

2. **The Gloucestershire Séance:** In December 1949, Taylor's friend Wanda Haines attended a séance in Gloucestershire. When Borley was mentioned, another sitter named **Edith Shaw** suddenly became enraged at the mention of [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s name, declaring the former rector's wife to be a **"liar, an adulteress and a bigamist"** [10]. Edith Shaw was the sister of [[henry-fisher|Johnny Fisher]] (Henry Francis Fisher) — the man with whom Marianne Foyster had engaged in a bigamous marriage [10].

This lead proved crucial: Taylor relayed it to Dingwall and Goldney, which led them to Edith Shaw and eventually to the full story of Marianne's post-Borley life — a key thread in both [[the-borley-report|the Borley Report]] and [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]]'s subsequent investigation.
