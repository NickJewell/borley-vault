# Edward Babbs

Co-author of *Borley Rectory: The Final Analysis* (with [[claudine-mathias]]). A former member of the Society for Psychical Research who had been studying the Borley case for approximately 40 years at the time of writing. He resigned from the SPR only because pressure of work made attendance at meetings difficult, not due to any disagreement with its work.

## SPR Connection

He attended [[kathleen-goldney]]'s lecture to the SPR in 1975 on "The Haunting of Borley Rectory" — the lecture in which Goldney maintained only three hypotheses could explain the alleged phenomena (genuine / Price fraud / others' fraud). EB was in the audience and proposed a **fourth hypothesis**: a mixture of all three. Goldney accepted this reluctantly; the majority of the audience accepted it enthusiastically. [[ajb-robertson|Dr Robertson]] of the Cambridge Commission was also present and disagreed with Goldney's skeptical conclusions.

## Research Methods

He and CM interviewed many local witnesses who had never spoken to previous researchers, explicitly declining to make payments for statements. He also examined original documents including the Bulls' wills, census returns, electoral rolls, and the 1938 surveyor's report.

He knew [[kathleen-goldney]] personally (met her at one point) and found her a "forceful personality."

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Edward Babbs** is a notable contemporary researcher and author in the Borley Rectory case. A resident of Long Melford, Essex, he was a long-standing member of the Society for Psychical Research (SPR) and is a strong supporter of the Borley haunting as a genuine paranormal case [1]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following facts, events, quotes, and details regarding his life and research:

**Biographical Details and Connection to Borley**
*   **SPR Membership:** Babbs was a member of the SPR for many years but resigned solely because work pressures made it difficult to attend meetings; he maintained great respect for the organization, dismissing claims by other authors that it was a "farcical organisation" [2].
*   **Family Connection:** His interest in the case, which spans nearly forty years, is partly rooted in family history. A distant relative of his was employed as a resident domestic at Borley Rectory before the First World War, but she was so frightened by what she saw and heard that she left after only a few days [2, 3]. 

**Major Works and The "Fourth Hypothesis"**
Babbs co-authored the well-regarded study ***Borley Rectory – The Final Analysis*** with Claudine Mathias, the widow of the last rector of Borley and Liston [1, 4]. *(Note: The texts present a minor contradiction regarding the publication date, citing it as either 2001 or 2003 [1, 4]).* 

Babbs is particularly known for formulating a **"fourth hypothesis"** regarding the haunting. During a 1975 SPR meeting where Kathleen M. Goldney lectured and dismissed the haunting, Babbs used the Q&A session to suggest that the phenomena could not be neatly categorised as entirely genuine, entirely faked by Price, or entirely faked by others [5]. He proposed that a very reasonable explanation would be a mixture of all three elements—some genuine phenomena, mixed with exaggeration and trickery by Price and other individuals [5, 6]. This view was reluctantly accepted by Goldney but enthusiastically received by the audience and other researchers like Dr. A.J.B. Robertson [6].

**Specific Investigative Research**
Drawing on his local proximity to Borley, Babbs conducted numerous first-hand interviews and document reviews to counter skeptical claims:
*   **Mary Pearson's Testimony:** Critics often cited Mabel Smith's claim that the rectory maid, Mary Pearson, laughed about the ghost stories and didn't take them seriously. Babbs interviewed two of Pearson’s children and discovered this was false; Pearson had always treated her experiences very seriously when recounting them to her own family [7].
*   **The Bellamy "Cat" Theory:** Babbs confirmed through mutual friends that Dr. H.F. Bellamy and his son totally rejected the skeptical theory that a stray cat had triggered their electrical contact-breaker experiment during Harry Price's tenancy [8]. 
*   **Document Inspections:** He personally inspected a newly discovered diary kept by the Rev. H.D.E. Bull at the Essex County Records Office (covering 1858 to 1860) [9], and he documented Geoffrey Croom-Hollingsworth's discovery of the "Dodie" Bull diary [10].
*   **Clairvoyant Visits:** He held multiple discussions with a local clairvoyant who had visited Borley Church on August 30, 1986, to gather their impressions [11].

**Public Statements and Quotes**
Babbs frequently defended the historical reality of Borley against hoaxes and official dismissals. The sources record his specific quotes on two major events:
*   **The Louis Mayerling Hoax:** When Louis Mayerling published a book claiming to have lived at the Rectory and faked the ghosts with Harry Price, Babbs confidently dismissed it. He was quoted in the *Suffolk Free Press* on January 25, 2001, stating: **"Far from Borley Rectory being Mayerling’s second home, one has to consider if he ever went there, in view of the glaring errors in his book"** [12, 13].
*   **The 2004 Bone Discovery:** When ancient human remains were discovered by workmen converting a 14th-century barn next to Borley Church, the authorities quickly dismissed any connection to the hauntings. In the *Sudbury Mercury* on March 12, 2004, Babbs drew comparisons to the bones found in the Rectory cellars and criticized the lack of investigation: **"If the facts concerning this skeleton remain undisturbed it could lead to more speculation about the area and to be fair to the coroner he may not be aware of all the history of Borley, but it is unfortunate that the authorities have simply dismissed the issue!"** [14].

**Media Appearances**
In addition to his writing, Babbs appeared on the Sky One television documentary *Harry Price: Ghost Hunter* (introduced by Tom Baker), which was broadcast on January 9, 2005 [15].
