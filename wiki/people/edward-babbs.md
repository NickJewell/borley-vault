# Edward Babbs

Co-author of *Borley Rectory: The Final Analysis* (with [[claudine-mathias]]). A resident of Long Melford, Essex, and a former member of the [[spr|Society for Psychical Research]] who had been studying the Borley case for approximately forty years at the time of writing. He resigned from the SPR solely because pressure of work made attendance at meetings difficult, not due to any disagreement with its work — dismissing other authors' characterisation of it as a "farcical organisation" [2].

## Family Connection

His interest in the case is partly rooted in family history. A distant relative was employed as a resident domestic at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] before the First World War, but was so frightened by what she saw and heard that she left after only a few days [2, 3].

## The "Fourth Hypothesis"

Babbs is particularly known for formulating a **"fourth hypothesis"** regarding the haunting. He attended [[kathleen-goldney|Kathleen Goldney]]'s 1975 SPR lecture on "The Haunting of Borley Rectory" — the lecture in which Goldney maintained only three hypotheses could explain the alleged phenomena (genuine / Price fraud / others' fraud). Babbs used the Q&A session to propose a **fourth hypothesis**: a mixture of all three. Goldney accepted this reluctantly; the majority of the audience accepted it enthusiastically. [[ajb-robertson|Dr Robertson]] of the Cambridge Commission was also present and disagreed with Goldney's sceptical conclusions [5, 6].

## Research Methods

He and CM interviewed many local witnesses who had never spoken to previous researchers, explicitly declining to make payments for statements. He also examined original documents including the Bulls' wills, census returns, electoral rolls, and the 1938 surveyor's report. He knew [[kathleen-goldney|Kathleen Goldney]] personally and found her a "forceful personality."

Specific investigative contributions include:

- **Mary Pearson's Testimony:** Critics often cited [[mabel-smith|Mabel Smith]]'s claim that the rectory maid [[mary-pearson|Mary Pearson]] laughed about the ghost stories and didn't take them seriously. Babbs interviewed two of Pearson's children and discovered this was false; Pearson had always treated her experiences very seriously when recounting them to her own family [7].
- **The Bellamy "Cat" Theory:** Babbs confirmed through mutual friends that [[dr-hf-bellamy|Dr H.F. Bellamy]] and his son totally rejected the sceptical theory that a stray cat had triggered their electrical contact-breaker experiment during Harry Price's tenancy [8].
- **Document Inspections:** He personally inspected a newly discovered diary kept by the Rev. H.D.E. Bull at the Essex County Records Office (covering 1858 to 1860) [9], and documented [[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth|Geoffrey Croom-Hollingsworth]]'s discovery of the [[dodie-bull-diary|"Dodie" Bull diary]] [10].
- **Clairvoyant Visits:** He held multiple discussions with a local clairvoyant who visited Borley Church on 30 August 1986 [11].

## Public Statements

Babbs frequently defended the historical reality of Borley against hoaxes and official dismissals. When [[louis-mayerling|Louis Mayerling]] published a book claiming to have lived at the Rectory and faked the ghosts with Harry Price, Babbs was quoted in the *Suffolk Free Press* on 25 January 2001: **"Far from Borley Rectory being Mayerling's second home, one has to consider if he ever went there, in view of the glaring errors in his book"** [12, 13]. When ancient human remains were discovered by workmen converting a 14th-century barn next to Borley Church in 2004, Babbs criticised the lack of investigation in the *Sudbury Mercury* (12 March 2004) [14].

> **Note on publication date:** The sources present a minor contradiction regarding the date of *Borley Rectory – The Final Analysis*, citing it as either 2001 or 2003 [1, 4].

## Media Appearances

Babbs appeared on the Sky One television documentary *Harry Price: Ghost Hunter* (introduced by Tom Baker), broadcast on 9 January 2005 [15].
