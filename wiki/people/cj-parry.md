# C.J. Parry

Solicitor of **Wickham Market**, Suffolk. He performed two contradictory services in relation to [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] in 1946: first helping her obtain probate of [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]]'s will, and then (unsuccessfully) attempting to trace her on behalf of the woman to whom she had abandoned [[john-knights|John Fisher]].

## Probate Work

Following the death of the Rev. Foyster, Parry acted as Marianne's solicitor, successfully helping her obtain the probate for her late husband's will [1]. In **February 1946**, Parry helped Marianne obtain the probate of Foyster's will, of which she was the sole beneficiary and executrix. She received approximately **£850** — the proceeds of Foyster's remaining estate.

## The Failed Trace

Several months later, after Marianne had abandoned John Fisher (leaving him with a Catholic neighbour "just as if she were going out to post a letter"), that neighbour found the house at Martlesham empty and Marianne vanished. She hired **C.J. Parry** to find her — the same solicitor who had just helped Marianne prove the will.

Despite his very recent professional dealings with Marianne, Parry's investigation was completely unsuccessful [1]. Robert Wood notes: "Mr Parry was no more successful than the police, which is extraordinary."

## Wood's Inference

Wood suggests that Parry had his own reasons for not pursuing too vigorously:

> "We can only suppose that he was, for his own reasons, glad that she was out of the way."

He raises — but cannot prove — the possibility that Marianne had compromised Parry as she apparently compromised other men in positions of local authority (notably [[kjt-keer|Dr Keer]] with the death certificate), through sexual entanglement and the threat of exposure.
