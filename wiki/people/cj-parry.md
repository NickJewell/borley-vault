# C.J. Parry

Solicitor of **Wickham Market**, Suffolk. He performed two contradictory services in relation to [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] in 1946: first helping her obtain probate of [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]]'s will, and then (unsuccessfully) attempting to trace her on behalf of the woman to whom she had abandoned [[john-knights|John Fisher]].

## Probate Work

In **February 1946**, C.J. Parry helped Marianne obtain the probate of Foyster's will, of which she was the sole beneficiary and executrix. She received approximately **£850** — the proceeds of Foyster's remaining estate.

## The Failed Trace

Several months later, after Marianne had abandoned John Fisher (leaving him with a Catholic neighbour "just as if she were going out to post a letter"), that neighbour found the house at Martlesham empty and Marianne vanished. She hired **C.J. Parry** to find her — the same solicitor who had just helped Marianne prove the will.

Parry made no headway. Robert Wood notes: "Mr Parry was no more successful than the police, which is extraordinary."

## Wood's Inference

Wood suggests that Parry had his own reasons for not pursuing too vigorously:

> "We can only suppose that he was, for his own reasons, glad that she was out of the way."

He raises — but cannot prove — the possibility that Marianne had compromised Parry as she apparently compromised other men in positions of local authority (notably [[kjt-keer|Dr Keer]] with the death certificate), through sexual entanglement and the threat of exposure.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**C.J. Parry** was a local solicitor based in **Wickham Market** who became entangled in the complicated legal and personal aftermath of Marianne Foyster’s life following the death of her husband, the Rev. Lionel Foyster. 

Although his appearance in the Borley sources is brief, a thorough reference profile highlights his involvement in the mysteries surrounding Marianne's sudden flight to America:

*   **Probate of Lionel Foyster's Will:** Following the death of the Rev. Foyster, Parry acted as Marianne's solicitor, successfully helping her obtain the probate for her late husband's will [1]. 
*   **The 1946 Investigation:** Only a few months after assisting Marianne with the probate, Parry was hired to track her down [1]. In 1946, Marianne suddenly vanished from her home in Martlesham, abandoning a young boy in the care of another woman before fleeing to the United States [1]. When the police searched for Marianne and drew a blank, the concerned caretaker initiated her own private investigation and specifically employed C.J. Parry to locate her [1].
*   **Suspicions and Lesser-Known Details:** Despite his very recent professional and legal dealings with Marianne, Parry's investigation was completely unsuccessful in tracing her whereabouts [1]. The sources describe his failure as "extraordinary" and suggest a highly suspicious or compromised contradiction regarding his actions, noting: **"We can only suppose that he was, for his own reasons, glad that she was out of the way"** [1].
