# Donald W. L. Newland, BA

Lecturer in history and current affairs at the Municipal College, Southend-on-Sea; residing at Antrim Road, Shoeburyness [1]. On 11 April 1947, Newland wrote to describe sounds he had heard inside [[borley-church|Borley Church]] on a previous visit.

## The Church Vigil and the "Earth Falling" Sound

Newland reported hearing a distinct noise that sounded "like earth falling," appearing to come directly from the [[waldegrave-monument|Waldegrave monument]] [1]. He noted that there was no apparent external cause, ruling out the presence of mice, birds, or workmen [1].

Demonstrating a rational and investigative approach, Newland wrote: "I thought the sounds might be subjective, but they repeated themselves on my returning to the church after leaving it to investigate the exterior" [1].

> "It was 'like earth falling' and quite distinct, coming from the [[waldegrave-monument|Waldegrave monument]]. There was no apparent external cause such as mice, birds or workmen."

He suggested the sounds might be subjective but was unconvinced of this, given their repetition.

## Thermal Variations

In addition to the auditory phenomenon, Newland also recorded a notable drop in temperature during his visit: "I also recollect it was very chilly in the church although the outside temperature must have been between 55° and 60° F" [1].
