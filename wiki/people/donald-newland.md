# Donald W. L. Newland, BA

Lecturer in history and current affairs at the Municipal College, Southend-on-Sea; writing from Antrim Road, Shoeburyness. On 11 April 1947, Newland wrote to describe sounds he had heard inside [[borley-church|Borley Church]] on a previous visit.

> "It was 'like earth falling' and quite distinct, coming from the [[waldegrave-monument|Waldegrave monument]]. There was no apparent external cause such as mice, birds or workmen."

He added that the sounds repeated when he returned to the church after going outside to investigate the exterior. He also noted that the church felt noticeably cold — "very chilly" despite an outside temperature of between 55° and 60° F. Newland suggested the sounds might be subjective but was unconvinced of this, given their repetition.
