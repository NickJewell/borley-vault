# Miss Vivienne Wilson

The 13-year-old sister-in-law of [[mrs-ag-wilson|Mrs A. G. Wilson]], with whom she cycled to Borley in the summer of 1949. On the side path to locked [[borley-church|Borley Church]], she independently called out "Can you hear the organ?" before Mrs Wilson could speak — confirming that both heard the music simultaneously without any prompt from each other.

Her age and spontaneous reaction are significant: she had no prior knowledge of the organ-music reports at Borley church, making this a clean independent observation. Peter Underwood met Vivienne in person and confirmed the details of her account.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Miss Vivienne Wilson** appears in the Borley sources as a witness to an unexplained auditory phenomenon at Borley Church in the late 1940s. She is identified as the sister-in-law of Mrs. A. G. Wilson [1].

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific details, dates, and quotes regarding her experience:

**The Visit of August 1949**
*   **The Excursion:** At the end of August 1949, Miss Vivienne Wilson and her sister-in-law cycled to Borley specifically to see the Rectory [1, 2]. 
*   **Lack of Prior Knowledge:** While they had heard the Rectory was haunted, they had no idea that the building had already been destroyed by fire [2]. Furthermore, they had absolutely no prior knowledge that the haunting phenomena allegedly extended to the nearby church [1, 2].
*   **The Churchyard:** Upon discovering only the ruined remains of the Rectory, the two women decided to look at the church out of mere "general interest" [2]. Finding the main door locked, they walked along a small path leading to a side door [2].

**The Phantom Organ Music and Quote**
*   While walking along this side path, both women independently heard the distinct sound of an organ playing from within the building [1, 2].
*   Before Mrs. Wilson could even voice her thoughts about the music, Vivienne explicitly called attention to it, asking: **"Can you hear the organ?"** [2].
*   Not realizing they were experiencing anything supernatural, the women did not give the music a "second thought" at the time [2]. Finding the side door was also locked and being unable to enter the church, they left the grounds [2]. 
*   **The Realization:** They stopped at a nearby cottage and purchased a copy of the Rev. A. C. Henning's recently published 1949 booklet, *Haunted Borley* [2]. It was only upon reading the booklet that they discovered the church's reputation for phantom organ music, leading Mrs. Wilson to write a formal report of their experience from her home in Cricklewood in February 1950, noting the significance of their "blind" encounter [1, 2].

The provided sources contain no further biographical details, events, or contradictions regarding Miss Vivienne Wilson beyond her participation in this single, corroborated sighting [1, 2].
