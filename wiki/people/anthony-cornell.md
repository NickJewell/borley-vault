# Anthony C. Cornell

Cambridge County Councillor, President of Cambridge University Society for Psychical Research, and Council Member of the [[society-for-psychical-research]]. Co-author (with [[alan-gauld]]) of *Poltergeists* (1979), and author of many papers in the *Journal and Proceedings of the SPR*.

## Cancelled Borley Investigation

In December 1954, Cornell announced he was "preparing to undertake extensive scientific tests for any psychical phenomena that might occur on the site of [[borley-rectory]] after appropriate psi-manufacturing conditions had been introduced."

The *Suffolk Free Press* carried a front-page story describing elaborate scientific apparatus to be deployed: the Rectory site was to be flooded with high-pitched sound waves, with a radar-like detector, photo-electric cells and delicate sound-recording equipment. The team was to be led by Cornell with two or three members of the Cambridge SPR.

The tests were cancelled due to publicity and the announcement of the then-forthcoming [[the-borley-report|Borley Report]] — the SPR presumably did not wish its planned critical publication to be pre-empted by a new investigation that might produce positive results.

## Later Career

A practical and respected psychical researcher for over fifty years, Cornell is co-author of *Poltergeists* (1979) with [[alan-gauld]]. Their book includes an illuminating passage on [[harry-price]] and the Borley haunting. Cornell has investigated many poltergeist cases and been a long-standing SPR Council member. [[peter-underwood]] noted him as a "wise and knowledgeable parapsychologist."

## Significance

The cancellation of Cornell's 1954 investigation is a revealing episode: at the moment when proper systematic scientific investigation using new technology was about to be brought to the Borley site, it was abandoned because of political considerations within the SPR. The forthcoming Borley Report's implicit message — that the case was closed — had in effect pre-empted a genuine new inquiry.

**Anthony C. A thorough reference profile based on the sources includes the following details:

**The Aborted 1954 Scientific Investigation**
Cornell is best known in Borley lore for planning what would have been a highly advanced, technological investigation of the haunted site. In December 1954, he announced that he was **"preparing to undertake extensive scientific tests for any psychical phenomena that might occur on the site of Borley Rectory after appropriate psi-manufacturing conditions had been introduced"** [1]. 

His plans made front-page news in the *Suffolk Free Press*, which reported that Cornell and a team of two or three members of the Cambridge SPR intended to use an elaborate array of scientific apparatus for their tests [1]. This equipment was to include:
*   Devices to flood the entire area with high-pitched sound waves.
*   A radar-like detector.
*   Photo-electric cells.
*   Delicate sound-recording equipment [1].

However, this ambitious high-tech investigation was never carried out. The tests were ultimately cancelled due to a combination of the intense media publicity the announcement generated and the impending publication of the highly critical 1956 SPR *Borley Report* (authored by Dingwall, Goldney, and Hall) which sought to thoroughly debunk the haunting [1].

**Role as Literary Executor**
Cornell was a trusted figure among his peers. When Mrs. Cecil Ryan Baines (a dedicated Borley researcher who extensively investigated the possibility of "Marie Lairre" being a murdered French Catholic novice) passed away, she appointed Tony Cornell and his frequent collaborator, Dr. Alan Gauld, as her Literary Executors. She bequeathed all of her books and copious Borley papers to them [3]. 

**Published Works and Legacy**
Cornell contributed many thought-provoking papers to the *Journal* and *Proceedings* of the SPR [2]. His major published books include:
*   ***Poltergeists* (1979)**, co-authored with Dr. Alan Gauld [2, 4]. In this authoritative volume, the authors defended Harry Price against the accusations of dishonesty levelled at him regarding Borley, stating that such allegations appeared "to be based upon very debatable evidence" [5]. 
*   ***Investigating the Paranormal***, published by the Parapsychology Foundation in 2002 [2].
