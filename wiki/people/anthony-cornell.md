# Anthony C. Cornell

Cambridge County Councillor, President of Cambridge University Society for Psychical Research, and Council Member of the [[spr|Society for Psychical Research]]. Co-author (with [[alan-gauld|Alan Gauld]]) of *Poltergeists* (1979) [2], and author of many papers in the *Journal and Proceedings of the SPR*. Recognised as a practical and highly respected psychical researcher with a career spanning over fifty years [2].

## The Aborted 1954 Scientific Investigation

Cornell is best known in Borley lore for planning what would have been a highly advanced, technological investigation of the haunted site. In December 1954, he announced he was "preparing to undertake extensive scientific tests for any psychical phenomena that might occur on the site of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] after appropriate psi-manufacturing conditions had been introduced" [1].

The *[[suffolk-free-press-article|Suffolk Free Press]]* carried a front-page story describing elaborate scientific apparatus to be deployed:
- Devices to flood the entire area with high-pitched sound waves
- A radar-like detector
- Photo-electric cells
- Delicate sound-recording equipment [1]

The tests were cancelled due to publicity and the announcement of the then-forthcoming [[the-borley-report|Borley Report]] — the SPR presumably did not wish its planned critical publication to be pre-empted by a new investigation that might produce positive results.

## Role as Literary Executor

When Mrs. Cecil Ryan Baines (a dedicated Borley researcher who extensively investigated the possibility of "Marie Lairre" being a murdered French Catholic novice) passed away, she appointed Cornell and [[alan-gauld|Alan Gauld]] as her Literary Executors, bequeathing all of her books and copious Borley papers to them [3].

## Later Career

Cornell investigated many poltergeist cases and been a long-standing SPR Council member. His major published books include *Poltergeists* (1979, with Alan Gauld) [2, 4] — which defended [[harry-price|Harry Price]] against the accusations of dishonesty levelled at him regarding Borley, stating that such allegations appeared "to be based upon very debatable evidence" [5] — and *Investigating the Paranormal* (Parapsychology Foundation, 2002) [2]. [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] noted him as a "wise and knowledgeable parapsychologist."

## Significance

The cancellation of Cornell's 1954 investigation is a revealing episode: at the moment when proper systematic scientific investigation using new technology was about to be brought to the Borley site, it was abandoned because of political considerations within the SPR. The forthcoming Borley Report's implicit message — that the case was closed — had in effect pre-empted a genuine new inquiry.
