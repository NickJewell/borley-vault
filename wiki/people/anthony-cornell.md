# Anthony C. Cornell

Cambridge County Councillor, President of Cambridge University Society for Psychical Research, and Council Member of the [[society-for-psychical-research]]. Co-author (with [[alan-gauld]]) of *Poltergeists* (1979), and author of many papers in the *Journal and Proceedings of the SPR*.

## Cancelled Borley Investigation

In December 1954, Cornell announced he was "preparing to undertake extensive scientific tests for any psychical phenomena that might occur on the site of [[borley-rectory]] after appropriate psi-manufacturing conditions had been introduced."

The *Suffolk Free Press* carried a front-page story describing elaborate scientific apparatus to be deployed: the Rectory site was to be flooded with high-pitched sound waves, with a radar-like detector, photo-electric cells and delicate sound-recording equipment. The team was to be led by Cornell with two or three members of the Cambridge SPR.

The tests were cancelled due to publicity and the announcement of the then-forthcoming [[the-borley-report|Borley Report]] — the SPR presumably did not wish its planned critical publication to be pre-empted by a new investigation that might produce positive results.

## Later Career

A practical and respected psychical researcher for over fifty years, Cornell is co-author of *Poltergeists* (1979) with [[alan-gauld]]. Their book includes an illuminating passage on [[harry-price]] and the Borley haunting. Cornell has investigated many poltergeist cases and been a long-standing SPR Council member. [[peter-underwood]] noted him as a "wise and knowledgeable parapsychologist."

## Significance

The cancellation of Cornell's 1954 investigation is a revealing episode: at the moment when proper systematic scientific investigation using new technology was about to be brought to the Borley site, it was abandoned because of political considerations within the SPR. The forthcoming Borley Report's implicit message — that the case was closed — had in effect pre-empted a genuine new inquiry.
