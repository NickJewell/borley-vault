# Arabella FitzJames / Sister Ignatia

Natural daughter of King James II by [[arabella-churchill|Arabella Churchill]], and sister of [[henrietta-fitzjames|Henrietta FitzJames (Lady Waldegrave)]]. She became a Benedictine nun at the English house at Pontoise, France, under the name **Sister Ignatia**. Ivan Banks investigates her as a candidate for the ghost nun of Borley Rectory before eliminating her.

## Family

- **Father:** King James II
- **Mother:** [[arabella-churchill|Arabella Churchill]]
- **Sister:** [[henrietta-fitzjames|Henrietta FitzJames]], who married [[henry-lord-waldegrave|Henry Lord Waldegrave]]

Arabella FitzJames is therefore the aunt of **Arabella Waldegrave (the granddaughter)** — the girl who vanished from the records, long considered a candidate for the ghost nun.

> **Note:** There were thus two women named "Arabella" in this family who became nuns: Arabella FitzJames (sister of Henrietta, aunt of the granddaughter Arabella) and the granddaughter [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella Waldegrave]] herself. Banks's research untangled the confusion between them.

## Religious Life

Arabella FitzJames became a novice at Pontoise on April 16, 1689, and was professed a nun on April 30, 1690, at the age of sixteen. She bore the religious name **Sister Ignatia**.

Banks's research into the Oulton Abbey archives confirms she was clothed at Pontoise — she did not go on to Brussels, which means the theory that she was removed from Pontoise to Brussels (to separate her from a suitor) is not supported by the records.

## Banks's Investigation

Banks considers Arabella FitzJames in the context of [[mary-lycett|Mary Lycett]]'s 1952 theory, which confused the identities of the two Arabellas. Lycett's scenario describes an "Arabella Waldegrave" placed in Pontoise as a child, who then becomes romantically involved with a cousin named Henry Waldegrave. Banks disentangles this by showing:

1. Arabella FitzJames was placed at Pontoise as an adult (novice at sixteen, professed at seventeen)
2. The granddaughter Arabella Waldegrave (Henry and Henrietta's daughter) was placed at Pontoise much later, as a child of seven

The adult Arabella FitzJames/Sister Ignatia is eliminated as the ghost nun candidate because she was professed, remained in religious life, and there is no evidence of her disappearing or dying under unusual circumstances.

## Significance

Arabella FitzJames/Sister Ignatia is significant to the Borley research primarily because her presence at Pontoise has caused persistent confusion with her niece the granddaughter Arabella Waldegrave. Untangling the two Arabellas was necessary for Banks's nun-identity investigation. Her elimination from candidacy points attention back toward the granddaughter and toward [[barbara-waldegrave|Barbara Waldegrave / Sister Apollonia]] as the most credible candidates.
