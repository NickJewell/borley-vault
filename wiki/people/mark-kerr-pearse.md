Staff of the British Legation in Geneva. On holiday in England in 1937 when he saw [[harry-price]]'s *Times* advertisement for Borley observers; he enrolled, thinking ghost-hunting would be "a pleasant way of spending part of his vacation." He ultimately became the most dedicated official observer, spending weeks in the Rectory — sometimes entirely alone.

Price: "Mr. Kerr-Pearse is a very shrewd observer and a model of what a psychical researcher should be." His minute-by-minute protocols were "the most interesting report" of all the observers.

## Time at Borley

First visit June 26, 1937. Subsequent long stays through November 1, 1937. Often spent nights on the camp bed in the octagonal summer-house watching the [[nuns-walk]] for the nun. The only other official observer who spent a night entirely alone was J.W. Burden (December 1937).

## Principal Phenomena Experienced

**Pencil marks appearing**: Subjected the walls to "microscopic scrutiny," checked at 7:25 p.m., returned at 8:30 p.m. and found two new marks not there before. New marks appeared on at least a dozen separate occasions during his stays.

**50-lb coal bag moved 18 inches**: Standing on the landing with Rupert Haig, both heard a rustling from below. Found the 56-lb coal bag (positioned by a stain on the floor) had moved approximately 18 inches — no explanation found.

**Locked in the Base Room**: While having supper alone (having just passed through from the pantry), heard a small click. Tried to leave — found himself locked in. The key was on the inside of the door. Whatever locked it must have been in the room while he was eating supper.

**Petrified frog**: Found a flat, desiccated frog in the passage outside the drawing-room one morning — all doors had been closed overnight, and it had not been there on his evening patrol.

**The strange coat**: Confirmed to Price and Glanville on July 16, 1937 that when he and Henning's man Herbert Mayes went through the Rectory on June 1, the coat was not in the house — and it appeared there between June 1 and June 2.

**Coat pulled twice**: On October 28, during his last long stay, his coat was pulled twice in quick succession by an invisible agency. The interval was about 15 seconds.

**"Cold spot" corroboration**: Confirmed that his cousin Rupert Haig was affected by sudden inexplicable cold at the same landing spot as other independent witnesses.

**Séance with Hennings**: On July 16, with Rev. and Mrs. Henning, rapped at the Base Room table; "a most extraordinary noise was heard as though coming from the kitchen, and slowly moving down passage towards them" — ceased when they stood up.

## What Price Suppressed from His Reports (spr-vi)

The SPR found that several of Kerr-Pearse's observations were omitted by Price in MHH because they pointed toward normal explanations:

- His June 1937 report noted "a rose tree which was repeatedly blown backwards and forwards against the wall causing knocks which 'might provide an excellent ghost for the imaginative'" — entirely omitted
- He noted "found many traces of mice in cupboards" — omitted (Price stated in MHH that "never once has any observer mentioned rats")
- He first suggested the "Well Tank Bottom Me" reading of the wall-message in July 1937 (predating Phythian-Adams's "discovery" of the same reading by nearly four years) and explored the well tank himself in August 1937, finding only old tins and broken glass — both facts omitted by Price, who later presented Phythian-Adams's reading as a revelation

## SPR's Assessment

The SPR regarded Kerr-Pearse as one of the two most credible official observers (alongside [[sidney-glanville]]). His evidence was taken seriously. However, the SPR offers normal explanations for his principal observations:

- **Coal bag moved:** The rustling noise that drew attention may have had other causes; no marks on the floor showing whether the bag was lifted or dragged; insufficient detail in the original report to treat as a genuinely anomalous incident
- **Locked in Base Room:** More probably he turned the key himself while having supper (habitual locking action not consciously remembered) than that it was locked paranormally
- **Pencil marks:** Given the evidence for Price adding marks when at the rear of touring parties, and the availability of the house to intruders via the cellar trapdoor, fresh marks cannot be attributed to paranormal agency

## Reliability

A diplomat, accustomed to precise observation and recording. His very long and detailed protocols are written with meticulous care and include appropriate qualifications — including explicit uncertainty about whether phenomena were caused by natural means. The SPR considered his original unpublished reports more reliably skeptical than Price's published versions, which selected only the more dramatic elements.
