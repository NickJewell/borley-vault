# Santiago Monk

(Born 1866; died London, 25 July 1939) Personal acquaintance and admirer of [[marianne-foyster]], and a family friend of the Foysters from their time in Canada between 1922 and 1929. An Englishman who served as a diplomat in the Chilean Embassy in London, listed as Commercial Attaché until 1939 [1–3]. Marianne described him as her "spiritual godfather."

## Relationship with the Foysters

Monk was a family friend of the Foysters dating back to their time living in Canada [1]. During the first year or so of the Foysters' stay at [[borley-rectory]] — particularly during 1932 — Marianne visited Monk in London and, according to [[edith-wildgoose|Miss Dytor]] (later Mrs. Wildgoose), received expensive presents from him [3, 5].

In a letter to [[ian-shaw|Ian Shaw]] written around 21 May 1956, Marianne vigorously denied both the gifts and any affair. She admitted seeing him "about twice a year" in London but stressed that he was "an old man of 76" and was well known to her husband, Lionel [7]. Regarding Miss Dytor's specific claim about presents, Marianne wrote: **"she must have kept them for I never saw those"** [7]. She firmly concluded: **"There was also no affair there"** [7].

## Marianne's Fantasies

Later in life, particularly while running the [[jonquille-et-cie|*Jonquille et Cie*]] florist shop in Wimbledon with her lover Frank Pearless ([[francois-darles|François d'Arles]]), Marianne wove Monk into a series of fictitious and grandiose stories about her own background [8, 9]. She told acquaintances — including [[mr-and-mrs-fenton|Mr. and Mrs. Fenton]], owners of a local chemist's shop — that she was not the daughter of her real father but was instead **"Marianne Monk"**, the daughter of Santiago Monk and a fabricated aristocrat she named the **Countess Sarah von Kiergraff, late of Schleswig-Holstein** [2, 8, 10, 11]. To explain her marriage to the much older Lionel Foyster, she falsely claimed that because her diplomat father was constantly away on business, Foyster had merely raised her as a child [8, 10]. She also told the Fentons that Pearless was Foyster's son.

## Séance Anomalies

Santiago Monk's name unexpectedly appeared during a Borley planchette séance. An entity claiming to be [[harry-bull|Harry Bull]] was asked **"Do you know Santiago Monk?"** — to which it replied **"Yes."** When asked **"Is he Pearless?"** the entity replied **"No"** [12]. Because his name appeared in this obscure séance transcript, later Borley researcher [[ivan-banks|Ivan Banks]] — apparently unaware of the Chilean diplomat's identity — was left thoroughly baffled and theorised that "Santiago Monk" might literally refer to a **"Spanish Catholic monk"** historically connected to the ghost nun [13].

## Significance

Monk's role is primarily as evidence of [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]]'s complicated social life during the Foyster period — a middle-aged woman of independent connections maintaining relationships beyond the Rectory and beyond the d'Arles affair. The BRC treats the relationship as non-sexual based on Marianne's own denials and the age gap. His death in July 1939 — five months after the [[fire-1939|Rectory fire]] — removes any possibility of his testimony being obtained. Sceptical investigator [[trevor-hall|Trevor H. Hall]], while researching Marianne's life, found a witness who claimed that while Marianne was living under the name "Voyster" at [[gyppeswyck-road-ipswich|Gyppeswyck Road in Ipswich]], she was often visited by an "elderly gentleman," and sarcastically speculated whether this might have been Monk — though as Monk died in 1939, this would only be possible if her time at Gyppeswyck Road predated his death [4].
