# Santiago Monk

(Born 1866; died London, 25 July 1939) Personal acquaintance and admirer of [[marianne-foyster]], and a family friend of the Foysters from their time in Canada between 1922 and 1929. A Chilean diplomat at the Embassy in London, listed as Commercial Attaché until 1939. Marianne described him as her "spiritual godfather."

## Relationship with Marianne

During the first year or so of the Foysters' stay at [[borley-rectory]] — particularly during 1932 — Marianne visited Monk in London and, according to Miss Dytor (see [[edith-wildgoose]]), received expensive presents from him.

In a letter to [[ian-greenwood]] written in May 1956, Marianne denied receiving gifts from Monk: "She must have kept them for I never saw those." She admitted seeing him "about twice a year" in London but described him as "an old man of 76" who was well known by [[lionel-foyster]] and insisted there was "no affair there."

## Marianne's Fantasies

When living with [[francois-darles|Francois d'Arles]] in Wimbledon (see [[jonquille-et-cie]]), Marianne told friends she was Santiago Monk's daughter by the imaginary "Countess Sarah von Kiergraff, late of Schleswig-Holstein" — one of several elaborate false identities Marianne invented for herself. She also told the Fentons (Mr and Mrs Fenton, owners of a local chemist's shop) that she was the daughter of a Chilean diplomat, a graduate of a Cambridge ladies' college, and that Pearless was Foyster's son.

## Significance

Monk's role is primarily as evidence of Marianne's complicated social life during the Foyster period — a middle-aged woman of independent connections maintaining relationships beyond the Rectory and beyond the d'Arles affair. The BRC treats the relationship as non-sexual based on Marianne's own denials and the age gap (he was 76 when she knew him in London). His death in July 1939 — five months after the [[fire-1939|Rectory fire]] — removes any possibility of his testimony being obtained.
