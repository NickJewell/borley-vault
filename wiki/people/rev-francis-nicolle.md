# Rev. Francis G. S. Nicolle

Vicar of St Thomas's, Bethnal Green. In a letter of October 18, 1942, Nicolle provided [[harry-price|Price]] with genealogical clarification of the Waldegrave family's connection to Borley — specifically identifying an earlier generation than the famous Sir Edward.

## Waldegrave Research

Price and [[georgina-dawson|Mrs. Dawson]] had identified Sir Edward Waldegrave (c.1517–61) as the first member of the family "actively connected with Borley." Nicolle supplied a correction, tracing the connection back another generation:

Sir Thomas (great-grandson of Sir Richard Waldegrave, Speaker of the House of Commons, d.1402) married Elizabeth Fray and died in 1500. He had three sons; the second son, **Edward** (ancestor of the present family), settled at Borley and married Elizabeth Chayney. This Edward died in 1500. His son **John Waldegrave** (who married Lora Rochester) died in 1514. **Sir Edward** (the famous Tower-prisoner) was the eldest son of John.

This genealogy establishes that the Waldegrave connection to Borley reaches back to c.1460–1480 — approximately a century earlier than previously assumed — through the Edward who "settled at Borley" before his more famous grandson became Lord of the Manor.

## Significance

Nicolle's letter extends the Waldegrave timeline at Borley by a full generation, meaning the family's Catholic history at the site is even more deeply embedded than Price had stated. This strengthens the historical plausibility of a Waldegrave-Catholic murder connection in 1667 — by that date the family had been at Borley for nearly two centuries.
