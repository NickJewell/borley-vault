# M. Knox (M. P. Knox)

An undergraduate student at University College, Oxford, and an official observer at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] during [[harry-price|Harry Price's]] 1937–38 tenancy.

## The February 1938 Visit

Knox conducted an overnight vigil on **February 16–17, 1938** as part of a four-man team of Oxford undergraduates from University College. His companions were **Mr. [[sg-welles|S. G. Welles]]** (a Rhodes Scholar), **Mr. [[r-hawkin|R. Hawkins]]** (also spelled Hawkin), and **Mr. Samuel J. Millberg** (also spelled Milberg).

**Reported phenomena:** During their night at the Rectory, the group reported a mysterious "luminous patch" appearing on the ceiling of the [[blue-room|Blue Room]], and unexplained footsteps apparently travelling from the kitchen along the passage toward the Drawing Room.

Knox also wrote to Price noting that he had observed **"several bushes near the house which might produce a rapping noise against the walls or windows if the wind blew,"** and that during the night he and his friends heard "repeated and distinct thuds or raps, one every ten or twenty seconds," which they attributed not to ghosts but to the bushes outside the house.

## Suppression by Price

The [[the-borley-report|SPR Report]] cites Knox in its introduction as a prime example of Price omitting qualified testimony in favour of a normal explanation:

> "Mr M. Knox, of University College, Oxford... stated that he noticed 'several bushes near the house which might produce a rapping noise against the walls or windows if the wind blew'... Price did not print the observations of either Mr Kerr-Pearse or Mr Knox, although he mentions bushes as possible causes of sounds in the 'Blue Book' of instructions."

Knox and Hawkin also reportedly entered the house via the cellars, confirming that the Rectory was easily accessible to unauthorised visitors throughout the tenancy — a fact that potentially explains many of the "fresh" pencil markings and moved objects found during visits.
