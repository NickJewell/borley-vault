# M. Knox (M. P. Knox)

M. Knox (full name M. P. Knox) was an official observer at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] from University College, Oxford. He visited on 19 February 1938 with R. Hawkin.

## Observations

Knox wrote to [[harry-price|Price]] noting that he had observed "several bushes near the house which might produce a rapping noise against the walls or windows if the wind blew," and that during the night he and his friends heard "repeated and distinct thuds or raps, one every ten or twenty seconds," which they attributed not to ghosts but to the bushes outside the house. This observation was suppressed by Price in his books.

The SPR report cites Knox in its introduction as a prime example of Price omitting qualified testimony in favour of a normal explanation:

> "Mr M. Knox, of University College, Oxford... stated that he noticed 'several bushes near the house which might produce a rapping noise against the walls or windows if the wind blew'... Price did not print the observations of either Mr Kerr-Pearse or Mr Knox, although he mentions bushes as possible causes of sounds in the 'Blue Book' of instructions."

Knox and Hawkin also entered the house via the cellars, confirming that the rectory was easily accessible to unauthorised visitors throughout the tenancy — a fact that potentially explains many of the "fresh" pencil markings and moved objects found during visits.

Knox's contribution to the Borley investigation centers on a single overnight vigil conducted on **February 16–17, 1938** [1, 2]. He investigated the Rectory as part of a four-man team comprised entirely of Oxford undergraduates from University College. His companions were **Mr. S. G. Welles** (a Rhodes Scholar), **Mr. R. During their night at the Rectory, Knox and his party reported experiencing both visual and auditory anomalies:
*   **The Luminous Patch:** The group witnessed a mysterious "luminous patch" appearing on the ceiling of the infamous Blue Room [2].
