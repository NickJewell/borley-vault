# Esme Lawton

Daughter of [[henry-lawton|Rev. Henry Lawton]] and [[irene-lawton|Irene Lawton]]; born September 1933 during or immediately after their summer residence at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]]; goddaughter of [[ethel-bull|Ethel Bull]].

## Background

Irene Lawton was heavily pregnant during the family's month-long stay at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] in August 1933, while [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] was in hospital. Esme was born in September 1933, as the family's stay was ending.

[[ethel-bull|Ethel Bull]] — the eldest surviving daughter of [[henry-bull|Rev. Henry Dawson Bull]], a long-term resident of the area, and one of the four sisters present at the famous 1900 nun sighting — became Esme's godmother. This connection formed during [[henry-lawton|Henry Lawton]]'s acquaintance with Ethel at Great Cornard and Chilton Lodge.

## Significance

The choice of Ethel Bull as godmother is a biographical footnote that illustrates how tightly the various human threads of the Borley story were interwoven. The Lawton family, who arrived as complete outsiders, departed with a permanent family link to the Bull dynasty through Esme's baptism.
