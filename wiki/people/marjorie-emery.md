# Marjorie Emery

A woman named in [[andrew-clarke]]'s alternative theory about the paternity of baby John Emery — the infant adopted by [[marianne-foyster]] in 1932 who died after 4.5 months. Clarke suggests that rather than the child being Marianne's own biological son by [[francois-darles]] (Frank Pearless), the baby may have been the child of Frank and Marjorie Emery, subsequently adopted by the Foysters.

## Clarke's theory

In essay 14 ("Lawless, the Lodger"), Clarke presents this as a speculative "alternative suggestion":

> *Frank seems to have convinced Ian that the child was actually his, and his show of grief when the child sadly died after a few months, suggests that he really believed it. In fact, an alternative suggestion, that the baby was the result of an affair between Frank and his mother, Marjorie Emery, and was subsequently adopted by the Foysters, seems much more likely.*
> — *Sidelights on Borley Rectory*, essay 14

The "his mother" in this sentence refers to Marjorie Emery as the child's mother — i.e., the baby John Emery's biological mother — not Frank's own mother.

## Context

The paternity of John Emery was contested. Frank Pearless believed the baby was his by Marianne and was angry when she denied it. [[edith-wildgoose]] (the nurse Miss Dytor) was under the impression Marianne had given birth herself. The existing wiki entry for [[john-emery]] notes the "d'Arles paternity question." Clarke's theory adds Marjorie Emery as a third possibility: that the baby was Frank's by a different woman entirely, and that Marianne adopted it as her own child (as she was to do repeatedly throughout her life).

## Significance

If Clarke's theory is correct, Marjorie Emery would be the biological mother of the baby who died at Borley Rectory in 1932. The theory remains speculative — no documentary evidence is cited — but it would explain why Frank showed grief at the baby's death while Marianne denied biological maternity, and why the nurse was confused about Marianne's apparent pregnancy.
