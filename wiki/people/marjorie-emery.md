# Marjorie Emery

A woman named in [[andrew-clarke]]'s alternative theory about the paternity of baby John Emery — the infant adopted by [[marianne-foyster]] in 1932 who died after 4.5 months. Clarke suggests that rather than the child being Marianne's own biological son by [[francois-darles]] (Frank Pearless), the baby may have been the child of Frank and Marjorie Emery, subsequently adopted by the Foysters.

## Clarke's theory

In essay 14 ("Lawless, the Lodger"), Clarke presents this as a speculative "alternative suggestion":

> *Frank seems to have convinced Ian that the child was actually his, and his show of grief when the child sadly died after a few months, suggests that he really believed it. In fact, an alternative suggestion, that the baby was the result of an affair between Frank and his mother, Marjorie Emery, and was subsequently adopted by the Foysters, seems much more likely.*
> — *Sidelights on Borley Rectory*, essay 14

The "his mother" in this sentence refers to Marjorie Emery as the child's mother — i.e., the baby John Emery's biological mother — not Frank's own mother.

## Context

The paternity of John Emery was contested. Frank Pearless believed the baby was his by Marianne and was angry when she denied it. [[edith-wildgoose]] (the nurse Miss Dytor) was under the impression Marianne had given birth herself. The existing wiki entry for [[john-emery]] notes the "d'Arles paternity question." Clarke's theory adds Marjorie Emery as a third possibility: that the baby was Frank's by a different woman entirely, and that Marianne adopted it as her own child (as she was to do repeatedly throughout her life).

## Significance

If Clarke's theory is correct, Marjorie Emery would be the biological mother of the baby who died at Borley Rectory in 1932. The theory remains speculative — no documentary evidence is cited — but it would explain why Frank showed grief at the baby's death while Marianne denied biological maternity, and why the nurse was confused about Marianne's apparent pregnancy.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Marjorie Emery** is recorded in the Borley sources solely in connection to a tragic and highly suspicious episode during the Foyster incumbency. She was the natural mother of **John Evemond Emery**, a baby boy who was officially adopted by the Rev. Lionel and Marianne Foyster but who died shortly after arriving at Borley Rectory [1, 2].

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific biographical details and the major contradictions surrounding her true identity:

**Biographical Details and the Birth of John Emery**
*   Marjorie Emery is described in the records as a **"spinster of no occupation"** whose home address was in **Cobham, Kent** [2, 3]. 
*   She gave birth to John Evemond Emery on **April 25, 1932**, at 30 Swanage Road, Southend-on-Sea in Essex, which was the home of a midwife [3, 4]. 
*   The space for the father's name on the child's birth certificate was left blank, indicating that the baby was illegitimate [3]. Following the birth, the baby was given up straight away to a London agency, through which he was supposedly adopted by Marianne Foyster and brought to Borley Rectory in May 1932 [4].

**The "M.E.R." Contradiction and Theories of an Alias**
Marjorie Emery's very existence as a separate person is heavily questioned by Borley investigators, who strongly suspect that she was merely a phantom identity created by Marianne Foyster. 
*   When the skeptical investigator Trevor Hall tracked down John Emery's birth certificate in the 1950s, he discovered a startling detail: the mother's initials on the certificate were **"M.E.R."**—which are the exact initials of **M**arianne **E**mily **R**ebecca Foyster [3]. 
*   This clue was compounded by the fact that Marianne Foyster had been absent from Borley Rectory in the early spring of 1932, allegedly "resting" from her experiences with the poltergeists [3]. 
*   Furthermore, when the baby was brought to Borley in May, Marianne hired a state-registered nurse, Miss Edith May Dytor (later Mrs. Wildgoose), to help care for him [4]. Based on her professional medical experience, Miss Dytor formed the firm opinion that Marianne Foyster had just recently given birth to a child [5]. 
*   To add to the intrigue, Marianne's lover, Francois d'Arles (Frank Pearless), who was residing at the Rectory cottage at the time, was reportedly convinced that the baby was his own biological son [4]. 

Due to Marianne's lifelong habit of utilizing false names, lying on official documents, and passing off children to various men as her own, investigators suspect "Marjorie Emery of Kent" was an elaborate subterfuge designed to hide an illegitimate pregnancy from the Foysters' friends, relatives, and parishioners [6].

**The Fate of the Child**
Whatever the true identity of Marjorie Emery, her baby suffered a tragic fate. Only a few months after being brought to the Rectory, John Emery died on **September 20, 1932**, at the age of four-and-a-half months [1, 3]. His death certificate, reported by Marianne Foyster, listed the causes of death as convulsions and protein malnutrition (marasmus) [3]. He was buried in an unmarked grave in Borley churchyard, where locals later recalled seeing Francois d'Arles weeping at the graveside [2, 4, 7].
