# Marjorie Emery

Officially recorded as the natural mother of **John Evemond Emery** — the infant adopted by [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] in 1932 who died after 4.5 months — and the subject of a major contradiction in Borley research. Her very existence as a separate person is heavily questioned by investigators who strongly suspect that "Marjorie Emery" was a phantom identity created by [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] herself.

## The Birth Record

Marjorie Emery is described in official records as a **"spinster of no occupation"** with a home address in **Cobham, Kent** [2, 3]. She gave birth to John Evemond Emery on **25 April 1932** at 30 Swanage Road, Southend-on-Sea — the home of a midwife [3, 4]. The father's name was left blank on the birth certificate, indicating the baby was illegitimate [3]. The infant was subsequently handed to a London agency, through which he was reportedly adopted by Marianne Foyster and brought to Borley Rectory in May 1932 [4].

## The "M.E.R." Contradiction and the False-Identity Theory

When skeptical investigator [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]] traced John Emery's birth certificate in the 1950s, he found a striking detail: the mother's initials were recorded as **"M.E.R."** — which are the exact initials of **M**arianne **E**mily **R**ebecca Foyster [3]. This, combined with several other factors, led investigators to conclude that "Marjorie Emery" was almost certainly Marianne operating under yet another false name:

- Marianne had been absent from Borley Rectory in early spring 1932, allegedly "resting" from her experiences with the poltergeists [3]
- When the baby was brought to Borley in May, Marianne hired a state-registered nurse, **Miss Edith May Dytor** (later [[edith-wildgoose|Mrs Wildgoose]]), to help care for him; based on her professional medical experience, Miss Dytor formed the firm opinion that Marianne had recently given birth herself [4, 5]
- Marianne's lover, [[francois-darles|Francois d'Arles]] (Frank Pearless), residing at the Rectory cottage at the time, was reportedly convinced the baby was his biological son [4]

Due to [[marianne-foyster|Marianne's]] lifelong habit of utilising false names, lying on official documents, and passing off children to various men as her own, investigators conclude that "Marjorie Emery of Kent" was an elaborate subterfuge designed to conceal an illegitimate pregnancy [6].

## Clarke's Alternative Theory

In essay 14 ("Lawless, the Lodger"), [[andrew-clarke|Andrew Clarke]] presents a different speculative suggestion: that Marjorie Emery was a real person — a woman who had an affair with [[francois-darles|Frank Pearless]] — and that the baby was their child, subsequently adopted by the Foysters:

> *Frank seems to have convinced Ian that the child was actually his, and his show of grief when the child sadly died after a few months, suggests that he really believed it. In fact, an alternative suggestion, that the baby was the result of an affair between Frank and his mother, Marjorie Emery, and was subsequently adopted by the Foysters, seems much more likely.*
> — *Sidelights on Borley Rectory*, essay 14

The "his mother" in this sentence refers to Marjorie Emery as the baby's mother — i.e., Frank's partner in the affair, not his biological mother. No documentary evidence is cited for this reading.

## The Fate of the Child

Whatever the true identity of Marjorie Emery, her baby suffered a tragic fate. John Emery died on **20 September 1932**, at the age of four-and-a-half months [1, 3]. His death certificate, reported by [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]], listed the causes of death as convulsions and protein malnutrition (marasmus) [3]. He was buried in an unmarked grave in [[borley-church|Borley churchyard]], where locals later recalled seeing [[francois-darles|Francois d'Arles]] weeping at the graveside [2, 4, 7].

## Significance

If the "Marjorie Emery" theory is correct, she would be the biological mother of the baby who died at Borley Rectory in 1932. Clarke's alternative — that she was Frank Pearless's partner — would explain why Frank showed grief while Marianne denied biological maternity, and why the nurse was confused about an apparent recent childbirth. The central question — whether "Marjorie Emery" was Marianne in disguise or a separate woman — remains unresolved.
