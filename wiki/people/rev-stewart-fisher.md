# Rev. Stewart Travers Fisher

Rector of Liston from the death of his father [[rev-thomas-ruggles-fisher|Rev Thomas Ruggles Fisher]] in 1893 until the amalgamation of the Borley and Liston livings in 1936 — a tenure of about 43 years. He was the father of [[nancy-goodale|Nancy Goodale (née Fisher)]].

## The Fisher Dynasty at Liston

Together with his father Thomas Ruggles Fisher (incumbent 1857–1893), Stewart Travers Fisher completed an almost unbroken 79-year family hold on the Liston living. Liston and Borley are adjacent parishes, and the Fisher dynasty at Liston ran concurrently with the Bull dynasty at Borley (1862–1927).

## His Own Home

Unlike many clergymen of the period, Stewart Travers Fisher had his own modern house built at Liston rather than living in the old rectory. He and his family lived in this private home, which became known as "Highfield." Following the departure of Fisher's family, [[violet-oates|Violet Oates]] (sister of Captain Lawrence Oates of Antarctic fame) lived in it.

The old Liston rectory, by contrast, became the residence of the Rev Alfred Clifford Henning from 1936 and subsequently of the [[rev-lanfranc-mathias|Rev Lanfranc Mathias]] from 1955.

## His Connection to the Borley Story

As a clergyman who lived immediately adjacent to Borley, Stewart Fisher was well acquainted with the Bull family and with Harry Bull's views on the haunting. According to [[nancy-goodale|Nancy Goodale's]] account to [[claudine-mathias|CM]], the Rev Stewart Fisher and his wife had once spent the night at Borley Rectory as guests of Harry and Ivy Bull during a snowstorm. When Mrs Fisher walked out of the front door the next morning, she heard footsteps crunching in the snow behind her, assumed it was her husband, and turned — only to find no one there.

The London Gazette notice of 23 November 1928 records Stewart Fisher as the incumbent of Liston at the time of the Order in Council uniting the Borley and Liston benefices, which came into effect in 1936.

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Reverend Stewart Travers Fisher** was the Rector of the neighboring parish of Liston and a close acquaintance of the Bull family at Borley Rectory. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following biographical details, family connections, and specific events linking him to the Borley haunting:

**The "Fisher Dynasty" of Liston**
*   **Lineage and Succession:** Rev. Stewart Travers Fisher was the son of the **Rev. Thomas Ruggles Fisher** (who served as the incumbent of Liston from 1857 until his death in 1893) [1]. 
*   **Tenure:** Stewart succeeded his father and remained the Rector of Liston until **1936**, when the tiny parish was finally amalgamated with Borley [1]. Between them, father and son held the living continuously for nearly eighty years, establishing a local "Fisher dynasty" at Liston that ran concurrently with the famous Bull dynasty of Borley and Pentlow [1]. 
*   **Family:** He had a daughter named Nancy Goodale (née Fisher), who later provided investigators with details about her parents' experiences [1].

**Residences and the Amalgamation Scheme**
*   **The "New Rectory":** Having spent his boyhood living in the Old Rectory at Liston, Rev. Fisher decided he wanted to live in his own modern house [1]. He therefore had a new house built, which became known as the **"New Rectory"** (and still exists today under the name 'Highfield') [1]. 
*   **Clerical Complications:** His decision to build and occupy his own private home confused the local housing arrangements when the Ecclesiastical Commissioners drafted a scheme in **1928** to officially combine the Borley and Liston parishes [2]. While the scheme successfully noted that the Liston benefice was "now full the Reverend Stewart Travers Fisher being the present Incumbent thereof" (while Borley was vacant), Fisher's private residence meant that his successors (the Rev. A. C. Henning and Rev. L. Mathias) had to move back into the Old Rectory at Liston to satisfy the provisions of the ecclesiastical scheme [2, 3].

**Relationship with Harry Bull and Borley Rectory**
*   **Confidant to Harry Bull:** Rev. Fisher knew the Rev. Harry Bull of Borley very well [4]. Bull spoke to him frequently about his paranormal experiences at Borley Rectory, freely claiming to Fisher that he regularly saw the phantom of the nun and heard the mysterious ghostly coach [4].
*   **The Snowy Footsteps Incident (c. early-to-mid 1920s):** The Fishers had their own brush with the Borley phenomena when they spent an evening as guests of Harry and Ivy Bull at the Rectory [4]. Because it started to snow heavily, the Fishers decided to sleep at Borley and return to Liston the next day [4]. When it was time to leave in the morning, Mrs. Fisher walked out the front door and headed toward the main gate [4]. As she walked, she heard the distinct sound of footsteps crunching in the snow directly behind her [4]. Assuming it was her husband following her, she turned around, only to find no one there; her husband was presumably still inside having a parting conversation with their hosts [4].
