# Rev. Stewart Travers Fisher

Rector of Liston from the death of his father [[rev-thomas-ruggles-fisher|Rev Thomas Ruggles Fisher]] in 1893 until the amalgamation of the Borley and Liston livings in 1936 — a tenure of about 43 years. He was the father of [[nancy-goodale|Nancy Goodale (née Fisher)]].

## The Fisher Dynasty at Liston

Together with his father Thomas Ruggles Fisher (incumbent 1857–1893), Stewart Travers Fisher completed an almost unbroken 79-year family hold on the Liston living. Liston and Borley are adjacent parishes, and the Fisher dynasty at Liston ran concurrently with the Bull dynasty at Borley (1862–1927).

## His Own Home

Having spent his boyhood in the Old Rectory at Liston, Stewart Travers Fisher decided to live in his own modern house. He had a new house built at Liston rather than occupying the old rectory; this property became known as the "New Rectory," later as "Highfield." Following the departure of Fisher's family, [[violet-oates|Violet Oates]] (sister of Captain Lawrence Oates of Antarctic fame) lived in it.

His decision to build and occupy a private home complicated local housing arrangements when the Ecclesiastical Commissioners drafted a scheme in **1928** to officially combine the Borley and Liston parishes. The London Gazette notice of 23 November 1928 records Fisher as the incumbent of Liston at the time of the Order in Council uniting the Borley and Liston benefices, which came into effect in 1936. Because Fisher's successors could not occupy his private house, they had to move back into the old Liston Rectory: first [[rev-ac-henning|Rev. A. C. Henning]] from 1936, then [[rev-lanfranc-mathias|Rev Lanfranc Mathias]] from 1955.

## His Connection to the Borley Story

As a clergyman who lived immediately adjacent to Borley, Stewart Fisher was well acquainted with the Bull family. He knew [[harry-bull|Rev. Harry Bull]] very well — Bull spoke to him frequently about his paranormal experiences at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]], freely claiming to Fisher that he regularly saw the phantom of the nun and heard the mysterious ghostly coach.

The Fishers had their own brush with Borley phenomena. They once spent the night at the Rectory as guests of Harry and [[ivy-brackenbury|Ivy Bull]] during a snowstorm. The next morning, Mrs Fisher walked out of the front door toward the main gate and heard the distinct sound of footsteps crunching in the snow directly behind her. Assuming it was her husband, she turned — only to find no one there. According to [[nancy-goodale|Nancy Goodale's]] later account to [[claudine-mathias|CM]], her husband was presumably still inside having a parting conversation with their hosts.
