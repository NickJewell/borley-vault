# Rev. H. S. Cheales

Rector of Wyck Rissington, Gloucestershire (The Rectory, Wyck Rissington). Cheales wrote to Peter Underwood describing experiences at his own church strikingly similar to those reported at [[borley-church|Borley Church]]:

> "...on separate occasions my wife and I have both heard singing from inside our beautiful old church here (similar to the Borley Church experiences) when we have been in the churchyard. It seems to be of a medieval character; plainsong chanting. I suppose it is a relic of worship hundreds of years old which still lingers on..."

His letter is cited by Underwood and Tabori as one of several instances of unexplained chanting reported from locked churches in England, supporting their view that [[borley-church|Borley Church]] was not unique in this respect but was the best-documented example of a wider phenomenon.
