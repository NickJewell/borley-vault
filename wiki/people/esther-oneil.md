# Esther O'Neil

Mother of [[robert-oneil|Robert Vincent O'Neil]]; lived with her own mother (O'Neil's grandmother) on the family farm outside [[hokah-minnesota|Hokah, Minnesota]], on Highway 44 in the Mississippi bluff country.

## The Hokah Farm

When [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] arrived in America after August 1946, her first home was the O'Neil family farm. Robert Wood describes the farm as having "never made more than the barest living." Esther and her mother shared the property with Robert and the newly arrived Marianne (and baby [[robert-oneil|Vincent]]).

Wood evokes the atmosphere for GI brides: "Marianne perhaps suffered the disillusionment of many GI brides when their expansive, wealthy, and victorious husbands took them home." The Hokah farm in particular represented a sharp contrast with the affluent American life that wartime propaganda had suggested.

Marianne and O'Neil soon moved from the farm into a small wooden house in Hokah itself, suggesting the extended-family arrangement at the farm did not last long.

## Significance

Esther O'Neil represents the American reality that Marianne entered: a working-class farming family with few resources, far removed from the comfortable life she had imagined. Her presence underscores the contrast between Marianne's elaborate English fantasies and the actual circumstances of her American life.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Esther O'Neil** has a very brief but specific presence in the Borley Rectory sources. She was the mother of Robert Vincent O'Neil, the young American GI who became Marianne Foyster's fourth husband [1, 2]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the sources reveals the following biographical details, events, and a notable geographical contradiction regarding her:

**Biographical Details and Residence**
Esther was a widow; her husband had died years earlier when her son Robert was eleven years old [2, 3]. She lived on the O'Neil family farm with her mother (Robert's grandmother) and other members of their extended family [2, 3]. Their home is described as a large, welcoming house, though the living conditions were basic, lacking electricity and indoor plumbing [3]. 

**Geographical Contradiction**
The sources record a specific contradiction regarding where Esther lived:
*   One passage explicitly states that she lived in **Wisconsin** [1].
*   However, other passages state that the O'Neil family farm where Esther lived was located in the Mississippi bluff country of **Minnesota**, deep in the hills a few miles outside the small town of Hokah on Highway 44 [2, 3]. 

**Involvement with Marianne Foyster (1945)**
Esther's only recorded active role in the narrative occurred shortly after the end of the Second World War. When Marianne married Robert O'Neil in England in August 1945, she secretly adopted a baby boy (whom she named Robert Vincent) and passed him off as her new husband's child [1]. To help establish this deception, Marianne took a photograph of "Junior" and sent it to Esther in America [1]. Believing the child was her grandson, Esther wrote back to Marianne and **advised her on the details of her entry into America** [1]. Marianne and the baby subsequently travelled to the United States and briefly lived with Esther and her family on the farm before moving into their own home [2, 3].
