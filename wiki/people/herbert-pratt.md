# Herbert Pratt

Honorary Librarian of the **Magic Circle**; employed by [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]] as an effective private detective to locate [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] in Suffolk in the early 1950s.

## Role

Hall needed to track down Marianne as part of his investigation for the [[the-borley-report|Borley Report]]. He engaged Pratt — who had cataloguing and research skills as well as access to Hall's Magic Circle connections — to travel from London to Suffolk and find her last known address.

Pratt made meticulous notes of his travel costs (train fares, bus fares, lunches) as he worked his way through the area. He traced Marianne's last known address to **Dairy Cottages, Rendlesham**, then interviewed former neighbours and local contacts, eventually reaching **Letty Knights** ("Kantara," Kingston Road, Woodbridge), who had adopted the boy John and had extensive knowledge of Marianne's activities.

Pratt admitted to Hall that he was "about twice as interested in Marianne as I am in Borley Rectory" — a comment that hints at the obsessive quality of the Hall/Pratt investigation.

## Significance

Pratt's investigative work for Hall was effective if ethically questionable. His discoveries at Rendlesham and Woodbridge gave Hall the material he needed to construct the detailed biography of Marianne that eventually became the five-volume *Marianne Foyster of Borley Rectory* — a document so sensational and legally dangerous that it was not published in Hall's lifetime.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Herbert E. Pratt** was a researcher and investigator who played a significant, behind-the-scenes role in the compilation of the highly skeptical 1956 *Borley Report* (*The Haunting of Borley Rectory*). 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following biographical details, research contributions, specific quotes, and his private detective work regarding Marianne Foyster:

**Biographical Details and Qualifications**
*   **Professional Background:** Pratt was a **Fellow of the Faculty of Surveyors of England** [1]. 
*   **Magical Connections:** He served as the **Honorary Librarian of the Magic Circle** [2]. 

**Contributions to the *Borley Report***
The authors of the *Borley Report* (Dingwall, Goldney, and Hall) officially thanked Pratt in their preface, noting that he had undertaken an **"immense amount of patient research into certain aspects of the Foyster case"** [3]. Additionally, Pratt was the person who **prepared the index** for their exhaustive report [3].

**The "Flying Keys" Air Pressure Theory**
During the Borley investigations, witnesses (including the Rev. G. Eric Smith) reported the supposedly paranormal phenomenon of keys shooting out of their locks. Pratt provided a specific natural explanation for this. Drawing on his own experience, he submitted an account to the S.P.R. investigators of a case he had personally observed where **keys in two doors were propelled outwards under conditions where air pressure was clearly responsible**, suggesting this normal mechanism caused the Borley key incidents [1]. 

**The Search for Marianne Foyster and a Specific Quote**
During the 1950s investigation, Trevor Hall was determined to track down Marianne Foyster to establish if she had psychic powers or had faked the phenomena. Hall employed Pratt to assist him, effectively utilizing him as a **"private detective"** [2]. 

The sources note that both Hall and Pratt became fascinated, and even obsessed, with Marianne as a *femme fatale* [2]. Demonstrating his intense focus on her character over the haunting itself, Pratt provided the following direct quote to Trevor Hall: 
> **"Quite frankly, I am about twice as interested in Marianne as I am in Borley Rectory"** [2].

**The Suffolk Tracking Expedition**
Pratt conducted physical fieldwork to locate Marianne. He travelled from Liverpool Street to Suffolk armed with a map, meticulously **making precise notes of his bus and train fares, as well as his lunches** [4]. 

His tracking led him to Marianne's last known address at Dairy Cottages in Rendlesham, Suffolk. Pratt was struck by how remote and rudimentary the former estate workers' cottage was, specifically noting that it had **no running water and was served by a single outside lavatory** [4]. While there, Pratt interviewed Marianne's former neighbor, who provided a valuable lead, advising Pratt to seek out a Mrs. Knights at a house called ‘Kantara’ on Kingston Road in Woodbridge [4].
