# Herbert Pratt

Fellow of the Faculty of Surveyors of England and Honorary Librarian of the **Magic Circle** [1, 2]; employed by [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]] as an effective private detective to locate [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] in Suffolk in the early 1950s.

## Contributions to the *Borley Report*

The authors of the *Borley Report* (Dingwall, Goldney, and Hall) officially thanked Pratt in their preface, noting that he had undertaken an **"immense amount of patient research into certain aspects of the Foyster case"** [3]. He also **prepared the index** for their exhaustive report [3].

During the Borley investigations, witnesses including [[rev-eric-smith|Rev. G. Eric Smith]] had reported the supposedly paranormal phenomenon of keys shooting out of their locks. Pratt provided a specific natural explanation: drawing on his own experience, he submitted an account of a case he had personally observed where **keys in two doors were propelled outwards under conditions where air pressure was clearly responsible** [1], suggesting this normal mechanism caused the Borley key incidents.

## The Search for Marianne Foyster

Hall needed to track down Marianne as part of his investigation for the [[the-borley-report|Borley Report]] and engaged Pratt — who had cataloguing and research skills as well as access to Hall's Magic Circle connections — to travel from London to Suffolk and find her last known address.

Both Hall and Pratt became fascinated, even obsessed, with Marianne as a *femme fatale* [2]. Demonstrating his intense focus on her character over the haunting itself, Pratt provided the following quote to Hall:

> **"Quite frankly, I am about twice as interested in Marianne as I am in Borley Rectory."** [2]

Pratt conducted physical fieldwork, travelling from Liverpool Street to Suffolk armed with a map and **meticulously making precise notes of his bus and train fares, as well as his lunches** [4].

His tracking led him to Marianne's last known address at [[dairy-cottage-rendlesham|Dairy Cottages, Rendlesham]], Suffolk. Pratt was struck by how remote and rudimentary the former estate workers' cottage was, noting it had **no running water and was served by a single outside lavatory** [4]. While there, Pratt interviewed Marianne's former neighbour, who advised him to seek out a Mrs. Knights at a house called 'Kantara' on Kingston Road in Woodbridge. He duly traced Marianne's last known address there and reached **[[letty-knights|Letty Knights]]**, who had adopted the boy John and had extensive knowledge of Marianne's activities.

## Significance

Pratt's investigative work for Hall was effective if ethically questionable. His discoveries at Rendlesham and Woodbridge gave Hall the material he needed to construct the detailed biography of Marianne that eventually became the five-volume *Marianne Foyster of Borley Rectory* — a document so sensational and legally dangerous that it was not published in Hall's lifetime.
