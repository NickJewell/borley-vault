# Eric Liberge

French author of the graphic novel *Tonnerre Rampant*, cited by [[andrew-clarke]] as giving the Marie Lairre narrative its "ultimate form."

## Reference

Clarke mentions Liberge in essay 15 ("The Well Tank Bothers Me") in the context of describing how the powerful Marie Lairre story developed from [[dr-w-j-phythian-adams]]'s Christmas 1943 analysis:

> *The story is a wonderful one, given its ultimate form in the recent book by Eric Liberge, 'Tonnerre Rampant'.*
> — *Sidelights on Borley Rectory*, essay 15

The narrative as Clarke describes it: Marie Lairre, an unquiet nun's spirit, haunts the place desperate for Christian burial, uses every available psychic means to direct the living to her bones, and is finally brought to rest when Harry Price arranges the burial and a Requiem Mass.

## Significance

Liberge is mentioned only in passing, but his existence confirms that the Borley story had reached French popular culture in graphic novel form. Clarke's somewhat ironic framing — noting the story "owes something to Oscar Wilde's *The Canterville Ghost*" — suggests he sees the Liberge version as the apotheosis of a romantic narrative that diverges substantially from the documented historical record.

No publication date is given, but Clarke describes it as "recent" relative to his writing of the Sidelights (mid-2000s).
