# Eric Liberge

French author of the graphic novel *Tonnerre Rampant*, cited by [[andrew-clarke]] as giving the [[marie-lairre|Marie Lairre]] narrative its "ultimate form."

## Reference

Clarke mentions Liberge in essay 15 ("The Well Tank Bothers Me") in the context of describing how the powerful Marie Lairre story developed from [[canon-phythian-adams|Canon Phythian-Adams]]'s Christmas 1943 analysis:

> *The story is a wonderful one, given its ultimate form in the recent book by Eric Liberge, 'Tonnerre Rampant'.*
> — *Sidelights on Borley Rectory*, essay 15

The narrative as Clarke describes it: Marie Lairre, an unquiet nun's spirit, haunts the place desperate for Christian burial, uses every available psychic means to direct the living to her bones, and is finally brought to rest when [[harry-price|Harry Price]] arranges the burial and a Requiem Mass. Liberge's full summary of the story, according to the sources:

> **"'Marie Lairre, the unquiet spirit of a Nun, haunts the place, desperate to communicate to the living the need for a Christian burial on consecrated ground. She uses every trick her dwindling psychic energy can muster to alert the living to the location of her bones and the need for a burial and Requiem Mass. The communications are misconstrued and poorly understood but, finally, through the brilliant intervention of a theologian, Harry Price locates the bones, arranges the burial, and peace descends on the area.'"** [1]

## Significance

Liberge is mentioned only in passing, but his existence confirms that the Borley story had reached French popular culture in graphic novel form. Clarke's somewhat ironic framing — noting the story "owes something to Oscar Wilde's *The Canterville Ghost*" — suggests he sees the Liberge version as the apotheosis of a romantic narrative that diverges substantially from the documented historical record.

No publication date is given, but Clarke describes it as "recent" relative to his writing of the Sidelights (mid-2000s). No biographical details, dates, or personal events regarding Liberge appear in the Borley sources beyond his authorship of *Tonnerre Rampant* [1].
