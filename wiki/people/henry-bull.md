# Reverend Henry Dawson Ellis Bull MA (d. 1892)

First rector of Borley; builder of [[borley-rectory]]. Father of thirteen baptized children (including [[harry-bull]] and [[ethel-bull]]); not fourteen as commonly stated.

## Life at Borley

Inducted to the living in 1862 while living at Borley Place. Immediately began building the Rectory, an L-shaped building that expanded over thirty years into an "irregular, sprawling conglomeration." He and his wife knew the legend of [[the-nun]] and reportedly built the large garden pavilion partly for the purpose of watching for her appearances. Under his watch the Nun's Walk acquired its name.

## Baptismal Records

[[Ivan Banks]] found 13 baptisms recorded at Borley (from the Rev. Christopher Cook's check of parish registers), not 14 as commonly stated. Full list with baptism dates:

1. Henry Foyster Bull — March 25, 1863
2. Caroline Sarah Elizabeth Bull (Dodie) — April 17, 1864
3. Winifred Margaret Bull — March 17, 1865
4. Alfred Richard Graham Bull — February 23, 1866
5. Basil Walter Bull — January 11, 1867
6. Ethel Mary Bull — January 20, 1868
7. Adelaide Mabel Bull — Christmas Day, 1868
8. Edward Gerald Bull — December 18, 1870
9. Constance StClere Bull — January 14, 1872
10. Hubert Ellis Bull — January 12, 1873
11. Emily Poundrow Bull — February 13, 1876
12. Cyril Garwood Bull — January 21, 1877 (died April 9, 1887, aged 16 weeks)
13. Alice Kathleen Bull — January 26, 1879

"Freda" does not appear in the register and may have been a nickname. Banks notes Henry Foyster Bull's baptism record lists his birthplace as Guestingthorpe in the 1871 and 1881 census, though he is usually said to have been born at Borley Place.

## Death

Died at Borley Rectory, May 2, 1892, aged 59. Buried in Borley churchyard. Succeeded by his son [[harry-bull]] after thirty years' incumbency.

## Cause of Death — Syphilis

[[Ivan Banks]] obtained Henry Dawson Bull's death certificate and found the cause of death recorded as **Locomotor Ataxia**, which medical dictionaries define as "a manifestation of tertiary syphilis" and "a degenerative disease of the spinal cord… allied to general paralysis of the insane." Banks takes this as documentary confirmation of longstanding village gossip that Henry indulged in affairs with village girls. He died at only 59, relatively young even by Victorian standards. No treatment for tertiary syphilis existed in 1892. See [[kate-boreham]] for Banks's theory about an affair and its consequences.

## The Katie Boreham Connection

Banks argues that [[kate-boreham|Kate Boreham]] was employed as a maid at the Rectory and became pregnant by Henry. Kate's first child was baptized at Borley on June 4, 1876 — the same year as her marriage to Walter Boreham on February 19, 1876 — suggesting possible premarital conception. A half-empty bottle of "sugar of lead" (lead acetate, usable as an abortifacient) was reportedly found in a Rectory cupboard after his death. Banks believes Kate died in 1888 as a result of trying to terminate a second pregnancy by Henry, and that her removal in the Bull family carriage (driven by coachman [[joseph-tyler|Joseph Tyler]]) accounts for the [[phantom-coach]] phenomenon.

## Séance Identity

At table-tipping sessions in October 1937, an entity identified itself as Henry Dawson Bull after giving the nickname "**Carlos**" (Q.24–25; confirmed). The same séance confirmed that the entity was distressed about something that happened in the kitchen involving a servant girl and a baby (Q.26–32: "Did a servant girl die in your presence? Yes. Did she die a natural death? No. Was there a baby? Yes").

## Paranormal Experiences

Several reported phenomena during his incumbency (accounts gathered much later, from his daughter [[ethel-bull]] and others):
- All twenty bells pealing simultaneously, without explanation
- One daughter slapped by an invisible presence
- [[ethel-bull]] saw a dark figure of an old man in a tall hat beside her bed
- His wife reportedly saw [[the-nun]] on at least one occasion
