# Sauer (nephew of Hokah barber)

A resident of [[hokah-minnesota|Hokah, Minnesota]], identified only by surname. He resided in or near the small town of Hokah and is specifically described as being the **"nephew of the Hokah barber"** [1]. Shortly after [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] and [[robert-oneil|Robert O'Neil]] moved into a small wooden house in Hokah, they purchased a car [1]. Sauer was driving this vehicle when it was involved in an accident and completely **destroyed on Highway 44** [1, 2]. Robert O'Neil was riding as a passenger and was **"quite badly hurt, suffering a fractured jaw"** [1].

## The Incident and Marianne's Response

The accident involving Sauer later became the subject of one of [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]]'s many documented falsehoods. Following the crash, Marianne wrote a letter to her friend [[mr-and-mrs-fenton|Mrs. Fenton]] back in Wimbledon, England [1]. In the letter, she **"pathetically"** recounted a fabricated story claiming that *she* (rather than her husband) had been the victim of the auto smash and had suffered the fractured jaw [1]. She had not even been in the car.

Robert Wood, author of *The Widow of Borley*, uses this incident as a prime example of Marianne's compulsive need to romance and tell pointless lies: **"This pointless piece of romancing is interesting because it demonstrates how Marianne simply could not tell the truth. Most of her lies were fantasy, not falsehoods told to manipulate others."**

## Significance

Sauer is a marginal figure — he matters only as the occasion for this revealing anecdote. The incident occurs shortly after Marianne's arrival in America and demonstrates that even in her new country, with her most dangerous deceptions behind her, she continued to fabricate for no strategic purpose.
