# Sauer (nephew of Hokah barber)

A resident of [[hokah-minnesota|Hokah, Minnesota]], identified only by surname, who was driving a car in which [[robert-oneil|Robert O'Neil]] was a passenger when it was involved in an accident on **Highway 44**. O'Neil suffered a fractured jaw.

## The Incident and Marianne's Response

Wood uses this minor accident to illustrate [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]]'s compulsive inability to tell the truth even in trivial matters. After the crash:

> "Marianne wrote to her friend in Wimbledon, Mrs. Fenton, pathetically recounting how she (not O'Neil) had been in an auto smash and of how her jaw had been fractured."

She had not been in the car. The lie gained her nothing; it was simply Marianne's default mode. As Wood observes: "This pointless piece of romancing is interesting because it demonstrates how Marianne simply could not tell the truth. Most of her lies were fantasy, not falsehoods told to manipulate others."

## Significance

Sauer is a marginal figure — he matters only as the occasion for this revealing anecdote. The incident occurs shortly after Marianne's arrival in America and demonstrates that even in her new country, with her most dangerous deceptions behind her, she continued to fabricate for no strategic purpose.
