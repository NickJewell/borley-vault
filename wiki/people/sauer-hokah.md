# Sauer (nephew of Hokah barber)

A resident of [[hokah-minnesota|Hokah, Minnesota]], identified only by surname, who was driving a car in which [[robert-oneil|Robert O'Neil]] was a passenger when it was involved in an accident on **Highway 44**. O'Neil suffered a fractured jaw.

## The Incident and Marianne's Response

Wood uses this minor accident to illustrate [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]]'s compulsive inability to tell the truth even in trivial matters. After the crash:

> "Marianne wrote to her friend in Wimbledon, Mrs. Fenton, pathetically recounting how she (not O'Neil) had been in an auto smash and of how her jaw had been fractured."

She had not been in the car. The lie gained her nothing; it was simply Marianne's default mode. As Wood observes: "This pointless piece of romancing is interesting because it demonstrates how Marianne simply could not tell the truth. Most of her lies were fantasy, not falsehoods told to manipulate others."

## Significance

Sauer is a marginal figure — he matters only as the occasion for this revealing anecdote. The incident occurs shortly after Marianne's arrival in America and demonstrates that even in her new country, with her most dangerous deceptions behind her, she continued to fabricate for no strategic purpose.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Sauer** appears very briefly in the Borley Rectory sources as a minor figure in the post-Borley life of Marianne Foyster (then living as Marianne O'Neil) and her fourth husband, the American GI Robert Vincent O'Neil. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific biographical details, events, and related contradictions regarding his involvement:

**Biographical Details**
*   **Identity:** The texts identify him only by his surname, **Sauer** [1]. 
*   **Location and Relation:** He resided in or near the small town of **Hokah, Minnesota**—the town where the O'Neils moved after initially living on the rural O'Neil family farm—and is specifically described as being the **"nephew of the Hokah barber"** [1].

**The Highway 44 Car Accident**
Sauer's sole appearance in the Borley literature is his role as the driver in a severe automobile accident involving Robert O'Neil. 
*   **The Crash:** Shortly after Marianne and Robert O'Neil moved into a small wooden house in Hokah, they purchased a car [1]. Sauer was driving this vehicle when it was involved in an accident and completely **destroyed on Highway 44** [1, 2]. 
*   **Injuries:** Robert O'Neil was riding as a passenger in the vehicle at the time of the crash. He was **"quite badly hurt, suffering a fractured jaw"** [1]. 

**Marianne's Fabrication Regarding the Accident**
The accident involving Sauer later became the subject of one of Marianne Foyster's many documented falsehoods. 
*   Following the crash, Marianne wrote a letter to her friend Mrs. Fenton back in Wimbledon, England [1]. 
*   In the letter, she **"pathetically"** recounted a fabricated story claiming that *she* (rather than her husband) had been the victim of the auto smash and had suffered the fractured jaw [1]. 
*   The author of *The Widow of Borley* uses this incident as a prime example of Marianne's compulsive need to romance and tell pointless lies, noting that **"she had not even been in the car when it crashed"** [1].
