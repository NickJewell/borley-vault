# R. F. Buckley

Author of a typescript essay titled "No Ghosts at Borley?" in which he examined statements made about [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s health during the Foyster incumbency. Buckley pointed out that witness statements about her physical condition were "highly conflicting" — with some saying she suffered from ill health or fainting fits while others described her as "in excellent health," "extremely vital and healthy," and with "superb" health.

Underwood and Tabori cited his essay as a useful source for this evidential contradiction without endorsing his overall sceptical conclusion. No further biographical details about Buckley appear in *Ghosts of Borley*.
