# R. F. Buckley

Author of a typescript essay titled "No Ghosts at Borley?" in which he examined statements made about [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s health during the Foyster incumbency. Buckley pointed out that witness statements about her physical condition were "highly conflicting" — with some saying she suffered from ill health or fainting fits while others described her as "in excellent health," "extremely vital and healthy," and with "superb" health.

Underwood and Tabori cited his essay as a useful source for this evidential contradiction without endorsing his overall sceptical conclusion. No further biographical details about Buckley appear in *Ghosts of Borley*.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**R. F. Buckley** features in the Borley Rectory literature as a staunch defender of Harry Price and a vocal critic of the Society for Psychical Research (SPR) following the publication of their highly critical *Borley Report* (*The Haunting of Borley Rectory* or *HBR*) [1, 2].

**Biographical Details**
*   **Early Life and Career:** Buckley was **born in Essex in 1905** and resided there until 1938 [2]. In **March 1939**, he took up an appointment in Sudbury, living at Great Cornard [2]. 
*   **Later Life:** He subsequently relocated to Newport on the Isle of Wight, where he eventually died [2]. 
*   **Acquaintances:** He was known personally to Peter Underwood, a prominent Borley investigator, who eventually came into possession of Buckley's original unpublished typescript [3].

**Role in the Borley Controversy**
Buckley had no direct connection to Harry Price or the original Borley investigations, and the texts note he had only a "passing interest" in the hauntings [2]. However, soon after the SPR authors (Dingwall, Goldney, and Hall) published their posthumous attack on Price's integrity, Buckley felt compelled to respond. He authored an **unpublished essay titled *No Ghosts at Borley?*** to refute their claims [2]. 

His primary motivation was to defend a man who was no longer alive to defend himself [4]. In his essay, Buckley compiled an annotated list challenging many of the SPR investigators' conclusions. He specifically defended Price against accusations that he faked auditory phenomena and the famous wall writings, challenged the claims of fraud concerning the excavated cellar bones, and addressed the SPR's reporting of a cat incident during an observation vigil on October 16–17, 1937 [3].

**Specific Quotes and Critiques**
Buckley’s defense of Price was marked by remarkably forceful language directed at the SPR authors. A thorough reference profile includes the following direct quotes from his essay:
*   He wrote that the *Borley Report* was **‘so biased and prejudiced against a man so recently dead that it offended my sense of fair play and decency’** [4].
*   He described the SPR's work as a **‘vicious and unmerited attack’** on Price's character [4].
*   Buckley stated his goal was to demonstrate **‘that suppression and distortion of known facts can be found in HBR quite as much as the authors allege to exist in MHH [The Most Haunted House in England] and EBR [The End of Borley Rectory]’** [4].
*   In his concluding remarks, he fiercely condemned the SPR Report by stating it was **‘inspired by “envy, hatred and malice”’** and that it **‘seethes with spleen like a suppurating ulcer’** [3].
