# R. F. Buckley

Born in Essex in 1905, Buckley resided there until 1938, when in March 1939 he took up an appointment in Sudbury, living at Great Cornard — three miles from Borley. He subsequently relocated to Newport on the Isle of Wight, where he eventually died. He was known personally to [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]], who came into possession of Buckley's original unpublished typescript.

## Role in the Borley Controversy

Buckley had no direct connection to [[harry-price|Harry Price]] or the original Borley investigations and had only a "passing interest" in the hauntings. However, following the publication of the SPR's *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (the *Borley Report*) in January 1956 — a posthumous attack on Price's integrity by [[eric-dingwall|Eric Dingwall]], [[kathleen-goldney|Kathleen Goldney]], and [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]] — Buckley felt compelled to respond in defence of a man no longer able to defend himself.

He authored an **unpublished essay titled *No Ghosts at Borley?*** in which he examined statements made about [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s health during the Foyster incumbency, pointing out that witness statements about her physical condition were "highly conflicting" — with some saying she suffered from ill health or fainting fits while others described her as "in excellent health," "extremely vital and healthy," and with "superb" health. He also compiled an annotated list challenging many of the SPR investigators' other conclusions, defending Price against accusations that he faked auditory phenomena and the famous [[wall-writings|wall writings]], challenging the claims of fraud concerning the excavated cellar bones, and addressing the SPR's reporting of a cat incident during an observation vigil on October 16–17, 1937.

## Specific Quotes

Buckley's defence of Price was marked by forceful language directed at the SPR authors:

- He wrote that the *Borley Report* was **'so biased and prejudiced against a man so recently dead that it offended my sense of fair play and decency'** [4].
- He described the SPR's work as a **'vicious and unmerited attack'** on Price's character [4].
- His goal, he stated, was to demonstrate **'that suppression and distortion of known facts can be found in HBR quite as much as the authors allege to exist in MHH and EBR'** [4].
- In his concluding remarks, he condemned the SPR Report as being **'inspired by "envy, hatred and malice"'** and declared that it **'seethes with spleen like a suppurating ulcer'** [3].

[[peter-underwood|Underwood]] and Tabori cited his essay as a useful source for evidential contradictions around Marianne's health without endorsing his overall sceptical conclusion.
