# Ian Geoffrey William Shaw (Greenwood) (19 April 1915 – 1986)

Born Larne, Northern Ireland; died Aylesbury, Buckinghamshire, 1986. [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster's]] natural son by her first marriage. His bitterness towards his mother became a major source of material for [[trevor-hall]]'s investigation of the Foyster incumbency — and hence for the [[the-borley-report|Borley Report]].

## Origins

Marianne married Harold Gifford Greenwood at Sheriff's warrant (Scotland) 8 June 1914, and again at St Anne's Cathedral, Belfast, 12 November 1914, when she was 15 (giving her age as 17). Ian was born 19 April 1915. Six weeks after the birth, Greenwood deserted the family under pressure from his mother. Ian was raised by Marianne's parents William and Annie Shaw, and used the Shaw surname for the rest of his life. Marianne sailed for Canada in 1922 to marry [[lionel-foyster]]; Ian stayed behind.

Ian joined the Foysters in Canada in 1925 for seven years of schooling. He rejoined Marianne at [[borley-rectory]] in late 1932 for a three-month stay. He then moved to Wimbledon with Marianne and [[francois-darles|d'Arles]], living above the flower shop (see [[jonquille-et-cie]]). After the shop failed he returned to Northern Ireland, married twice.

## Role in the Borley Controversy

In 1956, shortly after the SPR published the Borley Report, Ian made contact with [[trevor-hall]] and offered information about his mother. He had "clearly hated his mother for 'abandoning him as a baby'" (the Companion's assessment) and used this opportunity to attack her. He met Hall at the Waldorf Hotel in London and the information flowed freely.

Ian told Hall:
- Details of Marianne's relationships in Canada and at Borley (including her affair with d'Arles)
- That he had himself played tricks on d'Arles at Borley — including creating a makeshift phantom from a bed sheet and rigging bell wires to ring
- That Marianne and d'Arles had faked phenomena, using the visit of the [[marks-tey-circle|Marks Tey Spiritualist Circle]] to bring the "haunting" to a convenient end
- His suspicion that d'Arles was the father of the adopted baby [[john-emery|John Emery]] who died at Borley

Hall used Ian's material to locate Marianne in America, ultimately leading to the [[robert-swanson|Swanson interviews]] and Hall's unpublished *Marianne Foyster of Borley Rectory* manuscript.

> **Caveat:** "A great deal of Shaw's recollections (much of which are uncorroborated) may have been coloured by his personal feelings for his mother whom he clearly resented; also Hall's reporting of these recollections are similarly coloured by his apathy towards the paranormal and his desire to totally debunk the Borley haunting." (The Companion)

## Irony

Ian's widow Margaret was still under the impression when he died in 1986 that William and Annie Shaw were Ian's parents. He had kept the whole secret — including his connection to Borley Rectory and his role in supplying the SPR's investigators — from his own family.

He warned Marianne in a letter (May 1956) that Hall intended to contact her. Her reply was poignant: "I am grateful to you beyond belief for your kindness to me and I am not a bad woman. I have been a driven woman."
