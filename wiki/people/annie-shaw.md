# Annie Elizabeth Shaw (née Woodyatt)

Mother of [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] (born Marianne Emily Rebecca Shaw); wife of [[william-steele-shaw|William Steele Shaw]]. Her role in concealing her daughter's first marriage from [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] was a pivotal act in the Borley story.

## Family and Background

Annie married William Steele Shaw and had two children:
- **[[geoffrey-shaw|Geoffrey Shaw]]** (born 1897)
- **Marianne Emily Rebecca Shaw** (born 26 January 1899)

At the time of Marianne's birth, the family lived in near poverty in a small terrace house at 5 Guy Wood Cottages in Romiley, near Stockport, Cheshire [2, 3]. To help make ends meet, Annie gave piano lessons while William gave private tutoring in shorthand, arithmetic, and bookkeeping [3, 4]. The family moved frequently during Marianne's early years, relocating to Coroner's Lane in Widnes to live with Annie's widowed mother Emily, before eventually settling in Drumnadreagh, County Antrim, Northern Ireland in 1907 [5–7].

## Complicity in Marianne's 1914 Marriage

On **November 12, 1914**, Annie and her son Geoffrey acted as the official witnesses at St. Anne's Cathedral, Belfast, when the fifteen-year-old Marianne married Harold Giffard [[harold-greenwood|Greenwood]] [8, 9]. The marriage certificate was falsified, listing Marianne's age as seventeen and William's occupation as "schoolmaster" [9]. Borley critic Robert Wood suggested that this complicity demonstrated a "pathetic dishonesty" as Annie and her husband desperately tried to cling to the outward appearances of gentility, which may have reinforced their daughter's lifelong tendency to fantasise and lie [9].

## Raising Ian Shaw and the Family Deception

After Greenwood abandoned Marianne shortly following the birth of their son, [[ian-greenwood|Ian]], in April 1915, Annie and William took over raising the child [10, 11]. Marianne later claimed during a 1958 interview that she was distressed by this arrangement, complaining that her mother "took over the baby and wouldn't let her care for him" [11].

To protect the family's image, Annie and William maintained a strict pretence that Ian was their own son, passing him off to the world as Marianne's younger brother [10]. This deception was remarkably successful and maintained for decades; when Ian died in 1986, his own widow Margaret was still under the impression that Annie and William Shaw were his biological parents [12].

## Shielding Lionel Foyster

When the Rev. Lionel Foyster — who had known the family since his days as a curate in Cheshire and would later become Marianne's second husband — visited the Shaws in Northern Ireland, Annie and her husband actively protected Marianne's reputation [13]. According to Ian, Annie and William were careful to accommodate Foyster well outside of Larne to ensure that none of the local gossip and scandal regarding Marianne's numerous affairs reached his ears [13].

Robert Wood considers this family-level deception to be foundational to Marianne's pathological dishonesty: "If Marianne's parents not only colluded with her in deceiving Foyster but actively encouraged her, it is not unreasonable to conclude that they had encouraged her to deceive in other situations."
