# J. H. Russell

Undergraduate at Trinity College, Oxford. Visited the Borley ruins alongside [[harry-marshall-oxford|Harry Marshall]] from January 3–6, 1944, submitting an independent written report to [[harry-price|Harry Price]].

## Phantom Voice Incident (January 5, 1944, 5:25 p.m.)

Russell was walking in the orchard, near the stream at the north-east end of the garden — in the vicinity of the [[nuns-walk|Nun's Walk]] — when he heard what he was certain was Marshall's voice calling him. He responded. Marshall had not spoken.

From Russell's own report:

> *"Russell was certain I called him. I did not. At the time (5:25 p.m.) he claimed to have recognized my voice. When he knew I had not called him he naturally began to doubt whether he really had heard a voice."*

Russell's independent account confirms the incident and specifies his position: by the stream near the Nun's Walk.

Price notes that the tradition connecting the Nun's Walk stream with the legend (one variant holds the nun committed suicide by throwing herself into the stream) makes it the most psychically "active" area of the garden, and that voices have been reported there and on the adjacent lane by multiple witnesses over decades.

## Other Observations

Russell also provides an independent confirmation of the cellar crash on Tuesday morning (January 4) — noting that neither observer was directly above the objects involved, and that a stone of sufficient size to produce such a noise would not have been inadvertently kicked.

Russell's reporting is notably measured: he explicitly says his certainty that he heard Marshall's voice weakened once he knew Marshall had not spoken — a candid acknowledgement of the psychological difficulty of evaluating auditory phenomena in a "haunted" location.
