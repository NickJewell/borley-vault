# Edith Shaw (Mrs Johnny Fisher's sister)

Sister of **[[henry-fisher|Johnny Fisher]]** ([[henry-fisher|Henry Francis Fisher]]), the man whom [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] bigamously married in 1935. Edith is the family member who uncovered Marianne's deceptions, helped rescue her brother from the disastrous marriage, and later provided crucial testimony to psychical researchers about Marianne's fraudulent activities [3, 4].

## Background

Edith was the sister of Johnny Fisher, a commercial traveller who met Marianne at Marks Tey station in 1934 and was seduced into a bigamous marriage with her. Johnny had suffered head injuries from a motorcycle accident in his youth and was described as particularly susceptible to the charms of women.

## Uncovering the Bigamy

Edith grew increasingly suspicious of her sister-in-law and the strange household arrangements — including [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] living with the couple while pretending to be Marianne's elderly father, "Old Mr. Voyster" [3, 5]. Edith's mother had stayed with the couple for a holiday of several weeks but was never allowed to see the "father," whose door remained locked [5].

To uncover the truth, **Edith wrote to [[ethel-bull|Ethel Bull]] in Great Cornard, who confirmed that Lionel and Marianne were actually husband and wife**, rather than father and daughter [3, 6]. Appalled by this discovery, Edith wrote directly to Marianne, denouncing her and accusing her of entrapping her brother into a bigamous marriage [3, 6]. Marianne responded with cynical delaying tactics, begging Edith not to expose her "for the sake of the children" [3, 6].

## Rescuing Henry Fisher

Following these revelations, Edith and her family prevailed upon Henry and managed to remove him from Marianne's manipulative influence, moving him to a more peaceful environment [3]. When Henry suffered a complete emotional breakdown in 1945 after discovering the full truth of his situation, **Edith cared for him in seclusion in the West Country** [7, 8]. To spare him further agony, Edith and her family decided not to pursue Marianne legally [8]. Henry eventually died in an old people's home in Worcester in the late 1960s [7, 8].

## Observations on Child Neglect

Edith witnessed shocking instances of child neglect by Marianne. During a visit to Edith's home, Edith noticed that the nappy of Marianne's adopted baby was a dirty piece of rag, and underneath it the child's bottom was "one vast red sore" [9]. Despite this, Marianne simply replaced the excrement-soiled rag back onto the baby [9]. **Marianne coldly confessed to Edith that she "loathed children and had no patience for them,"** and admitted that she never used babysitters but simply left the baby at home alone [9]. Edith also noted that her brother Henry had shared doubts with her and their mother regarding the true paternity of [[astrid-fisher|Astrid]], another child Marianne falsely claimed was his [10, 11].

## The Christmas Séance Lead

In December 1949, at a séance in Gloucestershire attended by one of [[gay-taylor-mass-obs|Gay Taylor]]'s friends (Wanda Haines), Edith Shaw "flared up" at the mention of Marianne Foyster's name, declaring her "a liar, an adulteress and a bigamist" who had enticed her weak-minded brother into a disastrous marriage [1, 12]. This overheard remark — relayed back to [[kathleen-goldney|Mollie Goldney]] and [[eric-dingwall|Dingwall]] — was the key that unlocked the full story of Marianne's post-Borley deceptions and bigamous marriages.

## Assistance to Researchers and Murder Suspicions

In the 1950s, SPR investigator [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]] contacted Edith Shaw, and she became a vital source of background information regarding the early stages of Marianne and Henry Fisher's relationship [4]. She confirmed to Hall that Marianne had called herself "Miss Voyster," had presented an exterior of devout Roman Catholicism, and that Lionel Foyster had actively impersonated her father [4]. Furthermore, **Edith was convinced that Lionel Foyster had been murdered, specifically claiming that he had been poisoned**, and concluded that Marianne had deliberately hastened his death [13, 14].
