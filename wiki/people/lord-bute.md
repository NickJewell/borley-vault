# John, third Marquess of Bute (1847–1900)

Scottish nobleman, architect, and psychical researcher. He rented Ballechin House, Strathtay, Perthshire in 1897 and commissioned a systematic investigation of its alleged hauntings — the case [[harry-price|Price]] presents as the closest parallel to Borley Rectory.

## Involvement with Ballechin

In 1892 Lord Bute met Father [[father-hayden-sj|Hayden, S.J.]], at a chance encounter; Hayden told him of his disturbing experiences at Ballechin in 1892 (nine nights of extraordinary noises). Bute, deeply interested in psychical research, took note. In 1896 a Spanish family rented Ballechin and experienced extensive phenomena; their butler Mr. Harold Sanders later wrote to *The Times* with a detailed account.

Bute rented Ballechin House from February 1897 and asked [[goodrich-freer|Colonel Lemesurier Taylor and Miss A. Goodrich-Freer]] (both experienced investigators) to conduct a thorough investigation with a staff of servants. They remained for approximately three months. The resulting investigation and report were published in 1899 as *The Alleged Haunting of B--- House*.

## Correspondences with Borley

Price draws up a parallel column comparing Ballechin with Borley. Key correspondences: both houses had a phantom nun seen many times haunting the grounds but never the house; both had Roman Catholic associations throughout; both cases were reported to psychical researchers by a Roman Catholic priest; both investigators rented the house and assembled panels of independent observers; exorcisms by both Anglican and RC priests failed in both cases; dogs reacted violently in both cases; maids refused to remain in both houses.

The Ballechin case is sometimes cited as a precursor-investigation to Borley — and Bute's systematic, funded approach prefigures Price's observer program thirty years later.

## Death

Bute died in 1900 before the Borley hauntings became publicly known. The Ballechin phenomena abruptly ceased at some point after the investigation.
