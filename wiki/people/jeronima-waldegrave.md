# Jeronima Waldegrave

One of two daughters of Sir Nicholas Waldegrave of Borley who became nuns in exile on the Continent. She is eliminated by Ivan Banks as a candidate for the ghost nun of Borley Rectory, by virtue of her known cause of death — which in turn focuses Banks's attention entirely on her sister [[barbara-waldegrave|Barbara Waldegrave (Sister Apollonia)]].

## Family

Jeronima was a daughter of Sir Nicholas Waldegrave of Borley (descended from [[sir-edward-waldegrave|Sir Edward Waldegrave]]'s nephew [[nicholas-waldegrave|Nicholas Waldegrave]]). Her mother was Catherine, née Browne of Wellhall, Essex. Her sister was Barbara, who became Sister Apollonia of the Brussels Benedictines.

## Religious Life

Unlike her sister Barbara who joined the Brussels English Benedictine community, Jeronima entered the **nuns of Ghent** — a different exiled English religious house in the Spanish Netherlands.

## Significance: Elimination

Banks's logic for identifying the ghost nun rests on a process of elimination. He establishes two key facts:

1. Both Jeronima and Barbara came from Borley itself, making them intrinsically relevant candidates.
2. Jeronima's cause of death is known — she **died young from consumption** (tuberculosis).

Because her cause of death is documented and unremarkable — consumption was a common fatal illness — Jeronima does not fit the profile of a troubled or murdered spirit. Banks therefore eliminates her and concludes:

> "Of the two sisters, we do know Jeronima died young from consumption and she belonged to the exiled English community of Ghent. That leaves Barbara."

This leaves [[barbara-waldegrave|Barbara Waldegrave / Sister Apollonia]], who also died young (aged ~36) but whose death is recorded only as "happily deceased" — an ambiguous entry that Banks finds suspicious, and which he argues could conceal inner spiritual conflict or an unusual death.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Jeronima Waldegrave** is briefly but specifically noted in the Borley sources during the historical search to identify the famous "phantom nun" of Borley Rectory. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific biographical details and facts regarding her life and relevance to the case:

**Family Background and Vocation**
Jeronima was from Borley and was the **daughter of Sir Nicholas Waldegrave** [1, 2]. She was the sister of Barbara Waldegrave (who became Sister Apollonia) [1, 2]. Like many women in the staunchly Catholic Waldegrave family, Jeronima took holy orders and became a nun [2]. However, while her sister Barbara joined the Benedictine order in Brussels, **Jeronima went to join the exiled English community of nuns in Ghent** [2, 3]. 

**Death and Relevance to the Borley Legend**
Researchers looking into the Borley haunting closely examined the Waldegrave daughters to see if any of them could be the tragic figure behind the ghost nun legend [3, 4]. Jeronima is explicitly ruled out as the victim of a violent murder at Borley because historical records confirm her ultimate fate: **she died young from consumption** (tuberculosis) while with her religious order in Ghent [3]. 

Because Jeronima's cause of death is clearly documented, investigators (such as Ivan Banks) focused their attention instead on her sister Barbara as the prime candidate for the Borley nun [3].
