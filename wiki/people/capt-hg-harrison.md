# Captain H. G. Harrison

One of [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s official observers at Borley Rectory during the 1937–38 tenancy. Harrison took part in the table-tipping and Planchette séance held at the Rectory on the night of November 20–21, 1937.

## Séance of November 20–21, 1937

Harrison sat alongside [[dr-hf-bellamy|Dr. H. F. Bellamy]], [[sidney-glanville|S. H. Glanville]], and [[roger-glanville|R. H. Glanville]] at this night séance in the Rectory. Two Planchette boards were used (Bellamy brought his own), and several unusual features were noted: some of the scripts were written in mirror-writing (backwards), and at one point the entity appeared to communicate that the "schoolroom window" would light up at midnight — though no observer saw it do so.

Key communications at this séance included confirmation that the dining-room window had been bricked up because of the Nun, an apparent offer from "Marie Lairre" to reveal whose skull had been found in the library cupboard (which the entity then refused to name), and a statement that the lights in the windows were caused by "Marie Lairre."

Harrison's role in the Borley record is otherwise not independently documented; he appears primarily as one of the group present at this specific séance. See [[communicating-entities]] for full séance context.
