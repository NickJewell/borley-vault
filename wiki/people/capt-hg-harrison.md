# Captain H. G. Harrison

One of [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s official observers at Borley Rectory during the 1937–38 tenancy, and **brother-in-law of [[sidney-glanville|Sidney H. Glanville]]**, Price's chief investigator [2, 3]. Harrison participated in multiple visits over the course of the tenancy and witnessed a range of reported phenomena.

## Visit of August 14–15, 1937

Harrison arrived with S. H. Glanville at 9:00 a.m. on Saturday, August 14, to inspect the house and grounds [3]. During the visit, they photographed four of the more elaborate wall writings [3]. During their evening patrols (between 9:40 p.m. and 1:00 a.m.), they **discovered fresh pencil marks** that had mysteriously appeared on a wall section they had left only shortly before [2, 3]. They also found that the Rectory's [[cats-cemetery|cats' cemetery]] had been inexplicably disturbed and dug over [4]. Harrison and Glanville co-signed the official report [3].

## Visit of August 28–29, 1937

Harrison returned for an overnight stay alongside [[sidney-glanville|Sidney Glanville]], [[roger-glanville|Roger Glanville]], and [[sqn-ldr-horniman|Squadron-Leader Horniman]] [5, 6]. During the night, between 11:00 p.m. and 4:00 a.m., the party reported light thuds and bumping noises they could not locate [6, 7]. The most dramatic incident occurred while the two Glanvilles were out for a meal, leaving Harrison and Horniman on duty: at exactly 8:24 p.m., while both men were singing along to a gramophone record, they **"clearly heard the sound of light tripping footsteps coming down the main stairs,"** though nothing was visible [5, 7].

## Visit of November 20–21, 1937

Harrison sat alongside [[dr-hf-bellamy|Dr. H. F. Bellamy]] and both Glanvilles. Two notable incidents were reported:

**The Swinging Blind.** At approximately 8:25 p.m., while the men were stationed in the Base Room (the library), they all noticed the window blind moving at the sides with a palpitating action [9, 10]. Because all the doors to the room were shut, they tested for a draught by puffing cigarette smoke into the narrow gap between the blind and the window [9]. The smoke did not disperse, leading the observers to conclude that there was no normal draught to account for the movement [9, 10].

**The Planchette Séance.** Harrison acted as an operator alongside the Glanvilles and Dr. Bellamy [11]. The script recorded communications from an entity identifying itself as the former rector "Harry Bull," who claimed it was his father (Henry) who had ordered the dining-room window to be bricked up [11]. The operators also ostensibly contacted "Marie Lairre" (the phantom nun), who confirmed she was from France, and at one point an entity appeared to communicate that the "schoolroom window" would light up at midnight — though no observer saw it do so. Several scripts were written in mirror-writing (backwards).
