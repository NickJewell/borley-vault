# Karl Barton, BSc

A civil engineer. One of three who spent the all-night vigil at Borley on **Saturday 8 April 1961** alongside [[st-john-saunders|St John Saunders]] (a publisher from Chelmsford) and [[captain-ben-martindale|Captain Ben Martindale]] (from Ockenham) [1].

## The April 1961 Night Vigil

During the investigation, the trio experienced a sudden and simultaneous failure of their mechanical and electrical equipment: their **torches, a flashlight camera, and even their car headlights all simultaneously "gave out"** [1].

Over the course of the night, Barton and his companions reported several inexplicable phenomena:

- **Footsteps and Voices:** The men heard unaccountable footsteps, as well as a disembodied voice originating from the roadside opposite the former Rectory site [1, 2].
- **Hovering Lights:** They witnessed bright, unexplained lights on the site of the former Rectory [2]. In a letter written several years later (dated 11 April 1969), St John Saunders elaborated on this sighting, describing them as **"pin-points of light that hovered over the Rectory garden at a height of ten to fifteen feet"** [1]. Saunders was absolutely certain these lights could not be dismissed as street lighting, reflections, or any other natural explanation [1]. (multiple witnesses)

No further details about Barton appear in *Ghosts of Borley*.
