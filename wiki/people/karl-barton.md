# Karl Barton, BSc

A civil engineer. One of the three who spent the all-night vigil at Borley on Saturday 8 April 1961 alongside [[st-john-saunders|St John Saunders]] and [[captain-ben-martindale|Captain Ben Martindale]]. All three reported hearing footsteps and seeing unexplained lights during the vigil. No further details appear in *Ghosts of Borley*.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Karl Barton, BSc**, was a civil engineer who participated in a notable "Later Borley" investigation alongside Mr. St. John Saunders (a publisher from Chelmsford) and Captain Ben Martindale (from Ockenham) [1]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following specific details about his involvement:

**The April 1961 Night Vigil and Equipment Failure**
On **Saturday, April 8, 1961**, Barton, Saunders, and Martindale conducted an all-night vigil in the Borley churchyard [1, 2]. During this investigation, the trio experienced a sudden and simultaneous failure of their mechanical and electrical equipment: their **torches, a flashlight camera, and even their car headlights all simultaneously "gave out"** [1]. 

**Auditory and Visual Phenomena**
During the course of the night, Barton and his companions reported encountering several inexplicable phenomena:
*   **Footsteps and Voices:** The men reported hearing unaccountable footsteps, as well as a disembodied voice originating from the roadside opposite the former Rectory site [1, 2].
*   **Hovering Lights:** They witnessed bright, unexplained lights on the site of the former Rectory [2]. In a letter written several years later (dated April 11, 1969), St. John Saunders elaborated on this sighting, describing them as **"pin-points of light that hovered over the Rectory garden at a height of ten to fifteen feet"** [1]. Saunders was absolutely certain that these lights could not be dismissed as street lighting, reflections, or any other natural explanation [1].
