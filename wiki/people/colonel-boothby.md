# Colonel Boothby

Husband of [[constance-brackenbury|Constance Brackenbury]], [[harry-bull|Harry Bull]]'s stepdaughter by his wife [[ivy-brackenbury|Ivy Brackenbury]]. Also recorded in one chronology as **Capt. Francis S.E. Boothy** [1-3].

## Marriage

Colonel Boothby married Constance Brackenbury at **All Saints Church, St Johns Wood, London, on February 21, 1938** [2]. He was described as "considerably older" than his bride [3].

> **Contradiction:** One source explicitly claims that Constance "married, at eighteen, a Colonel Boothby" [3]. However, the timeline makes this impossible: if Constance was born on October 7, 1901, she would have been 36 years old at the time of their 1938 wedding, not eighteen [1, 2]. The claim of "at eighteen" appears to be an error in the sources.

## Death and Legacy

Colonel Boothby **died in Cheltenham**, predeceasing his wife [3]. He had no direct involvement with the Borley hauntings during his life, but following his death his widow (then Mrs. Boothby) was tracked down and interviewed by investigator [[norah-walrond|Norah Walrond]] at the Ellenborough Hotel in Cheltenham in 1945 [1, 3]. During this interview, Constance provided the complete, mundane explanation for the famous "skull in the cupboard" that had supposedly terrorised the Smith incumbency at Borley Rectory — the skull had been turned up by gravediggers and sent by parcel post to Ivy after Harry Bull's death; Ivy had merely returned the package to the Rectory with instructions for it to be reburied in the churchyard [17]. After his death, Constance moved to South Africa, where she died in 1970 [3].
