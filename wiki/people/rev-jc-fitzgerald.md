# Rev. J. C. Fitzgerald

Member of the Mirfield Community (the Community of the Resurrection, an Anglican religious community at Mirfield, Yorkshire). Performed the second and permanently effective exorcism of "Beth-oni," the house of [[ada-sharpe|Miss Ada M. Sharpe]] at Tackley, Oxfordshire.

After the first exorcism by [[rev-l-de-clare|Rev. de Clare]] proved only partially effective, and phenomena had resumed in January 1908, Miss Sharpe called in Fitzgerald. He again exorcized the "entities" with the standard technique (prayers, incense, holy water). The phenomena "stopped immediately" and, writing five years later, Miss Sharpe confirmed there had been no further disturbances.

This makes Fitzgerald's exorcism the only case [[harry-price|Price]] cites in his extensive literature review where clerical exorcism produced **permanent and verified cessation** of a poltergeist infestation.

[[harry-price|Price]] speculates that the prayer specifically effective at Beth-oni was the petition against the *Spiritus percutiens* (the "percussive spirit") in the Church's prayers — a prayer whose existence is not widely known even among the clergy who attempted exorcism at Borley.

Fitzgerald's success at Beth-oni stands in contrast to the complete failure of multiple exorcisms at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] by [[dom-richard-whitehouse|Dom Richard Whitehouse]] and others. Price suggests the distinction is that the Borley "entities" were not malevolent spirits suitable for exorcism but distressed souls requiring Christian burial and Requiem Mass.
