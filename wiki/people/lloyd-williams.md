# Lloyd Williams (and Mrs. Lloyd Williams)

Lloyd Williams was "a Director of the B.B.C. Educational Department." He and his wife visited [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] on **February 18–19, 1938** as part of a BBC party that included [[c-gordon-glover|Mr. and Mrs. C. Gordon Glover]]. Mrs. Lloyd Williams is the key witness of the visit: she reported seeing a dark figure moving along the [[nuns-walk]] — the most specific BBC-period sighting of the nun.

## Auditory Phenomena (February 18, 1938)

During an inspection of the house at around **11:00 p.m.**, while in the scullery, Mrs. Lloyd Williams reported hearing **"six quick, young footsteps"** in the passage outside. Glover, acting as the primary report writer, was highly dismissive. Noting that no one else in the party heard the noises, he wrote: **"It is our opinion that these steps were imagined."**

> **Contradiction:** One chronological summary claims Mrs. Lloyd Williams heard a door quietly close downstairs while standing outside the Chapel. However, Glover's detailed report explicitly attributes this experience to his own wife, while noting that Mr. and Mrs. Lloyd Williams were at the other end of the Chapel and heard nothing when a later "thud" occurred.

## Mrs. Lloyd Williams's Nun Sighting

At approximately **5:45 p.m.**, the party positioned themselves in the large summer-house to watch the famous Nun's Walk. Glover and his wife watched the open section of the Walk; Mr. and Mrs. Lloyd Williams kept their eyes on the line of trees at the Rectory end.

At **6:10 p.m.**, Mrs. Lloyd Williams "suddenly tensed" and said nothing for ten seconds before declaring she had "distinctly seen 'a round, dark object.'" She described it as a **"short, stooping figure"** that appeared to move from the tree closest to the Rectory to the central fir-tree, where it vanished. She "had not seen it actually progress, but it had somehow covered the distance between the two trees." The light was "deep twilight." The rest of the party saw nothing.

The other three members immediately walked across the lawn and in front of the trees to test their visibility from the summer-house — Mrs. Lloyd Williams confirmed she could just see them, clothed in light-coloured coats.

**Her conviction was unshaken:** "One witness only whose faith that she saw something is not to be shaken." (single witness)

[[harry-price]] placed this alongside the [[vc-wall|V. C. Wall]] sighting from the same summer-house in June 1929 as comparable experiences from the same vantage point nearly a decade apart.

## The Aftermath: The "Row over the Nun"

The sighting triggered a significant dispute over the validity of the evidence.

- **Glover's Skepticism:** In his report to Price on February 26, 1938, Gordon Glover attached no importance to the sighting, implying Mrs. Lloyd Williams was "a little imaginative and impressionable." He dismissed the claim with the quote: **"One witness only whose faith that she saw something is not to be shaken."**
- **Price's Sensationalism:** Despite the primary report completely discounting the event, [[harry-price|Harry Price]] excitedly wrote to [[sidney-glanville|Sidney Glanville]] on **March 3, 1938**, boasting, "You will be interested to hear that the wife of a B.B.C. official saw the 'Nun' at Borley last Saturday week." Price subsequently published the incident in *The Most Haunted House in England* under the caption **"THE 'NUN' SEEN AGAIN"** and officially added her to his list of fourteen people who had seen the apparition.
- **The S.P.R. Critique vs. Robert Hastings:** The authors of the 1956 *Borley Report* used this episode to attack Price, arguing that Mrs. Lloyd Williams only saw a ghost because of the power of suggestion planted by Price's [[blue-book-of-instructions|*Blue Book of Instructions*]]. However, researcher [[robert-hastings|Robert J. Hastings]] later defended the sighting's independence by pointing to Glover's own report: **"We have *since*, as you know, ascertained that this 'Nun,' when seen, has always been observed at this particular spot."** Hastings argued that the use of the word *since* proved the party did not know the exact traditional spot of the haunting beforehand, rendering the S.P.R.'s theory of prior suggestion "nonsense."

## Also at the Visit

**Lloyd Williams himself** is listed in Appendix D as a witness to "Clicks or Cracks" and "Knocks, Bumps, Thuds" (the collective experiences at 12:30 a.m. in the [[blue-room|Blue Room]]) but has no individual paranormal experience recorded.

The sources record the dates "F. Lloyd Williams 15.02.38" and "M. Lloyd Williams 23.02.38" in a master list of observers — likely the dates they respectively signed Price's *Haunted House Declaration Form* or submitted their formal reports.
