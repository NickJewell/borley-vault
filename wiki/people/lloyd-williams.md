# Lloyd Williams (and Mrs. Lloyd Williams)

Lloyd Williams was "a Director of the B.B.C. Educational Department." He and his wife visited Borley Rectory on February 18, 1938 as part of a BBC party that included [[c-gordon-glover|Mr. and Mrs. C. Gordon Glover]]. Mrs. Lloyd Williams is the key witness of the visit: she reported seeing a dark figure moving along the [[nuns-walk]] — the most specific BBC-period sighting of the nun.

## Mrs. Lloyd Williams's Nun Sighting

At approximately 6:10 p.m., the party was positioned in the large summer-house watching the Nun's Walk. Glover and his wife watched the open section of the Walk; Mr. and Mrs. Lloyd Williams kept their eyes on the line of trees at the Rectory end.

Mrs. Lloyd Williams "suddenly tensed" and said nothing for ten seconds. She then declared that she had "distinctly seen 'a round, dark object.'" It had appeared to move from the tree closest to the Rectory to the central fir-tree, where it vanished. She "had not seen it actually progress, but it had somehow covered the distance between the two trees." The light was "deep twilight."

Glover notes that the other three members of the party immediately walked across the lawn and in front of the trees to test their visibility from the summer-house — Mrs. Lloyd Williams confirmed she could just see them, clothed in light-coloured coats.

**Her conviction was unshaken:** "One witness only whose faith that she saw something is not to be shaken."

[[harry-price]] places this alongside the V. C. Wall sighting from the same summer-house in June 1929 as comparable experiences from the same vantage point nearly a decade apart.

## Also at the Visit

**Mrs. Lloyd Williams** additionally reported hearing "six quick, young footsteps" in the passage outside the scullery during an 11 p.m. house tour — though the others present heard nothing, and Glover notes in his report that "it is our opinion that these steps were imagined."

**Lloyd Williams himself** is listed in Appendix D as a witness to "Clicks or Cracks" and "Knocks, Bumps, Thuds" (the collective experiences at 12:30 a.m. in the Blue Room) but has no individual paranormal experience recorded.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Mr. Lloyd Williams** (a Director of the B.B.C. Educational Department) and **Mrs. Lloyd Williams** were part of the rota of official observers recruited by Harry Price to investigate Borley Rectory during his 1937–1938 tenancy [1]. While Mr. Lloyd Williams is largely absent from the active phenomena reports, his wife was at the center of several auditory and visual claims that ultimately sparked a significant controversy between skeptics and believers. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following specific events, quotes, and contradictions regarding their involvement:

**The February 1938 Visit and Auditory Phenomena**
The Lloyd Williamses visited the Rectory on **February 18–19, 1938**, accompanied by another B.B.C. producer, Mr. C. Gordon Glover, and his wife [1, 2]. 
*   **The Footsteps:** During an inspection of the house at around 11:00 p.m., while in the scullery, Mrs. Lloyd Williams reported hearing **"six quick, young footsteps"** in the passage outside [3, 4]. Mr. Glover, acting as the primary report writer for the group, was highly dismissive. Noting that no one else in the party heard the noises, he wrote: **"It is our opinion that these steps were imagined"** [4-6].
*   **A Minor Contradiction:** The sources contain a slight discrepancy regarding another auditory event that afternoon. One chronological summary claims Mrs. Lloyd Williams heard a door quietly close downstairs while standing outside the Chapel [2]. However, Glover's detailed report explicitly attributes this experience to his own wife, while noting that Mr. and Mrs. Lloyd Williams were at the other end of the Chapel and heard nothing when a later "thud" occurred [3, 4].

**The "Nun" Sighting**
Mrs. Lloyd Williams is most famous in the Borley literature for claiming to see the phantom nun. 
*   At 5:45 p.m. on the evening of their visit, the party sat in the large summer-house to keep watch over the famous Nun's Walk [7, 8]. 
*   At **6:10 p.m.**, Mrs. Lloyd Williams suddenly tensed and remained silent for ten seconds before declaring she had distinctly seen a **"round, dark object"** which she described as a **"short, stooping figure"** [7, 8]. 
*   She reported that it appeared to move from the tree closest to the Rectory to the central fir tree, and then vanished. She noted it had not seemed to physically progress, but had "somehow covered the distance" between the two trees [7, 8].
*   The rest of the party saw nothing in the "deep twilight" and decided to test her visibility by walking the route themselves in light-coloured coats; Mrs. Lloyd Williams admitted they were only "just visible" [7, 8]. 

**The Aftermath: Contradictions and the "Row over the Nun"**
The sighting triggered a row over the validity of the evidence [9]:
*   **Glover's Skepticism:** In his report to Price on February 26, 1938, Gordon Glover attached no importance to the sighting, implying Mrs. Lloyd Williams was "a little imaginative and impressionable" [5]. He dismissed her with the quote: **"One witness only whose faith that she saw something is not to be shaken"** [5, 7, 8].
*   **Price's Sensationalism:** Despite the primary report completely discounting the event, Harry Price excitedly wrote to Sidney Glanville on **March 3, 1938**, boasting, "You will be interested to hear that the wife of a B.B.C. official saw the 'Nun' at Borley last Saturday week" [5]. Price subsequently published the incident in *The Most Haunted House in England* under the unrestrained caption **"THE 'NUN' SEEN AGAIN"** and officially added her to his list of fourteen people who had seen the apparition [5].
*   **The S.P.R. Critique vs. Robert Hastings:** The authors of the highly critical 1956 *Borley Report* (Dingwall, Goldney, and Hall) used this episode to attack Price, arguing that Mrs. Lloyd Williams only saw a ghost because of the power of suggestion planted by Price's *Blue Book of Instructions* [5]. However, the researcher Robert J. Hastings later defended the sighting's independence by pointing to a specific quote in Glover's own report: **"We have *since*, as you know, ascertained that this 'Nun,' when seen, has always been observed at this particular spot"** [7, 10]. Hastings argued that the use of the word *since* proved the party did not know the exact traditional spot of the haunting beforehand, rendering the S.P.R.'s theory of prior suggestion "nonsense" [10].

**Lesser-Known Documentary Details**
The sources record the dates **"F. Lloyd Williams 15.02.38"** and **"M. Lloyd Williams 23.02.38"** in a master list of observers [11]. Given the timing of their visit, these are most likely the dates they respectively signed Price's *Haunted House Declaration Form* or submitted their formal reports.
