# Mrs Alex English (née Ethel Beenham)

Former secretary to [[harry-price|Harry Price]] who served as note-taker and reporter during the critical August 1943 Borley excavation. With twelve years of experience as Price's assistant in many investigations, she was "ideal for the purpose" — and, according to Price, "indispensable."

## Early Career

Ethel Beenham worked as Harry Price's secretary at the University of London Council for Psychical Investigation, employed by Price intermittently between 1928 and 1935 [2]. Although she did not accompany him to [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] during this initial secretarial period, she remained closely connected to his work [2]. She was present at the Rectory ruins for the famous [[psychic-fete-1939|Psychic Fête]] on June 21, 1939, alongside twenty-five members of Price's Ghost Club [1].

## The August 1943 Excavation

By 1943, having married and become Mrs. Alex English, she played a vital role in Price's famous excavations of the Rectory cellars [3, 4]. Mrs English interrupted her domestic duties to join Price at [[liston-rectory|Liston Rectory]] as a guest of [[rev-ac-henning|Rev. and Mrs Henning]], accompanying the digging party to the Rectory ruins. The official digging logs record her as present on Day 1 (August 17), Day 2 (August 18), and Day 3 (August 30) of the investigation [5].

Her handwriting is the original record of the two most significant finds:

- **1:45 p.m., August 17, 1943:** "Jaw-bone (pig?) found" — immediately revised by Dr. Bailey's expert identification as human.
- **1:50 p.m., August 17, 1943:** "Part of skull dug up" — no ambiguity; immediately confirmed as human.

These minute-by-minute notes were kept in real time as Jackson excavated. They constitute the primary contemporaneous document for the discovery of the [[bones-excavation|Borley human remains]], and their precision — with exact times and initial field identifications — gives them particular evidential weight. Her notes are deposited with the Borley dossier at the University of London.

## Defence of Harry Price and the SPR Omission

Following Harry Price's death, journalist [[charles-sutton|Charles Sutton]] published an article in the *Inky Way Annual* accusing Price of fraudulently faking poltergeist phenomena at Borley [6]. Mrs. English reacted to these allegations with "astonished incredulity" [6].

In 1949, the SPR investigator [[kathleen-goldney|Mollie Goldney]] formally interviewed Mrs. English regarding Price's integrity, in the presence of SPR Council Member Miss Ina Jephson [2]. Goldney's own notes from the interview described Mrs. English as having made "a most genuine effort to record the facts as she remembered them" [2].

However, researcher [[robert-hastings|Robert J. Hastings]] explicitly pointed out that although Mrs. English provided testimony defending Price against the *Inky Way* allegations, the highly critical authors of the [[the-borley-report|1956 Borley Report]] — which included Goldney herself — deliberately chose not to quote Mrs. English's defensive testimony in their final publication [6].
