# Rev. Father John Wright

Roman Catholic priest of S. Philip Neri, Arundel, Sussex. He was the first priest to say a documented Requiem Mass for the soul of [[marie-lairre|'Marie Lairre']], thus fulfilling the request that the Borley entities had made via Planchette scripts and [[wall-writings|wall-messages]] for over a decade.

## The Requiem Mass

On October 29, 1943, Father Wright wrote to [[harry-price|Price]] to say that "Mass is being said here to-morrow morning for the repose of the soul of the good lady" — that is, the Borley Nun. The Mass was celebrated on October 30, 1943 — a Requiem Mass, exactly as the wall-messages had repeatedly requested.

Wright is also acknowledged in Price's Preface to *The End of Borley Rectory*:

> *"I must mention the Rev. Father John Wright, of S. Philip Neri, Arundel, who was, perhaps, the first priest to say a Requiem Mass for the soul of 'Marie Lairre'—who so often requested it. I thank him for meeting her wishes."*

The Arundel Mass preceded the [[reburial-marie-lairre|formal reburial at Liston Churchyard]] on May 29, 1945 by approximately nineteen months, making it the first specific fulfilment of the Planchette entities' religious requests.

## Significance

The entities communicating at Borley — whether paranormal, subconscious, or otherwise — consistently requested a specific thing: Requiem Mass, prayers, light, incense, holy water, and Christian burial. Wright's Mass was the first institutional response to those requests from a Roman Catholic priest. The [[rev-ac-henning|Rev. Henning]]'s 1945 burial service completed the programme.
