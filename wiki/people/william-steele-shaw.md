# William Steele Shaw

Father of [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] (born Mary Anne Emily Rebecca Shaw). His unsuccessful career and near-poverty shaped Marianne's lifelong tendency to fabricate a grander background for herself.

## Career

The sources describe William Shaw's professional life as "chequered and unsuccessful" [3]. He initially worked as a schoolmaster at **Grafton House**, a small public school near Manchester, but left for reasons unknown. He then attempted to become a publican at the **Woodman Inn at Hyde, near Stockport**, but was as unsuccessful at that as he had been in teaching [3]. By the time Marianne was born on **26 January 1899** he was eking out a precarious existence as a **private tutor of shorthand, arithmetic, and book-keeping**, while his wife gave piano lessons [1, 3, 4]. The family lived in near poverty at **5 Guy Wood Cottages, Romiley, Cheshire** — a terrace of very small houses with a single outside lavatory shared by neighbouring families [3, 4].

William was married to **Anne Elizabeth Shaw** (formerly Woodyatt), with whom he had a son, Geoffrey (born 1897), and Marianne [1, 2]. Robert Wood observes that Shaw was "probably a frustrated man, who had aspired to, but failed to achieve, better things." He speculates that his unfulfilled aspirations were transferred to his daughter, who spent her adult life claiming an aristocratic background.

## The Connection to Lionel Foyster

Despite their humble circumstances, the Shaws became acquainted with a young local curate named [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]], who actually baptised Marianne when she was seven years old [5]. The Shaws and Foyster maintained a friendship over the years, exchanging Christmas and Easter cards even after Foyster emigrated to Canada in 1910 [6, 7].

## Moves

- *Birth of Marianne (1899)*: living in Romiley, near Stockport, Cheshire
- *c. 1901*: family moved to **Lymm, near Warrington** — where the fateful encounter with the curate [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] occurred
- *1907*: moved to **Northern Ireland**, settling in the rural hamlet of Drumnadreagh, near Larne [8, 9], where Shaw finally secured a steady position working as a time-keeper for the **British Portland Cement Company** [9]

## Collusion with Marianne

When fifteen-year-old Marianne secretly married a clerk named Harold Giffard Greenwood in 1914, William's occupation on the marriage certificate was listed as "schoolmaster" — which he once had been but no longer was [9]. Researchers point to this "pathetic dishonesty" as the psychological catalyst that reinforced Marianne's lifelong tendency to fantasise about her own background [9].

When Greenwood abandoned Marianne only six weeks after she gave birth to their son [[ian-greenwood|Ian]], William Shaw intervened by having a solicitor draw up a document of legal separation [10]. To protect Marianne from gossip, William and his wife took over the upbringing of her infant son, Ian Geoffrey William, maintaining a strict pretense that the boy was actually their own son and Marianne's younger brother [10, 11]. This deception was so successfully maintained that when Ian Shaw died decades later in 1986, even his own widow was still under the impression that William and Annie Shaw had been his true parents [13].

When [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]] later visited the Shaws in Northern Ireland (on leave from Canada), William and Annie Shaw are alleged to have concealed Marianne's first marriage and son from him entirely. Ian was passed off as Marianne's younger brother. Wood suggests this family dishonesty directly modelled the deceptions Marianne practised throughout her adult life.

Marianne herself attributed her tendency to fantasise partly to her childhood poverty: "As a child I used to make up stories that I was a princess, all kinds of silly things."

> **Contradiction:** Marianne consistently gave false details about her father on official documents. On her 1914 marriage certificate to Greenwood, Shaw's occupation is stated as "schoolmaster" — which he once had been, but no longer was. On her 1935 bigamous marriage certificate to Fisher, her father is rendered as "Leon Alphonse Voyster, of independent means" — a conflation of [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]]'s name.
