# William Steele Shaw

Father of [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] (born Marianne Emily Rebecca Shaw). His unsuccessful career and near-poverty shaped Marianne's lifelong tendency to fabricate a grander background for herself.

## Career

Shaw's career was a series of failures. He had been a schoolmaster at **Grafton House**, a small public school near Manchester, but left for reasons unknown. He then attempted to become a publican, running the **Woodman Inn at Hyde, near Stockport**, but was as unsuccessful at that as he had been in teaching. By the time Marianne was born in January 1899 he was eking out a precarious existence as a **private tutor of shorthand and book-keeping**, and the family lived in near poverty at 5 Guy Wood Cottages, Romiley — a terrace of very small houses with a single outside lavatory shared by neighbours.

Robert Wood observes that Shaw was "probably a frustrated man, who had aspired to, but failed to achieve, better things." He speculates that his unfulfilled aspirations were transferred to his daughter, who spent her adult life claiming to have an aristocratic background.

## Moves

- *Birth of Marianne (1899)*: living in Romiley, near Stockport, Cheshire
- *c. 1901*: family moved to **Lymm, near Warrington** — where the fateful encounter with the curate [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] occurred
- *1907*: moved to **Northern Ireland**, where Shaw obtained a post as **time-keeper for the British Portland Cement Company**
- Marianne was later born to say she lived near **Larne**, County Antrim, and it was there that she met and secretly married [[harold-greenwood|Harold Greenwood]] in 1914

## Collusion with Marianne

When Foyster visited the Shaws in Northern Ireland (on leave from Canada), William and Annie Shaw are alleged to have concealed Marianne's first marriage and son [[ian-greenwood|Ian Greenwood Shaw]] from him. Ian was passed off as Marianne's younger brother. Wood suggests this family dishonesty directly modelled the deceptions Marianne practised throughout her adult life.

Marianne herself attributed her tendency to fantasise partly to her childhood poverty: "As a child I used to make up stories that I was a princess, all kinds of silly things."

> **Contradiction:** Marianne consistently gave false details about her father on official documents. On her 1914 marriage certificate to Greenwood, Shaw's occupation is stated as "schoolmaster" — which he once had been, but no longer was. On her 1935 bigamous marriage certificate to Fisher, her father is rendered as "Leon Alphonse Voyster, of independent means" — a conflation of [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]]'s name.
