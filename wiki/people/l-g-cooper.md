# L. G. Cooper

L. G. Godfrey-Cooper was one of [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s official observers at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]]. He visited on the night of **8 December 1937** with [[dr-pe-ryberg|Dr P. E. Ryberg]] and [[r-m-christie|R. M. Christie]].

Their joint report documented active mouse infestation, no unexplained sounds, and easy access via the cellar — all findings reportedly suppressed by Price, who had published the claim that "never once has any observer, to my knowledge, mentioned rats." No phenomena were reported during the visit.
