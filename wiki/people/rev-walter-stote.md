# Rev. Walter G. Stote, M.A. (Oxon)

Former Vicar of All Saints, Sudbury, Suffolk, having taken up the post in 1919. By the early 1940s he had relocated to "Jenvey," 40 Farwell Road, Sidcup, Kent. Writing to [[harry-price|Harry Price]] on March 22, 1941, Stote provided independent corroboration of the Rev. [[harry-bull|Harry Bull]]'s nun sightings — testimony particularly valuable because it came from Harry Bull himself, in person, during his lifetime.

## Direct Testimony

Through his post in neighbouring Sudbury, Stote had known most of the Bull family since 1919, and knew Harry Bull "very well — not so the succeeding Rectors." Having just read Price's *The Most Haunted House in England*, he wrote to confirm the following:

> *"The Rev. Harry Bull told me himself about seeing the nun. Others have told my wife about the coach, etc."*

He also recalled his own suspicions about the property's underground passages: "I remember at the time that I thought the underground passages might well repay attention" — an observation that would prove accurate when [[canon-phythian-adams|Canon Phythian-Adams]] later focused on the cellars.

## Significance

This is one of very few accounts in which Harry Bull directly communicated his experiences to an independent witness who later provided a signed statement to Price. Most of what is known about Harry Bull's nun sightings comes through his sisters (chiefly [[ethel-bull|Ethel Bull]]); Stote's letter establishes that Bull discussed his sightings with clerical contemporaries. The letter was written in March 1941, about twelve months after the publication of *The Most Haunted House in England*, and represents a response from someone with independent prior knowledge who had nothing to gain from supporting the legend.
