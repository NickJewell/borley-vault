# Rev. Walter G. Stote, M.A.

Former Vicar of All Saints, Sudbury, Suffolk. Writing from "Jenvey," 40 Farwell Road, Sidcup, Kent, on March 22, 1941, Stote provided [[harry-price]] with independent corroboration of the Rev. [[harry-bull|Harry Bull]]'s nun sightings — testimony that is particularly valuable because it came from Harry Bull himself, in person, during his lifetime.

## Direct Testimony

Stote had known most of the Bull family since 1919, when he became Vicar of All Saints, Sudbury:

> *"The Rev. Harry Bull told me himself about seeing the nun. Others have told my wife about the coach, etc."*

This is one of very few accounts in which Harry Bull directly communicated his experiences to an independent witness who later provided a signed statement to Price. Most of what is known about Harry Bull's nun sightings comes through his sisters (chiefly [[ethel-bull|Ethel Bull]]); Stote's corroboration establishes that Bull discussed his sightings with clerical contemporaries.

Stote also mentions that he had, at the time of his Sudbury incumbency, thought that the underground passages at Borley "might well repay attention" — an observation that would prove accurate when [[canon-phythian-adams|Canon Phythian-Adams]] later focused on the cellars.

## Significance

As a neighbouring vicar who knew the Bull family well and heard Harry Bull's own account, Stote provides a corroborating link between the family's private experience and the broader record that Price was assembling. His letter was written in March 1941, about twelve months after the publication of *The Most Haunted House in England*, and represents a response to that publication from someone with independent prior knowledge.
