# Emily Warren

Wife of [[george-warren|George Warren]] and active participant in the [[marks-tey-spiritualist-circle|Marks Tey Spiritualist Circle]]'s January 1932 séance at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]].

## Role

Emily accompanied the Marks Tey group to Borley for the overnight séance on 23 January 1932. She brought the **planchette** — a small, heart-shaped wooden board with castors for automatic writing — which was used that night. During the sitting, it was Emily who spoke directly to the spirit of the Borley nun (channelled through medium [[guy-lestrange|Guy L'Estrange]]):

> 'No, dear child. Go with the lady who stands behind you and is helping you now. She will take you to a place of rest and beauty. Your terrible sufferings are over. Put them out of your mind. You are now going to progress in your new life and be very, very happy.'

She also brought **a bowl of primroses** to the table when the spirit asked for spring flowers. L'Estrange buried his face in them and smiled.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Emily Warren** was a spiritualist who, alongside her husband, played a significant role in attempting to rid Borley Rectory of its phenomena during the controversial Foyster incumbency in January 1932. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following facts, events, quotes, and lesser-known biographical details regarding her involvement:

**Biographical Details and The Marks Tey Circle**
Emily was the wife of George Warren, a 79-year-old grocer and postmaster in the village of Marks Tey, located about eight miles from Borley [1]. Together, Emily and George headed the **Marks Tey Spiritualist Circle**, a loose-knit group of spiritualist friends drawn from across East Anglia [1, 2]. Emily and her husband were uniquely central to this group, as they were the only members who actually lived in Marks Tey, hosting séances in the back of their post office on Station Road [1, 2].

**The 1932 Borley Rectory Visit**
In January 1932, George Warren wrote to the Rev. Lionel Foyster offering the Circle's help to rid the Rectory of its ghosts [1]. After some initial hesitation and resistance from Marianne Foyster, the Foysters agreed to the visit on the condition that Marianne would not be accused of faking the phenomena [2]. 

When the group was gathered to travel to the Rectory, a lesser-known domestic detail regarding Emily was recorded in Lionel Foyster's manuscript. Foyster’s account quotes a participant describing her interruption: **“We found Mrs. [Warren] in the middle of giving the children their baths; but all the same she got ready and came”** [3]. 

Emily arrived at the Rectory at about 10 p.m. on a night of torrential rain, turning up "drenched" alongside her husband, Mr. Frost, Captain V. M. Deane, and a Mr. Footman [4-6]. 

**The Planchette**
A specific and crucial detail regarding Emily's role that night is that she was the one who physically provided the equipment for the séance. The sources note that **Mrs. Warren brought with her a planchette**—described as a small, heart-shaped wooden board fitted with castors and a pencil socket, used by sitters to obtain "automatic writing" from spirits [5].

**Aftermath**
Following the dramatic events of the Circle's visit—which involved a barrage of thrown bottles and ringing bells—the severe poltergeist phenomena at the Rectory abruptly ceased for a long period [7, 8]. Emily and her husband firmly believed that their group’s intervention was responsible for bringing the haunting to a halt, and they continued to conduct their séances back at Marks Tey [9].
