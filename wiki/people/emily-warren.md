# Emily Warren

Wife of [[george-warren|George Warren]] and co-head — alongside her husband — of the [[marks-tey-spiritualist-circle|Marks Tey Spiritualist Circle]]. Active participant in the Circle's January 1932 séance at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]].

## Background

Emily and her husband were uniquely central to the Marks Tey group, as they were the only members who actually lived in Marks Tey, hosting séances in the back of their post office on Station Road [1, 2]. George Warren was a 79-year-old grocer and postmaster in the village of Marks Tey, located about eight miles from Borley [1].

## The 1932 Borley Rectory Visit

In January 1932, George Warren wrote to the Rev. [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] offering the Circle's help to rid the Rectory of its ghosts [1]. After some initial hesitation and resistance from [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]], the Foysters agreed to the visit on the condition that Marianne would not be accused of faking the phenomena [2].

A lesser-known domestic detail recorded in Lionel Foyster's manuscript captures the domestic reality of the night: when the group assembled to travel to the Rectory, a participant noted — **"We found Mrs. [Warren] in the middle of giving the children their baths; but all the same she got ready and came"** [3].

Emily arrived at the Rectory at about 10 p.m. on a night of torrential rain, "drenched" alongside her husband, Mr. Frost, Captain V. M. Deane, and a Mr. Footman [4–6].

## Role at the Séance

Emily brought the **planchette** — a small, heart-shaped wooden board fitted with castors and a pencil socket — which was used that night for automatic writing [5]. During the sitting, it was Emily who spoke directly to the spirit of the Borley nun (channelled through medium [[guy-lestrange|Guy L'Estrange]]):

> 'No, dear child. Go with the lady who stands behind you and is helping you now. She will take you to a place of rest and beauty. Your terrible sufferings are over. Put them out of your mind. You are now going to progress in your new life and be very, very happy.'

She also brought **a bowl of primroses** to the table when the spirit asked for spring flowers. L'Estrange buried his face in them and smiled.

## Aftermath

Following the dramatic events of the Circle's visit — which involved a barrage of thrown bottles and ringing bells — the severe poltergeist phenomena at the Rectory abruptly ceased for a long period [7, 8]. Emily and her husband firmly believed that their group's intervention was responsible for bringing the haunting to a halt, and they continued to conduct their séances back at Marks Tey [9].
