# Bishop Richardson (of Fredericton)

Anglican bishop of the Diocese of Fredericton, New Brunswick, Canada, during [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]]'s Canadian incumbencies (c. 1910–1929). His diocesan registers are the principal documentary evidence for the chronology of Foyster's Canadian postings.

## Diocesan Register Entries

Robert Wood quotes directly from Bishop Richardson's register, providing a precise timeline of Lionel Foyster's final years in Canada:

**Jan 15, 1927** — "The Bishop accepted the resignation of the Rev. L.A. Foyster from the parish (mission) of Drummond to take effect from Feb 7th, 1927." [2]

**Feb 26, 1927** — "The Bishop on February 13th, instituted the Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster into the Rectory of St Paul's church in the county of Westmorland [Sackville]." [4]

**July 22, 1929** — "The Bishop accepted the resignation of the Rev. L.A. Foyster (to take effect Sept 15th next) from Parish of Sackville." [5]

**Sept 9, 1929** — "The Bishop, Sept 7th 1929, granted leave of absence from his duties in the space of twelve months to Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster." [5]

**June 20, 1930** — The Bishop officially transferred Lionel Foyster to the diocese of Chelmsford in England, paving the way for him to take up the living at Borley [6].

> **Contradiction:** The register entry of September 7, 1929 records a leave of absence "in the space of twelve months" [5], while another passage states that in October 1929 Lionel was granted "three months' leave of absence" to be treated at St. Luke's hospital in London for his declining health [11]. The true duration is unclear.

## Alleged Pressure to Leave

[[ian-greenwood|Ian Greenwood Shaw]] alleged that Bishop Richardson grew so displeased with [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]]'s conduct — which reportedly included affairs with a young Anglican clergyman and a Roman Catholic priest [7] — that he "asked Foyster to leave the diocese" [8]. The sources note that Lionel "would hear nothing against his adored wife, not even from his spiritual superior, the Bishop of Fredericton" [3]. Robert Wood concludes that the Bishop finally losing patience with Marianne was one of three primary reasons (alongside Foyster's failing health and the 1929 Wall Street Crash) that drove the Foysters to leave Canada in 1930 and take refuge in Borley Rectory [9].

Wood notes that "no records about this survive in the diocese of Fredericton" and that Ian Shaw's account is uncorroborated by official Church documents [10]. He cannot confirm or deny the allegation from documentary evidence alone.
