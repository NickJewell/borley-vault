# Mabel Davies

Wife of [[dr-davies|Dr Davies]]; died of cancer, date not given by Robert Wood. Her death precipitated her husband's emotional collapse and his migration to the Grosvenor Hotel, Victoria, where he took up spiritualism in hopes of contacting her — which eventually led him into [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s hands.

## Significance

Mabel Davies is a posthumous presence in the story: it is her death that created the vulnerability [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] exploited. Dr Davies even called her "Tweatie" (a pet name). During fake trances in the hotel lounge, Marianne impersonated Mabel's spirit, allowing Davies to believe he was being embraced by his dead wife rather than by Marianne. Wood describes this as one of the cruellest of Marianne's many manipulations.

After the relationship ended, Marianne returned Mabel's clothes to Davies "twisted into strange doll-like shapes, with mysterious pieces of cardboard upon which what Davies described as 'cabbalistic symbols' were inscribed" — an apparent attempt to frighten him.
