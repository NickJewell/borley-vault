# Willi Schneider (1903–1971)

Austrian physical medium; the first Schneider brother tested by [[harry-price|Harry Price]] and [[eric-dingwall|Eric Dingwall]] in Munich in May 1922; older brother of [[rudi-schneider|Rudi Schneider]]. Though his brother Rudi plays a larger role in the Borley literature, Willi's own mediumship was the crucial catalyst that first convinced Price of the reality of psychic phenomena.

## Background

Willi Schneider was born in **Braunau, Austria** — also the birthplace of Adolf Hitler — and was one of twelve children [1, 2]. He began giving séances in the **spring of 1919** [1]. While in a trance state, he was guided by a spirit personality called **"Olga"**, who claimed to be **Lola Montez** — the stage name of Eliza Gilbert, a notorious nineteenth-century actress and courtesan who had been the mistress of the King of Bavaria [1, 2]. His reported psychic abilities included telekinesis and materialisation; when transforming into the "Olga" persona, he exhibited rapid breathing, extreme muscle tension, and restlessness [3].

[[albert-von-schrenck-notzing|Dr Albert von Schrenck-Notzing]], a prominent researcher who heard sensational stories about Willi's abilities, visited the Schneider home and persuaded Willi's father to let him take the nineteen-year-old to Munich to be **trained as a dentist** while simultaneously testing his mediumistic skills [2].

## The 1922 Munich Investigation and Harry Price

In **May 1922**, [[eric-dingwall|Dr. Eric J. Dingwall]] (then Director of the Department of Physical Phenomena at the American Society for Psychical Research) invited [[harry-price|Harry Price]] to Munich to investigate Willi, who was actively being tested in Schrenck-Notzing's apartment [4, 5]. After dozens of sittings, including ones in which Willi was bound to a chair with ropes [2], Price was particularly impressed when he observed **Willi's tambourine rattle and saw "ectoplasm" dribble from the boy's mouth** [2]. Both Dingwall and Price were entirely convinced that Willi's phenomena were genuine [2, 6].

Price famously declared: **"Speaking personally, I was deeply impressed, as it was the first medium through whom I had witnessed phenomena under unimpeachable conditions"** [2]. The *American Society for Psychical Research* journal published a feature on this event titled **"The Conversion of Harry Price"** [2]. It was directly upon his return from observing Willi in Munich that Price resolved to establish a laboratory in London — which became the [[national-laboratory|National Laboratory of Psychical Research]] — specifically to test mediums under similar controlled conditions [7].

## Later Career

The sources contain a chronological contradiction regarding when Willi's powers began to fade:

> **Contradiction:** One account states that during a sitting in **1919**, Willi's powers had already started to fade. The spirit guide 'Olga' failed to produce phenomena and explicitly "demanded that Willi's eleven-year-old brother [Rudi] should be a medium," at which point Rudi entered the room sleepwalking and adopted the 'Olga' persona [3]. However, another text notes that "Willi Schneider himself continued as a medium with a new trance personality into the mid-1920s after which his powers ultimately began to fade" [1] — a later timeline supported by the fact that Price and Dingwall successfully tested his materialisation abilities in May 1922.
