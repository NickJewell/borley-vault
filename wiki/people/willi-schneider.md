# Willi Schneider

Austrian medium; the first Schneider brother tested by [[harry-price|Harry Price]] and [[eric-dingwall|Eric Dingwall]] in Munich in May 1922; older brother of [[rudi-schneider|Rudi Schneider]].

## Background

Willi Schneider was nineteen years old when [[albert-von-schrenck-notzing|Dr Albert von Schrenck-Notzing]] heard sensational reports of his psychic abilities from the local press in his hometown of **Braunau**, Austria — also the birthplace of Adolf Hitler. Schrenck-Notzing persuaded Willi's father to allow him to take the boy to Munich and have him trained as a dentist while simultaneously testing his mediumistic skills.

After dozens of sittings, including one in which Willi's hands were bound in ropes, Schrenck-Notzing invited [[harry-price|Harry Price]] and [[eric-dingwall|Eric Dingwall]] to observe. In a trance, Willi was guided by a spirit called **"Olga"** who claimed to be **"Lola Montez"** — the stage name of Eliza Gilbert, a nineteenth-century actress and courtesan who had been the mistress of the King of Bavaria.

## Price's Assessment

Price was "deeply impressed" by Willi, calling it "the first medium through whom I had witnessed phenomena under unimpeachable conditions." His tambourine rattled and "ectoplasm" dribbled from his mouth. The American Society for Psychical Research journal published a feature entitled **'The Conversion of Harry Price'**, claiming he had "found something genuine at last."

This declaration of belief in Willi's authenticity was — temporarily — international news and significantly raised Price's profile.

## Later Career

By around 1919, Willi's powers had begun to fade. During a sitting, "Olga" had demanded that Willi's younger brother Rudi replace him as medium. This transition marked the beginning of [[rudi-schneider|Rudi Schneider]]'s career, which would eventually eclipse Willi's entirely.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Willi Schneider** (1903–1971) was a famous Austrian physical medium and the older brother of the even more celebrated medium, Rudi Schneider [1]. Though his brother Rudi plays a larger role in the Borley Rectory literature due to Harry Price's extensive investigations, Willi's own mediumship was the crucial catalyst that first convinced Price of the reality of psychic phenomena. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the sources reveals the following biographical details, events, quotes, and chronological contradictions regarding Willi Schneider:

**Biographical Details and Early Mediumship**
*   **Origins:** Willi was born in **Braunau, Austria** [2, 3]. He was one of twelve children [1]. 
*   **The Trance Personality:** Willi began giving séances in the **spring of 1919** [1]. While in a trance state, he was guided by a spirit personality called **‘Olga’**, who claimed to be the spirit of **Lola Montez**, a notorious actress, courtesan, and mistress to the King of Bavaria [1, 2]. 
*   **Physical Manifestations:** When transforming into the ‘Olga’ persona, Willi exhibited rapid breathing, extreme muscle tension, and restlessness [3]. His reported psychic abilities included telekinesis and materialisation [2].
*   **Dentistry Training:** Dr. Albert von Schrenck-Notzing, a prominent researcher who heard sensational stories about Willi's abilities, visited the Schneider home in Braunau and persuaded Willi's father to let him take the nineteen-year-old boy under his wing to be **trained as a dentist in Munich** [2].

**The 1922 Munich Investigation and Harry Price**
Willi Schneider's greatest significance to the Borley story lies in his profound impact on the career of ghost hunter Harry Price. 
*   **The Invitation:** In **May 1922**, Dr. Eric J. Dingwall (who was then Director of the Department of Physical Phenomena at the American Society for Psychical Research) invited Harry Price to Munich to investigate Willi, who was actively being tested in Schrenck-Notzing’s apartment [4, 5]. 
*   **The Séance:** During dozens of sittings, Willi was bound to a chair with ropes [2]. Price was particularly impressed when he observed **Willi's tambourine rattle and saw "ectoplasm" dribble from the boy's mouth** [2]. 
*   **The Quote and "Conversion":** Both Dingwall and Price were entirely convinced that Willi’s phenomena were genuine [2, 6]. Price famously declared: **"Speaking personally, I was deeply impressed, as it was the first medium through whom I had witnessed phenomena under unimpeachable conditions"** [2]. The *American Society for Psychical Research* journal even published a feature on this event titled "The Conversion of Harry Price" [2]. 
*   **The Laboratory:** It was directly upon his return from observing Willi in Munich that Price resolved to establish a laboratory in London (which became the National Laboratory of Psychical Research) specifically to test mediums under similar controlled conditions [7].

**Contradictions Regarding the Fading of His Powers**
The sources contain a chronological contradiction regarding exactly when Willi's powers began to wane, leading to the rise of his younger brother Rudi:
*   **The 1919 Claim:** One account states that during a sitting at their home in **1919**, Willi’s powers had already started to fade [3]. The spirit guide ‘Olga’ failed to produce phenomena and explicitly **"demanded that Willi’s eleven-year-old brother [Rudi] should be a medium,"** at which point Rudi entered the room sleepwalking and adopted the 'Olga' persona [3]. 
*   **The Mid-1920s Claim:** In direct contradiction to the idea that his powers faded in 1919, another text notes that **"Willi Schneider himself continued as a medium with a new trance personality into the mid-1920s after which his powers ultimately began to fade"** [1]. This later timeline is also supported by the fact that Price and Dingwall successfully tested Willi's materialisation abilities three years later, in May 1922 [2, 4].
