# Mr and Mrs Tom Gooch

A farmer and a schoolmistress (she taught at a girls' church school in the next village to Borley) who purchased approximately five acres of the former Borley Rectory orchard and garden for £300 — reported in the *Evening News* of March 14, 1946. The purchase was made sometime in 1945–46, after the demolition of the Rectory ruins.

## Their Plans

The Gooches were described by the *Evening News* reporter as "a matter-of-fact couple" who intended to build their home on the land — which included the [[nuns-walk|Nun's Walk]] and the area where the phantom nun was said to have walked for nearly a century.

## Mrs Gooch's Experiences

When questioned closely, Mrs Gooch admitted to having "heard 'noises' and witnessed some rather odd goings-on in the days of the old Rectory." She had never seen the headless coachman. However, when she had attended a church council meeting in the Rectory proper, a picture fell from the wall and "bits of brick and twig came hurtling down the chimney."

## Significance

The Gooches represent the post-war chapter of the Borley story: new, secular, practical owners who had no connection to the haunting mythos, yet even Mrs Gooch acknowledged personal experiences. Their planned house — which would cover the Nun's Walk — provoked speculation from Price about what manifestations, if any, would occur in a new structure on the site. The story of what actually happened on the site after 1946 falls outside Price's published account.
