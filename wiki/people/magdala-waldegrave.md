# Magdala Waldegrave (d.1598)

Third daughter of Sir [[sir-edward-waldegrave|Edward Waldegrave]] and Frances Neville. She married **John Southcote** and died in 1598. Commemorated by a painted memorial tablet on the north wall of the chancel in [[borley-church|Borley Church]].

## The Memorial Tablet

The painted tablet was installed after her death in 1598. It depicts Magdala kneeling in prayer, wearing:
- A flat headdress and ruff
- Tight-fitting bodice and loose skirt
- A sleeveless mantle over her shoulders

The monument is flanked by Ionic columns, with a shield of arms above the cornice.

## Significance

Magdala's tablet provides physical evidence — visible in Borley Church to this day — of the Waldegrave family's intimate connection with the church across generations. The [[waldegrave-monument|Waldegrave monument]] in the nave commemorates her father Sir Edward; her own tablet on the chancel wall commemorates her separately.

[[harry-price|Price]] notes that in addition to the famous altar-tomb of Sir Edward, the church contains Magdala's tablet — establishing that at least two members of the Waldegrave family had permanent memorial presence in Borley Church. The coffins of the family were likely interred in the crypt beneath.
