# Magdala Waldegrave (d.1598)

Third daughter of Sir [[sir-edward-waldegrave|Edward Waldegrave]] and Frances Neville. She married **John Southcote** and died in 1598. Commemorated by a painted memorial tablet on the north wall of the chancel in [[borley-church|Borley Church]].

## The Memorial Tablet

The painted tablet was installed after her death in 1598. It is set into the **north wall of the chancel**, flanked by **Ionic columns**, and features a **shield of arms above the cornice**. Directly above Magdala's head, the tablet depicts **"cherubs encircled with rays of glory."**

Two detailed accounts agree exactly on her Elizabethan attire, describing her as wearing a **flat headdress, a ruff, a tight-fitting bodice, a loose skirt, and a sleeveless mantle** draped over her shoulders.

> **Contradiction:** One text records that the effigy is shown "kneeling in prayer"; another provides the more specific detail that she is "kneeling on a cushion with an open book before her."

## Significance

Magdala's tablet provides physical evidence — visible in [[borley-church|Borley Church]] to this day — of the [[waldegrave-family|Waldegrave]] family's intimate connection with the church across generations. The [[waldegrave-monument|Waldegrave monument]] in the nave commemorates her father Sir Edward; her own tablet on the chancel wall commemorates her separately. [[harry-price|Price]] notes that the church contains at least two permanent Waldegrave memorials — establishing that the family had enduring presence in Borley Church. The coffins of the family were likely interred in the crypt beneath.
