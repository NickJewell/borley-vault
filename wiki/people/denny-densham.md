# Dennis Herbert (Denny) Densham

(Born Putney, 6 March 1921; died South Glamorgan, September 1992) Film cameraman and cinematographer. Worked as a cameraman for the Crown Film Unit and Gaumont British Screen Services during the Second World War, and was Director of Photography on several pictures in the 1950s including *Man of Africa* (1953), *Devil on Horseback* (1954), *Make Me an Offer* (1954) and *Child's Play* (1954).

## Borley Investigations

Densham was interested in ghosts and had previously obtained recordings of alleged phenomena in the haunted squash court at RAF Bircham Newton, broadcast on the Jack de Manio radio show.

In 1972 Densham approached [[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth]] with a request to join his investigations at Borley and subsequently took part in several vigils inside [[borley-church]]. These sessions were recorded; when tape recorders were left running inside the locked, empty church, mysterious sounds including footsteps, clicks, knocks, sinister sighing and grunting sounds were picked up. These recordings are described in detail in [[ghost-of-borley-church|*The Ghost of Borley Church*]].

## The Recordings

The results were later featured in the BBC's *Ghost Hunters* television programme. Densham quoted as saying:

> "Frankly, I am at a loss to explain what goes on at Borley. We made every effort to ensure that our legs weren't being pulled, and the tapes were new and untampered with. No theory I have tried to put forward seems to pan out ... One's left with the feeling that whatever causes the phenomena is indifferent to or perhaps unaware of observers."

## Controversy

When an article by Frank Smyth in *The Unexplained* published in the early 1980s described Densham's experiences, [[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth]] complained that he had not been informed and that the impression given was that Densham had been in charge of proceedings.

[[tony-broughall]] later demonstrated that two of the supposedly paranormal sounds on the Densham recordings — the opening of a phantom door and an eerie sigh — could easily be replicated inside the church, and Croom-Hollingsworth himself subsequently acknowledged that the BBC may have selected sounds made by the investigation party itself.

## Published Work

Densham also produced an audio programme *Is There Anybody There?* on survival after death, issued by Mindpower Records in the mid-1970s, with EVP experiments and séance recordings. The [[ghost-tapes|Ghost Tapes]] CD (originally *The Ghost of Borley Church*) was reissued by Revenant Recordings UK and remains one of the few audio documents associated with the Borley case.
