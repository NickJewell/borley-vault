# Mrs A. G. Wilson

Wife of Mr A. G. Wilson Sr of Chapel House, Long Melford. In the summer of 1949, Mrs Wilson and her 13-year-old sister-in-law [[miss-vivienne-wilson|Miss Vivienne Wilson]] cycled to Borley to see the supposedly haunted Rectory, "having no knowledge that it had been burnt down or that the haunting extended to the church." Finding only ruins, they went to look at [[borley-church|Borley Church]].

As they approached via a side path to the locked church, Mrs Wilson heard organ music before she could speak — whereupon Vivienne immediately said "Can you hear the organ?" Both heard it. They tried both doors, found them locked, and bought a copy of [[rev-ac-henning|Rev. Henning]]'s booklet *Haunted Borley* at a nearby cottage. Opening it at random, they found a photograph of the church with a cross marking the precise location where they had heard the music.

Mrs Wilson wrote a full letter about the experience in February 1950. Her husband confirmed that when the two women returned and told him of their experience, he looked up the *Radio Times* to check whether any organ music had been broadcast at that time — but no such programme was listed for that day and hour.
