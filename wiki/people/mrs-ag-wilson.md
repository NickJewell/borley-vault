# Mrs A. G. Wilson

Wife of Mr A. G. Wilson Sr of Chapel House, [[long-melford|Long Melford]]; in February 1950 she was writing from Dollis Hill Lane, Cricklewood, London. In the summer of 1949 — specifically on a Wednesday around 6:30 p.m. at the end of August — Mrs Wilson and her 13-year-old sister-in-law [[miss-vivienne-wilson|Miss Vivienne Wilson]] cycled to Borley to see the supposedly haunted Rectory, "having no knowledge that it had been burnt down or that the haunting extended to the church." The weather was fair and calm, with no wind.

## The Visit and the Phantom Organ Music

Finding only ruins, they decided to look at [[borley-church|Borley Church]] purely out of general interest. Finding the main door locked, they walked along a small path to a side door.

While on this path, Mrs Wilson heard the distinct sound of an organ playing before she could speak — whereupon Vivienne immediately asked, "Can you hear the organ?" Both heard it. Thinking nothing of it at the time, they continued to the side door, found it also locked, and left the churchyard.

Their perspective changed entirely when they stopped at a nearby cottage and purchased a copy of [[rev-ac-henning|Rev. Henning]]'s 1949 booklet *Haunted Borley*. Opening it at random, they found a photograph of the church marked with a cross indicating the precise location where ghostly organ music had previously been heard — which was the exact position where they had just been standing. They immediately returned to the church, but the building was completely silent.

Mrs Wilson described the visit in a formal report to investigators in **February 1950**:

> "Vivienne and I had cycled to Borley to see the Rectory, having heard that it was haunted, but having no knowledge that it had been burnt down or that the haunting extended to the church. Having discovered only the remains of the Rectory we decided to have a look at the church out of general interest. We went to the main door and found it locked, so we went by a small path to a side door. While on the path I heard the sound of an organ playing but before I could speak my thoughts, Vivienne said, 'Can you hear the organ?' We didn't give it a second thought but carried on to the side door which was also locked..."

## Corroboration and Investigation

When Mrs Wilson and Vivienne returned to Long Melford and recounted their experience to Mr A. G. Wilson Sr, he immediately checked the *Radio Times* to see if any organ music had been broadcast at that time from a nearby house. He discovered that no such programme was listed for that day and hour.

The evidential weight of this account lies in its "blind" quality: both witnesses experienced the specific phenomenon without any prior knowledge or suggestion of it, and only realised its significance retroactively upon discovering the Henning booklet.
