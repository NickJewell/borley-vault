# Mrs A. G. Wilson

Wife of Mr A. G. Wilson Sr of Chapel House, Long Melford. In the summer of 1949, Mrs Wilson and her 13-year-old sister-in-law [[miss-vivienne-wilson|Miss Vivienne Wilson]] cycled to Borley to see the supposedly haunted Rectory, "having no knowledge that it had been burnt down or that the haunting extended to the church." Finding only ruins, they went to look at [[borley-church|Borley Church]].

As they approached via a side path to the locked church, Mrs Wilson heard organ music before she could speak — whereupon Vivienne immediately said "Can you hear the organ?" Both heard it. They tried both doors, found them locked, and bought a copy of [[rev-ac-henning|Rev. Henning]]'s booklet *Haunted Borley* at a nearby cottage. Opening it at random, they found a photograph of the church with a cross marking the precise location where they had heard the music.

Mrs Wilson wrote a full letter about the experience in February 1950. Her husband confirmed that when the two women returned and told him of their experience, he looked up the *Radio Times* to check whether any organ music had been broadcast at that time — but no such programme was listed for that day and hour.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Mrs. A. G. Wilson** (referred to in the texts as the "junior Mrs. Wilson") features in the Borley records as a witness to a highly corroborated incident of unexplained auditory phenomena at Borley Church. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the sources reveals the following biographical details, specific events, dates, and quotes regarding her experience:

**Biographical Details**
*   **Residence:** In February 1950, she was writing from her home at **Dollis Hill Lane, Cricklewood**, London [1]. 
*   **Family:** She was the daughter-in-law of **Mr. A. G. Wilson Sr.**, who lived at Chapel House in the nearby village of Long Melford [2]. She had a thirteen-year-old sister-in-law named **Vivienne Wilson** [2]. 

**The Visit of August 1949**
At the end of **August 1949**, specifically on a Wednesday around 6:30 p.m., Mrs. Wilson and young Vivienne cycled to Borley [1, 2]. The weather was fair and calm, with no wind blowing [2]. 
*   **Lack of Prior Knowledge:** The two women had heard that Borley Rectory was haunted and had gone specifically to see it [3]. However, they had absolutely no idea that the house had already been destroyed by fire, nor were they aware that the haunting allegedly extended to the nearby church [3]. 
*   **The Churchyard:** Discovering only the ruins of the Rectory, they decided to look at the church purely "out of general interest" [3]. Finding the main door locked, they walked along a small path leading to a side door [3].

**The Phantom Organ Music and Realization**
While walking along this side path, Mrs. Wilson heard the distinct sound of an organ playing [3]. Before she could even voice her thoughts, Vivienne asked her, **"Can you hear the organ?"** [3]. 

Thinking nothing of it at the time, they carried on to the side door, found it also locked, and left the churchyard [3]. Their perspective changed entirely a short time later when they stopped at a nearby cottage and purchased a copy of the Rev. A. C. Henning’s 1949 booklet, *Haunted Borley* [3]. 
*   Upon opening the booklet, they found a photograph of the church marked with a cross indicating where ghostly organ music had previously been heard—which was the exact position where they had just been standing [2, 3]. 
*   They immediately returned to the church, but the building was completely silent [2]. 

**Aftermath and Investigation**
When Mrs. Wilson and Vivienne returned to Long Melford and recounted their adventure to Mr. A. G. Wilson Sr., he immediately checked the *Radio Times* to see if any organ music had been broadcast at that time which might have been overheard from a nearby house [2]. He discovered that no such program was listed for that day and hour [2]. 

Realizing the significance of their "blind" encounter—having experienced the specific phenomena without any prior knowledge or suggestion of it—Mrs. Wilson wrote a formal report of the event to investigators in **February 1950** [1]. She provided the following detailed quote summarizing their excursion:
> **"Vivienne and I had cycled to Borley to see the Rectory, having heard that it was haunted, but having no knowledge that it had been burnt down or that the haunting extended to the church. Having discovered only the remains of the Rectory we decided to have a look at the church out of general interest. We went to the main door and found it locked, so we went by a small path to a side door. While on the path I heard the sound of an organ playing but before I could speak my thoughts, Vivienne said, 'Can you hear the organ?' We didn't give it a second thought but carried on to the side door which was also locked..."** [3].
