# Edith May Dytor (later Mrs Wildgoose)

State Registered Nurse (SRN). Employed as nurse-companion at [[borley-rectory]] from approximately April to November 1932 — eight months of continuous residence, longer than almost any of [[harry-price]]'s official observers. She responded to an advertisement in the *Nursing Mirror* placed by [[marianne-foyster]] seeking a nursemaid for the recently adopted baby [[john-emery|John Emery]].

Later became a nurse at Derbyshire Royal Infirmary, where [[trevor-hall]] eventually located and interviewed her in the late 1940s–early 1950s as part of his investigation of the Foyster period.

## Conditions at Borley

Edith arrived to find:

- Baby John was just two weeks old
- [[marianne-foyster|Mrs Foyster]] was attractive and well-educated, a Roman Catholic (rosary under her pillow; fluent French); she was anxious and taking small white pills (Luminal, a barbiturate with side effects including confusion and hallucinations)
- [[lionel-foyster|Mr Foyster]] was much older, crippled with arthritis and with swollen hands; "very tight with money" — meals consisted of rice and macaroni cooked in water; she found him a good and kind man who adored the children
- [[francois-darles|Francois d'Arles]] dominated the household: "manipulative and sly, sometimes cruel, belittling Mrs Foyster." He would claim Marianne was poisoning him with her pills and ask Edith to remove them. Edith neither liked nor trusted him

She saw the [[wall-writings]] immediately on arrival and presumed they could only have been written by Mrs Foyster, as Adelaide was too young to write. The first night she heard limping footsteps outside her room — frightening her — until she realised it was simply [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]]'s arthritic gait.

Contrary to the majority view, she thought it was most likely **Lionel** who was behind the strange activity, not Marianne.

Mrs Wildgoose was also under the impression — based on information supplied by both Marianne and Lionel — that baby John was Marianne's own child, and her professional opinion as an SRN was that the rector's wife had recently given birth. This contributed to the suggestion that the child was the result of Marianne's relationship with d'Arles (see [[john-emery]]).

## Baby John's Death

On 20 September 1932, at only four and a half months old, baby John Emery died of convulsions and protein malnutrition and was buried in an unmarked grave in Borley churchyard.

## Adelaide's Nun Sighting

Once, [[adelaide-foyster]] came downstairs to say there was a lady she didn't recognise on the doorstep. When Edith and Marianne reached the front door, no one was there. When asked to describe the visitor, Adelaide said it was a woman wearing black with something white around her face "like a nun." *(single witness; childhood account)*

## No Paranormal Experience

Mrs Wildgoose gave the SPR a signed statement: she had "no unaccountable experiences during her residence in 'the most haunted house in England'" and was convinced the rectory was not subject to paranormal disturbances. She described [[borley-rectory]] under the Foysters as "a strange household, aptly dominated by Mr d'Arles." She noted that Mrs Foyster "loathed" Borley, and that active preparations for the [[jonquille-et-cie|flower-shop venture]] in London with d'Arles were already under way in 1932.

The SPR notes that her testimony was given "willingly" and that she spoke of her experiences at Borley with complete equanimity. She was not frightened, not credulous, and not suggestible — yet experienced nothing.

## Significance

The presence of an independent professional nurse for eight months in the rectory during 1932 — the period immediately following the peak [[foyster-phenomena]] of 1930–32 and the [[marks-tey-spiritualist-circle|Marks Tey Circle]] visit — with a complete absence of paranormal experience, supports the SPR's hypothesis that the phenomena were dependent on Marianne's presence and willingness to produce them. By April 1932 the phenomena had largely ceased.

Her suspicion that Lionel rather than Marianne was behind the activity is a minority view but worth noting as the professional opinion of someone who lived in the household for eight months.
