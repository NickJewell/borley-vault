# Dr P. E. Ryberg

Dr P. E. Ryberg was one of [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s official observers at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] during the 1937–38 tenancy. He visited on the night of 8 December 1937 with [[r-m-christie|R. M. Christie]] and [[l-g-cooper|L. G. Cooper]].

## Observations

Their report to Price was notable for its thoroughness and scepticism — and for the fact that Price suppressed its key findings:

- "No sounds of unexplained origin were heard."
- "Mice were heard ravaging in some boxes under the table. Gnawed paper was found in these boxes."
- "Mice were seen to scuttle around."

This report was one of several the SPR found in Price's files that had not been quoted in either *The Most Haunted House in England* or *The End of Borley Rectory*, despite Price's claim that "never once has any observer, to my knowledge, mentioned rats." The Ryberg–Christie–Cooper report was among the most explicit in documenting rodent activity.

Ryberg also noted that the house was easily entered via the cellars and the well in the courtyard — a security vulnerability that Colonel Westland had already reported in July and which several other observers independently confirmed.
