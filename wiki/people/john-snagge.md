# John Snagge

BBC broadcaster and official observer during [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s tenancy of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] (1937–38). Snagge was at this time a prominent BBC announcer and sports commentator — his name on Price's observer list, alongside [[sj-de-lotbiniere|S. J. de Lotbinière]] and others from the BBC, reflects the wide public-interest dimension of the Borley investigation.

## Witness to the Wall Writings

Snagge is cited by [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] as a credible witness to the mysterious words and squiggles that appeared on the walls of Borley Rectory [1]. His testimony was considered particularly significant because he witnessed the [[wall-writings|wall writings]] appearing long after the Rev. [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] and [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] had vacated the Rectory, during a time when the building was "empty of all human beings" [1]. This detail was used by investigators to counter the sceptical theory that the messages were exclusively produced by Marianne Foyster. Grouped alongside the philosopher [[cem-joad|Professor C. E. M. Joad]], Snagge reportedly **"insisted... that marks appeared in their presence which could not be explained in terms of normality"** [1].

The sources do not record the exact dates of his visits beyond their inclusion in the 1937–38 tenancy programme, and *Ghosts of Borley* records no specific phenomena against his name. He appears in the list of observers alongside de Lotbinière, Major H. Douglas-Home, Lloyd Williams (BBC Educational Department director), and the television engineer M. Savage.
