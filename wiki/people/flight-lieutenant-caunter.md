# Flight-Lieutenant Caunter

RAF officer and one of [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s official observers during the 1937–38 Borley tenancy. Caunter conducted observational periods at the Rectory alongside [[carter-jonas|Group-Captain R. Carter Jonas]].

## November 1938 Visit

In November 1938, some months after Price's tenancy had ended and the Rectory was empty and unoccupied, Caunter and Carter Jonas were on leave and decided to pay an unannounced visit. They told no one they were going.

Nothing particularly dramatic occurred, but both observers independently reported **"an overpowering smell of incense"** in one of the rooms. The significance lay in the circumstances: the house had been empty for some time, all windows were presumably closed, and there was no mundane source for such an odour.

This account is cited by Price as one of the recurring olfactory phenomena at Borley — lavender, incense, and cooking smells were reported by multiple independent witnesses across different periods of the haunting. The smell of incense in particular carries Catholic associations that Price regarded as "indicators" pointing toward the [[marie-lairre|Marie Lairre]] narrative.

## Séance Participation

Caunter is also recorded as a sitter at the table-tipping séance held at Borley Rectory on the night of October 23–24, 1937, alongside [[roger-glanville|Squadron-Leader R. H. Glanville]], [[alan-cuthbert|Squadron-Leader Alan J. Cuthbert]], and [[mark-kerr-pearse|M. Kerr-Pearse]]. These sessions produced some of the earliest systematic Planchette evidence about the buried nun. See [[communicating-entities]] for the séance records.

> **Contested:** Price's enthusiasm for incense as evidence has been challenged by [[andrew-clarke|Andrew Clarke]], who identified the nearby [[bush-boake-allen|Bush Boake Allen]] essence factory as a plausible source of olfactory phenomena.
