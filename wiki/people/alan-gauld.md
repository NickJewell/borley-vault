# Alan Gauld

**Dr. Alan Gauld (MA, PhD, D.Litt.)** read History and Natural Science at Emmanuel College, Cambridge, where he later became a Research Fellow. King George VI Memorial Fellow at Harvard University (1956–7). Subsequently Reader in Psychology at the University of Nottingham. A long-standing SPR Council member, known as a "wise and knowledgeable" researcher who contributed numerous valuable book reviews and papers to the SPR's *Journal* and *Proceedings* [2]. Following the death of Borley researcher Mrs. Cecil Ryan Baines, Gauld and [[anthony-cornell|Tony Cornell]] were appointed as her Literary Executors [3].

## Published Works

His publications include *The Founders of Psychical Research* (1968), *Human Action and its Psychological Investigation* (1977, with John Shotter), and *Poltergeists* (1979, with [[anthony-cornell|Anthony Cornell]]) — the latter includes an illuminating passage on [[harry-price|Harry Price]] and the Borley haunting in which Gauld states (the relevant section written by him):

> "He is a controversial figure, and accusations of dishonesty have been levelled against him in connection with Borley Rectory and one or two other cases. I can only say here that these allegations appear to me to be based upon very debatable evidence... Price was too fond for the fastidious of sunning his by no means diminutive ego in the light of newspaper publicity. None the less he had every claim to be reckoned an expert in all matters to do with the unmasking of trickery."

He also authored "The Haunting of Abbey House in Cambridge" (*Journal of the SPR*, September 1972) and a careful study of "drop-in" communicators (*Proceedings of the SPR*, July 1971) [2].

## Role in the Borley Case

A long-standing SPR Council member, Gauld did much mediation behind the scenes before [[robert-hastings|Robert Hastings]]' Borley report saw the light of day. He personally interviewed [[charles-sutton|Charles Sutton]] and other people involved in the Borley story.

Gauld never agreed with the conclusion of the 1956 [[the-borley-report|SPR Borley Report]] that Harry Price had definitively cheated at Borley Rectory [2]. When Hastings sought to publish his counter-report in the 1960s, Gauld performed "a lot of mediation behind the scenes" to help the report see the light of day, and Hastings explicitly thanked him in the final publication [2, 7].

## The Sutton Interview (1966)

Gauld's most significant direct contribution to the Borley saga was his crucial **1966 interview with *Daily Mail* journalist Charles Sutton**. Sutton had published an article claiming he had caught Price red-handed faking poltergeist phenomena — specifically throwing stones — at Borley in 1929 [8–10]. On June 6, 1966, Gauld interviewed Sutton on behalf of Hastings to rigorously cross-examine these allegations [11–13].

Through detailed questioning and a follow-up letter sent on June 7, Gauld dismantled the certainty of Sutton's claims:

- **The stone-throwing:** Gauld clarified that Sutton did **not** actually see Price throw any stones or half-bricks [14, 15]. Sutton suggested that Mollie Goldney (a co-author of the 1956 SPR Report who had previously interviewed him) must have "got hold of the wrong end of the stick" regarding his statements [15]. Sutton told Gauld he had never actually seen Price throw stones, but that he might have been "retrospectively influenced" by finding pebbles in his own pocket.
- **Ventriloquism:** Regarding Sutton's claim that Price used ventriloquism to fake ghostly voices, Gauld challenged the logistics, pointing out that "the use of the ventriloquist technique relies on the misdirection of attention," making it difficult to execute under the circumstances [16, 17].

Gauld concluded from the interview that Sutton tried to be reasonable and did not hold a deeply prejudiced view against Price, but ultimately Sutton could not properly substantiate his claims of fraud [13, 14, 18]. This admission, recorded in Gauld's interview notes (published as an appendix to the Hastings Report), significantly undermined the Sutton accusation.

## Defence of Harry Price

Gauld never agreed that [[harry-price|Harry Price]] faked the [[rudi-schneider|Rudi Schneider]] photograph or that it has been proved he cheated at Borley. He firmly dismissed the claims made by SPR member Anita Gregory that Price had deliberately faked his famous exposure photograph of Schneider [2, 19]. His scholarly caution and firsthand interviews with surviving witnesses gave his views particular authority.

## Significance

Gauld represents a rare figure in the Borley literature: a senior SPR researcher with no axe to grind who carefully examined the accusations against Price and found them wanting. His mediation between [[robert-hastings|Robert Hastings]] and the SPR was essential to the 1969 report's publication. The Gauld-Sutton interview notes remain one of the most important primary documents about the founding fraud accusation.
