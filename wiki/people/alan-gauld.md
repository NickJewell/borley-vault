# Alan Gauld

(MA, PhD, D.Litt.) Formerly Reader in Psychology at the University of Nottingham. Read History and Natural Science at Emmanuel College, Cambridge, where he later became Research Fellow. King George VI Memorial Fellow at Harvard University (1956–7).

## Published Works

His publications include *The Founders of Psychical Research* (1968), *Human Action and its Psychological Investigation* (1977, with John Shotter), and *Poltergeists* (1979, with [[anthony-cornell]]) — the latter includes an illuminating passage on [[harry-price]] and the Borley haunting in which Gauld states (the relevant section written by him):

> "He is a controversial figure, and accusations of dishonesty have been levelled against him in connection with Borley Rectory and one or two other cases. I can only say here that these allegations appear to me to be based upon very debatable evidence... Price was too fond for the fastidious of sunning his by no means diminutive ego in the light of newspaper publicity. None the less he had every claim to be reckoned an expert in all matters to do with the unmasking of trickery."

## Role in the Borley Case

A long-standing SPR Council member, Gauld did much mediation behind the scenes before [[robert-hastings]]' Borley report saw the light of day. He personally interviewed [[charles-sutton]] and other people involved in the Borley story.

Crucially, Gauld interviewed Sutton in 1966 — after the [[hastings-report|Hastings Report]] had identified inconsistencies in the original accusation. Sutton told Gauld that he had never actually seen Price throw stones, but that he might have been "retrospectively influenced" by finding pebbles in his own pocket. This admission, recorded in Gauld's interview notes (published as an appendix to the Hastings Report), significantly undermined the Sutton accusation.

Gauld never agreed that [[harry-price]] faked the [[rudi-schneider|Rudi Schneider]] photograph or that it has been proved he cheated at Borley. His scholarly caution and firsthand interviews with surviving witnesses — including Sutton himself — gave his views particular authority.

## Significance

Gauld represents a rare figure in the Borley literature: a senior SPR researcher with no axe to grind who carefully examined the accusations against Price and found them wanting. His mediation between [[robert-hastings]] and the SPR was essential to the 1969 report's publication. The Gauld-Sutton interview notes remain one of the most important primary documents about the founding fraud accusation.
