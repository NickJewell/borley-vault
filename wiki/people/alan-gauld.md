# Alan Gauld

(MA, PhD, D.Litt.) Formerly Reader in Psychology at the University of Nottingham. Read History and Natural Science at Emmanuel College, Cambridge, where he later became Research Fellow. King George VI Memorial Fellow at Harvard University (1956–7).

## Published Works

His publications include *The Founders of Psychical Research* (1968), *Human Action and its Psychological Investigation* (1977, with John Shotter), and *Poltergeists* (1979, with [[anthony-cornell]]) — the latter includes an illuminating passage on [[harry-price]] and the Borley haunting in which Gauld states (the relevant section written by him):

> "He is a controversial figure, and accusations of dishonesty have been levelled against him in connection with Borley Rectory and one or two other cases. I can only say here that these allegations appear to me to be based upon very debatable evidence... Price was too fond for the fastidious of sunning his by no means diminutive ego in the light of newspaper publicity. None the less he had every claim to be reckoned an expert in all matters to do with the unmasking of trickery."

## Role in the Borley Case

A long-standing SPR Council member, Gauld did much mediation behind the scenes before [[robert-hastings]]' Borley report saw the light of day. He personally interviewed [[charles-sutton]] and other people involved in the Borley story.

Crucially, Gauld interviewed Sutton in 1966 — after the [[hastings-report|Hastings Report]] had identified inconsistencies in the original accusation. Sutton told Gauld that he had never actually seen Price throw stones, but that he might have been "retrospectively influenced" by finding pebbles in his own pocket. This admission, recorded in Gauld's interview notes (published as an appendix to the Hastings Report), significantly undermined the Sutton accusation.

Gauld never agreed that [[harry-price]] faked the [[rudi-schneider|Rudi Schneider]] photograph or that it has been proved he cheated at Borley. His scholarly caution and firsthand interviews with surviving witnesses — including Sutton himself — gave his views particular authority.

## Significance

Gauld represents a rare figure in the Borley literature: a senior SPR researcher with no axe to grind who carefully examined the accusations against Price and found them wanting. His mediation between [[robert-hastings]] and the SPR was essential to the 1969 report's publication. The Gauld-Sutton interview notes remain one of the most important primary documents about the founding fraud accusation.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Dr. Alan Gauld (MA, PhD, D.Litt.)** was a distinguished British academic, a long-standing Council Member of the Society for Psychical Research (SPR), and a meticulous parapsychologist who played a crucial role in defending the legacy of Harry Price. 

**Academic and Professional Background**
Alan Gauld read History and Natural Science at Emmanuel College, Cambridge, where he later became a Research Fellow [1]. In 1956–1957, he served as a King George VI Memorial Fellow at Harvard University, and he subsequently became a Reader in Psychology at the University of Nottingham [1]. A respected investigator of poltergeist activity, Gauld was known as a "wise and knowledgeable" researcher who contributed numerous valuable book reviews and papers to the SPR's *Journal* and *Proceedings* [2]. Following the death of Borley researcher Mrs. Cecil Ryan Baines, Gauld and Tony Cornell were appointed as her Literary Executors [3].

**Published Works**
Gauld authored and co-authored several important texts, including:
*   ***The Founders of Psychical Research* (1968)** [1].
*   ***Human Action and its Psychological Investigation* (1977)**, written with John Shotter [1].
*   ***Poltergeists* (1979)**, co-authored with Tony Cornell, which contained an illuminating defense of Harry Price's work [1, 4, 5].
*   **"The Haunting of Abbey House in Cambridge" (*Journal of the SPR*, September 1972)**, which was the first reliable report on the Abbey House haunting [2, 6].
*   **A careful study of "drop-in" communicators (*Proceedings of the SPR*, July 1971)** [2].

**Involvement in the Borley Rectory Case & The Sutton Interview**
Gauld never agreed with the conclusion of the highly critical 1956 SPR *Borley Report* that Harry Price had definitively cheated at Borley Rectory [2]. When Robert J. Hastings sought to publish his counter-report defending Price in the 1960s, Gauld performed "a lot of mediation behind the scenes" to help the report see the light of day, and Hastings explicitly thanked Gauld for his assistance in the final publication [2, 7]. 

Gauld's most significant direct contribution to the Borley saga was **his crucial 1966 interview with *Daily Mail* journalist Charles Sutton**. Sutton had previously published an article claiming he had caught Price red-handed faking poltergeist phenomena (specifically throwing stones) at Borley in 1929 [8-10]. On June 6, 1966, Gauld interviewed Sutton on behalf of Hastings to rigorously cross-examine these allegations [11-13]. 

Through detailed questioning and a follow-up letter sent to Sutton on June 7, Gauld dismantled the certainty of the journalist's claims:
*   **The Stone-Throwing:** Gauld clarified that Sutton **did *not* actually see Price throw any stones or half-bricks** [14, 15]. Sutton suggested that Mollie Goldney (a co-author of the 1956 SPR *Borley Report* who had previously interviewed him) must have "got hold of the wrong end of the stick" regarding his statements [15]. 
*   **Ventriloquism:** Regarding Sutton's claim that Price used ventriloquism to fake ghostly voices, Gauld astutely challenged the logistics of this, pointing out to Sutton that "the use of the ventriloquist technique relies on the misdirection of attention," making it difficult to execute under the circumstances [16, 17]. 

Gauld concluded from the interview that Sutton tried to be reasonable and did not hold a deeply prejudiced view against Price, but ultimately, Sutton could not properly substantiate his claims of fraud [13, 14, 18]. 

**Defense of Harry Price**
Gauld remained steadfast in his belief that the accusations of dishonesty leveled against Price were built upon **"very debatable evidence"** [5]. In his 1979 book *Poltergeists*, he defended Price, though he candidly acknowledged Price's character flaws, writing that Price was "an exceptionally able man, [but] was too fond for the fastidious of summing his by no means diminutive ego in the light of newspaper publicity" [5]. 

Beyond Borley, Gauld also defended Price in other controversies; for instance, he firmly dismissed the claims made by SPR member Anita Gregory that Price had deliberately faked his famous exposure photograph of the physical medium Rudi Schneider [2, 19].
