# Edna Tower

Sister of [[adelaide-foyster|Adelaide Tower Cartwright]] (formerly Barbara Tower); one of the seven Tower children left orphaned in 1928–29 when their parents [[alice-tower|Alice]] and [[seward-tower|Seward Tower]] died in succession.

## Family Tragedy and Separation

Edna was one of seven children born to Seward and Alice Tower, a farming family from the isolated rural village of Westcock, New Brunswick, Canada [1]. In January 1929, the family suffered a double tragedy: their mother died of a brain tumour; just a month later, their father fractured his skull and died after falling off a load of marsh hay on his farm [1]. The infant Barbara (Adelaide) was taken in by the Foysters; Edna and her siblings (Wallace, Fred, Royal, and Merton) were "parcelled out among their various aunts and uncles" [1].

## Marianne's Deception and Suppressed Contact

A state of "very bad feelings" existed between the Tower family and [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] [2]. Marianne actively deceived her adopted daughter, telling Adelaide she was an only child and deliberately concealing the fact that she had blood relatives in North America [2]. Despite the separation, Edith wrote letters to Adelaide regularly — but **Marianne forced Edna to refer to herself in these letters only as a "cousin, not a sister"**, suppressing the true family connection [3].

[[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] had instructed Adelaide to treat Edna as a cousin, not a sister. The correspondence continued until [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]]'s death in 1945, after which Marianne moved to Ipswich and eventually left for America. The letters stopped, and the last one Edna sent was returned marked "Address Unknown." For twenty-nine years the Tower siblings heard nothing [3].

## Role in Adelaide's Rediscovery

When [[paulene-mitchell|Paulene Mitchell]] wrote to a New Brunswick newspaper in 1976 in search of information about Adelaide, Edna was the one who responded. Within a couple of weeks Mitchell was put in contact with her [2]. Edna explained to Paulene that:

1. Adelaide had very unhappy memories of her life at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] and did not want to talk about it.
2. There were very bad feelings between the Tower family and Marianne.
3. Marianne had never told Adelaide that she had a family of blood relatives in North America.

Edna's response to Paulene's letter set in motion the process that eventually led to [[wallace-tower|Wallace Tower]] finding Adelaide in 1974.
