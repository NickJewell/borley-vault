# Katie (Foysters' Maid)

A young maid employed by the Foysters at Borley Rectory, named only as "Katie" in the sources. She had been in the household "only a week or so" when she witnessed one of the most dramatic poltergeist incidents of the entire Foyster period. Listed by name in both Appendix D and Appendix E of *The Most Haunted House in England*.

## The November 13 Incident

On the evening of Friday, November 13, 1931, [[dom-richard-whitehouse]] was staying at the Rectory while [[lionel-foyster]] was in London on business. Katie, [[marianne-foyster]], and Dom Richard had taken refuge in the kitchen with doors and windows closed.

Dom Richard describes what Katie witnessed alongside him and Marianne:

> "Without warning of any kind, there was a sudden crash right underneath her chair, a bottle bursting into fragments on the stone floor beneath her. A quarter of an hour or so later, precisely the same thing happened to me, as I was drawing up a small chair to sit down to my supper. Katie was kept busy sweeping up the broken glass and looked quite mystified, having been in the house only a week or so."

Most significantly, all three witnesses together — Katie, Marianne, and Dom Richard — saw a bottle rise into the air:

> "Suddenly, before our very eyes, a bottle poised itself in mid-air within a foot or so of the kitchen ceiling. It remained here for a second or two and then fell with a crash on the floor before us. I repeat that all three of us witnessed this incident."

Dom Richard describes the sequence: a bottle rolled from the top of the back stairs into the kitchen without breaking; small china struck his shoulder; more objects were thrown; the bells in the passage rang repeatedly; and the large yard bell also rang out sharply.

## Significance

Katie is a key witness because she was a complete newcomer to the household with no prior investment in the haunting's history, and her reaction — "quite mystified" — is consistent with genuine novelty. The bottle-in-mid-air incident is one of the few Borley phenomena witnessed simultaneously by three independent named persons, all of whom are on record.

She also appears in Appendix D as a witness to bell-ringing, footsteps, and knocks — indicating her exposure to phenomena extended beyond this one dramatic night.

> **Note:** Only her first name is recorded. Her surname, age beyond "young," and later history are unknown from the MHH source.
