# Henry Samuel Foyster

Anglican clergyman; rector of All Saints Church, Hastings; father of [[caroline-bull|Caroline Foyster]] (who married [[henry-bull|Rev. Henry Dawson Bull]]) and grandfather of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]] through his son [[rev-george-alfred-foyster|George Alfred Foyster]].

## Background

Henry Samuel Foyster was rector of All Saints, Hastings during a period when the Bull and Foyster families were close. His son George was a contemporary of Henry Dawson Bull, and the two families paid each other regular visits. It was while staying with the Foysters in Hastings that Henry Dawson Bull became acquainted with George's younger sister Caroline, whom he subsequently married.

## Significance for the Borley Story

Henry Samuel Foyster is the critical ancestral link between the two clerical dynasties whose intersection created the Borley haunting. Through his daughter Caroline's marriage to [[henry-bull|Henry Bull]], and through his son George's later appointment of his own son Lionel to the Borley living, both the creation and the most dramatic period of the Borley haunting trace directly back to Henry Samuel Foyster's pastoral network.

The pattern of the Foysters and Bulls sharing the same ecclesiastical circle had its roots in Henry Samuel's friendship with [[rev-edward-bull-pentlow|Rev. Edward Bull of Pentlow]].

His son George Alfred Foyster served as rector of All Saints, Hastings for forty-two years (see [[rev-george-alfred-foyster]]), and was named as executor of [[henry-bull|Henry Dawson Bull]]'s will.
