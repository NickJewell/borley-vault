# Henry Samuel Foyster

Anglican clergyman; rector of All Saints Church, Hastings; father of [[caroline-bull|Caroline Foyster]] (who married [[henry-bull|Rev. Henry Dawson Bull]]) and grandfather of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]] through his son [[rev-george-alfred-foyster|George Alfred Foyster]].

## Education and Early Career

According to the *Alumni Cantabrigienses*, Henry Samuel Foyster matriculated at **Queen's College, Cambridge, at Michaelmas, 1811** [1]. By **1841**, he was serving as the **'Lecturer at St Clement's and All Saints'** in Hastings [1].

## Rectorship at Hastings

In **1849**, he officially became the **Rector of All Saints', Hastings** [1]. The sources record that he presumably received this appointment at the instance of his older brother, John Foyster [1]. John was over ten years senior to Henry Samuel, served as the Rector of St Clement's until 1855, and was the patron of the All Saints' living [1].

Henry Samuel Foyster served as Rector of All Saints' until his death on **July 6, 1862** [1]. He left behind two sons who followed him into the priesthood and continued the family's local dominance over the Hastings parishes:

- His elder son, the **Rev. Henry Brereton Foyster** (born 1834), took over from his uncle John as the Rector and patron of St Clement's [2].
- His younger son, the **[[rev-george-alfred-foyster|Rev. George Alfred Foyster]]** (Lionel Foyster's father), directly succeeded Henry Samuel as the Rector and patron of All Saints' from 1862 to 1904 [2].

## Significance for the Borley Story

Henry Samuel Foyster is the critical ancestral link between the two clerical dynasties whose intersection created the Borley haunting. Through his daughter Caroline's marriage to [[henry-bull|Henry Bull]], and through his son George's later appointment of his own son [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] to the Borley living, both the creation and the most dramatic period of the Borley haunting trace directly back to Henry Samuel Foyster's pastoral network.

It was while staying with the Foysters in Hastings that Henry Dawson Bull became acquainted with George's younger sister Caroline, whom he subsequently married. The pattern of the Foysters and Bulls sharing the same ecclesiastical circle had its roots in Henry Samuel's friendship with [[rev-edward-bull-pentlow|Rev. Edward Bull of Pentlow]].

His son George Alfred Foyster served as rector of All Saints, Hastings for forty-two years (see [[rev-george-alfred-foyster]]), and was named as executor of [[henry-bull|Henry Dawson Bull]]'s will.
