# Henry Samuel Foyster

Anglican clergyman; rector of All Saints Church, Hastings; father of [[caroline-bull|Caroline Foyster]] (who married [[henry-bull|Rev. Henry Dawson Bull]]) and grandfather of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]] through his son [[rev-george-alfred-foyster|George Alfred Foyster]].

## Background

Henry Samuel Foyster was rector of All Saints, Hastings during a period when the Bull and Foyster families were close. His son George was a contemporary of Henry Dawson Bull, and the two families paid each other regular visits. It was while staying with the Foysters in Hastings that Henry Dawson Bull became acquainted with George's younger sister Caroline, whom he subsequently married.

## Significance for the Borley Story

Henry Samuel Foyster is the critical ancestral link between the two clerical dynasties whose intersection created the Borley haunting. Through his daughter Caroline's marriage to [[henry-bull|Henry Bull]], and through his son George's later appointment of his own son Lionel to the Borley living, both the creation and the most dramatic period of the Borley haunting trace directly back to Henry Samuel Foyster's pastoral network.

The pattern of the Foysters and Bulls sharing the same ecclesiastical circle had its roots in Henry Samuel's friendship with [[rev-edward-bull-pentlow|Rev. Edward Bull of Pentlow]].

His son George Alfred Foyster served as rector of All Saints, Hastings for forty-two years (see [[rev-george-alfred-foyster]]), and was named as executor of [[henry-bull|Henry Dawson Bull]]'s will.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Reverend Henry Samuel Foyster** appears in the Borley sources as the paternal grandfather of the Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster (the prominent Borley rector from 1930 to 1935) and the patriarch of a Victorian ecclesiastical dynasty centered in Hastings [1, 2]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific biographical details and dates regarding his life:

**Education and Early Career**
According to the *Alumni Cantabrigienses*, Henry Samuel Foyster matriculated at **Queen’s College, Cambridge, at Michaelmas, 1811** [1]. By **1841**, he was serving as the **‘Lecturer at St Clement’s and All Saints’** in Hastings [1].

**Rectorship at Hastings**
In **1849**, he officially became the **Rector of All Saints’, Hastings** [1]. The sources record the lesser-known biographical detail that he presumably received this appointment at the instance of his older brother, John Foyster [1]. John was over ten years senior to Henry Samuel, served as the Rector of St Clement’s until 1855, and was the patron of the All Saints' living [1]. 

**Death and Clerical Legacy**
Henry Samuel Foyster served as the Rector of All Saints' until his death on **July 6, 1862** [1]. 

He left behind two sons who followed him into the priesthood and continued the family's local dominance over the Hastings parishes:
*   His elder son, the **Rev. Henry Brereton Foyster** (born 1834), took over from his uncle John as the Rector and patron of St Clement’s [2].
*   His younger son, the **Rev. George Alfred Foyster** (Lionel Foyster's father), directly succeeded Henry Samuel as the Rector and patron of All Saints' from 1862 to 1904 [2].
