# Prof. John Gerald Taylor

(Born 1931) Professor of Mathematics at King's College, University of London. Author of several books including *Black Holes: The End of the Universe* (1973) and *Science and the Supernatural* (1980). Interested in scientifically recording paranormal activity [1].

## Borley Church Investigation (June 1976)

In **early June 1976**, Taylor and a technical research assistant visited [[borley-church|Borley Church]] to conduct sound-recording and other scientific experiments [1, 2]. Their equipment included a **magnetometer** — a machine to record the strength and direction of magnetic fields — and other sophisticated apparatus [1].

While the investigation was ultimately deemed "not very rewarding," Taylor and his assistant did record a few anomalies [1]. During their time in the church, they experienced sudden **"sensations of coldness and unease"** that they could not explain, and heard some **"unexpected sounds of movement"** which they rationally theorised might be due to the shifting of the church's physical foundations [1].

At the end of the investigation, Taylor decided that **"no psychic force or presence manifested itself"** during their visit, but felt that **"more work of this type could well be rewarding"** — a conclusion notable coming from a Professor of Mathematics [1]. A full account of the Borley visit is included in [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]]'s book *Hauntings* [1].

## Significance

Taylor's investigation represents the kind of systematic scientific approach that [[anthony-cornell|Anthony Cornell]] had proposed in 1954 (before his investigation was cancelled). The magnetometer work at Borley Church is consistent with the [[ley-lines-at-borley|ley-lines hypothesis]] explored by [[edward-babbs|Edward Babbs]] in the *Final Analysis* — both approaches attempt to find measurable physical correlates for the reported phenomena.

## The BBC Broadcast (July 1976)

Shortly after his site visit, Taylor participated in a one-hour BBC World Service radio programme entitled *Ghosts*, broadcast on **31 July 1976** (and repeated 1 August) [1, 3]. He specifically discussed his recording experiments at Borley, appearing alongside Peter Underwood, the S.P.R. President Prof. Arthur Ellison, and Dr. Jonathan Miller [3].
