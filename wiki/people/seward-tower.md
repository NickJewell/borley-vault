# Seward Tower

Farmer at Westcock, New Brunswick; husband of [[alice-tower|Alice Tower]] and father of the seven Tower children, including the infant Barbara who became [[adelaide-foyster|Adelaide Foyster]].

## Background

Seward Tower worked a farm at **Westcock**, approximately 8 kilometres from Sackville. His wife [[alice-tower|Alice]] died of a brain tumour in January 1928, leaving him with seven young children. A month later, Seward fell off a load of marsh hay on his farm and **fractured his skull**, leaving the children effectively orphaned.

The children — including Wallace, Edna, Fred, Royal, Merton, and the infant Barbara — were to be parcelled out among their various aunts and uncles. When the Foysters were asked if they would take the infant Barbara, they agreed and renamed her [[adelaide-foyster|Adelaide]].

## Significance

Seward Tower's double tragedy — widowed and then incapacitated within the space of a month — was what caused his youngest child to be removed from the family and placed with [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] and [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]. This adoption brought Adelaide into [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] and made her one of the youngest witnesses to the haunting.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Seward Tower** appears in the Borley Rectory sources exclusively in connection to his biological daughter, Adelaide, who became the adopted daughter of the Rev. Lionel and Marianne Foyster. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following biographical details and events surrounding his life and family:

*   **Family Background:** Seward Tower lived in or near West Sackville, New Brunswick, Canada, with his wife, Alice [1]. Together they had seven children, including Edna, Fred, and Wallace Tower [1, 2]. Their youngest child, originally named Barbara Alice Tower, was born on **March 20, 1928**, and baptized on **March 3, 1929** [1]. 
*   **Tragic Death:** Seward Tower's life ended prematurely when he was **killed in an agricultural accident** [1]. His wife, Alice, also died in childbirth, leaving their children orphaned [1]. 
*   **The Adoption of his Youngest Daughter:** Following the deaths of Seward and Alice, their youngest child, Barbara Alice, was seemingly adopted in Canada by the Rev. Lionel Foyster and his wife Marianne [1]. The child was renamed Adelaide and brought to Borley Rectory by the Foysters in October 1931 [1].
*   **Marianne's Deception Regarding the Tower Family:** Following Seward's death, his other children were separated from their youngest sister [2]. While his daughter Edna Tower wrote to Adelaide regularly, **Marianne Foyster strictly forced Edna to refer to herself only as a "cousin"** rather than a sister [2]. As a result, Adelaide was raised believing she was an only child and remained entirely ignorant of her true biological parents and siblings [2]. 
*   **The Tower Family Reunion:** When Lionel Foyster died in 1945, the letters from the surviving Tower siblings were returned marked "Address Unknown," and the family lost contact with Adelaide for twenty-nine years, though her brother Fred noted that it "really bothered us" [2]. It was not until **November 1974** that Seward's son, Wallace Tower (then living in Chepachet, Rhode Island), managed to telephone Adelaide and inform the speechless woman that she was his sister [2]. Ghost Club member Alan Roper is also credited in the sources with helping to trace Adelaide and reunite her with her true relatives in America [1].
