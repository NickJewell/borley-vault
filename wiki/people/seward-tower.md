# Seward Tower

Farmer who lived in or near **West Sackville, New Brunswick**, Canada, with his wife [[alice-tower|Alice]] [1]. Together they had seven children, including Edna, Fred, Wallace, and their youngest child, originally named Barbara Alice Tower, born on **March 20, 1928**, and baptized on **March 3, 1929** [1] — who would become [[adelaide-foyster|Adelaide Foyster]].

## Background

Seward Tower worked a farm at **Westcock**, approximately 8 kilometres from Sackville. His wife [[alice-tower|Alice]] died — reportedly of a brain tumour — in early 1928, leaving him with seven young children. A month later, Seward was killed in an agricultural accident when he fell off a load of marsh hay on his farm and **fractured his skull**, leaving the children effectively orphaned [1].

> **Contradiction:** One source states Alice died in childbirth rather than of a brain tumour [1]. The specific cause of Seward's own death is variously described as a fall from hay (the more detailed account) or simply as a farm accident.

The children — including Wallace, Edna, Fred, Royal, and Merton — were parcelled out among their various aunts and uncles. When the Foysters were asked if they would take the infant Barbara, they agreed and renamed her [[adelaide-foyster|Adelaide]], bringing her to [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] in October 1931.

## The Deception and Reunion

Following Seward's death, his other children were separated from their youngest sister. While Seward's daughter Edna Tower wrote to Adelaide regularly, **[[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] strictly forced Edna to refer to herself only as a "cousin"** rather than a sister [2]. As a result, Adelaide was raised believing she was an only child and remained entirely ignorant of her true biological parents and siblings [2].

When [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] died in 1945, the letters from the surviving Tower siblings were returned marked "Address Unknown," and the family lost contact with Adelaide for twenty-nine years. It was not until **November 1974** that Seward's son, Wallace Tower (then living in Chepachet, Rhode Island), managed to telephone Adelaide and inform the speechless woman that he was her brother [2]. Ghost Club member [[alan-roper|Alan Roper]] is also credited with helping to trace Adelaide and reunite her with her true relatives in America [1].

## Significance

Seward Tower's double tragedy — widowed and then incapacitated within the space of a month — was what caused his youngest child to be removed from the family and placed with the Foysters. This adoption brought Adelaide into [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] and made her one of the youngest witnesses to the haunting.
