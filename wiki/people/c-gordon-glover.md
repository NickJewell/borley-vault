# C. Gordon Glover (and Mrs. C. Gordon Glover)

BBC official observer; visited Borley Rectory with his wife and [[lloyd-williams|Mr. and Mrs. Lloyd Williams]] on February 18, 1938. Glover submitted the written report for the party. During their visit, Mrs. Lloyd Williams reported the most significant BBC-period nun sighting, and both Glovers independently witnessed auditory phenomena.

## The Visit

The party arrived at 4:30 p.m. and conducted a thorough inspection of the house. They then positioned themselves in the large summer-house to watch the [[nuns-walk]].

At approximately 6:10 p.m., while the Glovers were watching one section of the Walk, Mrs. Lloyd Williams "suddenly tensed" and — after ten seconds of silence — declared she had "distinctly seen a round, dark object." (See [[lloyd-williams]] for the full account.)

**Mrs. C. Gordon Glover** heard a door quietly close in the house "while standing outside the Chapel" during the initial inspection — "a dead still afternoon, and all doors and windows were shut."

At about 11 p.m., while both Glovers were standing in the doorway of the Chapel:

> "Both of us heard coming from downstairs a dull, heavy thud followed by a short shuffle. The other two of the party were at the back of the Chapel and heard nothing."

At 12:30 a.m. the whole party in the Blue Room collectively heard: two heavy thuds and a lighter thud; "a kind of vague shuffling, 'giving an impression of general activity of some kind going on'"; and "a staccato and isolated crack like an electric light switch being snapped on." These were independently witnessed by all four observers.

Glover also discovered a new pencil mark during the visit — ringed as a new apparition.

## Report

Glover's written report is dated February 26, 1938 — written up shortly after the visit from notes made at the time. He concludes:

> "One witness only whose faith that she saw something is not to be shaken. We have since, as you know, ascertained that this 'Nun,' when seen, has always been observed at this particular spot."

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**C. Gordon Glover** (a BBC producer) and his wife, **Honor Glover**, were among the official observers recruited by Harry Price during his year-long tenancy of Borley Rectory in 1937–1938 [1-3]. Despite experiencing several inexplicable auditory phenomena during their stay, Glover emerged as a highly critical and skeptical voice whose reports and correspondence directly challenged Price's conclusions and the integrity of other witnesses [4, 5]. 

A thorough reference profile based on the Borley sources reveals the following facts, dates, quotes, and contradictions regarding their involvement:

**The Visit of February 18, 1938, and Auditory Phenomena**
The Glovers visited the Rectory on Friday, February 18, 1938, accompanied by Mr. Lloyd Williams (a Director of the BBC Educational Department) and his wife [3]. During this visit, the Glovers personally experienced and reported several phenomena:
*   **The Phantom Door:** Between 4:30 and 5:00 p.m., while standing outside the Chapel, Mrs. Glover declared she heard a door downstairs quietly close [6]. Glover noted that it was a "dead still afternoon, and all doors and windows were shut" [6]. 
*   **The Heavy Thud:** Around 11:00 p.m., while the Glovers were standing in the doorway of the Chapel, they both heard a "dull, heavy thud followed by a short shuffle" coming from downstairs [6]. Their companions, who were at the back of the Chapel, heard nothing [6].
*   **The Whip-like Crack:** Between 12:30 a.m. and 1:00 a.m., while all four observers were stationed in the darkness of the Blue Room, they agreed on hearing two heavy thuds from downstairs, a lighter thud, and a "vague shuffling" suggesting "general activity of some kind going on" [7]. This was followed by a staccato "crack"; Glover described the sound as being "like the crack of a whip," while the others likened it to an electric light switch being snapped on [7]. 

**Skepticism and the "Imagined" Footsteps**
Despite their own experiences, Glover was highly critical of his fellow observers. When Mrs. Lloyd Williams claimed to have heard "six quick, young footsteps" in the passage outside the scullery, Glover bluntly dismissed the claim in his official report, writing: **"It is our opinion that these steps were imagined"** [4, 6]. 

In his February 26, 1938 letter to Price enclosing his report, Glover expressed his severe doubts about the reliability of witness testimony in a haunted house setting [4, 6]. He provided a memorable and insightful quote regarding the psychology of ghost-hunting: 
> **"The tendency to exaggerate phenomena, be it ever so slightly, we have found to be a dangerous and facile one—even when talking since among our four selves we have found one another guilty from time to time of making fairly sensational statements which were based upon a slender modicum of truth"** [8].

**Challenging Price Over Marianne Foyster**
Glover annoyed Price considerably by stating that he regarded the overall evidence for the haunting at Borley Rectory as "flimsy" [2, 4]. Glover specifically targeted the famous "Foyster phenomena," taking a strong psychological angle. In a letter to Price dated February 26, 1938, he questioned Marianne Foyster's state of mind:
> **"Most of them appear to have come to his [Rev. Foyster's] notice by report from her - i.e. monstrosities in particular - and I do not think it impossible that a woman of highly-strung and nervous character could not only imagine things happening but even go so far as to reproduce them herself"** [2, 5].

Price high-handedly dismissed Glover's concerns [9]. In his reply on February 28, Price asserted that Marianne struck him as "particularly self-possessed and normal" (contradicting his own private earlier beliefs that she was responsible for the fraud), and told Glover that the Foysters played "a very small part - so far as we are concerned - in the Borley story" [10, 11]. 

**Contradictions in the Sources: The "Nun" Sighting**
During their visit, Mrs. Lloyd Williams also claimed to have seen a "round dark object" or "short, stooping figure" on the Nun's Walk while the party waited in the summer-house at dusk [4, 12]. The Borley sources contain a specific contradiction regarding how Glover treated this sighting:
*   The skeptical Society for Psychical Research (SPR) authors (Dingwall, Goldney, and Hall) later used Glover's report to attack Price. They argued that Glover attached *no importance* to Mrs. Williams's sighting, attributing it to bad lighting and the psychological suggestion planted by Price's *Blue Book of Instructions* [4]. They criticized Price for sensationalizing the event in his book under the caption "THE 'NUN' SEEN AGAIN," despite Glover's dismissive attitude [4].
*   However, investigator Robert J. Hastings later contradicted the SPR authors' assessment. Hastings pointed out that Glover *did* take the summer-house incident seriously enough to investigate it immediately by conducting visibility tests [13]. Far from dismissing it outright, Glover concluded his report on the matter by noting that the figure was seen in the exact spot the Nun was traditionally observed, stating: **"I give this whole story wholly without comment"** [14]. Hastings argued that the SPR authors misrepresented Glover's level of dismissal to suit their own anti-Price narrative [14].
