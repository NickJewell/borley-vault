# C. Gordon Glover (and Mrs. C. Gordon Glover)

BBC producer; official observer who visited Borley Rectory with his wife Honor Glover and [[lloyd-williams|Mr. and Mrs. Lloyd Williams]] on February 18, 1938 [1-3]. Glover submitted the written report for the party. Despite experiencing several inexplicable auditory phenomena during their stay, Glover emerged as a highly critical and sceptical voice whose correspondence directly challenged [[harry-price|Price]]'s conclusions and the reliability of other witnesses.

## The Visit of February 18, 1938

The party arrived at 4:30 p.m. and conducted a thorough inspection of the house. They then positioned themselves in the large summer-house to watch the [[nuns-walk]].

**The Phantom Door.** Between 4:30 and 5:00 p.m., while **Mrs. C. Gordon Glover** was standing outside the Chapel, she heard a door downstairs quietly close [6]. Glover noted that it was "a dead still afternoon, and all doors and windows were shut" [6].

**The Nun Sighting.** At approximately 6:10 p.m., while the Glovers were watching one section of the Walk, Mrs. Lloyd Williams "suddenly tensed" and — after ten seconds of silence — declared she had "distinctly seen a round, dark object." (See [[lloyd-williams]] for the full account.)

**The Heavy Thud.** At about 11 p.m., while both Glovers were standing in the doorway of the Chapel, they both heard "a dull, heavy thud followed by a short shuffle" coming from downstairs [6]. Their companions, who were at the back of the Chapel, heard nothing [6].

**The Whip-like Crack.** At 12:30 a.m., while all four observers were in the Blue Room in darkness, they collectively heard two heavy thuds and a lighter thud; "a kind of vague shuffling, 'giving an impression of general activity of some kind going on'"; and what Glover described as "like the crack of a whip," while others likened it to an electric light switch being snapped on [7]. These sounds were independently witnessed by all four observers.

Glover also discovered a new pencil mark during the visit — ringed as a new apparition.

## Report

Glover's written report is dated February 26, 1938. He concludes regarding Mrs. Lloyd Williams's sighting:

> "One witness only whose faith that she saw something is not to be shaken. We have since, as you know, ascertained that this 'Nun,' when seen, has always been observed at this particular spot."

Listed in Appendix D under "Clicks or Cracks," "Sound of Door Closing," and "Knocks, Bumps, Thuds."

## Scepticism and the "Imagined" Footsteps

Despite their own experiences, Glover was highly critical of fellow observers. When Mrs. Lloyd Williams claimed to have heard "six quick, young footsteps" in the passage outside the scullery, Glover bluntly dismissed the claim in his official report, writing: **"It is our opinion that these steps were imagined"** [4, 6].

His February 26, 1938 letter to Price offered a memorable observation regarding the psychology of ghost-hunting:
> **"The tendency to exaggerate phenomena, be it ever so slightly, we have found to be a dangerous and facile one—even when talking since among our four selves we have found one another guilty from time to time of making fairly sensational statements which were based upon a slender modicum of truth"** [8].

## Challenging Price Over Marianne Foyster

Glover annoyed Price considerably by stating that he regarded the overall evidence for the haunting as "flimsy" [2, 4]. He took a strong psychological angle on the Foyster period, writing:
> **"Most of them appear to have come to his [Rev. Foyster's] notice by report from her - i.e. monstrosities in particular - and I do not think it impossible that a woman of highly-strung and nervous character could not only imagine things happening but even go so far as to reproduce them herself"** [2, 5].

Price high-handedly dismissed Glover's concerns [9], asserting that [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] struck him as "particularly self-possessed and normal" and telling Glover that the Foysters played "a very small part - so far as we are concerned - in the Borley story" [10, 11]. This contradicted Price's own private earlier beliefs that she was responsible for the fraud.

## Contradictions in the Sources: The "Nun" Sighting

> **Contradiction:** The [[spr|SPR]] authors (Dingwall, Goldney, and Hall) used Glover's report to attack Price, arguing that Glover attached *no importance* to Mrs. Williams's sighting, attributing it to bad lighting and psychological suggestion planted by Price's [[blue-book-of-instructions|Blue Book of Instructions]] [4]. However, investigator [[robert-hastings|Robert J. Hastings]] later contradicted this assessment, pointing out that Glover *did* investigate the sighting immediately by conducting visibility tests, and concluded his report by noting that the figure was seen in the exact spot the Nun was traditionally observed, stating: **"I give this whole story wholly without comment"** [14]. Hastings argued that the SPR authors misrepresented Glover's level of dismissal to suit their own anti-Price narrative [14].
