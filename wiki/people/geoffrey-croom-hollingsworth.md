# Geoffrey Croom-Hollingsworth (30 October 1917 – 2 May 1987)

Born Stretford, Greater Manchester; died Harlow, Essex, aged 70 of heart disease. Highways labourer, assistant caretaker and lay paranormal investigator who conducted the most extensive investigations at Borley in the early 1970s and claimed the most spectacular post-demolition sightings of the phantom nun.

## Investigations

Working with a team including "Chief Investigator" [[roy-potter|Roy Potter]], Frank Connell, and later film cameraman [[denny-densham|Denny Densham]], Croom-Hollingsworth visited Borley extensively between 1970 and 1980. He used infra-red cameras, walkie-talkies, and tape recorders, and was featured in the 1975 BBC programme *The Ghost Hunters*. He modelled himself consciously on [[harry-price]] — he is photographed pipe in hand in a Price-like close-up.

### The 1970 Nun Sighting

The most significant event from his investigations. On 20 June 1970, at 1.50 a.m., Croom-Hollingsworth was observing the former Nun's Walk area from a bungalow garden when he saw a "nun" drifting about a foot above ground level. He contacted Potter and Frank Connell by walkie-talkie. According to their various accounts, all three saw the figure, which appeared as a woman in a grey habit and cowl. The figure passed through a fence and disappeared towards the cottage.

> **Contradiction:** The duration of the sighting changed repeatedly in different accounts — 8 minutes, "eight to eleven minutes," then 12 minutes, then back to 8. The figure was described as an "elderly woman in her sixties" — at variance with virtually all previous sightings which described a young woman.

### The 1972 Sighting

On 13 July 1972 Potter and Croom-Hollingsworth again reported seeing the same nun near the Rectory site in bright moonlight. Potter threw a brick at the figure; the object passed clean through it, which remained visible and unmoved.

### Borley Church Recordings

Denny Densham joined the group in late 1972 and began recording allegedly paranormal sounds inside Borley Church. The tapes — featuring footsteps on bare wood, knocks, and a deep human sigh — were featured in *The Ghost Hunters* and released on cassette as *The Ghost of Borley Church*. Peter Underwood, during a tour of the church for the BBC programme, located objects that "could have made it possible for every noise in the recording to have a natural explanation."

## Assessment

The Companion notes "ample evidence that he changed his stories on several occasions to suit later developments." His unpublished book *The Ghosts Remain at Borley* (co-written with Roy Potter) had a scheduled December 1975 publication but never appeared; the manuscript was destroyed by his widow after his death. His photographically caught "nun" photograph from 1972 (the [[brazil-borley-photograph|Brazil Borley Photograph]], actually taken by Eddie Brazil that September) was "interpreted" rather than captured by him.

[[mrs-baines|Mrs Cecil Ryan Baines]] — who was a supporter — "soon became extremely sceptical of Mr Croom-Hollingsworth" after his investigations began.

He ended his Borley investigations in 1980 "due to yobs and such like causing problems during night-time vigils." He is buried in Princess Alexandra Hospital, Harlow.

## Additional Details from *The Final Analysis*

Babbs and Mathias (brfa-ch09, brfa-app) provide additional context:

- The 12-minute 1970 sighting is presented by them as one of the most remarkable post-demolition sightings: Croom-Hollingsworth contacted the other two observers by radio, giving one of them time to position himself only ~20 feet from the figure before it disappeared. At 15 feet he could see: eyes shut, sad expression, a mole or blemish on her left cheek, head and shoulders covered by a cowl, dark grey robes reaching down to and covering her feet
- His assessment of the nun as **elderly** conflicts with the Marie Lairre planchette theory (a young novice of about 19 in 1667); Babbs and Mathias suggest she may be a different entity — perhaps a nun associated with the Waldegrave household, who were staunch Catholics
- **Dodie's diary:** Croom-Hollingsworth found this diary (1 January – 28 October 1885) in a second-hand shop in Sudbury and offered it to the Harry Price Library for **£1000** (worth ~£9500 in 2002); the Library declined to pay this price
- **Tape recorder tangled in church porch:** On one occasion the Harlow group set up recording equipment in Borley church porch and kept watch from a distance. Nobody entered. They heard a loud noise from the porch, investigated, and found the tape **removed from its reel and lying in a tangled heap on the floor** — the same problem [[denny-densham]] experienced with his equipment about ten years later (brfa-ch12)
