# Hilary Wright

A former Borley resident, living in a small Suffolk town at the time of interview (1995). A former nurse. Daughter of [[marie-poore]]. Interviewed twice by Babbs and Mathias in 1995.

## Nun Sighting — Autumn 1971

Being driven home at about 2am from a social function, the car approached Borley from Rodbridge Corner. The night was clear and moonlit with good visibility. Both Mrs Wright and her companion saw a figure of a nun standing beyond the end of Rodbridge bridge, to their left, slightly before the turning up to Borley.

She could not discern facial features but the figure was:
- Dressed in **long grey robes**
- Either notably tall, or apparently "**standing upon high ground which is no longer there**"
- Associated with a feeling she described as "**bad vibes**" — unpleasant without being violent

She is a former nurse and "seemed to us to be a reliable witness."

## BBC Television — August 1974

Living at Pond Cottage near the rectory site, she appeared in a BBC television programme hosted by [[peter-underwood]] in August 1974, along with her mother [[marie-poore]], [[ursula-pennell]], and Canon Pennell. Participants received £10 each.

## Phantom Cat — 1974

During the summer of 1974, being driven through Borley at dusk, a large cat dashed out into the road at a specific spot and then vanished. The driver did not see the animal. [[marie-poore]] saw the same phenomenon at the same location in the same year on a different occasion.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Hilary Wright** appears in the Borley Rectory literature as a modern-era witness to the famous phantom nun. A former nurse and resident of Borley, her testimony is frequently cited to demonstrate that apparitions continued to be seen in the area decades after the Rectory was destroyed. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following biographical details, events, and specific quotes regarding her involvement:

**Biographical Details and Family Connections**
*   **Profession and Residence:** Mrs. Hilary Wright was a former nurse [1, 2]. During the 1970s, she lived at **Pond Cottage**, which was situated very close to the site of the former Borley Rectory [3]. She later relocated to a small Suffolk town [4]. 
*   **Her Mother's Experiences:** Her mother was the late **Marie Poore**, who lived nearby at a bungalow called "The Willows" on Lower Road [3]. Marie Poore also claimed to have seen an "old-fashioned coach" in the area and stated she had once seen a youthful diary belonging to Miss Ethel Bull containing the entry, "we saw the nun in the garden today" [1]. 

**The Phantom Nun Sighting (Autumn 1971)**
Hilary Wright's most significant contribution to the Borley case was a vivid sighting of the phantom nun. 
*   **The Event:** At approximately **2:00 a.m. on a night in the autumn of 1971**, Wright was being driven home from a social function by a companion [2]. 
*   **The Conditions:** It was a **clear, moonlit night with good visibility** [1, 2]. 
*   **The Location:** After crossing the bridge from Rodbridge Corner into Essex, both Wright and her companion saw the figure standing just beyond the bridge on the left, on **Lower Road** (just before the sharp curve where it becomes Hall Lane near Borley Mill) [1, 2]. 
*   **Description of the Figure:** Wright noted that she could not discern the figure's facial features [2]. However, she observed that the figure was dressed in **long grey robes** and appeared to be **"either rather tall, or standing upon high ground which is no longer there,"** as the figure stood above the level of the road [1, 2].
*   **Specific Quotes and Sensations:** Because Wright and her companion were inside a car, they did not notice any sudden drops in temperature typically associated with Borley apparitions [2]. However, Wright was extremely surprised and experienced a most unpleasant feeling, which she specifically described with the quote: **"bad vibes"** [2]. 

**Media Involvement and Interviews**
*   **The 1974 BBC Broadcast:** In **August 1974**, while living at Pond Cottage, Wright took part in a BBC television programme hosted by paranormal investigator Peter Underwood [3]. The programme was filmed at her home and featured Wright, her mother (Marie Poore), and Canon and Mrs. Pennell relating their Borley experiences [3]. The sources record the lesser-known detail that **the participants received a "generous reward" of £10 each for their efforts** [3]. 
*   **The 1995 Interviews:** Wright was interviewed on two separate occasions in 1995 by the authors of *Borley Rectory - The Final Analysis*, who noted that her background as a nurse made her a level-headed and highly **"reliable witness"** [2, 4].
