# Hilary Wright

A former Borley resident, living in a small Suffolk town at the time of interview (1995). A former nurse. Daughter of [[marie-poore|Marie Poore]]. Interviewed twice by Babbs and Mathias in 1995, who noted her background made her a level-headed and highly **"reliable witness"** [2, 4].

## Background and Family

During the 1970s, Wright lived at **Pond Cottage**, situated very close to the site of the former Borley Rectory [3]. Her mother, the late [[marie-poore|Marie Poore]], lived nearby at a bungalow called "The Willows" on Lower Road [3]. Marie Poore also claimed to have seen an "old-fashioned coach" in the area and stated she had once seen a youthful diary belonging to Miss Ethel Bull containing the entry, "we saw the nun in the garden today" [1].

## Nun Sighting — Autumn 1971

Wright's most significant contribution to the Borley case was a vivid sighting of the [[the-nun|phantom nun]].

At approximately **2:00 a.m. on a night in the autumn of 1971**, Wright was being driven home from a social function by a companion [2]. The night was **clear and moonlit with good visibility** [1, 2]. After crossing the bridge from Rodbridge Corner into Essex, both Wright and her companion saw a figure standing just beyond the bridge on the left, on Lower Road — just before the sharp curve where it becomes Hall Lane near Borley Mill [1, 2].

Wright could not discern the figure's facial features but observed that it was dressed in **long grey robes** and appeared to be **"either rather tall, or standing upon high ground which is no longer there,"** as the figure stood above the level of the road [1, 2]. Because Wright and her companion were inside a car, they did not notice any sudden drop in temperature typically associated with Borley apparitions [2]. However, Wright experienced a most unpleasant feeling, which she specifically described as **"bad vibes"** [2].

## BBC Television — August 1974

While living at Pond Cottage, Wright took part in a BBC television programme hosted by [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] in **August 1974** [3]. The programme was filmed at her home and featured Wright, her mother ([[marie-poore|Marie Poore]]), [[ursula-pennell|Ursula Pennell]], and Canon Pennell relating their Borley experiences [3]. The sources record the detail that **the participants received a "generous reward" of £10 each** for their efforts [3].

## Phantom Cat — 1974

During the summer of 1974, being driven through Borley at dusk, a large cat dashed out into the road at a specific spot and then vanished. The driver did not see the animal. [[marie-poore|Marie Poore]] saw the same phenomenon at the same location in the same year on a different occasion.
