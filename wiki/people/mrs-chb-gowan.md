# Mrs C. H. B. Gowan

Widow of Captain Gowan, an acquaintance of [[harry-price|Harry Price]]. Mrs Gowan served as an important conduit between Price and independent witnesses, twice providing testimony of her own.

## The Carpenter Letter

Mrs Gowan's mother, Mrs Savile, received a letter from the Rev. [[rev-harry-carpenter|Harry Carpenter]] (122 Woodstock Road, Oxford) which contained a first-hand account of the Rev. [[harry-bull|Harry Bull]]'s personal experience of the phantom coach and headless coachman — heard directly from Bull during wartime special constable duty. Mrs Gowan forwarded the relevant portion to Price.

This particular chain of transmission — Carpenter to Mrs Savile to Mrs Gowan to Price — is entirely independent of Price's primary contacts and provides an unsolicited corroboration of the coach phenomena via a witness who encountered Price's investigation only after publication.

## Wall-Writing Interpretation

Writing on October 23, 1940, Mrs Gowan also contributed to the correspondence about the [[wall-writings|Marianne wall-messages]], interpreting the message in Plate IX (reading "Marianne-Please-help-get...") as **incomplete** — suggesting the writer was "forcibly pulled away just as she was beginning the last word," leaving the message ending in a scrawl rather than a period. She reads the message as: "Marianne, please help! Get…"

This interpretation — that the entity was interrupted mid-message rather than reaching a natural conclusion — adds to the picture of frantic urgency that [[canon-phythian-adams|Canon Phythian-Adams]] would later develop in his analysis of the French word *trompée*.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Mrs. C. H. B. Gowan** appears in the Borley Rectory sources as a helpful correspondent who supplied investigator Harry Price with documentary evidence regarding the early history of the hauntings. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific details about her involvement:

**Biographical Details**
*   **Family:** She was the daughter of **Mrs. Savile** and the widow of the late **Captain Gowan**, a man whom Harry Price noted that he "knew well" [1]. 

**Contribution to Harry Price's Research**
*   Mrs. Gowan is explicitly listed in the acknowledgments of Harry Price's 1946 book, *The End of Borley Rectory*, as one of the contributors to whom he was "greatly indebted" [2].
*   Her specific contribution was providing Price with "more evidence of what Harry Bull saw" [1]. 

**The October 1941 Letter and Quote**
*   Mrs. Gowan forwarded to Price a copy of a letter that had been sent to her mother, Mrs. Savile, by the **Rev. Harry Carpenter** (who was writing from 122 Woodstock Road, Oxford) [1].
*   In this letter, dated **October 30, 1941**, the Rev. Carpenter confirmed his familiarity with the Bull family and the early ghost sightings, providing the following specific quote:
    > **"E. has handed on to us the part of your letter about the ghosts at Borley. As you say, the village is close to Foxearth, and we have read with much interest the book about the apparitions, especially as we knew the Bull family well and some of the people who are mentioned by Mr Price as having seen the ghosts."** [1].

The available sources contain no further contradictions, events, or biographical details regarding Mrs. Gowan beyond her role in transmitting this corroborating correspondence to the official investigation.
