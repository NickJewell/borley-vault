# Lady Frances Waldegrave

Wife of [[sir-edward-waldegrave|Sir Edward Waldegrave]], Lord of the Manor of Borley; arrested with her husband in April 1561 and interrogated throughout June and July of that year on charges of harbouring the forbidden Catholic Mass.

## Background

Lady Frances was present at Borley Hall when the priest [[john-cox-devon|John Cox / John Devon]] stayed for a month and celebrated the Catholic Mass. During this period she gave Cox **a book of the Sarum liturgy** — the old liturgical form previously used at Salisbury — which was itself an illegal item under Elizabethan Protestant law.

## Arrest and Interrogation

The *Calendars of State Papers Domestic* record that following the betrayal of Cox/Devon and the arrest of Sir Edward, Frances Waldegrave was interrogated alongside her husband at the Commission of Oyer held at Brentwood, Essex, before the Earl of Oxford on June 3, 1561. The interrogations continued through July 1561.

Banks does not provide details of what Frances said under interrogation, or what happened to her after Sir Edward's death in the Tower on September 1, 1561. He notes only the fact of her arrest and her role in providing the Sarum liturgy book to Cox.

## Significance

Lady Frances represents the collateral cost of Sir Edward's religious convictions and political activities. Her gift of the Sarum liturgy book is one of the concrete details from Cox's confession that corroborates the account of organized Catholic worship at Borley Hall.

> **Contested:** Banks notes that the precise cause of Sir Edward's death — illness vs. execution vs. torture — remains unresolved. Lady Frances's own fate after 1561 is not recorded in the sources Banks consulted.
