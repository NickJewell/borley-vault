# Maud Stearn

A relief organist who lived at The Vicarage, Lindsey, Suffolk. She played at Borley church and at Liston church (which had no regular organist) on occasion. She moved away from Lindsey shortly after the incident described below, which can be dated to **1955** — shortly after [[rev-lanfranc-mathias]] took the living.

## Walking Toward the Playing Organ — 1955

On a day when she had been asked to play for a service at Borley, she was walking toward the church when she was **surprised and annoyed to hear the organ already being played** — apparently by someone else. When she reached the porch the music ceased. When she entered the building, **no one else was present**. The organ was closed.

This is an exact parallel to the experiences reported by [[rev-ac-henning]] and Mrs Walrond in October 1947, and by E. W. Jephcott in September 1947. The consistent pattern across multiple witnesses separated in time is noted by Babbs and Mathias as significant.

The incident was confirmed by Gerald Hazell's brother, providing a second-hand corroboration.

[[claudine-mathias|CM]] remembers Mrs Stearn and her husband's reaction: "It would be best if the children did not hear about this."

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Maud Stearn** (recorded as the late Mrs. Maud Stearn) was a local resident whose connection to the Borley saga involves a strange auditory phenomenon at Borley Church during the mid-1950s. 

A reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following biographical details, events, and quotes regarding her experience:

**Biographical Details**
*   **Occupation and Residence:** Mrs. Stearn served as a **relief organist** in the area and lived at **The Vicarage, Lindsey, Suffolk** [1]. 
*   **Travel and Work:** To fulfill her musical duties, she **drove an Austin *Seven*** to various local churches [2]. She played occasionally at Borley but more frequently at the nearby **Liston church**, which lacked a regular organist at the time [2].
*   **Departure:** She reportedly **moved away from Lindsey shortly after 1955** [1].

**The Phantom Organ Incident (1955)**
Mrs. Stearn's primary contribution to the Borley case centers on an unexplained auditory event that occurred in **1955**, during the incumbency of the Rev. Lanfranc Mathias [1, 2]. 
*   She had been asked to play for a service at Borley Church. As she was walking toward the building, she was **"surprised and very annoyed to hear the organ being played, apparently by someone else"** [1].
*   However, the moment she reached the church porch, **the music abruptly ceased**, and upon entering the building, she discovered that **there was no one else present** [1]. 
*   Her account of this eccentric behavior of the Borley organ was later confirmed by the brother of Mr. Gerald Hazell, a local resident from a nearby Suffolk village who knew her [1]. 

**Reactions and Quotes**
Mrs. Stearn openly shared her uncanny experience with other local residents [2]. The texts record a specific quote detailing the reaction of the rector, the Rev. Lanfranc Mathias, who was seemingly anxious to prevent the story from spreading and frightening the younger parishioners. Upon hearing her account, he stated: **"It would be best if the children did not hear about this"** [2].
