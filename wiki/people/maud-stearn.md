# Maud Stearn

A relief organist who lived at The Vicarage, Lindsey, Suffolk. She played at Borley church and at Liston church (which had no regular organist) on occasion. She moved away from Lindsey shortly after the incident described below, which can be dated to **1955** — shortly after [[rev-lanfranc-mathias]] took the living.

## Walking Toward the Playing Organ — 1955

On a day when she had been asked to play for a service at Borley, she was walking toward the church when she was **surprised and annoyed to hear the organ already being played** — apparently by someone else. When she reached the porch the music ceased. When she entered the building, **no one else was present**. The organ was closed.

This is an exact parallel to the experiences reported by [[rev-ac-henning]] and Mrs Walrond in October 1947, and by E. W. Jephcott in September 1947. The consistent pattern across multiple witnesses separated in time is noted by Babbs and Mathias as significant.

The incident was confirmed by Gerald Hazell's brother, providing a second-hand corroboration.

[[claudine-mathias|CM]] remembers Mrs Stearn and her husband's reaction: "It would be best if the children did not hear about this."
