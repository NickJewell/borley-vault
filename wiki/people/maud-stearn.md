# Maud Stearn

A relief organist who lived at The Vicarage, Lindsey, Suffolk, and drove an Austin Seven to serve various local churches [2]. She played at [[borley-church|Borley Church]] on occasion, but more frequently at the nearby Liston church, which had no regular organist [2]. She moved away from Lindsey shortly after the incident described below, which can be dated to **1955** — shortly after [[rev-lanfranc-mathias|Rev. Lanfranc Mathias]] took the living.

## Walking Toward the Playing Organ — 1955

On a day when she had been asked to play for a service at Borley, she was walking toward the church when she was **"surprised and very annoyed to hear the organ being played, apparently by someone else"** [1]. When she reached the porch the music abruptly ceased. When she entered the building, **no one else was present** and the organ was closed [1].

She openly shared her experience with local residents [2]. Her account was later confirmed by the brother of Mr Gerald Hazell, a local resident from a nearby Suffolk village who knew her [1].

[[rev-lanfranc-mathias|Rev. Lanfranc Mathias]], seemingly anxious to prevent the story from frightening younger parishioners, told her: **"It would be best if the children did not hear about this"** [2].

## Significance and Pattern

This experience is an exact parallel to those reported by [[rev-ac-henning|Rev. A. C. Henning]] and Mrs Walrond in October 1947, and by [[ew-jephcott|E. W. Jephcott]] in September 1947. The consistent pattern across multiple witnesses separated in time is noted by Babbs and Mathias as significant. The incident was also externally corroborated by Hazell's brother, providing second-hand confirmation.
