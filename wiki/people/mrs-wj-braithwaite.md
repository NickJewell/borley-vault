# Mrs W. John Braithwaite

Widow of [[wj-braithwaite|W. John Braithwaite]]. She lived at "Greenwood," Bidborough Ridge, Tunbridge Wells. After her husband's death, she found among his effects two significant documents relating to the Borley case — both of which she made available to [[harry-price|Harry Price]] in 1945.

## The Braithwaite Report

Mrs Braithwaite sent Price the 5,000-word verbatim report her husband had compiled from notes taken during the séance at Borley Rectory on August 11, 1931 — the "missing link" that Price had needed but could not find when writing *The Most Haunted House in England*. See [[wj-braithwaite]] for the full content of the séance.

She "very kindly" permitted Price to quote from the report, which she sent on her own initiative. Price had been directed to her by [[miss-mary-braithwaite|Miss Mary E. Braithwaite, J.P.]], her late husband's sister.

## The Paper-Message

More remarkably, among her husband's effects was the **only surviving paper-message** from the Foyster period — a specimen in [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s handwriting on the back of an old envelope. The handwriting of the single word "Marianne" at the top was, according to Price, "identical in every way" with the word "Marianne" as it appeared in all four of the wall-messages photographed and published in *The Most Haunted House in England*.

Mrs Braithwaite **presented the envelope to Price** as a gift, and he reproduces it as Plate XXVI. This is the only paper-message (as distinct from wall-message) documented and preserved in the Borley record.

## Significance

Mrs Braithwaite's contributions to the archive are twofold: a major primary source document (the séance report), and a physical artefact (the paper-message envelope) that connects the Planchette record to the Foyster-era wall-writings via a demonstrably identical handwriting specimen.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Mrs. W. John Braithwaite** was the widow of W. John Braithwaite, a psychical researcher who participated in a significant séance at Borley Rectory during the Foyster incumbency [1, 2]. While she was not present at the Rectory during these events herself, she played a vital posthumous role in supplying investigator Harry Price with crucial documentary evidence for his publications [1, 2].

A thorough reference profile drawn from the sources reveals the following biographical details, actions, and specific artifacts she contributed to the investigation:

**Biographical Details**
*   **Residence:** In 1945, she was residing at **"Greenwood," Bidborough Ridge, Tunbridge Wells** [1]. 
*   **Family Connection:** Her sister-in-law was Miss Mary E. Braithwaite, J.P., of Brook House, Long Melford [1]. 

**The 5,000-Word Séance Report**
Mrs. Braithwaite's involvement with the official investigation began following a visit Harry Price made to the Borley area in May 1945 [1]. 
*   Price met Miss Mary Braithwaite, who informed him that her late brother had attended a marathon séance at the Rectory on **August 11, 1931**, alongside the medium Mr. Johnson and interrogator Ernest Meads, and had compiled a lengthy report based on his contemporary notes [1, 2]. 
*   Following Miss Braithwaite's suggestion, Price contacted Mrs. Braithwaite, who **kindly sent him the 5,000-word report and granted him permission to quote it** [1, 2]. 
*   Price was thrilled with the document, describing it as the **"missing link"** he had required for writing his second Borley monograph, *The End of Borley Rectory* [2]. 

**The Envelope and "Paper-Message"**
Along with the séance report, Mrs. Braithwaite made a significant physical contribution to Price's archives. 
*   She **found a specimen of a mysterious "paper-message" among her late husband's effects and presented it to Price** [3]. 
*   This specific artifact was written on the back of an old envelope [4]. It featured the word "Marianne" (Mrs. Foyster's Christian name), beneath which Mrs. Foyster had written the question, "What do you want?"—followed by the word "Rest" which subsequently appeared on the paper [4]. 
*   Price utilized this artifact provided by Mrs. Braithwaite by reproducing it photographically as Plate XXVI in *The End of Borley Rectory* [3, 4].

**Direct Quote and Context**
When sending the report and the envelope to Price, Mrs. Braithwaite provided historical context regarding her husband's familiarity with the Rectory's long-standing reputation. In her covering letter, she explicitly informed Price that **"from childhood her husband had known Borley Rectory and the Bull family, and heard of the haunting even in those early days"** [3].
