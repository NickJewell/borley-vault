# Mrs W. John Braithwaite

Widow of [[wj-braithwaite|W. John Braithwaite]]. In 1945 she was residing at "Greenwood," Bidborough Ridge, Tunbridge Wells. Her sister-in-law was Miss Mary E. Braithwaite, J.P., of Brook House, [[long-melford|Long Melford]].

After her husband's death, she found among his effects two significant documents relating to the Borley case — both of which she made available to [[harry-price|Harry Price]] in 1945. In her covering letter she informed Price that "from childhood her husband had known Borley Rectory and the [[henry-bull|Bull]] family, and heard of the haunting even in those early days."

## The Braithwaite Report

Price had been directed to Mrs Braithwaite by Miss Mary E. Braithwaite, who informed him that her late brother had attended a marathon séance at the Rectory on **August 11, 1931**, alongside the medium [[mr-johnson-medium|Mr Johnson]] and interrogator [[ernest-meads|Ernest Meads]], and had compiled a lengthy report based on his contemporary notes.

Mrs Braithwaite "very kindly" sent Price the 5,000-word verbatim report and granted him permission to quote from it. Price described it as the **"missing link"** he had required for writing his second Borley monograph, *The End of Borley Rectory* — the full content of the séance he had needed but could not find when writing *The Most Haunted House in England*.

## The Paper-Message

More remarkably, among her husband's effects was the **only surviving paper-message** from the Foyster period — a specimen in [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s handwriting on the back of an old envelope. The artifact featured the word "Marianne" (Mrs Foyster's Christian name), beneath which Mrs Foyster had written the question, "What do you want?" — followed by the word "Rest" which subsequently appeared on the paper.

According to Price, the handwriting of the single word "Marianne" at the top was "identical in every way" with the word "Marianne" as it appeared in all four of the [[wall-writings|wall-messages]] photographed and published in *The Most Haunted House in England*.

Mrs Braithwaite **presented the envelope to Price** as a gift, and he reproduces it as Plate XXVI. This is the only paper-message (as distinct from wall-message) documented and preserved in the Borley record.

## Significance

Mrs Braithwaite's contributions to the archive are twofold: a major primary source document (the séance report), and a physical artefact (the paper-message envelope) that connects the planchette record to the Foyster-era wall-writings via a demonstrably identical handwriting specimen.
