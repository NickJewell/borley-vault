# Hilda Foyster

Unmarried sister of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]]; later Mrs. Hilda Hanbury [3]. The daughter of [[rev-george-alfred-foyster|Rev. George Alfred Foyster]], she had another sister named Adelaide alongside several brothers [1]. A key confidante and adviser to Lionel during the Borley poltergeist period, and later the custodian of the Foyster manuscripts.

## Background and Visits to Canada

Hilda had an interest in spiritualism that made her a natural sounding board when Lionel began experiencing unexplained phenomena at the Rectory. When he emigrated to Canada in 1910 to work as a missionary in New Brunswick, his life there was hard and lonely, but it was alleviated by the occasional visit from Hilda, who was still unmarried at the time [2].

## Role During the Poltergeist Period

As the poltergeist activity at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] escalated in early 1931, Lionel wrote repeatedly to Hilda for advice. When Lionel wondered aloud whether [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] might unconsciously be producing the phenomena, Hilda advised him that she might be psychic and encouraged him to **record every incident when it was impossible for Marianne to have been responsible** — a methodological suggestion that shaped how he compiled his famous diary. This scientific approach is reflected in the structure of the *Diary of Occurrences*.

Hilda also visited Lionel and Marianne during their time in Canada (at Salmonhurst) and visited Lionel on his return trips to England, where they would stay with her in Northamptonshire.

## Marianne's Alibi

Hilda's name was used as an alibi by [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] when defending herself against accusations that she was frequently away from Borley Rectory conducting an illicit affair in London. During a 1958 interview, Marianne insisted she was rarely away from the Rectory, stating: **"I was away on only two occasions: once when I visited my people in Ireland - just before my grandmother died, and once when I stayed with Hilda... Mrs. Hanbury, Lionel's sister."** [3]

## Custody of the Manuscripts

After [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] died in April 1945, [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] gave everything she had of Lionel's Borley writings to Hilda, stating she "wanted no part" of his books and papers [4]. When Marianne emigrated to America in 1946, she assumed that [[harry-price|Harry Price]] subsequently acquired the manuscript of *[[foyster-manuscripts|Fifteen Months in a Haunted House]]* directly from Hilda [4]. This manuscript became a critical — and contested — document in the Borley controversy.
