# Hilda Foyster

Unmarried sister of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]]; a key confidante and adviser to him during the Borley poltergeist period, and later the custodian of the Foyster manuscripts.

## Background

Hilda was one of Lionel's siblings from the clerical Foyster family. She had an interest in spiritualism that made her a natural sounding board when Lionel began experiencing unexplained phenomena at the Rectory. When he emigrated to Canada in 1910, Hilda made occasional visits to keep him company in New Brunswick.

## Role During the Poltergeist Period

As the poltergeist activity at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] escalated in early 1931, Lionel wrote repeatedly to Hilda for advice. She encouraged him to **record every incident when it was impossible for Marianne to have been responsible** — a methodological suggestion that shaped how he compiled his famous diary.

When Lionel wondered aloud whether [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] might unconsciously be producing the phenomena, Hilda advised him that she might be psychic and that he should make a careful note of cases where Marianne could not have been involved. This scientific approach is reflected in the structure of the *Diary of Occurrences*.

Hilda also visited Lionel and Marianne during their time in Canada (at Salmonhurst) and visited [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] on his return trips to England, where they would stay with her in Northamptonshire.

## Custody of the Manuscripts

After [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] died in April 1945, [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] gave everything she had of Lionel's Borley writings to Hilda. When Marianne emigrated to America in 1946, she assumed that [[harry-price|Harry Price]] subsequently acquired the manuscript of *[[foyster-manuscripts|Fifteen Months in a Haunted House]]* directly from Hilda. This manuscript became a critical — and contested — document in the Borley controversy.
