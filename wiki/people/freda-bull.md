# Freda Bull

One of the fourteen children of the Reverend [[henry-bull|Henry Dawson Ellis Bull]], and a sister of [[ethel-bull|Ethel Bull]], [[isabel-bull|Isabel Bull]], [[walter-bull|Walter Bull]], and [[gerald-bull|Gerald Bull]].

Freda Bull is named alongside Isabel Bull and "Mary H. Gladwin" as one of the daughters who gave full accounts of life and phenomena at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] to [[harry-price|Harry Price]] and [[sidney-glanville|Sidney Glanville]]. As related by Ethel Bull, these three women provided detailed testimony about the haunting during their father's thirty-year incumbency.

## 1900 Nun Sighting

Freda was almost certainly one of the four sisters present at the landmark July 1900 [[the-nun|Nun]] sighting. Three sisters returning from a garden party entered by the lower gate and glimpsed a dark female figure gliding along the [[nuns-walk|Nun's Walk]]; a fourth was fetched from the house. All four remained consistent in describing the figure for the rest of their lives.

The survivors gathered at [[chilton-lodge|Chilton Lodge]], Great Cornard, where Peter Underwood interviewed them multiple times. "These meetings," Underwood and Tabori recorded, "made it difficult to dismiss the 1900 'incident' as mere hallucination."

## Later Life

Like her sisters, Freda settled at Chilton Lodge after leaving the Rectory. She predeceased the last surviving Bull daughter, Constance, who died in 1963.
