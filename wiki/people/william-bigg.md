# William Bigg

Farmer and churchwarden at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]], closest neighbour of the rectors throughout the Smith and Foyster periods.

## Background

Bigg was a farmer's family man who lived at **Borley Place**, the historic manor house across the lane from the Rectory, which he leased from the Payne family. He was churchwarden at Borley Church during [[harry-bull|Harry Bull]]'s long incumbency and continued in that role after Bull's death.

## Smith Period

When the Smiths arrived in 1928, Bigg was one of the first to call. Though not unfriendly, he resented relinquishing the authority he had accumulated during the vacancy since Bull's death. This made Eric Smith's church responsibilities difficult from the start.

Bigg's wife (Mrs Bigg) was a hospital nurse who had frequently attended Harry Bull in his final months. When [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] was trying to confirm whether Harry Bull had worn a plum-coloured dressing gown — she had been seeing his apparition — she asked Mrs Bigg discreetly. Mrs Bigg confirmed that Reverend Bull did indeed wear a plum-coloured smoking jacket and was barely seen out of it in the months before he died.

## Foyster Period

During the poltergeist peak of early 1931, Bigg served as a practical lifeline to the besieged Foysters. When [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] collapsed after the failed blessing of the house, [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] rushed to Borley Place to fetch brandy from Mrs Bigg. When phenomena were at their worst and the Foysters debated whether to spend the night with their neighbours rather than face the poltergeist, they feared that "the Biggs would think they were mad or suffering with their nerves."

By the time [[henry-lawton|Henry Lawton]] served as locum at the Rectory in summer 1933, Bigg had retired as churchwarden but remained a key source of local oral history. He told Lawton his belief that the "haunt" during the Smith period had been **engineered by Eric Smith** to depreciate the Rectory's market value so that a friend could buy it cheaply.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**William Bigg** (frequently referred to as Mr. Bigg) was a local farmer, churchwarden, and significant neighbor to the residents of Borley Rectory. His close physical proximity to the Rectory and his involvement with three different rectors make him a notable secondary figure in the Borley story. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the sources reveals the following biographical details, interactions, and specific opinions regarding the hauntings:

**Residence and Profession**
*   **Location:** William Bigg and his wife lived at **Borley Place**, a historic manor house situated immediately across the road from Borley Church and almost exactly opposite the Rectory [1-3]. 
*   **Tenancy:** He was a **farmer** who leased Borley Place from the wealthy Payne family (who owned Borley Hall) [3]. The Biggs were tenants there throughout the incumbencies of both the Rev. Guy Eric Smith and the Rev. Lionel Foyster [4]. 
*   **Church Role:** Bigg served as a **churchwarden** for Borley Church [5, 6].

**Relationship with the Bull Family**
The Biggs appear to have been on close terms with the Rev. Harry Foyster Bull. 
*   During Harry Bull's final illness in 1927, Mrs. Bigg visited him and **"saw him a good deal"** [1]. 
*   Following Harry Bull's death, William Bigg attended his funeral on Tuesday, June 14, 1927, a large county affair where he represented the parish as the churchwarden [5, 7].

**Hostility Toward the Smiths and Skepticism**
When the Rev. Guy Eric Smith and his wife Mabel took over the Borley living in 1928, William Bigg remained the churchwarden, but the relationship was highly strained. 
*   The sources note that William Bigg and the Smiths **"had not got on"** [6]. 
*   Bigg was highly skeptical of the paranormal phenomena reported during the Smiths' time at the Rectory. During several conversations with Canon H. Lawton (who visited Borley during the 1930s), Bigg bluntly revealed his personal belief that **the "haunt" during the Smiths’ time had been deliberately "engineered by Eric Smith"** [6].

**Interactions with the Foysters**
Despite his skepticism of the Smiths, Bigg was directly involved in at least one incident during the highly active Foyster incumbency. 
*   The Rev. Lionel Foyster recorded in his manuscript that he had a premonition that a paranormal disturbance was about to occur while he was out of the house. 
*   Wanting an independent witness, Foyster specifically crossed the road to ask Mr. Bigg—who Foyster knew was **traveling into the nearby town of Sudbury**—to drop off a message at the home of Edwin Whitehouse, asking the young man to come to the Rectory to observe [2].
