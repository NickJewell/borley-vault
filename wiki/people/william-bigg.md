# William Bigg

Farmer and churchwarden at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]], closest neighbour of the rectors throughout the Smith and Foyster periods.

## Background

Bigg was a farmer's family man who lived at **Borley Place**, the historic manor house across the lane from the Rectory, which he leased from the Payne family. He was churchwarden at Borley Church during [[harry-bull|Harry Bull]]'s long incumbency and continued in that role after Bull's death.

## Smith Period

When the Smiths arrived in 1928, Bigg was one of the first to call. Though not unfriendly, he resented relinquishing the authority he had accumulated during the vacancy since Bull's death. This made Eric Smith's church responsibilities difficult from the start.

Bigg's wife (Mrs Bigg) was a hospital nurse who had frequently attended Harry Bull in his final months. When [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] was trying to confirm whether Harry Bull had worn a plum-coloured dressing gown — she had been seeing his apparition — she asked Mrs Bigg discreetly. Mrs Bigg confirmed that Reverend Bull did indeed wear a plum-coloured smoking jacket and was barely seen out of it in the months before he died.

## Foyster Period

During the poltergeist peak of early 1931, Bigg served as a practical lifeline to the besieged Foysters. When [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] collapsed after the failed blessing of the house, [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] rushed to Borley Place to fetch brandy from Mrs Bigg. When phenomena were at their worst and the Foysters debated whether to spend the night with their neighbours rather than face the poltergeist, they feared that "the Biggs would think they were mad or suffering with their nerves."

By the time [[henry-lawton|Henry Lawton]] served as locum at the Rectory in summer 1933, Bigg had retired as churchwarden but remained a key source of local oral history. He told Lawton his belief that the "haunt" during the Smith period had been **engineered by Eric Smith** to depreciate the Rectory's market value so that a friend could buy it cheaply.
