# Mrs G. Taylor of Chelsea

Also referred to in the sources as **Mrs Gay Taylor** and **Mrs Guy Taylor of London**. A reporter for the **Mass-Observation Archive** (which had begun recording everyday life in Britain in 1937), Mrs Taylor became a deeply invested follower of the Borley haunting whose theories ultimately took a uniquely dark turn toward Satanism and black magic.

She became interested in the Rectory phenomena after reading [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s books and began corresponding with him in 1942 to discuss the [[waldegrave-family|Waldegrave family]] and the Catholic background of the story. About a year before Price's death, she wrote to him claiming that during a séance she had been given a message to pass on: it stated that the reputed ghost nun, [[marie-lairre|Marie Lairre]], was born near Le Havre on September 27, 1647, and had later entered a nunnery.

## Visits to Borley

Mrs Taylor visited the Borley area and became acquainted with key figures in the saga, including Miss [[ethel-bull|Ethel Bull]] and James and Catherine [[james-turner|Turner]], then living at the [[rectory-cottage|Rectory Cottage]]. She claimed to have experienced "a variety of small experiences" of a paranormal nature. However, she was disappointed to find that the local villagers met her with widespread scepticism; one local bluntly advised her that **"there waunt no ghosts till Harry Price brought 'um."**

## The June 1948 Séance

One of the participants in the planchette séance held at the [[rectory-cottage|Borley Priory cottage]] on the evening of **8 June 1948** (shortly after Harry Price's death). Her fellow sitters comprised Mr and Mrs [[james-turner|James Turner]], the Reverend [[john-dening|John Dening]], and [[ts-frankland|T. S. Frankland]] of Trinity College Cambridge.

During this sitting, the group reportedly established contact with an entity calling itself "Harry Price," as well as an entity claiming to be the late **"Harry Bull"**. The "Harry Bull" entity made a bizarre prediction that he would haunt Borley for **"4004 years"** (a number reflecting Archbishop Ussher's 18th-century calculation for the date of Creation), alleged that there had been foul play in the death of the Rev. [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]], and stated that it was "Lionel Foyster" whose footsteps had been heard on the churchyard path. The group subsequently proceeded to [[borley-church|Borley Church]] porch at the entity's invitation.

## Theories of Satanism and Black Magic

Mrs Taylor's most distinct contribution to the Borley literature is her eventual conviction that the phenomena were rooted in profound evil and black magic, developed after holding several Borley-devoted séances in 1947. She detailed these beliefs in a series of "very curious letters" sent to [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]] in 1957, which Hall kept in his unpublished papers.

- **The 1480 Curse (January 26, 1957):** "I am unalterably convinced that something happened in that countryside in the past that caused it to be disturbed, at times, to this day . . . it was begun by [a priest and two others] who round about 1480 raised something they couldn't control."
- **Satanic Murder (February 22, 1957):** She elaborated that a local group practised black magic at [[long-melford|Long Melford]] and Borley (preferring Borley because it was out of the way). She claimed that when she asked her séance if a crime had occurred at Borley before the 17th century, the response was "Death - Violent," and that it was due to **Satanism**.
- **The Devil and the Medium:** She further claimed a medium had once gone into a trance at Borley and "woke up in a terrible state, declaring that she had seen the devil and that the whole place was reeking in Black Magic," after which no medium for miles around would agree to go into a trance at the site.
- **Accusations against Harry Price:** In her sprawling theory, she alleged that even [[harry-price|Harry Price]] had fallen under this evil spell, causing him to manufacture or misrepresent the paranormal evidence.

## Later Investigations and Reception

During her research, Mrs Taylor spoke with [[mrs-henning|Mrs Annette Henning]], who confided that "the whole of the Stour Valley was far more disturbed than anyone knew" — adding that after she gave talks to local Women's Institutes, village women would secretly approach her to share similar stories of paranormal happenings in their own homes.

The SPR investigators [[eric-dingwall|Eric Dingwall]] and Mollie Goldney arranged to meet Mrs Taylor at the Society for Psychical Research headquarters in Tavistock Square to discuss her findings. Trevor Hall ultimately dismissed her extreme theories, concluding in his unpublished manuscripts: "I do not at any rate accept Mrs. Taylor's theory that there is evidence to show that certain people behaved criminally because they came to live in the supposedly evil orbit of Borley."
