Daughter of [[sidney-glanville]] (Price's chief official observer). She had never used a Planchette before her father experimented with it in October 1937. She is the central figure in the two most dramatic events in the MHH record: the naming of "Mary Lairre" and the "Sunex Amures" fire prediction.

## The Planchette Sessions

**Session 1 — alone at Streatham, October 28, 1937**:

Helen was shown the earlier Borley Planchette scripts but given *no information about their content*. Alone at home in Streatham (not at Borley), she produced:
- The name "Lairre" (confirming on follow-up: "Mary Lairre")
- "Are you buried in the garden?" → Yes
- "When did you pass over?" → *1667*, month *May*, day *17*
- "Did they hurt you?" → Yes
- "What did you want before you passed over?" → *Water*
- "Was the monk's order that of St. Benedict?" → Yes

**Session 2 — with family and Kerr-Pearse, October 31, 1937**:
- Name confirmed: *Mary Lairre*
- Age at death: *19*
- Status: *novice*
- "Were you murdered?" → Yes; "How?" → *Stran...* (strangled)
- "Whose fault?" → *Waldegrave*
- "Are you French?" → Yes; "Where from?" → *Havre*
- Nunnery: *Bure* (Bures)
- Requests: Requiem Mass (by Roman Catholic priest), burial, holy water, incense

**Session 3 — "Sunex Amures," March 27, 1938** (with brother Roger only):

Five months after the October sessions, something prompted Helen to get out the Planchette. She asked "Does anyone want to speak?" — immediately "yes." "Who are you?" — the Planchette wrote in a large scrawly hand:

> "Sunex Amures and one of his men MEAN TO BURN THE RECTORY (o-night at 9 o'clock end of the haunting go to the rectory and you will be able to see us enter into our own and under the ruins you will find bone of murdered wardens under the ruins..."

"In which room will the fire start?" → **"Over the hall."**

The script was sent to Price's office immediately and preserved. Eleven months later, on February 27, 1939 at midnight, Borley Rectory burned — the fire starting over the hall.

## Significance

Helen Glanville had no prior information about the Rectory's history. She was alone in Streatham for the first session. She produced: a French name ("Lairre"), a date of death (May 17, 1667), a cause (strangulation), a perpetrator (Waldegrave), an origin (Havre), a nunnery (Bures). The Sunex Amures fire prediction — sent to Price in writing before the fire — is the only advance-documented fulfilled prediction in the Borley record. Price regards it as the strongest single piece of evidence for something genuinely supernormal.
