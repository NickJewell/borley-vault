Daughter of [[sidney-glanville]] (Price's chief official observer). She had never used a Planchette before her father experimented with it in October 1937. She is the central figure in the two most dramatic events in the MHH record: the naming of "Mary Lairre" and the "Sunex Amures" fire prediction.

## The Planchette Sessions

**Session 1 — alone at Streatham, October 28, 1937**:

Helen was shown the earlier Borley Planchette scripts but given *no information about their content*. Alone at home in Streatham (not at Borley), she produced:
- The name "Lairre" (confirming on follow-up: "Mary Lairre")
- "Are you buried in the garden?" → Yes
- "When did you pass over?" → *1667*, month *May*, day *17*
- "Did they hurt you?" → Yes
- "What did you want before you passed over?" → *Water*
- "Was the monk's order that of St. Benedict?" → Yes

**Session 2 — with family and Kerr-Pearse, October 31, 1937**:
- Name confirmed: *Mary Lairre*
- Age at death: *19*
- Status: *novice*
- "Were you murdered?" → Yes; "How?" → *Stran...* (strangled)
- "Whose fault?" → *Waldegrave*
- "Are you French?" → Yes; "Where from?" → *Havre*
- Nunnery: *Bure* (Bures)
- Requests: Requiem Mass (by Roman Catholic priest), burial, holy water, incense

**Session 3 — "Sunex Amures," March 27, 1938** (with brother Roger only):

Five months after the October sessions, something prompted Helen to get out the Planchette. She asked "Does anyone want to speak?" — immediately "yes." "Who are you?" — the Planchette wrote in a large scrawly hand:

> "Sunex Amures and one of his men MEAN TO BURN THE RECTORY (o-night at 9 o'clock end of the haunting go to the rectory and you will be able to see us enter into our own and under the ruins you will find bone of murdered wardens under the ruins..."

"In which room will the fire start?" → **"Over the hall."**

The script was sent to Price's office immediately and preserved. Eleven months later, on February 27, 1939 at midnight, Borley Rectory burned — the fire starting over the hall.

## Significance

Helen Glanville had no prior information about the Rectory's history. She was alone in Streatham for the first session. She produced: a French name ("Lairre"), a date of death (May 17, 1667), a cause (strangulation), a perpetrator (Waldegrave), an origin (Havre), a nunnery (Bures). The Sunex Amures fire prediction — sent to Price in writing before the fire — is the only advance-documented fulfilled prediction in the Borley record. Price regards it as the strongest single piece of evidence for something genuinely supernormal.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Helen Glanville** (later **Helen Carter**) was the only daughter of Sidney H. Glanville, the chief official observer during Harry Price's 1937–1938 tenancy at Borley Rectory [1, 2]. A 27-year-old woman at the time of the investigation, she played a pivotal, behind-the-scenes role in establishing some of the most famous lore associated with the Borley hauntings [3]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the sources reveals the following biographical details, events, quotes, and controversies regarding her involvement:

**Lesser-Known Biographical Details**
Helen lived at the Glanville family home in Streatham, South London, and later resided at Ivy Cottage in Fittleworth, and in Pulborough, West Sussex [2, 4, 5]. She married George Carter, a man who notably had zero interest in the Borley case and had never even read Harry Price’s books [2]. A lesser-known biographical fact is that she was a personal friend of Arthur H. Foyster’s daughter [4]. 

**The Planchette Séances and the "Marie Lairre" Legend**
Helen’s primary contribution to the Borley investigation did not occur at the Rectory itself, but rather at her home in Streatham, where she passed the time experimenting with a planchette board [2, 6]. Although she had never used a planchette before, she decided to try it alone on **October 28, 1937**, after her father showed her some relatively unsuccessful scripts obtained at the Rectory [3, 5, 7]. 

Her solo séance produced remarkable results that effectively formed the entire "French Roman Catholic Nun" legend [8]. Without a question being asked, the very first word the planchette wrote was **"Marianne"** (the name of the former rector's wife, which had frequently appeared in mysterious wall-writings at Borley) [3, 9]. The communicating entity identified herself as a nun named **"Lairre"** from a convent in Le Havre (spelled "Haiv" in the scripts) [7, 10, 11]. The entity claimed to have been murdered by strangulation on **May 17, 1667**, requested "Mass and prayers", and gave the investigators a highly specific directive to search for her remains in the **"well in the cellar"** that had been **"filled in"** [7, 12, 13]. This specific clue directly inspired Harry Price's famous 1943 cellars excavations [12].

**The "Sunex Amures" Prophecy**
On **March 27, 1938**, Helen and her brother Roger held another planchette séance at Streatham [14, 15]. During this sitting, they received a famous, threatening prophecy from an entity calling itself **"Sunex Amures"**. The entity provided the specific quote:
> **"Sunex Amures and one of his men... mean to burn the rectory to-night at 9 o'clock end of the haunting go to the rectory and you will be able to see us enter into our own and under the ruins you will find bone of murdered..."** [15, 16]

When Helen asked which room the fire would start in, the planchette replied, **"Over the hall"** [15, 17]. Although the Rectory did not burn that night, it was destroyed by a disastrous fire exactly eleven months later, on February 27, 1939—a fire that started in the hallway [14, 18].

**Blind Testing and Other Séances**
Helen conducted several other important sessions. On **December 12 and 19, 1937**, she and Roger communicated with an entity named "Henry", who confirmed that a heap of bones belonging to "Lairre" would be found under the cellar floor [19-21]. To test the validity of the planchette, on **January 8, 1938**, Helen and Squadron-Leader A. J. Cuthbert acted as sitters while Roger Glanville silently wrote questions on paper so the operators would have no idea what was being asked. During this blind test, an entity identifying itself as "Jeanne Waldegrave" communicated with them [21, 22].

**Contradictions and Skepticism**
The highly skeptical authors of the 1956 *Borley Report* (Dingwall, Goldney, and Hall) heavily criticized Helen's planchette scripts. They argued that the messages originated entirely in the subconscious mind of the operator and pointed out a logical flaw in the evidence: because the scripts consisted mostly of direct answers to questions rather than spontaneous statements, they argued that Miss Glanville **"must have known a good deal about Borley which enabled her to ask so many detailed questions"** [23, 24]. 

**Later Views and Quotes**
Helen remained involved enough to attend Harry Price's lantern slide lecture at the Ghost Club on **April 18, 1939** [25]. Years later, following her father's death and the publication of the highly critical *Borley Report*, Helen wrote a letter to investigator Peter Underwood on **February 19, 1959** [4]. In it, she revealed the lesser-known detail that her mother had **"always disliked Borley and everything to do with it"** [4]. 

Defending Harry Price and her father's work, she provided a definitive quote expressing her disgust with the S.P.R.'s *Borley Report*:
> **"I hated the whole thing and thought it a disgraceful and underhand attack on Price, the entire book was nothing but criticism taken as truth, except Price’s. I can’t remember now if my father read the complete book or not, but I know that the part he saw changed very little his conviction that some very strange and unexplained things happened at Borley."** [4]
