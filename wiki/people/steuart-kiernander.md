# Steuart F.C. Kiernander

Also recorded in the sources under variant spellings including **Steuart Kiernan**, **Stewart Kiernan**, and **Steuart Kierrander** [1, 3, 4, 6, 8]. A dedicated lay investigator with considerable experience who resided in Bramhall, Cheshire [1, 2]. A member of [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s original [[ghost-club|Ghost Club]] (Number 80), and later Life Member of the Ghost Club Society [1]. He was a close friend and fellow investigator of [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] for approximately forty years — one of the most enduring associations in Underwood's investigative career [1].

## Borley Involvement

Kiernander first visited [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] in early 1939 when he met [[captain-gregson|Captain William Gregson]], the last owner of the Rectory, shortly after the [[fire-1939|fire]]. During this visit, the door was opened by a girl (presumably one of Gregson's nieces) who reported the family had been hearing unexplained footsteps and noises [3].

During a later visit to the ruins in the 1940s, Kiernander salvaged a tangle of bell-wires and one of the Bull family's wine bottles, which he later gave to [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] as souvenirs [4].

**September 1943 (Equipment Failure and Odours):** While being shown over the shell of the Rectory, Kiernander's torch inexplicably refused to function in the kitchen passage, despite working perfectly before he entered and after he left the ruins [5]. Additionally, outside the old study, he noticed an "overwhelming smell of something sweet and unusual, possibly a scent" — highly localised and only detectable within a six-foot area [5].

## Borley Church Porch Phenomena (1947–1949)

Following the demolition of the Rectory, Kiernander focused his attention on [[borley-church|Borley Church]], where he experienced several distinct auditory and visual anomalies:

- **The White Shape (18 September 1947):** While sitting in the church porch in the afternoon, he saw a "mysterious figure or white shape" move across his field of vision in an east-west direction [6, 7].
- **The Phantom Footsteps:** On a night of a full moon (the sources date this variously to 7 September 1947 [6] or 7–8 September 1949 [8, 14]), Kiernander had been sitting in the church porch for about half an hour when he heard heavy footsteps hurriedly approaching. The sounds ceased instantly when he stood up, and the path was empty. Half an hour later the footsteps returned, coming to within a yard or two of the porch; he could clearly hear the "swish of dry grass as well as the even thuds on the turf" [8, 9]. Rushing outside to find the path bathed in bright moonlight and completely deserted, Kiernander recreated the event the next day with the Rev. [[rev-ac-henning|Alfred Henning]], whose normal footsteps on the grass verge sounded similar but were noticeably heavier [8, 9]. He recounted this experience to the Ghost Club in January 1955 [8].
- **Taps and Clicks (September 1949):** Later that same month, while sitting alone in the porch at approximately 9:00 p.m., he heard a "considerable number of taps and clicks" on the church door and from the rafters in the porch roof — sounds he never heard again before or after [10].

## Excavations

Kiernander participated in post-Price excavations at the Borley site in the 1950s, in the company of [[len-sewell|Len Sewell]] and [[philip-paul|Philip Paul]]. [[leonard-rayner|Leonard Rayner]] regarded Kiernander as the best man involved in the Paul diggings [11].

## Opinions and Interviews

Kiernander offered a practical counter-argument to the sceptical theory that the famous lights seen in the Rectory windows were merely reflections from passing trains or cars. He reminded researchers that at the time of the sightings, the Rectory was **"almost surrounded by high trees which would negate such an explanation"** [2].

He accompanied [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] on two significant interviews: visiting [[constance-price|Harry Price's widow Constance]] at Arun Bank on 20 October 1968 [1, 12], and interviewing the former medium Guy L'Estrange at Bungay on 23 August 1969 [1, 13].

## Other Investigations

Kiernander's other haunted house investigations included Newark Park, Ringley Vicarage, a Nottingham Council house, Bretforton Manor, Chingle Hall, Loweton Manor, Berrier Pomfret Castle, Bovey House, and Eton Vicarage. He also took a keen interest in the [[langenhoe]] haunting alongside [[len-sewell|Len Sewell]] and [[john-dening|John Dening]].
