# Steuart F.C. Kiernander

A dedicated lay investigator with considerable experience. A member of the Harry Price [[ghost-club]], Number 80, and later Life Member of the Ghost Club Society.

## Borley Involvement

Kiernander first visited Borley in 1939 when he met [[captain-gregson]], the last owner of [[borley-rectory]]. He also experienced apparent psychic activity himself at Borley of a minor type (see Chronology, 18 September 1947 & 7–8 September 1949).

He visited [[guy-lestrange]] with [[peter-underwood]] and also [[constance-price]], widow of Harry Price. Steuart Kiernander and his wife Freda were friends and fellow investigators with Peter Underwood for around forty years — among the most enduring associations in Underwood's investigative career.

## Excavations

Kiernander participated in excavations at the Borley site in the 1950s, in the company of [[len-sewell]] and [[paul-philip|Philip Paul]]. [[leonard-rayner]] regarded Kiernander as the best man involved in the Paul diggings.

## Rescued Relics

Kiernander rescued from the Rectory ruins following a visit in the 1940s a tangle of bell-wires and one of the Bull wine bottles. These were given as souvenirs to [[peter-underwood]].

## Other Investigations

Kiernander's other haunted house investigations included Newark Park, Ringley Vicarage, a Nottingham Council house, Bretforton Manor, Chingle Hall, Loweton Manor, Berrier Pomfret Castle, Bovey House and Eton Vicarage (see Peter Underwood's *Nights in Haunted Houses*, 1994). He also took a keen interest in the [[langenhoe]] haunting alongside [[len-sewell]] and [[john-dening]].
