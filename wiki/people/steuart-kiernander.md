# Steuart F.C. Kiernander

A dedicated lay investigator with considerable experience. A member of the Harry Price [[ghost-club]], Number 80, and later Life Member of the Ghost Club Society.

## Borley Involvement

Kiernander first visited Borley in 1939 when he met [[captain-gregson]], the last owner of [[borley-rectory]]. He also experienced apparent psychic activity himself at Borley of a minor type (see Chronology, 18 September 1947 & 7–8 September 1949).

He visited [[guy-lestrange]] with [[peter-underwood]] and also [[constance-price]], widow of Harry Price. Steuart Kiernander and his wife Freda were friends and fellow investigators with Peter Underwood for around forty years — among the most enduring associations in Underwood's investigative career.

## Excavations

Kiernander participated in excavations at the Borley site in the 1950s, in the company of [[len-sewell]] and [[paul-philip|Philip Paul]]. [[leonard-rayner]] regarded Kiernander as the best man involved in the Paul diggings.

## Rescued Relics

Kiernander rescued from the Rectory ruins following a visit in the 1940s a tangle of bell-wires and one of the Bull wine bottles. These were given as souvenirs to [[peter-underwood]].

## Other Investigations

Kiernander's other haunted house investigations included Newark Park, Ringley Vicarage, a Nottingham Council house, Bretforton Manor, Chingle Hall, Loweton Manor, Berrier Pomfret Castle, Bovey House and Eton Vicarage (see Peter Underwood's *Nights in Haunted Houses*, 1994). He also took a keen interest in the [[langenhoe]] haunting alongside [[len-sewell]] and [[john-dening]].

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Steuart F.C. Kiernander** (frequently recorded in the sources under variant spellings such as **Steuart Kiernan**, **Stewart Kiernan**, and **Steuart Kierrander**) was a highly experienced lay investigator and a prominent figure in the post-Price era of Borley Rectory research. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following biographical details, specific investigations, phenomena experienced, quotes, and textual contradictions regarding his involvement:

**Biographical Details and Background**
*   **Personal Life:** Kiernander resided in Bramhall, Cheshire, and his wife's name was Freda [1, 2]. 
*   **Ghost Club Memberships:** He was a dedicated investigator who became a member of Harry Price’s original Ghost Club (holding member Number 80) and later a Life Member of the Ghost Club Society [1]. 
*   **Colleagues:** He was a close friend and fellow investigator of Peter Underwood for approximately forty years [1]. 
*   **Other Investigations:** Beyond Borley, Kiernander investigated numerous other haunted properties, including Newark Park, Ringley Vicarage, Chingle Hall, Bretforton Manor, Loweton Manor, and Bovey House [1].

**Early Visits and Experiences at the Rectory (1939–1943)**
*   **Early 1939:** Kiernander first visited Borley Rectory shortly after the fire and met the owner, Captain William Gregson [1]. During this visit, the door was opened by a girl (presumably one of Gregson's nieces) who showed him around the house and reported that the family had been hearing unexplained footsteps and noises [3]. 
*   **Rescued Artifacts:** During a visit to the ruins in the 1940s, Kiernander salvaged a tangle of bell-wires and one of the Bull family's wine bottles, which he later gave to Peter Underwood as souvenirs [4].
*   **September 1943 (Equipment Failure and Odours):** While being shown over the shell of the Rectory, Kiernander's torch inexplicably refused to function in the kitchen passage, despite working perfectly before he entered and after he left the ruins [5]. Additionally, outside the old study, he noticed an "overwhelming smell of something sweet and unusual, possibly a scent" [5]. He could find no cause for the odour, which was highly localized and only detectable within a six-foot area [5].

**Borley Church Porch Phenomena (1947–1949)**
Following the demolition of the Rectory, Kiernander focused his attention on Borley Church, where he experienced several distinct auditory and visual anomalies:
*   **The White Shape (18 September 1947):** While sitting in the church porch in the afternoon, he saw a "mysterious figure or white shape" move across his field of vision in an east-west direction [6, 7].
*   **The Phantom Footsteps (7 September 1949):** On a night of a full moon, Kiernander had been sitting in the church porch for about half an hour when he heard heavy footsteps hurriedly approaching [6, 8]. The sounds ceased instantly when he stood up, and the path was empty [6, 8]. Half an hour later, the footsteps returned, coming to within a yard or two of the porch; Kiernander could clearly hear the "swish of dry grass as well as the even thuds on the turf" [8, 9]. Thinking it might be the rector, the Rev. Alfred Henning, Kiernander rushed outside so as not to startle him in the dark porch, only to find the path bathed in bright moonlight and completely deserted [8, 9]. The next day, he and Henning recreated the event; Henning's normal footsteps on the grass verge sounded similar but were noticeably heavier [8, 9]. He later recounted this experience to the Ghost Club in January 1955 [8].
*   **Taps and Clicks (September 1949):** Later that same month, while sitting alone in the porch at approximately 9:00 p.m., he heard a "considerable number of taps and clicks" on the church door and from the rafters in the porch roof [10]. He noted that despite sitting in the church many times before and afterward, he never heard anything like those noises again [10].

**Opinions, Quotes, and Later Involvements**
*   **The "Mysterious Light" Theory:** Kiernander offered a practical counter-argument to the skeptical theory that the famous lights seen in the Rectory windows were merely reflections from passing trains or cars. He reminded researchers that at the time of the sightings, the Rectory was "almost surrounded by high trees which would negate such an explanation" [2].
*   **Excavations:** Kiernander closely followed the post-Price excavations at Borley and stated that he regarded L.G. Rayner as "the best man involved in the Paul diggings" [11].
*   **Interviews:** He accompanied Peter Underwood on two significant interviews: visiting Harry Price's widow, Constance, at Arun Bank on 20 October 1968 [1, 12], and interviewing the former medium Guy L'Estrange at Bungay on 23 August 1969 [1, 13].

**Textual Contradictions and Anomalies**
*   **Name Variations:** The sources are highly inconsistent with his surname, referring to him interchangeably as **Steuart F.C. Kiernander** [1], **Steuart Kiernan** [3, 5, 6], **Stewart Kiernan** [8], and **Steuart Kierrander** [4]. 
*   **Date of the Footsteps Incident:** There is a distinct chronological contradiction regarding the September 7th footsteps incident. The narrative text in the *Borley Rectory Companion* places the event directly after an August 1947 incident, implying the footsteps occurred in **1947** [6]. However, the book's own Chronology section and the text of *The Ghosts of Borley* explicitly date the 7-8 September footsteps under the full moon to **1949** [8, 14].
