# Fred Cartwright

A journeyman carpenter from Sudbury. One of the most significant "independent" witnesses in the Borley case — he had no prior knowledge of the haunting when he saw the Nun.

## The Sightings (Autumn 1927)

Cartwright was working at farm buildings between Borley and Clare. Each morning he walked past [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] before dawn (c. 6:45 am) on his way to work. The Rectory was empty at this time — between [[harry-bull|Harry Bull's]] death and the [[rev-eric-smith|Smiths']] arrival.

He saw [[the-nun|a Sister of Mercy]] at the drive gate on **four separate occasions**:
1. First morning: standing very still by the gatepost; appeared perfectly normal
2. Following Friday: same spot; eyes closed; assumed she was resting after nursing the sick
3. Following Wednesday: pale, tired, ill-looking; eyes closed; turned back to offer help — she had vanished
4. Final occasion: glimpsed her and decided to speak; she vanished before he reached the gate; he searched the entire drive — found no one (the Rectory was empty)

He told his landlord and others that same evening. It was *only then* that he heard the stories of the Borley haunting for the first time.

## Significance

Cartwright is the strongest example in the book of an independent witness who saw the Nun before knowing anything about the case. Critics tried to dismiss him as a pub-story spinner who had "had a few" when he told Price the story "over a pint." The authors note: he told his landlord and others the same evening of the experiences, before any publicity existed.

## Reliability

- His accounts were given to Price approximately three years after the events
- No corroboration from other witnesses
- Price made some attempts to trace him later, without success
- Despite this, his pre-knowledge innocence is hard to explain away
