# James Ballantyne

[[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s chauffeur, who drove the party to [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] on 13 October 1931 for Price's first visit during the [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]] incumbency. The party comprised Price, [[mrs-henry-richards|Mrs Henry Richards]], [[kathleen-goldney|Mrs K. M. Goldney]], [[miss-may-walker|Miss May Walker]], and Ballantyne, all of whom stayed at the Bull Hotel in [[long-melford|Long Melford]] during the visit.

Ballantyne was present as an observer for the all-night sitting during which the entire household was confined to the kitchen after the wine-into-ink and bell-ringing incidents. He is named once and no further details about him appear in *Ghosts of Borley*.

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**James Ballantyne** was a chauffeur employed by Miss May Walker, a friend of the psychical researcher Mrs. Henry Richards [1]. He drove a party consisting of Harry Price, Mrs. Kathleen (Mollie) Goldney, Mrs. Richards, and Miss Walker to Borley Rectory for an overnight investigation on **October 13, 1931** [1, 2]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following biographical details, events, and specific quotes regarding his involvement in the case:

**Biographical Details and Character**
Price described Ballantyne as a **"dour, unexcitable old Scotsman"** whom a hundred black hands could not move [3]. He had no real interest in ghosts or the investigation itself; rather than joining Price and the council members on their tour of the Rectory, he preferred to sit downstairs and read [4]. Later in his career, the paranormal investigator Peter Underwood interviewed Ballantyne, citing the chauffeur as one of the many direct witnesses who provided him with a tremendous insight into the Borley mystery [5]. 

**The "Grisly Black Hand" Incident**
While the rest of the party was exploring the house on the evening of October 13, Ballantyne ensconced himself on a chair by the kitchen fire to quietly read his evening newspaper [6]. Looking up—perhaps disturbed by a sound—his eyes traveled toward the kitchen door near the dresser, where he was surprised to see a **"grisly black hand" move slowly up and down between the door and the jamb** [6]. The manifestation occurred twice, and whatever appendage or body was attached to the hand remained completely hidden behind the door [6].

**Reactions and Specific Quotes**
Despite the startling nature of the apparition, Ballantyne remained entirely unfazed. Because he knew their mission concerned a haunted house and had heard the Rectory's history on the drive down from London, he assumed the black hand was "just part of the business" and simply went back to reading his newspaper [3, 4]. 

When the investigators later questioned him about the event and asked if he had been afraid, Ballantyne flatly replied, **"No"** [3]. During that same evening, he also heard the loud smash of a glass bottle falling in the house, but remained completely unbothered by the noise, summarizing his experiences with the specific quote that he knew **"it was them ghosts larking about"** [3].
