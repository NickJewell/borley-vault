# Rev Edward Lanfranc Morgan Mathias MA

The last rector of Borley and Liston (1955–1967), and husband of co-author [[claudine-mathias]]. He died before the book was written; CM's account provides the only record of his incumbency at this level of detail.

## Background

Born in Hampshire of Welsh descent, his lifelong ambition was to join the clergy. Educated at Cambridge (two degrees, including attendance at Cheshunt Theological College); reputation as a sportsman (rugby union, badminton). He married [[claudine-mathias|Claudine]] on 3 July 1946; they had a son and two daughters. Served curacies at the Church of the Holy Redeemer, Lamorbey, Sidcup, and the Church of St Michael and All Angels, Barnes, Surrey. First living: St Philip's, Sydenham, south-east London, patron being King George VI.

Mathias and his wife first became acquainted with Borley during a month-long locum at nearby Belchamp St Paul. Having read [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s book *The Most Haunted House in England* at the local library, they visited the tiny church and the remains of the Rectory. In early 1955, Lanfranc underwent major surgery at Beckenham Hospital, making another busy London living impossible. [[canon-edward-powell|Canon Edward Powell]] of Belchamp St Paul suggested the vacancy at Borley and Liston; the Bishop of Southwark recommended the appointment.

## Incumbency at Borley and Liston (1955–1967)

Inducted at [[borley-church|Borley Church]] on **28 July 1955** — the exact 55th anniversary of the famous [[the-nun|Nun]] sighting by the four Bull sisters, and the traditional "appearance date." The bishop was unaware of the significance; a crowd of journalists was waiting outside after the service.

Before the induction, Bishop Faulkner Allison of Chelmsford gave Lanfranc firm but friendly advice: his first loyalty should be pastoral care of his parishioners, not investigation of the paranormal — noting that the previous incumbent had spent too much time on the supernatural. Lanfranc agreed entirely, and kept his thoughts on the haunting close to his chest to avoid controversy.

Like his predecessor [[rev-ac-henning|Rev. A. C. Henning]], Mathias chose not to live in Borley but resided in the older [[liston-rectory|Liston Rectory]]. The house had no mains electricity and relied on an old generator; Mathias took personal responsibility for cleaning and maintaining the family's collection of oil lamps. He never learned to drive a car and used a bicycle throughout his incumbency — both he and CM learned to cycle for the rural parish. After completing his pastoral visits he would often rest in Borley Church before cycling home. Despite his dedication, he was pettily barred from giving voluntary religious instruction at the nearby Foxearth Church of England Primary School by its authoritarian headmistress, [[edna-backhouse|Miss Edna Backhouse]].

He handled many requests from psychical researchers and television companies for access to the church, granting permission to responsible groups and declining sensation-seekers who were increasingly causing vandalism to the church and the graves of the Bull family.

## Attitude Towards the Haunting

Although Mathias publicly deferred to his bishop's guidance, he privately believed in the haunting. He said that on occasions when he was "alone" in Borley church he sometimes felt **another presence** there — which he attributed to the cumulative effect of centuries of prayer and worship, declining to claim anything overtly supernatural. When the local relief organist [[maud-stearn|Maud Stearn]] described hearing the organ playing in an empty and locked church (1955), Lanfranc's protective reaction regarding his family was: "It would be best if the children did not hear about this."

## Specific Incidents

**The Parchment Discovery:** Sometime in the late 1950s, a visitor, Mrs Phyllis Cowan-Douglas, found an old piece of parchment with mysterious writing lying directly on top of the [[waldegrave-monument|Waldegrave tomb]] in an otherwise empty church. When she wrote to Mathias about the strange object, he confessed that he "had not heard of such a happening in the church before."

## Departure and Legacy

After 12½ years at Borley and Liston, Lanfranc moved to his last living at [[gestingthorpe|Gestingthorpe]], another nearby north Essex village, resigning from the Borley living in **1967**. He was always in delicate health following his 1955 surgery. After his departure, the church authorities combined Borley and Liston with Foxearth and Pentlow; [[canon-leslie-pennell|Canon Leslie Pennell]] became the first incumbent of the four-parish group. Mathias thus holds the historical distinction of being the last rector to administer Borley and Liston as their own independent combined parish.
