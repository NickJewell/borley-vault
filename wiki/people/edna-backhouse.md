# Edna Backhouse

Headmistress of the Church of England Primary School at nearby Foxearth, remembered by Claudine Mathias. She reported an unusual paranormal experience at Borley Church in the autumn of 1947.

## Character

Edna Backhouse was, in CM's description, the old-fashioned type of authoritarian teacher who had her favourites and unfavourites. She is noted — with some asperity — for having refused to allow the [[rev-lanfranc-mathias|Rev Lanfranc Mathias]] to give voluntary religious instruction at what was technically a Church of England school. Despite her strict manner, the authors regard her as neither a fool nor a liar.

## The Borley Church Experience (~1947)

Miss Backhouse visited Borley Church to see the recently restored stone altar (the *mensa*), which the [[rev-ac-henning|Rev Henning]] had worked to reinstate in its original position. As she walked towards the altar she felt conscious of a "presence" near her, then heard footsteps following her.

When she turned and walked away from the altar, she again heard the footsteps following. The sound ceased when she left the church. She noted the following:
- The aisle was carpeted
- She was certain no one else was present in the building
- The footsteps were **uneven**, as if the person responsible walked with a limp

The authors note that in his last years, [[henry-bull|the Rev Henry Dawson Ellis Bull]] had a pronounced limp. He died in 1892, aged only 59. However, they are careful not to over-speculate about identification.

## Significance

The Backhouse account contributes to the body of evidence for paranormal footstep phenomena in [[borley-church]], adding the specific detail of the asymmetric gait — a feature not found in most other footstep reports.
