# Edna Backhouse

Headmistress of the Church of England Primary School at nearby Foxearth, remembered by Claudine Mathias. She reported an unusual paranormal experience at [[borley-church|Borley Church]] in the autumn of 1947.

## Character

The sources record a minor geographical contradiction regarding her position, describing her both as a "school teacher from Sudbury" and as the headmistress of the Church of England Primary School in the nearby village of Foxearth [1, 2]. [[claudine-mathias|Claudine Mathias]] remembered Miss Backhouse personally, though "not with any excessive degree of affection" [2]. She described her as a "formidable" woman and the "old type of authoritarian teacher with her favourite and unfavourite pupils" [2, 3]. Despite her strict manner, she demonstrated a surprising level of "pettiness" by refusing to allow the [[rev-lanfranc-mathias|Rev Lanfranc Mathias]] to give voluntary religious instruction at what was technically a Church of England school [2].

The authors of *The Final Analysis* maintain her credibility as a witness regardless, arguing that her inflexibility "does not mean that she was a fool or a liar" [3].

## The Borley Church Experience (~1947)

Miss Backhouse's paranormal experience occurred sometime between 22 August and 15 October 1947 [1]. She visited [[borley-church|Borley Church]] specifically to see the recently restored stone altar (the *mensa*), which the [[rev-ac-henning|Rev Henning]] had worked to reinstate in its original position.

As she entered the empty church and walked down the carpeted aisle toward the altar, she became acutely conscious of a "presence" near her, then heard the distinct sound of footsteps tracking her from the porch door to the chancel step [1, 3]. When she turned around and walked away from the altar, the footsteps followed her back [3]. The sounds only ceased when she physically exited the building [3]. She was absolutely convinced that no one else was inside the church with her at the time [3].

## The "Limping" Footsteps

A highly specific detail distinguishes this account from most other footstep reports. She described the sounds as **"uneven, as if whoever was responsible for them walked with a limp"** [3]. The aisle was carpeted, which makes the report of audible footsteps the more striking.

The authors note that in his last years, [[henry-bull|the Rev Henry Dawson Ellis Bull]] had a pronounced limp. He died in 1892, aged only 59. However, they are careful not to over-speculate about identification.

## Significance

The Backhouse account contributes to the body of evidence for paranormal footstep phenomena in [[borley-church]], adding the specific detail of the asymmetric gait — a feature not found in most other footstep reports. It belongs to the cluster of Church phenomena from the post-rectory-fire period, overlapping with the [[mysterious-footsteps]] reported in the same months by other witnesses.
