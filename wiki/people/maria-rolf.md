# Maria Rolf (or Rolt)

Swiss governess listed at Borley Rectory in the **1881 Borley parish census**, born in **Lausanne, Switzerland**. She is officially recorded on the census returns as a **"visitor"** with occupation **"governess"** [2]. Because she was recorded as a visitor, the duration of her actual stay cannot be readily ascertained [2]. Because the name "Rolf" (spelled without an 'e') is German, researchers conclude she was "almost certainly German Swiss" [2]. Her presence at the Rectory in 1881 — and possibly still in 1888, the year of [[kate-boreham|Kate Boreham]]'s death — makes her a witness candidate in [[ivan-banks|Banks's]] theory about the Katie episode.

## Banks's Analysis

Banks drew attention to Maria Rolf in the context of his analysis of the October 1937 séance sessions. He proposed several speculative possibilities:

1. **The "Jane" entity** in the table-tipping sessions may be Maria Rolf, not Mrs Caroline Sarah Bull — she may have known the truth about what happened to [[kate-boreham|Kate Boreham]] and be trying to communicate it [6].
2. The séance reply **"LUNOLOWTUMNALITYC"** — which Banks tentatively deciphered as incorporating the German expression "Limmel" (ruffian) — could be connected to Maria Rolf's Swiss-German background [2, 4].
3. There is a "very real possibility" that Maria Rolf was still living at the Rectory in **1888**, the year the maid [[kate-boreham|Kate Boreham]] died tragically. If so, she "might well have been aware of what really happened to Kate" [5].
4. **An alternative identity for "Marie Lairre":** Because Maria Rolf was born in Lausanne, Banks presents a "fresh possibility" that the planchette name was never meant to be [[marie-lairre|Marie Lairre]] at all, but rather was **"intended to read Maria-Lausanne, or part thereof"** [3]. If true, this completely disconnects the famous séance messages from the Borley nun legend [3]. Banks regards this as speculative.

## Significance

No further records of Maria Rolf have been found beyond the 1881 census entry. Banks's theories assign her a potentially central role in the séance communications, but all four propositions remain speculative — each resting on the prior assumption that she remained at the Rectory for years beyond the one census snapshot that confirms her presence. The name "Rolf" (without the E) is German; her place of birth, Lausanne, is in the German-French border region of Switzerland, which is consistent with German-language knowledge.
