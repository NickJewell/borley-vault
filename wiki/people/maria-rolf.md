# Maria Rolf (or Rolt)

Swiss governess listed at Borley Rectory in the **1881 Borley parish census**, born in **Lausanne, Switzerland**. Described as a "visitor" with occupation "governess." Her presence at the Rectory in 1881 — and possibly still in 1888, the year of [[Kate Boreham|Kate Boreham]]'s death — makes her a witness candidate in [[Ivan Banks|Banks's]] theory about the Katie episode.

## Banks's Analysis

Banks drew attention to Maria Rolf in the context of his analysis of the October 1937 séance sessions. He proposed two possibilities:

1. **The "Jane" entity** in the table-tipping sessions may be Maria Rolf, not Mrs Caroline Sarah Bull — she may have known the truth about what happened to Kate Boreham and be trying to communicate it
2. The séance reply "**LUNOLOWTUMNALITYC**" — which Banks tentatively read as possibly German (Lügner = liar; Lümmel = ruffian) — could be connected to Maria Rolf's Swiss-German background

Banks also raised the possibility that "Marie Lairre" might be a mis-transcription of "Maria-Lausanne" or similar — though he regards this as speculative.

The name "Rolf" (without the E) is German. Her place of birth, Lausanne, is in the German-French border region of Switzerland.

## Significance

If Maria Rolf was still at the Rectory in 1888, she would have been a witness to events surrounding Kate Boreham's alleged death. Her presence in the German-language séance responses could, Banks suggests, indicate her as an unexpected source of contact at the 1937 sessions.

No further records of Maria Rolf have been found beyond the 1881 census entry.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Maria Rolf** (also speculated to be **Maria Rolt**) is a lesser-known historical figure connected to Borley Rectory whose existence was uncovered through the **1881 Borley parish census returns** [1, 2]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following biographical details and the significant theoretical roles researchers have assigned to her regarding the Borley hauntings:

**Biographical Details**
*   **Origin and Nationality:** The 1881 census records her place of birth as **Lausanne, Switzerland** [2, 3]. Because the name "Rolf" (spelled without an 'e') is German, researchers conclude that she was "almost certainly German Swiss" [2]. 
*   **Occupation at Borley:** She is officially listed on the census returns as a **"visitor"**, with her occupation recorded as a **"governess"** [2]. 
*   **Duration of Stay:** Because she was recorded as a visitor, the texts note that it cannot be readily ascertained exactly how long she remained at the Rectory [2].

**Theoretical Connections to the Borley Haunting**
In the book *The Enigma of Borley Rectory*, the author proposes several fascinating theories that place Maria Rolf at the center of some of Borley's greatest séance mysteries:

*   **The "Limmel" (Ruffian) Séance Message:** During one of the séances, a confusing message deciphered as "LUNOLOWTUMNAL" was produced [4]. The author suggests this may have incorporated the German expression **"Limmel"** (a version of ruffian), theorizing that Maria Rolf’s German-Swiss background is where this specific German expression "might come into play" [2, 4].
*   **The Death of Kate Boreham:** The author suggests there is a "very real possibility" that Maria Rolf was still living at the Rectory seven years later in **1888**—the year the maid Kate Boreham died tragically [5]. If she was still the governess, the text speculates that she **"might well have been aware of what really happened to Kate"** [5].
*   **The Unrecognized Séance Communicator:** When analyzing specific table-tipping questions (Q374 to Q388) that centered on Katie Boreham's dead baby and the Rectory well, the author senses "an indefinable something" suggesting the responding entity wasn't Katie Boreham at all [6]. Instead, the author theorizes the communicator was actually **"the formerly unknown and ignored Swiss governess, Maria Rolf or Rolt!"** [6].
*   **An Alternative Identity for "Marie Lairre":** Maria Rolf's background provides a major contradiction to the popular legend of the murdered French nun, "Marie Lairre" [3]. Because Maria Rolf was born in Lausanne, the text presents a "fresh possibility" that the name produced by the planchette was never meant to be Marie Lairre, but rather was **"intended to read Maria-Lausanne, or part thereof"** [3]. If true, this completely disconnects the famous séance messages from the Borley nun legend [3].
