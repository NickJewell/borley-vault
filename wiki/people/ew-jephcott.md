# E. W. Jephcott

A resident of Alcester, Warwickshire, who became a secondary but corroborating witness to the phantom organ music phenomenon at [[borley-church|Borley Church]] in September 1947.

## The Phantom Organ Music (16 September 1947)

On the afternoon of Tuesday 16 September 1947, at approximately 3:30 p.m., Jephcott and a friend visited the site of the ruined [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] [1]. After inspecting the ruins, the two men walked across the road to [[borley-church|Borley Church]]. As they were walking up the church pathway, Jephcott's companion suddenly announced, "the organ's playing" [1].

A notable specific point: **Jephcott himself could hear absolutely no music** — only his companion reported it [1]. Despite this, when the two men entered the church, they found it completely empty and the organ closed [1, 2]. Jephcott's friend maintained that his impression of the organ music was so strong and distinct that he had actually hesitated to enter the building, assuming a funeral service was in progress [1]. Crucially, Jephcott later confirmed that his companion **had not read Harry Price's book and had no prior knowledge of the local legends** claiming that ghostly organ music and singing were frequently heard issuing from the empty church [1].

## A Second Visit (19 September 1947)

Three days later Jephcott returned to Borley [3, 4]. This follow-up visit was uneventful; he **"had no paranormal experience"** on this second occasion [3, 4].

## Letter to Reverend Henning

Jephcott's experience remained private until he read an article in *The East Anglian Daily Times* published on 24 October 1947 [5]. The article detailed a nearly identical experience had by the [[rev-ac-henning|Rev Alfred Clifford Henning]] and novelist [[norah-walrond|Mrs Norah Walrond (Norah Burke)]], who also heard phantom organ music while walking up the same church path in October of that year [5, 6]. Prompted by this, Jephcott wrote to the Rev Henning a few days later, noting that Henning and Walrond's encounter provided **"astonishing confirmation"** of what had happened to his friend [5]:

> "You will see from this how remarkably similar this was to Mrs Walrond's experience. I should be much interested to know whether you yourself heard the music and whether any definite tune was apparent. If the playing should recur, it might be possible to take note of the melody and get it identified."

Jephcott's letter, reproduced in Henning's booklet *Haunted Borley* and discussed by Babbs and Mathias, is reproduced in Chapter 5 of *Borley Rectory: The Final Analysis*.

## Context

The Jephcott letter provides independent corroboration of the organ phenomenon at Borley Church reported in the same period by Henning and Mrs Walrond in October 1947, and later by [[thomas-musk|Thomas Musk]] in 1987. In each case, the church was confirmed to be empty and the instrument locked. The experience was one-sided on this occasion — only Jephcott's unnamed companion heard the music while Jephcott himself did not.
