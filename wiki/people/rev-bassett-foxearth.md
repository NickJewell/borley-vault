# Rev Bassett (Rector of Foxearth)

The Rector of Foxearth — the adjacent parish — during the Foyster incumbency at [[borley-rectory]]. He is mentioned in [[marianne-foyster]]'s account as the person about whom [[francois-darles]] (Frank Pearless) became "fiendishly jealous," using him as a pretext for threats against Marianne.

## Clarke's account

In essay 14, Clarke quotes Marianne from the Swanson interviews:

> *'He said that it was a God damn lie, and that he knew, he knew, he knew. He was always given to snapping his fingers and making gestures….At that time he was accusing me of [having an affair with] a person who used to come to the rectory to visit Lionel, whom I only saw on very rare occasions… His name was Reverend Bassett, rector of Foxearth. I had said he was a very nice looking man and d'Arles was always fiendishly jealous.'*
> — Marianne Foyster, Swanson Interview, February 1958, quoted in *Sidelights*, essay 14

## Context

Frank Pearless was using Marianne's casual remark about Bassett's appearance to manufacture grounds for blackmail and emotional control over her. Clarke notes that D'Arles threatened to expose damaging information about Marianne's marital history (her prior marriage to [[harold-greenwood]] and that [[adelaide-foyster]] was adopted) if she did not comply with his demands.

Bassett himself appears to have visited the Rectory only to call on Lionel — "a person who used to come to the rectory to visit Lionel, whom I only saw on very rare occasions." There is no suggestion that Marianne had any actual relationship with him.

## Significance

Bassett is one of the few named Foxearth rectors during the Foyster period. He is distinct from the earlier [[rev-john-foster-foxearth]] (the 1871 rape-case rector). His mention establishes that normal clerical visiting between adjacent parishes continued during the Foyster incumbency, and that Frank Pearless was exploiting even innocuous pastoral contacts to extend his psychological grip on Marianne.
