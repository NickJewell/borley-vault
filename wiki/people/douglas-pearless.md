# Douglas Stuart Pearless

(Born 31 July 1928) Son of [[francois-darles|Frank Pearless]] (Francois d'Arles) and Kate Emily Fernie. When Frank Pearless went to [[borley-rectory]] under the name Francois d'Arles in March 1931, Douglas was known as Francois d'Arles Jr.

## Role at Borley

Most accounts describe Douglas as having been brought to the Rectory as a playmate for [[adelaide-foyster]]. [[edward-babbs]] suggests that it was Frank Pearless who advertised in *The Times* for a home for his young son, and the [[lionel-foyster|Foysters]] responded — though it could have been the other way around.

A famous photograph of [[harry-price]] together with [[kathleen-goldney]] and the Foysters was taken outside the Rectory in October 1931. The young boy holding [[marianne-foyster]]'s hand beside little Adelaide is almost certainly Douglas.

One of the few documented instances of phenomena directly involving Douglas: he and Adelaide were walking along the kitchen passage when a stone came tumbling down the back stairs. On another occasion both toddlers are reported to have "cowered together at the entrance to the Dining Room in some discomfort, with Adelaide mumbling that they did not like things flying about."

## After Borley

Little is known about Douglas following Frank Pearless's departure from Borley after the collapse of the [[jonquille-et-cie|Jonquille et Cie]] flower business. He appears to have married in June 1947. His subsequent life has never been traced or documented by researchers.

## Significance

Douglas Pearless's presence at Borley is primarily evidence of the domestic complexity of the [[foyster-incumbency]] and his father's deep integration into the Rectory household. Like Adelaide, he was a child witness to some of the phenomena, lending an additional dimension to the case: the events were experienced not just by adults with possible motives, but by young children with none.
