# Canon James Henry Leslie Pennell (1905–1994)

A tall, elderly Scotsman described as "not at all the sort of person who would have allowed his imagination to get out of control." Career high point: Provost of St Andrew's Cathedral, Inverness (1949–1965). He resigned this position at 60 and sought lighter duties; his wife Ursula was a native of East Anglia.

The Pennells moved to Foxearth, Essex in November 1965, where Leslie became rector of Foxearth and Pentlow — two small north Essex parishes adjacent to Borley and Liston. He later became the first incumbent of the four-parish group after [[rev-lanfranc-mathias]] resigned from Borley and Liston in 1967.

## The Dormobile Incident — 3 December 1965

A few days after arriving in Foxearth, the Pennells drove to Long Melford railway station to collect their son David. It was about 8pm, a wet evening.

On the return journey, as they reached the road between the Borley and Liston turnings, **a hooded figure walked out immediately in front of their Dormobile (small camper van)**. Ursula shouted "you fool" and Leslie braked hard. The figure was only feet from the very short bonnet. There was no impact.

They got out and searched the area thoroughly. Nobody was about.

Both described the figure as:
- Bowed, wearing a **hood and long robe**
- Colour appeared **dark grey** (allowing for headlight distortion)
- Crossed the road from right to left

David Pennell in the back seat did not see the figure and was unaware of anything until the brakes were applied.

[[claudine-mathias|CM]] says: "I believe that the Pennells' extraordinary experience defies a normal explanation."
