# Canon James Henry Leslie Pennell (1905–1994)

A tall, elderly Scotsman described as "not at all the sort of person who would have allowed his imagination to get out of control." Career high point: Provost of St Andrew's Cathedral, Inverness (1949–1965). Having decided to seek lighter responsibilities upon reaching 60, and because his wife [[ursula-pennell|Ursula]] was a native of East Anglia, the couple relocated to the region in late November 1965 [1, 2].

The Pennells moved to Foxearth, Essex in November 1965, where Leslie became rector of Foxearth and Pentlow. Following the resignation of [[rev-lanfranc-mathias|Rev. Lanfranc Mathias]] in 1967, Pennell was additionally appointed Curate-in-Charge and rector of Borley and Liston — becoming the very first priest responsible for the pastoral care of all four parishes (Foxearth, Pentlow, Borley, and Liston) as a combined group ministry [3-5]. From his incumbency onward, the priests administering Borley have permanently resided in Foxearth [3].

## The Dormobile Incident — 3 December 1965

A few days after arriving in Foxearth, the Pennells drove to Long Melford railway station to collect their son David. It was about 8pm, a wet evening. While driving on the road between the turnings to Borley and Liston, **a bowed figure wearing a hood and long dark-grey robes suddenly stepped out into the road immediately in front of their Dormobile** (a small camper van with a very short bonnet), crossing from right to left [7]. Ursula shouted "you fool" — directed at the figure, not her husband — and Leslie braked hard [7, 8]. Both believed an accident was unavoidable, but there was no impact whatsoever. They got out and searched the area thoroughly; nobody was about.

Both described the figure as:
- Bowed, wearing a **hood and long robe**
- Colour appeared **dark grey** (the Pennells pragmatically acknowledged that headlights can distort colours, which may account for discrepancies with other sightings of the "black" or "blue" nun) [7]
- Crossed the road from right to left

**David Pennell** in the back seat did not see the figure and was completely unaware of anything until his father suddenly braked [8].

> **Note:** When the incident was written up in the local press, the reporter created the inaccurate impression that Ursula Pennell had shouted "you fool" at her husband. In reality, she was addressing the mysterious figure [8].

Another source mentions ambiguously that the "last rector and his wife" were reported to have seen the phantom coach in the same area, noting they were "evidently unwilling to discuss it" [9].

[[claudine-mathias|CM]] says: "I believe that the Pennells' extraordinary experience defies a normal explanation."

On June 8, 1968, psychical researcher [[grant-vallender|Grant Vallender]] visited Foxearth Rectory and spoke with Canon Pennell for about half an hour regarding his knowledge of the case [10].
