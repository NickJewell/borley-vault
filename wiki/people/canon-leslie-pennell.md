# Canon James Henry Leslie Pennell (1905–1994)

A tall, elderly Scotsman described as "not at all the sort of person who would have allowed his imagination to get out of control." Career high point: Provost of St Andrew's Cathedral, Inverness (1949–1965). He resigned this position at 60 and sought lighter duties; his wife Ursula was a native of East Anglia.

The Pennells moved to Foxearth, Essex in November 1965, where Leslie became rector of Foxearth and Pentlow — two small north Essex parishes adjacent to Borley and Liston. He later became the first incumbent of the four-parish group after [[rev-lanfranc-mathias]] resigned from Borley and Liston in 1967.

## The Dormobile Incident — 3 December 1965

A few days after arriving in Foxearth, the Pennells drove to Long Melford railway station to collect their son David. It was about 8pm, a wet evening.

On the return journey, as they reached the road between the Borley and Liston turnings, **a hooded figure walked out immediately in front of their Dormobile (small camper van)**. Ursula shouted "you fool" and Leslie braked hard. The figure was only feet from the very short bonnet. There was no impact.

They got out and searched the area thoroughly. Nobody was about.

Both described the figure as:
- Bowed, wearing a **hood and long robe**
- Colour appeared **dark grey** (allowing for headlight distortion)
- Crossed the road from right to left

David Pennell in the back seat did not see the figure and was unaware of anything until the brakes were applied.

[[claudine-mathias|CM]] says: "I believe that the Pennells' extraordinary experience defies a normal explanation."

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Canon James Henry Leslie Pennell** (sometimes referenced as James Pennell or Leslie Pennell) was a pragmatically minded clergyman who became an important figure in the later administrative history of the Borley parishes, as well as a prominent witness to one of its most dramatic modern apparitions. 

**Career and Move to East Anglia**
Described as a "tall, elderly Scotsman and not at all the sort of person who would have allowed his imagination to get out of control," Pennell reached the peak of his career serving as the provost of St Andrew’s Cathedral in Inverness from 1949 until 1965 [1]. Having decided to seek a living with lighter responsibilities upon reaching the age of 60, he and his wife Ursula left Scotland [1]. Because Ursula was a native of East Anglia, the couple relocated to the region in late November 1965, where Pennell became the rector of Foxearth and Pentlow, residing at Foxearth Rectory [1, 2].

**Ecclesiastical Role at Borley (1967 Onward)**
Following the resignation of the Rev. Lanfranc Mathias in 1967, Canon Pennell was additionally appointed as the Curate-in-Charge and rector of Borley and Liston [2-5]. This appointment was historically significant: Pennell became the very first priest responsible for the pastoral care of the newly combined group ministry of all four parishes—Foxearth, Pentlow, Borley, and Liston [3-5]. From his incumbency onward, the priests administering Borley have permanently resided in Foxearth [3].

**The *Dormobile* Encounter (December 3, 1965)**
Pennell’s most famous connection to the Borley haunting occurred just days after moving to Foxearth, on the wet evening of December 3, 1965 [6, 7]. At approximately 8:00 p.m., Canon Pennell and his wife were driving back from the Long Melford railway station after picking up their son, David [7]. They were travelling in a *Dormobile*, a small camper van with a very short bonnet [7]. 

While driving on the road between the turnings to Borley and Liston, a bowed figure wearing a hood and long dark-grey robes suddenly stepped out into the road immediately in front of their vehicle, crossing from right to left [7]. Ursula shouted, **"you fool"**, and Canon Pennell braked hard [7]. Both Pennell and his wife saw the figure clearly and believed an accident was unavoidable, but there was no impact whatsoever [7]. When they got out into the lane to search the area, there was absolutely nobody around [7]. 

**Clarifications and Contradictions Regarding the Sighting**
The sources record several specific clarifications and minor contradictions regarding this event:
*   **The Shouted Warning:** When the incident was written up in the local press, the reporter created the inaccurate impression that Ursula Pennell had shouted "you fool" at her husband [8]. In reality, she was directing her admonishment directly at the mysterious figure in the road [8].
*   **The Son's Perspective:** David Pennell did not witness the apparition [8]. He was sitting in the back of the *Dormobile* and was completely unaware of the event until his father suddenly slammed on the brakes [8].
*   **The Color of the Robes:** The Pennells noted the figure's robes were dark grey, but they pragmatically acknowledged that the headlights of a car can have a distorting effect on colors (which may account for discrepancies with other sightings of the "black" or "blue" nun) [7].
*   **Reluctance to Discuss:** Another source ambiguously mentions that the "last rector [James Pennell] and his wife" were reported to have seen the phantom coach in this same area, noting that they were "evidently unwilling to discuss it" [9].

Despite any initial reluctance or press distortions, Pennell did occasionally engage with researchers. On June 8, 1968, the young psychical researcher Grant Vallender visited Foxearth Rectory and spoke with Canon Pennell for about half an hour regarding his knowledge of the case [10].
