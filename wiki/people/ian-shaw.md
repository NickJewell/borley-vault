# Ian Shaw

[[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s young "brother" — the quotation marks in the appendix to *Ghosts of Borley* signal uncertainty about the actual relationship. Ian Shaw accompanied the Foysters to Canada when [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] and Marianne married and emigrated in 1922; Foyster paid for the boy's education there.

Whether Ian Shaw was genuinely Marianne's sibling, a half-brother, or a child of another family passed off as a relative is not established in *Ghosts of Borley*. The ambiguity fits Marianne's documented pattern of arranging custody of children under various pretexts (see also [[adelaide-foyster|Adelaide Tower]] and [[john-emery|John Emery]]).

> **Contradiction:** The wiki's [[ian-greenwood|Ian Greenwood Shaw]] page covers Marianne's natural son by Harold Greenwood, who was raised in Northern Ireland and concealed as Marianne's "younger brother." The broader sources (particularly *hbrs* and *wob*) suggest that Ian Shaw (this entry) and Ian Greenwood Shaw (the Greenwood page) are likely the same person — that is, Marianne's natural son, passed off as her younger brother and taken to Canada. If so, this separate entry reflects an earlier, incomplete understanding derived solely from *Ghosts of Borley*. Later sources clarify that Ian used the Shaw surname for his entire life and did accompany the Foysters to Canada.

Ian Shaw's later life is unrecorded in *Ghosts of Borley* itself. For the fuller account, see [[ian-greenwood]].
