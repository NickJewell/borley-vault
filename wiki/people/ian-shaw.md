# Ian Shaw

[[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]]'s young "brother" — the quotation marks in the appendix to *Ghosts of Borley* signal uncertainty about the actual relationship. Ian Shaw accompanied the Foysters to Canada when [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] and Marianne married and emigrated in 1922; Foyster paid for the boy's education there.

Whether Ian Shaw was genuinely Marianne's sibling, a half-brother, or a child of another family passed off as a relative is not established in the source. The ambiguity fits Marianne's documented pattern of arranging custody of children under various pretexts (see also [[adelaide-foyster|Adelaide Tower]] and [[john-emery|John Emery]]). Ian Shaw's later life is unrecorded in *Ghosts of Borley*.

> **Contradiction:** The wiki's [[ian-greenwood|Ian Greenwood Shaw]] page covers Marianne's son by Harold Greenwood who remained in England. Ian Shaw (this entry) is a separate individual — Marianne's supposed "brother" who went to Canada.
