# Gerald Bull

One of the children of the Rev. [[henry-bull|Henry D. E. Bull]], born at Borley Rectory during the family's long occupancy. Interviewed by [[harry-price]] on March 29, 1939, during one of Price's last visits to the site before the demolition of the ruins.

## Phantom Footsteps on the Lane

Gerald Bull provided striking testimony about one of the most persistently reported phenomena at Borley: the phantom footsteps heard on the lane approaching the Rectory.

He told Price that when walking up the lane and approaching the Rectory, he had heard footsteps following him **at least fifty times**. On each occasion he turned round but never saw anyone, and could find no normal explanation.

The accumulation of fifty experiences over a number of years — gathered by a person born in the house and thoroughly familiar with its surroundings — makes this one of the more robust corroborations of the lane-footsteps phenomenon. It rules out startle-effect, unfamiliarity with the environment, and suggests the sounds were distinctive enough to be noticed consistently throughout a lifetime's acquaintance with the place.

## Context

This testimony confirms accounts from multiple other witnesses of auditory phenomena on and near the road outside the Rectory. The "hollow road" — noted by Price as producing unusually resonant sounds when walked on — runs immediately past the front of the Rectory and was suggested by Price to overlay an underground tunnel.
