# Dick Mitchell

Seventeen-year-old son of the Mitchell family, tenants of [[rectory-cottage|Rectory Cottage]] during the early Foyster period; a key helper and incidental witness to poltergeist phenomena at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]].

## Background

Mr and Mrs Mitchell and their son Dick moved into the Rectory Cottage when it became available at the start of the Foyster tenancy in 1930. They helped the Foysters with unpacking and odd jobs, and Dick in particular became a reliable worker around the house and grounds.

## Involvement with the Phenomena

During the intense poltergeist period of early 1931, Dick was a regular presence at the Rectory and experienced the phenomena directly:

- He was working in the house when a stone was thrown at him. [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] insisted this could not have been [[adelaide-foyster|Adelaide]], as she had suggested.
- On the morning of 9 March, when a plumber came to thaw frozen pipes, Dick was also doing odd jobs; Marianne found a log "bowling along outside" in the kitchen passage.
- A separate incident confirmed by Dick happened when stones were thrown at him while he worked in the house during the period of Arthur Sellwood's attempted exorcism.

His willingness to work in the house despite these incidents, and his corroboration of individual phenomena, made him a small but credible witness from outside the Foyster household.

## Departure

When the Mitchells gave up the tenancy of the Rectory Cottage, the Foysters quickly missed having an energetic young man like Dick to help with maintenance and gardening. This prompted Lionel to place an advertisement in *The Times* personals — the advertisement that brought [[francois-darles|Francois D'Arles]] to Borley.
