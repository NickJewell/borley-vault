# Rev. Henry Samuel Foyster

Paternal grandfather of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster]]; founder of the Foyster clerical dynasty at Hastings. He matriculated at **Queen's College, Cambridge, at Michaelmas 1811** [1], and by **1841** was officially working as the **"Lecturer at St Clement's and All Saints'"** in Hastings [1].

## Career

In **1849**, he officially became the Rector of All Saints' Church in Hastings [1], gaining this highly desirable living **"presumably at the instance of his brother John"** [1]. John Foyster, more than ten years his senior, was the patron of the living at All Saints' and simultaneously served as the Rector of the neighbouring St Clement's until 1855, when he "disappears from the Clergy List" [1].

Rev. Henry Samuel Foyster remained the Rector of All Saints' until his death on **6 July 1862** [1] — the date coinciding almost exactly with the year his son [[rev-george-alfred-foyster|George Alfred]] took the same living.

## The Dynasty

Henry Samuel's two sons perpetuated the dynasty:
- [[rev-henry-brereton-foyster|Rev. Henry Brereton Foyster]] (born 1834) — took St Clement's
- [[rev-george-alfred-foyster|Rev. George Alfred Foyster]] — took All Saints'; Lionel's father

Robert Wood notes the dynasty's Cambridge tradition, Pembroke connections, and patron-rector model as the background against which [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]]'s failure to inherit any family living stands out as anomalous.
