# Rev. Henry Samuel Foyster

Paternal grandfather of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster]]; founder of the Foyster clerical dynasty at Hastings. He matriculated at **Queen's College, Cambridge**, at Michaelmas 1811.

## Career

Henry Samuel Foyster built the family's clerical presence in Hastings over several decades:
- **1841**: Lecturer at St Clement's and All Saints', Hastings
- **1849**: Became Rector of All Saints', Hastings, "presumably at the instance of his brother John, over ten years his senior, who was patron of that living"
- **6 July 1862**: Died in office; the date coincides almost exactly with the year [[rev-george-alfred-foyster|his son George Alfred]] took the same living

His brother **John Foyster** was Rector of St Clement's until 1855, when he "disappears from the Clergy List."

## The Dynasty

Henry Samuel's two sons perpetuated the dynasty:
- [[rev-henry-brereton-foyster|Rev. Henry Brereton Foyster]] (born 1834) — took St Clement's
- [[rev-george-alfred-foyster|Rev. George Alfred Foyster]] — took All Saints'; Lionel's father

Robert Wood notes the dynasty's Cambridge tradition, Pembroke connections, and patron-rector model as the background against which [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]]'s failure to inherit any family living stands out as anomalous.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Reverend Henry Samuel Foyster** was the paternal grandfather of the Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster (the famous incumbent of Borley Rectory from 1930 to 1935). He was a foundational figure in the wealthy Victorian ecclesiastical dynasty of the Foyster family based in Hastings [1]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific dates, biographical details, and quotes regarding his life and clerical career:

**Education and Early Career**
*   **Education:** According to the records of the *Alumni Cantabrigienses*, Henry Samuel Foyster matriculated at **Queen’s College, Cambridge, at Michaelmas, 1811** [1].
*   **Lecturer:** By **1841**, he was officially working as the **"Lecturer at St Clement’s and All Saints’"** in Hastings [1]. 

**Rectorship and Family Patronage**
*   In **1849**, he officially became the Rector of All Saints' Church in Hastings [1]. 
*   The sources note that he gained this highly desirable living **"presumably at the instance of his brother John"** [1]. John Foyster, who was more than ten years his senior, was the patron of the living at All Saints' and simultaneously served as the Rector of the neighboring St Clement's until 1855 (when he "disappears from the Clergy List") [1].

**Death and Succession**
*   **Death:** Rev. Henry Samuel Foyster remained the Rector of All Saints' until his death on **6 July 1862** [1].
*   **Succession:** He had two sons who both followed him into the priesthood and subsequently inherited the family's lucrative Hastings livings. His elder son, the Rev. Henry Brereton Foyster (born 1834), became the Rector of St Clement's, while his younger son, the Rev. George Alfred Foyster, eventually took over his father's parish to become the Rector of All Saints' [2].
