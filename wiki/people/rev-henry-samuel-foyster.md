# Rev. Henry Samuel Foyster

Paternal grandfather of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Algernon Foyster]]; founder of the Foyster clerical dynasty at Hastings. He matriculated at **Queen's College, Cambridge**, at Michaelmas 1811.

## Career

Henry Samuel Foyster built the family's clerical presence in Hastings over several decades:
- **1841**: Lecturer at St Clement's and All Saints', Hastings
- **1849**: Became Rector of All Saints', Hastings, "presumably at the instance of his brother John, over ten years his senior, who was patron of that living"
- **6 July 1862**: Died in office; the date coincides almost exactly with the year [[rev-george-alfred-foyster|his son George Alfred]] took the same living

His brother **John Foyster** was Rector of St Clement's until 1855, when he "disappears from the Clergy List."

## The Dynasty

Henry Samuel's two sons perpetuated the dynasty:
- [[rev-henry-brereton-foyster|Rev. Henry Brereton Foyster]] (born 1834) — took St Clement's
- [[rev-george-alfred-foyster|Rev. George Alfred Foyster]] — took All Saints'; Lionel's father

Robert Wood notes the dynasty's Cambridge tradition, Pembroke connections, and patron-rector model as the background against which [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]]'s failure to inherit any family living stands out as anomalous.
