# Dorothy Lane (née Tabbart)

Interviewed by Babbs and Mathias in November 1995 at her home in a Suffolk village. During WWII she served in the NAAFI and was stationed at Liston Hall, most of which no longer exists. At the time of the incident she was 20 years old.

## The Bedside Figure — Summer 1944

Shortly after D-Day (6 June 1944), Dorothy was sleeping in a Nissen hut in the Liston Hall grounds with three other young women. She awoke suddenly at 5am and saw:

- A **shadowy figure dressed in long grey robes** standing at her bedside
- The figure was of average height
- She felt **no malevolence** from the presence
- She reached out her hand — and felt **nothing**; the figure was not solid
- The figure stood still for approximately **ten seconds**, then **faded gradually** (did not vanish suddenly)
- She screamed, woke the other three women, who found her clearly shocked and believed her account

She was so shaken she remained shocked for several days. She had the feeling it might be a premonition, but nothing bad happened to her.

Years later she learned the bones found under the rectory had been buried in Liston churchyard on **29 May 1945** — nearly a year after her sighting. The authors note the figure has also been reported at various points throughout the district, not only at the rectory site.

## Note on Location

Liston Hall is about a mile from [[borley-rectory]], in the adjacent hamlet of Liston. The [[the-nun]] sightings are not confined to the rectory grounds; they extend throughout the wider district.
