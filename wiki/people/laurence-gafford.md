# Laurence C. Gafford

Of Stevenage, Hertfordshire, where he constructed a detailed model of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] at his home. A persistent post-fire visitor to Borley who compiled detailed reports of his experiences and assisted [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] in his investigations. He made at least two documented visits.

## 4 August 1949 — Night Visit with Sugg

Gafford and [[james-sugg|James M. Sugg]] deliberately refrained from making detailed inquiries about the haunting beforehand, then obtained permission from [[james-turner|James Turner]] to spend a night on the Rectory site. They arrived at **10:15 p.m.** and parked on the Rectory drive, approximately outside where the Rectory's Sewing Room used to be.

At **11:15 p.m.**, while Gafford and Sugg were sitting inside the car with James and Cathy Turner, all four of them distinctly heard **"light, brisk footsteps"** coming down the lane outside the cottage, accompanied by the sound of a bicycle freewheeling downhill. When Gafford lowered the car window to look, all sounds abruptly and completely ceased — a subsequent search revealed no one in the area to account for the noises. Gafford subsequently discovered that a similar incident (bicycle freewheeling, but in that case *uphill*, followed by an empty lane) had been reported by a previous visitor.

## 4–5 March 1950 — Overnight Vigil with Underwood

Gafford and [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] spent a night in the Turner's lounge at the cottage. During this visit, Gafford assisted Underwood with photographic experiments that convinced both men "beyond any shadow of a doubt" that tunnels or hollows existed beneath the road between the church and the Rectory site. The indoor vigil began at approximately **1:00 a.m.**

> **Contradiction:** While all accounts agree that a second set of overhead footsteps occurred at 4:15 a.m., they conflict on the timing of the first instance. Some accounts state the first heavy footsteps and muffled voices were heard at **2:15 a.m.**, while another account by Underwood explicitly records the time as **3:15 a.m.**

During the early morning hours, both men simultaneously heard **"heavy footsteps and muffled voices"** coming from the Turners' bedroom directly overhead — precisely where the Turners were sleeping. Gafford commented that the Turners were having a "disturbed night." Each episode lasted about twenty seconds. The noises occurred again at **4:15 a.m.** under similar circumstances.

Next morning Mr. and Mrs. Turner assured the investigators they had slept soundly throughout the night and had not woken up. When told of the sounds, they "were not surprised" and mentioned that the Coopers had also reported inexplicable whisperings from the same corner of that room.

> "There was certainly no motive whatsoever for the Turners to mislead the two investigators, and it would have been a tiresome and aimless procedure for them to get up three times during the night just for that purpose." — Underwood & Tabori
