# Laurence C. Gafford

Of Stevenage, Hertfordshire. A persistent post-fire visitor to Borley who compiled detailed reports of his experiences and assisted Peter Underwood in his investigations. He made at least two documented visits.

## 4 August 1949 — Night Visit with Sugg

Gafford and [[james-sugg|James M. Sugg]] obtained permission from [[james-turner|James Turner]] to spend a night on the Rectory site. They arrived at 10:15 pm and parked on the Rectory drive. At about 11:15 pm they heard "light, brisk footsteps" coming down the lane outside the cottage, followed by a sound like a bicycle freewheeling downhill. When Gafford lowered the car window to look, the squeaking mechanism drowned the footsteps — and when it stopped both footsteps and bicycle sound had ceased, with no figure visible in the moonlit lane. Gafford subsequently discovered that a similar incident (bicycle freewheeling, but in that case *uphill*, followed by an empty lane) had been reported by a previous visitor.

## 4–5 March 1950 — Overnight Vigil with Underwood

Gafford and Peter Underwood spent a night in the Turner's lounge at the cottage. Electrical and other controls were placed around the church and the site. At 2:15 am, both men simultaneously heard heavy footsteps and muffled voices from overhead — precisely where the Turners were sleeping. They checked the controls and returned to the lounge; the sounds were heard again. At 4:15 am the same thing occurred a third time, each episode lasting about twenty seconds before ceasing.

Next morning Mr and Mrs Turner assured them they had slept soundly and had not woken up. When told of the sounds, they "were not surprised" and mentioned that the Coopers had also reported inexplicable whisperings from the same corner of that room.

> "There was certainly no motive whatsoever for the Turners to mislead the two investigators, and it would have been a tiresome and aimless procedure for them to get up three times during the night just for that purpose." — Underwood & Tabori
