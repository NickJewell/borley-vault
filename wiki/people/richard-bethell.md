# Hon. Richard Bethell

(1881–1929) Egyptologist and private secretary to the famous archaeologist Howard Carter. Bethell held the historic distinction of being one of the first people to step inside the tomb of Tutankhamun when it was opened in 1922.

## Connection to Borley

[[harry-price|Harry Price]] invited Bethell to [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] on 28 July 1929 with [[lord-charles-hope|Lord Charles Hope]] and [[lucie-kaye|Lucy Kaye]], Price himself being unable to attend due to illness (see also [[charles-sutton|the Sutton incident]] of 25 July). This visit fell on the traditionally significant [[july-28th]] date — the day local tradition held as the annual appearance date of the phantom nun. The party stayed at the Rectory for two days, but no paranormal phenomena were reported, with Price later noting that the house seemed "particularly peaceful" during their stay.

## Death and the "Tutankhamun Curse"

Later the same year, Bethell was found dead of heart failure at the Bath Club in London. His father, the 3rd Baron Westbury, subsequently committed suicide a few months later. Because of Bethell's direct connection to the excavation of Tutankhamun's tomb, the back-to-back deaths gave rise to the widespread rumour that both men were victims of the curse of "King Tut." The connection — between a man associated with Borley and the supposed Egyptian royal curse — was noted by [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]], who mentioned it in the context of the seemingly fatal associations some drew with the Borley case.

## Significance

Bethell's presence at Borley on the significant July 28th date is a minor footnote in the case. Unlike [[charles-sutton]] and [[lord-charles-hope]], who both visited in the same July 1929 period, Bethell left no written account of what he observed. His death so soon after the visit contributed to folklore around the Borley haunting attracting misfortune to those involved.
