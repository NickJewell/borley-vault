# Hon. Richard Bethell

(1881–1929) Egyptologist and private secretary to Howard Carter. Bethell was one of the first people to step inside the tomb of Tutankhamun when it was opened in 1922.

## Connection to Borley

[[harry-price]] invited Bethell to Borley on 28 July 1929 with [[lord-charles-hope]] and [[lucie-kaye]], Price himself being unable to attend due to illness (see also [[charles-sutton|the Sutton incident]] of 25 July). This visit fell on the traditionally significant [[july-28th]] date.

## Death and the "Tutankhamun Curse"

Later the same year, Bethell was found dead of heart failure at the Bath Club in London. His father, the 3rd Baron Westbury, subsequently committed suicide a few months later. These deaths gave rise to the widespread rumour that both men were victims of the curse of "King Tut." The connection — between a man associated with Borley and the supposed Egyptian royal curse — was noted by Peter Underwood, who mentioned it in the context of the seemingly fatal associations some drew with the Borley case.

## Significance

Bethell's presence at Borley on the significant July 28th date is a minor footnote in the case. Unlike [[charles-sutton]] and [[lord-charles-hope]], who both visited in the same July 1929 period, Bethell left no written account of what he observed. His death so soon after the visit contributed to folklore around the Borley haunting attracting misfortune to those involved.

*   Bethell was a friend of psychic investigator Harry Price, who invited him to take part in the Borley Rectory investigation [1, 2].

*   The party stayed at the Rectory for two days, but **no paranormal phenomena were reported**, with Price later noting that the house seemed "particularly peaceful" during their stay [2, 3].

**Death and the "King Tut" Curse**
*   Later in the exact same year of his Borley visit (1929), Bethell was **found dead of heart failure at the Bath Club in London** [1].
*   Tragically, **his father, the 3rd Baron Westbury, committed suicide** only a few months later [1].
*   Because of his direct connection to the excavation of Tutankhamun's tomb, the back-to-back deaths of Bethell and his father gave rise to the persistent **rumour that they were both victims of the famous "curse of King Tut"** [1].
