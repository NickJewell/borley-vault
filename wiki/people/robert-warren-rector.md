# Robert Warren (Rector of Borley)

Rector of Borley from 1607 until 1642, when he was dismissed during Oliver Cromwell's Commonwealth; then reinstated from 1660 to 1661. His removal and reinstatement mark the disruption to Borley parish life during the Civil War period.

## Dismissal and Reinstatement

Warren held the Borley living for 35 years before being removed in 1642 at the commencement of the Commonwealth period. He was replaced by [[john-deeks|John Deeks]], a Puritan "Intruder" imposed on the parish. When the Commonwealth ended in 1660, Warren was reinstated but held the living for only one more year (1660–1661), presumably because of his age and possibly failing health.

Warren's designation in the rectors' list with the letters "AMSTP" after his name suggests a specific academic or ecclesiastical qualification.

## Significance

Warren's tenure represents the longest unbroken incumbency at Borley prior to the Bull dynasty, spanning 35 years. His dismissal illustrates the upheaval imposed on the parish during the Civil War period — a time that Babbs and Mathias suggest may have been important in the history of the Roman Catholic underground at Borley, connected to the [[waldegrave-family|Waldegrave family]] and their use of the [[borley-church-crypt|crypt]] for secret Catholic Mass.
