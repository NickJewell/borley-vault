# Robert Warren (Rector of Borley)

Listed in formal registers as **"Robert Warren AMSTP"**, he served as Rector of Borley from 1607 until 1642, when he was dismissed during Oliver Cromwell's Commonwealth; then reinstated from 1660 to 1661. His removal and reinstatement mark the disruption to Borley parish life during the Civil War period.

## Dismissal and Reinstatement

Warren held the Borley living for 35 years before being removed in 1642 during the religious and political upheavals of the Civil War — East Anglia was a stronghold of Puritanism. He was replaced by [[john-deeks|John Deeks]], described in historical records as an "intruder" — the term for a Puritan minister imposed upon a parish when the existing incumbent was unwilling or unable to accept the new Puritan rules.

When the Commonwealth ended in 1660, Warren was formally reinstated as Rector of Borley. His second term lasted only one year (1660–1661), presumably because of his age and possibly failing health. He was officially succeeded by [[william-playne|William Playne]] in 1661.

## Significance

Warren's tenure represents the longest unbroken incumbency at Borley prior to the Bull dynasty, spanning 35 years. His dismissal illustrates the upheaval imposed on the parish during the Civil War period — a time that Babbs and Mathias suggest may have been important in the history of the Roman Catholic underground at Borley, connected to the [[waldegrave-family|Waldegrave family]] and their use of the [[borley-church-crypt|crypt]] for secret Catholic Mass.
