# Robert Warren (Rector of Borley)

Rector of Borley from 1607 until 1642, when he was dismissed during Oliver Cromwell's Commonwealth; then reinstated from 1660 to 1661. His removal and reinstatement mark the disruption to Borley parish life during the Civil War period.

## Dismissal and Reinstatement

Warren held the Borley living for 35 years before being removed in 1642 at the commencement of the Commonwealth period. He was replaced by [[john-deeks|John Deeks]], a Puritan "Intruder" imposed on the parish. When the Commonwealth ended in 1660, Warren was reinstated but held the living for only one more year (1660–1661), presumably because of his age and possibly failing health.

Warren's designation in the rectors' list with the letters "AMSTP" after his name suggests a specific academic or ecclesiastical qualification.

## Significance

Warren's tenure represents the longest unbroken incumbency at Borley prior to the Bull dynasty, spanning 35 years. His dismissal illustrates the upheaval imposed on the parish during the Civil War period — a time that Babbs and Mathias suggest may have been important in the history of the Roman Catholic underground at Borley, connected to the [[waldegrave-family|Waldegrave family]] and their use of the [[borley-church-crypt|crypt]] for secret Catholic Mass.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Robert Warren, AMSTP**, served as the Rector of Borley during the tumultuous seventeenth century, a period in which his tenure was directly impacted by the English Civil War and the rise of Oliver Cromwell's Commonwealth. 

A reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following specific dates, facts, and events regarding his time in the parish:

**First Incumbency and Dismissal**
*   Robert Warren was initially instituted as the Rector of Borley in **1607** [1, 2].
*   During the religious and political upheavals that swept through East Anglia (a stronghold of Puritanism) during the Civil War, Warren was "dismissed abruptly from his duties" [3]. 
*   In **1642**, he was replaced by a clergyman named John Deeks [1, 2]. Deeks was classified as an "intruder," which was the historical term for an obnoxious Puritan minister who was imposed upon a parish when the existing incumbent was unwilling or unable to come to terms with the new Puritan rules [3]. 

**Restoration and Final Year**
*   Following the collapse of the national Commonwealth and the end of John Deeks's local rule in **1660**, Robert Warren was formally reinstated to his rightful position as the Rector of Borley [1-3].
*   However, his second term at Borley lasted for "one year only" [3]. 
*   He was officially succeeded as rector by William Playne in **1661** [1, 2]. The sources suggest that Warren's swift departure following his reinstatement was "presumably because of his age and perhaps because poor health was an additional factor" [3].

In the formal historical registers of Borley Rectors provided in the texts, he is officially listed under the name and title **"Robert Warren AMSTP"** [2].
