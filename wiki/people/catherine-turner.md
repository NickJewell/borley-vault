# Catherine Turner

Wife of the poet and writer [[james-turner|James Turner]], with whom she lived at [[rectory-cottage|Borley Priory cottage]] and the Rectory site from May 1947 until July 1950. Catherine was a calm, balanced witness who, like her husband, accepted the unexplained happenings at Borley as "part of the furniture" rather than a source of fear.

## Phenomena Experienced

**Orchard voices.** During May and June 1947 James Turner cleared the [[nuns-walk|Nun's Walk]] with a sickle each evening. As he broke through the undergrowth into the old orchard, the sound of laughter and chatter came clearly to both of them from the direction of the Rectory site — voices that were "happy and laughing" and fled whenever Turner dashed toward them. As soon as he stopped work, the voices ceased. Mrs Turner often came to listen as her husband worked. Once the path was clear, they returned many times but heard nothing more.

**Phantom cat — bedroom.** On the night of 28 July 1949 (the traditional "Nun's day"), Catherine was awakened by something scratching at the bedclothes from the floor beside the bed. Assuming one of their cats had got into the room, she lit a candle and searched — but found no cat in the room, on the landing, or at the window, which could not have been scaled. When they returned to bed they noticed a distinct dip or hollow in the surface of the bedclothes, "just as if some animal had recently been lying there." They could not account for it.

**Phantom cat — kitchen doorway.** On 28 August, exactly a month later, both Turners were looking out of the open kitchen doorway when they saw a mysterious "furry form" chasing their own cat Fred. Thin, grey-white, with a scraggy tail, unlike any local cat, it followed Fred under the car. Fred emerged and shot up the walnut tree. The mysterious creature was never seen to leave; a careful search under the open-ground car found nothing.

**Final phantom-cat incident.** On the night of 5 March 1950, Catherine was in the kitchen while her husband was in the sitting room with Fred asleep beside him. Holly (their other cat) had just been let outside. A piercing feline squeal came from within the cottage, apparently from the direction of the landing. Both cats were accounted for elsewhere; no explanation was found.

**Furniture sounds.** The day after the excavations in Borley church in May 1947, both Turners at breakfast were startled by loud sounds of heavy furniture being moved in the upper rooms. James Turner mounted the stairs to investigate — the sounds stopped and nothing had been disturbed. A similar "tremendous bang, like a pistol shot," occurred some weeks later while both were in the kitchen.

**Footsteps.** In July 1948, sitting in a deck chair under the lime trees near the Rectory foundations, Catherine became aware of heavy footsteps that approached, intensified, and gradually died away — "like someone walking along a passage in the house that no longer existed." The experience was repeated two weeks later at the same spot.

## Character

Underwood and Tabori emphasised the Turners' refusal to dramatise their experiences. When [[ronald-blythe|Ronald Blythe]] saw the [[phantom-cat|phantom cat]] in the bathroom, James Turner reported it the following morning "in a calm, nonchalant way, just as if the incident were quite unimportant." The Turners left Borley in July 1950 primarily because of the "constant stream of uninvited and inquisitive visitors" — not because they were frightened.

After leaving [[rectory-cottage|Borley]], the Turners lived at Belchamp Walter, then Grundisburgh, and eventually settled in Cornwall.
