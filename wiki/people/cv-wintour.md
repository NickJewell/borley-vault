# C. V. Wintour

Oxford undergraduate. Official observer at Borley Rectory, July 18–24, 1937, alongside [[jm-bailey|J. M. Bailey]]. Slept in the Blue Room on at least one night; co-witness to the appearance of new pencil marks, including the instance that both he and Bailey "thought they saw grow in front of their eyes."

## Experiences

Wintour and Bailey arrived together on July 18, 1937. They stretched black threads across doorways and passages at night to detect any intrusion; the threads were found undisturbed on subsequent inspections.

The key incident Wintour confirmed alongside Bailey: on the evening of July 22, returning from Long Melford, they found in Room 3 a new mark "like the beginning of an 'M.'" Wintour had looked at the wall specifically on entering the room and noted nothing; "flashing his torch on to the wall again a minute or two later" the mark was present. "Both Bailey and Wintour thought they saw it grow in front of their eyes, but are now inclined to think it was an optical illusion."

At 11:23 on July 20, both confirmed that three circles had appeared behind a door in Room 8 within eight minutes — "We are both certain that these were not there at 11:15."

Wintour slept in the Base Room on one night (Bailey in the Blue Room) and in the summer-house on another, hearing nothing unusual.

Appendix D lists Wintour under "Wall and Paper-writings" and "The Light in the Window."

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**C. V. Wintour** is a very minor figure in the Borley Rectory story, and the provided sources contain only brief information about his involvement. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following limited biographical details and events regarding his investigation:

**Biographical Details and Role**
Wintour is identified in the sources simply as an **"undergraduate"** [1]. He was recruited by Harry Price to serve on the rota of **"official observers"** during Price's year-long tenancy investigation of the Rectory in 1937–1938 [1, 2]. He conducted his investigations alongside a fellow undergraduate, Mr. J. M. Bailey [1].

**The Rectory Visit (July 1937)**
Wintour and Bailey stayed at Borley Rectory for several days in **July 1937** [1]. The sources provide a brief chronological timeline of the latter part of their observational period:
*   **July 21:** Harry Price arrived at the Rectory at 4:00 p.m., accompanied by S. J. de Lotbinière and Major H. Douglas-Home, and found Wintour and Bailey **"still in possession"** of the house [1]. Wintour and Bailey left an hour later and **"slept out"** [1]. 
*   **Subsequent Night:** During another night of their investigation, **Wintour slept in the famously haunted Blue Room, but "heard nothing"** [3]. 
*   **July 23:** The two undergraduates carried out the "usual inspections" of the building during the day, but **"no new pencilings were recorded, and no other phenomena were noted"** [3].
*   **July 24:** Wintour and Bailey concluded their visit and left for home at 10:15 a.m. [3].

Ultimately, Wintour's time at Borley is recorded as a completely blank visit, with absolutely no paranormal phenomena or unusual events reported by him [3].
