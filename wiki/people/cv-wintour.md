# C. V. Wintour

Oxford undergraduate recruited by [[harry-price|Harry Price]] to serve as an official observer during Price's year-long tenancy investigation of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] in 1937–38 [1, 2]. He conducted his investigations alongside a fellow undergraduate, [[jm-bailey|J. M. Bailey]], staying at the Rectory from 18–24 July 1937.

## Experiences

Wintour and Bailey arrived together on 18 July 1937. They stretched black threads across doorways and passages at night to detect any intrusion; the threads were found undisturbed on subsequent inspections.

On **21 July**, [[harry-price|Harry Price]] arrived at the Rectory at 4:00 p.m., accompanied by [[sj-de-lotbiniere|S. J. de Lotbinière]] and [[major-douglas-home|Major H. Douglas-Home]], and found Wintour and Bailey "still in possession" of the house [1]. Wintour and Bailey left an hour later and "slept out" [1].

The key incident Wintour confirmed alongside Bailey: on the evening of 22 July, returning from [[long-melford|Long Melford]], they found in Room 3 a new mark "like the beginning of an 'M.'" Wintour had looked at the wall specifically on entering the room and noted nothing; "flashing his torch on to the wall again a minute or two later" the mark was present. "Both Bailey and Wintour thought they saw it grow in front of their eyes, but are now inclined to think it was an optical illusion."

At 11:23 on 20 July, both confirmed that three circles had appeared behind a door in Room 8 within eight minutes — "We are both certain that these were not there at 11:15."

On **23 July**, the two undergraduates carried out the "usual inspections" of the building during the day, but "no new pencilings were recorded, and no other phenomena were noted" [3]. Wintour slept in the [[blue-room|Blue Room]] on one night (Bailey in the Base Room), hearing nothing unusual, and in the summer-house on another night with similarly negative results. They concluded their visit and left for home at 10:15 a.m. on **24 July** [3].

Appendix D lists Wintour under "Wall and Paper-writings" and "The Light in the Window."
