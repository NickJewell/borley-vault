# Stella Cranshaw (later Cranshawe)

The first major medium tested by [[harry-price|Harry Price]] at his new National Laboratory of Psychical Research in 1923–24; her sittings were both Price's public breakthrough and an early source of controversy about his methods.

## Background

Stella was a twenty-one-year-old nursing assistant when Price was put in touch with her early in 1923. Her family felt she had "natural ability" and were keen to have her tested. Since puberty she had been the focus of curious happenings: cool breezes, objects moving, knuckles heard around her. Price found her "perfectly normal, healthy, good-looking and a typical specimen of the modern, well-educated English girl." He was convinced she had genuine mediumistic powers.

## The Sittings (1923–24)

The sittings with Stella took place at the offices of the **London Spiritualist Alliance** rather than under [[eric-dingwall|Dingwall]]'s preferred more rigorous SPR oversight, which caused immediate friction. Stella's trance personality or "control" was a dead child known as **"Palma"**.

The most spectacular claimed result was Stella's visualisation, on 12 April 1923, of the front page of the *Daily Mail* from 19 May — thirty-seven days in the future — carrying the name **"ANDREW SALT"** and an image of a boy and his father. On 19 May, the *Mail*'s front page carried a full-page advertisement for Andrews Liver Salt, matching Stella's description.

A table reportedly levitated above the heads of sitters during one sitting before falling to pieces.

## Controversy

Dingwall was dissatisfied with Price's methods — he hadn't selected experienced observers, allowed clearly partial spiritualists to observe, and didn't circulate notes for approval. He accused Price of potentially being complicit in fraud if Stella were fraudulent. By October 1923, the relationship between Price and Stella appears to have ended.

O'Connor suggests Price and Stella had an intimate, possibly sexual relationship — he wrote to and about her "like a suitor." When he pursued her, she evaded him, yet remained tantalising. Significantly, Price later added an extra letter 'e' to both Stella's first and last name — from *Cranshaw* to *Cranshawe* — "as if she had been branded by him." [[lucie-kaye|Lucie Kaye]] later adopted the same modification of her own name, from *Kay* to *Kaye*, in homage to this habit of Price's.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Stella Cranshaw** (frequently referred to as **Stella C.**) was a young English medium whose investigation by Harry Price became his first major solo success as a psychical researcher [1]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the sources reveals the following biographical details, specific dates, phenomena, and the complex personal dynamics surrounding her involvement with Harry Price:

**Biographical Details and Background**
*   **Early Life:** In early 1923, Stella was a **21-year-old nursing assistant** [2]. Price described her as **"perfectly normal, healthy, good-looking and a typical specimen of the modern, well-educated English girl"** [2]. She was blonde and wore her hair bobbed in the fashionable style of the era [3]. 
*   **Early Phenomena:** According to her family, who felt she possessed a "natural ability," Stella had been the focus of curious happenings ever since puberty [2]. These included **strong cool breezes** blowing across rooms she occupied, objects inexplicably jerking out of her way as she approached, and knocking sounds (particularly on her bedstead) [2]. 

**The 1923 Investigation and Methodology**
Stella was introduced to Price in the spring of 1923 because her family was keen to have her tested by the London Spiritualist Alliance [1, 2]. Price eagerly took up the case, hoping that her abilities might win him a $2,500 prize offered by *Scientific American* magazine for evidence of a genuine medium [2]. 
*   **Protecting the Medium:** Price deliberately shielded Stella from the skeptical Society for Psychical Research (SPR) and its investigator Eric Dingwall. Price argued that the SPR's **"cold, critical and academic methods"** might **"deter [Stella] from sitting at all,"** insisting that they were not suitable for her at that juncture [4]. 
*   **Séance Conditions:** The sittings were conducted in a dark, locked room with carefully monitored lighting and temperature, accompanied by music and burning incense [5]. The sources note these sittings possessed an **"erotic frisson"** [5]. Price maintained direct physical control over her, taking her pulse and noting: **"at every sitting we linked hands and kept contact with our feet"** [5]. 

**Specific Phenomena and "Palma"**
The formal sittings began on **22 March 1923** [6]. 
*   **The Trance Control:** During the séances, Stella's trance personality or "control" identified itself as a dead child known as **"Palma"** [6]. Price claimed Stella would go into uncontrolled trances and even produce ectoplasm, observing: **"Never was a medium easier to control... she did exactly as she was requested, made no comments – in fact, she hardly spoke at all"** [5].
*   **Telekinesis:** Physical phenomena were violent; during one sitting, a table levitated over the sitters' heads before crashing down and being **"reduced to matchwood"** [6].
*   **The Prophetic Vision:** On **12 April 1923**, Stella (guided by "Palma") allegedly visualized the front page of the *Daily Mail* for May 19—thirty-seven days in the future. She stated the page carried the name **"ANDREW SALT"** in capital letters, and described an image of a boy falling while a man bent over him pouring white powder from a tin [6]. When May 19 arrived, the front page of the *Mail* featured a full-page advertisement for Andrews Liver Salt bearing the exact image Stella had described, alongside the slogan: **"A tin kept handy always makes for health and happiness in the home!"** [6].

**Relationship with Harry Price and the Name Change**
Beyond the scientific parameters of the investigation, the sources reveal that Price and Stella shared an **"intimate and possibly sexual relationship"** [5]. 
*   Price pursued her ardently, writing to her "like a suitor," but while Stella remained tantalizing, she largely evaded or avoided his advances [5]. 
*   **The Spelling of "Cranshawe":** In his subsequent writings about her, Price deliberately altered the spelling of her surname, adding an extra "e" to make it "Cranshawe"—an act the sources describe as being **"as if she had been branded by him"** [5]. 
*   By **October 1923**, whatever relationship existed between the two was entirely over, but the fame generated by the sittings successfully launched Price as a major public figure in psychical research [7, 8].

**Later Sittings and Publications**
*   Lord Charles Hope, a prominent SPR member, attended some of Stella's séances in **1923 and 1928**, subsequently writing a report of the later experiments for the *British Journal of Psychical Research* [9]. 
*   A report detailing Price's sittings with Stella was published in the *Journal* of the American Society for Psychical Research (ASPR) in **May 1924** [10].
*   A dedicated book on her mediumship, simply titled *Stella C* and edited by James Turner, was eventually published in **1973** [11].
