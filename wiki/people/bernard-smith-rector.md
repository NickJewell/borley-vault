# Bernard Smith (Rector of Newton Green)

Anglican clergyman and **distant cousin of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]]** who participated in the attempted exorcism of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] on 11 March 1931.

## Role at the Exorcism

Smith was the rector at **Newton Green**, approximately six miles from Borley. He accompanied [[arthur-sellwood|Rev. Arthur Sellwood]] to Borley when Sellwood decided that a formal exorcism was necessary to address the poltergeist activity plaguing the Foysters. **Smith and his wife supplied the censer and incense** for the ritual [1].

Since there was no Anglican rite of exorcism at the time, the pair used the **Catholic ritual from 1614**. On the morning of March 11, 1931, they went over the entire building from attic to cellar, meticulously fumigating every room and passageway with burning incense and blessing with holy water from the shrine at Walsingham [1].

Though the house was quiet immediately after the rite was completed, the phenomena resumed the same afternoon in Sellwood and Smith's absence, with stones thrown and bells ringing. The exorcism was judged to have failed.

## The Warm Stone Phenomenon

While standing in the hall of Borley Rectory alongside Rev. Foyster and Rev. Sellwood, **a stone inexplicably fell from nowhere** [2]. When Sellwood picked it up, he discovered that it felt **physically warm to the touch** [2]. This incident was not widely known until years later, when Sellwood's son attended a lecture by [[peter-underwood]] to the London Appreciation Society and came forward to share his father's experience [2].
