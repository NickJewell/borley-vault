# Bernard Smith (Rector of Newton Green)

Anglican clergyman and **distant cousin of [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]]** who participated in the attempted exorcism of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] on 11 March 1931.

## Role at the Exorcism

Smith was the rector at **Newton Green**, approximately six miles from Borley. He accompanied [[arthur-sellwood|Rev. Arthur Sellwood]] to Borley when Sellwood decided that a formal exorcism was necessary to address the poltergeist activity plaguing the Foysters. Smith brought a **censer and incense** for the ritual.

Since there was no Anglican rite of exorcism, the pair used the **Catholic ritual from 1614**. They went over the entire building from attic to cellar, fumigating every room and passageway with burning incense and blessing with holy water from the shrine at Walsingham.

Though the house was quiet immediately after the rite was completed, the phenomena resumed the same afternoon in Sellwood and Smith's absence, with stones thrown and bells ringing. The exorcism was judged to have failed.
