# Roy Potter

A leading member of a Harlow-based psychical research group and the close associate of its founder, [[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth]], who referred to Potter as his "Chief Investigator" — mirroring the title [[harry-price|Harry Price]] gave to [[sidney-glanville|Sidney Glanville]]. Potter carried out extensive investigations on the former [[borley-rectory|Rectory]] site and in [[borley-church]] between 1970 and 1973.

## Investigation Methods and Equipment (1970–1973)

Between 1970 and 1973, Potter and his colleagues conducted protracted, overnight vigils at the former Rectory site and Borley Church. The group utilised modern technology including delicate scientific instruments, infra-red cameras, tape recorders, and short-range walkie-talkie sets. During a typical vigil, their six-man team would divide into pairs — two on the Rectory site, two on the roadway, and two in the churchyard — rotating every two hours and communicating by radio.

## Phantom Nun Sighting (20 June 1970)

Potter is best known for co-witnessing one of the most famous post-war sightings of [[the-nun|the phantom nun]]. After seeing a mysterious light in the chancel window of [[borley-church|Borley Church]], Potter and investigator Frank Connell were patrolling the roadway. At 1:50 a.m., Croom-Hollingsworth radioed that he was watching an apparition approaching his position in the back garden of a bungalow built on part of the old [[nuns-walk|Nun's Walk]].

Potter ran down the road and saw what he took to be two figures — Croom-Hollingsworth and the apparition, a motionless figure draped from head to foot in grey material. He and Connell felt intense coldness when the ghostly figure was under observation. Potter then watched as the figure turned, **walked straight through a fence**, crossed a ditch, and vanished into a pile of rubble. The investigators claimed the figure had been under observation for approximately twelve minutes — one of the longest sightings ever recorded in the case.

## The "Flying Brick" Incident

Potter's most sensational claim is that during a subsequent investigation he physically interacted with the phantom by throwing a projectile at it. Wondering if the figure was a human masquerading as a ghost, **Potter threw a brick at the apparition; the object went clean through the figure, which remained completely visible and unmoved.**

> **Contradiction:** The sources provide conflicting dates for this event. One entry states this occurred during an encounter in **1970**, while another explicitly records the brick-throwing incident took place on a bright moonlit night in **early 1972**. The duration of the original nun sighting also shifted between versions — described as 8 minutes in some accounts and 12 minutes in others.

## Auditory Phenomena and the Car Experiment

During a misty night vigil at 3:00 a.m., the group heard the sounds of "laughter and merriment" resembling a late-night party coming from the lower end of Hall Lane. Potter coasted his car down the hill with the engine switched off to investigate without warning the presumed revellers — but found no one there. To test the acoustics of the area, he drove onto the Long Melford road and called out at various intervals; his colleagues reported via walkie-talkie that they could only hear his voice when he was relatively near, leaving the source of the party sounds unexplained.

## Interviews and Documentaries

Potter provided independent written reports of his experiences to [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] and was personally interviewed by him in June and July 1972. He also featured in a television documentary about Borley in which he and Croom-Hollingsworth described their extensive visits and visual encounters with the apparition.

## Assessment

[[tony-broughall|Tony Broughall]] became sceptical of both Potter's and Croom-Hollingsworth's experiences, particularly the shifting duration of the nun sighting and the authenticity of the audio recordings. The description of the nun as appearing "elderly" (in her sixties) also conflicts with most earlier accounts which describe a younger woman.
