# W. S. Hammond (BBC)

Member of the BBC staff; official observer at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] on **8–9 January 1938**. Visited with three colleagues — Mr. C. S. Taylor, Mr. G. J. Bell, and Mr. J. Thurley — and submitted a jointly-signed report [1–3]. Listed in Appendix E among the named official witnesses.

## The Visit

Hammond and his group took their duties seriously: upon arrival, they conducted a thorough inspection of the Rectory, sealed all doors, and established a routine of patrolling the building at hourly intervals [2, 4].

**Phenomena recorded:**

- **10 p.m.:** While standing in the passage opposite the Base Room, heard a door being closed. Investigation found no cause [2].
- **~11:30 p.m.:** Coming down the main staircase, heard "the sound of a door being gently closed." Careful investigation found no source [4].
- **2 a.m.:** Found a piece of zinc on the floor of Room 10 that had moved partly outside its chalk mark.
- **2 a.m.:** Noticed "a rather unpleasant odour" in Room 5 "which somewhat suggested the lavatory," which then moved to the [[blue-room|Blue Room]].
- **3:15 a.m.:** Heard a door closing sound again; the observers maintained a critical perspective, noting in their report that this final sound "was apparently outside and might have been caused by the wind which had risen slightly" [6].

Hammond is listed individually in Appendix D under "Displaced or Projected Objects," "Clicks or Cracks," "Sound of Door Closing," "Odours — Pleasant and Unpleasant."

During the vigil, the group also spotted what appeared to be a dark figure moving in the vicinity. Demonstrating the value of objective observation, they investigated and discovered it was not an apparition but merely Mr. Arbon, the caretaker who lived in the adjoining [[rectory-cottage|Rectory Cottage]] [3].

## Context

Hammond's visit falls between the better-documented BBC observations ([[sj-de-lotbiniere|de Lotbinière]]'s July 1937 visit and the Glover/Lloyd Williams February 1938 visit). The door-closing sounds heard three times during a single night — with the house sealed and the night initially quiet — are consistent with phenomena recorded by [[mark-kerr-pearse|Mark Kerr-Pearse]] during his extended residencies.
