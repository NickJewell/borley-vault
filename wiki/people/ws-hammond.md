# W. S. Hammond (BBC)

Member of the BBC staff; official observer at Borley Rectory on January 8, 1938. Visited with three colleagues — Mr. C. S. Taylor, Mr. G. J. Bell, and Mr. J. Thurley — and submitted a jointly-signed report. Listed in Appendix E among the named official witnesses.

## The Visit

The party conducted a thorough inspection, sealed doors and windows, and made hourly patrols through the night.

**Phenomena recorded:**

- At 10 p.m., while standing in the passage opposite the Base Room, heard a door being closed. Investigation found no cause.
- At 12:25 a.m., coming down the main staircase, heard "the sound of a door being gently closed." Again, careful investigation found no source.
- At 2 a.m., found a piece of zinc on the floor of Room 10 that had moved partly outside its chalk mark.
- At 2 a.m., noticed "a rather unpleasant odour" in Room 5 "which somewhat suggested the lavatory," which then moved to the Blue Room.
- At 3:15 a.m., heard a door closing sound again, "apparently outside" — possibly caused by wind.

The four observers jointly signed the report. Hammond is listed individually in Appendix D under "Displaced or Projected Objects," "Clicks or Cracks," "Sound of Door Closing," "Odours — Pleasant and Unpleasant," and "Sound of Door Closing."

## Context

Hammond's visit falls between the better-documented BBC observations (de Lotbinière's July 1937 visit and the Glover/Lloyd Williams February 1938 visit). The door-closing sounds heard three times during a single night without any wind (confirmed quiet early in the evening) and with the house sealed are consistent with phenomena recorded by [[mark-kerr-pearse]] during his extended residencies.
