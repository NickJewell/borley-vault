# Rev. L. de Clare

Clergyman who performed the first exorcism of "Beth-oni," the house of [[ada-sharpe|Miss Ada M. Sharpe]] at Tackley, Oxfordshire, on July 12, 1907.

De Clare employed the standard technique of prayers, incense, and holy water. The result was immediate: the atmosphere of the house "changed" and all was quiet. The effectiveness lasted for approximately six months before phenomena resumed in January 1908, prompting a second exorcism by [[rev-jc-fitzgerald|Rev. J. C. Fitzgerald]] of the Mirfield Community.

De Clare's exorcism is thus a case of partial rather than permanent effectiveness — distinguishing it from Fitzgerald's, which produced permanent cessation. [[harry-price|Price]] mentions de Clare in his survey of clerical exorcisms at poltergeist-infested rectories and houses, comparing them unfavourably (generally) to Beth-oni's eventual resolution.

The name "de Clare" may suggest Norman French descent; no further biographical details are available in Price's account.
