Son of [[henry-bull]] (the builder-Rector) and brother of [[harry-bull]]. Born at [[borley-rectory]]. Spent much of his life at sea, giving him few opportunities to witness phenomena directly, though he lived at Chilton Lodge, Sudbury with his sisters later in life. Interviewed by [[harry-price]] and [[sidney-glanville]] at Chilton Lodge on March 29, 1939.

## Phenomena Witnessed

**Footsteps in the lane**: Walter Bull's primary testimony — and an important one: although he "never saw anything unusual in the Rectory during the time he was living there" (noises were frequent but not disturbing enough to excite comment), he did have a consistent outdoor experience:

> When walking up the lane and approaching the Rectory, "he, at least fifty times, heard footsteps following him." He always turned round, but never saw anyone, and could not account for them.

This testimony matches [[harry-bull]]'s own description of hearing a "great clatter of hoofs" on the same hollow lane while returning to the Rectory, and [[herbert-mayes]]'s 1939 experience of phantom horses on the same stretch of road. The lane outside the Rectory was consistently associated with unexplained sounds.

Walter Bull was present at the Rectory when his father [[henry-bull]] died, but was away when his brother [[harry-bull]] died.

## Significance

A conservative witness who claims minimal direct experience — which makes his fifty-odd footstep encounters the more striking. His testimony contributes to the pattern of outdoor phenomena concentrated on the lane between the Rectory gates.
