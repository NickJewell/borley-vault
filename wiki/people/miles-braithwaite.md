# Miles Braithwaite

Town clerk and former mayor of Sudbury; brother of [[miss-mary-braithwaite|Mary Braithwaite]] and [[wj-braithwaite|John Braithwaite]], the Long Melford family closely connected to the Borley story.

## Background

Miles was one of three siblings from a prominent local family. His father, [[francis-j-braithwaite|Francis J. Braithwaite]], had served as rector of All Saints Church in the nearby village of Acton. Miles lived at **Brook House**, opposite The Bull in Long Melford, sharing the house with his sister Mary. He served as town clerk of Sudbury and had earlier served as mayor of the town.

## Presence at Harry Bull's Funeral

Miles Braithwaite was among the local dignitaries present at [[harry-bull|Harry Bull]]'s funeral at Borley on 14 June 1927, described in the prologue to O'Connor's book as "a former mayor of the nearby town of Sudbury." His family's long connection with the Bulls and the district made him a natural attendee.

## Role in the Foyster Period

When strange phenomena resumed at the Rectory during the Foyster period, it was Miles who first suggested writing to the Society for Psychical Research. He had a sister whose friend was the sister of [[william-salter|William Salter]], the SPR's honorary secretary — a connection that his sister Mary exploited.

> 'The village,' Mary Braithwaite wrote to Salter, 'is becoming almost like a plague spot.'

Miles's concern was primarily reputational: the furore caused by the *Daily Mirror* and Harry Price during the Smith period had brought unwanted attention to the district, and he was anxious to manage the situation through more respectable channels.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Miles Braithwaite** was a highly respected local official and resident of the Borley district who, while never a primary witness to the phenomena, played a crucial administrative role in bringing the Society for Psychical Research (SPR) to Borley Rectory during the Foyster incumbency. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following biographical details and specific events regarding his involvement:

**Biographical Details**
*   **Family Background:** He belonged to a prominent local family; his father, Francis J. Braithwaite, had formerly served as the rector of All Saints Church in the nearby village of Acton [1]. He had a sister, **Mary Braithwaite, J.P.**, and a brother, **John Braithwaite** (later Sir John Braithwaite), who was a civil servant based in London [1]. 
*   **Occupation and Residence:** Miles served as the **town clerk of Sudbury** and had previously held the prestigious position of **mayor of the town** [1]. He lived with his sister Mary at **Brook House**, situated opposite The Bull Hotel in Long Melford [1].
*   **Local Connections:** The Braithwaite family had known Borley Rectory and the Bull family since their childhood, and they were also good friends with Sir George and Lady Whitehouse [1]. 

**Initiating the SPR Investigation (1931)**
During the height of the alleged poltergeist activity surrounding the Rev. Lionel and Marianne Foyster in 1931, the village was filled with dramatic rumors, with Mary Braithwaite noting the area was becoming "almost like a plague spot" [2]. 
*   Miles grew increasingly concerned about the circulating stories regarding the Foysters' experiences [2]. Remembering the massive media furore caused by the arrival of the *Daily Mirror* and investigator Harry Price during the previous Smith incumbency, **Miles Braithwaite explicitly suggested that the family write to the Society for Psychical Research in London to have them formally investigate the phenomena** [2]. 
*   Because his sister Mary had a friend who was the sister of **William Salter** (the honorary secretary and treasurer of the SPR), she acted on Miles's suggestion and contacted Salter directly to intervene [2]. This action eventually led to the skeptical SPR investigation of the Foysters.

**Absence During the Investigations**
Despite instigating the SPR's involvement, Miles Braithwaite was absent during the initial stages of their inquiry. The sources record that in the late summer of 1931, the Braithwaite family traveled to stay with friends in **Malton, Yorkshire**, for a holiday [3]. Following this, Mary Braithwaite informed William Salter that she and Miles were **going abroad for a holiday in September**, suggesting that Salter delay his visit to Borley or coordinate with their brother John (who had taken his own notes on the haunting) until she and Miles returned [3].
