# Miles Braithwaite

**Town clerk and former mayor of Sudbury**; brother of [[miss-mary-braithwaite|Mary Braithwaite]] and [[wj-braithwaite|John Braithwaite]]. His father, [[francis-j-braithwaite|Francis J. Braithwaite]], had served as rector of All Saints Church in the nearby village of Acton [1]. Miles lived with his sister Mary at **Brook House**, situated opposite The Bull Hotel in Long Melford [1].

The Braithwaite family had known Borley Rectory and the Bull family since their childhood, and were also good friends with Sir George and Lady Whitehouse of Arthur Hall [1].

## Presence at Harry Bull's Funeral

Miles Braithwaite was among the local dignitaries present at [[harry-bull|Harry Bull]]'s funeral at Borley on 14 June 1927, described in the prologue to O'Connor's book as "a former mayor of the nearby town of Sudbury." His family's long connection with the Bulls and the district made him a natural attendee.

## Initiating the SPR Investigation (1931)

During the height of the alleged poltergeist activity surrounding the Rev. [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] and [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] in 1931, Miles grew increasingly concerned about the circulating stories [2]. Remembering the massive media furore caused by the arrival of the *Daily Mirror* and [[harry-price|Harry Price]] during the Smith incumbency, **Miles explicitly suggested that his family write to the Society for Psychical Research in London** to have them formally investigate the phenomena [2]. His concern was primarily reputational: he was anxious to manage the situation through more respectable channels.

Because his sister Mary had a friend who was the sister of [[william-salter|William Salter]] (the SPR's honorary secretary and treasurer), she acted on Miles's suggestion and wrote to Salter directly in August 1931, describing the village as becoming "almost like a plague spot" [2]. This action eventually led to the sceptical SPR investigation of the Foysters.

## Absence During the Investigations

Despite instigating the SPR's involvement, Miles was absent during the initial stages of the inquiry. In the late summer of 1931 the Braithwaite family travelled to stay with friends in **Malton, Yorkshire**, and subsequently went abroad for a holiday in September [3]. Mary accordingly asked Salter to delay his visit until their return, advising him to speak to both the Bulls and the Foysters because "it is almost impossible to explain things, as everyone has a different version" [3].
