# Violet Oates

Sister of Captain Lawrence Oates, the Antarctic explorer who died on the ill-fated Scott expedition of 1911–12. Violet Oates became a friend and parishioner of the Mathias family during their years at Liston, and is remembered by [[claudine-mathias|Claudine Mathias]] as very generous to Liston church.

## Background

The Oates family home was the Hall at nearby Gestingthorpe, a few miles from Liston. Following the death of her mother, Violet sold the large Hall to a London businessman and moved to a more modest establishment at Liston, where she came under the pastoral care of the [[rev-lanfranc-mathias|Rev Lanfranc Mathias]].

Violet was highly regarded in the local area and, according to CM, did a great deal of work for and was very generous to Liston church. It was through Violet Oates that CM met the sister of Dr Edward Adrian Wilson, another member of the ill-fated Scott expedition who also failed to return.

## Connection to Gestingthorpe

After Violet Oates died, Lanfranc Mathias moved to his last living at Gestingthorpe — the very village where the Oates family had lived. The family home he had heard about from Violet became part of his final parish.

## Captain Lawrence Oates

Violet's brother, Captain Lawrence Edward Grace Oates (1880–1912), is one of the most celebrated figures of the Heroic Age of Antarctic Exploration. He was a member of Captain Robert Falcon Scott's ill-fated Terra Nova expedition. Suffering severely from frostbite on the return journey from the South Pole, he walked out of the tent into a blizzard saying, reportedly, "I am just going outside and may be some time" — a gesture of self-sacrifice to spare his companions. He died on or around 17 March 1912.

**Miss Violet Oates** was a wealthy local parishioner who became a close friend to the family of the Rev. Lanfranc Mathias, the final rector of the combined parishes of Borley and Liston [1]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the sources reveals the following biographical details and connections regarding her life in the Borley area:

**Family and the Antarctic Connection**
*   Violet was **one of the two sisters of Captain Lawrence Oates**, the famous British explorer who famously perished during Captain Robert Falcon Scott's ill-fated expedition to the Antarctic in 1911–1912 [1]. 
*   Through her personal connections, she later introduced the Borley rector's wife (Mrs. Claudine Mathias) to the sister of Dr. Edward Wilson, another tragic member of the Scott expedition who failed to return [1].

**Residences in the Borley Area**
*   The Oates family was extremely wealthy, and their primary family home was **the Hall at nearby Gestingthorpe** [1]. 
*   Following the death of her mother, Violet sold this massive house to a London businessman and chose to move to a "more modest establishment" in the parish of **Liston** [1]. 
*   Her specific home in Liston was a house called **'Highfield'** [2]. This house had originally been built as a private, modern home (sometimes referred to as the "New Rectory") by the Rev. Stewart Travers Fisher, a former rector of Liston who wished to raise his family outside of the Old Rectory [2]. 

**Church Involvement and Death**
*   She was highly regarded as a **very generous benefactor** who "did a great deal of work" for the **Liston church** [1]. 
*   Violet Oates died shortly before the Rev. Lanfranc Mathias was transferred to Gestingthorpe for his final incumbency, a move that the Borley chronology records as taking place in **1967** [1, 3].
