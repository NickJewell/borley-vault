# Grant Vallender

A young psychical researcher who conducted a focused, overnight investigation of the Borley site over the weekend of **June 8–9, 1968** [1, 2].

## Visit

Vallender began his trip by visiting the Rectory at Foxearth, where he spoke with Canon [[canon-leslie-pennell|J. H. L. Pennell]] for about half an hour [2]. From there, he walked to Borley via a cart track leading to Brook Hall, a building he noted looked **"quite astonishingly like the vanished Borley Rectory"** [2]. He spent the afternoon taking photographs and was struck by the site's **"serenity and quiet"** [2].

## Interviews with Historic Witnesses

During the afternoon of June 8, Vallender spoke with two significant local residents:

- **[[mr-and-mrs-arbon|Lionel Arbon]]:** The caretaker who had lived in the [[rectory-cottage|Rectory Cottage]] during [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s 1937–38 tenancy. Arbon described Price as **"a good fellow"** [2].
- **[[payne-family|Mrs. E. E. Payne]]** of [[borley-place|Borley Place]]: She recounted witnessing the strange lights in the Rectory windows during the incumbency of [[rev-eric-smith|Rev. Guy Eric Smith]] [2]. Regarding the Foyster period, she thought [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] was a very "funny" woman and felt certain Marianne was personally responsible for the famous [[wine-into-ink-trick|"wine-into-ink" trick]] during Price's 1931 visit [2]. Like Arbon, she affirmed to Vallender that she believed Harry Price was an honest man [2].

## The Tithe-Barn Incident (7:00 p.m.)

At 6:00 p.m. Vallender took up a position in [[borley-church|Borley churchyard]] [3]. About an hour later, at approximately **7:00 p.m.**, he experienced what he explicitly described as a **"typical poltergeist phenomenon"** [3].

> "He was standing in the churchyard by the wall of the tithe-barn; when he looked up, he noticed a large pebble travelling over the roof. It hit the roof and fell into the churchyard. He immediately rushed round to the other side of the tithe barn but found no one there." [3]

He noted that the angle of the pebble's trajectory indicated that the throwing force must have been located very close to the opposite side of the barn — making the lack of a culprit highly unusual [3]. Vallender spent the rest of the night keeping vigil in the churchyard but recorded nothing further of interest [3].
