# Grant Vallender

A young psychical researcher who visited Borley on Saturday 8 June 1968, taking photographs and talking to local residents.

## Visit

Vallender called at Foxearth Rectory where he met Canon [[canon-leslie-pennell|J. H. L. Pennell]] and talked for about half an hour. He then walked to Borley via the cart track to Brook Hall, which he found "astonishingly like the vanished Borley Rectory." He spent the afternoon photographing the site and spoke with [[mr-and-mrs-arbon|Mr Arbon]], who had been caretaker during [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s tenancy, and with [[payne-family|Mrs E. E. Payne]] of Borley Place.

At 6 pm, sitting in the churchyard by the wall of the tithe barn, he witnessed what he described as "a typical poltergeist phenomenon":

> "He was standing in the churchyard by the wall of the tithe-barn; when he looked up, he noticed a large pebble travelling over the roof. It hit the roof and fell into the churchyard. He immediately rushed round to the other side of the tithe barn but found no one there."

The angle of the pebble's trajectory indicated that the throwing force must have been very close to the far side of the barn — yet there was no one there when he investigated. Vallender spent the rest of the night in the churchyard but noted nothing further of interest.
