# Miss Jane Conolly

One of [[gerard-kelman|Gerard Kelman]]'s companions on a July 1966 overnight visit to Borley. In the early hours of Sunday 24 July, at approximately 5 am, she and [[miss-margaret-conolly|Miss Margaret Anne Conolly]] independently described a grey figure near the Rectory site that was also seen by Kelman.

## Her Account

Jane Conolly and the group had stood for "some considerable time" outside the drive gate without incident. Then they all heard the crunch of footsteps on gravel without being able to see anyone:

> "As I looked across the Rectory site, I thought I could make out a grey figure standing by the door of the shed. Suddenly the figure seemed to move, but it did not advance in a normal way. I could make out a small head on broad shoulders, but from the waist downwards, the figure was blurred... Then it moved about a yard or so to the left and eventually disappeared near the large tree at the edge of the former site of Borley Rectory. I was not afraid in any way, only delighted to have seen this 'apparition.'"

Her account is complementary to but independent of [[gerard-kelman|Kelman]]'s, and both differ in detail from that of her sister [[miss-margaret-conolly|Miss Margaret Anne Conolly]], who could see only the lower half of the figure from her vantage point. The three partial but consistent observations are treated by Underwood and Tabori as mutual corroboration of an actual three-dimensional form.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Miss Jane Conolly** (whose surname is also recorded as **Connally** in some chronological entries) was a visitor to the grounds of Borley Rectory whose primary contribution to the Borley literature is an independent, first-hand account of a phantom sighting in the 1960s [1, 2]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the texts reveals the following specific details, dates, and quotes regarding her experience:

**The Visit of July 23–24, 1966**
Miss Conolly paid an overnight visit to the ruined site of Borley Rectory over the weekend of **July 23–24, 1966**. She was accompanied by a ghost-hunter from Buckhurst Hill, Essex, named Gerald Kelman, and another woman named Miss Margaret Anne Conolly [1, 2]. 

**Auditory and Visual Phenomena**
According to her own written report of the night's events, the group stood outside the Rectory drive gate for a considerable amount of time without anything happening. Eventually, the silence was broken when they all **"heard the crunch of footsteps on gravel but they could see no one"** [3]. 

The visual climax of the night occurred at **about 5:00 a.m. on July 24**. In the half-light just before daybreak, Miss Conolly looked across the deserted Rectory site and saw a **"grey-blue shape"** standing by the door of a shed [1, 3]. She noted that the shape was **"vaguely human and covered from head to toe with what appeared to be a flowing garment,"** leading the observers to classify it as a "nun-like" figure [1, 2]. 

**Direct Quotes and Emotional Reaction**
Miss Conolly provided specific details regarding the apparition's abnormal movement and physical appearance, writing in her report:
> **"Suddenly the figure seemed to move, but it did not advance in a normal way. I could make out a small head on broad shoulders, but from the waist downwards, the figure was blurred... Then it moved about a yard or so to the left and eventually disappeared near the large tree at the edge of the former site of Borley Rectory"** [3].

Notably, whereas the majority of witnesses at Borley reported intense fear or an overwhelming sense of evil when encountering phenomena, Miss Conolly specifically documented a highly positive reaction to her experience. She concluded her report by stating: **"I was not afraid in any way, only delighted to have seen this 'apparition'"** [3].
