# Sergeant Downing

A British serviceman from **Bromley, Kent**, whose two sons **David** and **Stewart Downing** were evacuees placed with the Fisher–Foyster household at [[dairy-cottage-rendlesham|Dairy Cottage, Rendlesham]] during the Second World War.

## Account

[[john-knights|John Fisher]] (Marianne's adopted son) recalled the Downing boys as part of the Rendlesham household in his childhood memoirs:

> "There were also two evacuees with us at Rendlesham, David and Stewart Downing and their father Sergeant Downing from Bromley Kent would visit them and seemed much taken with Mrs Fisher. In fact I believe that he wanted to marry her."

The evacuees' father visiting and becoming attracted to Marianne fits her established pattern of drawing men into her orbit — even those whose contact began in mundane domestic circumstances. Whether anything came of his interest is not recorded.

## Significance

The brief reference suggests that Marianne's life at Dairy Cottage was not entirely isolated, despite the remote location. Evacuees and their visiting parents created at least some domestic traffic. John Fisher's observation that Sergeant Downing "seemed much taken with Mrs Fisher" and may have wanted to marry her is consistent with the picture Wood paints of men consistently falling for Marianne regardless of her circumstances.
