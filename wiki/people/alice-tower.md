# Alice Tower

Farmer's wife in Westcock, New Brunswick; birth mother of **Barbara Tower** who became [[adelaide-foyster|Adelaide Foyster]] after being adopted by [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]] and [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] in 1928.

## Background

Alice Tower was married to **[[seward-tower|Seward Tower]]**, a farmer in **Westcock**, an isolated rural village approximately 8 kilometres from Sackville, New Brunswick [1]. Together the couple had seven children [1, 2]. Her youngest child was born on 20 March 1928 and was originally named Barbara Alice Tower before the Foysters adopted her and renamed her [[adelaide-foyster|Adelaide]] [1, 2].

> **Contradiction:** The Borley sources contain a direct contradiction concerning exactly how Alice Tower died. *The Borley Rectory Companion* states that Alice "died in childbirth" [2], while *The Widow of Borley* asserts that she died of a "brain tumour that January" [1].

Alice Tower died tragically while her youngest child was still an infant [1].

## The Adoption

Approximately a month after Alice's death, her husband [[seward-tower|Seward]] fell off a load of marsh hay on his farm and fractured his skull, leaving all seven children effectively orphaned [1, 2]. While the older children were parcelled out among various aunts and uncles, the Foysters were asked to look after the infant Barbara, leading to her adoption and relocation to England [1].

[[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] found the baby pretty and Nordic-looking, reminding him of a photograph of his own late mother as a child. He and [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] renamed the baby **Adelaide** — after Lionel's mother — and she was brought up as the Foysters' adopted daughter.

## Significance

Alice Tower's early death, and the subsequent orphaning of the Tower children, set in motion a chain of events with long-lasting consequences. [[adelaide-foyster|Adelaide]] grew up never knowing she had siblings in Canada. [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] told her that her "sister Edna" was only a cousin, suppressing the truth of her origins. The Tower family spent forty-six years searching for Adelaide before finally locating her in 1974 (see [[wallace-tower]]).
