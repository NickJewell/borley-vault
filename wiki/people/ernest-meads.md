# Ernest Meads

Friend of [[wj-braithwaite|W. John Braithwaite]] who played a central role in conducting a séance at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] on 12 August 1931 during the [[foyster-phenomena|Foyster incumbency]], alongside Braithwaite and the medium [[mr-johnson-medium|Johnson]] [1]. The Rev. [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] was also present.

## Introduction to Borley

While Braithwaite was on a summer holiday in Long Melford, he visited the Rev. Lionel Foyster and suggested bringing Meads and a 67-year-old medium named Mr. Johnson to the Rectory [1, 2]. Lionel Foyster was initially conflicted and uncomfortable about being drawn into spiritualist practices [1]. Braithwaite successfully placated the devout rector by assuring him that **Meads resolved such issues by utilising prayer** [1]. The men arrived at the Rectory at 6 p.m. on Wednesday 12 August 1931, inspected the house, and gathered in the library, with Marianne Foyster choosing not to participate [2].

## The Séance

Sitting around the table and joining hands, **Meads officially began the séance with a prayer** [3]. As Johnson fell into a trance, he channelled several personalities, and Meads took an active role in communicating with the entities:

**The spirit "Mary":** An entity identifying herself as "Mary" came through sighing and crying in a high voice, claiming she felt rebellious because she had been turned out into the rain to sleep under a hedge, and confessing that she had murdered her baby [4]. **Meads bluntly cut in: "Why, you're dead"** [4]. He then made a concerted effort to get the spirit to pray and turn to better things, securing a partial promise from her [4].

**Joe Miles:** Another entity identified itself as a drunken ex-publican named "Joe Miles," who kept calling for drinks and did not appear to realise he was dead [3, 4]. Meads actively coaxed him during the session, attempting with Braithwaite to persuade the entity to accept its death [3].

## Conclusion and Advice

Following the departure of the Joe Miles entity, both Meads and Johnson were highly confident that their séance had been a success [3]. Convinced they had pacified the troublesome spirit responsible for the haunting, Meads offered the following specific advice to Foyster and Braithwaite:

> **"There you have, I think, the cause of your worries. You will, I believe, be troubled no further. If you are, speak nicely and gently to him as I did. Don't threaten, but coax; remind him of his promise and soothe him down. He will soon respond."** [3]

The 5,000-word report compiled by Braithwaite was drawn from notes taken on the night, and Meads was presumably among those present when those notes were made. His role in the broader Borley investigation is not independently documented beyond this single séance.
