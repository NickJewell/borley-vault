# Ernest Meads

One of three sitters at the séance held at Borley Rectory on August 11, 1931, alongside [[wj-braithwaite|W. John Braithwaite]] and the medium [[mr-johnson-medium|Johnson]]. The Rev. [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] was also present.

Meads played an active role in communicating with the "entities" channeled by Johnson, particularly during the appearance of the "Mary" entity (who claimed to have murdered her baby):

> *"'Why, you're dead,' Meads cut in."*

He also engaged with "Joe Miles" (the drunken ex-publican entity), attempting with Braithwaite to persuade the entity that he was dead and encouraging him to accept it.

Meads's role in the broader Borley investigation is not independently documented beyond this single séance; he appears as part of the group brought to the Rectory by Braithwaite. The 5,000-word report compiled by Braithwaite was drawn from notes taken on the night, and Meads was presumably one of the people present when those notes were made.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Ernest Meads** was a friend of W. John Braithwaite who played a central role in conducting a séance at Borley Rectory during the Foyster incumbency to rid the house of its ghosts [1]. 

A thorough reference profile drawn from the Borley sources reveals the following facts, events, and quotes regarding his involvement:

**Introduction to Borley (August 1931)**
While Braithwaite was on a summer holiday in Long Melford, he visited the Rev. Lionel Foyster and suggested bringing Meads and a 67-year-old medium named Mr. Johnson to the Rectory [1, 2]. Lionel Foyster was initially conflicted and uncomfortable about being drawn into spiritualist practices [1]. However, Braithwaite successfully placated the devout rector by assuring him that **Meads resolved such issues by utilizing prayer** [1]. 

**The "Joe Miles" Séance (August 12, 1931)**
Braithwaite, Meads, and Johnson arrived at the Rectory at 6 p.m. on Wednesday, August 12, 1931 [1, 2]. After inspecting the house, the men gathered in the library with Lionel Foyster (Marianne Foyster having chosen not to participate) [2]. Sitting around the table, the four men joined hands, and **Meads officially began the séance with a prayer** [3]. 

**Interactions with Spirits**
As Mr. Johnson fell into a trance, he channeled several personalities, and Meads took an active role in questioning and speaking to the entities:
*   **The Spirit "Mary":** An entity identifying herself as "Mary" came through sighing and crying in a high voice, claiming she felt rebellious because she had been turned out into the rain to sleep under a hedge, and confessing that she had murdered her baby [4]. **Meads bluntly cut in, telling her, "Why, you’re dead"** [4]. Meads then made a concerted effort to get the spirit to pray and turn to better things, securing a partial promise from her that she would do so [4].
*   **Joe Miles:** Another entity was summoned identifying as a drunken ex-publican named "Joe Miles," who kept calling for drinks and did not appear to realize he was dead [3, 4]. Meads actively coaxed him during the session [3].

**Meads's Conclusion and Advice**
Following the departure of the Joe Miles entity, both Meads and Mr. Johnson were highly confident that their séance had been a success [3]. Convinced that they had pacified the troublesome spirit responsible for the haunting, Meads offered the following specific advice to Lionel Foyster and Braithwaite: 
**"There you have, I think, the cause of your worries. You will, I believe, be troubled no further. If you are, speak nicely and gently to him as I did. Don’t threaten, but coax; remind him of his promise and soothe him down. He will soon respond"** [3].
