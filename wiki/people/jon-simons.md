# Jon Simons

Of Higham Park, London, with a long-standing interest in the ghosts and folklore of Essex. Simons visited Borley many times without having any supernormal experiences. On 4 February 1970, a Saturday, he and [[miss-marcia-matthews|Miss Marcia Matthews]] were reading entries in the Visitors' Book at [[borley-church|Borley Church]] when they heard footsteps coming up the path. No one entered the church; there was no one in the porch or anywhere in the churchyard when they looked.

His experience was one of many post-fire reports of auditory phenomena at the church, despite the absence of any Rectory and no formal investigation under way.

**Jon Simons** is recorded in the Borley sources as a resident of Higham Park, London, who harbored a long-standing interest in the ghosts and folklore of Essex [1, 2]. 

A thorough review of the texts reveals the following specific details regarding his involvement:

**Prior Investigations**
Despite his deep interest in local folklore, Simons visited Borley many times without ever experiencing any supernormal phenomena during his initial trips [1, 2].

**The 1970 Churchyard Footsteps**
His single recorded paranormal experience occurred on **February 4, 1970** (which the texts explicitly specify was a Saturday) [1, 2]. Simons was visiting Borley Church accompanied by Miss Marcia Matthews [1, 2]. 

While the two of them were inside the building reading entries in the church's Visitors' Book, they clearly heard the sound of footsteps coming up the outside path [1, 2]. Despite the approaching sounds, no one entered the church [1, 2]. When Simons and Matthews looked outside a moment later to investigate, they found that there was absolutely no one in the church porch or anywhere in the surrounding churchyard to account for the footsteps [1, 2]. 

*(Note: The sources universally state that February 4, 1970, was a Saturday, though historically this date actually fell on a Wednesday. The texts provide no further quotes, contradictions, or biographical details regarding Jon Simons beyond this single incident.)*
