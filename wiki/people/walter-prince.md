# Dr Walter F. Prince

An American psychical researcher who wrote a detailed critical analysis of the [[great-amherst-mystery|Great Amherst Mystery]] — **"A Critical Study of 'The Great Amherst Mystery'"** — published in Vol. XIII of the *Proceedings of the American Society for Psychical Research* (New York, 1919, pp. 89–130) [1]. He argued formidably for trickery by [[esther-cox|Esther Cox]] while in a state of dissociation.

## The Amherst Analysis

Prince's 1919 paper demonstrated two things: first, that the published account by [[walter-hubbell|Walter Hubbell]] was greatly embellished compared with earlier and shorter original testimony [2]; and second, that in December 1878 Esther Cox visited her married sister in **Sackville** — the very town where [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] would be rector almost exactly fifty years later.

This analysis is directly parallel to what the [[spr|SPR]]'s 1956 investigators would apply to [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]]'s manuscripts in the Borley case:

| Amherst | Borley |
|---|---|
| Dr Walter F. Prince demonstrated published account was much embellished vs. the earlier shorter Diary | The SPR investigators demonstrate *Fifteen Months in a Haunted House* was much embellished vs. the *Diary of Occurrences* |

Despite this thorough debunking, the results of his investigation reportedly **"never reached the public"** [2]. As a result, the Amherst case continued to be regarded as a classic haunting and was still being cited by writers as genuine decades later — as it was by [[hereward-carrington|Hereward Carrington]] and [[nandor-fodor|Nandor Fodor]] in their 1953 book, which credited the Amherst case on p. 42 as authentic.

## Harry Price's Library

[[harry-price|Harry Price]] owned two volumes containing Prince's work on Amherst: Carrington's *Personal Experiences in Spiritualism* (London, 1913, pp. 95–124) and the relevant volume of the *Proceedings of the American S.P.R.* The [[spr|SPR]] investigators noted this to establish that the same Price who claimed the Borley [[wall-writings|wall-writings]] were "unique in the annals of psychical research" actually possessed books demonstrating that almost identical wall-writings had occurred in the Amherst case — and thus cannot have been unaware of the parallel [1].
