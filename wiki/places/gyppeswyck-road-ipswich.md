# Gyppeswyck Road, Ipswich

A cul-de-sac (also spelled **Gippeswyk Road** on official documents) off Ancaster Road, Ipswich, a few minutes' walk from Ipswich railway station. **No. 12 Gyppeswyck Road** was where [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] first lodged in Ipswich in the mid-1930s, renting from [[mrs-amy-saunders|Mrs Amy Saunders]].

## The Street

Robert Wood describes Gyppeswyck Road and its neighbouring streets as **"substantial, red-brick Victorian houses."** By the 1930s many had been divided into flats for working-class families; by Wood's writing (presumably 1980s) the area had become "smart and fashionable."

## Marianne's Use

Marianne rented a room at No. 12 as a base for her single-woman masquerade, using the name **"Miss Foyster"** or **"Miss Voyster"** (later corrupted from Foyster), presenting herself as an unattached woman. A neighbour recalled "an attractive dark-haired woman who had lived at No. 12, and who called herself by many names."

The same neighbour confirmed to [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]] (investigating in the 1950s) that the woman "was often visited by an elderly gentleman" — presumably [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] visiting his wife's lodgings (though author Robert Wood also speculated this might have been "Santiago Monk," her "spiritual godfather").

From this base Marianne met [[henry-fisher|Henry Francis Fisher]], possibly at Colchester or Marks Tey station (she was uncertain in her Swanson interview). The affair quickly developed, and Fisher was taken to visit the "elderly father" at Borley before the bigamous marriage at **St Pancras Roman Catholic Church, Ipswich**, on 23 February 1935. Marianne officially gave her address on the marriage certificate as "12 Gippeswyk Road, Ipswich."

> **Contradiction:** Despite "12 Gippeswyk Road" being recorded as her residence on the 1935 marriage certificate, Marianne explicitly claimed in a letter dated July 3, 1956, that **she had never lived at this address**.

## After 1935

After the bigamous marriage, the household moved first to 4 Ancaster Road (next to the railway station), then to [[hill-farm-house-chillesford|Chillesford]] in 1938. Marianne maintained her Ipswich connections throughout: she rented rooms in Ranelagh Road (also from Mrs Saunders) in 1945–46 before her departure to America.
