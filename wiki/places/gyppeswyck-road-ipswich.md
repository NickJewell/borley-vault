# Gyppeswyck Road, Ipswich

A cul-de-sac off Ancaster Road, Ipswich, a few minutes' walk from Ipswich railway station. **No. 12 Gyppeswyck Road** was where [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] first lodged in Ipswich in the mid-1930s, renting from [[mrs-amy-saunders|Mrs Amy Saunders]].

## The Street

Robert Wood describes Gyppeswyck Road and its neighbouring streets as "substantial, red-brick Victorian houses." By the 1930s they had undergone the usual vicissitudes of residential areas near English city centres — many divided into flats for working-class families. By Wood's writing (presumably 1980s) the area had become "smart and fashionable."

## Marianne's Use

Marianne rented a room at No. 12 as a base for her single-woman masquerade. Using the name **"Miss Foyster"** or **"Miss Voyster"** (later corrupted from Foyster), she presented herself as an unattached woman. A neighbour recalled "an attractive dark-haired woman who had lived at No. 12, and who called herself by many names."

The same neighbour confirmed to Trevor Hall (investigating in the 1950s) that the woman "was often visited by an elderly gentleman" — presumably [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] visiting his wife's lodgings.

From this base Marianne met [[henry-fisher|Henry Francis Fisher]], possibly at Colchester or Marks Tey station (she was uncertain in her Swanson interview). The affair quickly developed, and Fisher was taken to visit the "elderly father" at Borley before the bigamous marriage at **St Pancras Roman Catholic Church, Ipswich**, on 23 February 1935.

## After 1935

After the bigamous marriage, the household moved first to 4 Ancaster Road (next to the railway station), then to [[hill-farm-house-chillesford|Chillesford]] in 1938. Marianne maintained her Ipswich connections throughout: she rented rooms in Ranelagh Road (also from Mrs Saunders) in 1945–46 before her departure to America.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Gyppeswyck Road** (also spelled **Gippeswyk Road** on official documents) in Ipswich played a significant role in the secret life of Marianne Foyster, the wife of the Borley Rector, during the mid-1930s. 

Here are the specific details recorded in the sources, suitable for a reference article:

### **Physical Description and Location**
*   **Location:** The road is a cul-de-sac located just off Ancaster Road, situated only a few minutes' walk from the Ipswich railway station [1]. 
*   **Architecture:** Gyppeswyck Road and its adjoining streets consist of **"substantial, red-brick Victorian houses"** [1].
*   **Atmosphere:** During the 1930s, many of these large houses had been divided into flats and were inhabited by poor, working-class families (though the area has since become smart and fashionable) [1]. 

### **Associated Persons**
*   **Marianne Foyster (living as Miss Foyster or Mariane Voyster):** She rented a room or rooms at **Number 12 Gyppeswyck Road** [2].
*   **Mrs. Amy Saunders:** The landlady who owned the property and rented the rooms to Marianne [2]. (Marianne would later rent another room from her in Ranelagh Road) [2].
*   **An "elderly gentleman":** An unnamed individual whom neighbors remembered frequently visiting Marianne at No. 12 [2]. (Author Robert Wood speculated this might have been "Santiago Monk", her "spiritual godfather") [2].
*   **Trevor H. Hall:** The psychical researcher who visited the road in the mid-1950s to investigate Marianne's past [2].

### **Key Events**
*   **Marianne's Escape (Mid-1930s):** Marianne used her rented accommodation at No. 12 as a retreat from Borley Rectory and the collapse of her flower shop business in Wimbledon [2, 3]. In Ipswich, she masqueraded as a single woman, assumed false identities, and was remembered by a neighbor as an attractive, dark-haired woman who called herself by many different names [2, 4].
*   **The Bigamous Marriage (February 23, 1935):** When Marianne bigamously married commercial traveller Henry Francis Fisher at St Pancras Roman Catholic Church in Ipswich, she officially gave her address on the marriage certificate as **"12 Gippeswyk Road, Ipswich"** [5].
*   **Hall's Investigation (Mid-1950s):** During his investigation into the Foyster incumbency, Trevor Hall visited the road and interviewed a neighboring woman who provided the corroborating memories of Marianne's time living at No. 12 [2]. 

### **Contradictions and Source Discrepancies**
*   **The Residence Denial:** The most significant contradiction regarding Gyppeswyck Road comes from Marianne Foyster herself. Despite "12 Gippeswyk Road" being recorded as her residence on her 1935 marriage certificate to Henry Fisher, Marianne explicitly claimed in a letter dated July 3, 1956, that **she had never lived at this address** [5]. 
*   **Spelling:** The sources reflect variations in the spelling of the street, citing it as "Gyppeswyck Road" in retrospective narratives, but noting it was spelled "Gippeswyk Road" on her marriage certificate [1, 5].
