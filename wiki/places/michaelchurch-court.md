# Michaelchurch Court, Herefordshire

The Fisher family home, located at **Michaelchurch, near Ross-on-Wye, Herefordshire**. It is where [[henry-fisher|Henry Francis Fisher]] was born on 22 December 1900 and raised; his father had been a farmer there.

## The Fisher Family Home

Wood describes Fisher's family as "well-to-do" and from "the west of England." Michaelchurch Court was a country house, with Fisher's father farming the land. After Fisher's final break with Marianne and his emotional collapse in 1945, he "retired into seclusion and never told his side of the story. He was cared for by his sister, and died at Worcester in the late 1960s."

## Marianne's Visit

Marianne visited Michaelchurch Court, introducing herself and [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] to the Fisher family under false pretences — Foyster posed as her father. Fisher's mother even came to stay at the bungalow in Ipswich (102 Woodbridge Road East) for a holiday of several weeks, during which she tried without success to see "old Mr Voyster" in his locked room upstairs.

Fisher's sister is the source for Wood's account of the family's growing suspicions: she confirmed that "her mother tried to see Mr Foyster (or Voyster) at the Ipswich bungalow but his door was always locked" and that Foyster "would sometimes refer to Marianne as his wife and then quickly correct himself."

It was also from Fisher's sister that Wood learned of Fisher's fate after the final break: that the Fisher family paid for the house at Woodbridge Road East ("according to Fisher's sister, the house was paid for by the Fisher family"), and that Fisher's family "decided not to pursue Marianne to spare him further agony."
