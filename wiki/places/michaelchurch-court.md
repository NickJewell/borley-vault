# Michaelchurch Court, Herefordshire

The Fisher family home, located at **Michaelchurch, near Ross-on-Wye, Herefordshire**, in the west of England. It is where [[henry-fisher|Henry Francis Fisher]] was born on 22 December 1900 and raised; his father operated as a farmer there. The sources describe it simply as a "country house" situated on a farm [1], with the Fisher family characterised as "well-to-do" [1].

## The Fisher Family Home

Fisher's father farmed the land at Michaelchurch Court. Henry Fisher — known to acquaintances as "Johnnie" — was the commercial traveller who later unknowingly entered into a bigamous marriage with [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] during the final year of her legal husband [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]]'s incumbency at [[borley-rectory]] [2].

After Fisher's final break with Marianne and his emotional collapse in 1945, he retired into seclusion and never told his side of the story. He was cared for by his sister and died at Worcester in the late 1960s. The Fisher family decided not to pursue [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] legally, reportedly to spare him further agony.

> **Note:** There is a minor discrepancy in the sources regarding Fisher's exact date of birth: Wood records it as 22 December 1900 in one place [1] and 21 December 1900 in another [2].

## Marianne's Visit

[[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] visited Michaelchurch Court, introducing herself and [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] to the Fisher family under false pretences — Foyster posed as her father. Fisher's mother even came to stay at the bungalow in Ipswich (102 Woodbridge Road East) for a holiday of several weeks, during which she tried without success to see "old Mr Voyster" in his locked room upstairs.

Fisher's sister is the source for Wood's account of the family's growing suspicions: she confirmed that "her mother tried to see Mr Foyster (or Voyster) at the Ipswich bungalow but his door was always locked" and that Foyster "would sometimes refer to Marianne as his wife and then quickly correct himself." It was also from Fisher's sister that Wood learned that the Fisher family paid for the house at Woodbridge Road East.
