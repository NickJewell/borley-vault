# Chale Rectory (Isle of Wight)

A rectory on the Isle of Wight where, from 1940, the Reverend [[rev-c-sinclair|C. Sinclair]] and his wife experienced phenomena strikingly similar to those reported at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]]. The Sinclairs' account was published in a letter to the *Church Times* in October 1945, the same issue in which [[mabel-smith|Mrs Mabel Smith]] denied that Borley had been haunted.

## Phenomena

Former maids at Chale Rectory claimed that a carriage and pair had been driven into the yard at midnight on many occasions — heard but not seen, a close parallel to the Borley [[phantom-coach|phantom coach]]. The Sinclairs themselves reported:

- Jingling as of harness; creaks, clangings, grinding and jarring sounds; a "coconutty" horses-hooves noise
- Preserves and objects knocked off shelves
- Stealthy footsteps on landings
- "Cold spots"
- Mysterious vigils by the family cat and dog outside closed doors of empty rooms
- Pencil-scribbling (established as due to a sub-tenant's child)

Two visitors then arrived and placed "little heaps of powder here and there with ancillary mutterings," returning three times at three-day intervals. After their final visit, all the curious noises ceased.

## Significance

The Sinclairs' account is cited in *Ghosts of Borley* as one of many parallel cases — churches and rectories across Britain reporting carriage sounds, footsteps, cold spots, and poltergeist-like activity. Underwood and Tabori note the irony that the Chale Rectory letter appeared in the same *Church Times* issue as Mrs Smith's denial: the parallel phenomena might have suggested to Smith that Borley's disturbances had normal explanations — though the authors pointedly questioned whether rats could account for "creaks, clangings, grinding and jarring sounds."
