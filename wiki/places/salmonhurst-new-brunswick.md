# Salmonhurst, New Brunswick

Small farming community in New Brunswick, Canada; the Danish-speaking settlement where [[lionel-foyster|Rev. Lionel Foyster]] served as missionary from approximately 1910, and where he and [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Shaw]] married on 28 August 1922.

## The Settlement

Salmonhurst was a community of approximately 150 people, mostly Danish immigrants. The church, **St Ansgar's**, flew the Danish flag and held services in Danish, with men and women segregated in the two aisles as was Danish custom. The settlement had a school, shop, church, post office, and hotel. The rectory had beautiful hardwood floors throughout.

Lionel had made a little chapel in one of the rooms. While the house had no heating, it was comfortable by the standards of rural New Brunswick.

## Lionel Foyster's Ministry

Lionel arrived in September 1910, sailing to Bay du Vin in New Brunswick. He served several congregations across a large area in extreme weather conditions, with bouts of ill health leading to periods of absence. He wrote to the Shaw family in Ireland describing "blizzards coming up out of nowhere" and "summer wild raspberries, wild strawberries, and fishing for trout and salmon."

He characterised his Canadian parishioners honestly: many were illiterate, some areas unable to appoint magistrates for want of educated inhabitants. The fishermen and lumbermen had, he found, "little interest in God."

## Marianne's Arrival and Marriage

When [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] accepted Lionel's marriage proposal in 1922, she arrived at Salmonhurst in August after a five-day voyage from Liverpool to Quebec. She was immediately charmed by the local Danes, who were "happy, hospitable, hardworking, and full of life." She judged the rectory manageable after the hardships of her Irish upbringing.

They married at St Ansgar's on 28 August 1922 in a ceremony conducted by **[[dr-allan-smithers|Dr Allan Smithers]]** — who then caused a scene at the reception by objecting that Lionel, as Marianne's childhood baptiser, could not also be her husband.
