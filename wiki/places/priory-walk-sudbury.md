# Priory Walk, Sudbury

The address recorded on Kate Boreham's death certificate as the place where she died on Easter Day, 1888. Ivan Banks's investigation establishes that this address is highly problematic, and uses the discrepancies to argue that Kate may in fact have died at Borley Rectory rather than in Sudbury.

## The Death Certificate

Kate Boreham's death certificate states she died in **Priory Walk, Sudbury**, from "acute cerebritis." The attending physician is recorded as **W. Nigel Mason**.

## Banks's Investigation

Banks traced the address through the Suffolk County Records Office at Bury St. Edmunds and the electoral register for 1888. His findings:

1. **The Borehams did not own or rent the house.** Walter Boreham, Kate's husband, was a farm labourer from Acton, Suffolk — his wages would not have permitted home ownership in Sudbury. The 1881 census shows no Boreham in Priory Walk; the 1888 electoral register shows no Boreham registered there either.

2. **The registered owner of the Priory Walk house was Jessie Mitchell** — a name with no established connection to either the Boreham family or the Bulls of Borley.

3. **A William Henry Bull** owned a property in Market Hill, Sudbury, according to the same electoral register. Banks notes the coincidence of the "Henry" middle name and the Bull surname, suggesting — though without proof — that this person may have been connected to the Borley Bulls, who owned substantial property in the Sudbury area.

## The Burial Problem

Kate Boreham is recorded as **buried at Borley**, not at a Sudbury church. Banks finds this anomalous: if she died in Sudbury, why was she transported back to Borley for burial? A parish levy was charged for each parish through which a body passed — an expensive and logistically unusual arrangement for a farm labourer's wife. Richard Lee-van den Daele, who visited Borley, reported that he could not find Kate's grave in Borley churchyard.

## Banks's Theory

Banks proposes two possible explanations:

1. Kate died at Borley Rectory and was moved to Priory Walk after death (where an inexperienced or compliant doctor issued a death certificate with a false location and false cause of death)
2. Alternatively, the Borley burial register entry is also false — Kate's body was never in Borley churchyard at all

In either case, the Priory Walk address functions as a **cover address** in Banks's theory, used to conceal that Kate's death occurred in the Bull household.

> **Contested:** None of Banks's theories about the death certificate can be proven from available records. Professor Bernard Knight's forensic analysis established that cerebritis as a cause of death is "meaningless as such and was probably speculative," but this does not confirm the alternative narrative.
