# The Whin, Snape

A large detached house in the village of **Snape**, Suffolk, a few miles from Aldeburgh. The Fisher–Foyster household moved here from [[hill-farm-house-chillesford|Chillesford]] on **22 May 1940** and remained until **June 1941**, when they moved to [[dairy-cottage-rendlesham|Dairy Cottage, Rendlesham]].

## Character

Wood describes Snape in the pre-festival era as "as remote as Chillesford." The village predates Benjamin Britten's annual music festival by decades; in 1940 it was a quiet Suffolk backwater. The sources note that the extreme isolation of Snape during the 1940s raised suspicions that its occupants were actively trying to hide [2]. The landlady confirmed to [[trevor-hall]] in the 1950s that it had seemed odd that "Mrs Foyster appeared to have no friends, and that no one called at the house."

## Significant Anomaly: the Reversion to "Foyster"

The most notable feature of the Snape period is that [[marianne-foyster]] reverted to the title **Mrs Foyster** rather than Mrs Fisher. At other residences in Ipswich and Rendlesham, the household had consisted of Marianne and Henry Fisher living as "Mr. and Mrs. Fisher," with the elderly [[lionel-foyster]] introduced to neighbours as merely "old Mr. Foyster," a retired clergyman [3]. At The Whin, however, Henry Fisher was nowhere in evidence, and Marianne temporarily dropped her bigamous identity and lived openly with Lionel as Mr. and Mrs. Foyster [2].

Wood: "The most likely explanation of Marianne's reversion to her true married name at Snape is, therefore, that there had been trouble with Fisher, whose doubts could no longer be contained."

> **Contradiction:** A significant discrepancy exists regarding why Henry Fisher vanished during the Snape period. Fisher's sister claimed his absence was purely medical, stating he was confined to a hospital in nearby Saxmundham suffering from duodenal trouble [2]. Researchers reject this as an incomplete explanation: a hospital stay for Fisher would not necessitate Marianne's sudden reversion to the title of "Mrs. Foyster" [2]. Author Robert Wood theorises that Fisher's "illness" may actually have been a mental breakdown, caused either by a falling out with Marianne or by him finally discovering the "true horror of his situation" regarding her legal marriage to Lionel [2].

[[harry-price]]'s first Borley book, *The Most Haunted House in England*, was published in August 1940 — several months after the move to Snape. Wood notes it is possible that Fisher read it and realised the truth about his "father-in-law." The timing cannot be confirmed as the direct cause of Fisher's absence, but Wood finds it suggestive.

## Absence of Paranormal Phenomena

As with all of Marianne's other residences following her departure from Borley, neighbours at Snape never hinted that there was anything psychically strange about her [4]. This poses a challenge to the theory that she was a genuine [[poltergeist-activity|poltergeist]]-focus, and is one of the details researchers point to when arguing that the Foyster-period phenomena were not inherent to Marianne herself.
