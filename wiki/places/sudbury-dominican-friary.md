# Sudbury Dominican Friary (St. Saviour's)

A Dominican friary founded in Sudbury, Suffolk, approximately one mile from Borley across the River Stour. Part of its remains survive, built into premises now occupied by a Masonic lodge near the present-day Sudbury Cricket Ground.

## Foundation and History

- **Founded:** c. 1248, believed by **Baldwin of Shipling**
- **Order:** Dominican (Order of Preachers)
- **Dedication:** St. Saviour
- **Official title:** Sudbury Friary
- **Known for:** Theological studies
- **Suppressed:** 1538

## Borley Relevance

Ivan Banks examines Sudbury Dominican Friary in his investigation of the monastery controversy (Chapter 17). Unlike [[st-bartholomews-priory|St. Bartholomew's Priory]] (which is Benedictine and has documented Borley land rights), Sudbury Dominican Friary is of less direct relevance to the Borley haunting tradition, for two reasons:

1. It is Dominican, not Benedictine — the Waldegraves and the ghost-nun tradition are associated with Benedictine orders
2. Banks finds no documented evidence of Dominican land rights in Borley

The friary is nevertheless relevant because:
- It lies on the same side of the Stour as Sudbury, in sight of the Borley knoll
- Its monks would have been known in the area during the same period (1248–1538) as [[st-bartholomews-priory|St. Bartholomew's Priory]]
- During the Black Death epidemic of 1348–49, its monks would have been at risk, and may have contributed to whatever burial traditions attached to the Borley site

## The Plague

Banks notes that the Dominican Friary, like all monastic communities, was devastated by the Black Death. He uses this to suggest that the supposed plague pit on the Rectory site could have received bodies from either or both monastic houses:

> "It is virtually inconceivable that either Sudbury itself or Sudbury Priory could have escaped the plague unscathed."

> **Note:** Banks uses "Sudbury Priory" loosely in this passage to refer to the Dominican Friary; elsewhere he distinguishes it from the Benedictine [[st-bartholomews-priory|St. Bartholomew's Priory]].
