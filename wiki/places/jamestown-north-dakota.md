# Jamestown, North Dakota

A prairie town in North Dakota, approximately 90 miles west of Fargo on the edge of the Red River country. [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] worked here as a reporter for the **Jamestown Sun** newspaper, and it is the town where [[robert-swanson|A. Robert Swanson]], the American private investigator hired by [[eileen-garrett|Eileen Garrett]]'s Parapsychological Foundation, found her in **February 1958**.

## Marianne at Jamestown

Marianne moved to Jamestown after the collapse of the [[hokah-minnesota|Hokah]] household. She obtained a job at the Jamestown Sun, initially without O'Neil. He later joined her and worked briefly as a plumber. Wood: "Unfortunately, his career as a plumber was blighted by his general dislike of work and his heavy drinking. Marianne ended up supporting not only the child, but also her hopeless husband, on a meagre salary of $45 per week."

In Jamestown Marianne was well-liked and apparently competent at her work. Wood spoke to several people who knew her: "there was no hint of scandal. No one remembered the drunken husband at all." She entertained the local ladies' club with dramatic accounts of London Blitz experiences — in fact she had spent the war in rural Suffolk.

## The Swanson Interviews

In **February 1958** Swanson arrived at Marianne's door. The interviews were conducted at the **Gladstone Hotel, Jamestown**, and tape-recorded. It is from these transcripts — extensively quoted throughout Wood's book — that much of the direct evidence about Marianne's activities in England comes.

> "At first, she refused to discuss anything with Swanson, but she later relented and, at the Gladstone Hotel, Jamestown, gave the interviews (tape-recorded by Swanson) which have been extensively quoted throughout this book."

The discovery that [[ian-greenwood|Ian Shaw]] had warned her about Swanson in advance did not prevent her being caught off-guard. Her early responses were evasive but she gradually gave ground when Swanson produced documentary evidence she could not deny.

## After Jamestown

The disruption caused by Swanson's investigation unsettled Marianne. She moved to [[fargo-north-dakota|Fargo]] in autumn 1958, then on to [[la-crosse-wisconsin|La Crosse]] in 1963.
