# Borley Green

The westernmost section of Borley parish, accessible by proceeding through the village from the church and rectory site. It consists of farmhouses, thatched cottages, and some more modern houses and bungalows. From Borley Green the road descends in two steep hills toward the lane leading to Sudbury in one direction and various small villages in the other.

## Who Lived There

Several key figures in the Borley story were associated with Borley Green:

- **[[alfred-finch|Alfred]] and [[margaret-finch|Margaret Finch]]** — their semi-detached cottage at Borley Green was home for most of their lives. One of the adjoining cottages was occupied during the early 1930s by [[marion-pitt|Miss Marion Pitt]], the village schoolmistress, who became friendly with [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]].
- **[[herbert-mayes|Herbert Mayes]]** — chauffeur-gardener to the [[rev-ac-henning|Rev Henning]] during the Henning incumbency; lived at Borley Green and cycled to and from work at Liston Rectory each day. On one evening while cycling up Hall Lane from Borley Green, he heard the sound of horses trotting toward him (the [[phantom-coach|phantom coach]] phenomenon), pulled his bicycle aside, and found nothing visible passing close by him.

## The Telephone Box

During the fire of 27 February 1939, [[captain-gregson|Capt Gregson]] sent his sons to the nearest telephone to call the fire brigade. According to [[alan-gregson|Alan Gregson's]] 1985 letters, this was at Borley Green — indicating that the only public telephone available was at the far end of the parish from the rectory.
