# Chilton Lodge

A solid Georgian building that stood in Cornard Road, Great Cornard, [[sudbury|Sudbury]], Suffolk, near to the Chilton Brick Works. Purchased in 1920 by the [[bull-family]].

## Bull Family Home

When the [[bull-family|Bull sisters]] left [[borley-rectory]] in 1920 — the year [[harry-bull]] married [[ivy-brackenbury]] and moved into the Rectory with his wife — Chilton Lodge became the family's new base. [[ethel-bull]] (who died 29 April 1961 aged 93), Constance Bull, and [[walter-bull|Alfred Bull]] (who died 17 March 1956) were the principal occupants in its later years.

## Peter Underwood's Visits

[[peter-underwood]] visited Chilton Lodge on approximately nine or ten occasions, sometimes alone and sometimes accompanied — on different occasions by Tom Brown, [[peter-hilton-rowe|Dr Peter Hilton-Rowe]], [[john-dening|the Revd John C. Dening]] and other friends. He always found the house "full of heavy furniture and bric-a-brac, portraits and pictures, typically Victorian," with the surviving Bulls appearing to have done their best to make the place as much like their former much-loved home at Borley as possible.

At the time of his first visit in 1951, the occupants were Constance, Ethel and Alfred Bull.

## Significance

Chilton Lodge was the principal location where researchers could interview the surviving Bull sisters — the only direct witnesses to the pre-Price period of the haunting, who had lived at [[borley-rectory]] from birth. Visits there by [[sidney-glanville]], [[lord-charles-hope]], Underwood, [[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth]] and others provided important first-hand testimonies about the Bull period.

## Demolition

In the late 1960s Chilton Lodge was demolished and new properties were constructed by a local builder. Like Borley Rectory itself, no sign of the historic building remains today, although its name survives in two modern estate roads — **Chilton Lodge Road** and **Chilton Court**.
