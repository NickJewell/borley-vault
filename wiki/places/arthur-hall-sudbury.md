# Arthur Hall, Sudbury

Home of [[lady-whitehouse|Sir George and Lady Florence Whitehouse]] and their visiting nephew Edwin, later to become a Benedictine monk (see [[dom-richard-whitehouse]]). The property stands just outside the centre of Sudbury on the Long Melford road. Built in the late Georgian period, it was originally called Holgate House and renamed Arthur Hall in the early nineteenth century.

## Borley Connection

The Whitehouses were friends of [[lionel-foyster|Lionel]] and [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] during the 1930–35 incumbency. When the phenomena became too much for the Foysters in the summer of 1931, Edwin Whitehouse persuaded Lady Florence Whitehouse (Sir George had died in March 1931) to offer the Foysters the sanctuary of Arthur Hall away from the Rectory. Marianne stayed for most of July, while Lionel only returned to the Rectory to carry out his parish duties. For most of June and July 1931 the Rectory was unoccupied at night.

## Later History

The Whitehouse family eventually left Arthur Hall at an unknown date. By the late 1970s the building was in poor condition after lying empty. In 1980 it was purchased by two Italian brothers, renamed Sudbury Hall, and converted to a restaurant with much subsequent alteration. The façade of the building remains largely unchanged, but much of the interior has been heavily renovated.

On the early hours of 25 January 2008, the building was badly damaged by fire — "scenes reminiscent of the destruction of Borley Rectory in February 1939." According to the *Suffolk Free Press*, at the height of the blaze 100 firemen fought to bring the fire under control.
