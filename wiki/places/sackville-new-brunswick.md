# Sackville, New Brunswick

Small Canadian town on the Isthmus of Chignecto; the Foysters' final posting in Canada before coming to England, where [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]]'s deteriorating health eventually forced him to seek treatment at St Luke's Hospital in London.

## The Town

Sackville had a population of just over 2,000 and was more civilised and social than [[salmonhurst-new-brunswick|Salmonhurst]]. It was defined by **Mount Allison University**, the first university in the British Empire from which a woman had graduated. The church was a Gothic Revival design in local New Brunswick timber, on the edge of the Sackville marshes, approached by wooden sidewalks. As at Salmonhurst, Lionel made over a room in the Rectory as a chapel for private prayer.

## The Foysters at Sackville

Lionel resigned from Salmonhurst in January 1927 following his mild heart attack during a swimming holiday at Prince Edward Island, and was inducted into the parish of **St Paul's, Sackville** a month later. [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] initially found the town "quiet, quaint and 'prim in an old world way'" and missed the freedoms of Salmonhurst. Lionel's liberal High Church ministry did not endear him to the tight-knit parish.

While at Sackville, Marianne:
- Enrolled at **Mount Allison Ladies College** and took university courses
- Was diagnosed with polycystic ovaries and had surgery at **Amherst Hospital** across the Nova Scotia border
- First heard the story of the **[[the-great-amherst-mystery|Great Amherst Mystery]]** (Esther Cox) while convalescing there
- Took on care of the infant **[[adelaide-foyster|Barbara Tower]]** when her parents died

## The Wall Street Crash and Departure

Lionel's investments were badly hit by the October 1929 crash. When he went to London for treatment at St Luke's Hospital for Clergymen, leaving Marianne in Sackville, his finances deteriorated further. By 1930, having received the invitation from the Bulls to take the Borley living, the Foysters decided to leave Canada.
