# Grosvenor Hotel, Victoria

A hotel in the Victoria district of London where [[dr-davies|Dr Davies]] resided after abandoning his Midlands medical practice following the death of his wife [[mabel-davies|Mabel]] from cancer. It was the setting for Marianne Foyster's six-month exploitation of him in 1941–42.

## Events Here

Dr Davies moved to the Grosvenor to pursue spiritualism, living on his capital. After reading [[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s *The Most Haunted House in England* and concluding that [[marianne-foyster|Marianne Foyster]] was a medium of outstanding power, he obtained her address from Price and met her in London.

The hotel lounge was where the most extraordinary scenes played out: Marianne fell into apparent trances and "embraced" Davies, whom she allowed to believe he was being held by the spirit of the dead Mabel. An official of the Society for Psychical Research (then at Tavistock Square) saw Davies and Marianne together and recalled they appeared on "very friendly terms." Davies confirmed she occasionally stayed overnight at the hotel.

The relationship was conducted during visits Marianne made by train from **Wickham Market** (nearest station to [[dairy-cottage-rendlesham|Dairy Cottage, Rendlesham]]), with Davies paying her fare.

## Significance

The Grosvenor Hotel is the physical setting for Wood's account of the [[dr-davies-episode|Dr Davies episode]] — a study in the wartime vulnerability of the bereaved and in Marianne's capacity for calculated cruelty toward isolated men. The fake seances here parallel the Borley wall-writings in their exploitation of religious or supernatural credulity; where at Borley [[dom-richard-whitehouse|Edwin Whitehouse]]'s religious intensity was targeted, at the Grosvenor it was Davies's grief for his wife.

### **Key Events**
*   **Dr. Davies's Seclusion and Spiritual Quest:** While residing at the Grosvenor Hotel in deep grief, Dr. Davies immersed himself in spiritualism with the desperate hope of contacting his deceased wife [2]. 
*   **The Meeting of Davies and Marianne (Autumn 1941):** During his time at the hotel, Davies read Harry Price’s newly published book, *The Most Haunted House in England*. Because the book highlighted Marianne Foyster's involvement in the spectacular Borley "phenomena" and described her as "psychic," Davies mistakenly concluded that she was "the most powerful medium who had ever lived" [2]. He asked Harry Price for her address, wrote to her, and arranged a meeting in London. The sources note that he subsequently "fell into the trap" of a disastrous relationship with her, becoming the only male victim to later speak at length about his experiences with her deceit [1], [2].
