# Pentlow

Essex village three miles northwest of Borley. Derives its name possibly from the old name for the River Stour (the "Paente") with "Low" meaning hill.

## Connection to the Bulls

Pentlow is the **birthplace of [[henry-bull|Henry Dawson Ellis Bull]]** in 1833, the son of Edward Bull. Edward Bull erected the 95-foot **Bull Tower** or Folly in the grounds of his Rectory as a memorial to his parents, John and Margaret. John Bull was Rector of Pentlow from 1756 to 1802.

As they did at Borley, the Bull family demolished the old Pentlow Rectory (an early seventeenth-century structure) and replaced it in 1886 with a large red-brick house not unlike Henry Bull's building at Borley. It is possible that the Pentlow Rectory, Pentlow Folly, and [[borley-rectory]] were all built by "Webb of Sudbury" and designed by architect Frederick Chancellor.

Henry Bull's brother Felix became Rector of Pentlow in 1887. When Felix died in 1927 — the same year his uncle [[harry-bull]] passed away in the [[blue-room]] at Borley Rectory — the Bull family connection with both Pentlow and Borley came to an end.

## P. Shaw Jeffrey

[[p-shaw-jeffrey|P. Shaw Jeffrey]], an Oxford friend of Harry Bull who stayed at Borley during the 1880s and later testified to seeing the [[the-nun|phantom nun]], described one poltergeist incident involving a French dictionary at Pentlow Rectory — the only documented case of phenomena extending outside Borley to the family's other property.

## Significance

Pentlow establishes the Bulls as a family with deep roots in north Essex church life across multiple generations. The choice of the same architect and builder for both Pentlow and Borley suggests that Henry Bull's building of [[borley-rectory]] was a family project consistent with an established pattern of church-building, not an unusual or eccentric enterprise.
