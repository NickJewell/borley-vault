# Gestingthorpe

A village in north Essex, a few miles from Borley, associated with the Borley story in two ways: as the last living of [[rev-lanfranc-mathias|the Rev Lanfranc Mathias]] after he left Borley and Liston in 1967, and as the family home of [[violet-oates|Violet Oates]], sister of Captain Lawrence Oates of Antarctic fame.

## The Oates Family Connection

The Hall at Gestingthorpe was the home of the Oates family. Captain Lawrence Edward Grace Oates (1880–1912), who died on Scott's ill-fated Antarctic expedition with the famous words "I am just going outside and may be some time," was born and raised here. His sister [[violet-oates|Violet Oates]] sold the large Hall after the death of her mother and moved to a more modest property at Liston, where she became a parishioner and friend of the Mathias family.

After Violet's death, [[rev-lanfranc-mathias|Lanfranc Mathias]] moved to Gestingthorpe to take up his final living — in the very village from which his friend and parishioner Violet had come.

## The Last Rector's Final Living

After 12½ years at Borley and Liston (1955–1967), Rev Lanfranc Mathias took the living at Gestingthorpe. This was his last incumbency. [[claudine-mathias|CM]] does not specify when he left or died, noting only that he was always in delicate health following his major surgery in 1955.

## Ley Lines

One of the four ley lines through Borley identified by Babbs and Mathias passes through Gestingthorpe:
- SW–NE line: Great Saling → Church End → Sible Hedingham → **Gestingthorpe** → Borley → Rodbridge → Thorpe Morieux

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

Based on the provided Borley sources, here are the specific details recorded about the village of Gestingthorpe:

### **Physical Description and Location**
*   **Location:** Gestingthorpe is described as a village located in north Essex, situated near to Borley [1, 2]. 
*   **Geography and Ley Lines:** The village sits on a proposed "ley line" running south-west to north-east through Great Saling, Church End, Sible Hedingham, Gestingthorpe, Borley, Rodbridge, and Thorpe Morieux [3].
*   **Notable Buildings:** The sources specifically mention "Gestingthorpe Rectory" [4] and a local estate known as "Dynes Hall" [5]. 

### **Associated Persons**
*   **The Rev. Henry "Harry" Foyster Bull:** The second of the Bull rectors of Borley. He was the eldest child of the Rev. Henry D. E. Bull and the only one of their 14 children not born at Borley; he was born in Gestingthorpe [4].
*   **Caroline Sarah Foyster (Mrs. Bull):** Mother of Harry Bull. Before her marriage to the Rev. Henry D. E. Bull, she lived and worked in Gestingthorpe [4, 5].
*   **The Rev. Edward Lanfranc Morgan Mathias:** The last rector to minister to the combined parishes of Borley and Liston on their own. He resigned from the Borley living in 1967 to take up a new post in Gestingthorpe [1, 2].

### **Key Events**
*   **Birth of Harry Bull (1862/1863):** Caroline Foyster gave birth to Harry Bull in Gestingthorpe on January 24 in either 1862 or 1863 [5-7]. 
*   **Relocation of Rev. Mathias (1967):** After twelve and a half years at Borley and Liston, the Rev. Mathias transferred his ministry to Gestingthorpe [2]. 

### **Contradictions and Source Discrepancies**
*   **Harry Bull's True Birthplace:** There was some historical confusion over exactly where Harry Bull was born. He was "usually recorded" as having been born at Borley Place, but the 1871 and 1881 Borley census returns explicitly prove his birthplace was Gestingthorpe [8]. 
*   **Where Caroline Foyster Lived/Worked:** The sources offer conflicting accounts regarding Caroline Foyster's residence in Gestingthorpe at the time of her son's birth. One account states she was "employed for a time as a resident helper at Gestingthorpe Rectory" and gave birth there [4]. Another account claims she was "in service at Dynes Hall" [5]. 
*   **Harry Bull's Birth Date and Wedlock Status:** There is a discrepancy regarding the year Harry Bull was born at Gestingthorpe, which raises questions about his legitimacy. Some records state he was born in 1863 [6, 7], but others list his birthdate as 24 January 1862 [5, 6]. Because his parents were not married until November 1862 [5], an 1862 birth at Gestingthorpe indicates he was born out of wedlock [6].
