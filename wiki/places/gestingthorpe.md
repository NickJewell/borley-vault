# Gestingthorpe

A village in north Essex, a few miles from Borley, associated with the Borley story in three ways: as the birthplace of [[harry-bull|Harry Bull]], as the family home of [[violet-oates|Violet Oates]] (sister of the Antarctic explorer), and as the last living of [[rev-lanfranc-mathias|the Rev Lanfranc Mathias]] after he left Borley and Liston in 1967. Notable buildings include Gestingthorpe Rectory and the local estate known as Dynes Hall.

## Harry Bull's Birthplace

The Borley sources record that Harry Bull was born in Gestingthorpe — though his birthplace was long mistakenly recorded elsewhere.

> **Contradiction:** Harry Bull was "usually recorded" as having been born at Borley Place, but the 1871 and 1881 Borley census returns explicitly prove his birthplace was Gestingthorpe, where his mother, **Caroline Sarah Foyster** (later Mrs Bull), was present at the time.

> **Contradiction:** Sources offer conflicting accounts of where Caroline Foyster was living in Gestingthorpe. One account states she was "employed for a time as a resident helper at Gestingthorpe Rectory"; another claims she was "in service at Dynes Hall."

> **Contradiction:** There is also a discrepancy in Harry Bull's birth year, with records citing either **1862** or **1863**. Because his parents were not married until November 1862, an 1862 birth date at Gestingthorpe would indicate he was born out of wedlock.

## The Oates Family Connection

The Hall at Gestingthorpe was the home of the Oates family. Captain **Lawrence Edward Grace Oates** (1880–1912) — who died on Scott's ill-fated Antarctic expedition with the famous words "I am just going outside and may be some time" — was born and raised here. His sister [[violet-oates|Violet Oates]] sold the large Hall after the death of her mother and moved to a more modest property at Liston, where she became a parishioner and friend of the Mathias family.

## The Last Rector's Final Living

After 12½ years at Borley and Liston (1955–1967), [[rev-lanfranc-mathias|Rev Lanfranc Mathias]] took the living at Gestingthorpe — in the very village from which his friend and parishioner Violet had come. This was his last incumbency. [[claudine-mathias|CM]] does not specify when he left or died, noting only that he was always in delicate health following his major surgery in 1955.

## Ley Lines

One of the four ley lines through Borley identified by Babbs and Mathias passes through Gestingthorpe:

- SW–NE line: Great Saling → Church End → Sible Hedingham → **Gestingthorpe** → Borley → Rodbridge → Thorpe Morieux
