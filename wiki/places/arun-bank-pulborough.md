# Arun Bank, Pulborough

[[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s home in **Pulborough, West Sussex**, where he lived from approximately 1910 until his death on 29 March 1948; the house from which he conducted all his major psychical research investigations, including the Borley project.

## The House

Arun Bank was a **large, double-fronted Edwardian house** that Price had commissioned on a plot of land adjacent to his mother-in-law Mrs Knight's house, "Riverside," in Pulborough. It was situated on the banks of the River Arun, with the long rear garden sloping down to the river and offering views across the South Downs. The house was set back about ten yards from the road, accessible via a shrub-lined stone path leading to a front porch.

Price had both a **workshop** and a **study** at Arun Bank, the interior furnished with solid Victorian pieces. The famous oil portrait of him as a young man by **John Dumayne** hung in the drawing room. He bred goldfish in two ponds he had made in the garden, and hung the **bronze bell from the Rectory courtyard** — presented to him by [[captain-gregson|Captain Gregson]] — above the studio in the garden, suspended over a set of green doors marked "Workshop." The Borley Bell was noted to be larger and heavier than visitors expected.

Price regarded Arun Bank as his "anchorage" and primary operational base for forty years. Although he commuted to his [[national-laboratory|National Laboratory of Psychical Research]] in London, he typically returned to Arun Bank by late afternoon, and generally resented any evenings spent away. He would travel up by train from Pulborough to meet [[lucie-kaye|Lucie Kaye]] at Victoria on Wednesdays and Fridays.

As of 2003 the exterior of Arun Bank remained largely unchanged from Price's day, though the interior had been divided into two separate flats.

## Deaths

Price died **in his armchair at Arun Bank on Easter Monday, 29 March 1948**, his freshly lit pipe still smoking in his hand, while [[constance-price|Constance Price]] had gone to make tea. The obituaries described him as dying of a heart attack, like his father before him, at the age of sixty-seven.

[[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] visited **Constance Price** at Arun Bank — along with [[ghost-club|Ghost Club]] member [[steuart-kiernander|Steuart Kiernander]] — and found Price's hats, coats and walking sticks still hanging in the hallway as if he had "just stepped out."

> **Contradiction:** Underwood's own records give conflicting dates for this visit. In the introduction to *Borley Postscript* and in later accounts, he states the visit took place in the autumn of **1967**. However, in a detailed diary-style entry in the same sources, he explicitly records it as **"Sunday 20th October 1968"**.

During the visit, Constance revealed her bitter feelings toward the [[spr|Society for Psychical Research]] authors — [[eric-dingwall|Eric Dingwall]], [[kathleen-goldney|Kathleen Goldney]], and [[trevor-hall|Trevor Hall]] — who had published the highly critical *Borley Report*. Constance admitted she knew nothing about Borley itself, but called Mrs Goldney and Dr Dingwall "two-faced," noting they had often visited Arun Bank and she had "looked on them as friends" before their posthumous attacks on her husband's integrity. She resented that investigators had been "snooping about" asking questions about what kind of man Harry Price had been.
