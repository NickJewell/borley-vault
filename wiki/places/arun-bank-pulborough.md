# Arun Bank, Pulborough

[[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s home in **Pulborough, West Sussex**, where he lived from approximately 1910 until his death on 29 March 1948; the house from which he conducted all his major psychical research investigations, including the Borley project.

## The House

Arun Bank was a **four-bedroom detached Edwardian house** that Price had commissioned on a plot of land adjacent to his mother-in-law Mrs Knight's house, "Riverside," in Pulborough. It was situated on the banks of the River Arun, with views across the South Downs.

Price had both a **workshop** and a **study** at Arun Bank, and the famous oil portrait of him as a young man by **John Dumayne** hung proudly in the drawing room. He bred goldfish in two ponds he had made in the garden, and hung the **bronze bell from the Rectory courtyard** — presented to him by [[captain-gregson|Captain Gregson]] — above his studio in the garden.

He liked the Pulborough routine and generally only changed it for late sittings in London, resenting any evenings spent away from Arun Bank. He would travel up by train from Pulborough to meet [[lucie-kaye|Lucie Kaye]] at Victoria on Wednesdays and Fridays.

## Deaths

Price died **in his armchair at Arun Bank on Easter Monday, 29 March 1948**, his freshly lit pipe still smoking in his hand, while [[constance-price|Constance Price]] had gone to make tea. The obituaries described him as dying of a heart attack, like his father before him, at the age of sixty-seven.

[[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] visited **Constance Price** at Arun Bank in the autumn of 1967 — nineteen years after Price's death — and found Price's hats, coats and walking sticks still hanging in the hallway as if he had "just stepped out."
