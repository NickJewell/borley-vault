# Arun Bank, Pulborough

[[harry-price|Harry Price]]'s home in **Pulborough, West Sussex**, where he lived from approximately 1910 until his death on 29 March 1948; the house from which he conducted all his major psychical research investigations, including the Borley project.

## The House

Arun Bank was a **four-bedroom detached Edwardian house** that Price had commissioned on a plot of land adjacent to his mother-in-law Mrs Knight's house, "Riverside," in Pulborough. It was situated on the banks of the River Arun, with views across the South Downs.

Price had both a **workshop** and a **study** at Arun Bank, and the famous oil portrait of him as a young man by **John Dumayne** hung proudly in the drawing room. He bred goldfish in two ponds he had made in the garden, and hung the **bronze bell from the Rectory courtyard** — presented to him by [[captain-gregson|Captain Gregson]] — above his studio in the garden.

He liked the Pulborough routine and generally only changed it for late sittings in London, resenting any evenings spent away from Arun Bank. He would travel up by train from Pulborough to meet [[lucie-kaye|Lucie Kaye]] at Victoria on Wednesdays and Fridays.

## Deaths

Price died **in his armchair at Arun Bank on Easter Monday, 29 March 1948**, his freshly lit pipe still smoking in his hand, while [[constance-price|Constance Price]] had gone to make tea. The obituaries described him as dying of a heart attack, like his father before him, at the age of sixty-seven.

[[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] visited **Constance Price** at Arun Bank in the autumn of 1967 — nineteen years after Price's death — and found Price's hats, coats and walking sticks still hanging in the hallway as if he had "just stepped out."

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**Arun Bank** was the long-time residence and operational base of psychical researcher Harry Price, located in Pulborough, West Sussex. Based on the Borley sources, the following concrete details outline its physical description, associated persons, key events, and related discrepancies.

**Physical Description**
*   **Location and Architecture:** Arun Bank was situated on the banks of the River Arun in Pulborough, West Sussex [1]. It was a large, double-fronted Edwardian home commissioned by Harry Price, built on a plot of land adjacent to his mother-in-law's property [2, 3]. 
*   **Exterior:** The house was set back about ten yards from the road, accessible via a shrub-lined stone path leading to a front porch [2, 4]. The long rear garden sloped down to the river and offered glorious views of the Sussex Downs [2].
*   **Interior and Layout:** The interior was furnished with solid Victorian pieces and included a study and a workshop for Price [3]. At the rear of the house, hanging over a set of green doors marked "Workshop," was the "Borley Bell" (the Great Bell salvaged from Borley Rectory), which was noted to be larger and heavier than visitors expected [4, 5]. 
*   **Modern Status:** The exterior of Arun Bank remained largely unchanged from Price's day as of 2003, though the interior had been divided into two separate flats [2].

**Associated Persons**
*   **Harry Price:** The famous ghost hunter used Arun Bank as his "anchorage" and primary operational base for forty years [6]. Although he commuted to his National Laboratory of Psychical Research in London, he typically returned to Arun Bank by late afternoon [6]. 
*   **Constance Mary Price:** Harry Price's wife (and later widow), who continued to live at Arun Bank after his death [1, 4]. 
*   **Peter Underwood and Steuart Kiernander:** Psychical researchers who visited Arun Bank in the late 1960s to interview Constance Price and view the Borley relics [4, 7].

**Key Events**
*   **Death of Harry Price (March 29, 1948):** Harry Price died suddenly of a fatal heart attack at Arun Bank on Easter Monday in 1948 [8, 9]. 
*   **Underwood and Kiernander Visit (1967/1968):** Peter Underwood and Ghost Club member Steuart Kiernander visited the widowed Constance Price at Arun Bank. During this visit, Underwood photographed the salvaged Borley Bell above the workshop and gathered Constance's personal reflections on her husband's former colleagues and critics [4, 5]. 

**Contradictions and Source Perspectives**
*   **Discrepancy Regarding the Date of the Visit:** There is a direct contradiction in Peter Underwood's own records regarding the exact date he visited Arun Bank. In the introduction to *Borley Postscript* and in later accounts, Underwood states he visited the house in the autumn of **1967** [1, 5]. However, in a detailed diary-style entry in the same sources, he explicitly records the visit as taking place at 11:00 a.m. on **"Sunday 20th October 1968"** [4, 7]. 
*   **Perspective on the SPR Investigators:** During the visit, Constance Price revealed her bitter feelings regarding the Society for Psychical Research (SPR) authors—Eric Dingwall, Kathleen Goldney, and Trevor Hall—who had published the highly critical *Borley Report*. Constance admitted she knew nothing about Borley itself, but she explicitly called Mrs. Goldney and Dr. Dingwall "two-faced." She noted that they had often visited Arun Bank and she had "looked on them as friends" before they launched their posthumous attacks on her husband's integrity, and she resented that investigators had been "snooping about" asking questions about what kind of man Harry Price had been [10].
