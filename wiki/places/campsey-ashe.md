# Campsey Ashe

Village two miles south of Wickham Market in Suffolk. The [[lionel-foyster|Revd Lionel Foyster]] was laid to rest here after passing away at Dairy Cottages, Rendlesham, on 18 April 1945.

## Lionel Foyster's Grave

The burial plot is at St John the Baptist Church and is marked with a plain stone bearing the inscription:

> Lionel Algernon Foyster, Priest 1878–1945. The Lord is my Shepherd.

## Post-War Investigation

In the 1990s, ghost writer Wesley H. Downes and Paul Moss-Kemp (see [[east-anglian-group|East Anglian Group for Psychic Research Studies]]) went to St John the Baptist Church on the anniversary of Foyster's death and held a vigil in the churchyard. According to their account (given to [[tony-broughall]]), around midnight they saw mist or smoke rise from Foyster's grave, move along horizontally, then rise vertically and drift across the churchyard, where it passed through the door of the closed and locked church. When Broughall asked what they did: "We ran for our lives!"

## Significance

Campsey Ashe is the final resting place of one of the most important figures in the Borley story — the rector who lived through the "fifteen months" and documented the most intense phase of the haunting. The village is remote, consistent with the isolated outposts that defined Foyster's ministry from Borley through to his final years at Rendlesham. His grave is the most accessible physical memorial to the Borley incumbency.
