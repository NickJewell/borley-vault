## Summary

[[lady-whitehouse]]'s signed personal account of two visits to Borley Rectory during the Foyster period, providing independent corroboration of the Foyster diary entries. The Whitehouses of Arthur Hall, Sudbury, were long-time friends of the Bull and Foyster families.

## Key Content

**First visit (May 4, 1931)**: Lady Whitehouse and Sir George accompanied by nephew [[dom-richard-whitehouse]]. Found cupboard contents strewn everywhere. As they stood in the kitchen, bells began ringing — "We are in for a bad time tonight. It generally starts like that" (Marianne). Offered to stay after dinner to "see them through." During the visit: discovered the skirting board on fire in an unused room, poured water on it; as they watched to confirm it was out, a flint "the size of a hen's egg fell between us: the window was shut and there were no holes in the ceiling." Two more flints fell while descending stairs, one hitting Foyster. In the kitchen, Lady Whitehouse burned lavender over embers to fumigate the house: shower of small stones fell around them. Finally: insisted the Foysters leave and took the whole party to Arthur Hall at 11:30 p.m.

**Second visit (summer 1931)**: Her nephew summoned her urgently. At the front door she heard things falling. Two small children crouched at dining-room door saying "We don't like so many things falling." Lady Whitehouse went up to Mrs. Foyster, who was in bed ill. As she left the room, Mrs. Foyster called "Your things are going!" — her gloves and parasol had moved to the dressing-table. As Lady Whitehouse returned from the kitchen with tea, a small glass bottle "seemed to start from the middle of the room and fell at her feet." On the next trip she heard a yell — Marianne had been thrown out of bed for the third time that day. Lady Whitehouse took the family to Arthur Hall again.

## Significance

Lady Whitehouse is an independent, upper-class witness of good reputation, with no stake in the investigation. Her account corroborates both the Foyster diary entries and Dom Richard's testimony on specific dates. The flying flint with windows shut and no ceiling holes is one of the more controlled instances of apport-style phenomena.
