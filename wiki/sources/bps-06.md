# Borley Postscript Ch.6: My First Visit to Borley in 1947

**Author:** [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]]
**Form:** Original investigation report, sent at the time to Harry Price (which led to Price inviting Underwood to join the Ghost Club, membership number 496)
**Date:** Saturday–Sunday, 17–18 May 1947
**Companions:** P.I.T. Brown (Ghost Club Life Member)
**Slug:** `bps-06`

## Summary

Underwood's first overnight stay at [[borley-rectory|Borley]], three weeks after the Turners took occupancy. This is his original contemporary report, written before the Borley case became fully established in published literature.

## Site Description (May 1947)

- Foundations intact but nothing above ground level; courtyard area plainly visible
- Kitchen passage still traceable; well under covered passage still in use (motor-driven, hand-wheel still present)
- South-east end: cellars three-quarters full of rubble; rose-tree shoots re-sprouting along old house wall
- [[nuns-walk|Nun's Walk]] largely traceable; fancy shaped bricks strewn either side; south-side hedge still standing
- The large summer-house had disappeared; Turners planned to rebuild it in the same position
- South-east entrance gate posts still standing; land to west (cats' cemetery) had been ploughed
- Bell-wire and bell-pulls lying in rubble; cow's skull found under a hedge

## Key Observations and Incidents

### Peter Eton's Raps
Two days before Underwood's visit, BBC producers Peter Eton and Alan Burgess heard "short sharp raps" from opposite sides of the cellar ruins. Eton asked them to stop and recommence; they obeyed. This was later incorporated into the BBC broadcast.

### Boundary Stone Location
Using Price's plan and scale, Underwood and Brown identified the probable location of the lost boundary stone and advised Turner to dig within a three-foot radius of a specific spot.

### Planchette Prediction
Turner reported a recent planchette prediction that Borley Church would burn down by September 1947. Underwood notes in brackets: "This prediction did not come true."

### Liston Church Visit
Underwood and Brown visited [[liston-church|Liston Church]] and located the approximate burial spot of the human remains (interred by Rev. Henning in 1945 with religious medallions and a cream jug). Nothing marks the interment.

### The Medium's "Control"
Two men arrived at the Rectory site — one a medium who claimed his "control" had said they would be "amply rewarded" if they stayed the night. They did stay the night in June 1947; no phenomena were reported.

### Faint Footsteps
At 3:15 a.m. and again at 3:40 a.m., Underwood and Brown both heard "faint footsteps from the direction of the bend in the Nun's Walk." The sounds continued for several minutes. Both had the entire area under observation from two different vantage points; Fred (the Turners' cat) was confirmed elsewhere. Torch flash revealed nothing; sounds continued.

> "It was quite an experience to watch a place that is clearly visible and hear distinct, if faint, footfalls at the place you are looking at and without anything visible being responsible."

## Significance

This is the earliest surviving Underwood account of Borley and demonstrates his methodical investigative approach (controls, watch-schedules, two-viewpoint observation) before he became the doyen of British ghost investigators. Its contribution is primarily as a site survey at a pivotal transitional moment — three weeks into the Turners' occupancy, ruins still settling, BBC just days away.
