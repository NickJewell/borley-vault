# The Haunting of Borley Rectory — Chapter 3: Sixteen Hours of Thrills!

**Source:** Sean O'Connor, *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (c. 2022)
**Slug:** `hbrs-ch03`
**Time period:** 12–13 June 1929

## Summary

Price and Lucie's first evening investigation, 12 June 1929. Key details:

### Physical Survey of Rectory

Price and Lucie map the house: L-shaped originally, rectangular after expansion; uncovered brick courtyard; heavy wooden shutters in all main rooms (O'Connor notes these are unusual for a remote hamlet); bars on maids' windows to prevent them slipping out at night; five entrances; trap door to cellar; two back staircases.

Garden: 3.7 acres; fine elms and copper beeches; ancient cedar of Lebanon; large octagonal summer house facing the Nun's Walk; stream dividing the garden; cats' cemetery (headstones for "Jem," "Rollo," "Sandy"); Gothic timber summer house at the east end.

### First Night's Phenomena

- Wall sighted something on the Nun's Walk but could not identify it before it disappeared.
- Pane of glass shattered from the veranda roof (hitting Price and Wall). O'Connor notes that Price admitted he threw a stone at the glass — a "conjuring trick" to entertain the journalist.
- A red-and-white vase flew down the stairs; mothballs were thrown at Wall.
- O'Connor: "He was, after all, an expert conjuror. All of this made a good feature for the *Daily Mirror*."

### The Blue Room Séance

Bull sisters Ethel and Mabel attended. Knockings from behind the mirror on the dressing table; Lucie communicated with an entity. The "spirit" of Harry Bull identified himself, claimed to have been murdered by his wife, discussed his misinterpreted will. Eric Smith ended the séance immediately (a journalist was present).

Price fled in the night, claiming one of his vicious headaches; insisted he would never undress in "that room." Lucie stayed on as company for Mabel.

### The Coach Party Invasion

The next evening, coachloads of locals arrived — drunk — with mouth organs, beer bottles, accordions. Hundreds trampled the garden, threw empty bottles at the house, broke the drawing room windows. Lucie drove to Sudbury (wrong county — she needed Braintree) to get police. By the time she returned an inspector had already arrived and dispersed the crowd.

O'Connor concludes: by inviting the press, the Smiths had "unwittingly awoken an entirely different variety of spirit: a poltergeist."
