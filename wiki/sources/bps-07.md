# Borley Postscript Ch.7: The Faker of Borley

**Author:** [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] (review) + Stewart P. Evans (interview)
**Form:** Full Ghost Club Society Journal review of Louis Mayerling's book + transcript of personal interview with Mayerling
**Slug:** `bps-07`

## Background

In 2000, a volume appeared called *We Faked the Ghosts of Borley Rectory* by "Louis Mayerling," claiming the entire Borley case was "an ingeniously orchestrated hoax." The author also claimed to have known Marilyn Monroe, George Bernard Shaw, T.E. Lawrence, and the Prince of Wales.

## Underwood's Eight Specific Objections

1. The 1900 sighting: Mayerling attributes it to home-made wine. But this involved four sisters (three simultaneously, then a fourth fetching from the house) in good daylight — not a private inebriated experience.
2. References to "newspapermen eager for stories" in 1919, when Mayerling would have been six — sourced from "schoolboy diaries" that are "no longer available."
3. His trick of dangling a nail on cotton to tap kitchen windows — but the kitchen windows were several rooms away from where he claims to have been.
4. [[mabel-smith|Mabel Smith]] depicted as gullible; every contemporary account and Underwood's personal experience contradicts this.
5. Repeated references to "hidey-holes" and "secret rooms" that [[harry-price|Price]] explicitly states he missed in a minute examination "from rafters to cellars."
6. The chronology of the Smith period does not add up: Smith left July 1929; his final sermon was April 1930 — not, as Mayerling claims, days apart.
7. Bulls described as suppressing knowledge of the haunting — completely contrary to what Underwood, his wife, Dr Hilton-Rowe, Rev. Dening and others separately experienced.
8. Mayerling claims his letters from Marianne were "meticulously written" — Underwood's own letters from Marianne were "invariably interesting but could hardly be described as 'meticulously written.'"

## The Birth Certificate Evidence

Alan Roper (Ghost Club Society investigator) unearthed the birth certificate of **George Carter**, born 4 September 1913 at 63 Mayes Road, Wood Green, father James Carter (kitchen porter), mother Amy Carter (formerly Cook). A 1953 marriage certificate shows "Louis Mayerling (name changed by Deed Poll)."

## Vincent O'Neil's Verdict

[[vincent-oneil|Vincent O'Neil]] (Marianne's adopted son): "I no longer believe that Mayerling knew my mother, and if he met her at all, it was only for a brief period of time. As with the other Borley-related memories from his book, the time spent with my mother happened only in his imagination."

## BBC Admission

On BBC Television, December 2000 (*The Ghosts of Christmas Past*), Mayerling admitted: "I have exaggerated here and there."

## Stewart Evans Interview (17 March 2001)

Key exchange: Evans asked how the 1940 Price book that Mayerling credits as his first inspiration could have informed his 1919 (age 6) activities at Borley. Mayerling replied: "Well it got me interested in the first place, yeah." Evans: "He is condemned out of his own mouth."

Mayerling also claimed "I started The Ghost Club with Eric Dingwall in 1938" and "I worked with Frank Whittle on the jets" and went "to Singapore on a one-man submarine to help blow up the harbour."

## Conclusion

Alan Wesencraft (Harry Price Library, 42 years' experience): over those 42 years he "almost certainly met and conversed with every person who has done any serious investigation of the Borley mystery" and was positive there was no mention of Mayerling or George Carter. Underwood: "Borley researcher Edward Babbs said, 'Far from Borley Rectory being Mayerling's second home, one has to consider if he ever went there.'" Colin Wilson (*Daily Mail*): the book is "intended either as fiction or as a hoax."
