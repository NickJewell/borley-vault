# The Haunting of Borley Rectory — Chapter 7: Confessions of a Ghost Hunter

**Source:** Sean O'Connor, *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (c. 2022)
**Slug:** `hbrs-ch07`
**Time period:** 17 January 1881 – 29 September 1930

## Summary

The most comprehensive biography of [[harry-price]] in the wiki. Key points:

### Price's Background

- Born 17 January 1881 to Edward Ditching Price (grocer/paper salesman from Shropshire) and Emma Meech (daughter of a newspaper reporter). Grandfather a journalist, father a salesman — storytelling and selling was in his blood.
- Grew up in Deptford/New Cross, south-east London.
- Left school at 15; studied photography, chemistry and mechanical engineering at Goldsmiths College evenings.
- Self-taught, deeply insecure about lack of university education; craved recognition and listed life goals in writing (including "To appear in *Who's Who*" and "To possess a Rolls-Royce car").
- Father had a 1900 portrait painted of Price by his friend John Dumayne — "almost a dandy... a statement of the man that Price intended to be."
- The fake Roman silver ingot: Price announced himself as a Sussex archaeologist before Professor E. J. Haverfield exposed his 1910 "find" as a copy of a Tower of London piece.

### Marriage and Personal Life

- Married Constance Knight (childhood sweetheart) at St Mary's, Pulborough. Their marriage was long but sexually estranged: Price "never slept with Constance again" after the wedding and had numerous affairs. No children.
- "Arun Bank" — their home in Pulborough, overlooking the River Arun and the South Downs.

### Lucie Kaye's Background (Continued)

- Met Price at the Laboratory opening Jan 1926; hired immediately.
- In 1927, became pregnant from an affair; went to Zurich to give birth to a son (Ian Kallenbach, later anglicised to Ian). Adopted out to an Oxford family. Price wrote a supportive adoption reference.
- Mother's family cut her off. She "was not a loving person" by her own admission.
- Named Price as her adoption reference; he wrote supportively.
- Her son Ian bore no resemblance to Price; DNA tests confirmed he was not Price's child.
- From 1929 she changed the spelling of her name from Lucy Kay to **Lucie Kaye**, mirroring what Price had done with Stella Cranshaw's name.

### The National Laboratory for Psychical Research

- Opened 26 January 1926 at Queensbury Place, South Kensington (London Spiritualist Alliance offices); top-floor laboratory, séance room, workshop, dark room, library.
- Price's dual life: commercial traveller Mon/Tue/Thu/Fri, psychical researcher Wed/Fri in London.
- Relations with the SPR were deeply fraught throughout.

### The Rudi Schneider Affair

- Price and Lord Charles Hope ran two acclaimed series of sittings with Austrian medium Rudi Schneider (1929–30).
- In April 1932, Price secretly photographed Schneider appearing to cheat — published this in the *Sunday Dispatch* without warning Lord Charles.
- All major Laboratory council members resigned in protest.
- This scandal effectively ended the National Laboratory and put Price's career in jeopardy.

### Arthur Conan Doyle's Death (July 1930)

By the time Ethel Bull visited Price's now-near-empty Laboratory on 29 September 1930, Conan Doyle had recently died; Price's former antagonist was gone. The Foysters had just arrived at Borley.
