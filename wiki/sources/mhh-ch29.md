## Summary

The destruction of Borley Rectory by fire at midnight on February 27, 1939 — fulfilling the Sunex Amures Planchette prediction — and the anomalous events reported during and immediately after the blaze.

## Key Content

**The fire**: Midnight, February 27, 1939. Gregson was sorting books in the main hall by oil lamp when the lamp overturned and the room "speedily caught fire." He noted the books were "stacked quite carefully and in ordinary commonsense should have remained in their stack without falling over at all." He rushed to the cottage to rouse his sons. The Sudbury Fire Brigade arrived at c.12:15 a.m. to find the roof collapsed immediately over the hall, the upper rooms well alight, and the staircase burning. After an hour and a quarter of pumping from a pond 50 yards away, the fire was mastered. The Rectory was "practically ruined."

**Gregson's second dog**: The second cocker spaniel (purchased after the first disappeared in December 1938) behaved identically to the first when taken through the courtyard — "whining and shrieking — and bolted. It has not been seen since."

**Strange occurrences before the fire**: Gregson's toad in the cellar — he tied it tightly inside a knotted cloth; a minute later, cloth still knotted, the toad was gone. Strange formless footprints in the snow, going diagonally across the lawn in one direction only, disappearing at the wall by the Nun's Walk.

**During the fire — apparitions**:
- Two people interviewed by Price saw "figures moving amongst the flames, near the Blue Room window"
- A police constable asked Gregson who were "the lady and gentleman who had just preceded me in crossing the courtyard." Gregson confirmed no woman was on the premises. Gregson later described the figures as "cloaked"; two figures at upper windows — one appearing to be a girl, the other "formless" — seen by several villagers.

## Significance

The fire fulfilled the Sunex Amures prediction exactly: it started at midnight (close to the "9 o'clock" timing), originated over the hall (exactly as predicted), and destroyed the Rectory. The apparitions during the fire extend the haunting into the moment of the building's destruction. See [[fire-1939]], [[captain-gregson]], [[mhh-ch27]], [[the-nun]].
