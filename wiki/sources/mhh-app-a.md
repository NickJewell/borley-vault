## Summary

The formal eleven-point Declaration Form signed by all official observers before being admitted to Borley Rectory during Price's 1937–38 tenancy.

## Key Conditions

1. Fulfil all verbal and written instructions
2. Pay own expenses
3. Not connected with the Press
4. Not to name or locate the house to others
5. Not to publish or lecture on experiences
6. Not to photograph without written permission
7. Not to bring unauthorized persons
8. Fulfil agreed observational periods
9. Submit a report after each period
10. Use the telephone only to report phenomena or request assistance
11. Lock all doors and deposit keys on leaving

*Note*: A telephone was originally intended to be installed in the house; it never was.

## Significance

The Declaration Form is the legal/ethical framework that gave Price confidence in the confidentiality and integrity of his observers. Its existence as a written, signed document also helps establish that the observers were formal participants in a structured inquiry, not casual visitors. See [[the-observer-program]], [[mhh-ch17]].
