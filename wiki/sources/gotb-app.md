# Ghosts of Borley — Appendix: The People Who Lived at Borley

**File:** `raw/ghosts of borley/appendix---the-people-who-lived-at-borley.md`

## Summary

Biographical summaries for key figures, providing details not fully covered in the main text. Essential for filling in life histories and cross-referencing identities.

## Key Entries

**Bull Family:** [[henry-bull]] (1833–1892) — confirmed as born at Wadham College, Oxford; fourteen children; died in the Blue Room. His daughters [[ethel-bull]] (died 1961) and Constance Bull (died 1963, leaving £50,000) were the last survivors.

**[[harry-bull]] (1863–1927):** Married Ivy Brackenbury on 12 September 1911 against family opposition; she had a daughter Constance (who married Colonel Boothby and died in South Africa in 1970). Harry died 9 June 1927 of chronic bronchitis in the Blue Room.

**[[marianne-foyster]] (born Emily Rebecca Shaw, 26 January 1899):** The appendix reveals the full complexity of her marriages:
- 1st: Harold Greenwood (1914, age misrepresented as 17; possibly bigamous given earlier ceremony in 1914)
- 2nd: [[lionel-foyster]] (1922, Canada; described herself as spinster)
- 3rd: Henry Francis Fisher (23 February 1935, while Foyster was still alive — bigamous)
- 4th: Robert Vincent O'Neil (11 August 1945, four months after Foyster's death; gave age as 32, actually 46 — bigamous if Fisher not divorced)
O'Neil died saving a child in a road accident. Marianne moved to America. Living in "a small American town" in 1972.

**[[lionel-foyster]] (1878–1945):** Rector of Hardwick NB 1910–18; missionary 1918–27; Rector of Sackville NS 1927–29; accepted Borley living 1930. Died 26 April 1945 at Dairy Cottages, Rendlesham. Completely crippled by arthritis in his last years.

**François d'Arles (real name Frank Charles Pearless, 1894–1966):** Boarded his small son with the Foysters after answering an ad; spent time at the cottage; ran a flower shop (Jonquille et Cie, 20a Worple Road, Wimbledon) with Marianne 1932–34. Died at St Ann's Hospital, Tottenham, 10 October 1966.

**[[rev-eric-smith]]:** A Eurasian who completed education at University of Calcutta; worked in Indian Civil Service; ordained 1926. Borley was his first living (offered to 12 clergymen before him who all refused). He always maintained Borley "had an evil atmosphere and was haunted." Died 3 August 1940. His widow's memories were "confused and unreliable."

**[[rev-ac-henning]] (Rector 1936–1955):** Ordained 1911; accepted Borley living 1936. Buried the human remains in Liston churchyard. Wrote *Haunted Borley* (1949). Died suddenly 14 January 1955. His wife died some years after him.

**[[captain-gregson]]:** Purchased the Rectory in December 1938 for £500; insured for £10,000. The fire occurred while he was sorting books in the hall. Insurance claim rejected; settled out of court for £750.

**Sir Edward Waldegrave (1517?–1561):** A Waldegrave entry is significant: a descendant of the Waldegraves may have murdered the Borley nun. Sir Edward died in the Tower as a Catholic martyr under Elizabeth I; his daughter Catherine is suggested as a possible identity for the ghost-nun.

**Marie Lairre:** Entry describes her as "a young French nun from a convent at Bures who was murdered by a member of the Waldegrave family in 1667." Price believed he had established her existence. No independent historical verification cited.
