# Sidelights — 20: No Hand Was Visible

**Source:** *Sidelights on Borley Rectory*, Essay 20
**Author:** Andrew Clarke

## Summary

A comprehensive analysis of the Borley wall-writings: their timeline, authorship, and the graphological evidence that they were written by [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]]. Includes handwriting comparisons (GIF images in original web version).

## Timeline

- **Pre-March 1931:** Scribbles on paper ("m's and u's, loops and letters") — probably by **Adelaide** or **Douglas Pearless** (the lodger's son, a bright child the same age as Adelaide).
- **Spring 1931:** [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]]'s name appears on walls — coinciding with [[dom-richard-whitehouse|Edwin Whitehouse]]'s encouragement that the scribblings were spirit communication. [[lady-whitehouse]] suggested Lionel write "What do you want?" underneath.
- **May–June 1931:** Developed wall messages: "Marianne please help get," "Marianne get help [well tank bother me]," "Get Light Mass and Prayers here." The "here" was added later — and the word "Marianne" was washed off but the "Get light..." message survived on painted woodwork.
- **After Marks Tey visit (January 1932):** No new wall-writings reported.

## Authorship

Multiple independent graphologist analyses:
- Lewis T. Ackerman (professional graphologist, for Price's unpublished third book): *"all the Borley scripts with the exception of 'Edwin' were executed by the same personality."*
- Peter Underwood (submitted samples to a graphologist): *"except for one word, they all seem to have originated from Mrs. Foyster."*
- Ivan Banks: confirmed same conclusion.

The website includes GIF comparisons of the wall-writing "Marianne" against samples of Marianne's later signatures (1960s). Clarke notes the characteristic "g" form in "get" matches letters from her later correspondence.

## Glanville's Compromise of the Evidence

When Sidney Glanville photographed the wall-writings, his camera produced poor results. **He traced over the faded writings with a pencil** at Price's encouragement, imposing his own interpretation and permanently destroying their evidential value. The "well tank bottom me" reading (vs. the original "bother me") may be partly an artefact of Glanville's tracing.

## Edwin's Role

Edwin was shown the wall-writings on his **first visit** — meaning he could not have initiated them. They had appeared weeks before his arrival. Marianne later suspected Edwin of responsibility, but this is incompatible with the timeline. The writings coincided with the start of Marianne's affair with Frank Pearless, and ceased around the time Edwin's visits became unwelcome.

## The Adelaide Sightings

Lord Charles Hope and Major Douglas-Home noted "Adelaide" written in similar handwriting on the kitchen wall **at the same height as the Marianne messages** — suppressed by Price. The housekeeper (Miss Dytor/Wildgoose) confirmed Adelaide *"was a terror for scribbling — she used to write on every wall — I spent hours washing them out."*

## Marianne's Probable Motivation

Clarke: the writings were a response to the attention and admiration Marianne craved, escalating from Adelaide's innocent scribbles through Marianne's first attempts to write her own name, stimulated by Lady Whitehouse's and Edwin's encouragement that this was spirit communication.
