# Sidelights — 2: The Thump Ghosts

**Source:** *Sidelights on Borley Rectory*, Essay 2
**Author:** Andrew Clarke

## Summary

A detailed reconstruction of the Smith incumbency (1928–29) and [[harry-price|Price]]'s first visit in June 1929, arguing that virtually all "objective" poltergeist phenomena coincided exclusively with Price's presence and were manufactured by him.

## Key Evidence

**The Smiths' Own View:**
- [[rev-eric-smith]] and [[mabel-smith]] never believed the house was haunted.
- Mabel Smith, in her own words: "neither my husband or myself believed the house haunted by anything else but rats and local superstition."
- The Smiths had merely asked the Daily Mirror for the address of the SPR; the paper engineered Price's arrival.

**Evidence of Price's Trickery:**
- Mrs C. Baines interviewed Fred Tatum (widower of [[mary-pearson]], the Smiths' maid): Mary saw Price throw a condiment at his first lunch; later, Price manipulated a storm lantern in the scullery.
- A dinner guest saw water in her glass turn to ink — an old conjuring trick — immediately after Price was nearby: "he wasn't a man they would like to offend."
- Mary Pearson told Mrs Smith she had "distinctly seen Price throw a coin."
- The Smiths noted: *"When Mr Price arrived down to investigate, immediately we were astonished at an onset of 'phenomena'—bangs, clattering, keys thrown etc. We could not help being led to suppose that he himself was producing some of the effects."*
- After Price left, "there were no further objective occurrences."

**The Bull Family Feud:**
- The Bull sisters' motivation was primarily an inheritance dispute, not belief in the paranormal.
- Harry Bull left his estate to his wife Ivy (not his sisters), who expected it.
- The sisters spread stories that Harry had been murdered by Ivy to alter his will.
- The séance on June 12 was orchestrated by the Bull sisters to publicise their version of Harry's will — which horrified the Smiths and caused them to halt it.

**The Sutton Incident (July 1929):**
- Charles Sutton (Daily Mail) kept Price in view, saw him throw a stone from the top of the stairs, grappled with him, and found his pockets full of pebbles.
- Lord Charles Hope was present and left "with the definite suspicion that Mr Price might be responsible for some at least of the phenomena."

## New Source

Fred Tatum interview (via Mrs C. Baines): Mary Pearson's observations corroborate that Price performed conjuring tricks during his first visit.

## Contradiction

> **Contradiction:** Price's own account in *The Most Haunted House in England* presents the Smith-period phenomena as genuine. Mabel Smith's later testimony flatly contradicts this, and is corroborated by Mary Pearson's observations and the Sutton incident.
