# Source: *Ghosts That Will Not Die* — Ch. 3: The Borley Legend 1930–1935

**Author:** Vincent O'Neil

The most detailed published account of the Foyster incumbency, compiled directly from Lionel Foyster's three unpublished manuscripts: the *Diary of Occurrences* (DO), *Fifteen Months in a Haunted House* (FM), and the *Summary of Experiences* (SE). O'Neil provides an unprecedented day-by-day chronology in Foyster's own words, with parenthetical notes cross-referencing all three manuscripts.

## The Three Foyster Manuscripts

**Diary of Occurrences (DO):** Probably written March–July 1931. 31 typewritten pages. Sent to Price October 3, 1931. Written primarily "to send round to members of my family."

**Fifteen Months in a Haunted House (FM):** Probably completed after May 1934. Much longer, with pseudonyms. Intended for publication. Contains the pseudonym "Fijis" for the poltergeist entities.

**Summary of Experiences (SE):** Written January 24 – February 11, 1938, for inclusion in Price's *The Most Haunted House in England* at Price's request.

> **Important note:** Hall concluded Foyster "did not keep a diary of the strange events that occurred from the very first day." The events were written from memory, sometimes years later. This finding is described in MHH in misleading terms by Price.

## Key Events from the Manuscripts (Foyster's Own Words)

**October 1930:** Adelaide (age 2 yrs 7 months) said "There's daddy" to empty space repeatedly. Marianne began seeing Harry Bull in "a peculiar (plum) coloured dressing gown" — always between the library and their bedroom.

**February 26, 1931:** "M.F. is given a terrific blow in the eye — a cut under it, black eye next day — by an invisible assailant on the landing just outside bedroom; she is carrying a candle." Bells rang repeatedly.

**February 27:** Cotton reel and hammer-head with broken handle thrown across bed after lights were out.

**March 7:** Lionel attempts exorcism using holy water from Rev. Sellwood; stone hits him on shoulder in response. Books thrown; pictures taken down from walls and laid on floor.

**March 9:** Rev. Sellwood visits, inspects attics (satisfied no one hiding), hears bell ring, sees large stone almost as it came to rest from the back stairs. Marianne says she saw an unseen object "being carried after me. I could not see the entity that was carrying it, but I could see it coming along."

**March 11:** Two Anglican priests go thoroughly over house with incense, holy water, and prayers. A regular peal of bells rang during the service.

**March 28:** Marianne encounters "The Horror" / "The Shadow" — referred to in FM as the principal haunting entity, distinct from the Nun and thought able to "move material objects."

**October 1931:** Price visits with his council. Bell rang from Adelaide's (3½-year-old's) room with all doors shut. Council next day declared opinion that Marianne was responsible; she was put under surveillance — but a bell then rang from Adelaide's room while Marianne was being watched. Foysters and Price part "on bad terms." **Marianne's version**: she invited him to leave, furious that Adelaide was suspected.

**October–November 1931:** Adelaide, sent to lie down in the "room over kitchen," comes downstairs with a bruise under her eye. When asked how she got it: "A nasty thing by curtain in my room gave it to me."

**January 23–24, 1932:** Marks Tey spiritualist circle holds seance; "great demonstrations." After their all-night sitting, "the house [felt] entirely different." Manifestations "definitely stop (with two exceptions noted below) till 1935."

**1935:** Some indications of trouble starting again. August bank holiday: 13–14 bangs heard from drawing room.

**October 1935:** Foysters move out.

## Lionel's Own Rationalisations

- Believed Marianne was protected from serious injury by her Scapular — "never seriously hurt when wearing Scapula"
- Thought the entities drew psychic power from "living people in their vicinity"
- Attempted multiple forms of exorcism
