# Ghosts of Borley — Chapter 6: The Haunting of Borley Church

**File:** `raw/ghosts of borley/chapter-6---the-haunting-of-borley-church.md`

## Summary

Documents reported paranormal phenomena at and around Borley church itself — distinct from the Rectory. The chapter establishes the church as an independent locus of activity with its own recurring phenomena.

## The Church

A twelfth-century building (dedication unknown; possibly St Andrew). Flint rubble construction. Contains two significant Waldegrave monuments: Sir Edward Waldegrave (d. 1561) and his wife Frances (d. 1599); and a tablet for their daughter Magdala (d. 1598). The Borley registers date from 1652.

## Types of Phenomena Reported

- **Footsteps** — the most frequently reported phenomenon; heard on the path, in the porch, inside the nave, and around the churchyard. Reported by dozens of independent witnesses across three decades.
- **Organ music** — heard by multiple independent witnesses when the church was locked and empty. Mrs Walrond and Rev Henning heard it clearly on the path (October 1947); Mrs A.G. Wilson and her sister-in-law heard it simultaneously (summer 1949). Note: one organ music incident was eventually explained by two local boys playing a prank — evidence that investigators were alert to normal causes.
- **Inexplicable smells** — incense reported by several visitors; one remarkable account (Miss Dudley, February 1949) where three of four visitors smelled an overwhelming, unclassifiable odour in a precise "bank" about three yards long, in a brisk wind, while the fourth smelled nothing.
- **Strange lights** — seen at windows; in the churchyard; at upper-story windows.
- **Objects moved** — the sanctuary lamp, a book placed over it (found on the floor after the locked church was left overnight), a bottle moved, a cap moved — small incidents but with good witnesses.
- **Apparitions** — the nun-like figure seen by Rev. Kipling (August 1949; "frail girl, about 18–23, nun's hood with thick veiling"); Terry Bacon (twice in the churchyard; figure floating ~2 feet above ground); Croom-Hollingsworth (June 1970; 12-minute sighting; grey-clothed figure passing through the fence).
- **Paranormal bell-ringing** — the church bell rang on Low Sunday 1949 when the Rector was at Liston; both Pearsons heard it; the church was locked.

## The Waldegrave Connection

Recurring reference to the Waldegraves. One theory: a Waldegrave daughter is the ghost-nun. Another: a Waldegrave descendant murdered a novice at Borley, triggering the haunting. The family were staunch Catholics who suffered under Elizabeth I; Sir Edward Waldegrave died in the Tower (1561).

## Key Accounts

- **Mr John May (October 1947):** Watched from the church porch midnight to 2:30 am; heard gate click, footsteps, key in lock, door open/close, then atonal organ chords for several minutes.
- **Mrs Walrond (October 1947):** She and Henning heard organ music on the path; rushed in and found the church empty. Her letter to Henning is a primary source.
- **Mr Kiernander (September 1949):** Heard heavy footsteps twice approach the porch in bright moonlight; rushed out both times to find the path completely empty.
