# Source: *Ghosts That Will Not Die* — Ch. 2: The Borley Legend 1066–1930

**Author:** Vincent O'Neil

A detailed chronological listing of all events, sightings, and historical data from 1086 through October 1930, drawing on all available published and unpublished research without attempting to verify authenticity.

## Key Events (selected highlights)

**1086** — Domesday Book lists Borley Manor.

**April 28, 1236** — Peter de Cacheporc installed as first rector on record.

**1362** — Benedictine Monks build monastery on site; Edward III gives them Borley manor.

**13?? (medieval)** — Monk and nun elopement; monk hanged, nun bricked up. Alternate version: nun fell in love with a coachman; both coachmen beheaded, girl walled up.

**May 17, 1667** — Marie Lairre strangled and buried at the Rectory site after leaving her convent at Le Havre to marry Charles Waldegrave.

**1730** — Henrietta Waldegrave, British spy, reportedly murders her daughter Arabella (a Stuart spy). Both are nuns.

**1807–1819** — Small rectory built by Rev. Will. or Rev. John P. Herringham. "William had experiences never documented."

**1836** — Legend of the nun traced back at least this far by Henning.

**1841** — Fire may have destroyed the earlier rectory (Downes). Ivan Banks disputes: the old Herringham rectory was "demolished to make way for Henry Bull's pile."

**May 7, 1892** — Henry dies in the Blue Room **from syphilis**. Succeeded by son Harry.

**July 28, 1900** — Three Bull daughters see figure on Nuns Walk. They recruit a fourth sister; the nun disappears when approached.

**November 1900** — Ethel Bull and a cook see the nun again.

**1916–1919** — Edward Cooper sees coach and horses; Mr. and Mrs. Cooper hear dog walking and see nun "many times." They see "a black shape" in their bedroom.

**1920** — Harry Price becomes lifetime member of SPR.

**1927–28** — Twelve clergy refuse the Borley position.

**October 2, 1928** — Rev. Guy Eric Smith and wife move to Borley; they are not told it is "haunted."

**June 10, 1929** — C.V. Wall's *Daily Mirror* article: first published report of paranormal activity. Wall sees mysterious light in window.

**June 12** — Price arrives at Rectory first time, with Lucie Kaye and Wall. Stone and object throwing; "apports"; Wall sees nun. Early morning Blue Room séance: Harry Bull appears.

**July 12–14** — Smiths move out.

**July 25** — Charles Sutton visits with Price; claims Price threw stones at him.

**April 1930** — Smiths leave Borley altogether. For 17 months Price is "supposedly not informed of any manifestations." Price apparently in area collecting information and interviewing Fred Cartwright.

**October 16, 1930** — Rev. Lionel Foyster, wife Marianne, and adopted daughter Adelaide move in.
