# The Most Haunted House in England — Ch. 1: Luncheon Interlude

*Harry Price (1940)*

Price's account of how he was recruited to the Borley case. First-person, present-tense narration of events beginning Tuesday, 11 June 1929.

## The Call

Price is at lunch in South Kensington, discussing poltergeist phenomena with a friend (poltergeists are mischievous but "never appear to harm anyone — they are merely playful and troublesome"), when a newspaper editor telephones. One of the editor's staff — [[vc-wall|V. C. Wall]] — is already at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] investigating "remarkable incidents." Price is asked to take charge of the case.

His own words on replacing the receiver: *"I little dreamed that there was ten years' work ahead of me probing the mystery of what was to become the best-authenticated case of haunting in the annals of psychical research."*

He telegrams the incumbent [[rev-eric-smith|Rev. G. E. Smith]], receives permission and an invitation to arrive for lunch the following day.

## Significance

This chapter establishes the chain of initiation. Price was brought in by a newspaper editor, not self-initiated; the story was already running in print before he had any influence over it. The pre-Price press coverage by V. C. Wall (see [[mhh-ch02]]) is therefore independent corroboration.
