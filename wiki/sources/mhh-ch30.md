## Summary

Post-fire phenomena at the ruins of Borley Rectory — demonstrating that the haunting continued after the building was destroyed.

## Key Content

**Captain Gregson's BBC broadcast (April 15, 1939)**: Gregson spoke on "In Town To-night," describing his pre-fire experiences and noting further incidents after he wrote to Price in March.

**[[herbert-mayes]]'s phantom horses (March 16, 1939)**: Henning's gardener, cycling home past the ruins at night. Heard sounds of horses stampeding toward him. "As a safety measure, Mayes jumped off his bicycle and, with it, stood flat against the fence and hedge in order to allow the horses to pass him." The sounds came nearer and "finally passed him. But there were no horses!" The galloping hoofs could still be heard dying away in the distance for 3–4 minutes total. Comparable to [[harry-bull]]'s and [[walter-bull]]'s lane experiences.

**The Blue Room window apparition (March 26, 1939)**: Rosemary M. Williams and C. Graham Browne visited the ruins with a party on a moonlit night. Miss Williams, standing on the Nun's Walk and looking at the gable walls, saw:

> "A small woman in the upstairs room (the Blue Room). I had the impression of someone in a light buff-coloured coat, but as the figure approached the window I could see that it was a woman clothed in blue. She remained for several seconds and then turned towards the wall and, as it seemed, walked through it."

Browne saw the figure "for a flash" as it disappeared. Miss Williams's signed written report confirms the floor of the Blue Room had burned away — "no human person could have been at the window." Price himself attempted to reach the Blue Room aperture on his March 29 visit and found it physically impossible to stand there.

**The "Psychic Fête" (June 21, 1939)**: Rev. Henning organized a church fundraiser among the ruins — skittles, clock-golf, a "white elephant" stall, pig-pelting, and coco-nuts along the Nun's Walk. Price's last official visit. No phenomena were observed. Price notes: "I could not help wondering...whether my observers would ever again keep their nightly vigil among the Cedars of Lebanon."

## Significance

Post-fire phenomena (invisible horses, Blue Room apparition at an inaccessible window) confirm that the haunting was not a product of the physical building. The apparition at a window with no floor beneath is among the strongest post-fire sightings. See [[the-nun]], [[phantom-coach]], [[herbert-mayes]], [[fire-1939]], [[borley-rectory]].
