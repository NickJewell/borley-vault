# The Haunting of Borley Rectory — Chapter 16: "The Most Haunted House in England"

**Source:** Sean O'Connor, *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (c. 2022)
**Slug:** `hbrs-ch16`
**Time period:** 28 February 1939 – 29 March 1948

## Summary

The publication and reception of *The Most Haunted House in England*; wartime investigation; the excavation; Price's death.

### Post-Fire Phenomena

Gregson told Price: on the night of the fire, a police constable asked him who the cloaked lady and gentleman were who had just passed through the courtyard — there was no such couple. Apparitions seen through the Blue Room window. Herbert Mayes (Henning's chauffeur) heard phantom horses approaching in the dark lane post-fire but saw nothing.

### The Book (Published August 1940)

- Distinctive black-and-blue dust jacket; first line stated "The Most Haunted House in England" in quotation marks — Price's subtle sleight of hand implying scepticism while asserting it.
- O'Connor's analysis: Price manipulated evidence but created a book that "deftly articulated the darkest fears of the war years: death, loss, grief." It told the wartime reader that ghosts exist and that human life survived after death — "Like all ghost stories, it suggested the comforting possibility that 'We'll Meet Again.'"
- Reviewed enthusiastically; Longmans's warehouse was destroyed twice by bombing; went out of print; commanded high prices in the *Times* personals.

### Wartime Investigation

The Cambridge Commission (1941–44): Dr Andrew J. B. Robertson from Cambridge organised 58 undergraduates making 25 visits; documented footsteps, knocking, white shapes, strange lights. Polish Army Medical Corps officers (including Lieutenant Nawrocki) also investigated 1943, claiming to see shadows.

### The 1943 Excavation

Price excavated the Rectory cellar in August 1943 with the Hennings' aged gardener Jackson. Found left jawbone of a woman and left side of a skull beneath the cellar floor. Canon Phythian-Adams had theorised these were the bones of "Marie Lairre."

The pre-Reformation altar: hidden for 400 years under a stone slab in the church — unearthed by Price's team exploring the crypt.

A Catholic medallion with an image of the Virgin Mary was also found in the debris.

### Price's Death (29 March 1948, Easter Monday)

Price had been working on a projected third Borley book. Went to post a letter to Mollie Goldney; saw Sidney Glanville drive past and wave. Returned home; sat in his favourite armchair by the fireplace; died of a heart attack with his freshly lit pipe still smoking.
