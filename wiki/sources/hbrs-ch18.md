# The Haunting of Borley Rectory — Chapter 18: The Widow of Borley

**Source:** Sean O'Connor, *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (c. 2022)
**Slug:** `hbrs-ch18`
**Time period:** 23 January 1932 – 8 June 1954

## Summary

Trevor Hall's investigation; Edith Dytor; Henry Lawton; Marianne's subsequent marriages.

### Trevor Hall

Introduced to Dingwall after a 1949 Magic Circle lecture. Joined the Borley Report team. O'Connor: "a supreme egotist, inflated to the breaking point with self-importance." His obsession with Marianne was "prurient"; his researcher Herbert Pratt admitted "I am about twice as interested in Marianne as I am in Borley Rectory."

### Edith Dytor (1932)

A 21-year-old nurse who answered an advertisement in the *Nursing Mirror*: needed as nurse-companion from April to November 1932, following the birth of baby John. Found:
- Marianne very attractive, well educated, a Roman Catholic (slept with rosary under her pillow; fluent French).
- Lionel: much older, crippled with arthritis, swollen hands. Very tight with money (meals of rice and macaroni in water). Good and kind man; adored the children.
- D'Arles: "manipulative and sly, sometimes cruel, belittling Mrs Foyster." Edith thought D'Arles was behind most of the odd things that happened.
- D'Arles's blackmail: claimed Marianne was trying to poison him with Luminal. He would tell Edith to remove her pills.

### Baby John Emery (died 20 September 1932)

Adopted by the Foysters as a newborn in April 1932 (born 25 April 1932 to an unmarried Kent woman; born in a Southend-on-Sea midwife's home). Died at 4.5 months of convulsions and protein malnutrition. Buried in an unmarked grave in Borley churchyard.

### Adelaide's Mastoiditis Crisis (1933)

Adelaide fell dangerously ill with mastoiditis; taken to Great Ormond Street Hospital. Two operations; near-death. Marianne placed the St John Vianney relic in her bandages. Adelaide recovered; Lionel attributed it to divine intervention.

Evelyn Gordon (ran a children's playgroup at Borley Lodge) raised concerns about Adelaide with the NSPCC. Investigation concluded: conditions substandard but "no technical cruelty."

### Henry Lawton's Testimony (Confirmed)

Henry Lawton stayed at Borley as locum for August 1933. Slept undisturbed in the Blue Room; wife sat happily in the drawing room near the Nun's Walk at twilight. No phenomena experienced. William Bigg told him that Eric Smith had tried to depreciate the Rectory's value through the haunting story so a friend could buy it cheaply.

### Marianne's Subsequent Marriages

1. **Johnny Fisher** (married 23 February 1935): a vulnerable commercial traveller she met at Marks Tey station in autumn 1934. Marianne told him she was Lionel's daughter. Fisher converted to Catholicism. Bigamous (Lionel still living). In August 1935, while Fisher was away, Marianne secretly adopted another baby, **Timothy Eves** (renamed John Fisher) — presented to Fisher as their son. Later adopted **Mary Lex** (renamed Astrid Fisher). Fisher was told she was pregnant; he had doubts.

2. In 1935, Lionel collapsed during a sermon; left the living. The family moved to a bungalow in Ipswich, then to a farmhouse at Chillesford.

3. Fisher was eventually removed from Marianne's care by his sister Edith Shaw, who had discovered the bigamous marriage from Ethel Bull.
