## Summary

[[helen-glanville]]'s solo Planchette sessions at Streatham — conducted alone with no prior information about the Rectory — which produced the name "Mary Lairre," her French origin, her death by strangulation in 1667 at the hands of a Waldegrave, and a request for Mass. The founding document of the Mary Lairre theory.

## Key Content

**Background and conditions**: The Glanville family's Planchette had previously refused to work at home for years. At Borley it "became extremely active." [[helen-glanville]] had never used it before. On October 28, 1937, *alone at home in Streatham*, she was shown the earlier (Borley) scripts but given no detail about their content. She sat alone and asked questions.

**Session 1 (October 28, 1937 — alone):**
- "Are you buried in the garden?" → Yes
- "Can you tell me the rest of your name?" → "La..." then indistinct
- "Do you mean 'Lairre'?" → Yes
- "Can we help? Are you unhappy?" → Yes
- "Can you tell me, are you buried in the garden?" → Yes
- "Can you tell me when you passed over?" → *1667*
- "The month?" → *May*
- "The day?" → *17*
- "Did they hurt you?" → Yes
- "Was there something you wanted very much before you passed over?" → *Water*
- "Was the monk's order that of St. Benedict?" → Yes
- Is the monk buried at Borley? → Yes

**Session 2 (October 31, 1937 — with family and Kerr-Pearse):**
- "What is your name?" → *Mary Lairre*
- "How old were you when you passed over?" → *19*
- "Were you a novice?" → Yes
- "Were you murdered?" → Yes
- "How?" → *Stran...* (strangled confirmed on follow-up)
- "Whose fault?" → *Waldegrave*
- "Are you French?" → Yes
- "Where did you come from?" → *Havre*
- "What was the name of your nunnery?" → *Bure*
- "Did Waldegrave take you from Bures?" → Yes
- "Do you want burial as well as Mass?" → Yes
- "What kind of water?" → *Holy*

**What she wants**: Mass (Requiem, by a Roman Catholic priest), burial, holy water, incense.

## Significance

Helen Glanville had never been told anything about the Rectory. She was alone at home in Streatham for the first session. She produced: the name "Lairre," a French origin, a death date (May 17, 1667), a method (strangulation), a perpetrator (Waldegrave), a home (Havre), a nunnery at Bures. These details align remarkably with the table-tipping session's burial location and with the broader Borley nun legend — yet Helen had been given no information.
