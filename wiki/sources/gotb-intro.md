# Ghosts of Borley — Introduction

**Authors:** [[peter-underwood]] (PU) and [[paul-tabori]] (PT)
**Published:** London, June 1973
**File:** `borley/ghosts of borley/introduction.md`

## Summary

The introduction serves two purposes: to frame the authors' approach, and to map the prior literature on the Borley case.

**The scale of the case:** Three books, a book-length SPR report, and "innumerable articles" have been written about Borley. "Several hundred trained investigators, journalists and laymen" have studied it. It has been used both as proof of survival after death and as an example of "unscrupulous trickery."

**The authors' declared bias:** Both authors believe *some* phenomena were genuine. They reject the indictment brought against [[harry-price]] in the SPR's critical 1956 report. They're writing the case for the defence — while acknowledging they'll try to be non-partisan.

**Harry Price's character:** Quick-tempered, vain, self-assertive, never averse to publicity. Made enemies easily. The accusations against him didn't surface until nine months after his death (1948), and the first critical survey wasn't published until eight years later (1956). The authors note this timing matters.

**The SPR controversy:**
- 1956: *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (Dingwall, Goldney, Hall) — the prosecution case, published simultaneously in SPR *Proceedings* and by Duckworth & Co.
- 1965: SPR authorises re-examination of Borley files (Robert J. Hastings)
- March 1969: Hastings report published; widely reviewed; some said it vindicated Price, the 1956 authors denied this

**On the epistemology of hauntings:** A brief but pointed passage on how belief and scepticism are both traits of character — and how the SPR's declared neutrality ("without prejudice or prepossession") can itself become a form of one-sidedness. The human element is paramount; Heisenberg's principle applies.

## Key Claims

- The Borley phenomena span "over seventy years" of reported activity.
- The controversy is unusually long and violent for a haunting case.
- Accusations against Price emerged posthumously, which the authors find significant.
