# The Haunting of Borley Rectory — Chapter 8: The Rector's Wife

**Source:** Sean O'Connor, *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (c. 2022)
**Slug:** `hbrs-ch08`
**Time period:** 26 January 1899 – 22 July 1930

## Summary

The fullest biography of [[marianne-foyster]] in any source. Key new facts:

### Marianne's Origins

- **Born:** 26 January 1899, Romiley, near Stockport; only daughter of Annie and William Shaw.
- Named after her paternal grandmother Mary Ann, who had died in a lunatic asylum in Macclesfield.
- Father: failed schoolteacher, failed publican; gave bookkeeping/shorthand lessons; mother gave piano lessons. Family moved repeatedly: Widnes, Oughtrington (Lymm), Drumnadreagh (Co. Antrim, Ireland).

### Lionel Foyster's Background

- Born into a clerical family in Hastings; his aunt Caroline married Henry Dawson Ellis Bull in 1862.
- Only brother to follow his father into the Church; graduated Pembroke College Cambridge 1900.
- Baptised Marianne at St Peter's, Oughtrington on 19 June 1906, aged 28; he was a curate there.
- Emigrated to Canada in 1910 as a missionary to Bay du Vin, New Brunswick; carried a Bible printed in 1537.

### Marianne's First Marriage and Child

- At 15, Marianne was introduced to Harold Greenwood (21), a Church of England clergyman's son. They became engaged.
- Greenwood disappeared; Marianne was left as an unmarried teenage mother.
- Her parents raised the baby as her "younger brother" — a common Belfast custom.
- She left for a munitions factory in Coventry; the child (Ian Shaw) was brought up without her.

### Marianne Goes to Canada

- 5 August 1922: boarded the Canadian Pacific steamer *Victorian* at £30 in her purse.
- They married at St Ansgar's, Salmonhurst on 28 August 1922.
- Marriage was not a love match: "a mutually beneficial arrangement." Lionel was twice her age.
- She declared herself a spinster in the marriage register. 

### Life in Canada (1922–1930)

- Salmonhurst: a Danish-speaking farming village; church flew the Danish flag. Marianne learned Danish, to sew, ski, skate, dance, golf, and started smoking cigarettes.
- 1927: moved to Sackville (on the Isthmus of Chignecto). Marianne enrolled at Mount Allison Ladies' College. Diagnosed with polycystic ovaries; surgery left her unable to have more children.
- It was at **Amherst Hospital** (Nova Scotia) that she first heard the Esther Cox story — the "Great Amherst Mystery" of 1878. O'Connor draws explicit parallels between Esther Cox's situation and Marianne's own.
- Adopted Adelaide (baby Barbara Tower, b. 1928, whose parents died within days of each other); renamed her Adelaide.
- Wall Street Crash devastated Lionel's investments; arthritis diagnosed.

### The Invitation to Borley

On 8 April 1930, Lionel returned from England treatment; the Bulls invited them to take the vacant living. Marianne had mixed feelings: England's rigid class system vs. Canada's freedom. But the living seemed ideal for a sick, ageing man. They arrived in England 26 July 1930.
