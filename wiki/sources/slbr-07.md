# Sidelights — 7: The Foyster Diaries

**Source:** *Sidelights on Borley Rectory*, Essay 7
**Author:** Andrew Clarke

## Summary

The most rigorous published textual analysis of [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]]'s writings about Borley. Clarke identifies four distinct documents and traces the confusion created by Harry Price and subsequent writers who treated them interchangeably.

## The Four Documents

### "Writing A" (The Inferred Diary)
A contemporary diary claimed by Foyster to cover the full Foyster period, referenced by Robert Hastings in his defence of Price. Clarke argues it never existed. Evidence:
- In the "Diary of Occurrences," Foyster writes vaguely about early dates ("Some time before Christmas..."), inconsistent with having a diary in hand.
- In "Fifteen Months," Foyster admits he had *"no exact record"* of post-June events and that some happenings had been *"lost in oblivion"* — language incompatible with a diary.
- [[marianne-foyster]] did not remember any such diary.

### The "Diary of Occurrences"
Three instalments written March–June 1931 (within ~4 months of the events). Written primarily for family circulation: *"Marianne, I am going to write a memorandum of our experiences before I forget them... I can send it round to members of my family."* The closest thing to a contemporary record. Foyster swore on oath that it was accurate. Price possessed the only copy (Marianne says he borrowed and "lost" it).

### "Fifteen Months in a Haunted House"
A fictionalized account written c.1933–34, intended for commercial publication. Marianne's characterisation: *"It was fun, and he started it as something to do... it would have been published as a book of fiction."* Features pseudonyms, dialogue, and a framing narrative. Edwin Whitehouse transformed into "Miss Edith Greycastle"; Frank Pearless into "Lawless." Completed post-May 1934; Price did not obtain a copy until after *The End of Borley Rectory* was published.

### "Summary of Experiences at Borley Rectory"
Written January–February 1938 at Price's request, seven years after the events. This is what appears in *The Most Haunted House in England* as the "diary." Lionel Foyster was by this time crippled by arthritis; his terse style makes it the most arresting section of Price's book. [[marianne-foyster]]: *"He didn't start that until long, long after we had left Borley."*

## Key Findings

1. **No "diary" was ever printed in Price's books.** The "Foyster diary" in MHH is the 1938 Summary, written 7 years after the events.
2. **The Diary of Occurrences is the most reliable document** but Price never quoted it directly.
3. **The fictional framing of "Fifteen Months" was understood by Marianne but not Lionel** — Lionel seems to have recorded jokes and pranks as genuine supernatural occurrences.
4. **Lionel's short-term memory deterioration** (cardiac condition) explains many trivial "disappearing objects" incidents but would not cause hallucinations.
5. **The SPR's implication** that Price persuaded Foyster to fake a diary format is unsupported; there was no correspondence to that effect.

## Contradiction

> **Contradiction:** Price presented the 1938 Summary in MHH as a verbatim day-by-day diary. The document itself was written seven years later, partly from memory and partly from the earlier "Diary of Occurrences." The precise dating in the Summary is partly genuine and partly reconstructed.
