# The Haunting of Borley Rectory — Chapter 10: The Evil in the Dark Closet

**Source:** Sean O'Connor, *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (c. 2022)
**Slug:** `hbrs-ch10`
**Time period:** January – April 1931

## Summary

The escalation of poltergeist activity and the arrival of Francois D'Arles.

### The Agricultural Context

Essex and Suffolk wheat farmers hit by 50% gross income drop 1929–31; tithe protests; bailiff seizures. The refurbished Rectory appeared insensitive in this context. By February 1931, 150 protest meetings against wage cuts had been organised.

### Poltergeist Escalation (February–March 1931)

- Teapot and milk jugs vanished then reappeared on the kitchen table.
- Something hit Marianne under her left eye, giving her a black eye (no satisfactory explanation).
- A hammer head and cotton reel fell on the bed.
- Pins on chair seats; an old lamp and battered saucepan placed to trip the family.
- A heavy doorknob flew past Marianne's head.
- Marianne started taking Luminal (a barbiturate sedative for anxiety and sleep disorders; side effects include confusion and hallucinations).
- Exorcism by Arthur Sellwood and Bernard Smith using Walsingham holy water and Catholic 1614 rite — worsened the activity.
- Lionel began writing circular letters to his family about the events.

### Francois D'Arles

- Lionel advertised in *The Times* personals for an odd-job man. D'Arles arrived on his motorcycle.
- Described as "a 37-year-old Canadian; short, dark, thick-set and handsome."
- O'Connor identifies D'Arles as **Frank Charles Pearless**, born Bermondsey, son of leather workers. His family lived for a time in Arles, France, where young Pearless became fluent in French and adopted his pseudonym. His war record was real: Royal Army Service Corps, France and Salonica; awarded the 1914 Star, British War Medal, Victory Medal.
- D'Arles's real son was **Douglas** (born to Emily Fernie 1928 from an adulterous affair; mother abandoned the child to D'Arles). He was NOT actually a widower.
- His wife Ada Ewens and two legitimate children (Renee and Leonard) remained in Stoke Newington.
- D'Arles took the job; his son became a playmate for Adelaide. "Within weeks, he was sleeping with the rector's wife."
