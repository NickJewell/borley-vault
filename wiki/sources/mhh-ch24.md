## Summary

Price's most systematic treatment of the wall-writing phenomenon — the most unusual phenomenon associated with Borley in the entire psychical research literature. Includes precise measurement data, handwriting analysis, and the accounts of multiple observers who watched marks appear.

## Key Content

**Scope**: All messages appeared during the Foyster occupation (1930–35). After they left, only meaningless scribbles appeared. The messages were all addressed to Marianne and requested Mass, prayers, and light — consistently Roman Catholic in flavour.

**Principal messages** (locations keyed to the floor plan):
- "Marianne Light Mass Prayers" (kitchen passage, 4'4" from floor)
- "Ma" (partial, 2'6" from floor, left-handed appearance)
- "Marianne" (staircase, 4½" in length)
- "MARIANNE PLEASE HELP GET" (bathroom exterior wall, 33" long at 4'3")
- "MARIANNE AT GET HELP—ENTRANT BOTTOM ME" (bathroom wall, 4'7") — Marianne wrote "I cannot understand, tell me more" underneath; a reply appeared
- "LIGHT IN \*\*\*\* WRITE PRAYER AND O \*\*\*\*?" — Kerr-Pearse added "I still cannot understand. Please tell me more." No further reply.
- "Edwin" (the name of a Foyster friend)
- "GET LIGHT MASS AND PRAYERS HERE" (landing archway near Chapel, 4'8") — appeared during Dom Richard Whitehouse's Novena

**Height analysis**: Most messages at 4'2" to 5'0" — consistent with a person approximately 5'2"–5'3". One or two lower marks (~2' from floor) might have been made by a child.

**Material**: Graphite indistinguishable from ordinary pencil under microscope; both hard and soft leads; marks so numerous Price needs half a page to list them all.

**Handwriting analysis**: Bank manager's examination of photographs: "at least two entities were operating." Different handwriting in different messages.

**Foreign phrasing**: "Marianne please help get" is "the phrasing of a person who does not properly understand the English language" — Price: "I think it is some proof of the authenticity of the messages."

**Paranormal appearances — witnessed**: Multiple observers watched marks appear:
- Kerr-Pearse: checked wall at 7:25 p.m., found new marks at 8:30 p.m.
- Savage and Bowden (BBC): three new marks appeared within a yard of the mantelpiece while they watched
- Glanville: photographed message; hour later new marks added; comparison of negatives shows marks in second photo only
- Bailey and Wintour: thought they "saw [a mark] grow in front of their eyes"
- Drinkwater: used up all his chalk ringing new marks that appeared at 4 a.m.

**Price's argument against hoax**: A practical joker would have written more sensational messages; the mere scrawls and half-formed letters are *not* what a hoaxer would produce.

## Significance

The wall-writing phenomenon is, Price argues, unique in psychical research. The systematic measurement data, handwriting analysis, multiple-observer appearances, and RC content all combine into a coherent picture.
