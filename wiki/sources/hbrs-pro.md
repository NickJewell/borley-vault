# The Haunting of Borley Rectory — Prologue: Death of a Rector

**Source:** Sean O'Connor, *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (c. 2022)
**Slug:** `hbrs-pro`
**Time period:** 14 June 1927

## Summary

The prologue covers the death and funeral of [[harry-bull]] on 9 June 1927, aged 64, at Borley Rectory. A county funeral filled the small church; the Bishop of Chelmsford delivered the eulogy: "He loved Borley with a great love. It was the great passion of his life." Mourners included Sir George Whitehouse, Miles Braithwaite (former mayor of Sudbury), and the schoolchildren of the village lining the path.

### The Murder Allegation

Among the bereaved were Harry's younger sisters Freda, Constance and Ethel, who suspected he had been **poisoned** — and that the person who administered the poison was present at the graveside (Ivy Bull, his widow).

- Official cause of death: heart disease and chronic bronchitis.
- The sisters' suspicion would persist for decades and become embedded in the Borley legend.
- A bottle of sugar of lead (lead acetate) was later found in the cellar, the compound used in cosmetics/paint-thinning but fatal in high doses.

## Significance

- Establishes the deep antagonism between Harry Bull's sisters and his widow Ivy that drives much of the early narrative.
- O'Connor quotes H. E. Pratt on Borley: "The spot's about as unromantic as a cabbage patch. That's why I think it's genuine."
