## Summary

Price's analytical chapter: his theoretical framework for the Borley phenomena, drawing on poltergeist theory and the "spirit hypothesis," with observations on the cyclic nature of the manifestations.

## Key Content

**The poltergeist theory**: Price is "certain" the physical phenomena (stone-throwing, furniture-moving, bottle-dropping, hair-ruffling, bell-ringing, belt-raising, door-locking) were the work of Poltergeister — mischievous entities of a type documented extensively in psychical research literature (he cites the Epworth Parsonage, 1716–17 as the closest comparable case).

**The spirit hypothesis**: For the forms, apparitions, footsteps, shufflings, code-tapping, wall-writings — Price proposes "persisting remnants of the egos or personalities, with some portions of intelligence still retained, of persons who once were associated with the Rectory, or with some building formerly on the same site." He does not claim to understand the mechanism.

**What he cannot explain**: The phantom coach, the strange light in the window, odours, cold sensations, the "enchanted insect," and various other phenomena fall outside either theory. "I can only say that I do not know. But I will also add that no one knows."

**Cyclic and recurrent patterns**: Price found that "the manifestations had a definite and determinable periodicity: that is, they recurred at definite intervals... And they became gradually weaker at each recurrence." This suggests a system with periodic forcing rather than random noise.

**Not hallucinatory**: Price systematically rejects the hallucination theory. The four Misses Bull saw the nun simultaneously; Glover's party heard the same thuds simultaneously; Kerr-Pearse was physically locked in a room (a fact, not a subjective impression).

**The diabolic theory**: He notes the old RC theologians' diabolic explanation but says we don't understand enough about the entities to assess it. He thinks the entities were "benevolent rather than malevolent."

**The Roman Catholic element**: Price emphasises repeatedly that the Borley phenomena have a consistent RC character — the nun, the monks, the medallions found, the wall messages (Mass, prayers, lights), the relic of the Curé d'Ars working on locked doors, the Novena producing a message.

## Significance

Price's theoretical synthesis. The poltergeist + persisting ego dual hypothesis, the cyclic periodicity observation, and the note about RC character are the key analytical contributions. See [[harry-price]], [[poltergeist-activity]], [[the-nun]], [[marie-lairre]].
