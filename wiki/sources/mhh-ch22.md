## Summary

Price's assessment of various attempts by professional mediums to illuminate the Borley mystery — largely unsuccessful — contrasted with the more productive amateur Planchette sessions conducted by the Glanville family.

## Key Content

**Professional mediums at Borley**: On June 16, 1929, two London mediums (Glover Botham and Harry Collard) held a séance in the Rectory without result. The Marks Tey spiritualist circle was more successful during the Foyster period.

**"Marion" (Josef Kraus)**: A well-known vaudeville telepathist and psychometrist who psychometrized the touch-wood "apport" found by Kerr-Pearse on July 18, 1937. [[sidney-glanville]] conducted the session (November 9, 1938) without disclosing any information. Marion said: the wood "goes rather far"; "it has been brought from another continent and later wandered about"; associated with "an old-fashioned building... a small island"; "a feud or quarrel between two families — two sorts of ideas, not enemies"; "one of the last owners was a very tall man, he is dead... a public man who played a big part... died lonely and not appreciated... became very disappointed with life in his last years." Price notes some of this fits but refrains from identifying who, as living people might object.

**Frau Sadler (Breslau)**: A German medium claimed to be receiving apports from Borley, including a book (Praise and Principle by McIntosh, grey mottled cover). Price dismisses: she had probably read German press accounts.

**Price's conclusion**: "I am afraid that the professional mediums threw little light on the Borley drama." The Glanville family's amateur table-tipping and Planchette sessions were "much more successful — or at least more entertaining."

## Significance

This chapter serves as a negative control for the Glanville sessions described in chapters 25–27. The contrast between failed professional mediums and the productive amateur Planchette (by someone given no information) strengthens the case that the Planchette results were not prompted by expectation or coached information.
