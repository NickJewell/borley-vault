# The Haunting of Borley Rectory — Chapter 1: A First Class Ghost Story

**Source:** Sean O'Connor, *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (c. 2022)
**Slug:** `hbrs-ch01`
**Time period:** 10–12 June 1929

## Summary

Covers Price's first arrival at Borley following the *Daily Mirror* story on 10 June 1929. Key details:

- *Daily Mirror* editor Alexander Campbell telephoned Price at Edward Saunders & Son (his paper sales employer) — not at the Laboratory.
- [[lucie-kaye]] is described as 27, "with blonde bobbed hair and fashionable clothes." She drove a two-seater Fiat.
- Price's physical description: bald, stocky, penetrating stare; migraine sufferer with darkened eyes; south-east London cockney-RP mix; heavy pipe smoker (Balkan Sobranie Mixture); indifferent to clothes; nervous and irritable.
- Smith telegram: "Thank God. Come Quickly. Will expect you for lunch."
- The ghost-hunter's kit is catalogued in full: overshoes, screw eyes, lead seals, electric bells, cameras, portable telephone, bowl of mercury and thermometer, plus a medical box with brandy.
- Essex/Suffolk context: flat pre-war rural landscape, Saxon dialect ("Stop your clanjanderin"), Dutch barn agriculture, isolation from London.
- On arrival: the Rectory hid behind tall dark elms; "a vast Victorian monstrosity in red brick"; the bricked-up window; the turret. Described as barracks or prison rather than parsonage.
- Eric Smith described as mixed race ("of mixed race"); his wife Mabel as "kindly" but "a very person."
- The 1929 general election context: first general election in which all women over 21 could vote; Ramsay MacDonald formed Labour minority government.

## Mrs Byford's Testimony

A letter to the *Daily Mirror* from a Mrs Ernest Byford of Newport, Essex, who as a girl 43 years earlier (c. 1886) worked at the Rectory as under-maidservant for a month. Heard slippered footsteps in the corridor at night; left immediately. This was a significant early independent account.
