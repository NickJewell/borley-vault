# The Haunting of Borley Rectory — Chapter 14: The Alleged Haunting at B—— Rectory

**Source:** Sean O'Connor, *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (c. 2022)
**Slug:** `hbrs-ch14`
**Time period:** 26 November 1932 – 8 October 1937

## Summary

Price's falling out with Lord Charles over Schneider; his writing career; the observer program.

### The Schneider Photograph Affair (1932–33)

Price secretly photographed Rudi Schneider appearing to cheat during a sitting in April 1932. Published this in the *Sunday Dispatch* before Lord Charles could publish his own findings. All major Laboratory council members resigned. Mollie Goldney warned Price he would never work with serious collaborators again.

### Price's Books

- *Leaves from a Psychist's Case-Book* (1933): restored reputation with general public.
- *Confessions of a Ghost-Hunter* (1936): included a chapter on "K—— Manor" (Borley) concluding the "supernatural played no part" in what he'd witnessed. Later removed the concluding sceptical line from serialisations.
- BBC broadcast from Dean Manor, 1936: "the first time that a ghost has had the opportunity of manifesting itself at the microphone." Nothing happened; the *Times* called it a "fiasco."

### The Observer Program

Price began his formal Borley tenancy in May 1937 (£30/year to Henning). The *Times* advertisement drew 200 applications. The Blue Book (*The Alleged Haunting at B—— Rectory: Instructions for Observers*) was the guide document.

Key observers:
- **Sidney Glanville**: applied immediately; first serious observer; drew ground plans and took photographs. Visited repeatedly; became Price's most loyal collaborator.
- **Mark Kerr-Pearse**: unemployed bachelor; became the most frequent observer; catalogued all portable objects. Found a dead frog in the hall. Noted the "cold spot" — Florence Whitehouse later had the place marked with a tiny pencilled cross. A tobacco tin was later found placed on that exact cross "with mathematical precision."

### Henry Lawton as Locum (August 1933)

O'Connor introduces Henry Lawton, who had stayed at the Rectory as locum tenens for August 1933 — **not mentioned in Price's books**. Lawton stayed a month in the Blue Room with his pregnant wife, experiencing nothing unusual. William Bigg told Lawton that Eric Smith had engineered the haunting story to depreciate the Rectory's value so a friend could buy it cheaply.
