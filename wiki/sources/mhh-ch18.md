## Summary

Price and Ellic Howe set up the Base Room (the old library/study) in June 1937 and immediately begin experiencing phenomena. The chapter also introduces the mystery of the "strange coat" — an unidentified woman's garment that appeared in the Blue Room between Price's visits.

## Key Content

**Choosing the Base Room**: Price selected the study/library because: it adjoined the hall (focal point of so many incidents); its French windows gave direct garden access for watching the Nun's Walk; it was below the Blue Room (focal centre of phenomena upstairs).

**Equipping the house**: Camp bed, paraffin lamp, kettle, crockery, 20 non-psychical-research books. "Ringed" every portable object in the house with coloured chalk in preparation for detecting any movement.

**The strange coat (June 2, 1937)**: In the Blue Room, hanging behind the door: "a long, dirty, mouldy, torn, rather moth-eaten dark blue serge lady's coat." It had NOT been there when Price and Henning inspected the house a fortnight earlier. Price inquired exhaustively — Henning knew nothing of it; village inquiries drew a blank; Arbon (cottage tenant) knew nothing; no maker's name on it. During the year's tenancy it disappeared for one week according to observers' reports, then reappeared. Never identified.

**First night disturbances (June 2, 1937)**: After 40 minutes of complete silence, a series of sharp taps from the passage outside the Base Room door — investigated, nothing found. Then fifteen minutes later: two loud "boot" thumps, then a door slamming upstairs. All investigated, nothing found. Chalk rings and seals all undisturbed.

**Second visit (June 16, 1937)**: During an absence of 45 minutes (dinner in Sudbury), found on return that a tobacco tin had moved 3 inches from its chalk outline and a small box had moved 7 feet. No explanation.

**The coldness**: Price describes the Rectory as "the quietest and coldest house I have ever been in" — confirmed by every observer. Unlike most old buildings, it emitted virtually no background noise. Temperature routinely near 48°F even in summer.

## Significance

The "strange coat" is one of the most intriguing minor mysteries: it appeared in a locked house between two Price inspections and was never identified. The Base Room setup documents the physical preparation for the observer program. See [[harry-price]], [[blue-room]], [[borley-rectory]].
