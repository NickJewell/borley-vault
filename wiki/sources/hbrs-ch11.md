# The Haunting of Borley Rectory — Chapter 11: Things That Go Bump in the Night

**Source:** Sean O'Connor, *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (c. 2022)
**Slug:** `hbrs-ch11`
**Time period:** April – June 1931

## Summary

The Marianne–D'Arles affair; the wall-writings; Edwin Whitehouse's arrival; the earthquake.

### The D'Arles Affair

O'Connor is explicit: "It was no romantic love-match, but a purely physical relationship driven by a desperate sexual need on her behalf and casual convenience on his." Marianne disliked D'Arles intensely but was "desperate" for sex. D'Arles was "controlling and domineering." The phenomena now became a convenient smokescreen for the affair. D'Arles's black eye (ostensibly from punching a ghost) was more likely from violent sex: "they fought like cats and dogs."

### The Wall-Writings

Around April–June 1931, pencilled messages began appearing:
- "Marianne" (4 ft 6 in above floor, in the bathroom passage).
- "Marianne please help get" (further along).
- Responses with Catholic flavour.
- Lionel asked "WHAT CAN WE DO?" beneath one message; response: "HERE."

The first messages to appear with a distinctly Catholic character — calling for a Mass in the Rectory.

### Edwin Whitehouse

Born 1899 in Istanbul; son of Sir George's brother Francis Cowley Whitehouse; mother from the Whittall trading family. Served in the Royal Navy at Gallipoli aged 16; invalided out 1917 with shell shock. Nervous breakdown; incomplete psychoanalysis under Dr Crichton-Miller at Bowdon House nursing home. Contemplating the priesthood.

Edwin arrived at Borley 6 June 1931; became fascinated by the wall-writings and convinced the phenomena was "primarily concerned with Roman Catholicism." He gave the Foysters scapulas to wear; gave Marianne a relic of St John Vianney. He asked Father Moran in Sudbury to hold a Mass in the Rectory; Moran sensed Edwin was "not exactly himself" and declined.

O'Connor: "The increasingly delicate Lionel and the neurotic Edwin became very close friends... 'exceedingly intimate', with the suggestion of an attraction between the two men."

### The Novena and the "Get Light, Mass, Prayers" Message

Marianne and Edwin prayed a nine-day Novena. On the final day (16 June) the message "Get Light, Mass, Prayers" appeared on a previously clean patch of wall. O'Connor notes that Edwin later admitted the wall-writing might have been his own work.

### The June 1931 Earthquake

On the night of Sunday 7 June 1931, the largest earthquake in East Anglia since 1884 struck. Whole houses swayed; livestock woke; doorbells rang spontaneously; crockery rattled. The Foysters blamed the subsequent chaos in the house on poltergeists; the earthquake was the actual cause of some of it.

### The Foysters' Evacuation to Arthur Hall

Florence Whitehouse, witnessing phenomena including a fire in a locked unused room, insisted the Foysters evacuate. During their 9-day stay at Arthur Hall, Adelaide "thawed out" and became almost a different child — an observation O'Connor uses to suggest the atmosphere in the Rectory was deeply unhealthy for the children.
