# Sidelights — 13: A Demented Female and the Marks Tey Spiritualist Circle

**Source:** *Sidelights on Borley Rectory*, Essay 13
**Author:** Andrew Clarke

## Summary

A full reconstruction of the January 23–24, 1932 night — the culmination of the Foyster-period phenomena — drawing on the Marks Tey Spiritualist Circle's own contemporary notes (in shorthand), Guy L'Estrange's various accounts, and Marianne's later testimony. Clarke identifies [[francois-darles|Frank Pearless]] as the chief agent.

## The Marks Tey Circle

Organised by Mr and Mrs Warren (Marks Tey village). A loose-knit group of East Anglian spiritualists who had been visiting the Foysters on and off. Captain VM Deane (who later published an account in *Psychic Science*, 1941) kept shorthand notes — **the only contemporaneous record of any Foyster-period event**.

## Reconstructed Timeline

- Warren and Frost arrived at 5pm with medium [[guy-lestrange|Guy L'Estrange]].
- Tea; briefing; immediate bottle-throwing on kitchen stairs; bell-ringing.
- Warren and Frost left to fetch rest of Circle; phenomena intensified.
- The Circle (six members) returned at 10pm; hymn-singing, prayers.
- At ~1am, Marianne wandered downstairs in a "diaphanous night-dress" in a trance-like state.
- At ~3am, the Circle discussed natural explanations in the library; Marianne overheard and came down.
- Party left at 5am.

## Guy L'Estrange's Unreliability

L'Estrange's accounts (1936 BBC broadcast, 1942 *Norwich Mercury* article, c.1970s interview with Peter Underwood) are mutually contradictory and diverge increasingly from Deane's contemporaneous notes:
- He wrote out the other Circle members entirely, claiming to have been the sole visitor.
- He "forgot" the séance in the Blue Room.
- His 1970s account is "worthy of a Hollywood film, complete with Marianne in a see-through nightie, bottles exploding like hand-grenades."
- Clarke: the escalation of L'Estrange's story directly correlates with his feud with the Warren faction, whom he was attempting to erase from the record.

## The Cellar Trapdoor Mechanism

The key to Clarke's analysis: an external cellar trapdoor in the courtyard (noted by several Price observers in 1937: Colonel Westland, Ryberg) gave unsecured access to the cellars from the yard. From there:
- Bottles could be thrown two at a time up the back staircase
- Cellar floor could be pressed to simulate footsteps in the library above
- The string in the ivy provided mass bell-ringing

Pearless had previously confessed to Ian Shaw that he had caused much of the haunting and that *"things had got out of hand."* He suggested the Marks Tey Circle visit be used as the pretext to end the phenomena. After the Circle's visit, phenomena effectively ceased.

## The Circle's Conclusion

Captain Deane's account: *"The occupants of the house showed signs of nervous tension throughout the night, especially the lady."* The Circle discussed natural explanations in the library; Clarke infers Marianne overheard and confronted them, leading to the abrupt 3am dismissal (with the excuse that Lionel had "a sermon to write").

The nun identified by L'Estrange in séance as "Evangeline Westcott" — not "Marie Lairre" — is noted without comment.
