# The Haunting of Borley Rectory — Chapter 13: The World's Best Ghost Story

**Source:** Sean O'Connor, *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (c. 2022)
**Slug:** `hbrs-ch13`
**Time period:** 18 November 1931 – 23 January 1932

## Summary

Edwin Whitehouse's last visits; Adelaide's black eye; the Marks Tey Circle séance; Jack Cannell's visit.

### Edwin Whitehouse's Final Visit (18 November)

In the kitchen with Marianne and Katie (the maid) during a thunderstorm. A bottle appeared poised in mid-air for a second or two near the ceiling, then crashed down. A second bottle rolled in from the staircase. Edwin took Marianne up the stairs; the lamp went out; she collapsed. Lionel had been delayed by heavy fog.

Edwin's father subsequently forbade his visits. Edwin was accepted for the priesthood.

### Adelaide's Black Eye

One afternoon, Adelaide appeared with a black eye. Asked how she got it: "A nasty thing gave it to me... by the curtain in my room." She said "other children" hid behind the curtains and hit her when she walked past.

### The Marks Tey Circle (22–23 January 1932)

George Warren, 79-year-old postmaster at Marks Tey and spiritualist; arrived with H. F. Frost, Captain Deane, Emily Warren, and medium **Guy L'Estrange** (32, freelance journalist from Norfolk; tested by Price in March 1931).

The séance in the Blue Room produced a dramatic contact: the nun gave her name as **Evangeline Westcott**. Via planchette the nun communicated: "Virgin! Do forgive me! Oh mother of Jesus, help me... I was thirsty and gave me empty bottles... I am now to go to the Judgement throne." Bowl of primroses brought; L'Estrange buried his face in them.

The morning after, the Foysters felt the house was changed: "renewed... warm." The phenomena had largely stopped.

### Jack Cannell's Visit (late January 1932)

Jack Cannell: journalist, magic club member, veteran "spook man." Arrived at the Rectory uninvited by taxi with ghost-detecting kit including a small pistol. Lionel refused to let him investigate: "Things are quiet in this house now and I believe they will remain so." Cannell left concluding the Borley story was "the world's best ghost story."
