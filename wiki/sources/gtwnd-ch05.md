# Source: *Ghosts That Will Not Die* — Ch. 5: Marianne Confesses

**Author:** Vincent O'Neil

A full transcript of the Robert Swanson interviews with Marianne (then Mrs. O'Neil), conducted February 1958 (Gladstone Hotel, Jamestown, North Dakota) and May 11–12, 1958 (Governor Clinton Hotel, New York City). Swanson was hired by the Parapsychology Foundation's Eileen Garrett at the instigation of Trevor Hall.

The chapter also includes Eileen Garrett's letters to Trevor Hall about the New York interviews — some of the most candid assessments of Marianne's character in the historical record.

## The Jamestown Interview (February 1958)

Marianne was reluctant to speak until Swanson assured her the interview would not be published until after her death.

### Key admissions and denials:

**Stone throwing:** "Lionel could have" been responsible. Stone throwing stopped when Lionel "got sick to the point where he couldn't walk."

**The fake ghost:** Marianne admitted making a luminous cardboard torso draped in a sheet, hung between two pulleys, to "ghost the ghost" and frighten d'Arles, who they believed was staging phenomena. "We made up a very crude figure and put this sheet around it. We happened to have some luminous paint... this did scare d'Arles and he never did play any more of his tricks again."

**Bells:** Marianne and her son Ian went up to the attic and tested the bell wires: "We found out that the bells could be made to ring by tampering with them." Attributed loose wires to rats or bats.

**Lionel's mental state:** "He was suffering from a form of heart trouble that deprived the brain of [oxygen]... He was very, very forgetful all his life... He would place things down and wouldn't be able to find them, then when he found them again, he would say the 'things' had carried it around."

**Price's visit (October 1931):** Marianne says Price "walked out because I invited him to leave and told him never to come back." She was furious that Adelaide was suspected.

**The manuscript:** Marianne says Lionel's manuscript was "supposed to be more or less fiction — the whole thing was." She never read it; it was supposed to amuse him.

**Eileen Garrett summarised the first interview:** "Marianne expressed the belief that everything connected with Borley during her husband's presence there was a fake. Her description of her husband is that he 'was always off his rocker.' Mention of Price's name is too much for her."

## The New York Interviews (May 11–12, 1958)

Swanson pressed on specific incidents from Hall's list of about two dozen questions. Marianne was less guarded but often contradictory.

**Harry Bull apparition:** Marianne denied ever seeing Harry Bull as an apparition. She admits she saw a flesh-and-blood man in a dark smoking coat who Lionel said looked like Harry Bull; she saw him go through the garden, into the churchyard. "He looked real." She did not speak to him. She denies Lionel's manuscript account that she saw him "between the library and bedroom."

**The monstrosity/Shadow:** "No, I do not recall seeing any monstrosity at all." Speculates it may have been Ian playing a trick.

**The stiletto incident:** "I certainly don't remember any stiletto incident... I have never, to the best of my beliefs, even seen a stiletto." She was only familiar with a "flat ivory paper knife."

**The Novena:** Denies reciting nine rosaries with Edwin Whitehouse: "I certainly didn't recite nine rosaries with Edwin Whitehouse." Confirms the wall writing "Get light Mass and prayers" was there for all to see; thought Edwin wrote it. "I washed it from the wall and was very annoyed at Edwin."

**The Blue Room:** Marianne says the room was "pale gentian-pink" — not blue. Confirms only one window (contradicting Whitehouse's description of windows at the back and side).

**Bottles/poltergeist:** "When Edwin was there I did see bottles fall, but I don't know where they came from."

**Wedding ring disappearances:** "My wedding ring... was at least 3–4 sizes too big. I could wear it easily on my middle finger. I would take it off... and it would always get lost. It was quite a joke."

**General stance:** Marianne confirms "certain rooms in the house were unhappy rooms" and that "there were unexplained drops in temperature." She says she never saw the headless coachman or the nun. But in her private notes she wrote of "many strange incidents... including the ghost lights, the bells, and the apparitions."

## Eileen Garrett on Marianne (Letter to Trevor Hall, May 10, 1958)

A remarkably candid letter. Garrett describes Marianne as:
- "Extremely Irish, born in Antrim, and filled with all the imagination, superstition, legends... of my race"
- "She quite unblushingly told Mr. Swanson that she had every intention of lying to him"
- "She lives in a perpetual temperature of romance"
- Marianne "denied emphatically any relationship with Whitehouse but rather intimated that the latter and Foyster were exceedingly intimate which suggested a spot of homosexuality"
- When Garrett asked point-blank if she had killed Foyster, Marianne "answered equally candidly that it had often been in her mind, but that he was too cunning for anything like that to happen to him"

Garrett's assessment: "I am perfectly willing to believe that if Price wanted some phenomena, and Mrs. Goldney obviously did, she would have no hesitation in producing it."
