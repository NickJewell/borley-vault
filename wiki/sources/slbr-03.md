# Sidelights — 3: In the Bedroom with Harry Price

**Source:** *Sidelights on Borley Rectory*, Essay 3
**Author:** Andrew Clarke

## Summary

A forensic analysis of the four contradictory accounts of the Blue Room séance on the night of June 12–13, 1929. Demonstrates that [[harry-price]], [[lucie-kaye|Lucie Meeker]], [[mabel-smith|Mrs Smith]], and V. C. Wall (Daily Mirror) cannot agree on almost any detail.

## Key Contradictions

| Element | Price's Account | Meeker's Account | Mrs Smith | Wall |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|-----------|------|
| Attendance | All gathered at dinner | Only she and Price in room | Smiths entered late with tea | Lists all except the Misses Bull |
| Lighting | "Powerful duplex paraffin oil lamp burning" | "fitful and depressing" | "In the Blue Room was darkness" | — |
| Séance duration | — | 90 minutes | — | "nearly three hours" |
| How séance ended | — | Knocks became faint | They stopped it due to disturbing content | — |
| Soap incident | "everybody saw the phenomenon" | Not mentioned | Heard about it second-hand after the fact | Confirmed |

**The Misses Bull:** Wall's contemporaneous account (written the next day) does not mention the Bull sisters at all, despite their supposed presence.

**The Séance Content:** Meeker says names were spelt out — Harry Bull's spirit claiming his will was misinterpreted (which benefited the Bull sisters who arranged the séance). Wall says no answer at all was received to questions about Bull's private affairs.

**The Soap:** Ethel Bull recalled *"We were none of us in the room when the piece of soap jumped out of its washstand"* — contradicting Price's "everybody saw the phenomenon."

## Conclusions

Clarke argues the contradictions reveal normal failures of memory under exciting conditions, not deliberate fraud by the witnesses. The séance was probably initiated by the Bull sisters (keen spiritualists) rather than by Price. The "tapping" entity was most likely thermal contraction of the mahogany mirror in changing humidity from the paraffin lamp.
