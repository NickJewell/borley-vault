## Summary

The transition from Foyster departure to Price's tenancy: the empty Rectory period 1935–37, Price's motivation for renting the house, and his first meeting with [[rev-ac-henning]].

## Key Content

**Empty Rectory 1935–37**: The Rectory lay empty after the Foysters left. Villagers occasionally saw the "strange light" in Room 7 window; the rumble of an invisible coach and the clatter of phantasmal horses were heard on the hollow road outside. Queen Anne's Bounty tried to sell or let the house without success; it had previously been up for sale during the Smith period. Price was told he could buy it "for a song."

**Price's motivation**: Two goals — (a) make a prolonged independent investigation, and (b) potentially establish a permanent "psychic trust" where researchers could spend time making experiments. He considered but rejected the idea of a "Home of Rest for Decayed Mediums" on practical grounds.

**Methodological principle**: Price explicitly wanted to *exclude* himself and all previous occupants from the inquiry: "I wanted first-hand information and evidence from intelligent and competent persons whom I did not know, and who were in no way connected with any previous occupant."

**First meeting with Rev. Henning (May 19, 1937)**: Henning met Price at Sudbury station, invited him to lunch at Liston Rectory (warm, cosy — "like going out of sunshine into a mausoleum" when they visited the Rectory afterwards). Agreed rent: £30 per year, Henning to pay rates. Price handed over a £15 cheque for six months in advance and received the keys. He thus became "the first lay tenant of Borley Rectory since it was built seventy-four years previously."

**Henning's character**: Price describes him as providing "Liston Rectory... presided over by his charming wife" — in sharp contrast to the deathly cold of Borley. Henning participates actively in the observer program as a cooperating rector, not a passive bystander.

## Significance

Establishes the formal independence of Price's 1937–38 investigation from all prior occupants. Rev. Henning becomes an active partner in the investigation rather than a distant authority. See [[rev-ac-henning]], [[harry-price]], [[borley-rectory]].
