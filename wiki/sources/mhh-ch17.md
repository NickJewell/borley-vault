## Summary

Price's account of placing the *Times* advertisement, selecting observers from ~200 applicants, and creating the formal structure of the 1937–38 investigation — the first systematically managed psychical research inquiry into a specific house.

## Key Content

**The Times advertisement (May 25, 1937)**: "Haunted House. Responsible persons of leisure and intelligence, intrepid, critical, and unbiased, are invited to join rota of observers in a year's night and day investigation of alleged haunted house in Home Counties..." The response was "phenomenal" — approximately 200 replies.

**The response field**: Charwomen and countesses; bored "society women"; half-pay officers offering to investigate "free if all expenses paid"; cranks with ghost-detecting machines; mediums wanting séances; journalists seeking copy; an old lady offering prayers; a man with a protective pentagram. Price consigned about 75% to the waste-paper basket.

**Selection criteria**: About 40 accepted — university graduates, scientists, doctors of medicine, consulting engineers, army officers on the active list. All intelligent, educated, cultured, trained, with scientific interest; all willing to give skill, time and money. Price chose them by personal interview and letter assessment; under no circumstances would he give permission to visit without a personal interview and a signed Declaration Form.

**The Declaration Form** (Appendix A): 11 conditions — pay own expenses; not connected with the Press; not to name the location; not to publish accounts; not to take unauthorized persons; to submit reports; to lock up on leaving.

**The Blue Book**: Formal printed instructions for observers investigating Borley Rectory. "For the first time in the history of psychical research, there was printed a book of instructions... for the guidance of those investigating a particular haunted house." The Monotype Corporation asked to place a copy in their museum.

**Price's self-exclusion**: The main reason for the elaborate structure was Price's desire to eliminate himself and his known associates from the inquiry, to produce evidence he could not be accused of influencing.

## Significance

The observer program represents the most systematic attempt at controlled psychical research in the Borley case. The Blue Book's existence — printed instructions for a specific haunted house — was unprecedented. See [[the-observer-program]], [[harry-price]], [[borley-rectory]].
