# Source: *Ghosts That Will Not Die* — Ch. 4: The Borley Legend 1936–1996

**Author:** Vincent O'Neil

A dense dated chronology of all Borley events from 1936 through to the mid-1990s, compiled from published and unpublished research. This is the most comprehensive single-source chronology of post-fire phenomena.

## Observer Programme Period (1937–1938) — Selected Entries

**May 1937:** Price rents Rectory for one year. 48 names accepted. Sidney Glanville becomes Price's "right hand man" and begins compiling the "Locked Book."

**June 16, 1937:** Price visits before lease is official. Chalk lines drawn around items. Tobacco tin moved 3 inches; small box moved 7 feet overnight with all doors and windows locked.

**June–July 1937:** A woman's coat appears in Room #7.

**July 16:** Kerr-Pearse, Hennings, move coat to Blue Room. While rapping a table in the Base Room, "a most extraordinary noise was heard, as though coming from the kitchen and slowly moving down the passage toward us. It lasted fully a minute." Price told Glanville the sound was "a lady's silk dress."

**July 26:** While noting every wall writing, Price and unnamed witnesses "saw several new marks appear right under our noses."

**August 5:** Miss Reid experiences "feeling of terror" outside the Blue Room — "pins and needles all over," intense cold on a warm evening.

**August 14:** Four new wall writings noticed, including "Marianne, light, mass, prayers." Kerr-Pearse writes underneath: "I still cannot understand, please tell me more."

**September 21:** Kerr-Pearse and cousin Rupert Haig hear "a succession of heavy metallic sounds" from bathroom lasting 20–30 seconds. Haig leaves "in the firm conviction that it *is* haunted."

**October 28:** In Streatham, Helen Glanville begins receiving planchette writings.

**November 1:** Kerr-Pearse and Glanville find "the foundation of an older house" while digging in cellar.

**March 27, 1938:** Planchette board in Streatham prophesies fire, encouraging investigation "under the ruins" to find "bone of murdered warden."

## Fire and Aftermath

**December 1938:** Capt. Gregson buys property. **Loses two dogs in succession** — they "shriek and bolt away — never to be seen again."

**January 1939:** Neighbours report flame-like lights in upper windows.

**February 27, 1939 (midnight):** Fire destroys Rectory. A woman seen going from window to window; a young woman seen hanging by fingertips from the Blue Room sill.

**March 1939:** Herbert Mayes hears "sound of horses stampeding" while bicycling past after dark.

**March 1939:** Rosemary Williams sees a girl standing in midair. Companion Charles Browne sees same figure as it disappears. It is wearing white.

## Cambridge Commission (1941–1944)

One of the most important post-fire investigative bodies — a group of University of Cambridge undergraduates led by Andrew Robertson.

**October 31, 1941:** 12:45 a.m. — luminous patch appears to Robertson and I.P. Williams on wall outside sewing room. 2:10 a.m. — "heavy noises like footsteps" on back stairs; "a moving dark outline" spotted.

**November 12, 1941:** H.F. Russell seized from behind by "an unseen something," thrown "face down in a puddle of dirty water."

**April 30, 1943:** Members Cook, Hay, and Wadsworth hear five footsteps near the back stairs, several intermittent thuds, three occurrences of moving furniture, a packing case being dragged, four mallet-on-wood taps, a "very loud noise as of a slamming door," and a noise like "a falling brick."

**August 17, 1943:** Harry Price discovers jawbone while digging as instructed by planchette message. Rev. Henning and a labourer named Jackson accompany him. Also found: religious items, a silver-plated cream jug, and a medal of French nun Zoé Laboure (died 1876). When bones were sent to a London photographer, "two pictures fell down, a broken clock started up, and the fragment of the skull fell on the floor and broke into four pieces. The photographer begged Price to remove it."

**June 28, 1943:** Lieutenants Kujawa and Nawrocki of Polish Army have stones thrown at them. Kujawa is hit. Moving shadow seen near Nun's Walk.

**April 5, 1944:** Harry Price visits with LIFE magazine photographer David Schermann and reporter Cynthia Ledsham. "Floating brick" photograph taken.

## Post-Demolition Phenomena (selected)

**August 2, 1947:** [[james-turner|James Turner]] hears **lame footsteps and swishing of clerical clothing** in the church yard.

**September 1947:** Miss Backhouse followed by lame footsteps in the church yard.

**September 16, 1947:** 12:16 a.m. — John May hears footsteps, feels "a slight pressure" he describes as "essentially evil." Also hears a key in a lock, a door creaking open and then closing, followed by "soft notes and chords" from the organ.

**October 1947:** 3:15 p.m. — Mrs. Noah Walrond and Rev. Henning hear organ "playing as it might while the collection is being taken or waiting for a bride." Both search the vacant church thoroughly.

**April 24, 1949:** Mrs. Pearson and husband hear church bells ringing; assume it is Rev. Henning, but he is at Liston. Door is locked and Mrs. Pearson held the key.

**July 1951:** James Turner smells **strong odour of incense**.

**1951:** Mr. and Mrs. Robert Bacon buy the site. Mrs. Williams (her parents) saw the nun "moving past the window." Terry Bacon saw the nun three times, "noting that it was some 3–4 feet off the ground."

**1956:** Photographs taken before and after a church visit turn out perfect; those taken at Borley turn out blank.

**1970–73:** Geoffrey Croom-Hollingsworth and two friends make tape recordings during several visits. Noises recorded include door slamming, rappings, furniture being moved, grunting. Recorder and tape mangled after a loud crash in a locked room. Dark form seen among graves. Glow seen around Waldegrave tomb. Nun seen within 12 feet for 12 minutes.

**June 20, 1970:** 1:45 a.m. — Investigators see "old nun with mole on her face" for about 10 minutes.

**1972:** Roy Potter sees nun; throws brick through the apparition.

**July 1974 (BBC visit):** Cable cut; recorder jams; machine ruins a valuable tape. Tape recorder locked in closed room picks up sounds of heavy door opening and slamming closed. "A glow is seen near the chancel door, followed by a terrifying grunt."

**July 6, 1988:** Team of investigators find hidden tunnel and crypt under church. Inside are found a skeleton and a plaque dated 1921 with initials H.F.B. "The heavy stone coffins move to abnormal positions several times during the years." Photograph taken shows "a veiled bride" about to enter a large door.

**October 1990:** Monica Brown visits with tour group; "the coldest I have ever been in my life."

**June 1995:** 2:00 a.m. — A visitor and fiancée hear "clear sounds of a church choir in full song."
