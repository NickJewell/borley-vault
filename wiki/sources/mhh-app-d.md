## Summary

A complete witness-by-witness breakdown of all phenomena recorded at Borley Rectory, organised by phenomenon type. The most comprehensive catalogue of evidential claims in the record.

## Key Witness Counts by Phenomenon

**Nun figure (various forms)**: 15 named witnesses — Ethel, Freda, Mabel, Elsie Bull; Henry Bull; Harry Bull; the Bulls' cook; the Bulls' cousin; Mrs. Lloyd Williams; Edward Cooper; Mrs. Cooper; Fred Cartwright; Mary Pearson; V.C. Wall; Marianne Foyster

**Harry Bull phantasm**: Mrs. Foyster (solo witness)

**Headless man or men**: Mary Pearson; Harry Bull

**Phantom coach/horses**: Mary Pearson; Harry Bull; Edward Cooper; Herbert Mayes (galloping hoofs post-fire)

**Bell-ringing**: 12 named witnesses including all major occupant groups

**Footsteps**: 21 named witnesses — every occupancy represented; multiple BBC and academic observers

**Displaced/projected objects (Poltergeist)**: 31 named witnesses — the largest category

**Wall and paper writings**: 10 witnesses (all during tenancy or Foyster period)

**The light in the window**: ~20 witnesses — includes Rev. Henning, many observers, unnamed villagers

**Fulfilled prediction**: Miss Helen Glanville (Planchette prediction of fire)

**Luminous phenomenon**: Mr. S.G. Welles (solo)

**Strange insect**: Mrs. Margaret E. Wilson

**Matter-through-matter** (Gregson's toad): Captain Gregson and his two sons

**Cold sensations**: 6 named witnesses — Squadron-Leader Horniman's friend, Miss Reid, Rupert Haig, Roger Glanville, Mark Kerr-Pearse, Alan Cuthbert

**Gluey substance**: 4 witnesses (Chapel)

## Significance

The definitive evidential catalogue. The 15-witness Nun figure count and the 31-witness poltergeist category are the most substantial. The cold-spot phenomenon (6 independent witnesses) is notable given its specific location. See [[the-nun]], [[poltergeist-activity]], [[wall-writings]], [[phantom-light]], [[fire-1939]].
