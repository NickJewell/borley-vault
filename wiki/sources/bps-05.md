# Borley Postscript Ch.5: My Chip Off the Borley Block

**Author:** Montague Elelman (journalist, d. before 1974)
**Form:** Script of a radio broadcast, sent to Underwood in April 1974 with a covering letter saying "the remarkable story had the added advantage of being completely true"
**Slug:** `bps-05`

## Summary

Elelman was a journalist who first visited [[borley-rectory|Borley]] ruins in March 1946 and picked up a 17-inch piece of charred oak beam. For the next nine years, he claimed, unusual incidents occurred in the presence of the wood — seen independently by multiple witnesses who had no knowledge of its origin.

## Chronology of Incidents

| Date | Location | Witness | Incident |
|------|----------|---------|----------|
| March 1946 | London (sister's home) | Sister | Saw black-garbed nun standing in gloom in Elelman's room while he was downstairs |
| Night 2 | London | Elelman | Dream of nun in Baker Street Underground; second loud scream (woke sister from deep sleep) |
| Night 3 | London | Brother Mike | Intense cold in room; heard a voice say "Sit up"; light switched itself on |
| Night 4 | London | Both | Chiming at 2:30 a.m. (no chiming clock in house) |
| Several weeks later | Westcliff-on-Sea (landlord's home) | Mr and Mrs Garnett | Doorbell rang at 8 p.m. Friday, Saturday, Sunday (no one at door); inner glass door confirmed no visitor; neighbour glimpsed a "darkly clad figure" on landing |
| Later | Surrey (in-laws) | Father-in-law | Shuffling footsteps at dawn in the spare room; stopped as Elelman and fiancée descended stairs |
| Final incident | Elelman's London flat | Wife Veronica | Saw a figure standing by the alcove where wood was hidden in a trunk; disappeared. Father-in-law then asked who was walking past the open door in the hall |

## Conclusion

After nine years of lending the wood out (few borrowers reported nothing; most described icy bedrooms, footsteps, "wives spotting nuns beside the wardrobe"), the last borrower and the wood both disappeared. Elelman reflects that it is "possible for people themselves to create ghostly sights and happenings out of the stories — false or otherwise — that are in their heads, and for those things to be seen by other people too."

## Assessment

The incidents spanned multiple independent witnesses with no prior knowledge of the wood's origin. The most evidentially interesting are the sister (who saw the nun before she knew where Elelman had been), the Garnetts (whose doorbell rang while Elelman was away and the wood was unopened in his luggage), and the neighbour (who glimpsed a figure on the landing without knowing the wood was in the house).
