# Source: *Ghosts That Will Not Die* — Overview

**Author:** Vincent O'Neil ("The Son of Borley")

A brief orienting summary of the Borley story and the author's personal connection to it, plus notes on physical remains, photography, and associated resources.

## Key Facts

- Rectory built 1863 by [[henry-bull|Rev. Henry Bull]]; destroyed by fire February 27, 1939; ruins razed spring 1944
- Foyster incumbency: October 16, 1930 to October 16, 1935; over 2,000 alleged paranormal events
- [[captain-gregson|Capt. Gregson's]] sons Alan and Anthony gave conflicting accounts of the fire to researcher Richard Lee
- Some rectory bricks used in WWII projects; site later cleared and three new homes erected
- **Physical survivals**: The Great Borley Bell and boundary stone owned by [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]]; the rectory gate posts owned by the late author Ivan Banks

## Post-Fire Excavations

- Harry Price dug around ruins from August 17, 1943; found a skull portion and jawbone
- Late 1950s: Philip Paul led excavations; discovered tunnel remains and servant bells
- Photographer Leonard Sewell was among the diggers; wrote a poem about the experience

## The "Floating Brick" Photograph

A LIFE magazine photographer visited the ruins April 5, 1944 with Price; the resultant photographs include one of what appears to be a "floating brick." Some attribute it to a spirit; others to a workman tossing debris caught by the camera.

## Author's Personal Stake

O'Neil explains his discovery of Borley began as genealogy research in 1994. [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] died in 1945; Marianne then married American G.I. Robert O'Neil from Minnesota. Vincent O'Neil (born Peter Richard, October 9, 1945) was adopted by Marianne and came to America in 1946 — unaware of his connection to the Borley legend until after Marianne's death in December 1992.

Books authored by O'Neil include:
- *Fifteen Months in the Most Haunted House in England* (combining Lionel's and Marianne's accounts)
- *The Most Haunted Woman in England* (Marianne's American life)
- *The Ghosts That Will Not Die* (this book)
- *Son of Borley — the Mysterious Search for My Identity*
