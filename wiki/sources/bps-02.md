# Borley Postscript Ch.2: The Haunted Rectory (1947 BBC Broadcast)

**Form:** Complete script of the BBC Home Service broadcast, Series "True Escape Stories" No.10
**Broadcast:** 29 June 1947, 9:30–10:15 p.m.
**Producers:** Peter Eton (producer), Alan Burgess (editor)
**Slug:** `bps-02`

## Summary

The full script of the 1947 BBC radio broadcast about the Borley haunting, prepared at the initiation of Harry Price and with his collaboration. The programme was based on Price's two books with additional material from witnesses recorded on location. Includes verbatim recordings from Borley (16–17 June 1947), Maldon (Captain Gregson), and Broadcasting House (Price, Henning, Glanville).

## Principal Contributors and Their Statements

### [[harry-price|Harry Price]] (recorded at BH, 20 & 24 June)
Claims the Borley case has become "the best authenticated case of haunting in the annals of psychical research." Endorses the Marie Lairre theory as "both reasonable and coherent... circumstantial evidence very strong — I might almost say conclusive."

### [[ethel-bull|Miss Ethel Bull]] (recorded at Borley, 17 June)
Verbatim first-person account: the 1900 sighting was on "28th July" (she couldn't remember the year); there had been three raps on her bedroom door every evening between nine and ten for a long time before; "I was terrified and so were they when they saw her – and it sent cold shivers down our backs and we simply flew up to the house."

### [[rev-ac-henning|Rev. Henning]] and [[mrs-henning|Mrs Henning]] (recorded at BH, 24 June)
Henning: convinced it is "a genuine haunt." Mrs Henning describes the footstep incident in the base room — "footsteps started very faintly and got louder – just as if someone was coming towards us. And as they got closer we distinctly heard the swish of trailing skirts."

### Mr and Mrs [[edward-cooper|Cooper]] (recorded at Borley, 17 June)
Mrs Cooper: "a large dog overhead" noises, then a nun seen leaving by the back entrance. Mr Cooper: moonlit coach with headlamps, seen for a moment, "not a sound whatever." Final night event: dark figure going round the bed; tremendous crash as of crockery but nothing broken.

### [[captain-gregson|Captain Gregson]] (recorded at Maldon, 18 June)
The full fire account: lamp fell over "automatically it seemed," whole library ablaze, fire brigade from Sudbury "quite useless." Someone asked who were the cloaked lady and gentleman who had come out of the Rectory and joined him on the lawn — he had been "quite alone at the time."

### [[james-turner|James Turner]] (recorded at Borley, 16 June)
A measured response: acknowledges being "a poet" whose reactions "ought to be super-sensitive"; says there has been "nothing like that at all" but maintains it is a place of "utter peace and quiet and happiness."

### [[samuel-seal|Samuel Seal]] (recorded at Borley, 17 June)
Sergeant from the Belchamp Water searchlight battery; walking home after midnight; two lights "coming at a devil of a speed" switched across the road with a dark shape following. On investigation, no gate had been opened. Then a "yelping scream" — he fled. First account dated 1942.

### Mr Shaw Jeffrey (read into the broadcast)
Harry Bull's Oxford friend: saw the nun several times; heard the spectral coach so often he "could sleep through the noise." Lost his French dictionary; found it the next morning "sprawling on the floor" after a bump in the night — bedroom door had been locked.

### Lady Whitehouse (read in)
Saw bottles fly across the room; stones thrown; messages "fluttered down from nowhere"; gloves and parasol flew; skirting board ablaze in locked empty room. "The Foysters were quite the bravest couple I ever knew."

### Mr Russell (sceptic)
Bought one plot; investigated tapping sounds (jackdaws), lights (Sudbury's glow). "Young students who asked to spend the night… all they caught was a cold, and that is all there is to catch."

### [[mrs-pearson|Mrs Pearson]] (sceptic)
"Don't mention the Rectory to me – I've never heard of such nonsense." Her sister's girl worked there four years, never heard or saw a thing.

## Key Details Not Found Elsewhere

- **Full broadcast schedule**: recordings at Borley on 16 and 17 June, Maldon on 18 June, Broadcasting House on 20 and 24 June, rehearsals 28–29 June.
- **"Don't Carlos don't"** is rendered verbatim in the dramatic reconstruction as "Don't Carlos – don't –" spoken by a woman's voice.
- **Walter Baird** is listed in the cast (otherwise unidentified in the Borley literature).
- The BBC investigators Eton and Burgess dug nine feet seven inches looking for buried church plate and "not a sausage."
