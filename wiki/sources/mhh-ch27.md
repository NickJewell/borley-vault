## Summary

The most dramatic single episode in the MHH record: a Planchette session on March 27, 1938, in which an entity named "Sunex Amures" dictated that he would burn the Rectory. Exactly eleven months later, on February 27, 1939, the Rectory burned at midnight — starting over the hall, exactly as predicted.

## Key Content

**The session (March 27, 1938)**: [[helen-glanville]] and her brother Roger, five months after their last Planchette session. Helen asked: "Does anyone want to speak to us?" — immediately: "yes." "Who are you?" — the Planchette wrote in a large scrawly hand:

> "Sunex Amures and one of his men MEAN TO BURN THE RECTORY (o-night at 9 o'clock end of the haunting go to the rectory and you will be able to see us enter into our own and under the ruins you will find bone of murdered wardens under the ruins mean you to have proof of haunting of the rectory at Borley the understanding of which tells the story of murder which happened there."

Helen asked: "In which room will the fire start?" → "**Over the hall.** Yes yes you must go if you want proof."

No further information was obtained.

**The script's preservation**: Price emphasises that the script was sent to his office at the time of writing, carefully preserved. The Glanville family did not travel to Borley that night — they did not take the threat seriously.

**The fire (February 27, 1939 at midnight)**: Captain Gregson's lamp overturned in the main hall; the first part of the house to burn was immediately over the hall. The fire started at midnight — eleven months to the day from the Planchette session.

**Price's assessment**: "This is quite definite. He said he would 'Burn the rectory' — and burnt it was! Not only did 'Sunex Amures' say that the Rectory would be burnt, but he said that it would start 'over the hall.' That is exactly where it did start."

**Connection to burial theory**: Price notes that the message also refers to "bone of murdered wardens under the ruins" — suggesting a connection to the buried nun and monk theory from the Planchette sessions. He speculates: did the disturbance of the cats' cemetery (chapter 20) indicate someone else had already been searching for these remains?

## Significance

The Sunex Amures prediction is the only fulfilled, documented-in-advance prediction in the entire Borley record. The script existed in Price's files before the fire. The specific detail — "over the hall" — matched the actual point of origin exactly. This is the strongest single piece of evidence for something genuinely supernormal in the Borley case, and the most difficult to explain away as coincidence or fabrication.
