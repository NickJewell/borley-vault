# The Haunting of Borley Rectory — Chapter 15: The End of Borley Rectory

**Source:** Sean O'Connor, *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (c. 2022)
**Slug:** `hbrs-ch15`
**Time period:** 22 September 1937 – 28 February 1939

## Summary

Price's publishing plans; the planchette sessions; Sunex Amures; the fire.

### Price's Book Plans

Longmans commissioning editor Allen Drinkwater offered £75 for a Borley book. Price and Richard Lambert (editor of *The Listener*) planned to collaborate; Lambert withdrew on his wife's advice. Price signed contract 18 August 1938.

### Glanville's Table-Tipping Séances

October–November 1937 at the Rectory with Kerr-Pearse and Alan Cuthbert:
- **Kate Boreham** communicated: confirmed she was a maid, died of unnatural causes in the kitchen. Parish register confirmed: "Kate Boreham of Sudbury died Easter Day 1888 aged 31."
- Harry Bull communicated: confirmed poisoned by his wife.
- Heavy muffled footsteps crossed the hall at 2 a.m. while the three men sat on the landing.

Helen Glanville's planchette sessions at Streatham produced the name **Marie Lairre** and the well-burial story. Essex Archaeological Society confirmed there was no religious house at Borley — but this didn't stop the story taking root.

### The Sunex Amures Prediction (27 March 1938)

Glanville planchette session at Streatham produced: "Sunex Amures and one of the men mean to burn the rectory tonight at 9 o'clock end of the haunting go to the rectory and you will be able to see us enter into our own and under the ruins you will find bone of murdered wardens."

This was made eleven months before the fire of 27/28 February 1939.

### Captain Gregson

O'Connor provides extensive new context:
- Born in Lancashire; father an architectural apprentice.
- Distinguished WWI record: supplied water to frontline trenches at the Somme under heavy fire for three weeks (two-thirds of men killed); survived a gas attack without his mask.
- Married Rita Pattard (Guernsey farmer's daughter) 1920; largest single landowner on Canvey Island by mid-1920s.
- **Fraud conviction**: November 1923, Essex assizes, fraudulently altering title deeds for a Canvey Island plot. Jury found him "guilty of uttering" but without intent to defraud; released with warning: "pretty lucky."
- **Fascist agent**: by 1935 appointed as British Union of Fascists' political agent for the Maldon Division.
- Bought the Rectory c. November 1938 at £500; renamed it "Borley Priory."

### The Fire (27/28 February 1939)

Gregson had boxes of books stacked in the hall. He went to extract an odd *Chambers's Encyclopaedia* and the stack collapsed, knocking over the oil lamp, igniting the burning oil in the hall. He "lost his head" — rushed to rouse his sons, drove to the fire station rather than alerting neighbours. Pearson family shouted from across the road to no avail. Sudbury fire brigade called at 12:15 a.m. Arrived 12:30 a.m.; roof collapsed; "as light as day" in the surrounding area.

Insurance claim: Gregson insured for £10,000; claimed £7,356. Loss adjusters found poverty-grade "darned and worn-out" underwear in a supposedly precious chest of drawers; only one Victorian farthing recovered (not the claimed coin collection). Henning concluded Gregson was "rather a bad egg."

O'Connor: "In a sad humiliation, the Rectory... was destroyed not by malevolent ghosts or destructive poltergeists, but at the hands of a fascist chancing his luck."
