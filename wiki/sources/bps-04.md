# Borley Postscript Ch.4: Lucy Meeker on Harry Price

**Author:** Mrs Lucie Meeker (formerly Miss Lucie Kaye, d. 7 May 1955)
**Form:** "Off the record" personal account sent to Underwood, 18 September 1951
**Original title:** "The Ghost that kept Harry Price Awake"
**Slug:** `bps-04`

## Summary

[[lucie-kaye|Lucie Kaye's]] own account of Price's first night at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]], 12 June 1929 — a personal, intimate account that fills gaps left by Price's more formal published version.

## Key Details

### The Candlestick Incident Reinterpreted
When Price and Kaye emerged from the Blue Room onto the cold spot landing, "something hurtled by us and crashed at the foot of the stairs. It had passed within inches of us and at considerable speed." They had just been in the Blue Room remarking on the candlesticks' ugliness. Kaye reflects: "I believe the thought uppermost in both our minds was that had we not made derogatory remarks about it, it might not have hurled itself at us."

### The Cold Spot
"It was not until some time later that the portion of the landing between the top of the staircase and the door of the Haunted Room became known as 'the cold spot.'" This establishes that on the first visit, the cold spot was not yet designated — confirming it was named from accumulated experience, not suggested in advance.

### The Knock Séance
An hour and a half of knocking communications from the wooden back of a swing mirror in the Blue Room. Using the spiritualist convention (1 tap = no, 2 = doubtful, 3 = yes), they established contact with an entity that claimed to be "the late rector" (almost certainly [[harry-bull|Harry Bull]]), communicating about his will being misinterpreted. "Names were spelled out — dates given." Much of this material was never published.

### Price's Migraine
Price suffered from severe migraines that turned the skin around his eyes "almost black." When he appeared at Kaye's door at 2 a.m. begging for a lift to catch a milk train, she initially thought she was seeing "enormous eyes" on his pale face — frightening her. He "looked awful" and was "generally fed up."

### The Reversal
Having prevailed on Kaye to wake him at 3:30 a.m., Price went back to bed. At 3:20 a.m. Kaye — who had forced herself to stay awake — crept to the Blue Room to find him sound asleep. She returned to her own bed, cold and miserable.

## Quote

> "That first night we spent at Borley Rectory ran true to form... the early spirit of adventure into the unknown without a thought of fear; and then, on the first signs of anything abnormal, the feeling of tension, the keyed-upness, the watchfulness — then the period of fear, and, as the night wanes on, cold encroaching upon fear, then the gradual descent into exhaustion."

## Assessment

This is the only known first-person account from Kaye's perspective of the first night. It confirms the candlestick incident, the knocking séance, and Price's physical distress. Her portrait of Price is warmer and more human than most published accounts.
