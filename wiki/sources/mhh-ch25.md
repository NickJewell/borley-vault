## Summary

The results of [[sidney-glanville]]'s table-tipping séances at the Rectory in October 1937, conducted by his son Roger, Mark Kerr-Pearse, and Alan Cuthbert. Entities claiming to be Harry Bull and a monk named "Fadenoch" communicated about the nun's burial location.

## Key Content

**Glanville's background**: A collateral descendant of Joseph Glanvill (the "Drummer of Tedworth" investigator), which Price notes as a possible reason for his interest. Built a special lightweight table (2–3 lbs, 2'2" high, 12" square) for the sessions. Code: one rock = No; two = Uncertain; three = Yes; letters spelled out by alphabet.

**Entity claiming to be Harry Bull** (October 23–25, 1937):
- Did you write the messages? No
- Is the nun buried in the garden? Yes (definite)
- South-west of the house? Yes (definite)
- Within two yards of a path? Yes
- Within two yards of a large fir-tree? Yes
- Is there a stone with "B" on it? Yes — and is she buried under the stone? Yes (definite)
- Is she buried more than 3 feet deep? No
- Has she ever been found? No
- Did the nun die violently *here*? No
- Did the trouble start before your father's time? Yes (a previous Rector, in the same house)

**On the messages**: Harry Bull denied writing them; denied a human wrote them; denied a man, woman, or child wrote them. "Is it a type of being we do not understand?" — Indefinite.

**"Fadenoch"**: A later entity, identifying itself as "Fadenoch" (a monk, not a prior, not a lay brother, but a monk). Said he was also buried in the Rectory grounds. Said "there is only good in this house" now.

**Price's caveat**: "I need hardly warn the reader not to take this séance 'information' too seriously." Explains why they didn't immediately excavate: the stone marked "B" is a property boundary marker from 1863, placed there when the Rectory was built — making it a very unlikely coincidence that it should also mark the burial spot.

**Consistency with Blue Room séance**: Some table-tipping answers corresponded closely with answers given in the June 1929 Blue Room séance.

## Significance

The Glanville table-tipping sessions are the immediate precursor to [[helen-glanville]]'s solo Planchette sessions, which produced the Mary Lairre name independently. "Fadenoch" as a buried monk later connects to planchette references. The specific location data (SW corner, fir-tree, path, stone marked B) is detailed enough to check against the known garden layout.
