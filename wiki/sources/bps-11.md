# Borley Postscript Ch.11: Welcome to Harry Price's Home

**Author:** [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]]
**Form:** Account of a visit to Constance Price, Harry Price's widow, at Arun Bank, Pulborough, Sussex
**Date:** Sunday 20 October 1968 (with Steuart Kiernander)
**Slug:** `bps-11`

## Summary

Underwood and Steuart Kiernander visited Constance Price at her home on the River Arun. Nearly twenty years after Harry Price's death, the house still felt as though he "had just popped out for a moment" — his coats, hats, and walking sticks still at the coat rack by the front door.

## Key Details

### Harry Price's Death
Constance Price confirmed he died on **28 March 1948** in his study chair. He had returned home after posting a letter and sat down. She made tea; when she returned, he was dead. "He had not been well the whole of his last week." Though he occasionally mentioned pounding in his heart, they had not taken it seriously and he had not consulted a doctor. "It was completely unexpected."

### *Borley III*
Price had "just written the first chapter for a third book on haunted Borley Rectory and drafted out what the book would contain." Constance did not know what would happen to it. "She had no interest in psychic research and there did not appear to be anyone to carry on his work."

### Constance on Goldney and Dingwall
"I think Mrs Goldney was a two-faced woman and Eric Dingwall was no better. They had often been there and she had looked on them as friends." She had not read the SPR book (1956): her trustee from Midland Bank "advised her not to and she had not done so."

### On Hall
She mentioned "another man, long after Harry's death" who had been "snooping about and asking the rector and other people questions about what kind of man Harry had been" — almost certainly Trevor Hall.

### The Borley Bell
The bell hung over the workshop/garage at Arun Bank; Constance sometimes used it to summon her brother and sister-in-law next door. When Underwood asked what would happen to it, she said "that rested with the executors and the University of London." In a bracketed note, Underwood records: after Mrs Price's death, "the University and the executors Midland Bank presented me with the massive Borley Bell which now rests in my garden."

### Home and History
Constance confirmed she and Harry "were school pupils together at New Cross, they had been sweethearts all their lives," married at Pulborough Church, and moved into Arun Bank (which they had built) in 1907 or 1909. The garden included an octagonal pond where Price had kept golden carp; a heron ate them all one winter after a hard frost.

### The Study
Underwood sat in Price's favourite chair — the one in which he had died — at Constance's invitation. On the walls: an original portrait by John Dumayne (1900), photographs from Price's psychical work, geological specimens. A complete mint-condition set of his own books in glass-fronted cases.
