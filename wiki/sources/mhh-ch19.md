## Summary

The operational setup for Price's investigation: observer instructions, protocols, the chalk-ringing system, and Price's reflections on the reports received.

## Key Content

**The observer cohort**: ~40 accepted (some with friends = ~48 who eventually took part). All strangers to Price and mostly strangers to each other. Price sometimes encouraged paired visits for corroboration; observers occasionally requested to work together after meeting at handover.

**Chalk-ringing protocol**: Every portable object throughout the house ringed with chalk and any pencil markings on walls similarly ringed with the date of discovery. Observers were specifically asked to look for new markings; hundreds of faint marks were already present that an observer might walk past a thousand times without noticing.

**Report requirements**: Written reports immediately after each observational period, from notes made on duty. Reports to be sent without delay for use in the published monograph.

**Instruments**: Price decided it was impractical to install permanent instruments without residing in the house almost continuously. However, he did install a delicate transmitting thermograph for 24 hours: it measured 48°F in the dining-room throughout, varying less than 1.5°F — confirming both the cold and the stability of the house.

**Negative reports**: Very few entirely blank records. Price does not publish the negative reports, noting they are preserved in the dossier. Only three observers received entirely nothing.

**Observer conduct**: Some observers placed electric contact-breakers, took photographs, made drawings and plans. Price found it "impracticable" to install anything permanent without personal residence.

**Organisation of reports in the book**: When a person visited more than once, all their records are treated as a group. Short single reports are grouped with others. Appendix C provides a full digest.

## Significance

Documents Price's deliberate methodology of independent witnesses. The key principle: sceptical strangers with no prior knowledge of Borley, using systematic control methods. Establishes the framework against which Appendix C's 48 observers must be evaluated. See [[the-observer-program]], [[harry-price]], [[borley-rectory]].
