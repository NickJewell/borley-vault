## Summary

Selected verbatim extracts from [[lionel-foyster]]'s day-by-day diary of manifestations, October 1930 – October 1935 — the most comprehensive primary record of the peak Foyster period. The complete diary ran to 180+ typed quarto sheets.

## Key Content

**First day (October 16, 1930)**: Voice calling "Marianne"; footsteps; phantasm of [[harry-bull]] in grey dressing-gown (recognized from description by villagers); jugs disappearing and reappearing; peculiar lavender smell in Blue Room; bells; [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]]'s bracelet taken from her wrist with no one near.

**Systematic poltergeist record (1931)**:
- February 25: mass return of crockery that had disappeared
- February 26: books found under bed; Marianne given a black eye by invisible blow on landing; cotton-reel and hammer thrown across bed at night
- March 5-16: continuous stone-throwing, aimed principally at Marianne; hairbrush on Foyster's head in bed; pictures taken off walls and laid flat; kitchen table found upside-down overnight
- March 23: a flat-iron thrown at Marianne on the stairs

**Seasonal patterns**: June worst month; June 8 (anniversary of Harry Bull's death) the worst single day (1931). Sunday quietest day, Monday worst. Absolute quiet during Holy Week and Easter Week. Fumigation with creosote sometimes stopped manifestations temporarily.

**The Curé d'Ars relic**: Foyster used a relic of Jean Baptiste Vianney (Curé d'Ars, 1786–1859) to unlock doors that had spontaneously locked. Effective, he reports; doors clicked open after prayer.

**Wall writings and paper messages**: From May 1931, papers with "Marianne" in childish handwriting found about the house; Marianne once saw a piece of paper fluttering in the air before falling.

**The spiritualist intervention**: January 23–24, 1932 — Marks Tey spiritualist circle held a séance during a great outbreak. After their departure (5 a.m.), "the house was entirely different" and manifestations "definitely stopped until 1935." A single stone thrown June 1932 was attributed by the spiritualists to "spare power floating around unused."

**Scale**: During worst period (Feb 1931 – Jan 1932), Foyster counted 45 major manifestations. 80% occurred in darkness. After spiritualist intervention: two minor recurrences in 1932, one in 1933, then nothing until 1935.

**Departure**: October 1935 — left due to Foyster's ill health, not the ghosts.

## Significance

The Foyster diary is the single most comprehensive primary record in the Borley archive. Price had not seen it at the time of his October 1931 visits; he received selected extracts later. The seasonal and weekly patterns (June/Monday worst; Holy Week quiet) are the most specific behavioral data in the record.
