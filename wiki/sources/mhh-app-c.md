## Summary

Full individual accounts from the principal official observers: Glanville, Kerr-Pearse, Westland, Bellamy, BBC observers (de Lotbinière, Hammond, Glover/Williams, Savage), Oxford undergraduates (Welles, Burden/Stainton, Bailey/Wintour), and Dr. C.E.M. Joad.

## Key Incidents by Observer

**[[sidney-glanville]] and family**: Multiple visits. Photographed "Marianne" message; compared with later photograph in which additional marks appeared. Found cats' cemetery disturbed; excavated and found large non-cat bones. Tobacco tin moved from chalk ring and placed with "mathematical precision" over a tiny cross mark on the floor — a cross that only Glanville, his son Roger, and Dr. Bellamy knew about. The moving blind in the Base Room. Temperature steady at 1°F variation.

**[[mark-kerr-pearse]]** (most time on site, multiple weeks): Pencil marks appearing during watches despite microscopic examinations. Marks found at 8:30 that weren't there at 7:25. 50-lb coal bag moved 18 inches. Petrified frog appeared in passage. Locked in Base Room with key on inside while having supper. Coat pulled twice. Cupboard door handle unscrewed. Icy cold draught experienced by Rupert Haig in sealed Base Room (60°F measured); Haig's letter from P&O Ranchi confirms his experience.

**"Cold spot" outside Blue Room**: Three independent witnesses — Squadron-Leader Horniman's unnamed friend, Miss Reid, and Rupert Haig — all experienced sudden intense cold at the identical spot on the landing outside the Blue Room, at different times and without knowing each other.

**Colonel Westland**: Found the mysterious blue pin-box in the cupboard at midnight after earlier thorough search. Found "strange coat" in Room 7.

**Dr. Bellamy**: Electric contact-breaker rang while party was in Blue Room; found all books displaced. Bellamy tested the device at 6 a.m. before leaving — found everything in working order.

**BBC observers (de Lotbinière)**: Sharp crack answered an aloud "challenge" before the sentence was finished. Carton moved 18" on chimney-piece.

**BBC (Glover/Williams party)**: Mrs. Lloyd Williams saw "round dark object" on Nun's Walk. Mrs. Glover heard quiet door-close (dead still afternoon). Both Glovers heard thud and scuffle from downstairs. All four heard two heavy thuds, lighter thud, vague shuffling, and staccato crack from kitchen while in Blue Room.

**BBC (Savage/Bowden)**: Gluey substance appeared on Chapel floor. New pencil mark appeared within a yard of mantelpiece while both watched; then another; then another. Room 5 markings: "Prince of Wales feathers" drawing.

**Oxford undergraduates (Welles et al.)**: S.G. Welles's luminous ceiling phenomenon — full detailed account. Lath moved from sink to floor in scullery during short absence. Three observers heard footsteps from kitchen at 12:17.

**Burden and Stainton**: Wailing sounds from Blue Room penetrating entire house at 1:30 a.m. Dragging sounds (too loud to mistake). Cat experiment: animal rubbed back against strange coat; particularly interested in Room 7.

**Dr. C.E.M. Joad** (head of Philosophy, Birkbeck College): Witnessed new pencil markings appearing under conditions he found "inconceivable" could be missed on previous inspection.

**Hardy, the foreman painter**: Saw column of smoke rising vertically from centre of lawn in bright afternoon — lasted 1–2 minutes, no explanation found.

## Significance

Appendix C is the most important single section in the book for the evidential record — 48 independent observers, almost all reporting phenomena. The cold-spot phenomenon (three independent witnesses, same location, without knowing each other) is particularly compelling.
