# Borley Postscript Ch.10: John L. Randall on Harry Price and Borley

**Author:** John L. Randall (SPR researcher)
**Form:** Excerpts from Randall's July 2000 SPR Journal article "Harry Price: The Case for the Defence" (18 pages), with commentary by Underwood
**Background:** Randall spent four years on an in-depth study of Price; wrote Price's entry for the *New Dictionary of National Biography*; addressed the SPR on the subject.
**Slug:** `bps-10`

## Summary

Randall provides a scholarly reappraisal of Harry Price and the Borley Report, arguing that the Report was produced by biased investigators and has been given excessive weight in the literature.

## Key Arguments

### The Two "Dirty Tricks"

**Trick 1:** On 9 October 1931, W.H. Salter travelled to Borley to try to persuade the Rev. Lionel Foyster to sever his links with Price and accept the SPR instead — "a clear example of unprofessional conduct." Price had been studying the phenomena since June 1929. Randall: "How would the BMA respond to a medical practitioner who tried to steal a colleague's patients?"

**Trick 2:** Lord Charles Hope secretly arranged to test the Rudi Schneider medium without telling Price, who had organised everything involving Rudi over a ten-year period including more than 2,000 letters (still in the Harry Price Library). This "dirty trick had more serious repercussions."

### The Borley Report (1956)

The Report was "essentially a systematic attack on the honesty and integrity of Harry Price... the case for the prosecution." Every event was interpreted in the worst possible light for Price; alternative interpretations were ignored.

### Trevor Hall's Character

Hall was "an estate agent, local magistrate, amateur conjurer and writer of popular books on Sherlock Holmes and Dorothy L. Sayers." In 1954 he was awarded the Perrott Studentship in Psychical Research, though not then an SPR member. He never met or corresponded with Harry Price but "seems to have conceived an almost fanatical hatred for the famous ghost-hunter." His participation gave the Report its "uncompromisingly hostile tone" (Randall's assessment).

### Lucie Kaye's Defence

Mrs Lucie Meeker (Kaye): "It is my considered conviction that Harry Price never, at any time, faked phenomena. I worked with him in close collaboration for some five years and, indeed, remained friends with him to the day of his death, and I am convinced he was a man of unimpeachable integrity."

### Sidney Glanville's Assessment

Glanville described Price as "an astonishing worker" for psychical research and a friend for whom he had "a very great regard and respect."

### Character Witnesses (all saying Price was a man of integrity)
- Geoffrey H. Motion (neighbour and Borley companion)
- Rev. George Royle (Rector of Pulborough 1944–70, Price's closest clerical friend)
- Bishop of Chichester, Dr George Bell: "a devoted member of the Church of England" — an address preserved at the County Record Office, Chichester
- Price made a churchwarden of St Mary's, Pulborough, weeks before his death

### The Hastings Report

The 1965 SPR grant of £50 to Hastings led to the 1969 *An Examination of the "Borley Report"* — widely interpreted as "official exoneration." The SPR's honorary secretary had to write to *New Scientist* clarifying the Society holds no corporate views. Randall: the Report "was badly flawed and very heavily biased, and Hastings believed that the Society should not have published it."

### Borley's Place in the Literature

"The Borley case remains the most fully documented example of a haunting in the annals of psychical research and Price's accumulation of a vast dossier of eye-witness reports is a major contribution to the study of spontaneous phenomena."
