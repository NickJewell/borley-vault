# Fifteen Months in a Haunted House — Foyster Manuscripts

A composite volume containing the two primary Foyster manuscripts in their original forms, prefaced by Peter Underwood's foreword and Vincent O'Neil's introduction. This is the most important primary source for the Foyster period: the raw diaries, not Price's edited extracts in *The Most Haunted House in England*.

**Curator note:** The Foyster manuscripts were acquired by Peter Underwood from A.H. Foyster (Lionel's brother-in-law), who presented them to him personally. The typescript originals are in the Harry Price Library, University of London.

---

## Foreword — Peter Underwood

Underwood's personal testimony after fifty years of involvement. Key points:

- Acquired the Foyster manuscript from A.H. Foyster (brother-in-law of Lionel), alongside a bottle "materialised" before Edwin Whitehouse's eyes and a border stone engraved "B"
- Met and knew personally: Ethel Bull, Constance Bull, Alfred Bull, the Hennings, the Paynes, Edwin Whitehouse, the Glanvilles, Mark Kerr-Pearse, Dr. Bellamy, Colonel Westland, Gordon Clover, Carter-Jonas, the Coopers, the Foysters, BBC personnel including Lotbinière and John Snagge, V.C. Wall, Rosemary Williams, Ballantyne, Herbert Mayes, Mollie Goldney, Dingwall, R.J. Hastings and many others
- His stated position: "it is a matter of complete indifference to me whether Harry Price was a charlatan of the first water or a man of integrity and honour (he was probably a bit of both) but what is indisputable is that paranormal activity was reported from Borley Rectory long before Harry Price went near the place and a wealth of reported paranormal activity took place there long after Harry Price was dead"
- "I defy anyone who has personally and fairly examined all the evidence and all the witnesses to say there is nothing to explain. There is in fact far too much good evidence from good witnesses to dismiss the ghosts of Borley"

## Introduction — Vincent O'Neil

A comprehensive narrative overview of the entire Borley case from O'Neil's perspective, adapted from his *Ghosts That Will Not Die* material. Covers the timeline 1863–present, all major phenomena, key witnesses, and the controversies. Of note: quotes the Owen/Mitchell 1978 report on Marianne's account and concludes the Foyster period left three unexplained phenomena even by Marianne's own testimony (the bottle, the tumbler, and the stiletto).

## Diary of Occurrences (Writing B) — Lionel A. Foyster

**What it is:** Foyster's family circular — three instalments of a typewritten narrative written between February and July 1931 for circulation among his family. This is Writing B in the Hastings classification system (not the hypothetical Writing A, which covered the whole period). It is *not* a daily diary in the strict sense but a retrospective narrative written during the events, signed by Foyster and dated.

**The three instalments:**

**First Instalment** (signed March 23, 1931):
- Arrival October 16, 1930; first phenomena immediately: voice calling "Marianne dear" (Lionel had not called)
- Phantom footsteps heard by multiple people including Adelaide and a workman; both mistook them for Lionel
- Marianne sees apparition of Harry Bull repeatedly — always in dressing gown, on stairs/landing between library and their bedroom; described the dressing gown to Mrs. Bigg who confirmed it exactly
- Smells: wonderful lavender/rose/cologne perfume in bedroom (friendly omen); unexplained cooking smells 11pm–midnight
- Marianne's bracelet mysteriously detached and vanished from the bathroom
- February 1931: books begin appearing on the WC windowsill — old theological volumes from Bulls' pantry shelf
- February 25: missing crockery returns; wedding ring appears and disappears
- **February 26 (black-letter day):** Books under the bed; bells ring spontaneously; hymn books appear on kitchen range; Marianne struck a "terrific blow" in the eye on the landing (cut under eye, black eye for days); she was not near the wall
- February 27: Cotton reel and hammer-head hurled across bedroom in the dark
- March: Things thrown at Marianne repeatedly; pins on chairs; obstacles set in passages; furniture barriers; stone breaks hall window; exorcism by Sellwood enrages spirits (stone hits Lionel on shoulder during prayers); pictures pulled off walls
- Sellwood witnesses a stone coming down the back stairs
- **Monday March 9 (desperate day):** Wild activity all day despite workmen in house; Marianne sees heavy iron object being carried before it's thrown at her; cannot see carrier but sees the object
- Tuesday: stones pile up behind Marianne's pillow while they sleep
- Wednesday: Exorcism with Sellwood, Smith and his wife; incense and holy water entire house; no demonstration during but "presence of evil was felt"
- Small tin trunk, powder bowl (identical to one Marianne had wanted), wedding ring — all appear as apparent gifts from the "friendly" spirits
- Two sets of spirits identified: hostile and friendly; friendly spirits announce their ascendancy with the lavender perfume smell
- **Sellwood witnesses** a stone on the back stairs; **George Bull** witnesses Marianne struck on return from stairs by piece of metal
- Piece of brick falls on dining table between plates without breaking anything
- Lionel never seriously injured; Marianne hurt four times but never when wearing her Scapula
- Marianne's name being written on odd scraps of paper in "shaky childish hand"
- Response to "What do you want?" appears as "Rest" or "Pest" and "Marianne help me"
- **Signed: L.A. Foyster, Thursday March 23rd, 1931**

**Second Instalment** (signed May 7, 1931):
- Lionel takes influenza; Marianne narrowly missed by inside piece of a flat iron coming up the stairs — if it had struck her on the head "it would pretty well have killed her"
- Harry Bull apparition seen again by Marianne (Mrs. Mitchell from cottage had mistaken the apparition for Lionel in his dressing gown, in the courtyard-visible landing window)
- Easter season: almost complete quiet
- **Saturday May 2: worst half-hour yet** — pepper thrown in Marianne's eyes; table knife hits Lionel on the hand; creosote used for fumigation works instantly
- Sir George and Lady Whitehouse invited; during their visit **a fire starts spontaneously in a locked, empty bedroom with no flue nearby** — skirting board and floor ignite; Sir George seizes a stone thrown while they are all together in the fire room
- Lady Whitehouse uses lavender fumigation; Foysters accept overnight refuge at Arthur Hall
- Written on a wall: "Marianne get help bother me"; Lionel responds in writing; "Get light mass and prayers here" appears later with "here" added subsequently
- **Signed: L.A. Foyster, Thursday May 7th, 1931**

**Third Instalment** (July 1931, with P.S. July 6):
- Visitors (Lady Whitehouse's nephew Edwin Whitehouse and son; Sir George and Lady Whitehouse) witness throwing
- **Edwin Whitehouse witnesses** Marianne thrown out of bed twice; doctor witnesses poltergeist effects; Lady Whitehouse insists Marianne leave the house
- June 6 and subsequent: after Ivy Bull visits and discussions about Harry's death, phenomena restart
- **Mr. Pearless** (François d'Arles's father) witnesses throwing during Sunday visit
- Marianne develops heart condition under strain
- P.S.: Four instances where Marianne clearly could not have thrown: (1) Sellwood witnessed stone from back stairs while Marianne was outside; (2) things thrown while Marianne was in a different part of the room; (3) Whitehouses present when stone thrown in fire room; (4) doctor present when things thrown over banisters while Marianne was in bed in another room

## The Record of an Experience (Writing C) — "Fifteen Months in a Haunted House"

**What it is:** Foyster's novelized account, written with a view to publication. Uses pseudonyms throughout (e.g., \[Lionel\] → John, \[Marianne\] → Marianne, \[Bull\] → \[Bull\], \[Adelaide\] → \[Adelaide\], \[Mitchell\] → Mitchell, \[Sellwood\] → Sellwood, \[Pearless\] → Pearless). In his letter to Price (10/10/1940), Foyster stated: "the psychic phenomena reported in this writing are all exactly as they happened to the best of my knowledge and belief, and most of them were recorded very soon after their occurrence."

**Key character:** This is a *much longer* and more detailed account than Writing B. It is written in the third person initially but shifts to dialogue and first-person passages. It covers the same events as the Diary of Occurrences but with invented conversations, additional context, and literary embellishment.

**The narrative uses pseudonyms but the phenomena match exactly.** The key scenes include:
- The phantom footsteps (multiple witnesses including Adelaide, Mitchell)
- Marianne seeing Harry Bull (in detail: the dressing gown confirmed by Mrs. Payne)
- The lavender bag appearing on the mantelpiece, disappearing, found in coat pocket
- The bell ringing at midnight as a warning they were up too late
- The full Sellwood exorcism sequence and its aftermath
- The desperate Monday March 9 episode in great detail
- The fire in the locked bedroom witnessed by the Whitehouses
- The creosote fumigation episodes
- "The Horror" / "The Shadow" — a giant bat-like entity in the kitchen passage (distinct from the Nun) that touched Marianne on the shoulder with "a hand of iron"; Foyster believes this entity may have been responsible for the initial black eye

**Critical content:**
- **"The Horror"**: A shadowy, "gigantic bat-like" entity that appeared in the kitchen passage, put out a hand and touched Marianne's shoulder. Seen by others before and after, and by Marianne again. Foyster: "I am glad I did not see it." This entity is distinct from the Nun, who had the rare power of being able to move physical objects
- The Nun: seen always in the garden before the Foysters' time; described as the central focus of the trouble, used by other spirits to torment them because she could move objects
- **Marianne was never hurt when wearing her Scapula** — on the three occasions she was hurt, twice she had forgotten it and once its fastening was mysteriously broken
- Foyster clearly never sees the actual phenomena originate — he always witnesses the aftermath or hears impacts. The objects always come "from behind" whoever is targeted
- The text is **literary but consistent** with Writing B in all phenomena details

## Evidence Classification

The Hastings Report (1969) identifies four Foyster writings:

| Writing | Description | Location |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| A | Original diary covering whole period 1930–35 | Not in HPL; existence inferred from references |
| B | Expanded family circular to July 1931 ("Diary of Occurrences") | HPL; S.P.R. files |
| C | "Fifteen Months in a Haunted House" — novelized, pseudonyms | HPL (acquired after EBR publication) |
| D | Tabulated summary 1930–33, compiled for Price 1938, used in MHH | HPL |

The SPR Report (HBR) and its critics debate whether "Writing A" ever existed separately or whether Foyster's claims about it were confused. Hastings concluded Writing A probably did exist but was lost; Clarke (*Sidelights*) argued it never existed independently of Writing B. This source contains Writings B and C in their complete original forms.
