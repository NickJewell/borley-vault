# Sidelights — 18: Two Ghost Stories

**Source:** *Sidelights on Borley Rectory*, Essay 18
**Author:** Andrew Clarke

## Summary

Two brief, amusing local anecdotes illustrating how "ghost" experiences at Borley could arise from mundane causes or deliberate hoaxing.

## Joe's Ex-Wife

A man named "Joe" (from Foxearth) cycled past the Rectory site at dusk, felt something chasing him with a horrible scraping and rustling sound. He pedalled faster; the noise kept pace. He panicked, flew down the hill, skidded at Rodbridge, and crashed into the ditch. Investigation revealed a paper carrier bag had hooked onto his rear mudguard as he passed the Rectory gate.

## Bunny and Friend

Two fighter pilots ("Bunny" and a friend) heard an American journalist boasting in the Long Melford pub that he was going ghost-hunting at Borley. "Bunny had been responsible for some of the phenomena that were reported in all seriousness as hauntings before the war." The two dressed up in cloaks and ethnic masks, hid near the gate. When the taxi arrived, they pranced in the bushes. The journalist yelled *"I can see the ghost!"* — and produced a revolver, firing at them. The taxi fled. The journalist reported the sighting as genuine in a New York newspaper. "Bunny is evidently still alive."

## Significance

Both stories reinforce the wider theme of the essay collection: many Borley "phenomena" arose from local pranksters, misidentified natural events, and over-eager observers projecting supernatural meaning onto ordinary occurrences. The second story explicitly confirms that deliberate hoaxing contributed to the Borley legend both before and during the Price investigations.
