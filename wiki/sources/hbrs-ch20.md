# The Haunting of Borley Rectory — Chapter 20: Most Haunted

**Source:** Sean O'Connor, *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (c. 2022)
**Slug:** `hbrs-ch20`
**Time period:** 11 August 1945 – 18 December 1992

## Summary

Marianne's escape to America; her life with Robert O'Neil; her death; the reunion of the children.

### Marianne and Robert O'Neil

Robert O'Neil: young, naive, Catholic Irish farmer's son from Wisconsin; member of the 390th Heavy Bombardment Group. He began visiting Dairy Cottages (Rendlesham) bringing gifts; Lionel encouraged the relationship, hoping Marianne would remarry after his death.

Marianne told O'Neil she was pregnant with his child (she had already arranged to take Kate Howlett's baby, Peter/Robert). Married O'Neil on 11 August 1945 by special licence; witnesses were two building cleaners; O'Neil arrived in civilian clothes with three minutes to spare.

### America

Marianne sailed to New York with baby "Vincent" (Kate Howlett's son, renamed Robert Vincent O'Neil); settled in Hokah, Minnesota, near O'Neil's family in La Crosse.

O'Neil: "feckless, more fond of drink and women than work." A car accident in 1947 changed his behaviour. Marianne had to take jobs — first at the *Hokah Chief* newspaper, then at a prairie school near Bangor (claiming a nonexistent degree from the University of London). By 1951 they separated; Vince placed in care. Marianne moved to Jamestown, North Dakota; worked at the *Jamestown Sun*, charmed the editor.

### The Swanson Interviews (1958)

Robert Swanson, a private detective employed by Eileen Garrett (at Trevor Hall's instigation), visited Marianne in Jamestown and later interviewed her in New York at the Governor Clinton Hotel (9–13 May 1958). Marianne denied all responsibility for phenomena. Key statements:
- Thought Lionel produced some phenomena himself for his fictional book.
- Thought Edwin Whitehouse was a confederate of Lionel's; the wall-writing was Edwin's.
- When the Marks Tey Circle came, Edwin left, and the phenomena stopped — "not a coincidence."
- Price was a fake: "a magician who might readily have produced the effects for his own purpose."
- "There are other things inhabiting the house beside human beings."

### Marianne's American Life

- 1955 baptised into the Mormon Church.
- O'Neil broke both her wrists (drunk); she divorced him.
- 1963 moved back to La Crosse; worked as a social worker.
- By late life: grey wig, declining hygiene, bottles of whisky. A stroke in 1990.
- Moved to a retirement home in Utah 1992; broke her hip August 1992; X-ray revealed a lung lump.
- Died 18 December 1992, Utah, aged 93.

### The Children Reunited

**John Knights** (formerly John Fisher/John Emery): adopted by Letty Knights; trained for priesthood; withdrew after discovering he and Astrid were not blood relatives (he wrote Astrid a letter cutting contact — which haunted him for decades). In 1992, heard Marianne was dying and sent a message: "To Morny. From all over here, love and forgiveness."

**Astrid Fisher** (formerly Mary Lex): raised at a convent in Braintree; emigrated to Australia. Her son Kevin Taylor (b. 1958) searched 14 years for his birth mother; documentary (*Haunted*, BBC2, 1994) recorded their reunion and Astrid's reunion with John Knights.

**Adelaide Foyster/Cartwright**: started nursing in Manchester 1944; married Gordon Cartwright, a diesel engineer, 1950. Reunited with her Tower siblings (blood family from New Brunswick) in 1974 after 46 years. Contacted Vincent O'Neil in 1994 to reclaim the Foyster Bible.

### Iris Owen's 1979 Interview

Parapsychology researcher [[iris-owen]] interviewed Marianne in La Crosse in 1979 with Paulene Mitchell; Marianne spoke openly for the first time on condition nothing was published until after her death. Owen's verdict: "She did the best she could with the life she had and the circumstances she had to deal with."
