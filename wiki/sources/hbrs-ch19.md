# The Haunting of Borley Rectory — Chapter 19: The Borley Report

**Source:** Sean O'Connor, *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (c. 2022)
**Slug:** `hbrs-ch19`
**Time period:** January 1956 – 1 October 1978

## Summary

Publication and legacy of the Borley Report; its reception and counter-criticism.

### The Report's Findings

Published January 1956 in the SPR *Proceedings* and simultaneously as *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (Duckworth). Three authors: Eric Dingwall, Mollie Goldney, Trevor Hall.

Key accusations:
- Price dismissed normal causes, silenced critics, magnified trivial phenomena.
- He invited amateurs rather than experienced observers to exclude potential fraud-detectors.
- The Blue Book primed observers with what they should expect — a form of suggestion.
- Sutton found Price's pockets full of stones.
- The "flying brick" in *The End of Borley Rectory* was a workman's demolition brick, not a poltergeist projectile.
- The bones found in 1943 were planted by Price (Sidney Glanville was his closest collaborator and nearest neighbour but was NOT invited to the excavation).
- Of 103 paranormal incidents during the Foyster tenancy, 99 depended on Marianne's good faith.

O'Connor's view: the Report was largely Salter's vendetta against Price, using Mollie and Hall as instruments. Hastings's 1969 counter-examination found the Report was a prosecutor's brief with no attempt to find arguments in Price's defence.

### Reception

- *Sunday Times*: wondered if Price had "deceived the world cynically from the beginning."
- *The Spectator*: "a shattering and fascinating document."
- Naron Fodor (in defence of Price): disgusted with the Report's tone; worried about damage to paranormal investigation.
- BBC recorded a programme (*The Haunted Rectory*) based on it; cancelled before broadcast over libel concerns about Marianne — **it has never been broadcast**.

### Robert Hastings's 1969 Counter-Examination

Funded by the SPR; appeared in *SPR Proceedings* March 1969. Argued: "Every mistake I noticed tended in a direction unfavourable to Price." The Report rested on Price's own archive (which he had openly deposited at the University of London — not the act of a man who thought it incriminating). The authors refused to continue the controversy.

### Trevor Hall's Legacy

1978 biography *Search for Harry Price* was "infused with unmitigated malice" (*The Times*). "Borley's reputation as the most haunted place in the world," Hall seethed, "seems to be completely restored."
