# The Haunting of Borley Rectory — Foreword

**Source:** Sean O'Connor, *The Haunting of Borley Rectory* (c. 2022)
**Slug:** `hbrs-fw`

## Summary

The foreword (epigraph: Audrey Niffenegger) contextualises Borley within British ghost belief and interwar culture. Key framing points:

- By 2014 over one-third of Britons believed in ghosts vs. ~10% in 1950; 39% believed houses could be haunted.
- Borley reached peak fame in the interwar period; regarded as "the most extraordinary and best documented case of haunting in the annals of psychical research."
- The Rectory's Gothic appearance (isolated ridge, elm trees, Victorian red brick) conformed to literary images from Poe, Brontë, James.
- Price's *The Most Haunted House in England* (1940) inspired "almost a franchise" including a planned Hollywood screenplay by Upton Sinclair.
- The story influenced Shirley Jackson's *The Haunting of Hill House*, Richard Matheson's *Hell House*, Stephen King's *The Shining*, and The Amityville Horror.
- O'Connor argues that to the war-anxious 1940s reader, Borley offered what religion had failed to provide: proof of survival after death.
