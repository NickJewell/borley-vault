## Summary

Price's summary of the most significant phenomena recorded by the 48 official observers during his 1937–38 tenancy, drawing from their reports ahead of the full digest in Appendix C.

## Key Content

**[[sidney-glanville]] and son Roger** (first observers, June 1937): Heard taps and clicks. Photographed a "Marianne get help" message, then found new markings had appeared alongside it within the hour and re-photographed — the new marks appear in the second photograph only, proof of appearance during a locked, sealed stay. Also discovered the cats' cemetery had been disturbed — someone had dug up the grave of "Gem" and filled it in again. Glanville excavated the same area and found large bones "that certainly never formed part of the anatomy of a cat."

**[[mark-kerr-pearse]]** (most time on site, weeks in residence): Pencil marks appearing during his watches — checked a wall at 7:25 p.m., found two new marks at 8:30 p.m. A 50-lb coal bag moved 18 inches without explanation. Found a petrified frog in the passage (all doors closed). Locked in the Base Room while having supper, key on the *inside* of the door. Objects appeared. At no point was an explanation found.

**Kerr-Pearse's informal séance with Hennings**: Rev. and Mrs. Henning and Kerr-Pearse rapped at the Base Room table — "a most extraordinary thumping sound" came from the kitchen and "slowly moved down passage towards them" before stopping when they stood up.

**Colonel Westland**: Found the mysterious round blue box appearing in a cupboard at midnight after thorough earlier search. Found the "strange coat" in Room 7 on a visit when it had not been visible before.

**Dr. Bellamy's electric contact-breaker experiment**: Device installed under pile of books on dining-room mantelpiece; all observers in Blue Room. At 12:50 a.m. the bell rang; on arrival in dining-room found all books displaced, none in original position, the bottom book completely off the contact-breaker.

**BBC observers**:
- Mrs. Lloyd Williams saw a "round, dark object" move from a tree near the Rectory to the central fir-tree on the Nun's Walk (nun sighting)
- Glovers and Lloyd Williams (Blue Room midnight watch): two heavy thuds, a lighter thud, "vague shuffling suggesting general activity," staccato crack like an electric light switch being snapped on

**S.G. Welles (Oxford)**: In the Blue Room alone for 25 minutes in darkness, saw a luminous rectangular patch (approximately 12" × 5") on the ceiling, moving slowly back and forth for about a minute. "Clearly outlined... leisurely yet precise." Extensive subsequent experiments with all types of external light sources could not reproduce it.

**Overall evidence**: Price submits the observers' evidence would be "acceptable in a court of law in consideration of their number, their integrity, their unemotionalism, their disinterestedness, and their skill."

## Significance

The observer program's results across 48 investigators — almost all independent sceptics who reported phenomena — constitute the strongest systematic evidence in the Borley record. The Welles luminous phenomenon is unique in the annals. See [[the-observer-program]], [[sidney-glanville]], [[mark-kerr-pearse]], [[wall-writings]], [[the-nun]], [[phantom-light]].
