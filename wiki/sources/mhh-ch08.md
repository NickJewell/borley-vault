## Summary

Price's account of the spontaneous séance held in the Blue Room in the early hours of June 13, 1929 — the first occasion on which the entity claiming to be [[harry-bull]] made contact.

## Key Content

**The séance party**: Price, his secretary, [[vc-wall]], Rev. and Mrs. Smith, and Misses Ethel and Freda Bull (who had come over from Sudbury). No medium was present.

**The rapping mirror**: Price addressed the room: "If any entity is present here tonight, will it please make itself known?" Taps began at the window, grew louder, localised to the wooden back of a large mahogany dressing-table mirror, which acted as a sounding board. Protocol established: three taps = Yes, one = No, two = doubtful.

**The entity claiming to be Harry Bull**:
- "Are you the Rev. H. Bull, the late Rector?" — three emphatic taps (Yes)
- "Is it your footsteps one hears in this house?" — Yes
- "Do you wish to worry or annoy anybody?" — No
- "Do you merely wish to attract attention?" — Yes
- "If we had a medium here, do you think you could tell us what is the matter?" — Yes
- The Misses Bull then conducted a long private family interrogation (contents not printable)

**The soap phenomenon**: At c.2 a.m., in full lamplight, a cake of soap on the washstand at the far end of the room leapt from its dish, struck a china jug on the floor, and bounced onto the floor. All present witnessed it. Wall's written account: "A cake of soap... was lifted and thrown heavily on to a china jug standing on the floor with such force that the soap was deeply marked. All of us were at the other side of the room when this happened."

**Wall's independent written record**: Price quotes Wall's signed account throughout, providing documentary corroboration.

**Price's own experience**: He slept in the Blue Room afterwards and was "quite undisturbed."

## Significance

The first and most important séance at the Rectory by Price's own account. The entity "Harry Bull" makes claims here that recur throughout the book — that it is his footsteps, that he wants attention, that a medium could help. Compare with Glanville's 1937 table-tipping séance ([[mhh-ch25]]) where similar questions were put and consistent answers received. See [[blue-room]], [[harry-bull]], [[mysterious-footsteps]].
