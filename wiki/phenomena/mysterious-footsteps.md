# Mysterious Footsteps

Unexplained footsteps heard both inside and outside [[borley-rectory]]. One of the most persistently reported and broadly attested phenomena across multiple witnesses, including outright sceptics.

## Inside the Rectory

- Mrs E. Byford (nursemaid, 1886): unexplained footsteps; left because of the atmosphere
- Repeated sounds of someone entering via the back door from early in [[harry-bull|Harry Bull's]] incumbency (1892 onward); "promptly and repeatedly investigated," no explanation found
- Footsteps at night (general; reported by [[ethel-bull]] and others)
- During the Smith incumbency (1928–29), [[rev-eric-smith|Rev. Smith]] heard footsteps patrolling passages and upper rooms; more frequent in winter than summer. Smith sat alone in a room with a hockey stick, heard the steps, swung at the source — "the stick whistled through the empty air, and the steps continued across the room." This was independently witnessed by journalist [[vc-wall|V. C. Wall]] during his pre-Price visit.

## Outside the Rectory

Walter Bull — described as "utterly unbelieving" and dismissive of all indoor phenomena — stated he heard footsteps *following him* down the lane leading to the Rectory "on at least fifty occasions." This is perhaps the most striking piece of evidence in Chapter 1: an avowed sceptic, fifty separate incidents, outdoors.

Mr J. Harley: "often felt as if he were being watched and followed in the Rectory; not by anyone hostile but by someone who took rather more than a cursory interest in his movements." Published in the *East Anglian Daily Times*, 15 March 1956.

## Significance

The footstep reports are notable because they extend to witnesses with no interest in believing (Walter Bull), both inside and outside the building, across multiple decades.
