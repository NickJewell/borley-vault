# Sunex Amures

Title of an alleged communicating entity that appeared during a planchette séance at the home of [[sidney-glanville]] in Streatham on 27 March 1938. The entity gave the famous threat to burn down [[borley-rectory]] that night, and which was fulfilled — at midnight on 27/28 February 1939, exactly eleven months later.

## The Séance

[[helen-glanville]] and her brother [[roger-glanville]] were the operators. On asking if there was anyone who wished to speak, the pen answered immediately. Miss Glanville asked "Who are you?" and received the following message:

> "Sunex Amures and one of his men [indistinct] mean to burn the rectory to-night at 9 o'clock end of the haunting go to the rectory and you will be able to see us enter into our own and under the ruins you will find bone of murdered [indistinct] mean you of the haunting of the rectory at Borley [indistinct] the understanding of which gamenl [large full stop written] game tells the story of murder which happened there."

When [[helen-glanville]] asked "In what room will the fire start?" the reply came: "Over the hall. Yes, yes you must go if you want proof."

## Prediction Accuracy

The fire prophecy ultimately proved accurate in two respects:
- The [[fire-1939|Rectory fire]] did occur — though eleven months later, not that night
- The fire did start "over the hall" — it began in the main hallway and quickly spread upwards

The fire took place on the night of 27/28 February 1939, making the [[sunex-amures]] prediction notable for its specific location accuracy even if the date was wrong.

## Identity of Sunex Amures

The BRC suggests one ingenious possibility: **"Senex Taurus"** translates from Latin as "Old Man Bull" — possibly a reference to either [[henry-bull]] or [[harry-bull]]. This would connect the fire prophecy to the Bull family themselves.

The name also appears in the context of other alleged communicators of the time in the Glanvilles' [[seance-data|séance transcripts]], such as "Dominic Drossy" and "Joseph Glanvil."

## Significance

[[harry-price]] in *MHH* (published October 1940) first made the Sunex Amures prophecy public, by which time the fire had already occurred. Price emphasised three "accurate" aspects: the fire did happen (though a year late), it did start "over the hall," and bones were indeed discovered in the ruins (though the 1943 excavation came four years after the fire). Critics note that a fire in a large Victorian rectory was not an unlikely event, and that "over the hall" is a central location in most such buildings.

The episode is further discussed in the context of [[communicating-entities]] and [[seance-data]].

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**"Sunex Amures"** is the name given by an alleged spirit entity that communicated through a planchette board, delivering one of the most famous and debated prophecies in the Borley Rectory saga [1, 2]. 

A comprehensive review of the Borley sources reveals the following recorded instance, the description of the message, and the proposed explanations from various perspectives:

### **The Recorded Instance and Witnesses**
*   **The Date and Location:** The communication took place on **March 27, 1938**, at the home of investigator Sidney Glanville in Streatham, London [1, 3, 4]. 
*   **The Witnesses/Operators:** The planchette board was operated by Sidney Glanville's daughter, **Helen Glanville**, and his son, **Roger Glanville** [1, 3, 4].

### **The Message (Witness Description)**
During the sitting, Helen Glanville asked if anyone wanted to speak to them, and the entity identified itself with a dramatic threat. The transcribed planchette script read:
*   *"**Sunex Amures** and one of his men [indistinct] **mean to burn the rectory to-night at 9 o'clock** end of the haunting go to the rectory and you will be able to see us enter into our own and **under the ruins you will find bone of murdered** [indistinct] wardens [not clear] under the ruins mean you to have proof of haunting of the rectory at Borley... "* [2, 5].
*   When Helen Glanville explicitly asked, *"In which room will the fire start?"*, the entity replied, ***"Over the hall. Yes, yes you must go if you want proof"*** [2, 5]. 

### **Proposed Explanations and Source Perspectives**

**1. The Paranormal Fulfillment Theory (Harry Price & Believers)**
Nothing happened to the Rectory on the night of the séance, and Harry Price admitted he did not take the threat very seriously at the time [3, 6]. However, exactly eleven months later, around midnight on **February 27–28, 1939**, Borley Rectory was gutted by a massive fire [1, 3, 7]. 
*   Price subsequently championed the "Sunex Amures" message as a spectacularly fulfilled prophecy [8, 9]. He argued that the fire *did* start in the main hallway, subsequently destroying the area "over the hall" [5, 9]. 
*   Furthermore, Price emphasized that the entity instructed them to search "under the ruins" for bones. Because the fire destroyed the house, Price was able to excavate the cellars in August 1943, where he discovered human bone fragments, thereby fulfilling the final part of the entity's prediction [6, 8, 9]. 

**2. The "Senex Taurus" Theory (Historical/Linguistic Interpretation)**
Later researchers attempting to identify "Sunex Amures" noted that the name appeared to be nonsensical. They proposed that the planchette operators had misinterpreted or received an anagram of the Latin phrase ***"Senex Taurus"***, which translates to ***"Old Man Bull"*** [4, 10]. 
*   Under this theory, the communicating entity was either the late **Rev. Henry Dawson Ellis Bull** or his son, the **Rev. Harry Bull** [10, 11]. 
*   Proponents of this view suggest that the Bull family's history at the Rectory was so troubled—potentially harboring dark secrets like the alleged tragic death of a pregnant maid, Katie Boreham—that the spirit of "Old Man Bull" desired to burn the house down to forcefully reveal the hidden truth and put an end to the haunting [10-12].

**3. The Skeptical / S.P.R. Perspective (Coincidence, Fraud, and Arson)**
Skeptics and critics heavily discount the paranormal nature of the "Sunex Amures" prophecy, pointing to mundane realities and Price's opportunistic journalism:
*   **Failed Timing:** Critics note that the prophecy failed on its most specific claims, missing the date of the fire by exactly 11 months [3, 6]. 
*   **Arson by Capt. Gregson:** The actual cause of the fire in 1939 is widely attributed to the Rectory's final owner, Capt. William Hart Gregson. Gregson insured the dilapidated £500 property for £10,000, leading the insurance company’s lawyer, Sir William Crocker, to repudiate Gregson's claim and bluntly accuse him of intentionally firing the place himself for the money [13, 14]. It is considered impossible that Gregson knew about the private planchette session in Streatham, making the fire a mundane act of arson that merely coincided with the séance [7]. 
*   **Retrospective Manipulation:** The skeptical authors of the *Borley Report* (Eric Dingwall, Kathleen Goldney, and Trevor Hall) criticized Price for suppressing the "Sunex Amures" script until *after* the fire had actually taken place, using it retrospectively to artificially bolster the Borley legend [15]. They also noted that local newspaper reports stated the fire started among stacked packages on the ground floor of the hall, directly contradicting the entity's claim that it would start *"over"* the hall [15].
