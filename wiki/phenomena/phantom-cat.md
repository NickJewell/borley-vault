# The Phantom Cat

A recurring and distinctive phenomenon at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] and the cottage — a mysterious feline presence reported by multiple independent witnesses across three decades.

## The Black Cat (Wentworth Day, 1939)

Four months after the fire: a gigantic black cat shot between James Wentworth Day's legs and hurtled into the ruins, never coming out. He later met a journalist who described an identical experience in the same location. Locals confirmed that multiple people had seen a cat go into the ruins at high speed, always vanishing without trace.

## The Turner Period (1947–1950)

The most sustained and documented series of cat experiences:

**Scratching and the dip in the bedclothes (28 July 1948):** Mrs Turner awakened by scratching at the bedclothes; lit a candle; no cat in the room (door had been closed all night). Discovered a hollow in the bed surface "as if an animal had recently been lying there."

**The chase (28 August 1948):** Both Turners watched a thin, miserable-looking, grey-white cat they didn't recognise chase their cat Fred under the car. Fred fled up a walnut tree. The unknown cat vanished — a careful search of the open-ground car park found nothing.

**Ronald Blythe (October 1949):** The writer and former Colchester librarian (with no prior knowledge of the Turners' cat experiences) visited Borley. In the moonlit bathroom, he saw "a distinct feline shape sliding along against the bath." Turned to look directly — room was empty. Fred and Holly were both elsewhere. His description of the animal's scrawniness "tallied to a remarkable degree" with the Turners' account of the pursuer.

**The final incident (5 March 1950):** A piercing cat-squeal from inside the cottage — clearly from the direction of the landing. Fred was on the sofa; Holly was outside. No cat was found anywhere in the building.

## Russell Herbert's Three Vanishing Cats — Summer 1941 (brfa-ch09)

A previously unpublished account from [[russell-herbert-bem|Russell Herbert BEM]] (born Sudbury 1922). As a teenager he entered the rectory grounds to answer a call of nature. Walking down the gravel driveway among tall cedars and firs toward the ruined rectory, he suddenly experienced:
- Intense cold and an awful smell of rotting vegetation
- Three large cats arching their backs and hissing at him

He continued walking toward them. They **vanished instantaneously** — not running away, simply ceasing to exist from one step to the next. He fled to his friends, who immediately noticed his distress. He regarded it as one of the most frightening experiences of his life.

This account predates Wentworth Day's sighting (and the Turners' experiences) and is particularly notable because Herbert had no intention of investigating the haunting; his purpose was mundane.

## Later Sightings — 1974 (brfa-ch09)

Both [[hilary-wright]] and [[marie-poore]] independently reported seeing a large cat dash into the road at the same spot in Lower Road/Hall Lane area during the summer of 1974 — at which point it vanished. Neither was able to find the animal.

## The Harry Bull Connection

Rev Harry Bull kept approximately 20–30 cats at a time before his 1911 marriage. After Ivy put her foot down the number reduced dramatically; eventually he had only one left, which he took to church. The cats' cemetery in the garden contains many little graves with individual headboards. The phantom cat phenomenon may be connected to this history. Babbs and Mathias note a 1938 mystery: an intruder dug up the cats' graves one night and no explanation has ever been offered.

## Significance

After the Turners left in 1950, no further cat incidents at the cottage were reported. However the 1941 (Herbert) and 1974 (Wright/Poore) accounts show the phenomenon in the wider grounds and lane area is not tied exclusively to the cottage period. Babbs and Mathias reference a 1937 Church of England Commission on psychical research and spiritualism, which debated whether animals could survive death and appear as phantoms.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**The Phantom Cat** (also referred to in later years as the **"Beast of Borley"**) is a recurring visual and auditory phenomenon in the Borley Rectory hauntings [1]. The presence of feline ghosts is historically tied to the **Rev. Harry Bull**, who was known to keep up to 34 cats at a time and created a special "cats' cemetery" in the Rectory grounds for his deceased pets [2-4].

A thorough review of the sources reveals the following recorded instances, witness descriptions, and proposed explanations:

### **Recorded Instances and Witness Descriptions**

**1. The Gigantic Black Cat (1939–1940)**
*   **Late June/Early July 1939:** Author and journalist **James Wentworth Day** visited the burnt-out shell of the Rectory on a moonlit night. He reported that a **"gigantic black cat"** with **"harsh bristles and snaky, undulating muscles"** suddenly shot between his legs like a bullet, rushed into the ruined house, and never came out [1, 5, 6]. 
*   **c. 1940:** An unnamed **London journalist** reported experiencing exactly the same phenomenon. He felt a huge black cat bolt between his legs into the empty ruins; local inquiries revealed no such animal was kept in the area [1, 6, 7]. 

**2. The Three Hissing Cats (Summer 1941)**
*   **Witness:** **Russell Herbert**, a young man hiking through Borley.
*   **The Sighting:** Herbert entered the Rectory grounds on a hot, sunny day and was suddenly hit by an awful smell of rotting vegetation [8]. Looking toward the house, he saw **three large cats arching their backs and hissing** [8]. As he walked toward them, **they vanished instantly** rather than running away into the undergrowth [8]. 

**3. The Grey-White Scraggy Cat (1949–1950)**
During the tenancy of **James and Catherine (Cathy) Turner** at the Rectory Cottage, a distinctly different "spectral cat" made several appearances:
*   **July 28, 1949:** Cathy Turner was awakened in the night by the distinct sensation of **something scratching at her bedclothes**. A thorough search with a candle revealed no animal in the sealed room, though they noticed a hollow dip at the foot of the bed as if an animal had just been lying there [1, 9-11].
*   **August 28, 1949:** The Turners looked out their kitchen window and saw a **thin, miserable-looking, grey-white cat with a scraggy tail** chasing their own cat, Fred [9, 12, 13]. The strange cat followed Fred under the Turners' car and vanished completely; a careful search revealed no sign of it [9, 12, 13]. Shortly after, they saw the same feline shape glide past an open doorway and vanish [9, 14]. 
*   **Late October 1949:** The Turners' visiting friend, author **Ronald Blythe**, was in the moonlit bathroom when he saw a distinct, lean, scraggy feline shape sliding against the bath. Assuming it was a house pet, he turned to look, but found he was completely alone in the room [9, 14].
*   **The Car Headlights (Undated 1949):** James Turner reported that while driving home late one night, they saw a lean grey cat dash under their car in the headlights. They got out assuming they had run it over, but there was nothing there [15].
*   **March 5–6, 1950:** A loud, piercing **"feline squeal"** was heard originating from the cottage landing/upstairs [16, 17]. The Turners' cats were accounted for (one was outside, the other asleep on the sofa), and no other animal was found inside [16, 17]. 

**4. The Vanishing Road Cats (Summer 1974)**
*   **Witnesses:** **Hilary Wright** and **Marie Poore** (on separate occasions).
*   **The Sighting:** Both women reported that while being driven near Rodbridge corner at dusk in good visibility, a **large cat dashed out into the road and vanished instantly** before their eyes [18]. 

**5. The "Beast of Borley" Footage (Early 2006)**
*   A large black cat-like animal was recorded from a distance on a hand-held camcorder in broad daylight hunting near Borley village [1].

### **Proposed Explanations and Source Perspectives**

**Paranormal Perspectives:**
*   **The Reverend's Pets:** Believers strongly associate the phantom cats with the ghosts of the Rev. Harry Bull's many beloved pets, theorizing they remained earthbound near the Cats' Cemetery [19]. 
*   **Animal Survival:** Psychical researchers who accept the phenomena point to it as evidence that animals, like humans, can survive physical death and manifest as spirits [19, 20]. 
*   **Psychic Barometers:** Some spiritualists view the terror displayed by the Turners' cat, Fred, as evidence of a genuine paranormal presence, referencing Elliott O'Donnell's theory that animals act as reliable "psychic barometers" when confronted with the supernatural [4, 13].

**Skeptical and Mundane Explanations:**
*   **Feral or Exotic Animals:** Skeptics suggest the "Beast of Borley" is merely a large, flesh-and-blood feral cat [1, 7]. The 2006 video footage bolsters the theory that an exotic big cat may have been illegally released and was roaming the Essex/Suffolk countryside [1]. 
*   **Misinterpretations and Natural Noises:** James Turner initially tried to rationalize the phenomena, suggesting his wife imagined the bed-scratching [11]. The loud feline squeal inside the cottage could easily be attributed to a stray cat or a fox outside, with its sound distorted by the building's acoustics making it seem as if it came from upstairs [16]. Similarly, the cat that dashed under the car headlights might simply have been struck and thrown into a hedge where it hid [21]. 
*   **Borley Hysteria:** Skeptics view the proliferation of "phantom cat" stories as an extension of the general hysteria surrounding the Borley site, wherein normal rural wildlife encounters were imbued with sinister, paranormal significance simply because they occurred near the "most haunted house in England" [1].
