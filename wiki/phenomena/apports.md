# Apports

Objects materialised or brought into [[borley-rectory]] by apparent paranormal means — as distinct from objects moved or thrown as part of general [[poltergeist-activity|poltergeist activity]]. The BRC provides the most comprehensive systematic survey of Borley apports across all periods.

## Bull Incumbency

Only one documented case: the **French dictionary incident** from the mid-1880s, reported to [[harry-price]] by [[p-shaw-jeffrey]]. A French dictionary appeared inexplicably in a room.

## Smith Incumbency

During Price's first visit on 12 June 1929, pebbles and a piece of slate were projected down the main staircase. On a later visit (date disputed — either 27 June or 5–6 July 1929), the main apport phenomenon of the Smith period occurred: the [[borley-medals|Borley Medals]] appeared at the bottom of the staircase along with stones and a collection of door keys.

## Foyster Incumbency

Over 2,000 incidents of phenomena were recorded in fifteen months, of which a smaller subset qualify as true apports:

- **Bag of lavender**: undated, appeared soon after the Foysters arrived
- **Hymn books** (26 February 1931): books unknown to Foyster found over the kitchen range
- **Small tin trunk, china powder box and wedding ring** (10 March 1931): the ring disappeared the following day
- **Paper slips with "Marianne" written on them**: found lying around the house
- **Wine bottles** (13 November 1931): the most spectacular episode; see [[bottles|Bottles entry]] for full account
- **Gold pendant and Miraculous Medal** (August 1943 in cellar): appeared during excavations

## Price Tenancy

Several items appeared under circumstances that investigators at the time considered paranormal:

- **Blue ladies' jacket** (early June 1937): see [[strange-coat-incident]]
- **Blue box containing pins** (25–26 June 1937): found by Colonel Westland in cupboard
- **Steel nail file** (shortly after): found by RAF observers in the same cupboard; unknown to the Westlands
- **Three-inch square of rotten wood** ("touchwood apport") (18 July 1937): found by [[mark-kerr-pearse]] in hearth
- **Dry flattened remains of a frog** (5 August 1937): found by Kerr-Pearse in ground floor passage
- **Gold 22-carat wedding ring** (19 May 1938, last night of tenancy): found by Price and Geoffrey Motion in the Blue Room doorway; hallmarked Birmingham 1864

## Disappearances

Several Borley items later went missing under suspicious circumstances from the University of London collection, including [[borley-medals|Medals B and C]] and the Miraculous Medal. Alan Wesencraft confirmed their absence after the SPR investigators had borrowed and returned Price's files.

## Analysis

Price believed genuine apports distinguished themselves from poltergeist thrown objects by: (1) appearing from locations where they could not logically have originated; (2) appearing at moments of apparent quiescence. Critics note that most Borley "apports" occurred in conditions of poor control where normal explanations could not be conclusively ruled out. The gold wedding ring of May 1938 is considered by the BRC to be among the most impressive, given the circumstances (multiple searches of the building that day, two witnesses finding it in the last moments before the tenancy ended).
