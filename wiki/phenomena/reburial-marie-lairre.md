# Reburial of Marie Lairre (May 29, 1945)

The formal Christian burial, at Liston Churchyard, of the human remains found at Borley Rectory in August 1943. [[harry-price|Price]] regarded this as the fulfilment of [[marie-lairre|Marie Lairre's]] most persistent request — expressed through Planchette scripts and [[wall-writings|wall-messages]] for over a decade — for a Christian burial, Requiem Mass, and prayers.

## The Ceremony

**Date:** Tuesday, May 29, 1945 — the eve of the Feast of Corpus Christi.

**Location:** Liston Churchyard, approximately a mile from the demolished Borley Rectory site.

**Present:** Price, the Rev. [[rev-ac-henning|A. C. Henning]] and Mrs Henning (with their young son Stephen), [[eric-calcraft|Eric G. Calcraft]] (photographer), and the sexton.

**The casket:** A well-made, dovetailed cedarwood box (5 × 4 inches) containing:
- Fragments of skull and jaw-bone
- The copper Miraculous Medal pendant
- The thin gold pendant
- A brass tablet engraved with the dates of discovery and reburial
- Henning's visiting card

The gold wedding ring, cream-jug, and nail-file were not included — Price could not visualise a direct connexion between them and the nun.

**The casket was lowered at exactly 5:42 p.m. B.D.S.T.**

Henning recited two prayers from *The Missal*: a "Commemoration of the Faithful Departed" and a prayer expanded from the *Gelasian Sacramentary*. The service concluded with the Committal and the Grace.

## The Missal Coincidence

When Price opened the prayer-book Henning used and found it titled *The Missal*, he immediately thought of the repeated Planchette references to a "missal" that would be found under the cellar floor. Price had always assumed this meant a manuscript illuminated Mass-book. It now occurred to him that the Planchette message might have referred to the service book used at the burial ceremony — that "Marie Lairre" had been asking for the Mass (Missal) to be said over her remains rather than for a physical book to be found.

Henning had not known of the Planchette references and had not chosen the prayer-book as a deliberate response to them. Price regards this as another "indicator" in the long series connecting the séance record to physical events.

## Aftermath

No phenomena were noted at the moment of burial or in the days following. Price wrote that he was watching specifically because of the parallel with the Bradshaw Hall skull case, where Christian burial triggered violent poltergeist activity.

From this point, Price believed, [[the-nun|the Nun]]'s manifestations declined toward cessation. See [[the-nun]] for the post-1945 record.
