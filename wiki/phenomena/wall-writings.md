# The Wall-Writings

Messages appearing on scraps of paper and on the walls of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] — mostly during the [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]] incumbency (1930–1935) and principally in the summer of 1931. The most distinctive and debated phenomenon of the Foyster period. [[harry-price|Price]] calls them "unique in the annals of psychical research."

## Content

All the coherent messages appeared during the Foyster occupation; after they left, only meaningless scribbles appeared. The principal messages (keyed to the Rectory floor plans) were:

- **Kitchen passage**: "Marianne Light Mass Prayers" (4'4" from floor); "Ma" (2'6", apparently left-handed)
- **Staircase**: "Marianne" (4'6")
- **Bathroom exterior wall**: "MARIANNE PLEASE HELP GET" (4'3", 33" long); "MARIANNE AT GET HELP—ENTRANT BOTTOM ME" (4'7") — Mrs. Foyster wrote "I cannot understand, tell me more" underneath; a reply appeared
- **Landing archway near Chapel**: "GET LIGHT MASS AND PRAYERS HERE" (4'8") — appeared during [[dom-richard-whitehouse]]'s and Mrs. Foyster's Novena
- **Other**: "Edwin" (a Foyster friend's name); "W" and "M" symbols in various rooms; hundreds of small marks throughout

The general tone is consistently Roman Catholic: Mass, prayers, lights. [[marie-lairre|Marie Lairre theory]] links the messages to a 17th-century French nun.

## Heights and Material

[[harry-price|Price's]] systematic measurements ([[mhh-ch24]]):
- Principal messages: 4'2" to 5'0" from floor — consistent with a writer approximately 5'2"–5'3" in height
- Lower marks (~2'–2'6"): possibly made by a child
- Material: graphite indistinguishable from ordinary pencil under microscope; both hard and soft leads used

Handwriting analysis by a bank manager: "at least two entities were operating."

Phrasing: "Marianne please help get" — Price notes this is "the phrasing of a person who does not properly understand the English language."

## The Paranormal Appearances

Multiple observers watched new marks appear during locked and sealed vigils ([[mhh-ch24]], [[mhh-app-c]]):
- [[mark-kerr-pearse]]: checked walls at 7:25 p.m.; found new marks at 8:30 p.m.
- [[sidney-glanville]]: photographed "Marianne" message; hour later found new marks alongside; re-photographed — new marks in second photo only (single locked house, two people always together)
- M. Savage and Bowden (BBC): three new marks appeared at the Blue Room mantelpiece within a yard of them while they watched
- Bailey and Wintour: "thought they saw [a mark] grow in front of their eyes"
- A. P. Drinkwater: found so many new marks at 4 a.m. that he used up all his chalk ringing them
- [[dom-richard-whitehouse]] and Mrs. Foyster: "Get light mass and prayers. M." appeared on the landing wall at the end of their Novena, while both were present
- Dr. C. E. M. Joad: found a new "squiggle" after a thorough examination — "inconceivable" that it could have been missed

## Marianne's Own Account

In the 1958 Swanson interviews, Marianne gave the most detailed first-hand account of the writings:

"The first writing that appeared was like m's and u's, loops and letters. It could have been Marianne, but I thought it might have been some little girl that had used our bathroom facilities. Edwin said that it was a spirit, trying to get in touch with me. He wrote 'What do you want?' I washed it from the wall and was **very annoyed at Edwin**, because I had to wash the walls."

There were two other occasions. Edwin said he saw a writing appear on the wall "without a hand as he was passing." Marianne discounted this.

On the "Get light mass and prayers" message: "I did not write them nor did I see them written on the wall, but Edwin Whitehouse said, to the best of my ability to remember, that as he was going up the stairs, suddenly these writings appeared on the wall."

In the Owen/Mitchell report (1979): "The wall writings were an example of phenomena that puzzled them. These would initially appear, apparently from nowhere, although she admits that she herself, and other members of the family 'answered' the remarks by writing underneath them. But she denies responsibility for initiating them... she was mad when they first appeared as the wall had been recently redecorated and it took her hours to clean it up. But she says the writings only appeared when Edwin Whitehouse was in the vicinity, and she felt he was in some way responsible, either deliberately or unconsciously."

Owen/Mitchell conclude Edwin Whitehouse is the most likely explanation — "either deliberately or as the focus of genuine poltergeist activity." The wall writings are the one class of phenomena from the Foyster period that Marianne consistently and emphatically denies producing, even under sustained adversarial questioning.

## Attribution

Nearly universally attributed to [[marianne-foyster|Marianne]] by critics — consciously or as a "secondary personality." Price acknowledged this possibility but argued it would not invalidate the "veridical content" (the Roman Catholic themes, the foreign phrasing, the specific location information).

The paranormal appearances — watched by multiple independent observers in locked, sealed houses after Marianne had left the Rectory — are not so easily attributed. Price's argument: a practical joker would have written more dramatic messages; the mere scrawls and half-formed letters are the opposite of what a hoaxer would produce.

[[dom-richard-whitehouse]] believed the messages were genuine: Dom Richard was present when one appeared at the end of a Novena. A graphologist he consulted confirmed at least two different hands, not one.

## The Retouching Controversy

The SPR revealed that Price wrote to Glanville on 29 October 1937 asking him to "strengthen carefully the marks, etc., so that they are photographable" — a request that could not help but influence Glanville's re-pencilling of the faint scrawl, given that [[mark-kerr-pearse]]'s "Well-Tank-Bottom-Me" reading was already circulating. The published photographs thus show the retouched version. Glanville's integrity is not questioned; but the retouching introduced unconscious bias into the permanent record.

## SPR's Handwriting Analysis (spr-v)

The 1956 SPR Report adds important handwriting evidence:

- **Miss Braithwaite's identification (August 1931):** In a letter to W. H. Salter dated 15 August 1931 — written while she was living 3 miles away and before any SPR investigation — Mary Braithwaite stated the wall-writings were "undoubtedly Mrs Foyster's, as she makes some letters in a funny way." Her brother Sir John Braithwaite concurred in a report dated 17 August 1931: "the wall-writing was obviously done by Mrs Foyster." This is a contemporary independent identification, not a retrospective judgment

- **The "i" dotting:** In a comparison of wall-messages with Marianne's normal signature, the SPR noted that in Marianne's signature the letter "i" is dotted with a downward dash rather than a point — and this same feature appears in the more deliberately-written examples of "Marianne" on the walls

- **The graphologist's dissent:** Professional graphologist Lewis T. Ackermann, engaged by Price for his projected third Borley book, stated he "totally disagreed" with Phythian-Adams's interpretation of the messages and that the Canon "did not understand the mechanics of handwriting." Ackermann produced a completely different reading of the crucial entangled message, including "tombs" rather than "well tank"

- **The speed variation:** The SPR noted two distinct styles in the "Marianne" messages — some written carelessly, some with care. They suggest this reflects whether the author had privacy (unhurried writing) or feared detection (hasty writing)

## Open Questions

- Were all the writings produced by the same person/entity?
- Can the paranormal appearances (marks appearing in locked rooms while observers watched) be explained without a paranormal agent?
- Do the Roman Catholic themes and the foreign phrasing support the [[marie-lairre]] theory?

## Clarke's Analysis (slbr-20)

Andrew Clarke provides the most detailed timeline and the decisive graphological summary:

**Timeline:**
- Pre-March 1931: Scribbles on paper ("m's and u's, loops and letters") — probably **Adelaide** or **Douglas Pearless** (lodger's son, same age, bright child).
- Spring–June 1931: The developed wall messages, coinciding with [[dom-richard-whitehouse|Edwin Whitehouse]]'s encouragement and Marianne's affair with [[francois-darles|Frank Pearless]].
- After the Marks Tey Circle visit (January 1932): No new writings reported.

**The "Well Tank" reading:** Glanville **retouched the wall-writings with a pencil** (at Price's encouragement) to improve photography, permanently destroying their evidential value. The "well tank bottom me" reading (vs. "bother me" in the original Diary of Occurrences) may partly reflect Glanville's own interpretation imposed during tracing.

**The Adelaide writings:** Major Douglas-Home noted "Adelaide" written in similar handwriting to the "Marianne" messages on the kitchen wall at **the same height** — suppressed by Price. Housekeeper Miss Dytor: *"Adelaide was a terror for scribbling — she used to write on every wall — I spent hours washing them out."*

**Graphology (definitive):**
- Lewis T. Ackerman (professional graphologist): *"all the Borley scripts with the exception of 'Edwin' were executed by the same personality."*
- Peter Underwood (separate submission): except for one word, all originated from Mrs. Foyster.
- Clarke includes GIF handwriting comparisons in the original web version, showing the characteristic "g" form matching Marianne's later letters.

**Edwin could not have initiated the writings** — he was shown them on his first visit; they predated his arrival.

## Companion Numbered Analysis (brc-ii-wz)

The Companion provides the most systematic account of the seven principal Foyster-period messages, using Sidney Glanville's numbering from the Locked Book:

| # | Location | Text | Height | First noted |
|---|----------|------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Bathroom passage near lavatory door | "Marianne" | 4'6" | July 1937 (Kerr-Pearse) |
| 2 | Bathroom passage between bathroom door and servant stairs | "Marianne Please help get" | 4'3" | July 1937 (Kerr-Pearse) |
| 3 | Bathroom passage left of bathroom door | "Marianne ... well tank bottom me / trompée / Répond ici" (multi-part) | 4'7" | June 1937 (Westland) |
| 4 | Ground-floor kitchen passage | "Marianne Light Mass Prayers" | 4'4" | July 1937 (various) |
| 5 | Kitchen passage under barred window | "Ma" (beginning of "Marianne") | 5'1" | July 1937 (Kerr-Pearse) |
| 6 | Half-landing of servant stairs | "Edwin" | — | 13 July 1937 (Kerr-Pearse) |
| 7 | First-floor landing arch opposite Blue Room | "Get Light Mass and Prayers Here" + further scribbling | 4'8" | 16 June 1931 (Whitehouse and Marianne witnessed appearance) |

**Writing No. 3's critical significance**: Price initially translated the second part as "light trompée" after Marianne wrote underneath asking for more. Canon Phythian-Adams's analysis changed the reading of the first part to "well tank bottom me" — which directed the 1943 excavations. See [[well-tank-controversy]] for why the "well tank" location may have been a misunderstanding imposed on ambiguous graphite marks.

**Writing No. 7 is the only one with a definite date and witnesses**: Edwin Whitehouse and Marianne were on the landing on 16 June 1931 when Whitehouse noticed the first part appeared on a "clean bit of wall." The "Here" addition appeared after they prayed asking *where* the Mass should be said.

**Writing No. 6 ("Edwin")**: The graphological analysis (Ackerman) found this was in a different hand from all other writings. It appears to name Edwin Whitehouse — the one witness who most powerfully defended the haunting's genuineness.

## Phythian-Adams's Analysis (ebr-ch10, ebr-ch11)

[[canon-phythian-adams|Canon Phythian-Adams]] made two discoveries that transformed understanding of the wall-writings (essay in *The End of Borley Rectory*, Ch. 10):

**'Trompée'**: The French word for "deceived" (feminine form) is clearly legible in Writing No. 3 — "the best-written word in all the messages," written in the flowing hand of a person using their own language. Every previous reader (including Price) had missed it entirely. Its presence confirms the entity was French and protests her innocence of any wrongdoing.

**'Répond ici'** (reply here): In the secondary message in Writing No. 3, Phythian-Adams reads the instruction "Répond ici" — the entity directing Marianne to continue questioning by writing on the wall (as Marianne had already begun doing). He reads "trompée" as the entity's first response to Marianne's request for "more" information.

**The 'Well-Tank-Bottom-Me' reading**: The first part of Writing No. 3, previously read as "Marianne At Get Help Entant Bottom Me," he re-reads as "Marianne, Get Help. Well… Tank… Bottom Me" — a direct instruction to search in the cellar below the well-tank. This reading ultimately prompted the 1943 excavation that found human remains at that exact location.

**The diagonal stroke**: Between 'trompée' and the English portion of the message, Phythian-Adams identifies a diagonal line separating the French response from the English requests — indicating two distinct parts to the message written at different moments.

**Readers' interpretations (ebr-ch11)**: Over 20 correspondents proposed further readings of the "odd marks" near the Blue Room. Notable alternatives: "Finis"; "Sibil, Mas by Boy"; "light" (inverted); Sanskrit characters (unconfirmed). Prof. Stevenson proposed the "Prince of Wales's feathers" spontaneous pencil marks (which appeared under observer conditions in 1938) were a fleur-de-lis — pointing to French nationality. Edwyn Bevan (kinsman of the Waldegraves) proposed the same marks were an attempt at the Waldegrave crest (five ostrich feathers), noting that one of the four sketches Mr Savage saw appear has exactly five strokes.

## Research Notes

*Sourced via NotebookLM corpus research, 2026-05-30.*

**The Wall-Writings** at Borley Rectory are perhaps the most famous and intensely debated phenomena in the site's history. They appeared primarily during the turbulent incumbency of the Rev. Lionel Foyster and his wife Marianne (1930–1935) and continued as less legible marks during Harry Price's year-long observer tenancy (1937–1938). 

Here is a detailed breakdown of the recorded instances, witness descriptions, and proposed explanations from the Borley sources:

### **1. The Foyster Incumbency (May – August 1931)**
During this period, the writings took the form of desperate, semi-legible appeals, often addressed directly to Marianne Foyster and carrying a distinctly Roman Catholic flavor. They initially began as messages on scraps of paper before migrating to the walls, primarily in the passages near the bathroom and kitchen [1-3]. 

**Recorded Instances and Witness Descriptions:**
*   **"Marianne please help get":** Found in the bathroom passage 4 feet 3 inches from the floor, it appeared hastily scrawled, with a dash at the end as if the writer had been suddenly pulled away [4-7].
*   **The Dialogues:** Another message read "Marianne get help (something undecipherable) bother me." Marianne Foyster wrote underneath it: **"I cannot understand. Tell me more. Marianne"** [5, 7, 8]. In response, another tangled scrawl appeared underneath, which Harry Price initially read as "Light In-Write Prayer and O," but was later interpreted as having French words (see Explanations below) [8, 9]. 
*   **"Get Light Mass and Prayers Here" (16 June 1931):** This is the only fully dated message from this era. Edwin Whitehouse (later Dom Richard Whitehouse) and Marianne Foyster had been reciting a Catholic Novena on the upstairs landing. After a brief separation, Whitehouse noticed the fresh, legible message on a previously clean patch of the landing wall, 4 feet 8 inches from the floor [10-13]. Later that day, the word "Here" and some additional scribbling were added to the end [11, 12].
*   **"Well Tank Bottom Me":** Originally read by Price as "Marianne At Get Help—Entant Bottom Me," this message was found outside the bathroom [14-16]. 
*   **"Edwin":** A single word found on the half-landing of the servants' stairs, referring to Edwin Whitehouse [17].

### **2. The Harry Price Tenancy (1937–1938)**
After the Foysters left, the legible appeals ceased. However, Harry Price’s official observers recorded hundreds of fresh, meaningless marks, "squiggles," and detached letters appearing on the walls [18-20].

**Recorded Instances and Witness Descriptions:**
*   **14–15 August 1937:** Observers Sidney Glanville and Capt. H.G. Harrison photographed one of the old "Marianne" messages. An hour later, they returned to find fresh pencil marks had appeared directly next to the message they had just photographed, despite the house being locked and sealed [21-23]. 
*   **16 February 1938:** Dr. C.E.M. Joad and Mark Kerr-Pearse made a careful tour of the wall marks. An hour later, Joad found a fresh "pencil squiggle" on a wall they had previously scrutinized. He declared it "inconceivable" that they could have missed it or made it themselves [24, 25].
*   **7–8 May 1938:** BBC television engineers M. Savage and Bowden were minutely examining the Blue Room walls. According to Savage, **a pencil mark appeared right before their eyes** while they were looking at the wall. Minutes later, more marks appeared near the mantelpiece. In another room, they discovered fresh marks resembling the "Prince of Wales's feathers" (or a Fleur-de-lis) [26-29].

### **Proposed Explanations and Source Perspectives**

The wall-writings deeply polarized investigators, leading to three main theories:

**1. The Paranormal / "Marie Lairre" Theory (Believers)**
*   **Canon W.J. Phythian-Adams** conducted a brilliant analysis of the writings in 1941, linking them to planchette séance messages. He re-examined the tangled response to Marianne's request for more information and discovered the French word ***"trompée"*** (deceived/betrayed) and ***"Répond ici"*** (Reply here) [30-32]. 
*   He also re-translated "Entant Bottom Me" to **"Well Tank Bottom Me"** [16]. 
*   Phythian-Adams theorized that a 17th-century French novice named **Marie Lairre** had been murdered by a Waldegrave and thrown into a well. Because spirits struggle to manipulate matter, he proposed that the ghostly entity **unconsciously "borrowed the hand and arm of Mrs. Foyster"** to physically write the desperate pleas for a Catholic Requiem Mass [33, 34]. 

**2. Conscious Fraud by Marianne Foyster (Skeptical)**
*   **The Society for Psychical Research (SPR)** investigators (Trevor Hall, Eric Dingwall, Kathleen Goldney) and neighbors like Sir John Braithwaite concluded that Marianne Foyster deliberately faked the writings [35, 36]. 
*   **Graphological Evidence:** Professional graphologist Lewis T. Ackermann analyzed the scripts and concluded that **all the messages (with the single exception of the word "Edwin") were executed by the exact same personality—Marianne Foyster** [37-39]. The SPR argued she created the phenomenon to gain attention, frighten her older husband into leaving the dreary Rectory, or to entertain her lover, François d'Arles [3, 40, 41].
*   **Marianne's Denials:** Decades later, Marianne adamantly denied writing them. She contradictorily blamed village children using the Rectory toilet, a 14-year-old maid, or Edwin Whitehouse, claiming Whitehouse was highly strung and emotionally disturbed [42-46]. (Some sources, however, note she did admit to writing her *responses* on the wall [47]).

**3. Misobservation and "Borley Hysteria" (The Squiggles)**
*   **Poor Investigative Control:** Regarding the fresh "squiggles" found by Price's observers, the SPR authors dismissed them as scientifically worthless. Because the Rectory walls were heavily littered with graffiti and the observers were working in poor lighting (torches/oil lamps), skeptics argued the observers simply missed the marks on their first rounds and erroneously assumed they had appeared paranormally an hour later [48, 49]. 
*   **Childish Pranks:** The Rectory housekeeper, Mary Dytor, noted that the Foysters' adopted 3-year-old toddler, Adelaide, was a "terror for scribbling" and regularly drew on the walls at the exact height (approx. 2 feet 6 inches) where many of the lower squiggles were found [50-52]. 

**4. Harry Price's Middle Ground**
Harry Price ultimately adopted a psychological middle ground. While he defended the "squiggles" found by his observers as genuinely paranormal [53], he conceded that Marianne Foyster was likely the physical author of the 1931 appeals. However, he suggested she acted in a state of dissociation, writing: *"Mrs Foyster MAY have been the instrument through which the scripts appeared on the walls—I mean as a secondary personality. If so, this would NOT invalidate the veridical content of the messages"* [54, 55].
