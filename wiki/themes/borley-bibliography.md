# Borley Bibliography

Borley Rectory is one of the most studied and written-about cases of haunting in the history of psychical research. Since first coming to public attention in 1929 it has been the subject of seven full-length books (not including the BRC) and substantial chapters in many others.

## Primary Book Sources (Chronological)

- **Harry Price**, *Borley Rectory Hauntings* (ASPR Journal, Aug 1929) — first published account; two months after Price's first visit
- **Harry Price**, *Confessions of a Ghost-Hunter* (Putnam, 1936) — disguised account as "K— Manor"; ch. 2
- **Harry Price**, *Blue Book of Instructions* (ULCPI, 1937) — printed observer handbook
- **Harry Price**, [[mhh|*The Most Haunted House in England*]] (Longmans, 1940)
- **Harry Price**, *Search for Truth* (Collins, 1942) — autobiography with Borley references
- **Harry Price**, [[ebr|*The End of Borley Rectory*]] (Harrap, 1946)
- **Inky Way Annual Book II** (1948) — contains Charles Sutton's "Meditations" (fraud accusation)
- **Alfred C. Henning**, *Haunted Borley* (privately printed, 1949) — rector's own account
- **Paul Tabori**, *Harry Price — The Biography of a Ghost-Hunter* (1950)
- **James Turner**, *My Life with Borley Rectory* (Bodley Head, 1950)
- **Dingwall, Goldney & Hall**, [[the-borley-report|*The Haunting of Borley Rectory*]] (SPR Proceedings and Duckworth, 1956)
- **Robert Hastings**, [[hastings-report|*An Examination of the "Borley Report"*]] (SPR Proceedings, 1969)
- **Peter Underwood & Paul Tabori**, *The Ghosts of Borley* (David & Charles, 1973)
- **Trevor Hall**, *Search for Harry Price* (Duckworth, 1978) — hostile biography
- **Iris Owen & Pauline Mitchell**, [[nhf|*The Alleged Haunting of Borley Rectory*]] (typescript 1979; SPR Journal 1979)
- **Robert Wood**, [[wob|*The Widow of Borley*]] (Duckworth, 1992)
- **Ivan Banks**, [[enbr|*The Enigma of Borley Rectory*]] (Foulsham, 1996)
- **Wesley Downes**, *The Ghosts of Borley* (Wesley's Publications, 1993)
- **Vincent O'Neil**, *Borley Rectory: The Ghosts That Will Not Die* (e-book, 2001)
- **Peter Underwood**, [[bps|*Borley Postscript*]] (White House Publications, 2001)
- **Edward Babbs & Claudine Mathias**, [[brfa|*Borley Rectory — The Final Analysis*]] (Six Martlets, 2003)
- **Paul Adams, Eddie Brazil & Peter Underwood**, [[brc|*The Borley Rectory Companion*]] (History Press, 2009)
- **Louis Mayerling (George Carter)**, *We Faked the Ghosts of Borley Rectory* (Pen Press, 2000) — exposed as fabrication; see [[louis-mayerling]]

## Key Papers

- **Iris Owen & Pauline Mitchell**, "The Alleged Haunting of Borley Rectory" (*Journal of SPR*, Sept 1979)
- **Robert Hastings**, *An Examination of the "Borley Report"* (SPR Proceedings Vol. 55, Part 201, March 1969)
- **Michael Coleman**, "The Flying Bricks of Borley" (*Journal of SPR*, 1996)
- **John Randall**, "Harry Price — The Case for the Defence" (*SPR Journal*, July 2000)

## Fictional Works Inspired by Borley

- Gladys Mitchell, *When Last I Died* (1942) — first fiction directly inspired by *MHH*
- Terrance Dicks, *The Borley Rectory Incident* (Piccadilly Press, 1998)
- Harry Price film script *Most Haunted House* by Upton Sinclair (1947 typescript; never produced)

## Note on the BRC Bibliography

The Borley Rectory Companion (2009) contains the most comprehensive bibliography in print, including rare papers and articles in specialist publications. [[vincent-oneil]]'s comprehensive bibliography compiled for borleyrectory.com was considered probably the most complete ever assembled before the site went offline in 2004.
