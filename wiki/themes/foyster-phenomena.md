# The Foyster Phenomena

General title for paranormal incidents alleged to have occurred at [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] between October 1930 and autumn 1935, during the incumbency of the [[lionel-foyster|Revd Lionel Foyster]] and his wife [[marianne-foyster]]. The most contested period in the entire case. Harry Price described it as the time "which has been regarded as affording most proof of the alleged hauntings."

## Scale and Nature

[[harry-price]] reckoned over 2,000 separate paranormal incidents in the first fifteen months alone. The phenomena included:

- **Physical assaults**: Marianne allegedly thrown from bed; given a black eye by unseen force; struck by flying objects
- **Poltergeist projection**: Stones, bottles, and other objects hurled across rooms; one stone came through a staircase window from *inside* the house while three people watched
- **Wall writings**: Messages addressed to "Marianne" appearing on walls and scraps of paper (see [[wall-writings]])
- **Apports**: Objects materialising and dematerialising; hymn books appearing over the kitchen range; a wedding ring found and then disappeared; bottles appearing in mid-air in the kitchen
- **Apparitions**: The figure of [[harry-bull]] repeatedly seen on the main staircase in his plum-coloured dressing-gown; a "monstrosity" near the kitchen door
- **Bell-ringing, door-locking, footsteps, voices, strange smells**: All reported by multiple witnesses including independent visitors

The peak of disturbances lasted approximately fifteen months, then reduced after the visit of the [[marks-tey-circle|Marks Tey Spiritualist Circle]] (January 1932), though isolated incidents continued until the Foysters' departure in 1935.

## Key Independent Witnesses

The case for genuine phenomena in this period does not rest on Marianne alone:

- **[[dom-richard-whitehouse|Edwin Whitehouse]]**: Made ~30 visits June–December 1931 during the worst period. Witnessed the [[stiletto-incident|stiletto levitation]] (brass paper knife landing on his lap while Marianne's hands were visible under the bedclothes); the tumbler incident; bottles materialising in the kitchen in the presence of himself, Marianne, and her maid Katie. Told Peter Underwood he "had never had any occasion to suspect any member of the household of trickery" across six months of observation.
- **[[lady-whitehouse|Lady Florence Whitehouse]]**: Saw a medicine bottle leave the mantelpiece and float through the air in the Blue Room, coming to rest by the bed; offered to swear an affidavit
- **[[rev-clive-luget|Revd Clive Luget]]** (rector of Middleton): Frequent visitor; witnessed bell-ringing, bottles, writing, and apparitions — "not the slightest shadow of a doubt" the phenomena was real
- **[[guy-lestrange|Guy L'Estrange]]**: Visit January 1932 — saw bell-ringing, footsteps, bottle materialisations, crockery movements; the rector told him directly "I saw a pencil rise from my desk and scribble words on the wall. No hand was visible."
- **[[captain-deane|Capt. V.M. Deane]]** and the Marks Tey group: January 1932 visit; three to five witnesses simultaneously saw and handled materialised bottles
- **Canon H. Lawton's *absence* of phenomena** (August–September 1933): Used by the SPR as evidence of fakery; but phenomena had already reduced greatly after the Marks Tey visit, making a quiet period in mid-1933 unsurprising

## The Explanatory Debate

### SPR Position (1956)

Dingwall, Goldney, and Hall concluded: Marianne Foyster was responsible for nearly all phenomena, either alone or with the assistance of [[francois-darles|d'Arles]] and possibly [[ian-greenwood|Ian Shaw]]. Motives proposed: boredom; desire to force Lionel to leave a house she hated; desire for excitement and attention.

### Counter-arguments

1. Multiple independent witnesses saw phenomena when Marianne was not near or in a position to have caused them
2. Phenomena predated the Foysters' arrival (and Marianne's birth)
3. [[canon-lawton|Canon Lawton's]] null experience only proves the phenomena was not constant — not that it was fabricated
4. The stiletto, tumbler, and bottle incidents have never been satisfactorily explained as fraud

### Marianne's Own Account (1958 Swanson interviews)

Marianne confirmed that she, Lionel, and Ian "answered" the wall writings by writing underneath them. She said Lionel threw objects to observe people's reactions for his book. She admitted "there were a great many things which he and I went through which no one can explain away."

### Owen/Mitchell Finding (1979)

The most careful independent analysis: "three main strands weave through the story of the Borley haunting. First of all, there were some inexplicable happenings of a poltergeist nature; secondly, there was undoubtedly an amount of trickery; and thirdly, there was misrepresentation and misinformation." They identified genuine poltergeist activity with centres in Edwin Whitehouse (Foyster period) and Mary Pearson (Smith period).

### The Amherst Parallel

Critics note parallels with the 1878–79 Amherst Nova Scotia case (Esther Cox): wall writings, mattress disturbances, stone throwing, fires, physical assaults, a young woman at the centre. The Foysters had lived near Amherst in Canada; Lionel knew Walter Hubbell's book about the case and intended to write something similar. Some critics use this to suggest the Foyster events were modelled on Amherst. See [[amherst-mystery]].

## The Manuscripts

Lionel wrote four accounts of the Foyster period at various times — see [[foyster-manuscripts]] for the full analysis. The core documents are the *Diary of Occurrences* (1931), *Fifteen Months in a Haunted House* (novel with pseudonyms, c.1932–34), and the *Summary of Experiences* (compiled 1938 for Price).
