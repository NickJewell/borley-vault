# The August 1931 Séance (Braithwaite)

A professional medium séance held at Borley Rectory on August 11, 1931, during the [[lionel-foyster|Foyster]] incumbency. Long regarded by [[harry-price|Price]] as a "missing link" in the Foyster-period record. The primary documentation is a 5,000-word verbatim report compiled by [[wj-braithwaite|W. John Braithwaite]] from notes taken on the night, supplied to Price by [[mrs-wj-braithwaite|Mrs. Braithwaite]] in 1945.

## Participants

- [[wj-braithwaite|W. John Braithwaite]] (organiser; friend of Bull family from childhood)
- [[ernest-meads|Ernest Meads]] (fellow sitter)
- [[mr-johnson-medium|Mr. Johnson]] (medium)
- Rev. [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] (present throughout)
- [[marianne-foyster|Mrs. Foyster]] (present during part of the séance)

## Duration

6 p.m. to 10 p.m., four hours.

## Communications

Johnson operated by successive entity-impersonation (assuming each "spirit's" personality in turn), using a Red Indian and a "Dr Haslam of Harley Street" as his primary "controls." Entities who communicated:

1. **"Dr. Haslam" (deceased Harley Street physician)**: Diagnosed Foyster with rheumatism; told him to avoid potatoes and starchy foods; noted Foyster suffered from depression (confirmed by Foyster)

2. **"Joe Miles" (drunken ex-publican)**: Called for drinks; refused to believe he was dead; associated with the Rectory's disturbances

3. **"Mary"**: The most significant entity. A young woman who said: *"I went to London and I had a baby, and I murdered it."* She described returning to the village and being turned out at night in the rain, sleeping under a hedge. She "felt very rebellious" because of the cruelty with which she was met. [[ernest-meads|Meads]] and the sitters attempted to persuade her toward peace.

4. **"John Wesley"**: Five minutes described by Braithwaite as "much better than the ordinary sermon, and much simpler, and in good language and taste"

5. **"Cardinal Newman"**: The closing entity

## The "Mary" Entity

"Mary" represents a fourth claimed identity for the Borley nun-ghost beyond [[marie-lairre|Marie Lairre]], [[arabella-waldegrave|Arabella Waldegrave]], and "Evangeline Westcott" ([[cw-warren|Warren]]'s Marks Tey entity). She is not a nun but a woman who committed infanticide and suffered social ostracism. Whether she has any connection to [[kate-boreham|Kate Boreham]] or any other historically documented person at Borley has not been established.

## The Paper-Message

Among Braithwaite's effects, his widow found the sole surviving **paper-message** from the Foyster period — an envelope in [[marianne-foyster|Marianne's]] handwriting, bearing the word "Marianne" (identical to the wall-message hand), her own written query "What do you want?", and the response "Rest." Price reproduces this as Plate XXVI.

## Reference in Price's Earlier Book

In *The Most Haunted House in England*, Price had noted this séance with the entry: '"Joe Miles" is among those "spirits" tackled'. He had no details at the time. The Braithwaite report, received in 1945 as Price was completing *The End of Borley Rectory*, filled this gap — making the séance available in the Borley record for the first time.
