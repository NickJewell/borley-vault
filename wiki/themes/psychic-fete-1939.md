# The Psychic Fête (21 June 1939)

A public event held in the grounds of [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] on 21 June 1939 — just four months after the [[fire-1939|fire that destroyed the Rectory]] on 27 February. It was organised by the [[rev-ac-henning|Reverend A. C. Henning]] and his church council with the purpose of raising funds for the restoration of [[borley-church|Borley Church]].

The fête was opened by [[mrs-ra-butler|Mrs R. A. Butler]], wife of the future Chancellor of the Exchequer and Master of Trinity. [[harry-price|Harry Price]] attended, as did a number of members of the old [[ghost-club|Ghost Club]].

## Significance

The Psychic Fête represents the last major gathering of key Borley figures while the ruins still stood as recognisable structure. Within a few months, the site and shell passed to a Mr Woods (a contractor who levelled the ruins and sold bricks — some for runways at nearby American Air Force bases), then to [[captain-ben-martindale|Captain Russell]]. The fête was thus an endpoint for the period of active investigation centred on the building itself.

Price visited Borley on the same day: 21 June 1939. [[james-wentworth-day|James Wentworth Day]] recalls visiting the ruins "some four months after the fire" — approximately the same period.

The fact that a church-fund fête was held in the Rectory grounds so shortly after the fire, and was sufficiently credible to attract Mrs Butler as opener, illustrates that the Rectory's haunting reputation was by this point mainstream enough to be commercially usable without stigma.
