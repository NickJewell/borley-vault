# Blue Book of Instructions

Title given to a slim 8-page pamphlet of printed instructions prepared by [[harry-price]] with the assistance of [[ellic-howe]] and issued to each of the [[official-observers]] during the [[price-tenancy]] of [[borley-rectory]]. Full title: *Private and Confidential — The Alleged Haunting at B— Rectory — Instructions for Observers*.

## Creation

Soon after becoming tenant of Borley Rectory on 19 May 1937, Price supervised the drafting and printing of guidelines for his investigators. Copies were ready for the first observers — [[sidney-glanville]] and [[roger-glanville]] — to use at Borley when they arrived on 19 June 1937; Glanville received his copy on 10 June.

Price described it in *MHH*:

> "For the first time in the history of psychical research, there was a printed book of instructions, with a blue cover, for the guidance of those investigating a particular haunted house ... It was a sort of handbook to the 'ghosts' of Borley Rectory, in addition to instructions how to deal with them."

## Contents

The *Blue Book* was divided into two sections:

**Section 1** — Instructions on conduct: what observers had to do at Borley, procedures for watching and recording phenomena, who to contact if strong phenomena occurred, where meals and rest breaks could be taken, where to send completed reports. Two areas were given special attention: the [[blue-room]] (spend at least part of the day and night there, in complete darkness) and the [[nuns-walk|Nun's Walk]] (observed from the large [[summer-houses|summer-house]] for one half-hour before and after dusk).

**Section 2** — A list of all varied phenomena alleged to have occurred over the preceding 40 years, with instructions for handling each type. Covered: bell-ringing, movement of objects, footsteps, [[apparitions]], raps and knockings, lights and [[apports]]. For apparitions:

> "If seen, do not move and on no account approach the figure. Note exact method of appearance... If figure speaks, do not approach, but ascertain name, age, sex, origin, cause of visit, if in trouble, and possible alleviation. Inquire if it is a spirit..."

The *Blue Book* ended with an *Important Note* urging observers to seek natural explanations for phenomena before considering paranormal ones.

## SPR Criticism

The [[spr-investigators|SPR investigators]] seized on the *Blue Book* as providing evidence that Price's intentions were to exploit Borley rather than carry out genuine investigation. The second section's detailed list of alleged phenomena was seen as providing "a barrage of suggestion" that would influence visitors to report experiences that would not otherwise have occurred. Examples cited: [[mark-kerr-pearse|Rupert Haig]]'s experiences in the [[base-room]] and [[lucie-kaye|Miss Rosemary Williams]]' claimed sighting of the nun (the only sighting during Price's entire tenancy).

## Assessment

The BRC takes a measured view: whether the *Blue Book* was a useful practical tool or an instrument of suggestion depends entirely on whether one views Price's activities at Borley as genuine psychical research or money-making deception. Its *Important Note* urging natural explanations first is often overlooked by critics. The document's unprecedented nature in the history of psychical research — the first printed investigation guide for a specific haunted building — is uncontested.

The full text of the *Blue Book* is reproduced as Appendix 1 of the [[brc|Borley Rectory Companion]].
