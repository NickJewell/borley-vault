# The Brazil Borley Photograph (1972)

A photograph taken in September 1972 by Eddie Brazil — one of the future co-authors of *The Borley Rectory Companion* (2009) — at Borley churchyard. When developed, it showed a seemingly robed figure in the frame that had not been visible when the shot was taken. The Companion's own co-author Paul Adams called it "the most important Borley photograph" to date.

## The Circumstances

Brazil, then aged 16, had visited Borley on many previous occasions with friends. On this visit he was alone, arriving around midday on a dull, overcast Saturday. He used a simple instamatic camera with a fixed-focus lens and fixed shutter speed — no specialist equipment.

He took photographs of the churchyard, the former Rectory site, and the lane. When the twelve-frame roll was developed, eleven frames were entirely normal. One shot of the south side of [[borley-church|Borley churchyard]] revealed a misty, cloaked figure on the left of the frame that corresponded with contemporary descriptions of the [[the-nun|Nun]] apparition, and was not visible when the photograph was taken.

## Reception and Publication

Brazil showed the photograph to family and friends, who agreed it contained "something strange," but he decided not to publish it at the time and filed it away for many years.

The photograph was eventually sent to [[vincent-oneil|Vincent O'Neil]], who included it on his borleyrectory.com website in 2004. His colleague Paul Adams reviewed it positively and described it as "the most important Borley photograph" to date. It was published for the first time in *Paranormal* magazine in June 2006.

## Interpretations

Many opinions about the figure have been advanced:

- **Pro-paranormal readings**: a hooded person dressed in a robe-like garment; the figure of a white-faced monk wearing a habit with a close-cropped medieval hairstyle; a person kneeling in prayer at a grave
- **Sceptical readings**: a blemish on the negative caused during developing; the effect of sunlight entering the camera lens

The Companion notes that all pro-paranormal interpretations agree the figure resembles a human shape, while sceptics deny it has any coherent form.

## Context: The 1970s Nun Sightings

The photograph corresponds with a notably active period for [[the-nun|Nun]] sightings. Between 1970 and 1979, six separate sightings of a robed figure were reported in the vicinity of the church and former Rectory grounds — more than in any previous post-demolition decade. The most controversial were those of [[geoffrey-croom-hollingsworth|Geoffrey Croom-Hollingsworth]], who claimed a twelve-minute sighting (a duration that was repeatedly revised: eight minutes → eight to eleven → twelve → eight again).

The Brazil photograph therefore fits a pattern of peak post-demolition activity during the early-to-mid 1970s, and is the only physical record from that period.

## Significance

The photograph is unusual in the Borley record for three reasons:

1. **Independent**: Brazil had no investigative agenda at the time; he was a teenager visiting alone out of interest.
2. **Simple equipment**: An instamatic camera with no adjustable settings minimises the range of technical explanations.
3. **Timing**: The 1970s were a documented peak period for [[the-nun|Nun]] sightings; the photograph fits the contemporaneous witness reports rather than post-hoc legend.

The Companion observes that paranormal photography may follow the same "rules" as other Borley phenomena: "twenty photographers could spend six months photographing Borley churchyard and not capture any phenomena; yet a throwaway family snapshot could unknowingly reveal conclusive proof of the paranormal."
