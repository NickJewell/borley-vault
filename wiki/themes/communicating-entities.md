# Communicating 'Entities'

From the time of the Smiths and the Foysters, through [[harry-price]]'s investigations in the late 1930s and early 1940s, and on into the 1950s, the records of the séances carried out both at Borley and elsewhere are full of messages, warnings and communications from a wide range of alleged discarnate spirits and 'entities' who have become part of the popular presentation of the Rectory haunting.

## Major Communicators

### 'Marie Lairre'
The most famous communicator. She claimed to be a French Roman Catholic nun brought to England by a member of the [[waldegrave-family|Waldegrave family]] in the seventeenth century, subsequently murdered and buried in the cellars of a building that once occupied the site of [[henry-bull]]'s house (see [[the-nun|Phantom Nun]]). She came to prominence in planchette sittings during the [[price-tenancy]]. Her murderer was later identified as 'Henry Waldegrave' acting on the orders of 'Dominic Drossy'; both entities communicated later (Drossy during [[len-sewell]]'s 1950s sittings where 'Leslie Waldegrave' was also named as the killer).

### 'Henry Bull' and 'Harry Bull'
Father and son from the Bull incumbencies. 'Harry Bull' was a frequent communicator at many Borley séances, beginning with the [[blue-room-seance]] on 12–13 June 1929 when he apparently acknowledged responsibility for footsteps the Smiths had heard. Later séances produced more dramatic — and contradictory — communications from him, including the claim in the 'Harry Bull séance' that he had murdered his brother and killed the nun. The BRC treats this later material as interesting Borleyana rather than serious evidence.

### 'Sunex Amures'
See the dedicated entry at [[sunex-amures]]. Prophesied the Rectory fire in March 1938 with the famous "over the hall" detail. One theory: Latin "Senex Taurus" = "Old Man Bull."

### Other Price Tenancy Communicators (Glanville Séances)
- 'Joseph Glanvil': the English writer/philosopher (1636–80)
- 'Caldecote': either Henry Bull's wife or Marie Lairre herself
- 'Mère Beaumaras': a French Mother Superior

### 'Joe Miles'
During the Foyster period, a sitting held at the Rectory on 11 August 1931 (see [[joe-miles-seance|'Joe Miles' Séance]]) produced contact with a drunken publican who claimed responsibility for the haunting. Other entities that evening: John Wesley and Cardinal Newman.

### Len Sewell's Communicators (1950s)
Sewell held many planchette sessions at Borley between 1955 and 1957. Communicators included:
- 'Dominic Drossy' (most prolific)
- 'J.O. Steed'
- 'Percy Long'
- 'Ronald James Hewitt'
- 'Stanley Gardiner'
- 'Harry Doy' (a butcher)
- 'Alfred Henning' (died 1955 — the year Sewell began)
- 'Harry Price' (who "assured Sewell and his friends that he did not fake phenomena at Borley")
- 'Marie Lairre'

### John Deeks
A former Borley cleric (rector from 1642) apparently communicated at a séance organised by [[john-dening|the Revd John Dening]] in Borley Church in 2004, claiming to have been the cause of the haunting over the years.

## Assessment

[[harry-price]] openly warned that certain parts of the séance data "is demonstrably untrue" and that results were probably "due to unconscious muscular action on the part of the Planchette sitters, who... drew on their subconscious minds and automatically recorded the 'messages.'" The BRC observes that séance information obtained where responses are the products of leading questions can be interpreted in many ways. [[ivan-banks]] subjected the séance data to detailed analysis in *The Enigma of Borley Rectory*, republishing several transcripts in full and filling in blanks Price had omitted.

[[robert-wood]] dismissed the entire séance material as "rubbish," calling the 'communications' from the Bull family "nothing but village gossip and the Misses Bulls' slanders of their sister-in-law."
