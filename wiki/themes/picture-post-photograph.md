# The Picture Post Photograph (1954–55)

A photograph taken by [[godfrey-thurston-hopkins|Godfrey Thurston Hopkins]] during a *Picture Post* assignment at Borley in late 1954 that showed an unexplained shadowy figure at the former rectory gate — a figure not visible in the viewfinder when the shot was taken. The photograph was published in *Picture Post* on 1 January 1955, alongside a clean comparison photograph taken from the same position.

## Background

In late 1954, the editors of *Picture Post* decided to investigate the Borley story. They sent journalist [[kenneth-allsop|Kenneth Allsop]] as reporter and Hopkins as photographer. Hopkins's assignment was to photograph one of the two former entrances to the gravel driveway that had passed the front door of the rectory — specifically, the gate at which [[fred-cartwright|Fred Cartwright]] claimed to have seen the phantom nun on four occasions in 1927.

## The Photograph

Hopkins aimed to capture the outline of a gatepost and a short section of the picket fencing (stakes of three different lengths forming a pattern, joined by horizontal wire strands) against a clear sky. It was mid-afternoon, visibility was good. The shadow or figure was **not visible in his viewfinder** at the time of taking.

When the film was developed, the photograph showed:
- The gatepost and fencing as intended
- A **wispy, shadowy outline of a figure** appearing to stand outside the fencing, at the precise point where the phantom nun had reportedly been seen

Hopkins's statement: "I wanted to emphasize the outline of the gatepost and the fencing against the clear sky and when the film was developed the dark shadow was found in the centre. I state definitely that the dark mass was not visible in the viewfinder and that I could not understand why it had appeared in the picture."

## Editorial Response

The *Picture Post* photographic department confirmed that the negative was genuine and had not been tampered with. The editors commissioned a second photographer to take a picture from exactly the same position under the same conditions. The second photograph showed only the gatepost and fencing with no unexplained figure.

The article, "Is this the Borley Rectory ghost?", was published 1 January 1955 with both photographs reproduced.

## Assessment

Babbs and Mathias, who examined a copy of the original photograph, note the shadowy figure appears to be superimposed upon or standing in front of the picket stakes — outside the fencing, at the historically significant gate location.

> **Contested:** The authors acknowledge the long history of fraudulent "spirit photography," including the Cottingley fairies hoax. However, they note that *Picture Post* was a reputable magazine with professional photographic experts, and that the editorial decision to publish — with the comparison photograph — was unusually careful.

Their suggestion: this may have been a mechanical "hiccup" at Borley (consistent with [[ley-lines-at-borley|the ley-lines hypothesis]]), or the camera may have recorded something genuinely present but invisible to the eye.
