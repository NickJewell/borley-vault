# *Fifty Years of Psychical Research* (Harry Price, London, 1939)

*Fifty Years of Psychical Research* was a book published by [[harry-price|Harry Price]] in 1939, one year before *The Most Haunted House in England*. The SPR's 1956 investigators cite it in two important contexts:

## On Apports

Price stated in the book (pp. 29 and 201):

> "Like 'spirit' photographs, the evidence for apports is bad."
> "Very few serious investigators of today accept 'apports'."

The SPR report notes that this directly contradicts Price's treatment of the Borley medals and other alleged apports, which he presented in *MHH* and *EBR* as paranormal phenomena worthy of serious consideration. The contradiction indicates that Price knew the apport evidence was weak yet continued to use it to bolster the Borley case.

## On the Nun Sighting

The book records (p. 297) that the Borley nun had been seen by "many people, including . . . the wife of a BBC director" — a claim that originated from [[c-gordon-glover|C. Gordon Glover]]'s February 1938 report that Mrs Lloyd Williams thought she "might" have seen "a round dark object" which could have been "a short, stooping figure" at dusk, which Glover himself attributed to imagination. Price promoted this doubtful observation through correspondence ("the 'Nun' seen at Borley last Saturday week") and published it without Glover's qualifications.

## On the Petit Trianon Witnesses

The book also contains Price's severe criticism (p. 280) of Miss Moberly and Miss Jourdain for not writing up their experiences at the Petit Trianon until a week after the event: "their reports were written just a week too late to be of any real value" (his italics). The SPR report cites this as ironic given Price's failure to ensure his own observers prepared reports promptly or contemporaneously.
