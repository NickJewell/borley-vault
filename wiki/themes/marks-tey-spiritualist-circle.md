# Marks Tey Spiritualist Circle

An Essex Spiritualist group centred at Marks Tey, five miles west of Colchester and about eight miles from Borley. The group's secretary was H.H. Frost, who lived at Layer-la-Haye near Colchester. One of their mediums was C.W. Warren. During an all-night visit to [[borley-rectory]] on 23–24 January 1932, the group apparently witnessed some of the most spectacular phenomena of the [[foyster-incumbency]].

## The January 1932 Visit

[[lionel-foyster]], hearing of [[guy-lestrange|Guy L'Estrange]]'s long interest in haunted houses through press accounts, invited him to visit. L'Estrange accepted and brought the Marks Tey Circle. The group included L'Estrange, Warren, [[captain-deane|Captain V.M. Deane]] and others. Phenomena reported included:

- Unexplained crashing noises
- Bell-ringing which L'Estrange was able to apparently control by commanding the spirits to be quiet: "instantly every bell became silent... as though each bell had been seized and held by a human hand"
- Materialisation in mid-air of glass bottles
- [[wall-writings|Wall writing]]
- Other activity

The rector sought relief in prayer and all members adjourned to the Rectory chapel, taking with them a relic of the [[cured-ars|Curé d'Ars]]. They proceeded from room to room making the sign of the cross with the relic. The phenomena practically ceased.

The group reported: "The atmosphere of the house was heavy and supernormally cold," and the disturbing entities were formally asked not to trouble the Rectory any more. After further prayers at 5:00 a.m. the party left.

## Aftermath

Lionel Foyster maintained that following the visit the whole atmosphere of the house changed and became more or less normal — though odd incidents continued, and later "there were indications of the trouble starting again." The rector contacted the Circle about two years after their visit and was told their medium had seen the ghost nun and prayed for her to leave.

## Sceptical Interpretation

[[andrew-clarke]] and others who maintain that [[marianne-foyster]] (with assistance from [[francois-darles|Frank Pearless]]) fabricated the Foyster phenomena cite the Marks Tey visit as the moment Marianne realised her ghost-playing games were getting out of hand and used the visiting Spiritualists as a convenient reason to "lay the ghost" and wind down the staged activities.

## L'Estrange and the SPR

The [[spr-investigators|SPR investigators]] severely criticised L'Estrange's published account of the visit (appearing in the *Norwich Mercury*, 26 December 1942), and were forced to publish an apology in the *SPR Journal* in December 1959 for some statements against L'Estrange personally which he considered libellous.

## Significance

The January 1932 Marks Tey Circle visit represents a key pivot point in the Foyster period — after it, the most intense phase of activity subsided. Whether this reflects a genuine laying-to-rest of unquiet spirits, a social performance whose audience had changed, or simply the natural end of a poltergeist cycle, continues to be debated.
