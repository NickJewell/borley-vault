# Henry D.E. Bull's Diary (1858–1860)

A diary kept by the [[henry-bull|Rev Henry Dawson Ellis Bull]] discovered at the Essex County Records Office, Chelmsford, covering the period 25 May 1858 to 1 August 1860. Edward Babbs (co-author of *Borley Rectory: The Final Analysis*) personally inspected the document [1]. Later pages appear to have been torn out.

## Contents and Early Clerical Life

The diary entries correspond to Henry D.E. Bull's time serving as a curate at Holy Trinity Church, Ely [2]. The diary records his very first sermon, which he preached at Pentlow on Sunday, 6 June 1858 [2]. Inspired by the Gospel of St Luke ("Except ye repent, ye shall all likewise perish"), the young curate confided in his diary: **"May God give me the grace both to know and to preach his holy word"** [2].

The diary covers Bull's life before he became rector of Borley in 1862. It contains **no reference to Borley** nor to his forthcoming incumbency — suggesting the surviving portion predates his appointment [1].

## Missing Pages and the Question of a Borley-Era Diary

Babbs notes a suspicious physical detail about the manuscript: **later pages appear to have been torn out of the book** [1]. This discovery leads Babbs to speculate whether [[henry-bull]] kept a later diary that might have covered his time at Borley, leaving open the question of whether more Bull family diaries are still awaiting discovery [1].

The full content of the diary has not been published; only Babbs's inspection report is available.

## Related Diary Documents

The authors pose the question of whether Bull kept a later diary as well. They note that Marie Poore was definite that she had seen a diary kept by his daughter [[ethel-bull|Ethel]], which referred to sightings of [[the-nun|the phantom nun]] in the garden (shown to her by Eileen Livock). This would be a different document from both this diary and the [[dodie-bull-diary|Dodie Bull diary of 1885]].

## Significance

The existence of the Henry Bull diary at the Essex County Records Office is an archival footnote that points toward a possible further document — a Borley-era diary — that has not yet been located. The question of whether [[henry-bull]] maintained a diary during his Borley incumbency (1862–1892) remains unresolved.
