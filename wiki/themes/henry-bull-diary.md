# Henry D.E. Bull's Diary (1858–1860)

A diary kept by the [[henry-bull|Rev Henry Dawson Ellis Bull]] discovered at the Essex County Records Office, Chelmsford, covering the period 25 May 1858 to 1 August 1860. Later pages appear to have been torn out.

## Contents and Significance

The diary covers Bull's life before he became rector of Borley in 1862. It contains no reference to Borley nor to his forthcoming incumbency — suggesting the surviving portion predates his appointment. The full content of the diary has not been published; Babbs and Mathias report that Edward Babbs (EB) inspected it.

The authors pose the question whether Bull kept a later diary as well. They note that [[marie-poore|Marie Poore]] was definite that she had seen a diary kept by his daughter [[ethel-bull|Ethel]], which referred to sightings of the phantom nun in the garden (shown to her by Eileen Livock). This would be a different document from both this diary and the [[dodie-bull-diary|Dodie Bull diary of 1885]].

## Significance

The existence of the Henry Bull diary at the Essex County Records Office is an archival footnote that points toward a possible further document — a Borley-era diary — that has not yet been located. The question of whether Bull maintained a diary during his Borley incumbency (1862–1892) has not been resolved.
