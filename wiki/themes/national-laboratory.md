# National Laboratory of Psychical Research (NLPR)

Institution devoted to the scientific study of paranormal phenomena, established by [[harry-price]] in 1923. Later reorganised as the University of London Council for Psychical Investigation (ULCPI).

## Foundation

Price's first solo success as a psychical researcher was his investigation of the medium Stella Cranshaw (*Stella C.*), whom he met in spring 1923. He booked the séance room of the London Spiritualist Alliance at Queen Square, WC1, and later acknowledged that the NLPR began its work at this time.

In spring 1925, the LSA chose 16 Queensberry Place in South Kensington as its new headquarters; through an agreement, Price rented the entire top floor for his new organisation.

On 25 March 1925, Price held an inaugural meeting of the NLPR at the Royal Societies Club. The basic structure was laid down: Price as Honorary Director; Lt Col W.W. Hardwick as Honorary Secretary; J. Arthur Findlay as Honorary Editor.

## Structure

Publications included:
- *British Journal of Psychical Research* (bi-monthly): short reports and news
- *Proceedings*: detailed reports on specific investigations (replaced in 1931 with the *Bulletin*)

Annual membership subscription: one guinea. Price described it as a "model psychic laboratory" — a suite of six rooms comprising chemical and physical laboratory, darkroom, workshop, séance room, and office, valued at £3,000. His book collection became the NLPR's Research Library.

## Operations

Price operated the NLPR from Queensberry Place until December 1930, when his lease with the LSA expired. In February 1931 it relocated to 13 Roland Gardens, South Kensington. Following negotiations with the [[university-of-london]], the NLPR was dissolved in June 1934 and re-established as the [[university-of-london-council|University of London Council for Psychical Investigation]].

## Honorary Officers

Among Vice-Presidents: Baron von Schrenck-Notzing, Dr Eugene Osty (Institut Métapsychique International), Prof. Hans Thirring (Vienna University).

## Significance for Borley

The NLPR/ULCPI was the institutional framework for [[harry-price]]'s investigation of Borley Rectory. The [[price-tenancy]] was conducted under the ULCPI banner; the [[blue-book-of-instructions|Blue Book of Instructions]] was printed under the Council's name; [[observers-reports|observers' reports]] were submitted to the Council's address. The [[harry-price-library|Harry Price Library]] was Price's enduring legacy to the University that housed his investigations.

[[harry-price|Dennis Wheatley]] was shown around the NLPR at Roland Gardens while researching *The Devil Rides Out* (1934). [[eileen-garrett]] served as Honorary Treasurer during part of the NLPR period.
