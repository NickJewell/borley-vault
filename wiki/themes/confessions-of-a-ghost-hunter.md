# *Confessions of a Ghost Hunter* (Harry Price, 1936)

*Confessions of a Ghost Hunter* was a book published by [[harry-price|Harry Price]] in London in 1936, four years before *The Most Haunted House in England* (1940). Chapter II of the book, entitled "The Most Haunted House in England," summarised the whole of the Foyster period phenomena at Borley.

## The Deleted Sentence

The book's first edition contained a final sentence in the Borley chapter that read:

> "But we came to the conclusion that the supernormal played no part in the 'wonders' we had witnessed."

This sentence — Price's private verdict, expressed publicly in 1936 — was deleted from the second and later editions of the book. When asked about it in 1938 by C. Gordon Glover of the BBC, Price confirmed the deletion and directed him to the first-edition passage.

The SPR report treats this deletion as central evidence of Price's lack of sincerity:

> "It would seem to have been to Price's advantage, possibly fortuitously and possibly not, that in the later editions of the book this sentence was deleted, for in 1937 he leased the rectory for a year and enrolled his band of observers."

## Price's Private and Public Views

The SPR investigators found four letters spanning 1931–1946 in which Price privately expressed his real opinion — that [[marianne-foyster|Mrs Foyster]] was responsible for the phenomena. The 1936 *Confessions* sentence is the one occasion when this private view briefly surfaced in print before being suppressed. These four documents, at roughly equal intervals over fifteen years, "demonstrate a lack of sincerity in his presentation of the evidence."

## The Reference to [[lionel-foyster]]

When confronted in 1938 by Foyster's reminder that Price's 1931 party had given the opinion that Mrs Foyster "was responsible for the phenomena," Price replied revealingly: "of course no word of this will appear in our Report."
