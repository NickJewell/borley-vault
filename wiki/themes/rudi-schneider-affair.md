# The Rudi Schneider Affair (1932)

The scandal in which [[harry-price|Harry Price]] publicly exposed [[rudi-schneider|Rudi Schneider]] — his own star medium — using a compromising photograph, provoking the collapse of the National Laboratory of Psychical Research and driving Price towards Borley Rectory as his next major project.

## Background

[[rudi-schneider|Rudi Schneider]] was the younger brother of [[willi-schneider|Willi Schneider]], both promoted by Munich researcher [[albert-von-schrenck-notzing|Dr Albert von Schrenck-Notzing]]. After Schrenck-Notzing's death in February 1929, Price had taken over Rudi's career, turning him into a London media celebrity ("the Austrian psychical superman") and conducting two profitable series of sittings in 1929–30.

The third series (October–December 1932) was organised independently by [[lord-charles-hope|Lord Charles Hope]] at Tavistock Square — without Price's involvement and specifically in collaboration with the [[spr-investigation|Society for Psychical Research]], both of which infuriated Price.

## The Photograph

During Price's own controlled sitting with Rudi on 28 April 1932, cameras had been set up to photograph any movement of a handkerchief placed on a table. When Price developed the photographs with his new secretary **Ethel Beenham**, he noticed that one image showed Rudi lifting his arm at exactly the moment the handkerchief was supposedly moved paranormally.

Price **deliberately waited** until Lord Charles was about to publish his findings from the independent Tavistock Square sittings, then went public with the photograph via an exclusive and destructive exposé in the *Sunday Dispatch* in spring 1933.

## Consequences

The scandal was devastating:
- [[kathleen-goldney|Mollie Goldney]] warned Price that nobody of any distinction would ever work with him again
- All the more important members of Price's Laboratory council resigned in protest
- The scandal **effectively ended the National Laboratory of Psychical Research**
- Price was left financially and professionally isolated

> **Contested:** Mollie Goldney remained convinced throughout her life that Rudi Schneider was genuinely mediumistic and that Price had fabricated or distorted the incriminating photograph to settle a personal score with Lord Charles Hope.

## The Road to Borley

With the Laboratory destroyed and major collaborators alienated, Price was left without a platform for his work. His solution was to pivot entirely towards writing. A commercially successful book about Borley Rectory — following the template of Ada Goodrich Freer's study of Ballechin House — was the vehicle he chose to restore his reputation and his finances. The Schneider scandal is therefore the direct cause of Price's renewed and intensified focus on Borley, leading to the 1937–38 tenancy and the publication of [[the-observer-program|the observer programme]] results as *The Most Haunted House in England* in 1940.
