# The Rudi Schneider Affair (1932)

The scandal in which [[harry-price|Harry Price]] publicly exposed [[rudi-schneider|Rudi Schneider]] — his own star medium — using a compromising photograph, provoking the collapse of the [[national-laboratory|National Laboratory of Psychical Research]] and driving Price towards Borley Rectory as his next major project.

## Background

[[rudi-schneider|Rudi Schneider]] was a young Austrian physical medium whose trance personality, "Olga", allegedly produced spectacular phenomena such as telekinesis, materialisations, and the playing of instruments [1, 2]. He was the younger brother of Willi Schneider, both promoted by Munich researcher Dr Albert von Schrenck-Notzing. After Schrenck-Notzing's death in February 1929, Price effectively took over Rudi's career, bringing him to London's NLPR and promoting him to the press as an "Austrian psychical 'superman'" [3]. Price was initially so convinced by the early sittings that he issued a certificate declaring the phenomena "absolutely genuine" and offered a £1,000 reward to any conjuror who could duplicate them [4, 5].

Two profitable series of sittings followed in 1929–30. The third series (October–December 1932) was organised independently by [[lord-charles-hope|Lord Charles Hope]] at Tavistock Square — without Price's involvement and specifically in collaboration with the Society for Psychical Research — both of which infuriated Price.

## The Photograph and the Revenge Motive

During Price's own controlled sitting with Rudi on 28 April 1932, cameras had been set up to photograph any movement of a handkerchief placed on a table. When Price developed the photographs with his new secretary Ethel Beenham, he noticed that one image showed Rudi lifting his arm at exactly the moment the handkerchief was supposedly moved paranormally [7].

The sources argue that Price's handling of this evidence was driven by malice rather than scientific objectivity. [[lord-charles-hope|Lord Charles Hope]], a major financial backer of the Schneider experiments and a prominent member of the rival SPR, had allowed Schneider to arrange sittings without consulting Price — which Price considered a "filthy trick" [9]. Price **deliberately suppressed the incriminating photograph for an entire year** [6], waiting until the spring of 1933, just as Lord Charles was about to publish his own positive findings on Schneider for the SPR, then published an explosive exposé in the *Sunday Dispatch* [10].

Price's secretary Ethel Beenham noted that Price was "terribly spiteful" against Lord Charles and had held onto the photo specifically waiting for a chance to "hit back" at him [7]. Price freely admitted this revenge motive to [[kathleen-goldney|Kathleen "Mollie" Goldney]], whom he had shown the photograph shortly after it was taken under a vow of secrecy [6].

## Counter-Arguments: Accusations of Forgery

Price's vindictive ambush sparked fierce debate within the psychical research community.

- **Price as Forger:** The psychical researcher Anita Gregory (*The Strange Case of Rudi Schneider*) accused Price of fraudulently faking the photograph himself to destroy the medium's credibility out of jealousy [6, 11].
- **Defending the Photograph:** This counter-argument was firmly rejected by other prominent SPR members, including Dr Alan Gauld, and by [[dr-vernon-harrison|Dr Vernon Harrison]], a forgery and photographic expert and former President of the Royal Photographic Society, who affirmed that the photograph was genuine and unaltered [6, 12].

> **Contested:** [[kathleen-goldney|Mollie Goldney]] remained convinced throughout her life that Rudi Schneider was genuinely mediumistic and that Price had fabricated or distorted the incriminating photograph to settle a personal score with Lord Charles Hope.

## Consequences

The scandal was devastating:
- [[kathleen-goldney|Mollie Goldney]] warned Price that nobody of any distinction would ever work with him again
- All the more important members of Price's Laboratory council resigned in protest
- The scandal effectively ended the [[national-laboratory|National Laboratory of Psychical Research]]
- Price was left financially and professionally isolated

Paradoxically, despite gleefully exposing the fraud in 1932, **Harry Price maintained that he had genuinely witnessed authentic paranormal phenomena from Schneider** during the earlier sittings, and he continued to champion the medium in later years [6].

## The Road to Borley

With the Laboratory destroyed and major collaborators alienated, Price was left without a platform for his work. His solution was to pivot entirely towards writing. A commercially successful book about [[borley-rectory|Borley Rectory]] — following the template of Ada Goodrich Freer's study of Ballechin House — was the vehicle he chose to restore his reputation and his finances. The Schneider scandal is therefore the direct cause of Price's renewed and intensified focus on Borley, leading to the 1937–38 tenancy and the publication of [[the-observer-program|the observer programme]] results as *The Most Haunted House in England* in 1940.

The researcher Robert J. Hastings points out an additional connection: Price's intense schedule with the Rudi Schneider sittings in late 1929 and 1930 consumed all of his time [13]. Hastings uses this to counter the skeptical argument that Price abruptly lost interest in Borley Rectory in 1929 because he had been caught faking phenomena there by a journalist; rather, Price was simply preoccupied with his "superstar" medium [13, 14].
