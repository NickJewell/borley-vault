# *We Faked the Ghosts of Borley Rectory* (Mayerling, 2000)

A book published in autumn 2000, written by "Louis Mayerling" (real name: George Carter), claiming that all the alleged phenomena at Borley Rectory were fraudulent and that Mayerling/Carter had personally participated in faking them. Babbs and Mathias devote an appendix to refuting it comprehensively.

## The Author's Identity

The author claimed to be the son of an Austrian lady, born in Vienna on 4 September 1913 ("at an unusually early age," as he put it). Babbs and Mathias discovered that a George Carter was born at Mayes Road, Wood Green on the same date. The Genealogical Society confirmed this. They conclude the "Vienna birth" story is invented.

"Mayerling" also claimed to have played piano in London jazz bands under the name Lee Lennox, and to have played banjo in a wartime Berlin night club while impersonating Marlene Dietrich — escaping from occupied Europe by walking over the Pyrenees. Two professional jazz musicians consulted by the authors had never heard of Lee Lennox.

## Claims Made

The book claims Mayerling/Carter:
- Stayed frequently at Borley Rectory as a boy and young man under the name George Carter
- Was responsible for the apparent piano playing itself (through a missing back panel and a grille in the wall — denounced as absurd by the authors, who note Sidney Glanville's plans show no such grille and the well-maintained Bull family would have repaired any missing panel)
- Arranged for the June 1929 Blue Room séance sounds to be produced by cottage tenants tapping on the window with a pole — also denounced as absurd (cottage was on the far side of the rectory; tenants could not have known when to tap)
- Attended a cellar séance in the early 1930s at which Montague Norman (then Governor of the Bank of England), George Bernard Shaw, Sir Bernard Spilsbury, Maurice Barbanell, and Lawrence of Arabia were present — Lawrence leaving because he objected to Shaw's presence

## Refutation

Key points made by Babbs and Mathias and by [[peter-underwood|Peter Underwood]] (in *Borley Postscript*):
- No local witness who genuinely remembered the Bulls, Smiths, or Foysters had any recollection of a "Louis Mayerling" or "George Carter" ever staying at the rectory
- No mention of him in any of the extensive documentation
- He described the dining room as "small and dark" — but it was one of the two largest and best-lit rooms in the house (large bay windows)
- The illustrations in the book are largely of poor quality; one purporting to show the author's mother with [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Foyster]] shows a man who is definitively not Foyster
- The book is "either a badly conceived and executed spoof or is in the realms of fantasy"
