# *Haunted Borley* (Henning's Booklet, 1949)

A short booklet written and published by the [[rev-ac-henning|Rev Alfred Clifford Henning]], rector of Borley and Liston from 1936 to 1955. Published in 1949, it includes a foreword by the well-known novelist **Elizabeth Goudge**. Copies are now extremely rare; Babbs and Mathias obtained one and draw on it extensively in *Borley Rectory: The Final Analysis*.

## Structure

The booklet contains ten short chapters, of which only three are directly concerned with the alleged haunting. Despite the title, Henning covers broader aspects of parish life and local history. Babbs and Mathias consider the non-paranormal chapters important context.

## Key Contents

Henning uses the booklet to record:

### Phenomena at Borley Rectory
- The July 1937 evening with [[mark-kerr-pearse|Mark Kerr-Pearse]] when the three heard footsteps approaching down the stone-flagged kitchen passage, accompanied by a swishing sound of trailing garments; the door flung open revealed no one
- The mysterious coats incident: his wife found a woman's tattered coat on a bedroom door; Mayes was asked to burn it; later Price found a different blue jacket in the Blue Room — both coats apparently appearing in a locked house
- [[alice-reid|Alice Reid]]'s experience of the "cold spot" on the landing outside the Blue Room in August 1937, observed to be 11°F colder than the surrounding area

### Phenomena at Borley Church
- The occasion when Sunday school children were locked inside the church with the key turned from outside, footsteps heard approaching but not departing
- The footsteps in the church porch on 4 June 1947 while Henning, Mrs Henning, and [[harry-price|Harry Price]] were inside (birds in the trees screeching, then quiet, then footsteps — no one found)
- Three days later: same phenomenon with two university student friends
- **Organ music**: On 15 October 1947, Henning and [[norah-walrond|Mrs Walrond (Norah Burke)]] heard the organ playing as they walked toward the church — music ceased on entry, organ locked, church empty; Henning quotes [[ew-jephcott|E.W. Jephcott's letter]] as independent corroboration of the same phenomenon

### Herbert Mayes's Experiences
- March 1939: phantom horses trotting down Hall Lane at night; Mayes pulled off road, nothing visible; Henning cross-questioned him repeatedly and concluded he was telling the truth
- 1937: Mayes saw lights in rectory windows, confirmed by [[payne-family|Mr and Mrs Payne]]

## Significance

*Haunted Borley* is the principal source for Henning's own firsthand testimony. Babbs and Mathias regard it as important partly because Henning was neither an outsider nor a professional paranormal investigator, but the resident rector who had lived with these phenomena as part of his daily life. His observations were supported by those who knew him as being a reliable if obsessive witness. The booklet is also of evidential interest for the Harry Price controversy: it clearly supports Price's observations and Henning's character assessment of him.
