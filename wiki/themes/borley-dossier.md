# Borley Dossier

Collective title given by [[harry-price]] to his case notes on [[borley-rectory]], frequently mentioned throughout both his books on the haunting. The mass of correspondence, photographs, drawings, and notes that Price accumulated from 1929 onwards formed part of his many papers deposited at the [[university-of-london]], where they form one of the named Special Collections.

## Physical Description

For his Borley material Price had specially made a leather-bound case containing 37 numbered envelopes, each envelope marked with a general description of its contents. 

## History

After Price's death, the case was borrowed from the University and passed through several hands. It was consulted by the [[spr-investigators|SPR investigators]] during the early 1950s when the [[the-borley-report|Borley Report]] was being prepared, after which it was returned to the then Librarian, [[ah-wesencraft|Alan Wesencraft]], who first saw the file in 1956.

## Missing Materials

Over the years a certain amount of material has either gone missing or been stolen and ended up in private collections. When [[ah-wesencraft|Alan Wesencraft]] examined the files after they were returned in March 1951, he found several items missing presumed stolen, including:
- [[borley-medals|Medals B and C]] (French Roman Catholic Confirmation medal and French Revolution medal)
- The 1943 excavation medallions (the [[miraculous-medal]] and gold pendant)
- Two further medals: one of the Young Pretender, one depicting the "Battle of Flodden"
- The gold wedding ring apport and the steel nail file from the Price tenancy

Wesencraft and Dr Pafford later stated that [[trevor-hall]] stole the [[locked-book]] from the Library. Whether he also took the medals is not recorded, but the correlation between their disappearance and the SPR investigation period is notable.

## Current State

The whole of Price's papers, including the Borley Dossier, were extensively catalogued by Gillian Murphy and Stefan Dickers in 2004 and are available for consultation by prior appointment with the Special Collections staff at Senate House Library, University of London.

## Contents Include

- [[observers-reports|Observers' reports]] from the [[price-tenancy]] (dozens of reports, several with sketches and temperature readings)
- The [[locked-book|Locked Book]] (originally, before it was removed by Glanville then transferred to Hall)
- Signed statements from witnesses
- Correspondence spanning 20,000+ letters
- Photographs including the key [[sidney-glanville]] series from 1937
- Price's extensive press-cutting books
- Séance transcripts
- Draft material for *Borley III*
