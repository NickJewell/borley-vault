# The Price Observer Program (1937–38)

The most systematically organised psychical investigation in the Borley case, conducted by [[harry-price|Harry Price]] during his one-year tenancy of [[borley-rectory]] (May 1937 – May 1938). The first time in the history of psychical research that formal printed instructions were produced for investigating a specific haunted house.

## Structure

**Tenancy**: Price rented the Rectory from [[rev-ac-henning]] at £30 per year (Henning paying the rates), becoming the first lay tenant since the building was erected in 1862.

**Recruitment**: A *Times* advertisement (May 25, 1937) attracted ~200 replies; Price selected ~40 through personal interview. Accepted observers were required to sign a Declaration Form ([[mhh-app-a]]) and received the printed Blue Book of instructions ([[mhh-app-b]]).

**Selection criteria**: Sceptics preferred; university graduates, doctors, engineers, army officers, BBC staff. All strangers to Price; all strangers to the Rectory and its previous occupants; most strangers to each other.

**Total participants**: 48 official observers; almost all reported at least some phenomena.

## Methodology

- Every portable object "ringed" with chalk; any movement to be noted
- All walls checked for pencil markings; new markings ringed and dated
- All external doors and windows sealed on entry
- Hourly patrols of the house
- Half-hour watch from the octagonal summer-house before and after dusk
- Written reports submitted immediately after each observational period
- Price appointed Arbon (cottage tenant) as key-keeper; only authorised observers could access the house

## The Blue Book

Price called the Blue Book "for the first time in the history of psychical research, a book of instructions... for the guidance of those investigating a particular haunted house." Includes:
- 20 formal instructions covering search procedures, sealing, patrols, meals, rest
- Specific protocols for each phenomenon type (bells, objects, footsteps, apparitions, raps)
- For apparitions: "do not approach the figure"; ask name, age, sex, origin, cause of visit
- For raps: use alphabet and yes/no to communicate
- Final caution: check all phenomena for normal explanations (rats, small boys, wind, wood, birds)

The Monotype Corporation asked to place a copy in their museum.

## Phenomena Recorded

All types of phenomena previously reported at the Rectory recurred during the observer program:

**Objects moving**: [[mark-kerr-pearse]] found a 50-lb coal bag moved 18 inches; tobacco tin moved upstairs and placed precisely over a secretly pencilled cross (Glanville); multiple control objects moved millimetres to feet from chalk rings; the mysterious round blue box appeared in a cupboard at midnight after thorough earlier search.

**Wall writings appearing**: Most dramatic systematic confirmation — Kerr-Pearse, Glanville (photographically proved), Savage (watched three marks appear within a yard of the mantelpiece), Bailey and Wintour, Drinkwater. All in locked, sealed houses.

**Sounds**: Footsteps, thuds, door-closings, clicks, scrabbling — across virtually all observers' reports.

**Locking**: Kerr-Pearse locked in the Base Room with the key on the inside, alone in the house.

**The nun**: Mrs. Lloyd Williams (BBC) saw "a round dark object" move on the Nun's Walk.

**Luminous phenomenon**: S. G. Welles saw a rectangular luminous patch moving on the Blue Room ceiling for about a minute — all subsequent experiments failed to reproduce it.

**Cold spot**: Three independent observers (Horniman's friend, Miss Reid, Rupert Haig) experienced sudden intense cold at the same spot on the landing outside the Blue Room at different times.

**Strange appearances**: The "strange coat" appearing in the Blue Room; the gold wedding ring (1864 hallmark) appearing after six thorough searches on the same day.

## Price's Self-Exclusion

Price's primary motivation for the formal program was to *exclude himself* from the inquiry: "I reiterate that I wanted, as far as possible, to eliminate myself and my friends, and all previous occupiers, from taking any active part in my proposed inquiry." His principle: if sceptical strangers with no prior connection to the house reported phenomena, the evidence would be more credible than his own.

## Evidential Assessment

Price ([[mhh-ch32]]): "The evidence submitted by our witnesses for the haunting of Borley Rectory would be acceptable in a court of law in consideration of their number, their integrity, their unemotionalism, their disinterestedness, and their skill."

Key point: observer-period phenomena — unlike Foyster-period phenomena — cannot be attributed to Marianne Foyster. The 48 observers had no connection to any previous occupant.

## Critique

The observer program has been less contested than the Foyster period. Critics note:
- Price selected and briefed the observers (though he maintained they were strangers who received only the Blue Book)
- The chalk-ring system could not detect very small displacements
- Some phenomena (pencil markings appearing) are hard to observe without producing

The Locked Book (compiled by [[sidney-glanville]]), preserved in Price's archive, is the primary documentary record of the observer program.
