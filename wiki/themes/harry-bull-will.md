# The Will of Rev Harry Foyster Bull (1912)

The will of [[harry-bull|the Rev Harry Foyster Bull]], dated 31 January 1912, examined by Edward Babbs and Claudine Mathias. It is a typewritten document with more straightforward provisions than [[henry-bull-will|his father's will]].

## Executors and Trustees

Three executors:
1. **Alfred Richard Graham Bull** — Harry's brother, a retired schoolmaster (see [[alfred-richard-graham-bull]])
2. **Bernard Brereton Foyster** — a London solicitor and one of [[lionel-foyster|Lionel Algernon Foyster's]] brothers; the Foyster family again named as trustees of the Bull estate
3. **Henry White** — also a solicitor, with offices at Winchester

## Key Provisions

- **£100 to his widow** ([[ivy-brackenbury|Ivy Bull]]) to be paid as quickly as possible
- All assets to be sold and converted to money
- A **trust** to be formed from the proceeds; the three executors to act as trustees
- The investment income from the trust to be paid to Mrs Ivy Bull for the rest of her life, **unless she should remarry** — in which case the income would be paid in equal shares to Harry's siblings

## The Estate

- Gross value: **£6,643**
- Net value: **£5,983**

The authors note this is larger than his father's reported estate — confirming their view that Henry Bull's comparatively small declared estate reflected deliberate use of trusts to keep assets out of probate. The authors also note:
- After Harry's death, the rectory was sold to the Ecclesiastical Commissioners (it had been left by the will as property to be "sold and converted into money")
- **Ivy Bull did not remarry**; she survived until 1955, dying in a nursing home in Hastings

## Significance

The will confirms the Bull-Foyster family connection: Bernard Brereton Foyster's involvement as a trustee — alongside Lionel Foyster's father having been a trustee of Henry Bull's will — shows the two families had intertwined financial and ecclesiastical arrangements across at least two generations before Lionel took the Borley living in 1930.
